'''Aloysius-Michaels Nnabugwu Okolie''', often known as '''A. M. N. Okolie''', is a [[Nigerians|Nigerian]] [[political scientist]]. He is a professor at the [[University of Nigeria, Nsukka]].<ref name=profile/> Okolie has been both the President of the Nigerian Political Science Association and a member of that organisation's Board of Trustees. He studies political institutions and the development in Nigeria, Nigeria's [[international relations]], and [[political economy]].<ref name=profile/>

==Education and positions==
Okolie was born in [[Awgu]], [[Enugu State]], Nigeria.<ref name=profile>{{cite web |url=http://www.unn.edu.ng/internals/staff/viewProfile/MzA5 |title=Aloysius-Michaels Nnabugwu Okolie Profile |publisher=University of Nigeria |access-date=25 May 2020}}</ref> He attended the [[University of Port Harcourt]] in [[Rivers State]], where he earned a BSc degree in Political/Administrative Studies in 1992.<ref name=profile/> Okolie then became a graduate student at The University of Nigeria in Nsukka, where he obtained an MSc in 1996 and a PhD in political science in 2001, both with a specialisation in international relations.<ref name=profile/> In 2004, he was a Fulbright Scholar studying United States foreign policy.<ref>{{cite book |editor1=Akanmu G. Adebayo |editor2=Olutayo Adesina |editor3=Rasheed Olaniyi Olaniyi |date=25 May 2010 |title=Marginality and Crisis: Globalization and Identity in Contemporary Africa |publisher=Lexington Books |page=276}}</ref>

In 1993, Okolie worked as a Government Teacher with the [[National Youth Service Corps]].<ref name=profile/> In 1995, he became a lecturer in the Department of Political Science at the Institute of Ecumenical Education Thinkers' Corner in Enugu State.<ref name=profile/> Okolie became a lecturer in the Department of Political Science at the University of Nigeria in Nsukka in 1997.<ref name=profile/> He was named a Professor there in 2009.<ref name=profile/>

In 2018, Okolie began a term as the President of the Nigerian Political Science Association,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.ipsa.org/na/news/ipsa-vice-president-prof-christopher-isikes-keynote-speeches-angola-and-nigeria |title=IPSA Vice President Prof. Christopher Isike's Keynote Speeches in Angola and Nigeria |publisher=International Political Science Association |date=12 November 2019 |access-date=26 May 2020}}</ref> which has been the main professional body of political scientists in Nigeria since the 1960s.<ref name=npsa>{{cite web |url=http://npsang.org/our-history/ |title=Our History |publisher=Nigerian Political Science Association |year=2020 |access-date=26 May 2020}}</ref> Okolie has also been a member of the Nigerian Political Science Association's Board of Trustees.<ref name=npsa/> Before he was elected President of the Nigerian Political Science Association, he was the National Research Director for the organisation.<ref>{{cite book |editor1=Aloysius-Michaels Okolie |editor2=Shuaibu Ibrahim |editor3=Hassan Saliu |year=2016 |title=Governance, Economy, and National Security in Nigeria |publisher=Nigerian Political Science Association |page=vii}}</ref>

==Research==
In addition to research in peer-reviewed academic journals, Okolie has been the sole author of several books. In 2004 he published a book called ''Political Behaviour'', published by the Academic press in Enugu.<ref name=profile/> With the same publisher in 2005 he wrote ''Statistics for Political Data Analysis''.<ref name=profile/> Okolie was a coauthor of ''Law, Politics and Mass Media in Nigeria'' in 2004 and ''Politics of Development and Underdevelopment'' in 2017.<ref name=profile/>

Okolie has also edited several volumes, largely focusing on the politics and political development of Nigeria. In 2009 he was an editor of ''Contemporary Readings on Nigeria's External Relations: Issues, Perspectives and Challenges''. He also edited the 2014 book ''Politics and Law in Africa: Current and Emerging Issues'', the 2015 book ''Norms and Practices of Global Political Economy'', and the 2016 volume ''Governance, Economy, and National Security in Nigeria''.<ref name=profile/>
