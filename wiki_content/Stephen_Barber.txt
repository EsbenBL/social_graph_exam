'''Stephen Barber''' (born 1974) is a [[United Kingdom|British]] [[Political science|political scientist]], [[political economist]] and author.  He is  [[Professor]] of Global Affairs at [[Regent's University London]]. <ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.regents.ac.uk/about/our-people/professor-stephen-barber=}}</ref> He is also a senior fellow at the Global Policy Institute.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.global-policy.com/index.php?id=125|title=Stephen Barber page at the Global Policy Institute|website=Global-policy.com|access-date=30 November 2017|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070929080704/http://www.global-policy.com/index.php?id=125|archive-date=29 September 2007}}</ref> He has also worked in the [[European Research Forum]] and is a former director of [[MBA]]. He is a specialist in British [[public policy]] and [[party politics]], [[political economy]] and having worked in the City of London, the [[globalisation]] of [[financial markets]]. He holds a [[Bachelor of Arts|BA]] in [[government]], an [[Master of Arts|MA]] in [[contemporary history]] and a [[Doctor of Philosophy|PhD]] in [[political science]], awarded by several London universities.  He is a [[Fellow]] of the [[Royal Historical Society]] and Member of the [[Securities & Investment Institute]].  Following the [[Northern Rock]] and banking credit crisis in 2008, he outlined his concept of a regulatory cycle of economic behaviour.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.selftrade.co.uk/research-education/in-the-media/pdf/22_SHARES_170408.pdf|title=UK Execution-Only Stockbrokers - Invest In Shares|website=Selftrade.co.uk|access-date=30 November 2017|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080807165656/http://www.selftrade.co.uk/research-education/in-the-media/pdf/22_SHARES_170408.pdf|archive-date=7 August 2008}}</ref>

He wrote and presented the [Radio 4] programme 'The Case for Doing Nothing' which was broadcast in October 2016.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b07x20bz|title=The Case for Doing Nothing - BBC Radio 4|website=[[BBC]]|accessdate=30 November 2017}}</ref>
