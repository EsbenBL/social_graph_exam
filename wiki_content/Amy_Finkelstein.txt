'''Amy Nadya Finkelstein''' (born November 2, 1973) is a professor of [[economics]] at the [[Massachusetts Institute of Technology]] (MIT), the co-director and research associate of the Public Economics Program at the [[National Bureau of Economic Research]], and the co-Scientific Director of [[Abdul Latif Jameel Poverty Action Lab|J-PAL]] North America.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.povertyactionlab.org/north-america|title=Abdul Latif Jameel Poverty Action Lab (J-PAL) North America|accessdate=15 July 2015}}</ref> She was awarded the 2012 [[John Bates Clark Medal]] for her contributions to economics.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://articles.boston.com/2012-04-28/business/31419515_1_economics-mit-paul-krugman|title=MIT economics professor awarded Bates Clark medal|date=28 April 2012|accessdate=28 April 2012|publisher=[[The Boston Globe]]}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://chronicle.com/blogs/ticker/mit-economist-of-health-care-wins-john-bates-clark-medal/42712|title=MIT Economist of Health Care Wins John Bates Clark Medal – The Ticker - Blogs - The Chronicle of Higher Education|website=chronicle.com|language=en-US|access-date=2018-04-04}}</ref> She was elected to the [[National Academy of Sciences]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.nasonline.org/member-directory/members/20021454.html|title=Amy Finkelstein|website=www.nasonline.org|access-date=2020-02-22}}</ref> and won a [[MacArthur Fellows Program|MacArthur "Genius" fellowship]]<ref>[https://www.chronicle.com/article/Meet-the-Academics-Who-Nabbed/244720 Meet the Academics Who Nabbed This Year’s MacArthur ‘Genius’ Grants], Julian Wyllie, ''[[The Chronicle of Higher Education]]'', October 4, 2018.</ref> in 2018.

== Education ==
Finkelstein studied government at [[Harvard University]], where she was a [[Truman Scholar]] and received an [[Bachelor of Arts|AB]] [[summa cum laude]] in 1995. At Harvard, her interest in economics was inspired in part by taking economist [[Lawrence F. Katz|Lawrence Katz]]'s course "Social Problems in the American Economy".<ref name="Dizikes">{{cite news |last1=Dizikes |first1=Peter |title=A healthy understanding |url=https://www.technologyreview.com/2020/04/15/999351/a-healthy-understanding/ |accessdate=23 April 2020 |work=[[MIT Technology Review]] |date=April 15, 2020}}</ref> She was a [[Marshall Scholar]] at [[University of Oxford|Oxford University]], where she received an [[M.Phil.]] in [[economics]] in 1997. She received her [[Doctor of Philosophy|PhD]] in economics from MIT in 2001 under supervision of [[James M. Poterba]] and [[Jonathan Gruber (economist)|Jonathan Gruber]].<ref name="Thesis Supervisors">Finkelstein, Amy (2001), ''[https://dspace.mit.edu/handle/1721.1/8652 Adverse selection and government intervention in life and health insurance markets]''. Ph.D. dissertation, Massachusetts Institute of Technology.</ref>

== Career ==
Finkelstein was a Junior Fellow at the [[Harvard Society of Fellows]] for three years, after which she joined the MIT faculty in 2005<ref name="shortbio">{{cite web|url=http://economics.mit.edu/faculty/afink/short|title=Amy Finkelstein – Short Biography|publisher=[[Massachusetts Institute of Technology]]|accessdate=28 April 2012}}</ref> and received tenure within three years.<ref name="Dizikes"/>

In 2016, MIT's School of Humanities, Arts, and Social Sciences named Finkelstein the John and Jennie S. MacDonald Professor for a five-year term.<ref name="d22517" /> The professorship was established with a gift by Edmund MacDonald, and recognizes Finkelstein's outstanding achievements in the field of economics.<ref name="d22517">{{cite web|url=https://news.mit.edu/2016/nine-shass-faculty-members-awarded-named-professorships-1110|title=Nine SHASS faculty members awarded named professorships|publisher=MIT News|accessdate=2016-11-10}}</ref>

== Research ==
Finkelstein's primary expertise is in [[public finance]] and [[health economics]], focusing particularly on [[health insurance]].<ref name="Dizikes"/> She conducts research into [[market failure]]s and [[government intervention]] in [[insurance]] markets, and the impact of [[public policy]] on health care and health insurance.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2011/07/07/health/policy/07medicaid.html?_r=1|title=First Study of Its Kind Shows Benefits of Providing Medical Insurance to Poor|work=[[The New York Times]]|date=7 July 2011|accessdate=28 April 2012}}</ref> Together with [[Katherine Baicker]], she is one of two principal investigators of the [[Oregon Medicaid health experiment|Oregon Health Insurance Experiment]], a randomized evaluation of the impact of expanding Medicaid to low-income adults.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nber.org/oregon/ |title=National Bureau of Economic Research (NBER): Oregon Health Insurance Experiment|accessdate=15 July 2015}}</ref> Her research has shown that newly enrolled Medicaid patients make more trips overall to providers after acquiring insurance, make more visits to emergency rooms, and benefit financially from having insurance, among other findings.<ref name="c47941">{{cite web|url=https://news.mit.edu/2017/medicaid-patients-wait-longer-doctors-0501|title=Testing their patients|publisher=MIT News|accessdate=2017-05-01}}</ref> Finkelstein said that the body of research, including her work on the effects of the 2008 Medicaid expansion in Oregon, have made her confident that health insurance improves health.<ref name="27016b">{{cite web|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/wonk/wp/2017/03/22/will-repealing-obamacare-really-kill-60000-people/|title=Will repealing Obamacare really kill 60,000 people?|work=Washington Post|accessdate=2017-03-22}}</ref>

== Awards ==
In 2008, Finkelstein was awarded the [[Elaine Bennett Research Prize]] by the Committee on the Status of Women in the Economics Profession (CSWEP), for her contributions to the economics profession.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cswep.org/awards.htm|title=CSWEP Awards and Prizes|publisher=Committee on the Status of Women in the Economics Profession|accessdate=28 April 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120315132129/http://www.cswep.org/awards.htm|archive-date=2012-03-15|url-status=dead}}</ref> In 2012, she was awarded the [[John Bates Clark Medal]] from the [[American Economic Association]]. The award cited her research as "a model of how theory and empirics can be combined in creative ways".<ref name=":0" /> In 2018, Finkelstein received a [[MacArthur Fellows Program|MacArthur "Genius" Grant]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.macfound.org/fellows/1010/|title = Amy Finkelstein}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Finkelstein is married to MIT economist [[Benjamin Olken]].<ref name="Dizikes"/>
