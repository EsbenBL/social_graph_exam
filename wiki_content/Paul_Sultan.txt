'''Paul Sultan''' was a [[Labour economics|labour]] [[economist]], born in 1924 in [[Vancouver]], [[Canada]], died in 2019 [[Edwardsville, Illinois]]

==Education==
After serving as an aircraft pilot during [[World War II]] for the [[Royal Canadian Air Force]], he pursued an academic career at [[Cornell University]], the [[University at Buffalo]], [[Claremont Graduate School]] in California, [[UCLA]], [[Simon Fraser University]] and the [[University of Southern Illinois]].

==Writings==
His early text, ''Labour Economics'',<ref>Sultan, Paul (1957), ''Labor Economics'', New York: Henry Holt and Company.</ref> pioneered the relationship between the inflation rate and the unemployment rate, now known as the [[Phillips curve]], which Sultan was the first to represent as a graph.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ideas.repec.org/a/fip/fedrer/y1985isep-octp17-24nv.71no.5.html|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20071025042051/http://ideas.repec.org/a/fip/fedrer/y1985isep-octp17-24nv.71no.5.html|archive-date=2007-10-25|title=The early history of the Phillips curve|publisher=[[Research Papers in Economics]]|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.richmondfed.org/-/media/richmondfedorg/publications/research/economic_review/1985/pdf/er710502.pdf|title=The early history of the Phillips curve|author=Thomas M. Humphrey|page=23|quote=Priority for drawing the Phillips curve goes to Paul Sultan, whose contribution predates Phillips’ by one year.|work=Economic Review|publisher=[[Federal Reserve Bank of Richmond]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal|url=https://ideas.repec.org/a/bla/econom/v38y1971i151p319-20.html|title=Sultan Schedule and Phillips Curve: An Historical Note|first1=E.|last1=Amid-Hozour|first2=D. T.|last2=Dick|first3=Richard L.|last3=Lucier|date=25 February 1971|journal=Economica|volume=38|issue=151|pages=319–320|access-date=25 February 2018|via=ideas.repec.org|doi=10.2307/2552849}}</ref> Sultan has written five books and hundreds of articles, monographs and position papers.  In recognition of his work in labour-management relations he was honoured in 1997 through being admitted to the Southwestern Illinois Labour Management Hall of Fame.
