'''Trevor J. Pinch''' (born 1 January 1952),<ref name=Congress>{{cite web |title= Pinch, T. J. (Trevor J.) | url =http://id.loc.gov/authorities/names/n81099695.html | publisher = Library of Congress | access-date = 12 February 2015 | quote = data sheet (b. 1-1-52) }}</ref> is a British sociologist, part-time musician and former chair of the [[Science and Technology Studies]] department at [[Cornell University]]. In 2018, he won the J.D. Bernal Prize from the [[Society for Social Studies of Science]] for "distinguished contributions to Science and Technology Studies over the course of [a] career".<ref>http://www.4sonline.org/prizes/bernal, retrieved 19 February 2018</ref>

==Early life==
Pinch was born in [[Lisnaskea]], Northern Ireland.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://chronicle.cornell.edu/stories/1999/10/christopher-ober-and-trevor-pinch-named-department-chairs|title=''Christopher Ober and Trevor Pinch named to department chairs at Cornell'', Cornell Chronicle|work=[[Cornell University]]|access-date=5 January 2015}}</ref>

== Education ==
He has a degree in [[Physics]] from [[Imperial College London]] and a PhD in Sociology from the [[University of Bath]].

== Career ==
He taught sociology at the [[University of York]] before moving to the United States.

Together with [[Wiebe Bijker]], Pinch started the movement known as [[Social Construction of Technology]] (SCOT) within the sociology of science.

==Works==
He is a significant contributor to the study of [[Sound culture]], and his books include a major study of [[Robert Moog]].

Pinch's book, ''Confronting Nature'' is widely considered the definitive sociological account of the history of the solar neutrino problem, and was mentioned by Raymond Davis in his 2002 Nobel Prize autobiography.<ref name=Nobel>{{cite web|title=Raymond Davis Jr. – Biographical|url=https://www.nobelprize.org/nobel_prizes/physics/laureates/2002/davis-bio.html|website=Nobelprize.org|publisher=Nobel Media AB 2014|access-date=31 December 2015}}</ref>
