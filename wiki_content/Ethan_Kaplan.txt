'''Ethan Kaplan''' is an associate professor of economics at the [[University of Maryland, College Park|University of Maryland]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.econ.umd.edu/faculty/profiles/kaplan |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2011-10-26 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20111118141218/http://www.econ.umd.edu/faculty/profiles/kaplan |archivedate=2011-11-18 }}</ref> 

== Career ==
Prior to his work at the University of Maryland, he was an assistant professor at the University of Maryland, College Park, a visiting assistant professor of economics at the [[University of California, Berkeley]], and an assistant professor of [[economics]] at the [[Institute for International Economic Studies]] at [[Stockholm University]] in [[Sweden]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.iies.su.se/~ekaplan/|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20081106205554/http://www.iies.su.se/~ekaplan/|url-status=dead|title=Ethan Kaplan at Institute for International Economic Studies at Stockholm University|archivedate=November 6, 2008}}</ref> 

=== Papers ===
A working paper<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://elsa.berkeley.edu/~sdellavi/wp/foxvote06-03-30.pdf|title="The Fox News Effect: Media Bias and Voting" (Working Paper with Stefano DellaVigna)}}</ref> authored by Kaplan and [[Stefano DellaVigna]] (also of the University of California at Berkeley) examining the impact of [[Fox News Channel]] on electoral outcomes received significant attention after being cited by [[Alan Krueger]] in a ''[[New York Times]]'' op-ed.<ref>[https://www.nytimes.com/2005/08/18/business/media/18scene.html "Fair? Balanced? A Study Finds It Does Not Matter" (New York Times)]. Similar piece is [https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2006/05/03/AR2006050302299.html "The Fox News Effect" (Article by Richard Morin in Washington Post)].</ref> In 2007, Kaplan and [[Stefano DellaVigna]] published their findings in the ''Quarterly Journal of Economics'', finding that Fox News had a mild but statistically significant effect in boosting Republican vote share. <ref>{{Cite journal|url=https://academic.oup.com/qje/article/122/3/1187/1879517|title=The Fox News Effect: Media Bias and Voting|first1=Stefano|last1=DellaVigna|first2=Ethan|last2=Kaplan|date=August 1, 2007|journal=The Quarterly Journal of Economics|volume=122|issue=3|pages=1187â€“1234|via=academic.oup.com|doi=10.1162/qjec.122.3.1187|s2cid=16610755}}</ref>

== Education ==
He received his Ph.D. in economics from the [[University of California, Berkeley]], in 2005, his M.A. in [[development economics]] from [[Stanford University]] in 1999 and his B.A. in [[history]] from UC Berkeley in 1992.
