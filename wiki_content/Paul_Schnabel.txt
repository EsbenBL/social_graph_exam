'''Paul Schnabel''' (born July 17, 1948 in [[Bergen op Zoom]]) is a Dutch [[sociology|sociologist]] and {{As of|2006|lc=on}} the director of an agency of the [[Politics of the Netherlands|Dutch government]] called [[Sociaal en Cultureel Planbureau]] (''Netherlands Institute for Social Research''), usually abbreviated as ''SCP'' (or ''SCPB'').<ref name=OECD>{{cite web | url=http://www.oecd.org/speaker/0,3438,en_21571361_31938349_38463975_1_1_1_1,00.html|title=Paul Schnabel|publisher=[[OECD]]}}</ref> He is also professor at [[Utrecht University]]. Since 9 June 2015, he has been a member of the [[Senate (Netherlands)|Senate]] on behalf of [[Democrats 66]] (D66).

== Biography ==
Schnabel grew up in [[Breda]]. He studied sociology at Utrecht and [[Bielefeld University|Bielefeld]] Universities. He received his [[Doctor of Philosophy|Ph.D.]] in 1982 for his dissertation on [[new religious movement]]s and [[mental health]].

Apart from his work for the SCP he has other appointments in several institutions in Dutch society such as the Netherlands Institute for Art History ([[RKD]]), and the Scientific Council for Government Policy. He is a columnist to the Dutch newspapers ''[[NRC-Handelsblad]]'' and ''[[Het Financieele Dagblad]]''.<ref name=OECD /> As of 2008, he is also the chairperson of the committee that selected  the 1,000 texts deemed as "key" of the Dutch and Flemish cultural history for the [[Digital library for Dutch literature]].<ref>Somers, Maartje ''Van abel spel tot Achterhuis: De basisbibliotheek â€“ het Nederlands erfgoed in duizend sleutelteksten'' in [[NRC Handelsblad]] 4 April 2008 section 'Boeken', page 10</ref>

In addition to his role in parliament, Schnabel has been serving as member of the Dutch delegation to the [[Parliamentary Assembly of the Council of Europe]] since 2015. As member of D66, he is part of the [[Alliance of Liberals and Democrats for Europe in the Parliamentary Assembly of the Council of Europe|Alliance of Liberals and Democrats for Europe]] group. He is a member of the Committee on Culture, Science, Education and Media; the Committee on Social Affairs, Health and Sustainable Development; the Sub-Committee on Public Health and Sustainable Development; and the Sub-Committee on Culture, Diversity and Heritage.

Since 2006 ''[[De Volkskrant]]'' lists him in their top ten of the most influential persons in the Netherlands.<ref name=VKtop200>{{cite news|author1=Wouter de Nooy |author2=Erasmus Universiteit Rotterdam. |author3=Erik Bloem |author4=Eveline Domevscek |author5=Martin Kettelarij |title=VOLKSKRANT TOP 200 2007|url=http://www.volkskrant.nl/vk/nl/2844/Archief/archief/article/detail/844921/2007/10/06/VOLKSKRANT-TOP-200-2007.dhtml|accessdate=21 August 2012|newspaper=de Volkskrant|date=06/10/2007}}</ref>
