{{Nihongo|'''Katsuya Kodama'''|児玉 克哉|Kodama Katsuya|born March 10, 1959<ref name="basic_profile">{{cite web|title=Katsuya Kodama|url=http://www.oldsite.transnational.org/SAJT/tff/people/k_kodama.html|publisher=TFF|accessdate=2017-10-22}}</ref>}} is a Japanese [[Peace and conflict studies|peace researcher]] and [[sociologist]] specialized in the research of [[nonviolence|non-violent]] peace activism, study on Survivors of Hiroshima and Nagasaki, [[refugee]] issues, [[foreign worker]]s, and peace-building. He is a current Secretary-General of [[International Peace Research Association]], former professor and vice president at [[Mie University]], Japan and former vice president of [[International Social Science Council]] at [[UNESCO]].

==Early life, education and career ==
Born as a son of a survivor of the [[Atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki|atomic bombing of Hiroshima]] in 1959 in [[Akitakata]], [[Hiroshima]],<ref name="inter_a" /> Kodama has dedicated his research on peacebuilding and [[nonviolence|non-violent]] peace activism. He received a master's degree in [[Sociology]] from [[Hiroshima University]] in 1984.<ref name="basic_profile" /> His research theme was life history of "Atomic Orphans" who lost their parents by [[Atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki|atomic bombing of Hiroshima]]. After receiving a Ph.D in Sociology from [[Lund University]] of Sweden in 1990<ref name="basic_profile" /> for his research on non-violent peace movements, he began his career at [[Mie University]] as Assistant Professor of Faculty of Humanities and Social Sciences<ref name="basic_profile" /> in Japan. Later he was promoted to Associate Professor in 1992<ref name="basic_profile" /> and further promoted to Professor in 2004 and the vice president in 2011. He is an author of various books on peace-building and sociology.<ref name="wdcat">{{cite web|title=WorldCat - Katsuya Kodama|url=https://www.worldcat.org/identities/lccn-n89110150/|publisher=WorldCat|accessdate=2017-11-05}}</ref>

He served as Secretary-General of [[International Peace Research Association]] (IPRA) from 2000 to 2004, and 2010 to 2012 and vice president of [[International Social Science Council]] (ISSC) at [[UNESCO]] from 2006 to 2010.<ref name="inter_a">{{cite web|title=Hiroshima-Nagasaki Process|url=http://sri.or.jp/hiroshimanagasaki-en/|publisher=Social Contribution Institute|accessdate=2017-11-05}}</ref> He was again elected as Secretary-General of [[International Peace Research Association|IPRA]] for 2016 – 2020 at the [[International Peace Research Association|IPRA]] Administrative Meeting held in [[Sierra Leonne]] in 2016.<ref name="IPRA _news">{{cite web|title=IPRA Newsletter Volume 6. No. 4|url=http://www.afes-press.de/html/pdf/2016/HGB/2016_04_ipra-news-letter6-vol-4.pdf|publisher=AFES-PRESS|accessdate=2017-11-05}}</ref> He received Anuvrat Ahimsa (non-violence) International Peace Award from ANUVRAT Global Organization, India in 2012.<ref name="inter_a" />

He is currently assigned as a professor at [[Swarnim Startup & Innovation University]] (SSIU), India, and [[visiting scholar]] at International Research Institute of [[Aichi University]], Japan, [[Sakarya University]], Turkey, [[Tribhuvan University]], Nepal and Malir University of Science & Technology, Pakistan.<ref name="yahoo_news">{{cite web|title=Yahoo! Japan News - Katsuya Kodama|url=https://news.yahoo.co.jp/byline/kodamakatsuya/|publisher=YAHOO! Japan News|accessdate=2017-11-05}}</ref>

He is the founder of UBrainTV Co., Ltd., an Internet television company founded in 2013 in [[Nagoya]], Japan.<ref>{{cite web|title=Company Overview of UBrainTV Co., Ltd.|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/research/stocks/private/person.asp?personId=324597783&privcapId=315610779|publisher=Bloomberg|accessdate=2017-10-22}}</ref>
