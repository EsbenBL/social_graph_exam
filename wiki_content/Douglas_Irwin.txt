'''Douglas A. Irwin''' is the John Sloan Dickey Third Century Professor in the [[Social Sciences]] in the [[Economics]] Department at [[Dartmouth College]] and the author of seven books. He is an expert on both past and present U.S. trade policy, especially policy during the Great Depression. He is frequently sought by media outlets such as [[The Economist]] and [[The Wall Street Journal|Wall Street Journal]] to provide comment and his opinion on current events.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.economist.com/blogs/freeexchange/2009/09/five_questions_for_douglas_irw|title=Five questions for Douglas Irwin|newspaper=The Economist|access-date=2017-06-05}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/douglas-irwin-trade-will-lead-to-freedom-1418863395|title=Trade Will Lead to Freedom|last=Irwin|first=Douglas A.|date=2014-12-18|work=Wall Street Journal|access-date=2017-06-05|language=en-US|issn=0099-9660}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Zarroli |first1=Jim |title=As U.S. Flexes Its Muscles On Trade, Other Countries Are Beginning To Push Back |url=https://www.npr.org/2018/06/08/618242932/as-u-s-flexes-its-muscles-on-trade-other-countries-are-beginning-to-push-back?t=1531051263082 |website=www.npr.org |publisher=National Public Radio, 8 June 2018. |accessdate=8 July 2018}}</ref> He also writes [[op-ed]]s and articles about trade for mainstream media outlets like ''[[The Wall Street Journal]]'', ''[[The New York Times]]'', and ''[[Financial Times]]''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ft.com/content/7a420e9c-29b9-11e6-8ba3-cdd781d02d89|title=Subscribe to read|website=www.ft.com|language=en-GB|access-date=2017-06-05}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/roomfordebate/2010/11/09/back-to-a-gold-standard/hindering-recovery-fostering-protectionism|title=Back to a Gold Standard?|work=The New York Times|access-date=2017-06-05|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.hoover.org/profiles/douglas-irwin|title=Douglas A. Irwin|work=Hoover Institution|access-date=2017-06-05|language=en}}</ref> He is also a nonresident senior fellow at the [[Peterson Institute for International Economics]]. 

Prior to his appointment to as professor at Dartmouth, Irwin was an associate professor of business economics at the [[Booth School of Business|University of Chicago Booth School of Business]], an economist for the [[Federal Reserve Board of Governors|Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve System]], and an economist for the [[Council of Economic Advisers]] Executive Office of the president.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.dartmouth.edu/~dirwin/#OPEDS|title=Douglas A. Irwin-Professor|website=www.dartmouth.edu|access-date=2017-06-04}}</ref>

== Education ==
Irwin received his Ph.D. in economics with distinction from [[Columbia University]] in 1988.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://economics.dartmouth.edu/people/douglas-irwin|title=Douglas A. Irwin {{!}} Department of Economics|website=economics.dartmouth.edu|date=2 April 2013|language=en|access-date=2017-06-04}}</ref> He also received a B.A. in [[political science]], [[magna cum laude]] from the [[University of New Hampshire]]. He has served on the editorial board of the ''[[Journal of Economic History]]'', the World Trade Review, the ''Journal of International Economics'', and ''[[Essays in Economic & Business History]]''.

== Research ==
At Dartmouth, he is the director of the Political Economy Project.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.dartmouth.edu/~dirwin/#OPEDS|title=Douglas A. Irwin-Professor|website=www.dartmouth.edu|access-date=2017-06-04}}</ref>  His published research is widely cited,<ref>{{cite web |title=citations: Douglas Irwin |url=https://scholar.google.com/citations?hl=en&user=4bJ4v_AAAAAJ&view_op=list_works&sortby=pubdate |website=scholar.google.com |publisher=Google Scholar |accessdate=8 July 2018}}</ref> and covers both modern trade policy and the history of the trading system.<ref>{{cite web |title=research output: Douglas Irwin |url=https://ideas.repec.org/e/pir25.html |website=ideas.repec.org |publisher=RePEc (Research Papers in Economics) |accessdate=8 July 2018}}</ref> 

His books include:
* ''Clashing over Commerce: A History of U.S. Trade Policy'', Chicago: University of Chicago Press, 2018. {{ISBN|9780226398969}};
* ''Free Trade Under Fire'', Princeton: Princeton University Press, 2015. {{ISBN|9781400866182}};
* ''Trade Policy Disaster: Lessons from the 1930s'', Cambridge, MA: MIP Press, 2012. {{ISBN|9780262016711}};
* ''Peddling Protectionism: Smoot-Hawley and the Great Depression'', Princeton: Princeton University Press, 2011. {{ISBN|9780691150321}};
* ''Founding choices: American economic policy in the 1790s'', Chicago: University of Chicago Press, 2011. {{ISBN|0226384748}};
* ''The Genesis of the GATT'', Cambridge UK: Cambridge University Press, 2008. {{ISBN| 9780511817953}};
* ''Against the Tide: An Intellectual History of Free Trade'', Princeton: Princeton University Press, 1998. {{ISBN|9780691058962}};

== Honors ==
Irwin has received numerous grants and awards, including the [[John Simon Guggenheim Memorial Foundation]] Principal Investigator fellowship and a [[National Science Foundation]] grant.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.dartmouth.edu/~dirwin/docs/Irwin%20CV.pdf|title=Irwin CV}}</ref> He received the Manhattan Institute for Policy Research's F.A. Hayek Book prize as well as the Economic History Association's Alice Hanson Jones prize for his 2017 book, ''Clashing over Commerce: A History of U.S. Trade Policy.''<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://www.press.uchicago.edu/ucp/books/book/chicago/C/bo24475328.html|title=Clashing over Commerce}}</ref>

== Personal ==
Irwin is married to Marjorie Rose, a Dartmouth economics professor and former staff economist at the [[International Monetary Fund]] and [[Council of Economic Advisers|Council of Economics Advisors]].
