'''Jadwiga Katarzyna Emilewicz''' (born 27 August 1974) is a Polish politician and political scientist. In 2020, she was [[Deputy Prime Minister of the Republic of Poland|Deputy Prime Minister of Poland]]. In 2019, she became [[Ministry of Development (Poland)|Minister of Development]], upon her three-year service as an undersecretary of state in the Ministry of Development, and from 2018 to 2019, she was [[Ministry of Entrepreneurship and Technology|Minister of Entrepreneurship and Technology]] in the government of [[Mateusz Morawiecki]].

==Biography==
Emilewicz was born in [[Kraków]] in 1974 to Antoni and Zdzisława Szylers.<ref>{{cite web|title=Dane osoby pełniącej funkcję publiczną|url=https://katalog.bip.ipn.gov.pl/informacje/152889|agency=katalog.bip.ipn.gov.pl|accessdate=18 February 2019}}</ref>

In 2002, together with Artur Wołek, she published "Reformers and Politicians: The Power Play for the 1998 Reform of Public Administration in Poland, as Seen by Its Main Players".<ref name="EmilewiczWołek2002">{{cite book|author1=Jadwiga Emilewicz|author2=Artur Wołek|title=Reformers and Politicians: The Power Play for the 1998 Reform of Public Administration in Poland, as Seen by Its Main Players|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=tYHTAQAACAAJ|year=2002|publisher=Elipsa|isbn=978-83-7151-493-7}}</ref>

On 27 November 2015, Emilewicz was appointed undersecretary of state in the Ministry of Development and held the function until 2018,<ref>{{cite web|title=Jadwiga Emilewicz wiceministrem rozwoju|url=http://www.radiokrakow.pl/wiadomosci/aktualnosci/jadwiga-emilewicz-zostala-wiceministrem-rozwoju/|agency=radiokrakow.pl|date=2 December 2015|accessdate=9 January 2018}}</ref> when she became the head of the [[Ministry of Entrepreneurship and Technology]] in the government of [[Mateusz Morawiecki]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Prezydent powołał nowych ministrów|url=http://www.prezydent.pl/aktualnosci/nominacje/art,69,prezydent-powolal-nowych-ministrow.html|agency=prezydent.pl|date=9 January 2018|accessdate=9 January 2018}}</ref> Emilewicz retained the office during its reorganization into [[Ministry of Development (Poland)|Ministry of Development]] upon the following election in 2019 and thus entered Morawiecki's second cabinet. Meantime, in 2017, she was one of the founders of the [[Agreement (political party)|Agreement]], a party whose one of the Vice Leaders she shortly became.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Gowin tworzy nową partię przez Ziobrę i… Zandberga|url=https://wiadomosci.wp.pl/gowin-tworzy-nowa-partie-przez-ziobre-i-zandberga-6175537834583681a|website=wp.pl|publisher=[[Wirtualna Polska]]|accessdate=20 December 2017}}</ref>

In 2020, Emilewicz was sworn in as [[Deputy Prime Minister of the Republic of Poland|Deputy Prime Minister of Poland]], simultaneously maintaining her so-far ministerial office, following [[Jarosław Gowin]] stepping down as Deputy Prime Mister and her candidature being proposed by Gowin's [[Agreement (political party)|Agreement]] party instead.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.prezydent.pl/aktualnosci/nominacje/art,163,prezydent-powolal-jadwige-emilewicz-na-urzad-wiceprezesa-rady-ministrow.html|title=Prezydent powołał Jadwigę Emilewicz na urząd Wiceprezesa Rady Ministrów|website=prezydent.pl|accessdate=9 April 2020}}</ref>
