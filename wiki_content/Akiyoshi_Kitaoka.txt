{{nihongo|'''Akiyoshi Kitaoka'''|北岡 明佳|Kitaoka Akiyoshi|extra=born August 19, 1961}} is a [[Professor]] of [[Psychology]] at the College of Letters, [[Ritsumeikan University]], [[Kyoto]], Japan.

In 1984, he received a [[BSc]] from the Department of [[Biology]], [[University of Tsukuba]], Tsukuba, Japan, where he studied animal psychology (burrowing behavior in rats) and (at the Tokyo Metropolitan Institute for Neuroscience) neuronal activity of the inferotemporal cortex in macaque monkeys.

After his 1991 [[PhD]] from the Institute of Psychology, University of Tsukuba,<ref>under the guidance of the late Japanese Gestalt psychologist and professor emeritus '''Osamu Fujita'''</ref> he specialized in [[visual perception]] and [[visual illusions]] of geometrical shape, brightness, color, in motion illusions and other visual phenomena like Gestalt completion and [[Perceptual transparency]], based on a modern conception of [[Gestalt Psychology]].<ref> Noguchi, K., Kitaoka, A., and Takashima, M. (2008) Gestalt-oriented perceptual research in Japan: Past and present. Gestalt Theory, 30, 11-28</ref>

[[File:Peripheral_drift_illusion_rotating_snakes.svg|thumb|400px|An optical illusion similar to ''Rotating Snakes'']]
He became renowned through his ''Rotating Snakes''<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Murakami|first=I.|last2=Kitaoka|first2=A.|last3=Ashida|first3=H.|date=2010-03-19|title=Artificial image oscillation enhances the rotating snakes illusion|url=http://dx.doi.org/10.1167/6.6.551|journal=Journal of Vision|volume=6|issue=6|pages=551–551|doi=10.1167/6.6.551|issn=1534-7362}}</ref> [[peripheral drift illusion]] (see below).

In 2006, he received the Gold Prize of the 9th L'ORÉAL Art and Science of Color contest.

In 2007, he received the Award for Original Studies from the Japanese Society of Cognitive Psychology.<ref>Kitaoka, A. (2008) Cognitive psychology of visual illusion. Japanese Journal of Cognitive Psychology,5, 177-185 (in Japanese with English abstract)</ref><ref>Kitaoka, A. (2007) Psychological approaches to art. Japanese Journal of Psychonomic Science,26, 97-102 (in Japanese with English abstract)</ref><ref>Kitaoka, A. and Ashida, H. (2007) A variant of the anomalous motion illusion based upon contrast and visual latency. Perception, 36, 1019-1035. </ref>

In 2008, his designs were the inspiration for the experimental pop band [[Animal Collective]]'s critically acclaimed album, ''[[Merriweather Post Pavilion (album)|Merriweather Post Pavilion]]'', which features a leaf covered [[optical illusion]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2018-07-20|title=Cubism on A Pop Song: Animal Collective’s Tangerine Reef and 5 other audio-visual spectacles|url=https://proxymusic.club/2018/07/20/animal-collective-tangerine-reef/|access-date=2021-05-06|website=proxy music|language=en-US}}</ref>

He was asked by [[Jeff Koons]] to provide illusions for the interior packaging of [[Lady Gaga]]'s 2013 album ''[[Artpop]]'', and a version of his "Hatpin urchin" image appears on the CD itself.<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Kitaoka |first1=Akiyoshi |date=2014 |title=Visual illusion in ARTPOP and pop art |url=https://www.jstage.jst.go.jp/article/psychono/32/2/32_KJ00009351489/_article |journal=The Japanese Journal of Psychonomic Science |volume=32 |issue=2 |pages=232-234 |doi=10.14947/psychono.KJ00009351489 |access-date=April 26, 2021}}</ref>
