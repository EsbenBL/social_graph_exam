'''Sylvia Beth Bashevkin''', {{Post-nominals|country=CAN|FRSC}} (born 1954) is Canadian academic and writer known for her research in the field of women and politics.<ref name="Bio">{{cite web|url=http://homes.chass.utoronto.ca/~sbashevk/bashevkin_bio.htm|title=University of Toronto biography}}</ref>

==Career==

Bashevkin is a professor in the Department of Political Science in the [[University of Toronto Faculty of Arts and Science]]. From 2005 to 2011, she was Principal of [[University College, Toronto]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Sylvia Bashevkin Interviewed by Opinion-Policy Nexus |url=https://politics.utoronto.ca/2013/06/sylvia-bashevkin-interviewed-by-opinion-policy-nexus/ |website=politics.utoronto.ca |accessdate=April 28, 2019 |date=June 20, 2013}}</ref> She is a senior fellow of [[Massey College, Toronto]].<ref name="Bio"/>

In 2001, she was elected a Fellow of the [[Royal Society of Canada]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Canadian Political Science Association BULLETIN |url=https://www.cpsa-acsp.ca/documents/pdfs/reports/2001_Nov_Bulletin.pdf |website=cpsa-acsp.ca |accessdate=April 28, 2019 |page=29}}</ref> In 2005, Bashevkin was named Canada's Most Powerful Women: Top 100 Award by the Women's Executive Network.<ref>{{cite web |title=SYLVIA BASHEVKIN |url=https://munkschool.utoronto.ca/profile/bashevkin-sylvia/ |website=munkschool.utoronto.ca |accessdate=April 28, 2019}}</ref> Later in 2014, she was awarded the Royal Society of Canada's Ursula Franklin Award in Gender Studies.<ref>{{cite web |title=Sylvia Bashevkin Receives the RSC Award in Gender Studies |url=https://politics.utoronto.ca/2014/09/sylvia-bashevkin-receives-the-rsc-award-in-gender-studies/ |website=politics.utoronto.ca |accessdate=April 28, 2019 |date=September 23, 2014}}</ref> That same year, she was also the recipient of the  Mildred A. Schwartz Lifetime Achievement Award from the [[American Political Science Association]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Sylvia Bashevkin Honoured with Mildred A. Schwartz Lifetime Achievement Award |url=https://politics.utoronto.ca/2014/06/sylvia-bashevkin-honoured-with-mildred-a-schwartz-lifetime-achievement-award/ |website=politics.utoronto.ca |accessdate=April 28, 2019 |date=June 19, 2014}}</ref>

In 2017, Bashevkin published an article titled "Listening to women leaders: Feminist narratives among US foreign policy" which was subsequently short listed for the 2018 Jill Vickers Prize by the [[Canadian Political Science Association]].<ref>{{cite web |title=CPSA Award Winners |url=https://politics.utoronto.ca/2018/06/cpsa-award-winners/ |website=politics.utoronto.ca |accessdate=April 28, 2019 |date=June 1, 2018}}</ref> The following year, Bashevkin was awarded the ISA Bertha Lutz Prize by the [[International Studies Association]].<ref>{{cite web |title=ISA Bertha Lutz Prize |url=https://politics.utoronto.ca/2019/03/isa-bertha-lutz-prize/ |website=politics.utoronto.ca |accessdate=April 28, 2019 |date=March 20, 2019}}</ref>

==Selected bibliography==
* ''Women As Foreign Policy Leaders: National Security and Gender Politics in Superpower America'' ([[Oxford University Press]], 2018)
* ''Women, Power, Politics: The Hidden Story of Canada's Unfinished Democracy'' ([[Oxford University Press]], 2009)
* ''Tales of Two Cities: Women and Municipal Restructuring in London and Toronto'' ([[University of British Columbia Press]], 2006)
* ''Welfare Hot Buttons: Women, Work and Social Policy Reform'' ([[University of Toronto Press]] and [[University of Pittsburgh Press]], 2002)
* ''Women on the Defensive: Living Through Conservative Times'' ([[University of Chicago Press]] and University of Toronto Press, 1998)
* ''Toeing the Lines: Women and Party Politics in English Canada'' (2nd ed., Oxford University Press, 1993)
* ''True Patriot Love: The Politics of Canadian Nationalism'' (Oxford University Press, 1991)
* ''Toeing the Lines: Women and Party Politics in English Canada'' (University of Toronto Press, 1985)
