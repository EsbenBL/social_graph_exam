'''Carole E. Joffe''' is an American [[Sociology|sociologist]] and [[reproductive rights]] advocate who has published several books on [[abortion]]. 
In 2013, she was awarded the [[Society of Family Planning]] Lifetime Achievement Award for her research on the sociology of abortion and [[family planning]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Carole Joffe receives SFP 2013 Lifetime Achievement Award - ANSIRH |url=http://www.ansirh.org/news-item/carole-joffe-receives-sfp-2013-lifetime-achievement-award/ |website=ANSIRH |accessdate=2015-11-29 |language=en-US |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151208135507/http://www.ansirh.org/news-item/carole-joffe-receives-sfp-2013-lifetime-achievement-award/ |archive-date=2015-12-08 }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title = Society of Family Planning: Dr. Carole Joffe|url = http://www.societyfp.org/About-SFP/SFP-awardees/Dr-Carole-Joffe.aspx|website = www.societyfp.org|accessdate = 2015-11-29}}</ref> 
She has also earned the UC Davis Public Service Award (2006), the Irwin Kushner Lecture by the [[Association of Reproductive Health Professionals]] (2010), and the [[David Gunn (doctor)|David Gunn]] Lifetime Achievement Award from the [[Abortion Care Network]] (2015).<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title = Carole Joffe, PhD {{!}} Bixby Center for Global Reproductive Health|url = https://bixbycenter.ucsf.edu/carole-joffe-phd|website = bixbycenter.ucsf.edu|accessdate = 2015-11-29}}</ref>

==Life==
Joffe earned her Ph.D. from the [[University of California, Berkeley]].<ref name=":0" />

She is a professor emerita at the [[University of California, Davis]], and currently a professor of obstetrics, gynecology, and reproductive sciences at ANSIRH (Advancing New Standards in Reproductive Health), part of the University of California, San Francisco.<ref name=":0" />

== Publications ==
* ''Friendly Intruders: Childcare Professionals and Family Life'' (1977)
* ''The Regulation of Sexuality: Experiences of Family Planning Workers'' (1986)
* ''Doctors of Conscience: The Struggle to Provide Abortion before and after Roe v Wade'' (1996)
* ''Dispatches from the Abortion Wars: The Costs of Fanaticism to Doctors, Patients and the Rest of Us'' (2009)
