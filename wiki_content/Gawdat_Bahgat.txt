'''Gawdat Bahgat''' (born {{abbr|c.|circa}} 1960s) is a professor of [[political science]] at the [[National Defense University]]. Bahgat was born and raised in [[Cairo]], [[Egypt]] and earned degrees at [[Cairo University]] and [[American University in Cairo]]. He emigrated to the [[United States]] and earned his [[Ph.D]] at [[Florida State University]] in 1991.

Bahgat served as a professor of political science at [[Indiana University of Pennsylvania]] from 1995 to 2009 where he also was the Director of the Center for Middle Eastern Studies. In 2006–07 he earned the Distinguished Faculty Award for research.<ref name=penn>{{cite news |url=http://www.thepenn.org/news/iup-professor-gawdat-bahgat-to-bid-farewell-further-career-1.836492 |title=IUP Professor Gawdat Bahgat to bid farewell, further career |publisher=The Penn |date=30 October 2009 |accessdate=14 December 2009 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110716061153/http://www.thepenn.org/news/iup-professor-gawdat-bahgat-to-bid-farewell-further-career-1.836492 |archivedate=16 July 2011 }}</ref>

Bahgat has published numerous books, chapters, and articles on various subjects including [[petroleum politics|oil politics]], [[nuclear proliferation]] and the [[Persian Gulf]] and [[Caspian Sea]] regions. His works have been translated into [[Arabic language|Arabic]], [[Russian language|Russian]], [[German language|German]], [[Italian language|Italian]], [[Portuguese language|Portuguese]] and [[Japanese language|Japanese]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mepc.org/resources/bahgat.asp |title=Gawdat Bahgat |publisher=[[Middle East Policy Council]] |accessdate=14 December 2009 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20081129031229/http://www.mepc.org///resources/bahgat.asp |archivedate=November 29, 2008 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://icar.gmu.edu/eurasia/Bahgat.htm |title=Speaker Biographies: Gawdat Bahgat |publisher=Institute for Conflict Analysis & Resolution |accessdate=14 December 2009 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20050506232759/http://icar.gmu.edu/eurasia/Bahgat.htm |archive-date=6 May 2005 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

In 2010, Bahgat began teaching at the [[National Defense University]] in [[Washington, D.C.]]<ref name=penn/>
