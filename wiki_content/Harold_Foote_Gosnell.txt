'''Harold Foote Gosnell''' (December 24, 1896 â€“ January 8, 1997) was an [[Americans|American]] [[political scientist]] and [[author]], known for his research and writings on [[American politics]], [[Elections in the United States|elections]], and [[Political parties in the United States|political parties]].

Gosnell attended the [[University of Rochester]], graduating [[summa cum laude]] in 1918. He went on to the [[University of Chicago]], where in 1922 he received his doctorate. He became a professor at Chicago and taught there until 1941.  During World War II, he went to Washington DC, as a budget analyst and later as an operations officer for the [[United States Department of State]], while he continued to study and write on politics.  He served in the federal government until 1960, and was on the faculty at [[American University]]. From 1962 to 1972, he was a professor of political science at [[Howard University]].<ref name="uchicago.edu">{{cite web|url=http://news.lib.uchicago.edu/blog/2010/04/09/harold-f-gosnell-papers/|title=Harold F. Gosnell Papers|author=|date=|work=uchicago.edu|accessdate=27 February 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Saxon|first=Wolfgang|title=Harold Foote Gosnell, 100, Scholar of American Politics|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1997/01/16/us/harold-foote-gosnell-100-scholar-of-american-politics.html|work=New York Times|accessdate=1 October 2011|date=January 16, 1997}}</ref>

A student of  political scientist [[Charles Edward Merriam]], Gosnell published work in the 1920s that pioneered new approaches using psychology to examine voting and political behavior.<ref name="uchicago.edu"/>  His dissertation on New York politics, [[Thomas C. Platt]] ("Boss" Platt) and [[Theodore Roosevelt]] was published, and then ''Non-voting, Causes and Methods of Control'' (1924, with Merriam) and ''Getting out the Vote: An Experiment in the Stimulation of Voting'' (1927). In 1936, Gosnell won the first [[Anisfield-Wolf Book Award]] for ''Negro Politicians: Rise of Negro Politics in Chicago''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.anisfield-wolf.org/books/negro-politicians-the-rise-of-negro-politics-in-chicago/?sortby=year|title=Anisfield-Wolf Book Awards - Negro Politicians: The Rise of Negro Politics in Chicago|author=|date=|work=Anisfield-Wolf Book Awards - The 80th Annual|accessdate=27 February 2016}}</ref>  In the 1930s, he also wrote about [[machine politics]] in Chicago, and then in the 1960s revised his work in this area. During the [[Cold War]], Gosnell studied the Soviet Union.<ref name="uchicago.edu"/>

Each year in honor of Gosnell's work, the Society for Political Methodology awards the ''Gosnell Prize for Excellence in Political Methodology''. The prize is given to the author(s) of the best work in political methodology, which has been presented at the political science conferences during the preceding year.<ref>{{cite web|title=About the Society|url=https://www.cambridge.org/core/membership/spm/about-us/awards|publisher=The Society for Political Methodology|accessdate=1 October 2011}}</ref>

==Works==
* ''Boss Platt and His New York Machine: A Study of the Political Leadership of Thomas C. Platt, Theodore Roosevelt, and Others'' (University of Chicago Press, 1924; repr. 1969).
* ''Getting out the vote: an experiment in the stimulation of voting.'' (University of Chicago Press, 1927)
* ''Machine Politics: Chicago Model'' (University of Chicago Press, 1937; 2d ed. 1969).

* ''Truman's Crises: A Political Biography of Harry S. Truman'' (Praeger, 1980).
