'''Marsh Marshall''' is an [[United States|American]] economist, [[venture capital]] and hedge fund manager, and television producer. He is the author of "Organizations and Growth in Rural China",<ref>{{cite book |title=Marshall, M. (1985) Organizations and Economic Growth in Rural China |location=London |publisher=Macmillan Press}}</ref> academic and newspaper articles on China's economy<ref>{{cite news|title= Marshall, M. (1979) 'Red and Expert at Tachai: A Sources of Growth Analysis'|work=World Development|volume=7 |pages=423–432}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=The Rains Came – and Then the Vietnamese |work=Washington Post |date=January 13, 1979}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Trends in Asian Trade, Industry and Finance|newspaper=The Wall Street Journal (Asia)|date=May 25, 1977}}</ref> and on venture capital investing.<ref>{{cite book |title=Marshall, M. (2005) 'Climbing Mount Everest' |location=Boston |publisher=Aspatore Press |pages=15–23}}</ref>  He is currently Chief Operating Officer at the quantitative hedge fund, Periwinkle Trading LLC, and is writing a book on investment, economic growth and corporate governance.

==Early life==
Marshall was born in 1953 and raised in Washington DC, where he graduated from [[Georgetown Preparatory School]] in 1971. In 1975, he earned a bachelor's degree in history from [[Tufts University]] (magna cum laude); a 1977 master's degree in [[development economics]] from [[Stanford University]], and a 1983 doctorate degree in economics from [[Oxford University]], where he was a Tutor in Economics at [[Magdalen College]].  His doctoral thesis focused on the relationship between institutions and economic growth.<ref>{{cite book|title=Marshall, M. 'Institutional Transformation and Economic Growth in Rural China'|date=1982|edition=D. Phil Thesis, Oxford University}}</ref> In July 1979, Marshall was a member of a small delegation of Oxford and Cambridge economists invited by the [[Chinese Academy of Social Science]] to discuss economic reforms with China's leader, [[Deng Xiaoping]].<ref>{{cite magazine |title=MAGDALEN & ASIA RHODES 110TH ANNIVERSARY |magazine=Floreat |date=2013 |issue=12 |url=http://www.magd.ox.ac.uk/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2013/09/Floreat-2013.pdf}}</ref>  In a 1979 article, Marshall presented evidence that the apparent success of [[Mao Zedong]]'s agricultural model, [[Learn from Dazhai in agriculture|Dazhai]], was a fraud.<ref>{{cite news |title=Marshall, M. (1979) 'Red and Expert at Tachai: A Sources of Growth Analysis' |work=World Development |volume=7 |pages=423–432}}</ref> In 1981, China's post-Mao leadership acknowledged the fraud in [[Beijing Review]].<ref>{{cite news |work=Beijing Review |issue=16 |page=25 |date=April 20, 1981}}</ref>

==Business career==
In 1993-1995 Marshall led a Silicon Valley-based team of designers and engineers for [[Bell Atlantic]] in the development of [[user interface]]s and [[electronic program guide]]s for [[Video on demand|video-on-demand service]], for which Marshall was issued a US patent.<ref>{{cite web |title=United States, US Patent Office, patent # 392,264. Set of Icons for the Display Screen for a Video Monitor |date=March 17, 1998}}</ref>   In 1996, Marshall was subsequently appointed Vice President at [[Tele-TV]], a partnership of [[Bell Atlantic]], [[NYNEX]] and [[Pacific Bell]].<ref>{{cite web|title=TELE-TV MEDIA SETS CREATIVE PRODUCTION AND TECHNOLOGICAL INTEGRATION PERSONNEL|url=http://www.thefreelibrary.com/TELETV+MEDIA+SETS+CREATIVE+PRODUCTION+AND+TECHNOLOGICAL+INTEGRATION...-a018037130|publisher=February 27, 1996}}</ref> In 1998, Marshall was IXI Corporation's Chief Operating Officer, and helped establish a data network of $10 trillion in directly measured financial assets from large mutual funds, banks, and insurance companies; after the 2009 sale of the company to [[Equifax]] (NYSE: EFX), Marshall became President of the IXI Digital division of Equifax.<ref>{{cite web|title=Data Industry Reaction: What Is The Biggest Challenge With The Demand-Side Platform Model?|url=https://adexchanger.com/data-exchanges/what-is-the-biggest-challenge-dsp/|date=2011-07-11}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=comScore Announces Collaboration with IXI, An Equifax Company, to Incorporate Consumer Financial Segmentation Data into AdEffx™ and Audience Advantage™ Product Suites|url=https://www.comscore.com/Insights/Press-Releases/2010/9/comScore-Announces-Collaboration-with-IXI|access-date=10 November 2016|work=comScore}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{cite news|title=Equifax Will Acquire IXI for $124M|url=http://www.americanbanker.com/issues/174_203/equifax_will_acquire_ixi_for_124m-1003143-1.html|access-date=10 November 2016|date=October 21, 2009}}</ref> In 2000, Marshall became a General Partner at a $90 million, [[Rothschild family|Rothschild]]-sponsored venture capital firm, ECentury Capital, where he made [[Equity investment|equity]] and debt investments in software, [[semiconductor]], and digital media companies and served on eight boards.<ref>{{cite web|title=Meet The Team|url=http://bcvc.com/why-blue-chip/meet-team/|access-date=10 November 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Marsh S. Marshall Jr., Ph.D.|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/research/stocks/private/person.asp?personId=1577446&privcapId=1497564|access-date=10 November 2016|work=Bloomberg}}</ref> Marshall is currently Chief Operating Officer at the statistical arbitrage, systematic hedge fund Periwinkle Trading LLC, founded by Scott Smallwood, former Managing Director at Morgan Stanley's quantitative hedge fund, Process Driven Trading.<ref>{{cite web|title=Periwinkle Trading, LLC.|url=http://periwinkletrading.com/|access-date=28 March 2018}}</ref>  In 2011, Marshall was appointed Chairman of the Board of Bluecava, a California software and digital device data company.<ref>{{cite web|title=BlueCava Gets $9.5M From Mark Cuban, S3, Tim Headington|url=https://www.pehub.com/2011/10/bluecava-gets-95m-from-mark-cuban-s3-tim-headington/|access-date=10 November 2016|date=2011-10-25}}</ref> He was a board director of Linkable Networks and a senior adviser at I2 Capital<ref>{{cite web|title=FinovateSpring 2012 / Linkable Networks|url=http://finovate.com/videos/finovatespring-2012-linkablenetworks/|access-date=10 November 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Marsh Marshall, Senior Advisor|url=http://i2capitalcorp.com/team/marsh-marshall/|access-date=10 November 2016}}</ref>

==Film and television career==
Marshall's television career was centered at the [[BBC London|British Broadcasting Corporation in London]], where he was trained in news and current affairs production. Marshall was with the British Broadcasting Corporation for five years and produced and directed more than a dozen major programs and films. His filmography includes Life After Debt for BBC Panorama; Moneylenders for PBS [[Frontline (U.S. TV series)|Frontline]]; On Course for War (for Panorama); Czech Mate (with BBC Foreign Affairs Editor [[John Simpson (journalist)|John Simpson]]); Review of the Year 1989 with [[Jonathan Dimbleby]], a 90-minute Christmas season film for BBC1,<ref>{{cite web|title=Marsh Marshall Profile|url=http://www.bfi.org.uk/films-tv-people/4ce2ba86d68bc|access-date=10 November 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|title=Moneylenders|oclc=14092285}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=The "General Belgrano"|url=http://hansard.millbanksystems.com/lords/1984/may/01/the-general-belgrano|website=Millbanksystems}}.</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Czech Revolution: A Secret Police Plot? : Intrigue: The BBC says leaders of the KGB and its Prague counterpart engineered the uprising ending Communist rule.|url=http://articles.latimes.com/1990-06-01/news/mn-156_1_secret-police|access-date=10 November 2016|work=Los Angeles Times}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Marsh Marshall|url=http://bcvc.com/team/marsh-marshall/|access-date=10 November 2016}}</ref>  In 1993, he directed a BBC drama, War with America, a special effects film set in the future, with a cast of British actors,.<ref>{{cite journal|title=Europe on the Brink|issue=3603|pages=74|url=http://genome.ch.bbc.co.uk/0a65ac6e3f664deeb18ce7c04984208b|journal=The Radio Times|date=1993-01-21}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=War with America (1993)|url=http://www.bfi.org.uk/films-tv-people/4ce2b7c5a7be4|access-date=10 November 2016}}</ref>
