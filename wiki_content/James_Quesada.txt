'''James Quesada''' (born August 29, 1953) is a [[Nicaraguan]] [[Nicaraguan American|American]] Anthropologist and professor at [[San Francisco State University]]'s Department of Anthropology.<ref>{{cite news | first=Tyche | last=Hendricks | title=Central Americans give mixed verdict to Reagan | date= 2004-06-11| url =http://www.sfgate.com/cgi-bin/article.cgi?file=/chronicle/archive/2004/06/11/MNGHM74MBE1.DTL | work =San Francisco Chronicle | access-date = 2008-01-15 }}</ref> (Ph.D. University of California, San Francisco/University of California, Berkeley). His work focuses on cultural and [[medical anthropology]], the ethnography of structural and political violence, social Suffering, critical medical anthropology, [[urban anthropology]], culture change, transnational migration and refugee migration, [[North America]], [[Central America]], and the [[inner city]].

He is currently Director and Principal Investigator of the Latino Laborers Initiative at the César E. Chávez Institute at San Francisco State University.<ref>{{cite news|title=Staff CVs and Biographies |url=http://cci.sfsu.edu/taxonomy/term/26 |work=César E. Chávez Institute |access-date=2008-01-16 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080201204005/http://cci.sfsu.edu/taxonomy/term/26 |archive-date=2008-02-01 }}</ref> He was the Chair of the Department of Anthropology at San Francisco State University between 2004-2007.
