'''Bennett T. McCallum''' (born July 27, 1935) is an American [[monetary economics|monetary economist]]. He is H. J. Heinz Professor of Economics at [[Carnegie Mellon University]]'s [[Tepper School of Business]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://research.stlouisfed.org/conferences/homer/bm.html|title=Bennett T. McCallum (Homer Jones Lecture) - St. Louis Fed|website=research.stlouisfed.org|access-date=2019-05-13}}</ref> He is known for the [[McCallum rule|McCallum Rule]], a monetary policy proposal advocating targeting the growth rate of the [[monetary base]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.investopedia.com/terms/m/mccallum_rule.asp|title=McCallum Rule|last=Chen|first=James|website=Investopedia|language=en|access-date=2019-05-13}}</ref><ref>McCallum, B. T. (1987). The case for rules in the conduct of monetary policy: a concrete example. ''Review of World Economics'', ''123''(3), 415-429.</ref>

McCallum earned a [[Bachelor of Arts|B.A.]] and a [[Bachelor of Science|B.Sc.]] (in chemical engineering) from [[Rice University]]. He then attended [[Harvard Business School]] to earn his [[Master of Business Administration|M.B.A.]], before returning to Rice in order to obtain his [[Doctor of Philosophy|Ph.D.]] in economics.

He became professor at Carnegie Mellon in 1981, after holding a professorship at the [[University of Virginia]] (1974â€“1982). Among his doctoral students was [[Charles L. Evans]], the current president of the [[Federal Reserve Bank of Chicago]].<ref>[http://www.tepper.cmu.edu/current-students/current-doctoral/doctoral-newsletter/download.aspx?id=1687 Tepper School of Business: ''Doctoral Program Newsletter'', Issue 14, September 2007.] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100613034046/http://www.tepper.cmu.edu/current-students/current-doctoral/doctoral-newsletter/download.aspx?id=1687 |date=June 13, 2010 }}</ref>
