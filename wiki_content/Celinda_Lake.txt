'''Celinda Lake''' is a [[pollster]] and [[political consulting|political strategist]] for the [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic Party]] in the [[United States of America]].

==Background and education==
A native of [[Montana]], Lake was born and raised on a ranch. She earned her [[master's degree]] in [[political science]] and [[survey research]] from the [[University of Michigan]], and also received a certificate in political science from the [[University of Geneva]]. She attended [[Smith College]] as an undergraduate. In 2002, Smith College awarded her its Distinguished Alumna Medal.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.smith.edu/newsoffice/releases/01-054.html|title=Smith Alumnae to Be Honored at Rally Day|last=|first=|date=2002-02-05|website=Smith College|access-date=2017-05-22}}</ref>

==Career==
Lake and her firm, Lake Research Partners, work on [[healthcare]], education and the environment.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lakeresearch.com/|title=Lake Research Partners|website=www.lakeresearch.com|language=en-gb|access-date=2017-06-12}}</ref> She has worked with a large variety of organizations, such as the [[AFL-CIO]], [[SEIU]], [[EMILY's List]], [[The White House Project]], [[Planned Parenthood]], [[Democratic National Committee]], [[Sierra Club]], [[Human Rights Campaign]], [[NARAL Pro-Choice America|NARAL]], [[Barbara Lee Family Foundation]], [[Kaiser Family Foundation]], [[VoteVets.org|VoteVets Action Fund]], the [[Wikimedia Foundation]],<ref>{{cite web|url=https://meta.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:Wikimedia_2014_English_Fundraiser_Survey.pdf|title=Wikimedia 2014 English Fundraiser Survey|last1=Lake|first1=Celinda|last2=Mermin|first2=David|last3=Newton|first3=Leisl|date=27 February 2015|website=Wikimedia Foundation|access-date=20 June 2017}}</ref> and more.

Lake has worked as a pollster for many prominent politicians. She has worked with [[Barbara Mikulski|Senator Barbara Mikulski]], [[Debbie Stabenow|Senator Debbie Stabenow]], [[Blanche Lincoln|Senator Blanche Lincoln]], [[Jon Tester|Senator Jon Tester]], former [[Janet Napolitano|Arizona Governor Janet Napolitano]], former [[Bob Wise|West Virginia Governor Bob Wise]], [[Tim Walz|Congressman Tim Walz]], Houston mayor [[Annise Parker]]. She also served as a pollster to Massachusetts Attorney General [[Martha Coakley]], whose loss she credited to "the failure of the White House and Congress to confront Wall Street."<ref>{{cite web|last=Grim |first=Ryan| author-link=Ryan Grim |url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2010/01/19/coakley-pollster-defends_n_428600.html |title=Coakley Pollster Defends Campaign Against White House |work=The Huffington Post |date= 21 March 2010|accessdate=2017-03-10}}</ref> She also served as leader of focus group research for [[Bill Clinton]]'s successful [[Bill Clinton presidential campaign, 1992|1992 Presidential Campaign]].{{Citation needed|reason= Broken link|date=October 2021}}
Lake focuses especially on women candidates and women's concerns. She [[co-authored]] a book, ''What Women Really Want'', with [[Republican Party (United States)|Republican]] pollster [[Kellyanne Conway]].
