'''Joel B. Slemrod''' (born July 14, 1951)<ref>{{cite web|url=http://webuser.bus.umich.edu/jslemrod/Slemrod_cv_11302017.pdf|title=Joel Slemrod|publisher=[[University of Michigan]]|date=30 November 2017}}</ref> is an American economist and academic, currently serving as a professor of economics at the [[University of Michigan]] and the Paul W. McCracken Collegiate Professor of Business Economics and Public Policy at the [[Stephen M. Ross School of Business]] at the [[University of Michigan]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://webuser.bus.umich.edu/jslemrod/|title=Joel Slemrod|website=webuser.bus.umich.edu|access-date=2020-04-13}}</ref>

== Education ==
He earned a Bachelor of Arts degree from [[Princeton University]] in 1973 and a Ph.D. in economics from [[Harvard University]] in 1980.<ref>{{Cite book|last1=Parkin|first1=Michael|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=QgkEqxMoIi8C&q=Joel+Slemrod+newark&pg=PA402|title=Microeconomía: versión para latinoamérica|last2=Esquivel|first2=Gerardo|date=2006|publisher=Pearson Educación|isbn=978-970-26-0718-2|language=es}}</ref>

== Career ==
Slemrod has served on the faculty of the [[University of Michigan]] since 1987, and does research on taxation, with a focus on taxation of personal income. He is co-author of ''Taxing Ourselves: A Citizen's Guide to the Great Debate over Tax Reform'' and the editor of Does Atlas Shrug? The Economic Consequences of Taxing the Rich.<ref>{{cite book|editor=Joel Slemrod|title=Does Atlas Shrug?: The Economic Consequences of Taxing the Rich|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=3mpiqxjB9AgC|year=2000|publisher=Harvard University Press|isbn=978-0-674-00154-1}}</ref> Slemrod also serves as Director of the Office of Tax Policy Research, which is a research center at the University of Michigan on matters of tax policy.<ref>{{cite book|author1=Joel Slemrod|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=5gwmDwAAQBAJ|title=Taxing Ourselves: A Citizen's Guide to the Debate Over Taxes|author2=Jon Bakija|date=2017|publisher=MIT Press|isbn=978-0-262-03567-5}}</ref>

In 2001, Slemrod shared an [[Ig Nobel Prize]] with Wojciech Kopczuk, of [[Columbia University]], for a paper concluding that people find a way to postpone their deaths if that would qualify them for a lower rate on the [[inheritance tax]].<ref>{{Cite journal| last1 = Kopczuk | first1 = W.| last2 = Slemrod | first2 = J.| title = Dying to Save Taxes: Evidence from Estate-Tax Returns on the Death Elasticity| journal = Review of Economics and Statistics| volume = 85| issue = 2| pages = 256| year = 2003| doi = 10.1162/003465303765299783| citeseerx = 10.1.1.117.1898}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Harding|first1=Lesley|title=Business prof wins not so noble Nobel|url=http://ur.umich.edu/0102/Oct15_01/12.htm|access-date=21 May 2015|publisher=University Record|date=15 October 2001}}</ref> In 2012, Slemrod was awarded the Daniel M. Holland Medal by the National Tax Association.<ref name="holland" />

Slemrod has authored op-ed articles for ''[[The New York Times]]'' and [[The Hill (newspaper)|''The Hill'']]. He has also been featured on [[CNBC]] and [[Fox Business]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://video.foxbusiness.com/v/4588593/|title=Would a $1T Corp. Tax Break Lead to Job Growth?|website=Fox Business|language=en|access-date=2020-04-13}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|last1=Burman|first1=Leonard E.|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2012/12/28/opinion/why-closing-tax-loopholes-isnt-enough.html|title=Opinion {{!}} Closing Loopholes Isn't Enough|date=2012-12-27|work=The New York Times|access-date=2020-04-13|last2=Slemrod|first2=Joel B.|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://thehill.com/opinion/finance/354538-with-tax-cuts-gop-pays-lip-service-to-limited-government|title=With tax cuts, GOP pays lip service to limited government|last=Weatherhead|first=Timothy|date=2017-10-09|website=TheHill|language=en|access-date=2020-04-13}}</ref>
