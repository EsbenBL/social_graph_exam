'''Charles A. Holt''' (born October 2, 1948) is a [[behavioral economics|behavioral economist]], the A. Willis Robertson Professor of Political Economy at the [[University of Virginia]]. He also teaches public policy at the [[Frank Batten School of Leadership and Public Policy]]. He developed with Susan K. Laury, the main test to measure risk aversion in 2002.

He wrote ''Markets, Games & Strategic Behavior'', {{ISBN|0-321-41931-6}}.
