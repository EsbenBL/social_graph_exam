'''John Wanna''' (born 20 May 1954)<ref>{{cite web|url=http://librariesaustralia.nla.gov.au/search/display?dbid=auth&id=36290337|website=Libraries Australia Authorities – Full view|title=Libraries Australia|accessdate=13 August 2017}}</ref> is Professor Emeritus at both the [[Australian National University]] (ANU) and Griffith University.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.griffith.edu.au/griffith-business-school/departments/government-international-relations/contact-us/john-wanna|title=Professor Emeritus John Wanna|last=|first=|date=|website=Griffith University|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref> Before retiring in 2019, Wanna was the Foundation Professor with the [[John Bunting (diplomat)|Sir John Bunting]] Chair of [[Public Administration]] at the [[Australia and New Zealand School of Government]] (ANZSOG) based at the ANU. He held this position from 2004-2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.anzsog.edu.au/about/contact-directory/john-wanna|title=John Wanna ANZSOG Directory|last=|first=|date=24 April 2020|website=|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref> Prior to that he was Professor of [[Public Policy]] at [[Griffith University]].

== Scholarly contribution ==
Prior to 2019, Wanna was the leading scholar with ANZSOG at ANU and held the position of National Research Director for the School. He was also Professor in Political Science at the School of Politics and International Relations in the College of Arts and Social Sciences at ANU. He joined ANZSOG in 2004, after having been involved in the planning for the new school of government that officially became ANZSOG in 2002.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/page/journal/14678500/john_wanna_career_reflection?|title=AJPA John Wanna Career Reflections|last=|first=|date=|website=|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref>

He has authored, co-authored, edited, and co-edited more than 50 books and over 100 articles and book chapters. His research focuses on public finance, and he has published three major international studies into government budgeting, including ''Controlling Public Expenditure'' (2003),<ref>{{cite book|last1=Wanna|first1=John|title=Controlling public expenditure: the changing roles of central budget agencies-better guardians?|date=2003|publisher=Edward Elgar|location=Cheltenham|isbn=1843760436|url=http://trove.nla.gov.au/work/9786273?selectedversion=NBD24513610|accessdate=13 August 2017}}</ref> ''The Reality of Budgetary Reform'' (2010),<ref>{{cite book|last1=Wanna|first1=John|title=The reality of budgetary reform in OECD nations: Trajectories and consequences|date=2010|publisher=Edward Elgar|location=Cheltenham|isbn=9781840646894|url=http://trove.nla.gov.au/work/37405934?selectedversion=NBD45616884|accessdate=13 August 2017}}</ref> and ''The Global Financial Crisis and its Budget Impacts'' (2015).<ref>{{cite book|last1=Wanna|first1=John|title=The global financial crisis and its budget impacts in OECD Nations: Fiscal responses and future challenges|date=2015|publisher=Edward Elgar|location=Cheltenham|isbn=9781784718961|url=http://trove.nla.gov.au/work/197591359?selectedversion=NBD55447307|accessdate=13 August 2017}}</ref>

He has also published many monographs on Australian public finance, including ''Managing Public Expenditure'' (2000) and From Accounting to Accountability (2001). He has worked on major comparative government projects; including the ''Future of Governance'' publications (2000–2003); and ''Comparing Westminster'' (2009). He has published a major study of an Australian parliament, entitled ''The Ayes Have It'' (2011). Before retiring, he was editor of the ANZSOG-ANU Press monograph series, which had at the time of his retirement over 50 titles in circulation ranging from thematic studies to topical issues in governance, problematic areas of management, and advisory manuals for public servants. The series had over two million downloads as of 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.anzsog.edu.au/about/contact-directory/john-wanna|title=ANZSOG About|last=|first=|date=|website=|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref>

In addition to his scholarly output, Wanna is a regular political commentator on TV (ABC, SBS, Sky, Channels 9 and 7, and international broadcasters) and the print media ([[The Australian|''The Australian'']], [[The Courier-Mail|''The Courier-Mail'']], [[The Saturday Paper|''The Saturday Paper'']], the [[Australian Financial Review|''Australian Financial Review'']], and ''[[The Conversation (website)|The Conversation]]''). He regularly appears as an Australian politics expert on other media outlets ([[Bloomberg News|Bloomberg]], the [[New York Times|''New York Times'']], the [[Daily Mail|''Daily Mail'']], AFP, [[Reuters]], Fairfax Media). He regularly makes public presentations and talks to practitioner and academic audiences.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.anzsog.edu.au/about/contact-directory/john-wanna|title=ANZSOG About John Wanna|last=|first=|date=|website=|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref>

== Education and career ==
Wanna has a BA Honours degree (1975) and a PhD (1985) from the [[University of Adelaide]]. He is a Fellow of the Academy of Social Sciences in Australia<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.assa.edu.au/fellows-list/|website=Academy of Social Sciences in Australia – Full view|title=Academy of Social Sciences in Australia|accessdate=18 October 2017}}</ref> and a National Fellow and former Councillor of the Institute of Public Administration, Australia.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sa.ipaa.org.au/About/aboutus_Fellows.asp|website=Institute of Public Administration, Australia, SA Division – Full view|title=Institute of Public Administration, Australia|accessdate=18 October 2017}}</ref> He sits on a number of editorial boards and was editor of the Australian Journal of Public Administration from late 1995 to 2015. Before retiring he was editor of the ANU Press ANZSOG monograph series, and some 50+ titles are all available to download free of charge from either the [[ANU Press]] website or from the ANZSOG website. He is a member of the Australian Political Studies Association and the Institute of Public Administration, Australia.

Wanna was formerly Professor of Politics and [[Public Policy]] at [[Griffith University]] in Brisbane, Queensland. He has held appointments and honorary positions at [[University of Adelaide]], [[Flinders University]], [[University of Canterbury]] New Zealand, [[York University]] in the United Kingdom, and the China University of Politics and Law in [[Beijing]], [[China]].<ref>{{cite book|last1=Podger|first1=Andrew|last2=de Percy|first2=Michael|last3=Vincent|first3=Sam|title=Politics, Policy and Public Administration in Theory and Practice: Essays in Honour of Professor John Wanna|date=2021|publisher=ANU Press|location=Canberra|isbn=9781760464370|url=https://press.anu.edu.au/publications/series/anzsog/politics-policy-and-public-administration-theory-and-practice}}</ref>

== Research supervision ==
Wanna has supervised more than 60 research students at the PhD, masters, and honours levels.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://experts.griffith.edu.au/18761-john-wanna/teaching|title=John Wanna Griffith University Profile|last=|first=|date=|website=|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref>

His research interests for supervision include: Australian politics and public policy, public finance and government budgeting, public administration and management, federalism and intergovernmental relations, government-business relations, legislative studies and Westminster systems, regional studies, biographical studies of politicians.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://scholar.google.com.au/citations?user=-6B5piEAAAAJ&hl=en|title=Google Scholar|last=Wanna|first=John|date=24 April 2020|website=Google Scholar John Wanna Profile|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://cass.anu.edu.au/research/people/professor-john-wanna|title=ANU CASS John Wanna Profile|last=|first=|date=|website=|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://researchers.anu.edu.au/researchers/wanna-jj|title=ANU Research Profile John Wanna|last=|first=|date=|website=|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref>
