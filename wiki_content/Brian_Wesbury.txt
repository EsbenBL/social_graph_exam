'''Brian S. Wesbury''' (born September 8, 1958) is an American [[economist]] focusing on [[macroeconomics]] and [[economic forecasting]]. He is the economics editor and a monthly contributor for ''[[The American Spectator]]'',<ref>{{cite web |url=http://spectator.org/people/brian-s-wesbury/all# |title=Brian Wesbury &#124; The American Spectator |publisher=Spectator.org |accessdate=2014-08-27 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130917210337/http://spectator.org/people/brian-s-wesbury/all# |archive-date=2013-09-17 |url-status=dead }}</ref> in addition to appearing on television stations such as [[CNBC]],<ref>{{cite web |url=http://search.cnbc.com/main.do?target=all&keywords=wesbury&categories=exclude |title=wesbury - CNBC |publisher=Search.cnbc.com |date= |accessdate=2014-08-27 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161220211203/http://search.cnbc.com/main.do?target=all&keywords=wesbury&categories=exclude |archive-date=2016-12-20 |url-status=dead }}</ref> [[Fox Business Network|Fox Business]],<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.foxbusiness.com/search-results/search?q=wesbury |title=Search for wesbury |publisher=Foxbusiness.com |date= |accessdate=2014-08-27 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150924045113/http://www.foxbusiness.com/search-results/search?q=wesbury |archive-date=2015-09-24 |url-status=dead }}</ref> [[Fox News Channel|Fox News]],<ref>{{cite news| url=http://www.foxnews.com/search-results/search?q=wesbury | work=Fox News | title=Search for wesbury}}</ref> and [[Bloomberg TV]]<ref>{{cite news | url=http://search1.bloomberg.com/search/?content_type=all&page=1&q=wesbury | work=Bloomberg | title=wesbury - Bloomberg Search | url-status=dead | archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140903081145/http://search1.bloomberg.com/search/?content_type=all&page=1&q=wesbury | archivedate=2014-09-03 }}</ref> frequently. He is a member of the Academic Advisory Council of the [[Federal Reserve Bank of Chicago]], and for five years served as an adjunct professor of economics at [[Wheaton College (Illinois)|Wheaton College]] in Wheaton, [[Illinois]].

==Early life and education==

Wesbury was born in Ann Arbor, Michigan. He attended [[Rock Bridge High School]] in [[Columbia, Missouri]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.columbia.k12.mo.us/rbhs/alumni/hall%20of%20fame.htm |title=Rock Bridge High School |publisher=Columbia.k12.mo.us |accessdate=2014-08-27 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140520040114/http://www.columbia.k12.mo.us/rbhs/alumni/hall%20of%20fame.htm |archivedate=2014-05-20 }}</ref>

In 1981, Wesbury received his BA in [[Economics]] from the [[University of Montana]].

Wesbury also attended [[Northwestern University]] for graduate level business studies, and received his [[MBA]] from [[Northwestern University]]'s Kellogg Graduate School of Management in 1989.

==Professional career==

In 1982, Wesbury began his career at the Harris Bank in [[Chicago]].

Wesbury served as Vice President and Economist for the Chicago Corporation from 1990 to 1992 and then as Senior Vice President and Chief Economist for Griffin, Kubik, Stephens, & Thompson, a Chicago Investment Bank from 1992 until 2005, except for 2 years while he served on Capitol Hill.

In 1995 and 1996, Wesbury served as Chief Economist for the [[Joint Economic Committee]] of the [[U.S. Congress]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.heartland.org/policybot/results/17250/Brian_Wesbury_Nationally_Renowned_US_Economic_Forecaster_Joins_The_Heartland_Institute_as_Senior_Fellow.html |title=Brian Wesbury, Nationally Renowned U.S. Economic Forecaster, Joins The Heartland Institute as Senior Fellow |publisher=[[Heartland Institute]] / Heartland.org |date= |accessdate=2014-08-27}}</ref>

In October 1999, McGraw-Hill published Wesbury's first book, "The New Era of Wealth".<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.amazon.com/dp/B0039OH68I |title=New Era of Wealth How Investors Can Profit from the 5 Economic Trends Shaping the Future Later Printing: Amazon.com: Books |date=1999-01-01 |accessdate=2014-08-27}}</ref>

In 2004, Wesbury was honored by ''[[USA Today]]'' as one of the top 10 economic forecasters<ref>{{cite news| url=https://www.usatoday.com/money/economy/2004-03-29-top-forecasters-2003_x.htm | work=USA Today | title=Top 10 economic forecasters | date=March 29, 2004}}</ref> in the United States, and  ranked by ''[[The Wall Street Journal]]'' as the nation's #1 U.S. economic forecaster in 2001.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.camillieconomics.com/docs/WSJ2002.html |title=Economic Forecasters Expect Moderate Recovery in 2002 - Wesbury Led a Group Of Bears Who Called The Recession of 2001 |publisher=Camillieconomics.com |date=2002-01-04 |accessdate=2014-08-27 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130213091237/http://www.camillieconomics.com/docs/WSJ2002.html |archivedate=2013-02-13 }}</ref>

Since 2006 Wesbury has been the Chief Economist at First Trust Advisors L.P, a financial services firm headquartered in Wheaton, Illinois.

In October 2009, Wiley & Sons published Wesbury's second book "It's Not as Bad as You Think"<ref>{{cite book|title=It's Not as Bad as You Think: Why Capitalism Trumps Fear and the Economy Will Thrive: Brian S. Wesbury, Amity Shlaes: 9780470238332: Amazon.com: Books |date= 9 November 2009|isbn=978-0470238332 |last1=Wesbury |first1=Brian S. }}</ref>

==Chief Economist of the Joint Economic Committee==
Beginning in January 1995, this Committee provided information for members of the [[US Congress]] regarding policy decision-making and economic growth objectives. Here, Wesbury directed and advised committee members and members of congress on policy matters and relating to the United States and other nations.

==Wesbury after Government==
After a 13-year stay as Chief Economist for Chicago investment bank Griffin, Kubik, Stephens, & Thompson, and time away from the private sector to serve in government, Wesbury is currently Chief Economist for First Trust Portfolios,<ref>http://www.ftportfolios.com/common/research/brianwesburybio.pdf</ref> a financial services firm located in [[Wheaton, Illinois]].
