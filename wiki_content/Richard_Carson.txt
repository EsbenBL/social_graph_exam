'''Richard Taylor Carson''' (born February 24, 1955, [[Jackson, Mississippi]]) is a [[professor]] of [[economics]] at the [[University of California, San Diego]]. He obtained a [[Bachelor of Arts|B.A.]] degree from [[Mississippi State University]] in 1977, an [[Master of Arts|M.A.]] in [[international relations|international affairs]] from [[George Washington University]] in 1979, and an M.A. in [[statistics]] and a Ph.D. in agricultural and resource economics from the [[University of California, Berkeley]], in 1985.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.econ.ucsd.edu/~rcarson/ |title=Richard T. Carson |publisher=University of California, San Diego |date= |accessdate=2012-10-05}}</ref> He is the author or editor of eight books and over a hundred journal articles, with over 4000 citations to his works.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.aere.org/meetings/richardcarson.php |title=2009 AERE Fellow: Richard Carson |publisher=Association of Environmental and Resource Economists |accessdate=2012-10-05 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110831130423/http://www.aere.org/meetings/richardcarson.php# |archive-date=2011-08-31 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

== Career ==
Carson has been a consultant to a number of non-profit organizations, major corporations, and government agencies, including the Alaska Department of Fish and Game, Australian Resource Assessment Commission, California Attorney General's Office, California Department of Fish and Game, Electric Power Research Institute, Environment Canada, Interamerican Development Bank, Los Angeles Department of Water and Power, Metropolitan Water District of Southern California, National Park Service, National
Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration, OECD, U.K. DEFRA, U.K. MOJ, United Nations, U.S. DOJ, U.S. EPA, and the World Bank. Carson has been a visiting professor at the University of New South Wales, University of Oslo, and the University of Sydney, a Faculty Research Fellow at the National Bureau of Economic Research and a Continuing Consultant at Resources for the Future. He served on the National Academy of Science's Committee on Oil Spill Research and Development and as a member of an Academy
committee reviewing procedures for water resource planning procedures. He was Program Chair for the Second World Congress of Environmental and Resource Economists.<ref>{{cite web|title=Biography|url=http://www.econ.ucsd.edu/~rcarson/rtcbio.pdf|accessdate=2 February 2014}}</ref>
