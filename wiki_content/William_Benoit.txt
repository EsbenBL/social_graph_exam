'''William Lyon Benoit''' (born March 17, 1953)<ref>{{Cite encyclopedia |title=Benoit, William L. 1953- |encyclopedia=Contemporary Authors |url=https://www.encyclopedia.com/arts/educational-magazines/benoit-william-l-1953 |date=2009 |access-date=2019-01-13}}</ref> is an American scholar in the field of [[political communication]].<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.columbiamissourian.com/news/higher_education/talking-politics/article_3bc49a6c-59bf-5c7f-bf70-1f8b2ca570cb.html |title=Talking politics |last=Waster |first=Maureen |date=2008-07-18 |work=Columbia Missourian |access-date=2019-01-13 |language=en}}</ref> He graduated from [[Ball State University]] in 1975 and obtained his Master of Arts degree from [[Central Michigan University]] in 1976. He also holds a PhD from [[Wayne State University]].{{citation needed|date=October 2019}}

He is a professor of [[Communication Studies]] at the [[University of Alabama at Birmingham]]. He previously taught at [[Miami University]], [[Bowling Green State University]], the [[University of Missouri]], and [[Ohio University]]. He was a faculty member at the University of Missouri for twenty-four years.<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.pantagraph.com/news/local/education/researcher-to-deliver-political-talk-at-isu/article_7e00ac57-ac1a-5fb6-bc24-b57ddcaacce7.html |title=Researcher to deliver political talk at ISU |date=2016-10-17 |work=Pantagraph |access-date=2019-01-13 |language=en}}</ref> He is known for developing [[image repair theory]] (originally called "Image Restoration") and for applying it to anecdotes in various real-world contexts.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.uab.edu/cas/communication/faculty-directory/william-l-benoit |title=William L. Benoit |website=University of Alabama at Birmingham |language=en-gb |access-date=2019-01-13}}</ref> He was the editor of the ''[[Journal of Communication]]'' from 2003 to 2005, and of ''[[Communication Studies (journal)|Communication Studies]]'' from 2007 to 2009.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.uab.edu/cas/communication/images/Documents/Faculty/William_Benoit_CV_2017.pdf |title=William Benoit Curriculum Vitae |date=2017 |website=University of Alabama at Birmingham |access-date=2019-01-13}}</ref>
