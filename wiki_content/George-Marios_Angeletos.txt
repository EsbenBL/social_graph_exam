'''George-Marios Angeletos''' ({{lang-el|Γεώργιος-Μάριος Αγγελέτος}}; born in 1975, [[Athens]], [[Greece]])<ref>[http://econ-www.mit.edu/files/3916 George-Marios Angeletos's CV on his MIT webpage]</ref> is a Greek economist who is a Professor of [[Economics]] at [[Massachusetts Institute of Technology]] (MIT).<ref>{{cite web |url=http://economics.mit.edu/faculty/angelet/contact |title=George-Marios Angeletos: Contact Information | date= |website=mit.edu |publisher=MIT |accessdate=4 January 2014}}</ref>

Angeletos was born in [[Athens]], [[Greece]]. He earned his [[Bachelor of Arts|B.A.]] degree in Economics from [[Athens University of Economics and Business]] in 1996, and received his [[M.Sc.]] in Economics in 1997 from the same university. Angeletos earned his [[Ph.D]] in Economics in 2001 from [[Harvard]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://sixmile.clemson.edu/cimba/2001/wsj%20stories/econphds.html |title=Wall Street Journal article |access-date=2009-08-07 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120226125850/http://sixmile.clemson.edu/cimba/2001/wsj%20stories/econphds.html |archive-date=2012-02-26 |url-status=dead }}</ref> Angeletos received a scholarship from the [[Alexander S. Onassis Foundation]] to complete his doctorate. After graduating from Harvard, Angeletos became a member of the MIT faculty, and was awarded tenure there in 2007. In 2006, Angeletos received a [[Sloan Fellowship]],<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.sloan.org/assets/files/annual_reports/2006_annual_report.pdf |title=Alfred P. Sloan Research Fellowship |access-date=2009-07-27 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110618093913/http://www.sloan.org/assets/files/annual_reports/2006_annual_report.pdf |archive-date=2011-06-18 |url-status=dead }}</ref> awarded by the [[Alfred P. Sloan Foundation]]. Shortly after receiving tenure, Angeletos won the Bodossaki Foundation Prize in Social Sciences for distinguished young Greek scientists in 2008.<ref>[http://www.voiceofgreece.gr/news-in-english/bodossaki-foundation-scientific-prizes-for-2008-2.htm Bodossaki Foundation Prize Announcement]</ref> During 2013-14, Angeletos held the Chair of Macroeconomics and Finance at the [[University of Zurich]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.econ.uzh.ch/faculty/angeletos.html |title=Prof. Dr. George-Marios Angeletos |website=uzh.ch |publisher=University of Zürich |accessdate=4 January 2014 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140104162119/http://www.econ.uzh.ch/faculty/angeletos.html |archivedate=4 January 2014 }}</ref>

Angeletos has published many articles in the field of [[macroeconomics]]. In particular, much of his research has investigated the impact of informational frictions in macro settings.<ref>G.M. Angeletos, C. Hellwig and A. Pavan (2006), [http://www.journals.uchicago.edu/doi/pdf/10.1086/504901 'Signaling in a Global Game: Coordination and Policy Traps'. ''Journal of Political Economy''] 114 (3), pp. 452–486.</ref><ref>G.M. Angeletos and I. Werning (2006), [http://hdl.handle.net/10.1257/aer.96.5.1720 'Crises and Prices: Information Aggregation, Multiplicity, and Volatility'. ''American Economic Review''] 96 (5), pp. 1720–1736.</ref><ref>G.M. Angeletos, C. Hellwig and A. Pavan (2007), [http://hdl.handle.net/10.1111/j.1468-0262.2007.00766.x 'Dynamic Global Games of Regime Change: Learning, Mutliplicity and Timing of Attacks'. ''Econometrica''] 75 (3), pp. 711–756.</ref><ref>G.M. Angeletos and A. Pavan (2007), [http://hdl.handle.net/10.1111/j.1468-0262.2007.00783.x 'Efficient Use of Information and Social Value of Information'. ''Econometrica''] 75 (4), pp. 1103–1142.</ref> He has made many notable contributions to the field of [[global games]], which followed the work of Stephen Morris and Hung Song Shin.<ref>Stephen Morris and Hyun Song Shin (1998), "Unique Equilibrium in a Model of Self-Fulfilling Currency Attacks," [[American Economic Review]], 88 (3), 587–97.</ref> More recently, his work has focused on the roles of expectations, information, and bounded rationality in the [[business cycle]].<ref>[https://ideas.repec.org/e/pan143.html Angeletos IDEAS page]</ref>
