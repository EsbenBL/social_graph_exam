'''Karnit Flug''' ({{lang-he|קרנית פלוג}}; born January 9, 1955) is a Polish-born Israeli economist. She held the position of [[governor]] of the [[Bank of Israel|Central Bank of Israel]] between November 2013 and November 2018.<ref>{{cite web|title=Karnit Flug named Bank of Israel governor |url=http://www.haaretz.com/business/1.553395|publisher=[[Haaretz]]|accessdate=20 October 2013}}</ref> She became the first female governor of the Bank of Israel.<ref>{{cite web|last=Cohen |first=Tova |url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-israel-centralbank-idUSBRE99J02C20131020 |title=Karnit Flug named Bank of Israel governor |publisher=Reuters |date=20 October 2013 |accessdate=30 October 2013}}</ref>

Flug won an "A" grade three times in Global Finance Magazine’s annual Central Banker Report Cards, and was repeatedly considered one of the best bank governors in the world.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.gfmag.com/media/video/getting-inflation-back-target-range-challenge-flug|title=Getting Inflation Back To Target Range A Challenge: Flug|website=Global Finance}}</ref>

==Biography==
Karnit Flug graduated with a master's degree in economics from [[Hebrew University of Jerusalem]] in 1980, and a PhD in economics at [[Columbia University]] in New York City in 1985.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://econ.columbia.edu/phd-alum-karnit-flug-named-bank-israel-governor|title=PhD Alum Karnit Flug named Bank of Israel Governor {{!}} Columbia University - Economics|website=econ.columbia.edu|language=|access-date=2017-06-01}}</ref> She was a member of the Board of Governors of the [[International Monetary Fund]] (IMF).<ref>[http://www.imf.org/external/np/sec/memdir/members.aspx Members] [[International Monetary Fund]] (IMF).</ref>
