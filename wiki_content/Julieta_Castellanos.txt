'''Julieta Castellanos''' (born 8 January 1954) is a [[Honduras|Honduran]] sociologist and the dean of the [[Universidad Nacional Autónoma de Honduras|National Autonomous University of Honduras]] (UNAH) since 2009. Castellanos is known for campaigning against violence in Honduras, focusing on both [[drug cartel]]s and [[police corruption]]. She has advocated for both judicial and police reform. Castellanos founded the Observatorio de la Violencia (Violence Observatory) at UNAH in 2004, a center that analyzes crime statistics in Honduras.<ref name="NYT"/> She was also a member of the Truth and Reconciliation Commission, which was tasked with clarifying the facts related to [[2009 Honduran coup d'état|the 2009 coup]] that ousted President [[Manuel Zelaya]].<ref>{{cite news|title=Honduras sets up truth commission|url=http://www.taipeitimes.com/News/world/archives/2010/05/06/2003472273|newspaper=Taipei Times|date=6 May 2010}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Julieta Castellanos Ruiz was born in [[San Francisco de Becerra]], [[Olancho Department|Olancho]] on 8 January 1954 to Rafael Castellanos of [[Santa Bárbara Department, Honduras|Santa Bárbara]], and Ernestina Ruiz of Olancho.<ref name="interview">{{cite news|last=Nivárrez|first=Agustín Lagos|title=Julieta Castellanos Ruiz|url=http://archivo.elheraldo.hn/content/view/full/450800|newspaper=El Heraldo|date=10 December 2010|language=es|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140411081256/http://archivo.elheraldo.hn/content/view/full/450800|archive-date=11 April 2014}}</ref> She grew up in the rural Honduran sugar fields.<ref name="NYT"/> In 1968 her father brought her an examination for admission to the Normal School for Girls in the city of [[Tegucigalpa]]. She was accepted and graduated in 1973 with a teaching degree. In 1974 she won two scholarships to study Social Work at the National Autonomous University of Honduras (UNAH) and Social Sciences in the College of Teachers. After several years of study, she finished her studies with a B.A. in Sociology from the [[University of Costa Rica]].<ref name="interview"/>

After completing her studies Castellanos became a professor at UNAH in 1978. She was head of the Social Sciences Department and President of the Association of Teachers from 1997 to 2001.<ref name="interview"/> Castellanos also served as President of the Association of Professors of the University Center of General Studies (CUEG) in 1986, Coordinator of the Violence Observatory since 2005, consultant to the Arias Foundation for Human Progress and the  [[Washington Office on Latin America]] (WOLA), and Research Associate of the Centro de Documentatión de Honduras (CEDOH).<ref name="interview"/> She is also a member of the [[Inter-American Dialogue]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.thedialogue.org/experts/?iad_experttype=75|title=Inter-American Dialogue {{!}} Experts|website=www.thedialogue.org|access-date=2017-04-11}}</ref>

For 13 years, Castellanos was also the author of a newspaper column.<ref name="NYT"/>

==Chancellor of the UNAH==
Castellanos was elected to a four-year term as Rector of the National Autonomous University of Honduras in 2009.<ref name="Proceso">{{cite news|title=Julieta Castellanos, electa Rectora de la UNAH|url=http://www.proceso.hn/2009/04/24/Nacionales/Julieta.Castellanos.C/12867.html|newspaper=Proceso Digital|date=24 April 2009|language=es}}</ref> At the time of her appointment she served as Coordinator of the UNAH Violence Observatory and Director of the Instituto Universitario en Democracia Paz y Seguridad (Institute for Democracy, Peace and Security, IUDPAS), which was created with support from the [[United Nations Development Programme]] (UNDP) and the [[Swedish International Development Cooperation Agency]] (SIDA) when Castellanos worked as a consultant for the UNDP.<ref>{{cite web|title=Honduras contará con centro de investigaciones único en Latinoamérica|url=https://www.unah.edu.hn/?art=1384|access-date=23 August 2013|publisher=Universidad Nacional Autónoma de Honduras|language=es}}</ref> Castellanos replaced Jorge Abraham Arita, who was recalled for incompetence. In a press conference, College Board president Olvin Rodriguez highlighted the accomplishments of Castellanos. According to Rodriguez, she was chosen for being well-respected, not only by the university community, but also by Honduran society and internationally.<ref name="Proceso"/>

Shortly after her appointment, President Manuel Zelaya was ousted in a coup. During a demonstration by University students, Castellanos was pushed down by police when she attempted to stop them from breaking up the gathering.<ref>{{cite news|title=Police quell student protest in Honduras|url=http://english.eastday.com/e/0806/u1a4561862.html|newspaper=Eastday|date=2009-08-06|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://archive.is/20130823225552/http://english.eastday.com/e/0806/u1a4561862.html|archive-date=2013-08-23}}</ref> During her tenure as rector, she has clashed with SITRAUNAH, the union for the University's employees, and has been criticized for the firing of 60 employees who were protesting on the University's premises in 2009.<ref>{{cite news|title=Sindicalistas de la UNAH protestan en la Corte|url=http://www.elheraldo.hn/Secciones-Principales/Pais/Sindicalistas-de-la-UNAH-protestan-en-la-Corte|newspaper=El Heraldo|date=21 August 2013|language=es}}</ref> Castellanos oversaw the construction of a sports complex,<ref>{{cite news|title=UNAH construirá Polideportivo para albergar Juegos Universitarios de 2013|url=http://www.proceso.hn/2011/02/10/Deportes/UNAH.construir.C/33641.html|newspaper=Proceso Digital|date=10 February 2011}}</ref> an administrative building, and a university clinic, for which UNAH invested [[Honduran lempira|L]]1,500 million.<ref name="interim">{{cite news|title=Julieta Castellanos asume como rectora interina|url=http://www.elheraldo.hn/Secciones-Principales/Pais/Julieta-Castellanos-asume-como-rectora-interina|newspaper=El Heraldo|date=30 April 2013|language=es}}</ref>

Castellanos' four years as rector ended in April 2013. She remained interim rector<ref name="interim"/> and was re-elected for another term as head of the university in September 2013.<ref>{{cite news|title=La rectora tiene ventaja porque ella nombró a miembros de la JDU|url=http://www.latribuna.hn/2013/08/21/la-rectora-tiene-ventaja-porque-ella-nombro-a-miembros-de-la-jdu/|archive-url=https://archive.is/20130823225504/http://www.latribuna.hn/2013/08/21/la-rectora-tiene-ventaja-porque-ella-nombro-a-miembros-de-la-jdu/|url-status=dead|archive-date=23 August 2013|newspaper=La Tribuna|date=21 August 2013|language=es}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Julieta Castellanos es reelecta como rectora de la Unah por 4 años|url=http://www.laprensa.hn/honduras/tegucigalpa/388340-98/julieta-castellanos-es-reelecta-como-rectora-de-la-unah-por-4-a%C3%B1os|access-date=18 May 2014|newspaper=[[La Prensa (Honduras)|La Prensa]]|date=20 September 2013}}</ref>

==Campaigning against violence and corruption==
Castellanos has been a vocal advocate for police reform and measures to curb violent [[crime in Honduras]].<ref>{{cite news|last=Salinas|first=Carlos|title=La madre coraje de Honduras|url=http://internacional.elpais.com/internacional/2013/04/01/actualidad/1364850378_369969.html|newspaper=El Pais|date=1 April 2013|language=es}}</ref> She pushed for an international commission to oversee a purge of the police, an idea that was approved by the [[National Congress of Honduras|National Congress]].<ref name="NYT"/> Castellanos has also weighed in on [[gun politics in Honduras]], calling on the Honduran armed forces to destroy illegal firearms, including [[AK-47]]s.<ref>{{cite news|title=No tuvimos reporte de a quién se le dio la recompensa: Julieta Castellanos|url=http://archivo.elheraldo.hn/Pa%C3%ADs/obj-relacionados/Ediciones/2011/11/19/Noticias/No-tuvimos-reporte-de-a-quien-se-le-dio-la-recompensa-Julieta-Castellanos|newspaper=El Heraldo|date=18 November 2011|language=es|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://archive.is/20130823225519/http://archivo.elheraldo.hn/Pa%C3%ADs/obj-relacionados/Ediciones/2011/11/19/Noticias/No-tuvimos-reporte-de-a-quien-se-le-dio-la-recompensa-Julieta-Castellanos|archive-date=23 August 2013}}</ref>

In October 2011, Castellanos' 22-year-old son was kidnapped and murdered by Honduran national police.<ref name="NYT">{{cite news|last=Hernandez|first=Javier C.|title=An Academic Turns Grief Into a Crime-Fighting Tool|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2012/02/25/world/americas/academic-turns-grief-into-crime-fighting-tool-in-honduras.html?pagewanted=all&_r=0|newspaper=The New York Times|date=24 February 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Conmoción y dolor por muerte de hijo de rectora Julieta Castellanos|url=http://www.radiocadenavoceshn.com/rcv/todas-las-noticias/nacionales/conmocion-y-dolor-por-muerte-de-hijo-de-rectora-julieta-castellanos.html|newspaper=Radio Cadena Voces|date=October 2011|language=es|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130802003213/http://www.radiocadenavoceshn.com/rcv/todas-las-noticias/nacionales/conmocion-y-dolor-por-muerte-de-hijo-de-rectora-julieta-castellanos.html|archive-date=2013-08-02}}</ref> The incident called attention to the degree of corruption within the Honduran police. Castellanos called for an end to foreign aid for the Honduran police and military, demanding that they "stop feeding the beast."<ref>{{cite news|last=Frank|first=Dana|title=Honduras: Which Side Is the US On?|url=http://www.thenation.com/article/167994/honduras-which-side-us#|newspaper=The Nation|date=June 11, 2012}}</ref>

==Awards==
Castellanos was presented with the inaugural Martin Luther King, Jr. Award on 20 April 2012 at a ceremony sponsored by the Martin Luther King, Jr. Foundation and the Instituto Hondureño de Cultura Interamericana.<ref>{{cite web|title=Rector Julieta Castellanos Receives Martin Luther King, Jr. Award|url=http://honduras.usembassy.gov/pr-042012-eng.html|publisher=Embassy of the United States Tegucigalpa, Honduras|date=20 April 2012|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131025131124/http://honduras.usembassy.gov/pr-042012-eng.html|archive-date=25 October 2013}}</ref>

Castellanos received the [[International Women of Courage Award]] in March 2013 from the U.S. State Department.<ref>{{cite web|last=Demers|first=Peter|title=Security and Human Rights in Honduras: A Conversation with Julieta Castellanos|url=http://www.thedialogue.org/page.cfm?pageID=32&pubID=3253|publisher=Inter-American Dialogue|date=7 March 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150316204553/http://www.thedialogue.org/page.cfm?pageID=32&pubID=3253|archive-date=16 March 2015}}</ref> The award was presented by US Secretary of State [[John Kerry]] and First Lady [[Michelle Obama]].<ref>{{cite news|title=EEUU entrega el Premio al Valor a Julieta Castellanos|url=http://www.tiempo.hn/portada/noticias/eeuu-entrega-el-premio-al-valor-a-julieta-castellanos|newspaper=Tiempo|date=8 March 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130314104041/http://tiempo.hn/portada/noticias/eeuu-entrega-el-premio-al-valor-a-julieta-castellanos|archive-date=14 March 2013}}</ref>
