'''Richard E. Baldwin''' is a professor of international economics at the [[Graduate Institute of International and Development Studies]] in [[Geneva]], where he has been researching [[globalization]] and [[trade]] for the past 30 years.<ref name="globalization">{{cite news|url=http://qz.com/854257/brace-yourself-the-most-disruptive-phase-of-globalization-is-just-beginning/|title=Brace yourself: the most news|url=http://www.economist.com/node/21559944|title=Free exchange: Chains of gold|date=Aug 4, 2012|newspaper=[[The Economist]]|accessdate=5 October 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite Balance|last=Krugman|first=Paul|date=16 November 2009|work=The New York Times|page=25|accessdate=5 January 2013}}</ref> He is also ex-President of the [[Centre for Economic Policy Research]] (CEPR) and current Editor-in-Chief of VoxEU,<ref>{{cite web |title=About VoxEU |url=https://voxeu.org/pages/about-vox |website=www.voxeu.org |publisher=CEPR |accessdate=9 October 2018}}</ref> which he Baldwin has been called "one of the most important thinkers in this era of global disruption".<ref>https://www.ft.com/content/892c6c1e-1d8f-11e9-a46f-08f9738d6b2b</ref>

== Career ==
After obtaining a bachelor's degree in economics from the [[University of Wisconsin–Madison|University of Wisconsin-Madison]] in 1980, he received a master's degree from the [[London School of Economics]] in 1981. He completed his [[PhD]] at [[MIT]] in 1986 under the guidance of [[Paul Krugman]], with whom he has co-authored half a dozen articles. He received honorary doctorates from the [[Turku School of Economics]] ([[Finland]]), [[University of St. Gallen]] ([[Switzerland]]) and [[Pontifical Catholic University of Peru]] (PUCP).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.utu.fi/fi/yksikot/tse/tutkimus/kunniatohtorit/Sivut/home.aspx|title=Turun kauppakorkeakoulun kunniatohtorit 1989‒2012|accessdate=5 January 2013}}</ref><ref name=GIIDCV/> The [[International Economic Association]] made him a Schumpeter-Haberler Distinguished Fellow in 2021.<ref>http://www.iea-world.org/</ref>

He was Associate Professor (1989–1991) and Assistant Professor (1986–1989) at [[Columbia University Business School]]. In 1990–1991 he followed trade matters for the President's [[Council of Economic Advisors]] in the Bush White House.  He worked as an Associate Economic Affairs Officer for [[UNCTAD]] in the early 1980s.  In 1991 he joined the [[Graduate Institute of International and Development Studies]] as Professor of International Economics, where he has remained. He has also been a visiting research professor at MIT (2003), Oxford (2012-2015), and is still an  Associate Member of [[Nuffield College]] at Oxford University. He has consulted for many governments and international organisations including the [[EU]], the [[OECD]], the [[World Bank]], [[EFTA]], and [[USAID]].

== Research ==

He has published extensively in the areas of globalisation, international trade, regionalism, WTO, European integration, economic geography, political economy and growth, and is recognised as an expert on the economic drivers and risks of globalisation.<ref>{{cite news |title=The past and future global economyThe third wave of globalisation may be the hardest |url=https://www.economist.com/books-and-arts/2016/11/19/the-third-wave-of-globalisation-may-be-the-hardest |accessdate=9 October 2018 |publisher=The Economist |date=19 November 2016}}</ref><ref name="graduateinstitute">{{cite web|url = http://graduateinstitute.ch/faculty/directory/baldwin|title = The Graduate Institute – Faculty directory|accessdate = 5 January 2013}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last1=Bowler |first1=Tim |title=Will globalisation take away your job? |url=https://www.bbc.com/news/business-38600270 |accessdate=9 October 2018 |agency=BBC News |publisher=BBC |date=1 February 2017}}</ref> His book, ''The Great Convergence: Information Technology and the New Globalization'', was published in November 2016 and listed among the Best Books of 2016 by The Financial Times and The Economist magazine.<ref name="HUP">{{cite web|url = http://www.hup.harvard.edu/catalog.php?isbn=9780674660489|title = Harvard University Press: The Great Convergence, Richard Baldwin|accessdate = 25 January 2017}}</ref>  He also writes extensively on current economic policy.<ref>{{cite web |title=Richard Baldwin on VoxEU |url=https://voxeu.org/users/richardbaldwin |website=voxeu.org |publisher=CEPR |accessdate=9 October 2018}}</ref> He has over 51,000 [https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=t3Hj5BgAAAAJ&hl=en Google Scholar] cites and an H-index of 94.

His latest book, ''[https://www.amazon.com/dp/B07LFLMRXW/ref=dp-kindle-redirect?_encoding=UTF8&btkr=1 The Globotics Upheaval: Globalization, Robotics and the Future of Work]'', addresses the role of digital technology in driving both globalisation and automation of service and professional jobs in advanced economies; it has been translated into six languages. With [[Charles Wyplosz]], he has a leading textbook on the [https://books.google.ch/books/about/The_Economics_of_European_Integration.html?id=UTYEywAACAAJ&redir_esc=y Economics of European Integration], which is in its 6th Edition with McGraw-Hill.
