'''Nancy Kassop''' is a Professor at the [[State University of New York at New Paltz]],<ref name=prg>{{cite web|title=PRG Steering Committee - 2006-2007|url=http://cstl-cla.semo.edu/renka/prg/prg_leadership_history.asp|work=THE PRESIDENCY RESEARCH GROUP|accessdate=18 April 2011}}</ref>  and former chair of the Political Science Department at the school.  Some of the courses she teaches are American Government and Politics and Constitutional Law. Her research focuses on [[constitutional law]] and the [[United States President|Presidency]]. Among her many articles and publications is "Expansion and Contraction: [[Bill Clinton|Clinton's]] Impact on the Scope of Presidential Power" from her book titled ''The Presidency and the Law: The Clinton Legacy by Adler and Genovese'' and "A Political Question By Any Other Name: Government Strategy in the Enemy Combatant Cases of Hamdi and Padilla" from her [[Lexington Books]] 2007 publication "The Political Question Doctrine and the Supreme Court of the United States."

Since the George W. Bush administration, her focus has shifted towards presidential war powers and national security law.<ref>{{cite journal|last1=Kassop|first1=Nancy|title=The War Power and Its Limits|journal=Presidential Studies Quarterly|date=September 2003|volume=33|issue=3|pages=509â€“529|url=http://www.uvm.edu/~dguber/POLS21/articles/kassop.htm|doi=10.1111/1741-5705.00004}}</ref> Some of her most recent research explores the role of the [[Presidency of Barack Obama|Obama administration's]] political staff and Office of Legal Counsel in making counterterrorism policy.<ref>{{cite journal|last1=Kassop|first1=Nancy|title=Rivals for Influence on Counterterrorism Policymaking in the Obama Administration: White House Political Staff vs. Executive Branch Legal Advisors|journal=APSA Annual Meeting Paper|date=August 20, 2013|ssrn=2300386}}</ref>

Dr. Nancy C. Kassop graduated from the [[University of Pennsylvania]] with a B.A. in Political Science and earned her Ph.D and M.A. in political science from [[New York University]].
