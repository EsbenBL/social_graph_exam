'''Krenar Gashi''' (born 1982) is a [[Kosovo|Kosovar]] [[Albanians|Albanian]] [[political scientist]], currently based in [[Belgium]] as a Basileus doctoral fellow at the Centre for EU Studies, [[Ghent University]].<ref>[http://ceus.ugent.be/researchers/krenar-gashi Official Website of the Centre for EU Studies]</ref>  His research interest include international relations, the [[European Union]] and the works of [[Jean Baudrillard]], while his expertise is in the political developments in the [[Balkans|Western Balkans]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=njRHJ9wAAAAJ&hl=en&oi=ao|title=Google Scholar Citations - Krenar Gashi|last=|first=|date=|website=scholar.google.com|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2018-03-04}}</ref>

Gashi studied sociology at the [[Universiteti i Prishtinës|University of Pristina]], and obtained an MA in Journalism and Communication. In 2013, Gashi obtained an MA (with distinction) in European Politics from the [[University of Sussex]], and was awarded the Jean Monnet Prize by the Sussex European Institute.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://capitadiscovery.co.uk/sussex-ac/items/1190356 |title=Sussex Library, Official Website |access-date=2014-10-12 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141024072519/http://capitadiscovery.co.uk/sussex-ac/items/1190356 |archive-date=2014-10-24 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

Gashi started his career as a reporter, having worked for key Kosovo print and broadcast media. He reported for the ''[[Financial Times]]''<ref>[http://www.ft.com/intl/cms/s/0/12ca9634-d6b2-11dc-b9f4-0000779fd2ac.html Gashi's stories in the Financial Times]</ref> and was the Kosovo Editor of the Balkan Investigative Reporting Network, where he edited ''[[Balkan Insight]]''<ref>[http://www.balkaninsight.com/en/article/ex-policemen-run-kosovo-passport-scam Gashi's stories in Balkan Insight]</ref> and established Kosovo's only English-language newspaper, ''Prishtina Insight''.

In 2009, Gashi joined KIPRED institute, Kosovo's first think tank, initially as a research director and later executive director. In 2011 he established the Institute for Development Policy (INDEP) a think tank that focuses on democratisation and sustainable development.<ref>[http://www.indep.info/ Official website of the Institute for Development Policy]</ref>  Under Gashi's leadership INDEP became the most influential think tank in Kosovo, working closely with the [[Assembly of Kosovo]] and its parliamentary committees.

His publications include papers on democratization, election reform, development policy and European integration.<ref>[http://www.krenargashi.com/  Krenar Gashi's official website ]</ref>  He wrote Kosovo sections of [[Bertelsmann Foundation]]'s [[Bertelsmann Transformation Index|Transformation Index]]<ref>[http://download.e-bookshelf.de/download/0000/6409/93/L-G-0000640993-0002386176.pdf Bertelsmann Transformation Index ]</ref>  and [[Freedom House]]'s Nation in Transit report, which is one of the most eminent publication assessing the democratization process in the world.

He appears frequently in national and international media and engages in public debates on politics of Kosovo, the Western Balkans and the European Union.<ref>[http://www.gazetatribuna.com/?FaqeID=2&LajmID=83164 Gashi's interview on Kosovo's 2014 political stalemate for the Tribuna daily (in Albanian)]</ref><ref>[http://gazetablic.com/kete-pdk-vullnet-ta-leshoje-pushtetin-veshtire-thote-krenar-gashi/ Gashi's interview for Blic Newspaper]</ref><ref>[http://voiceofrussia.com/2011/10/01/57381990/ Gashi's interview on peacekeeping in Kosovo for Voice of Russia]</ref>
