'''Simon H. Johnson''' (born January 16, 1963)<ref>''U.S. Public Records Index'' Vol 1 (Provo, UT: Ancestry.com Operations, Inc.), 2010.</ref> is a [[British American]] economist. He is the Ronald A. Kurtz Professor of Entrepreneurship at the [[MIT Sloan School of Management]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=101360253|title=Simon Johnson On Bank Bailout Plan|website=NPR.org}}</ref> and a senior fellow at the [[Peterson Institute for International Economics]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.piie.com/experts/senior-research-staff/simon-johnson|title=Simon Johnson|date=March 2, 2016|website=PIIE}}</ref> He has held a wide variety of academic and policy-related positions, including Professor of Economics at [[Duke University]]'s [[Fuqua School of Business]].<ref>''LA Times'', 29 November 1991, [http://community.seattletimes.nwsource.com/archive/?date=19911129&slug=1320104 "Muscovites: Want Shares In Boeing For 44 ½?"]</ref> From March 2007 through the end of August 2008, he was [[List of IMF Economic Counsellors|Chief Economist]] of the [[International Monetary Fund]].<ref name=MITbio>{{Cite web|url=http://mitsloan.mit.edu/faculty/detail.php?in_spseqno=198&co_list=F|title=Simon Johnson's biography at MIT}}</ref>

He is author, with [[James Kwak]], of the 2010 book ''[[13 Bankers: The Wall Street Takeover and the Next Financial Meltdown]]'' ({{ISBN|978-0307379054}}), with whom he has also co-founded and regularly contributes to the economics blog The Baseline Scenario.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://baselinescenario.com/about/|title=About|date=September 25, 2008}}</ref>

==Education==
Johnson's first degree was a [[bachelor of Arts|BA]] from the [[University of Oxford]], which was followed by an [[Master of Arts|MA]] from the [[University of Manchester]],{{citation needed|date=October 2014}} and finally in 1989 he earned a [[Doctor of Philosophy|Ph.D.]] in [[economics]] from MIT, with a dissertation entitled ''Inflation, intermediation, and economic activity''.<ref>{{cite web | url   =http://library.mit.edu/item/000403287 | title = Inflation, intermediation and economic activity | publisher = MIT library}}</ref>

==Career==
In November 2020, Johnson was named a volunteer member of the [[Presidential transition of Joe Biden|Joe Biden presidential transition]] Agency Review Team to support transition efforts related to the [[United States Department of Treasury]] and the [[Federal Reserve]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Agency Review Teams |url=https://buildbackbetter.com/the-transition/agency-review-teams/ |website=President-Elect Joe Biden |accessdate=10 November 2020}}</ref>

==Affiliations==
Among other positions he is a Research Associate at the [[National Bureau of Economic Research]], a Research Fellow at the [[Centre for Economic Policy Research]],<ref name="CEPR">{{Cite web|url=http://www.cepr.org/research/resfel_long.asp|title=List of Center for Economic Policy Research Fellows}}</ref> and a member of the International Advisory Council at the Center for Social and Economic Research (CASE). He is also a member of the [[Congressional Budget Office]]'s Panel of Economic Advisers.<ref name=MITbio/> From 2006 to 2007 he was a visiting fellow at the [[Peterson Institute for International Economics]], where he is currently a senior fellow.<ref name=MITbio/> He is on the editorial board of four academic economics journals.<ref name=MITbio/> He has contributed to ''[[Project Syndicate]]'' since 2007.
