'''Ruth M.J. Byrne''', [[Trinity College Dublin|FTCD]], [[Royal Irish Academy|MRIA]], (born 1962) is an [[Republic of Ireland|Irish]] [[cognitive scientist]] and [[author]] of several books on human [[psychology of reasoning|reasoning]].  {{as of|2020}} she is Professor of Cognitive Science, in the Institute of Neuroscience & School of Psychology, [[Trinity College Dublin]]; she is also the former Vice [[Provost (education)|Provost]] of Trinity College Dublin.

==Career==
Byrne earned a BA in Psychology from [[University College Dublin]] in 1983.  She pursued a PhD in Cognitive Psychology from [[Trinity College Dublin]] (TCD) in 1987, while also working as a research scientist at the [[Applied Psychology Unit]], [[University of Cambridge]], from 1986 to 1989.  She worked as a lecturer in Psychology at [[Cardiff University]] from 1989 to 1991, and then lectured in the same area at Trinity College Dublin from 1991 to 1999, as well as, briefly, on Computer Science at University College Dublin.  Byrne was elected as a Fellow of TCD in 1995, and became an Associate Professor in 1999, and the holder of a full professorial chair in 2005.  She also served as Vice Provost of TCD from 2005 to 2008.<ref name="TCD_prfl">{{cite web |title=Professor Ruth Byrne Professor of Cognitive Science, Psychology |url=https://www.tcd.ie/research/profiles/?profile=rmbyrne |website=Trinity College Dublin |accessdate=8 August 2020}}</ref>

Byrne was elected as a member of the [[Royal Irish Academy]] in 2007.<ref name="TCD_prfl"/>

==Publication==
Her writings include: 
* [http://mitpress.mit.edu/catalog/item/default.asp?tid=10544&ttype=2 ''The Rational Imagination: How People Create Alternatives to Reality'']  (2005, [[MIT Press]])
* ''Deduction'' (1991, co-author [[Philip Johnson-Laird]], [[Erlbaum]])
* ''Human Reasoning'' (1993, with Jonathan Evans & Stephen Newstead, [[Erlbaum]])
