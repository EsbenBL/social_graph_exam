'''Douglas Steven Massey''' (born October 5, 1952 in [[Olympia, Washington|Olympia]], Washington, United States) is an American [[Sociology|sociologist]]. Massey is currently a professor of Sociology at the [[Princeton School of Public and International Affairs]] at [[Princeton University]] and is an adjunct professor of Sociology at the [[University of Pennsylvania]].  

Massey specializes in the sociology of immigration, and has written on the effect of [[residential segregation]] on the black underclass in the United States.
He has been president of the [[Population Association of America]], the [[American Sociological Association]] and the [[American Academy of Political and Social Science]]. He is a [[editor-in-chief|co-editor]] of the ''[[Annual Review of Sociology]]''.<ref name="ARS">{{cite journal |title=Front Matter |journal=Annual Review of Sociology |date=2005 |volume=31 |url=https://www.jstor.org/stable/29737708 |access-date=27 October 2021 |issn=0360-0572}}</ref>

==Academia==
Massey received his [[Bachelor of Arts]] in Sociology, Psychology, and Spanish, from [[Western Washington University]] in 1974, and in 1977 he received a [[Master of Arts]] in Sociology from [[Princeton University]].  Massey continued at [[Princeton University]] and received his PhD in 1978. He was a [[Guggenheim Fellowship|Guggenheim fellow]] in 1990–1991. 

Douglas S. Massey is the founder and co-director of the Latin American Migration Project,<ref name="LAMP">{{cite web |title=Latin American Migration Project |url=http://lamp.opr.princeton.edu |website=Princeton University |access-date=27 October 2021}}</ref> and the Mexican Migration Project with his long-time collaborator [[Jorge G. Durand]].<ref name="MMP">{{cite web |title=Mexican Migration Project |url=http://mmp.opr.princeton.edu |website=Princeton University |access-date=27 October 2021}}</ref> 
He is Board Member of the [[Institute for Interdisciplinary Research on Conflict and Violence]] (''Institut für interdisziplinäre Konflikt und Gewaltforschung'') at [[Bielefeld University]],  a past editor of the ''[[International Journal of Conflict and Violence]]'' and a  [[editor-in-chief|co-editor]] of the ''[[Annual Review of Sociology]]''.<ref name="ARS"/>

Massey was president of the Population Association of America in 1996. He served as the 92nd president of the [[American Sociological Association]], 2000–2001,<ref name="ASA">{{cite web |title=Douglas S. Massey |url=https://www.asanet.org/about/governance-and-leadership/council/presidents/douglas-s-massey |website=American Sociological Association |access-date=27 October 2021 |language=en |date=4 June 2009}}</ref> From 2006 to 2015, he was the president of the [[American Academy of Political and Social Science]].<ref>{{cite web |title=American Academy of Political and Social Science |url=http://www.hetwebsite.net/het/schools/aapss.htm |website=The History of Economic Thought |access-date=27 October 2021}}</ref> In 2008, he received a special recognition from the [[World Cultural Council]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Special Recognitions 2008 |publisher=[[World Cultural Council]] |url=http://www.consejoculturalmundial.org/memories-ceremony2008-SpecialRecognitions.php |access-date=August 19, 2013 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131105183105/http://www.consejoculturalmundial.org/memories-ceremony2008-SpecialRecognitions.php |archive-date=November 5, 2013 |df=mdy }}</ref>

Massey's research areas include:
[[demography]], [[urban sociology]], [[race and ethnicity]], [[international migration]], and [[Latin America]]n society, particularly [[Mexico]].
He has won several awards for his works.<ref name="NAS">{{cite web |title=Douglas S. Massey |url=http://www.nasonline.org/member-directory/members/2519299.html |website=National Academy of Sciences |access-date=27 October 2021}}</ref>
