'''Domingo Isabelino Laíno Figueredo''' (July 8, 1935 in [[Asunción]], [[Paraguay]]<ref>[http://www.mec.gov.py/cmsmec/wp-content/uploads/2009/07/8-de-julio.doc 8 DE JULIO - Hace tiempo, un día como hoy ocurría…], at the Ministerio de Educación y Cultura - Paraguay; published 2009; retrieved July 23, 2015</ref>) is a [[Paraguay]]an politician, economist, and activist. The leader of the [[Authentic Radical Liberal Party]], a [[Senate of Paraguay|Senator]], and a former [[Chamber of Deputies of Paraguay|Member of the Chamber of Deputies]], Laino first became known for his opposition to the [[Alfredo Stroessner|Stroessner]] dictatorship: in 1956, he was arrested for having publicly opposed the arrest of university students. In the years that followed, he was arrested so many times that he "lost count".<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20121102121105/http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P2-1168957.html Stroessner Foe Takes Jailings In His Stride; In Paraguay, Politics Put Police in Touch] from ''[[The Washington Post]]'', January 14, 1989, by Tyler Bridges, archived at [http://Highbeam.com highbeam.com] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20020331041952/http://highbeam.com/ |date=2002-03-31 }}</ref>

In September 1979, Laíno was confined to the town of [[Mbuyapey]], 182&nbsp;km from Asuncion.<ref>[http://www.desclasificados.com.ar/index.php?ref=http://www.desclasificados.com.ar/i.php?i=1154 ''Paraguay Watch'', October 24 1979] archived at desclasificados.com.ar</ref><ref>[http://www.cidh.org/countryrep/Paraguay87eng/chap.4.htm Paraguay 1987, Chapter 4] at the [[Inter-American Commission on Human Rights]]</ref> In December 1982, he was arrested, allegedly for painting anti-government slogans on walls;<ref>[http://www.cidh.oas.org/annualrep/83.84.eng/Paraguay4563.htm RESOLUTION Nº 3/84: CASE 4563: PARAGUAY] at the [[Inter-American Commission on Human Rights]]</ref> he was subsequently deported to [[Argentina]]. He attempted to return in March 1983, in March 1985, twice in December 1985, and in June 1986; each time, he was blocked.

In April 1987, Laíno was finally allowed to return to Paraguay;<ref>[https://www.nytimes.com/1987/04/26/world/paraguay-lets-an-exile-return.html Paraguay Lets an Exile Return], ''[[The New York Times]]'' , April 26, 1987</ref><ref>[http://www.cidh.org/countryrep/Paraguay87eng/chap.4.htm Paraguay 1987, Chapter 4] at the [[Inter-American Commission on Human Rights]]</ref> however, he continued his opposition to the Stroessner regime, leading to his being arrested again in September 1987<ref>[https://www.nytimes.com/1987/09/28/world/twenty-opposition-leaders-seized-by-police-in-paraguay.html Twenty Opposition Leaders Seized by Police in Paraguay] at ''The New York Times'' , September 28, 1987</ref> and February 1988.<ref>[https://www.nytimes.com/1988/02/15/world/paraguayan-wins-his-eighth-term.html PARAGUAYAN WINS HIS EIGHTH TERM] at ''The New York Times'' , by Alan Riding, February 15, 1988</ref>

In February 1989, Alfredo Stroessner was overthrown by [[Andrés Rodríguez (President)|Andrés Rodríguez]], who implemented widespread reforms; these reforms included a presidential election in May 1989. Laino ran for President, but lost to Rodriguez. Laino ran for President in the two subsequent elections as well, but came second each time (losing to [[Juan Carlos Wasmosy]] in 1993, and to [[Raúl Cubas Grau]] in 1998).

==Awards==
In 1991, [[Liberal International]] named Laino co-winner of that year's [[Prize For Freedom]].<ref>[http://www.liberal-international.org/editorial.asp?ia_id=705 Profile of Domingo Laino] at [[Liberal International]]</ref>
