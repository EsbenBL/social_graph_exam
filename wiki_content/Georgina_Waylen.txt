'''Georgina Nicola Alexandra Waylen''', {{Post-nominals|country=GBR|size=100%|sep=,|FBA|FAcSS}} (born 1959) is a British [[political scientist]], specialising in [[comparative politics]], [[political economy]], and [[Gender studies|gender and politics]]. Since April 2012, she has been Professor of Politics at the [[University of Manchester]]. She previously taught at the [[University of Sheffield]], the [[University of Salford]] and the [[University of East Anglia]]. She was a [[visiting scholar]] at the Minda de Gunzburg Center for European Studies, [[Harvard University]] from 2016 to 2017, and has been a visiting professor in the Department of Gender Studies at the [[London School of Economics]] since 2018.<ref name="bio Man">{{cite web |title=Prof Georgina Waylen |url=https://www.research.manchester.ac.uk/portal/georgina.waylen.html |website=The University of Manchester |accessdate=27 June 2019 |language=en}}</ref><ref name="bio CES">{{cite web |title=Georgina Waylen |url=https://ces.fas.harvard.edu/people/georgina-waylen |website=Center for European Studies |publisher=Harvard University |accessdate=27 June 2019 |language=en |date=26 June 2019}}</ref><ref name="bio LSE">{{cite web |title=Georgina Waylen |url=http://www.lse.ac.uk/gender/people/visiting-scholars/Georgina-Waylen |website=London School of Economics and Political Science |accessdate=27 June 2019 |language=en-gb}}</ref>

==Education==
Waylen studied politics and economics at the University of Manchester, graduating with a [[Bachelor of Arts]] (BA) degree in 1981.<ref name="bio AN" /> She remained at Manchester to undertake postgraduate studies in political development between 1981 and 1983, graduating with a [[Master of Arts]] (MA) degree.<ref name="bio AN" /> She then joined [[Huddersfield Polytechnic]] to undertake her [[Doctor of Philosophy]] (PhD) degree.<ref name="bio AN" /> Her [[doctoral thesis]] was titled "British capital, local capital and the role of the state in the political economy of Jamaica 1920-1940".<ref>{{cite thesis |last1=Waylen |first1=Georgina Nicola Alexandra |title=British capital, local capital and the role of the state in the political economy of Jamaica 1920-1940 |url=https://ethos.bl.uk/OrderDetails.do?uin=uk.bl.ethos.233575 |website=E-Thesis Online Service |publisher=The British Library |accessdate=27 June 2019 |date=1988|type=Ph.D }}</ref> As Huddersfield Polytechnic did not have degree awarding powers, she was awarded her PhD by the [[Council for National Academic Awards]] (CNAA) in 1988.<ref name="bio AN" />
