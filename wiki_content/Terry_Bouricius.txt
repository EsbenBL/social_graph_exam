'''Terrill G. Bouricius''' (born March 27, 1954) is an American politician who served in the [[Vermont House of Representatives]] from the Chittenden-7-4 district from 1991 to 2001, as a member of the [[Vermont Progressive Party]]. Prior to his tenure in the state house, he served on the city council in [[Burlington, Vermont]], from 1981 to 1991, from the 2nd district, and served as president of the city council.

Bouricius was born in [[New Mexico]], and educated at [[Middlebury College]]. He entered politics with his activity in the [[Liberty Union Party]], where he served as chair of the [[Addison County, Vermont|Addison County]] affiliate and the statewide party, and ran for a seat in the [[Vermont Senate]] twice with their nomination. He left the Liberty Union Party to aid in the creation of the [[Citizens Party (United States)|Citizens Party]], and ran for state senate and [[List of lieutenant governors of Vermont|lieutenant governor]].

Bouricius was elected to the city council in Burlington, becoming the first member of the Citizens Party elected in the United States. He was an ally to Mayor [[Bernie Sanders]], and served one term as president of the city council. He ran for a seat in the state house in 1986, and lost by six votes to Ben Truman, but later defeated Truman in the 1990 election and served until his retirement in 2000. He and [[Tom Smith (Vermont politician)|Tom Smith]] were the first members of the [[Vermont Progressive Party|Progressive Coalition]] elected to the state house.

==Career==
===Early campaigns===

Bouricius served as chair of the [[Liberty Union Party]] in [[Addison County, Vermont|Addison County]].<ref name="life" /> Bouricius was selected to serve as chair of the Liberty Union Party after [[Bernie Sanders]] left the party in 1977, and served until he was replaced by Jack Craven following his resignation in 1978.<ref>{{Cite news |date=October 11, 1977 |title=Sanders Announces He'S Leaving Party |page=8 |work=[[Barre Montpelier Times Argus]] |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/79846186/the-times-argus/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://archive.today/2QWGX |archive-date=June 19, 2021 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |date=August 6, 1978 |title=Liberty Union Adopts Platform Statement |page=6 |work=[[Rutland Herald]] |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/79846355/rutland-daily-herald/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://archive.today/hTpIE |archive-date=June 19, 2021 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref> In 1980, Earl Gardner, Jame Franco, and Bouricius, who were all former members of the Liberty Union Party, aided in the creation of the Citizens Party in Vermont.<ref>{{Cite news |date=May 14, 1980 |title=Strauss Resigns Chairmanship Of Liberty Union |page=5 |work=[[The Burlington Free Press]] |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/79847302/the-burlington-free-press/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://archive.today/P5sSC |archive-date=June 19, 2021 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref>

====Tenure====

[[Bernie Sanders]], who had been endorsed by the Citizens Party, defeated incumbent Democratic Mayor [[Gordon Paquette]] by ten votes in the [[1981 Burlington mayoral election|1981 mayoral election]].<ref>{{Cite news |date=March 14, 1981 |title=Sanders' Victory Affirmed |page=1 |work=[[Barre Montpelier Times Argus]] |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/64539653/the-times-argus/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://archive.today/TwHDP |archive-date=June 18, 2021 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref><ref name="citizens"/> Following his victory in the 1981 election Sanders faced difficulties with the city council due to eleven of the thirteen members of the board of alderman opposing Sanders. The council would oppose measures proposed by Sanders and override his vetoes on legislation.<ref>{{Cite news |date=November 27, 2019 |title=Bernie Sanders vs. The Machine |work=[[The New York Times]] |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2019/11/27/us/politics/bernie-sanders-mayor-burlington-vt.html |url-status=live |archive-url=https://archive.today/j3u4M |archive-date=December 5, 2020}}</ref> Bouricius and Sadie White were the only members of the city council aligned with Sanders.<ref>{{Cite news |date=March 4, 1982 |title=Sanders' Backers Break Democrats' Tight Grip On Burlington Politics |page=8 |work=[[The Burlington Free Press]] |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/79809422/the-burlington-free-press/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://archive.today/lgPts |archive-date=June 18, 2021 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref>

====Tenure====

Bouricius supported [[Ralph G. Wright]] for [[List of speakers of the Vermont House of Representatives|Speaker of the House]] in 1991.<ref>{{Cite news |date=January 10, 1991 |title=Senate avoids partisan politics |page=18 |work=[[The Burlington Free Press]] |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/79932633/the-burlington-free-press/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://archive.today/NcR9s |archive-date=June 20, 2021 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref> During his tenure in the state house Bouricius served on the Commerce, Ways and Means, and Agriculture committees, but he stated that he was placed on the Agriculture committee as punishment for trying to get other Progressives elected.<ref>{{Cite news |date=January 16, 1991 |title=Committee assignments |page=5 |work=[[The Burlington Free Press]] |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/79932164/the-burlington-free-press/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://archive.today/PLavV |archive-date=June 20, 2021 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |date=April 12, 1993 |title=Lawmakers frustrated on ag panel |page=3 |work=[[Brattleboro Reformer]] |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/79941323/the-brattleboro-reformer/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://archive.today/GuEzK |archive-date=June 20, 2021 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |date=January 13, 1999 |title=House Committees |page=17 |work=[[Rutland Herald]] |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/79945605/rutland-daily-herald/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://archive.today/zeRln |archive-date=June 20, 2021 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref>

==Later life==

During the [[2002 Vermont gubernatorial election|2002 gubernatorial election]] Bouricius supported Progressive nominee Michael Badamo.<ref>{{Cite news |date=September 8, 2002 |title=Badamo has answers |page=45 |work=[[The Burlington Free Press]] |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/79947499/the-burlington-free-press/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://archive.today/tSIWq |archive-date=June 21, 2021 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref> He became a policy analyst for [[FairVote]].<ref>{{Cite news |date=February 10, 2005 |title=Vote: Parties disagree on same-day registration |page=17 |work=[[The Burlington Free Press]] |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/79947555/the-burlington-free-press/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://archive.today/7OAq8 |archive-date=June 21, 2021 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref> He endorsed [[David Zuckerman (politician)|David Zuckerman]] for lieutenant governor during the 2016 election.<ref>{{Cite news |date=April 6, 2016 |title=Majority of Burlington Councilors Endorse Zuckerman |work=Vermont Digger |url=https://vtdigger.org/2016/04/06/majority-of-burlington-councilors-endorse-zuckerman/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://archive.today/EPY1K |archive-date=June 21, 2021}}</ref> During the 2019 elections he supported Perri Freeman for a seat on the Burlington city council.<ref>{{Cite news |date=February 2, 2019 |title=Perri Freeman shares Central District City Council endorsements |work=Vermont Digger |url=https://vtdigger.org/2019/02/08/perri-freeman-shares-central-district-city-council-endorsements/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://archive.today/u0O3w8 |archive-date=June 21, 2021}}</ref>

==Political positions==

In 1992, Bouricius sponsored legislation to increase the minimum wage in Vermont from $4.25 to $5.50.<ref>{{Cite news |date=February 6, 1992 |title=Workers wrangle over wage |page=1 |work=[[The Burlington Free Press]] |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/79935984/the-burlington-free-press/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://archive.today/gzibK |archive-date=June 20, 2021 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref> Bouricius supported legislation to ban smoking in public areas in 1993.<ref>{{Cite news |date=March 28, 1993 |title=Roll-Call Vote On Smoking Bill |page=10 |work=[[The Burlington Free Press]] |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/79941220/the-burlington-free-press/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://archive.today/NENQY |archive-date=June 20, 2021 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref> He supported the creation of a [[single-payer healthcare]] system.<ref>{{Cite news |date=December 4, 1993 |title=Support For Single-payer Plan |page=16 |work=[[The Burlington Free Press]] |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/79942097/the-burlington-free-press/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://archive.today/5DfaV |archive-date=June 20, 2021 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref> In 2000, the state house voted seventy-six to sixty-nine, with Bouricius in favor, in favor of legislation to allow same-sex couples to have marriage benefits through [[Same-sex unions in the United States|civil unions]].<ref>{{Cite news |date=March 17, 2000 |title=How they voted |page=15 |work=[[The Burlington Free Press]] |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/79944192/the-burlington-free-press/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://archive.today/xF2Bg |archive-date=June 20, 2021 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref>

===Foreign policy===

[[Fergus O'Hare]], a civil rights activist from [[Northern Ireland]], asked for members of the city council to introduce a resolution calling for the British government to give prisoners in the [[HM Prison Maze|H-Block wing of the Maze Prison]] political status. Bouricius introduced the resolution for O'Hare.<ref>{{Cite news |date=July 11, 1981 |title=Irish Activist Plans Speech In Burlington |page=14 |work=[[The Burlington Free Press]] |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/79851244/the-burlington-free-press/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://archive.today/P6xf0 |archive-date=June 19, 2021 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref> In 1983, the city council voted eight to five, with Bouricius in favor, against a resolution protesting the [[United States Department of State]]'s decision to ban Soviet diplomats, journalists, and businessmen from entering Chittenden County.<ref>{{Cite news |date=November 29, 1983 |title=Travel Ban Protest Rejected |page=13 |work=[[The Burlington Free Press]] |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/79865705/the-burlington-free-press/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://archive.today/bbzqC |archive-date=June 19, 2021 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref> Bouricius opposed President [[Ronald Reagan]]'s decision to implement an embargo against [[Nicaragua]].<ref>{{Cite news |date=March 11, 1985 |title=Bouricius Calls for Embargo Opposition |page=14 |work=[[The Burlington Free Press]] |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/79869763/the-burlington-free-press/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://archive.today/BIJvb |archive-date=June 19, 2021 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref> Bouricius criticized President [[George H. W. Bush]]'s plan to send $3 million to anti-[[Sandinista National Liberation Front]] candidates in Nicaragua's 1990 elections.<ref name="treasurer">{{Cite news |date=September 19, 1989 |title=Progressives criticize plan for aid to Nicaraguan opposition |page=14 |work=[[The Burlington Free Press]] |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/79875562/the-burlington-free-press/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://archive.today/tChZW |archive-date=June 19, 2021 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref>

===Government===

Bouricius proposed a change to Burlington's city charter which would have changed the Board of Aldermen to the gender-neutral city council which the city council voted nine to four in favor of the legislation and was approved by a referendum.<ref>{{Cite news |date=January 9, 1990 |title=Board votes on Charter changes |page=4 |work=[[The Burlington Free Press]] |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/79928722/the-burlington-free-press/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://archive.today/FNShV |archive-date=June 20, 2021 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite report|date=March 6, 1990 |title=Statement of Votes Annual City Meeting |publisher=[[Burlington, Vermont]] |page=2}}</ref> He supported the abolition of the [[Vermont Senate]].<ref>{{Cite news |date=June 28, 1993 |title=Vt. should abolish Senate |page=6 |work=[[The Burlington Free Press]] |url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/79938805/the-burlington-free-press/ |url-status=live |archive-url=https://archive.today/62vJ1 |archive-date=June 20, 2021 |via=[[Newspapers.com]]}}</ref>
