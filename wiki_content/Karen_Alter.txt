'''Karen J. Alter''' is an American academic, well known for her interdisciplinary work on international law's influence in international and domestic politics. Alter is a figure in comparative [[international courts]]<ref name="northwestern2">{{cite web|url=http://faculty.wcas.northwestern.edu/~kal438/KarenJAlter2/Comparative_International_courts.html|title=Comparative International Courts|publisher=faculty.wcas.northwestern.edu|accessdate=2014-04-14}}</ref> and the politics of international regime complexity.<ref name="cambridge">{{cite journal|url=http://journals.cambridge.org/action/displayAbstract?fromPage=online&aid=3998488|title=Cambridge Journals Online - Perspectives on Politics - Abstract - The Politics of International Regime Complexity|journal=Perspectives on Politics|volume=7|issue=1|pages=13–24|publisher=journals.cambridge.org|accessdate=2014-04-14|doi=10.1017/S1537592709090033|last1=Meunier|first1=Sophie|last2=Alter|first2=Karen J.|s2cid=56245410}}</ref> Her early work focused on the [[European Court of Justice]], a topic on which she published two books and many articles.<ref name="northwestern3">{{cite book|url=https://global.oup.com/academic/product/the-european-courts-political-power-9780199558353?cc=us&lang=en&|title=The European Court of Justice's Political Power|publisher=faculty.wcas.northwestern.edu|accessdate=2014-04-14|isbn=9780199558353|date=2009-02-15}}</ref> Karen Alter is a [[Guggenheim Fellow]],<ref name="gf">{{cite web|url=http://www.gf.org/fellows/all-fellows/karen-j-alter/|title=Karen J. Alter - John Simon Guggenheim Memorial Foundation|publisher=gf.org|accessdate=2014-04-14}}</ref> and the winner of a [[Berlin Prize]] from the [[American Academy in Berlin]].<ref name="americanacademy">{{cite web|url=http://www.americanacademy.de/home/person/karen-alter|title=Karen Alter &#124; American Academy in Berlin|publisher=americanacademy.de|accessdate=2014-04-14}}</ref> Alter has a courtesy appointment at [[Northwestern Law School]].<ref name="northwestern6">{{cite web|url=http://www.law.northwestern.edu/errorPages/404-IIS.html?404;https://www.law.northwestern.edu:443/faculty/profiles/KarenAlter/|title=Karen Alter, Research & Faculty: Northwestern University Law School|publisher=law.northwestern.edu|accessdate=2014-04-14}}</ref> Fluent in [[French language|French]], [[Italian language|Italian]] and [[German language|German]], Alter has conducted research throughout [[Europe]], [[Africa]] and [[Latin America]].

Alter is well known for integrating the study of [[international relations]] and law.<ref name="PDF-1152880564">{{cite journal|url=http://faculty.wcas.northwestern.edu/~kal438/KarenJAlter2/ECJsPolitical_Power_files/law&polsci.pdf|title=Law, Political Science and Legal Studies|journal=European Union Politics|volume=3|issue=1|pages=113–136|first1=Karen J.|last1=Alter|first2=Renaud|last2=Dehousse|first3=Georg|last3=Vanburg|date=1 February 2002|accessdate=14 April 2014|doi=10.1177/1465116502003001006|s2cid=38992467}}</ref><ref name="Jstor25659991">{{Cite journal|jstor=25659991|title= Proceedings of the Annual Meeting (American Society of International Law)| volume= 99|date= March 30 – April 2, 2005 | pages=138–141|last1= Alter|first1= Karen J.|publisher= American Society of International Law}}</ref> Although she never pursued a JD, Alter spent a year as an Emile Noel Fellow at [[Harvard Law School]], and she has been a [[visiting scholar]] at [[Northwestern Law School]] and the [[American Bar Foundation]].<ref name="northwestern5">{{cite web|url=http://www.scholars.northwestern.edu/expertAddInfo.asp?n=Karen+Alter&u_id=44|title=Curriculum Vitae of Karen Alter - Northwestern Scholars - SciVal Experts 4.6|publisher=scholars.northwestern.edu|accessdate=2014-04-14}}</ref> Alter is a permanent visiting professor at the [[University of Copenhagen]] Faculty of Law, where she participates in the iCourts Center of Excellence,<ref name="jura">{{cite web|url=http://jura.ku.dk/icourts/staff/|title=Staff at iCourts &ndash; University of Copenhagen|publisher=jura.ku.dk|accessdate=2014-04-14|date=2006-01-10}}</ref> and she is a frequent co-author with [[Laurence Helfer]]. Alter is also a member of the [[Council on Foreign Relations]],<ref name="karenjalter">{{cite web|url=http://www.karenjalter.org|title=Research, Writing and Syllabi of Professor Karen J. Alter|publisher=karenjalter.org|accessdate=2014-04-14}}</ref> and serves on the editorial board of the [https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/american-journal-of-international-law American Journal of International Law], [https://academic.oup.com/isr/issue International Studies Review] and the [[Journal of International Dispute Settlement]].<ref name="karenjalter"/>

==Early life==
Karen Alter grew up in [[Los Angeles]], where she attended [[Pacific Palisades High School]] (Class of 1983).<ref name="classmates">{{cite web|url=http://www.classmates.com/people/Karen-Alter-Hanson/8744265097|title=Karen Alter Hanson &#124; Class of 1983 &#124; Palisades High School|publisher=classmates.com|accessdate=2014-04-14}}</ref> She attended [[Cornell University]], where she graduated [[magna cum laude]], with distinction in all subjects, and worked with [[Peter J. Katzenstein]].<ref name="northwestern6"/> Alter received her doctorate in [[political science]] at [[MIT]] (1996), where she worked with [[Suzanne Berger]] and [[Anne-Marie Slaughter]].

==Career==
Karen Alter began her career at [[Smith College]] (1996–2000), after which she moved to [[Northwestern University]]. She teaches courses at the graduate and undergraduate level, and on occasion at [[Northwestern Law School]].<ref name="northwestern7">{{cite web|url=http://faculty.wcas.northwestern.edu/~kal438/KarenJAlter2/Teaching.html|title=Department of Political Science Faculty of Law|publisher=faculty.wcas.northwestern.edu|accessdate=2014-04-14}}</ref> Alter has been a visiting faculty member at [[University of Copenhagen]] Faculty of Law, [[American Academy of Arts and Sciences|American Academy]], [[Northwestern Law School]], [[Institute d'Etudes Politiques]] (Sciences Po), [[American Bar Foundation]], [[Universität Bremen]], Germany, [[Seikei University]], [[Harvard Law School]], and the European Union Center, Harvard University.<ref name="northwestern5"/>

Alter is also a frequent commentator on international relations. Her commentary has appeared in the Boston Globe,<ref name="boston">{{cite web|url=http://www.boston.com/news/packages/iraq/globe_stories/091602_alter.htm|title=Boston.com / News / Rebuilding Iraq|publisher=boston.com|accessdate=2014-04-14}}</ref> CNN,<ref name="cnn">{{cite web|url=http://www.cnn.com/2014/03/05/opinion/alter-ukraine-international-law/|title=Opinion: Has Putin broken international law? - CNN.com|publisher=cnn.com|accessdate=2014-04-14}}</ref> ''[[U.S. News & World Report]]'',<ref name="usnews">{{cite web|url=https://www.usnews.com/topics/author/karen-alter|title=Karen Alter - US News|publisher=usnews.com|accessdate=2014-04-14}}</ref> the Chicago Tribune,<ref name="chicagotribune">{{cite web|url=http://articles.chicagotribune.com/2003-06-29/news/0306290008_1_saddam-hussein-defense-intelligence-agency-president-bush|title=Iraq: Who lied? - Chicago Tribune|publisher=articles.chicagotribune.com|accessdate=2014-04-14}}</ref> and the Huffington Post.<ref name="huffingtonpost">{{cite news|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/karen-j-alter/|title=Karen J.
