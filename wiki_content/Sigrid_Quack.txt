'''Sigrid Quack''' (born 17 July 1958) is a German [[social scientist]] working in the field of [[comparative sociology]]. She is a professor of sociology at the [[University of Duisburg-Essen]] in Germany, where she is the Director of the Centre for Global Cooperation Research,<ref>{{cite web |title= Centre for Global Cooperation Research |url= https://www.gcr21.org/en/the-centre/team/directors/prof-dr-sigrid-quack}}</ref> and was a senior fellow at the [[Watson Institute for International Studies]], [[Brown University]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Sigrid Quack, Watson Institute|url=http://watson.brown.edu/people/fellows/quack|accessdate=7 November 2014|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141107195028/http://watson.brown.edu/people/fellows/quack|archivedate=7 November 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Universität Duisburg-Essen, Institut für Soziologie|url=http://www.uni-due.de/soziologie/quack.php|accessdate=1 October 2013}}</ref>

==Biography==
Quack studied [[sociology]] in Paris and Berlin. Her doctorate was awarded by the [[Free University of Berlin]] in 1992 on the topic of dynamics of part-time work,<ref>{{cite web|title=Dynamik der Teilzeitarbeit : Implikationen für die soziale Sicherung von Frauen|url=http://www.gesis.org/sowiport/search/id/iz-solis-90170796|accessdate=15 July 2013|publisher=GESIS - Datenbank SOLIS}}</ref> and she subsequently worked at the [[Wissenschaftszentrum Berlin für Sozialforschung]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Pfadkolleg Research Center|url=http://www.wiwiss.fu-berlin.de/en/forschung/pfadkolleg/_NEU_Mitglieder/Tr__ger_der_3_Kohorte/Quack/index.html|accessdate=15 July 2013|publisher=Freie Universität Berlin}}</ref> In 2007, she received her [[habilitation]] for scholarship on cross-border institutional development, and became a professor at the [[University of Cologne]] based at the [[Max Planck Institute for the Study of Societies]] from 2007 until 2013.<ref>{{cite web|title=Vita apl. Prof. Dr. Sigrid Quack|url=http://www.mpifg.de/people/sq/vita_en.asp|accessdate=15 July 2013|publisher=Max-Planck-Institut für Gesellschaftsforschung}}</ref>

==Research==
[[File:Governance across borders.pdf|thumb|{{ill|Leonhard Dobusch|de}}, [[Philip Mader]] and Sigrid Quack]]

Quack is the head of an international research group on transnational institution-building.<ref>{{cite web|title=Research Group "Institution Building across Borders"|url=http://www.mpifg.de/projects/govxborders/index_en.asp|accessdate=15 July 2013|publisher=Max-Planck-Institut für Gesellschaftsforschung}}</ref> Her work has examined the phenomenon of [[transnationality]] and [[governance]], with a focus on how [[global governance]] institutions increasingly interact with politics and practices on the ground.<ref>{{cite book|title=Transnational Communities: Shaping Global Economic Governance|last1=Djelic|first1=Marie-Laure|first2=Sigrid|last2=Quack|publisher=Cambridge University Press|year=2012|url=http://www.cambridge.org/us/academic/subjects/management/organisation-studies/transnational-communities-shaping-global-economic-governance}}</ref> In particular, she has studied governance across borders in the fields of [[copyright]] and [[open access]], [[financial reporting]], the regulation of [[multinational companies]], [[environmentalism]], and [[labor rights]]. She is the author or editor of numerous books and articles on [[globalization]], [[institutions]] and [[regulation]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Google Scholar: Sigrid Quack|url=https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=9a9GCdAAAAAJ&hl=en|accessdate=15 July 2013}}</ref>

Quack is the President-Elect and incoming President of the [[Economic sociology#Academic associations|Society for the Advancement of Socio-Economics]], a key international [[scholarly society]] in the field of [[economic sociology]].<ref>{{cite web|date=October 29, 2019|title=SASE President-Elect: Sigrid Quack|url=https://sase.org/uncategorized/sase-president-elect-sigrid-quack/|accessdate=29 October 2019|publisher=SASE}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Officers, Honorary Fellows, Executive Council, and Committees of SASE|url=https://sase.org/about/leadership/|accessdate=29 October 2019|publisher=SASE}}</ref>
