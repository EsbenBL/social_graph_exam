'''Maurice Harry Peston, Baron Peston''' (19 March 1931 – 23 April 2016<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/politics/2016/apr/26/lord-peston-maurice-peston-obituary|title=Lord Peston obituary|first1=Roy|last1=Hattersley|first2=William|last2=Keegan|work=The Guardian|date=26 April 2016}}</ref>) was a British [[economist]] and [[Labour Party (UK)|Labour]] life peer.<ref name="Peerage">{{cite book|title=Burke's Peerage, Baronetage & Knightage, 107th edition, 3 volumes|url=http://www.thepeerage.com/p19170.htm#i191695|publisher=Burke's Peerage (Genealogical Books) Ltd|date=2003|accessdate=5 November 2014}}</ref> His research interests included macroeconomic policy and the economics of education.<ref name="QMUL">{{cite web|url=http://www.econ.qmul.ac.uk/staff/peston.htm|title=Department of Economics|date=22 July 2008|accessdate=9 December 2008|publisher=Queen Mary College, London|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20080529210342/http://www.econ.qmul.ac.uk/staff/peston.htm|archivedate=29 May 2008}}</ref>

==Personal==
Peston was born in 1931 in [[London]], the son of Abraham Peston and Yetta R. (née Malt) Peston. He was educated at [[Belle Vue Boys' School]], [[Bradford]], [[West Yorkshire]],<ref name="Peerage"/> and [[Hackney Downs School]]. He graduated from the [[London School of Economics]] and undertook postgraduate study at [[Princeton University]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/obituaries/2016/04/24/lord-peston--obituary/|title=Lord Peston – obituary|date=24 April 2016|work=The Telegraph}}</ref>
He married Helen Conroy in London in 1958.<ref name=MP&HCperFreeBMD>{{Cite web|url=http://www.freebmd.org.uk/cgi/information.pl?cite=guldqfzIPfzvmqJ%2FWly7ZA&scan=1|title=Transcription of index of Births, Marriages and deaths in England and Wales|accessdate=24 April 2016|work=FreeBMD|publisher=ONS}}</ref>

The couple believed passionately in state education, and sent all of their three children to the local comprehensive, [[Highgate Wood School]], [[Crouch End]], [[north London]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/portal/main.jhtml?xml=/portal/2008/01/24/ftpeston124.xml|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080325060534/http://www.telegraph.co.uk/portal/main.jhtml?xml=/portal/2008/01/24/ftpeston124.xml|url-status=dead|archive-date=25 March 2008|title=Robert Peston: 'I'm not going to become smooth and phoney'|publisher=The Telegraph|date=24 January 2008|accessdate=8 October 2008|location=London, UK|first=Elizabeth|last=Grice}}</ref> One of their children is the journalist [[Robert Peston]], currently [[ITV (TV network)|ITV]]'s political editor.

==Career==

===Academia===
Peston founded the economics department at [[Queen Mary, University of London|Queen Mary College, London]], and advised various government departments and [[Labour Party (UK)|Labour]] Secretaries of State from the 1960s through to the 1990s.<ref name="Golden">{{cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/business/2004/jan/11/politics.economicpolicy1|title=Treasure our golden oldies|work=The Guardian|date=11 January 2004|accessdate=8 October 2008|location=London, UK|first=Maurice|last=Peston}}</ref> He remained an Emeritus Professor of Economics at the College until his death in 2016.<ref name="QMUL"/>

===House of Lords===
Peston was created a [[life peer]] as '''Baron Peston''', of [[Mile End]], in [[Greater London]], on 24 March 1987.<ref>{{London Gazette|issue=50879|page=4493|date=3 April 1987}}</ref> He immediately became Opposition Spokesperson for Energy (until 1997) and Education & Science (until 1997).<ref name="HOL">{{cite web|url=http://biographies.parliament.uk/parliament/default.asp?id=26960|title=Member Profile – Lord Peston|publisher= UK Parliament|accessdate=9 October 2008}}</ref> He served as Opposition Spokesperson on the Treasury (1990–92) and Trade & Industry (1992–97).<ref name="HOL"/> He chaired the [[House of Lords]] Offices Refreshments Sub-committee from 1993 to 1997.<ref name="HOL"/>

When [[1997 United Kingdom general election|Labour took over government]], he chaired the influential House of Lords [[Economic Affairs Committee (House of Lords)|Committee on Economic Affairs]] from 1998 until 2005.<ref name="HOL"/> Since then he worked on the Lords [[Constitution Committee (House of Lords)|Constitution Committee]], and on the committee reviewing the [[BBC Charter]].<ref name="HOL"/>

===Other interests===
He was chairman of the [[Football_pools#Pools_Panel|Pools Panel]] during the 1990s, adjudicating on the expected results of football matches in case any were postponed.<ref>{{cite news|work=[[The Independent]]|title=Almanack: Pools panel draw a veil|date=27 March 1994|accessdate=14 March 2020|last=Baker|first=Andrew|url=https://www.independent.co.uk/sport/almanack-pools-panel-draw-a-veil-1431838.html}}</ref> 

Lord Peston was a patron of the [[British Humanist Association]],<ref name="BHAobit">{{cite web|url=https://humanism.org.uk/2016/04/25/bha-mourns-its-patron-lord-peston/|title=BHA mourns its patron, Lord Peston
|accessdate=16 January 2017|date=25 April 2016|work=British Humanist Association}}</ref> as well as an Honorary Associate of the [[National Secular Society]]. In the House, he spoke candidly about his existential views, describing himself as someone "who regards all religious belief as failing to meet even the most elementary epistemological and deontological criteria".<ref name="BHAobit"/>
