'''Janet Liebman Jacobs''' (born 1948) is an American sociologist specializing in gender and religion. Jacobs' research focuses on women, religion, ethnicity, genocide and the social psychology of gender. She has authored seven books, including ''Hidden Heritage: The Legacy of the Crypto-Jews'',<ref name=hidden>{{cite book|last1=Jacobs|first1=Janet|title=Hidden Heritage: The Legacy of the Crypto-Jews|date=2002|publisher=University of California Press|url=http://www.ucpress.edu/book.php?isbn=9780520235175}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last1=Ward|first1=Seth|title=Hidden Heritage, the Legacy of the Crypto-Jews (review)|url=http://muse.jhu.edu/article/188365|website=Muse|publisher=muse.jhu.edu|accessdate=14 October 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal|last1=Neulander|first1=Judith S|title=Reviewed Work: Hidden Heritage: The Legacy of the Crypto-Jews by Janet Liebman Jacobs|jstor=1500440|publisher=Western Folklore}}</ref><ref name="Science 2.0">{{cite web|last1=Entine|first1=Jon|title=The 'Jew' In All Of Us|url=http://www.science20.com/jon_entine_contrarian/jew_all_us-90698|website=Science 2.0|publisher=Science 2.0|accessdate=2 April 2015}}</ref> for which she won the Distinguished Book Award from the Society for the Scientific Study of Religion,<ref name="University of Colorado2">{{cite web|title=Faculty Awards |url=https://sociology.colorado.edu/people/faculty-awards |website=Department of Sociology |publisher=University of Colorado |accessdate=2 April 2015 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150404160625/https://sociology.colorado.edu/people/faculty-awards |archivedate= 4 April 2015 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.sssrweb.org/PastWinners.cfm#Award1 |title=Past Award Winners |website=Society for the Scientific Study of Religion |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/5nkN0WHJc?url=http://www.sssrweb.org/PastWinners.cfm |archivedate=2010-02-23 }}</ref> ''Memorializing the Holocaust: Gender, Genocide and Collective Memory'',<ref name=memorializing>{{cite book|last1=Jacobs|first1=Janet|title=Memorializing the Holocaust: Gender, Genocide and Collective Memory|date=2010|publisher=I.B. Tauris|url=http://www.ibtauris.com/Books/Humanities/History/History%20specific%20events%20%20topics/Genocide%20%20ethnic%20cleansing/The%20Holocaust/Memorializing%20the%20Holocaust%20Gender%20Genocide%20and%20Collective%20Memory.aspx?menuitem=%7B6D00AE6F-976D-4418-ACF0-B75CB610346A%7D}}</ref> and The Holocaust Across Generations: Trauma and its Inheritance Among Descendants of Survivors, for which she won the 2017 Outstanding Book Award from the [[American Sociological Association]].<ref name="NYU 2017">{{cite web|title=The Holocaust Across Generations Trauma and its Inheritance Among Descendants of Survivors|url=https://nyupress.org/books/9781479839292/|website=NYU Press|publisher=NYU Press|accessdate=12 October 2017}}</ref>

Jacobs is currently Professor of Sociology<ref name=uc>{{cite web|url=https://sociology.colorado.edu/people/jacobs-janet |title=Janet Jacobs |website=Department of Sociology |publisher=University of Colorado Boulder |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150216083940/http://sociology.colorado.edu/people/jacobs-janet |archivedate=2015-02-16 }}</ref> and of Women and Gender Studies<ref name=wgst>{{cite web|url=http://wgst.colorado.edu/faculty/jacobs|title=Janet Jacobs|website=Women and Gender Studies|publisher=University of Colorado Boulder|access-date=2015-03-09|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150216093058/http://wgst.colorado.edu/faculty/jacobs|archive-date=2015-02-16|url-status=dead}}</ref> at the [[University of Colorado]], and she directs the University of Colorado Honors Program. She received her PhD from the University of Colorado in 1985.<ref name="auto4">{{cite web|url=http://www.colorado.edu/honors/janetjacobs|title=Janet Jacobs|website=Honors Program|publisher=University of Colorado Boulder}}</ref>

==Career==
Jacobs earned a [[Bachelor of Science|BS]] in journalism in 1970, a [[Master of Public Administration]] degree in 1977, and a PhD in sociology at the University of Colorado in 1985. Jacobs is a [[tenure]]d full professor in the Department of Sociology and professor of Women's Studies at the University of Colorado, [[Boulder, Colorado|Boulder]].<ref name=uc/> She served as the director of the Women's Studies Program at this institution in 1987-1988 and again during 1997-2000;<ref name=wgst/> she also served as director of the Farrand Academic Program from 2005-2011. Since 2014 Jacobs has directed the University of Colorado College of Arts and Sciences Honors Program. Jacobs chaired the committee and has supervised research for graduates of the University of Colorado, Boulder and has received Teaching Excellence Awards from the University of Colorado in 1985, 1992 and 2001.<ref>{{cite web|title=Teaching Excellence Awards|url=https://www.colorado.edu/bfa/excellence-awards/bfa-excellence-awards-archives/teaching-excellence-awards|website=Boulder Faculty Assembly|publisher=University of Colorado Boulder|accessdate=13 October 2017}}</ref>

==Awards and professional activities==
Janet Jacobs was awarded the 2017 Outstanding Book Award from the American Sociological Association Section on Peace, War and Social Conflict for her book ''The Holocaust Across Generations:  Trauma and its Inheritance Among Descendants of Survivors''.<ref name="NYU 2017" /><ref>{{cite web|title=Awards|url=https://nyupress.org/news/awards/|website=NYU Press|publisher=NYU Press|accessdate=9 October 2017}}</ref> In 2016 Janet Jacobs presented a lecture to the International Conference on Women and War in Yerevan, Armenia entitled "Gender and the Intergenerational Transmission of Trauma: Family Narratives and the Construction of Identity Among Descendants of the Holocaust".<ref>{{cite web|title=AFTER GENOCIDE: GENDERED TRAUMA, TRANSMISSION AND REINVENTION, YEREVAN SEPTEMBER 17-18-19|url=http://womeninwar.org/wordpress/after-genocide-gendered-trauma-transmission-and-reinvention-yerevan-may-21-22-23-2016/|website=Women in War|publisher=Women in War|accessdate=11 October 2017}}</ref> Jacobs also presented a lecture entitled "Gender and Holocaust Memorialization" at the Conference in Jewish and Gender Studies in honor of Debra Kaufman at [[Northeastern University]] in 2012. In 2011 Jacobs presented the Stanley L. Saxton Address "Gender and Genocide:  Collective Memory and Holocaust Memorials" at the [[University of Dayton]].

Jacobs in 2010 presented the Keynote Paul Hanley Furfey Lecture for the [[Association for the Sociology of Religion]] entitled "Sacred Space and Collective Memory: Memorializing Genocide at Sites of Terror".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sociologyofreligion.com/wp-content/uploads/2013/03/Presidents-Themes-and-Addresses.pdf|title=List of ASR/ACSS Presidents|website=Sociology of Religion}}</ref> Jacobs was awarded the [[Hazel Barnes]] Prize at the University of Colorado in 2005.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://chancellor.colorado.edu/cu-boulder-sociologist-janet-jacobs-wins-universitys-top-teaching-and-research-award |title=CU-Boulder Sociologist Janet Jacobs Wins University's Top Teaching And Research Award |website=University of Colorado |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20141012155820/https://chancellor.colorado.edu/cu-boulder-sociologist-janet-jacobs-wins-universitys-top-teaching-and-research-award |archivedate=2014-10-12 }}</ref> She was also awarded the Distinguished Book Award in 2003 from the Society for the Scientific Study of Religion for her Book ''Hidden Heritage: The Legacy of the Crypto-Jews.''<ref>{{cite web |title=Annual Awards & Grants: Past Winners |url=http://www.sssrweb.org/PastWinners.cfm |website=Distinguished Book Awards |publisher=Society for the Scientific Study of Religion |accessdate=2 April 2015 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/5nkN0WHJc?url=http://www.sssrweb.org/PastWinners.cfm |archivedate=23 February 2010 }}</ref> In 2000 Jacobs was awarded the Gender Scholar Award from the Association for the Sociology of Religion. She gave the 2005 [[commencement speech|Commencement Address]] at the University of Colorado.<ref name=Commencement>{{cite web|title=Janet Jacobs Commencement speech|url=http://www.colorado.edu/commencement/2005/12/16/janet-jacobs-winter-2005|website=Commencement UNIVERSITY OF COLORADO BOULDER|publisher=UNIVERSITY OF COLORADO BOULDER|accessdate=5 October 2015|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304111954/http://www.colorado.edu/commencement/2005/12/16/janet-jacobs-winter-2005|archivedate=4 March 2016}}</ref> In 2012, Jacobs received the Distinguished Career Award from the American Sociological Association's Children and Youth section.<ref name="University of Colorado2" />

===Affiliations===
Jacobs has served on the Awards Committee (2013–15); Nominating Committee (2004–10 and 1994–96); and on the Executive Council (1997–98) for the [[Society for the Scientific Study of Religion]] and was the Annual Meeting Program Chair in 1990.<ref>{{cite book|title=Review of Religious Research|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=vd4nAAAAYAAJ|year=1990|publisher=Religious Research Association.}}</ref> Jacobs served as the editor for [[NYU Press]] Qualitative Study of Religion series. Jacobs has also served as a Referee for the ''[[American Sociological Review]]''; ''Gender and Society''; ''[[Journal of Contemporary Ethnography]]''; ''Journal for Research on Adolescence''; ''Signs: Journal of Women and Society''; ''Social Problems''; ''Sociology of Religion'' and was both Referee and on the Editorial Board (2003–12) for the ''[[Journal for the Scientific Study of Religion]]''. Jacobs has served as a Manuscript Consultant for the [[Columbia University Press]]; [[Oxford University Press]]; [[Duke University Press]]; [[University of Tennessee Press]], [[University of California Press]]; and New York University Press. Professional Affiliations include the [[American Academy of Religion]]; American Sociological Association; [[Association for the Sociology of Religion]]; Society for the Scientific Study of Religion; and the [[International Visual Sociology Association]].<ref name="NYU Press">{{cite web|title=Qualitative Studies in Religion|url=http://nyupress.org/series/qualitative-studies-in-religion/|website=NYU Press|publisher=NYU Press|accessdate=2 April 2015}}</ref>
