'''Patricia Ann Weitsman''' (October 23, 1964 – March 30, 2014) was an American [[political scientist]] and [[international relations]] professor at [[Ohio University]] where she was also Director of War and Peace Studies.<ref name="an-obit"/>  She specialized in [[security studies]] and [[international relations theory]], especially on topics related to [[military alliance]]s.<ref name="ohio-notice"/>

==Life and career==
Weitsman attended [[Indiana University]] as an undergraduate and spent one year during that time at [[Hebrew University of Jerusalem]].<ref name="ohio-dir">{{cite web | url=http://www.internationalstudies.ohio.edu/people/weitsman.html | title=Director, War and Peace Studies: Patricia Weitsman | publisher=Ohio University | accessdate=February 14, 2015}}</ref>  She did her graduate studies at [[Columbia University]], resulting in M.A., M. Phil., and Ph.D. degrees, the last coming in 1994.<ref name="ohio-notice"/><ref name="ohio-dir"/>  She was a pre- and post-doctoral fellow at the [[Graduate Institute of International and Development Studies|Graduate Institute for International Studies]] in Geneva.<ref name="ohio-dir"/>

She began at [[Ohio University]] in 1995.  There she developed a reputation for teaching, winning the College of Arts & Sciences Outstanding Teacher Award in 1997, the campus-wide University Professor honor in 1997–98 and again in 2000–01 and the Outstanding Graduate Faculty Award in 2008.<ref name="ohio-awards">{{cite web | url=http://www.ohio.edu/pols/faculty-teaching-awards.cfm | title=Recent Faculty Awards | publisher=Ohio University | accessdate=February 14, 2015}}</ref><ref name="isa-parker"/>  In 2009 she was the Ohio University Graduate Commencement Speaker.<ref name="ohio-notice">{{cite news | url=http://www.ohio-forum.com/2014/04/university-mourns-dr-patricia-weitsman-political-science-professor/ | title=University Mourns Dr. Patricia Weitsman, Political Science Professor | publisher=Ohio University | date=April 3, 2014}}</ref><ref name="ohio-address"/>  She was also nominated for the Statewide Excellence in Education Award by ''Ohio Magazine''.<ref name="ohio-awards"/>     She noted that working with students was vitally important to her as it lightened her feelings given the "very dark" subject matter of her research.<ref name="ohio-address"/>   In 2010, Weitsman created a new interdisciplinary War and Peace Studies major, as well as a certificate program under that name, within the university's Center for International Studies.<ref name="ohio-notice"/><ref name="isa-parker"/>  She became Director of War and Peace Studies in 2012.<ref name="ohio-notice"/>

Weitsman's 2004 book ''Dangerous Alliances: Proponents of Peace, Weapons of War'' analyzed [[military alliance]]s as not just a means by a state to protect against threats from other countries, but also to improves ties with a particular nation (she termed it "hedging") or to manage conflict with a particular nation (she termed it "tethering").<ref name="rev-grieco">{{cite journal | title=''Dangerous Alliances: Proponents of Peace, Weapons of War'' by Patricia A. Weitsman | first=Joseph M. | last=Grieco | journal=Political Science Quarterly | volume= 119 | number= 3 | date=Fall 2004 | pages= 540–541 | doi=10.2307/20202407 | jstor=20202407}}</ref>  It used case studies from European history during the 1873–1918 period and sought to synthesize previous scholarly research on alliance formation and cohesion into a more robust theoretical framework that incorporated [[Realism (international relations)|realist]], [[Rationalism (international relations)|rationalist]], and [[Institutionalism (international relations)|institutionalist]] thinking on the subject.<ref name="rev-pressman">{{cite journal | title=''Dangerous Alliances: Proponents of Peace, Weapons of War'' by Patricia A. Weitsman | first=Jeremy | last=Pressman | journal= The Journal of Interdisciplinary History | volume= 36 | number= 1 | date=Summer 2005 | pages= 80–81 | doi=10.1162/0022195054026194 | jstor=3656874| s2cid=140468117 }}</ref>  The book was heavily reviewed in academic journals, with scholars generally praising the work as a valuable contribution to the study of alliances while sometimes taking issue with particular points or judging that the work did not fulfill all of its ambitions.<ref name="rev-grieco"/><ref name="rev-pressman"/><ref>{{cite journal | title=''Dangerous Alliances: Proponents of Peace, Weapons of War'' by Patricia A. Weitsman | first=Ronald R. | last=Krebs | journal=International Studies Review | volume=6 | issue= 3 | date=September 2004 | pages= 496–498 | doi=10.1111/j.1521-9488.2004.00440.x | jstor=3699714}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal | title=Patricia A. Weitsman: ''Dangerous Alliances: Proponents of Peace, Weapons of War'' | first=John S. | last=Duffield | journal=The Review of Politics | volume= 67 | number= 1 | date=Winter 2005 | pages= 177–179 | doi=10.1017/S0034670500043461 | jstor=25046398| s2cid=164984988 }}</ref><ref>{{cite journal | title=''Dangerous Alliances: Proponents of Peace, Weapons of War'' by Patricia A. Weitsman | first=Jeffrey M. | last=Ritter | journal=Perspectives on Politics | volume=3 | number= 1 | date=March 2005 | pages= 210–211 | jstor=3688170 | doi = 10.1017/s1537592705720144 | s2cid=145277730 }}</ref><ref>{{cite journal | jstor=10.1111/j.1468-2508.2007.00513.x | title=''Dangerous Alliances: Proponents of Peace, Weapons of War'' | first=James J. | last=Wirtz | journal=The Journal of Politics | volume=69 | number=1 | date=February 2007 | pages=257–259 | doi=10.1111/j.1468-2508.2007.00513.x| s2cid=152907029 }}</ref><ref name="rev-onuf">{{cite journal | title= Patricia A. Weitsman. ''Dangerous Alliances: Proponents of Peace, Weapons of War'' | first=Nicholas | last=Onuf | journal=The International History Review | volume=27 | number= 3 | date=September 2005 | pages= 694–696 | jstor=40109668}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal | title=Weitsman, Patricia A., 2004. ''Dangerous Alliances: Proponents of Peace, Weapons of War'' | first=Erik | last=Gartzke | journal=Journal of Peace Research | volume=42 | number= 5 | date=September 2005 | page= 650 | jstor=30042405 | doi = 10.1177/0022343305056239 | s2cid=220990767 }}</ref>  For one, [[Joseph Grieco|Joseph M. Grieco]] wrote that it was an "original and thoughtful analysis ... and contributes greatly to our understanding of alliances and their impact on war and peace."<ref name="rev-grieco"/>  [[Nicholas Onuf]] said that her emphasis on "tethering" was a valuable new concept validated by her historical survey.<ref name="rev-onuf"/>  ''Dangerous Alliances'' was a finalist for several book prizes.<ref name="ohio-address">{{cite news | url=http://www.ohio.edu/outlook/08-09/June/673.cfm | title=Noted professor, author to give keynote | publisher=Ohio University | date=June 8, 2009}}</ref>

Her second book, ''Waging War: Alliances, Coalitions, and Institutions of Interstate Violence'', was published in 2014.  It sought to establish a theoretical model for how coalition warfare in Iraq and elsewhere had affected the U.S. projection of power.<ref>{{cite journal | url=https://www.ausa.org/publications/armymagazine/archive/2014/Documents/09September14/Reviews_Sept2014.pdf | title=The 'Science' of Joint Warfighting | first=Georgory, Col. (ret.) | last=Fontenot | journal=ARMY Magazine <!--publisher=Association of the United States Army--> | volume=64 | number=9 | date=September 2014 | url-status=dead | archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150214200345/https://www.ausa.org/publications/armymagazine/archive/2014/Documents/09September14/Reviews_Sept2014.pdf | archivedate=2015-02-14 }}</ref>  She also published a number of articles in scholarly journals on various topics, including ones on sexual violence and identity in war which covered events such as [[rape during the Bosnian War]] and [[rape during the Rwandan Genocide]].<ref name="isa-parker"/><ref>{{cite journal|last1=Weitsman|first1=Patricia A.|title=The Politics of Identity and Sexual Violence: A Review of Bosnia and Rwanda|journal=Human Rights Quarterly|date=August 2008|volume=30|issue=3|pages=561–578 |doi=10.1353/hrq.0.0024|jstor=20072859|s2cid=143000182}}</ref>

Weitsman was very active in the 1,400-member International Security Studies Section (ISSS) of the [[International Studies Association]] (ISA); the section is the world's largest association of security studies scholars.<ref name="ohio-notice"/>   She was elected to its governing board in 2006 and was selected as its vice chair in 2009.<ref name="ohio-notice"/>  She then served as its chair from 2011 to 2013.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.isanet.org/ISA/Sections/ISSS/Leadership | title=ISSS Governance and Leadership | publisher=International Studies Association | accessdate=February 13, 2015}}</ref>  During her time as chair, the section became the largest within the ISA.<ref name="isa-parker">{{cite news | url=http://www.isanet.org/News/ID/1344/In-Memoriam--Patricia-Ann-Weitsman | title=News From ISA – In Memoriam – Patricia Ann Weitsman | first=Jay | last=Parker | publisher=International Studies Association | date=April 18, 2014}}</ref>

Weitsman was married to [[David L. Hoffmann]], professor of Russian History at [[Ohio State University]], with two children. They lived in [[Lancaster, Ohio]].<ref name="cd-bar">{{cite news | url=http://www.dispatch.com/content/stories/faith_and_values/2011/02/11/hell-be-a-charitable-man.html | title=He'll be a charitable man | first=Meredith | last=Heagney | newspaper=The Columbus Dispatch | date=February 11, 2011 }}</ref>  In 2010 Weitsman was diagnosed with life-threatening [[myelodysplastic syndrome]].<ref name="ohio-notice"/><ref name="cd-bar"/>  She received a [[bone marrow transplant]] for it in 2011, went into remission and was able to resume work.<ref>{{cite news | url=https://www.ohio.edu/compass/stories/11-12/8/Weitsman-bone-marrow.cfm | title=Patricia Weitsman to discuss her life saving bone marrow transplant on Wednesday | publisher=Ohio University | date=August 23, 2012}}</ref>  But the disease returned as [[leukemia]] in 2013 and she succumbed to it in 2014 at age 49.<ref name="an-obit">{{cite news | url=http://www.athensnews.com/ohio/article-42079-patricia-ann-weitsman.html | title=Obituaries: Patricia Ann Weitsman | newspaper=The Athens News | date=April 1, 2014}}</ref><ref name="ohio-notice"/>

The International Security Studies Section's annual Patricia Weitsman Award for Outstanding ISSS Graduate Paper is named in her honor.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.isanet.org/Programs-Resources/Awards/ISSS-Outstanding-Grad-Paper | title=Patricia Weitsman Award | publisher=International Studies Association | accessdate=February 13, 2015}}</ref>  A panel session honoring her contributions to the field was held at the 2015 ISA Conference in New Orleans.<ref>{{cite web | url=http://web.isanet.org/Web/Conferences/New%20Orleans%202015/New%20Orleans%202015%20-%20Full%20Program.pdf | title=ISA's 56th Annual Convention: ISA 2015 Program | publisher=International Studies Association | accessdate=February 20, 2015 | page=131}}</ref>
