'''David Brady''' is the Bowen H. and Janice Arthur McCoy (chaired) Professor of Political Science and Leadership Values at [[Stanford University]]. While at Stanford, he has received the Dinkelspiel Award for service to undergraduates, the Richard Lyman Prize for service to alumni, the Bob Davies award, the Jaedicke silver cup from the Graduate School of Business, and the first [[Phi Beta Kappa]] Teaching Award given at Stanford. He is also a member of the American Academy of Arts and Sciences.<ref name="gsb.stanford.edu">http://www.gsb.stanford.edu/faculty-research/faculty/david-w-brady</ref>

==Biography==
One of six children, David Brady grew up in Kankakee, IL.  Like his siblings, he was educated in parochial schools, and following high school he went to work in a furniture factory.  After injuring his hand in an industrial accident, he enrolled at Western Illinois University, graduating in 1963.<ref>{{Cite book | url=https://books.google.com/books?id=CcjUgFX-BCYC&pg=PT77 | title=It's My Party: A Republican's Messy Love Affair with the GOP| isbn=9780759520950| last1=Robinson| first1=Peter| date=January 2001}}</ref> Brady would go on to receive a Ph.D. from the [[University of Iowa]] in 1970. Before coming to Stanford, he taught at [[Rice University]], the [[University of Houston]], and [[Kansas State University]].<ref name="gsb.stanford.edu"/> He spent several years as Chairman of the Department of Political Science at both schools.<ref name="files-politicalscience-stanford-edu.s3-us-west-2.amazonaws.com">https://files-politicalscience-stanford-edu.s3-us-west-2.amazonaws.com/s3fs-public/brady_vita.pdf</ref>

At Stanford, he previously held the Morris M. Doyle Centennial Chair in Public Policy <ref name="politicalscience.stanford.edu">{{Cite web |url=https://politicalscience.stanford.edu/faculty/david-w-brady |title=Archived copy |access-date=2014-09-27 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141018070835/http://politicalscience.stanford.edu/faculty/david-w-brady |archive-date=2014-10-18 |url-status=dead }}</ref> and was the Associate Dean for the Stanford Graduate School of Business.<ref name=psci>https://politicalscience.stanford.edu/sites/default/files/cv_brady_november_2013.pdf</ref> He also served as the Deputy Director of the [[Hoover Institution]], where he is currently a Senior Fellow.<ref name="politicalscience.stanford.edu"/> Brady's other institutional appointments have included Vice Provost for Distance Learning and the Chairmanship of the University's Committee on Undergraduate Studies.<ref name="files-politicalscience-stanford-edu.s3-us-west-2.amazonaws.com"/>

==Research==
Brady is a leading on expert on issues of equality and political effectiveness; that is: when political systems and organizations work better or worse, and has written or co-written numerous articles and books on these topics, including: ''Leadership and Growth'' (World Bank Publications, 2010) coedited with Michael Spence, ''Revolving Gridlock: Politics and Policy from Carter to Bush II'' (Westview Press, 2006), and ''Red and Blue Nation? Characteristics and Causes of America’s Polarized Politics'' with Pietro Nivola (Brookings Institution Press, 2007).<ref name="politicalscience.stanford.edu"/> His current research focuses on the U.S. Congress, the party system, and congressional decision-making.<ref name=hoover>http://www.hoover.org/press-releases/david-brady-appointed-hoover-institution-deputy-director</ref> Brady is also a frequent contributor to the popular political website [[RealClearPolitics]].<ref>http://www.realclearpolitics.com/authors/david_brady/</ref>
