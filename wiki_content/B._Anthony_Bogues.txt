{{Short description|Caribbean political theorist, intellectual historian, writer and curator and currently Director of the Center for the Study of Slavery & Justice at Brown University and the Asa Messer Professor of Humanities and Critical Theory}}'''B. Anthony Bogues''' is a Caribbean  political theorist, intellectual historian, writer and curator and currently Director of the Center for the Study of Slavery & Justice at [[Brown University]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://cssj.brown.edu/news/2012-06-22/bogues-direct-slavery-and-justice-center|title=Bogues to direct Slavery and Justice Center|publisher=Center for the Study of Slavery and Justice|date=June 22, 2012}}</ref> and the Asa Messer Professor of Humanities and Critical Theory.  He was an Honorary Research Professor at the [[University of Cape Town]] and is currently a visiting professor and curator at the Visual Identities in Art and Design Research Center, [[University of Johannesburg]].

In 2012, he was also the Distinguished Faculty Fellow, Marta Sutton Weeks Distinguished Visitor at [[Stanford University]]. He has written extensively on African and African Diaspora political theory and intellectual history. As a curator he curates and writes about Haitian Art.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.brown.edu/initiatives/slavery-and-justice/people |title=B.
