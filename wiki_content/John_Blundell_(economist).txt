'''John Blundell''' (9 October 1952 – 22 July 2014) was a British economist who served as Director General and the [[Ralph Harris, Baron Harris of High Cross|Ralph Harris]] Fellow at the [[Institute of Economic Affairs]].<ref>[http://www.iea.org.uk Institute of Economic Affairs] official website.</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.arnettandassociates.com/SpeakerDetails/28719/John-Blundell.html |title=Arnett and Associates: Premier Speakers, Talent, and Leadership International: Speaker detail: John Blundell |access-date=16 April 2011 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120315160420/http://www.arnettandassociates.com/SpeakerDetails/28719/John-Blundell.html |archive-date=15 March 2012 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://focusdailynews.com/northwood-university-hosts-worldrenowned-economist-prolific-author-p3349-1.htm |title=Northwood University hosts world-renowned economist, prolific author |publisher=Focusdailynews.com |date=2011-03-16 |accessdate=2014-07-25 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140808125905/http://focusdailynews.com/northwood-university-hosts-worldrenowned-economist-prolific-author-p3349-1.htm |archivedate=2014-08-08 }}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.iedm.org/30724-jean-a-pouliot-lecture-series|title = Jean A. Pouliot Lecture Series &#124; Montreal Economic Institute}}</ref> Blundell had been involved in the creation and development of numerous free-market think tanks.

==Early life==
Born in [[Congleton]], [[Cheshire]], on 9 October 1952, he was educated at [[The King's School, Macclesfield]], and at the [[London School of Economics]].

==Career==
He headed the Press, Research and Parliamentary Liaison Office at the [[Federation of Small Businesses]] from 1977 to 1982, and was a [[Lambeth London Borough Council]]lor from 1978 to 1982. From 1982 to 1993 he was resident in the United States, where he was President of the [[Institute for Humane Studies]] (1988–1991), President of the [[Atlas Economic Research Foundation]] (1987–1991), President of the Board of the Congressional Schools of Virginia (1988–1992) and President of the Charles G. Koch and Claude R. Lambe Charitable Foundations (1991–1992).<ref name= "about">[https://archive.org/download/the-author.-blundell-john.-waging-the-war-of-ideas.-london-institute-of-economic-affairs-2003.-p.-9./The%20Author.%20Blundell%2C%20John.%20Waging%20the%20War%20of%20Ideas.%20London-Institute%20of%20Economic%20Affairs%2C%202003.%20p.%209..pdf "The Author".] Blundell, John. [https://iea.org.uk/wp-content/uploads/2016/07/Blundell-interactive.pdf ''Waging the War of Ideas''.] London: [[Institute of Economic Affairs]], 2003. [https://archive.org/download/the-author.-blundell-john.-waging-the-war-of-ideas.-london-institute-of-economic-affairs-2003.-p.-9./The%20Author.%20Blundell%2C%20John.%20Waging%20the%20War%20of%20Ideas.%20London-Institute%20of%20Economic%20Affairs%2C%202003.%20p.%209..pdf p. 9.] {{ISBN|0255365470}} / {{ISBN|978-0255365475}}. </ref>

Blundell became Director General of the [[Institute of Economic Affairs]] on 1 January 1993.

In addition he served as Co-founder and Chairman of the Institute for Children in [[Boston, Massachusetts]] (1993–1997); Founder and Director of the [[Institute for Justice]] in [[Washington D.C.]] (1991–1993); International Trustee of [[The Fraser Institute]] in [[Vancouver, British Columbia]] (1988–1993); and Founder Trustee of the [[Buckeye Institute]] in [[Ohio]]. <ref name= "about"/>

Blundell was a Director of Fairbridge and of the [[International Policy Network]], and Chairman of the Institute Development and Relations Committee on the board of [[Atlas Economic Research Foundation]]. He was also a board member of the [[Institute for Humane Studies]] at [[George Mason University]] in [[Fairfax, Virginia]]; of the [[Institute of Economic Studies]] in Paris, France; and, until 2012,{{when|date=October 2012}} of the [[Mont Pelerin Society]].<ref name="about" />

==Death==
Blundell died on 22 July 2014 at the age of 61.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.conservativehome.com/parliament/2014/07/john-blundell-the-man-who-helped-to-form-iealondon-is-dead.html|title=John Blundell, the man who helped to form the IEA, is dead|publisher=Conservativehome.com|accessdate=23 July 2014}}</ref>
