'''Christina Hull Paxson''' (born February 6, 1960), is an American [[economist]] and [[public health]] expert, currently serving as the 19th President of [[Brown University]]. Previously, she was the Hughes Rogers Professor of Economics & Public Affairs at [[Princeton University]] as well as the Dean of [[Princeton School of Public and International Affairs]].<ref name=BrownBio>{{cite web| title = Biography – Office of the President| publisher = Brown University| url = https://brown.edu/about/administration/president/biography| accessdate = October 27, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2012/03/03/education/christina-hull-paxson-to-lead-brown-university.html|title=Christina Hull Paxson Chosen as President of Brown|last=Lewin|first=Tamar|date=2012-03-02|work=The New York Times|access-date=2018-01-17|language=en-US|issn=0362-4331}}</ref>

In March 2012 Paxson was selected as the 19th [[president of Brown University]]. She officially succeeded [[Ruth Simmons]] on July 1, 2012 and was inaugurated on October 27, 2012.<ref>{{cite news| title = The Inauguration of Christina Hull Paxson| publisher = Brown University| url = https://www.brown.edu/campus-life/events/paxson-inauguration/| accessdate = October 27, 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://news.brown.edu/pressreleases/2012/03/paxson|title=Economist Christina Hull Paxson elected 19th president of Brown University|date=March 2, 2012|accessdate=October 27, 2012|publisher=Brown University}}</ref> 

==Early life and education==
After spending her childhood in [[Forest Hills, Pennsylvania|Forest Hills]], a suburb of [[Pittsburgh]], Pennsylvania, Paxson received her B.A. from [[Swarthmore College]] in 1982, where she majored in [[Economics]] and minored in [[English language|English]] and [[Philosophy]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.browndailyherald.com/2012/03/05/people-person-leaves-mark-on-princeton/|title='People person' leaves mark on Princeton|last=Thornton|first=Lucy Feldman,Sahil Luthra,Kat|date=2012-03-05|website=Brown Daily Herald|language=en-US|access-date=2019-01-23}}</ref> Originally a graduate student at [[Columbia University]]'s [[Columbia Business School|Business School]], Paxson transferred to Columbia's [[Columbia Graduate School of Arts and Sciences|Graduate School of Arts and Sciences]], receiving her M.A. and Ph.D. in economics, in 1985 and 1987, respectively, with a focus on labor.<ref name=BrownBio /><ref name=":0">{{cite web|url=https://www.brownalumnimagazine.com/articles/2012-11-27/who-is-christina-paxson|title=Who is Christina Paxson?|website=www.brownalumnimagazine.com|language=en|access-date=2019-01-23}}</ref> Paxson was advised by [[Joseph Altonji]] and her dissertation involved analyzing the effects of consumer interest rates on the consumer credit market.<ref name=":0" /> Paxson is married to Ari Gabinet and has two children, Nicholas and Benjamin.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Who is Christina Paxson?|url=https://www.brownalumnimagazine.com/articles/2012-11-27/who-is-christina-paxson|access-date=2021-03-29|website=www.brownalumnimagazine.com|language=en}}</ref> 

== Career ==
In 2000, she founded the Center for Health and Wellbeing at [[Princeton University|Princeton]], an interdisciplinary research center based in the [[Woodrow Wilson School of Public and International Affairs|Woodrow Wilson School]]. She served as the chair of Princeton's Economics Department in academic year 2008–09. She was also the founding director of an NIA Center for the Economics and Demography of Aging at Princeton.<ref name="BrownBio" /> During her time at Princeton, Paxson also served as a visiting professor at the [[University of Pennsylvania]]'s [[Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania|Wharton School]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nber.org/aginghealth/2011no1/paxson.html|title=NBER Bulletin on Aging and Health|website=www.nber.org|access-date=2018-01-17}}</ref>

Paxson's most recent research focuses on the impact of childhood health and circumstances on economic and health outcomes over the lifecourse; the impact of the [[HIV/AIDS|AIDS]] crisis on children's health and education in Africa; and the long run consequences of [[Hurricane Katrina]] on the mental and physical health of vulnerable populations. Paxson has been a Senior Editor of ''[[The Future of Children]]'', an interdisciplinary journal that works to build a bridge between cutting edge social science research and the policy community.

In 2013, Paxson wrote a ''[[The New Republic|New Republic]]'' op-ed, arguing for ongoing relevance of the humanities from an economist's perspective.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://newrepublic.com/article/114392/christina-paxson-president-brown-humanities-can-save-us|title=The Economic Case for Saving the Humanities|work=New Republic|access-date=2018-01-17|language=en-US}}</ref>

Paxson has also maintained numerous institutional affiliations: in addition to being a member of the [[Council on Foreign Relations]], she was elected to the [[American Academy of Arts and Sciences]] in 2017.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://news.brown.edu/articles/2017/04/academy |title=Brown's president named to American Academy of Arts and Sciences |publisher= Brown University |date= April 11, 2017 |accessdate=May 14, 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cfr.org/about/membership/roster.html?letter=B|title=Membership Roster|work=Council on Foreign Relations|accessdate=1 May 2016}}</ref> The previous year, she became a member of the Board of Directors of the [[Federal Reserve Bank of Boston]]. After serving as its deputy chair, she became the chair of its Board of Directors in 2021.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bostonfed.org/people/bank/christina-hull-paxson.aspx|title=Christina Hull Paxson - Federal Reserve Bank of Boston|last=Boston|first=Federal Reserve Bank of|website=Federal Reserve Bank of Boston|date=January 2017|language=en|access-date=2018-01-17}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.browndailyherald.com/2019/01/22/goldberg-paxson-fed-reserve/|title=Paxson named deputy chair of Boston Fed|last=Goldberg|first=Daniel|date=2019-01-22|website=Brown Daily Herald|language=en-US|access-date=2019-01-23}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Boston|first=Federal Reserve Bank of|date=2017-01-01|title=Christina Hull Paxson|url=https://www.bostonfed.org/people/bank/christina-hull-paxson.aspx|access-date=2021-01-10|website=Federal Reserve Bank of Boston|language=en}}</ref> In 2018, Paxson received an [[Honorary Doctorate|honorary doctorate]] from [[Williams College]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://news.brown.edu/articles/2018/09/honorary|title=Williams College awards honorary degree to President Paxson|access-date=2018-09-13|language=en}}</ref>

She has been a member of the Kol Emet congregation a Jewish [[Reconstructionist Judaism|Reconstructionist]] synagogue, committed to the growth of a spiritually and intellectually engaging [[Judaism]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://kolemet.org/|title=Congregation Kol Emet|language=en-US|access-date=2019-03-02}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.kolemet.org/documents/Summer_10_Voice.pdf|title=Masks|publisher=www.kolemet.org|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131202230753/http://www.kolemet.org/documents/Summer_10_Voice.pdf|archive-date=December 2, 2013|accessdate=November 26, 2013|df=mdy-all}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.kolemet.org/documents/dec_09-jan_10_Voice.pdf|title=Promises|publisher=www.kolemet.org|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131203003056/http://www.kolemet.org/documents/dec_09-jan_10_Voice.pdf|archive-date=December 3, 2013|accessdate=November 26, 2013|df=mdy-all}}</ref>

As President of Brown University, Paxson has focused on cultivating entrepreneurship and collaboration between the students and faculty. Under her leadership, the University has also opened a new School of Public Health, launched the Institute at Brown for Environment and Society, and is in connection with the Watson Institute for Public and International Affairs. She has also sustained undergraduate financial aid as the fastest growing area of Brown's budget, by increasing scholarships for low income families.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.brown.edu/about/administration/president/about/president-christina-hull-paxson|title=President Christina Hull Paxson {{!}} Office of the President {{!}} Brown University|website=www.brown.edu|access-date=2019-04-15}}</ref>

In wake of the [[COVID-19 pandemic]], Paxson penned a ''New York Times'' op-ed and appeared on [[CNN]], outlining her views on the importance of reopening colleges safely in the fall of 2020.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Paxson|first=Christina|date=2020-04-26|title=Opinion {{!}} College Campuses Must Reopen in the Fall. Here's How We Do It.|language=en-US|work=The New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2020/04/26/opinion/coronavirus-colleges-universities.html|access-date=2020-06-10|issn=0362-4331}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|author1=Meg Wagner |author2=Mike Hayes |author3=Elise Hammond |author4=Veronica Rocha|date=2020-04-28|title=Schools might not recover if they don't reopen in the fall, university president says|url=https://www.cnn.com/us/live-news/us-coronavirus-update-04-28-20/h_048da8854be6d3044535c287d4110d7f|access-date=2020-06-10|website=CNN|language=en}}</ref> On June 4, 2020, Paxson testified before the U.S. Senate Committee on Health, Education, Labor and Pensions, during a hearing entitled “COVID-19: Going Back to College Safely."<ref>{{Cite web|title=Paxson tells U.S. Senate stakes are high as universities seek to safely reopen|url=https://www.brown.edu/news/2020-06-04/senate-committee|access-date=2020-06-10|website=Brown University|language=en}}</ref>
