'''Keisha Lynne Ellis''' is a [[Bahamas|Bahamian]] [[political scientist]], known for her political and creative writing.

==Early life==
Ellis earned a master's degree in International Political Economy from the [[University of Kent]], [[Brussels School of International Studies]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Brussels School of International Studies: Graduate Studies|url=https://www.kent.ac.uk/brussels/documents/prospectus.pdf|website=Brussels School of International Studies|accessdate=9 February 2018}}</ref>

==Career==
Ellis is a political science lecturer at the [[University of the Bahamas]].<ref name="NassauGuardian">{{cite news|title=Bahamian political scientist pens simplified version of the Bahamian Constitution|url=https://thenassauguardian.com/2017/03/27/bahamian-political-scientist-pens-simplified-version-of-the-bahamian-constitution/|accessdate=9 February 2018|work=The Nassau Guardian|date=27 March 2017}}</ref> She wrote ''The People’s Constitution: A Layman’s Interpretation of the Constitution of The Bahamas'' after realizing that many of her students did not understand the underlying issues in the [[Bahamian referendum, 2002]] and [[Bahamian constitutional referendum, 2016]].<ref name="NassauGuardian" /> The book is being used in classrooms.<ref name="NassauGuardian" /> She was named a member of the Bahamas National Reparations Committee in 2014.<ref name="CARICOM">{{cite web|last1=Rojas|first1=Don|title=Establishment of the Bahamas National Reparations Committee|url=http://caricomreparations.org/establishment-bahamas-national-reparations-committee/|website=[[CARICOM Reparations Commission]]|date= 15 October 2014| accessdate=9 February 2018}}</ref> This committee is tasked with establishing the case for reparations to Caribbean nations from former European colonial states for Native genocide and the effects of the transatlantic slave trade.<ref name="CARICOM" />

Ellis is also known for her creative writing. Her work was featured in Eighth National Exhibition (NE8) at the [[National Art Gallery of The Bahamas|National Art Gallery of the Bahamas]].<ref>{{cite web|title=NE8|url=http://nagb.org.bs/events/2016/12/15/national-exhibition-8-opening-reception|website=National Art Gallery of the Bahamas|accessdate=9 February 2018}}</ref> She has been published in several online magazines and print collections. She is the Strategic Initiatives Manager at Hands for Hunger, an organization that redistributes food and reduces [[food waste]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Team|url=http://www.handsforhunger.org/learn/team/|website=Hands for Hunger|accessdate=9 February 2018}}</ref>
