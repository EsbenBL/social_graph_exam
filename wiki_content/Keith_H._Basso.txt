'''Keith Hamilton Basso''' (March 15, 1940 – August 4, 2013) was a [[cultural anthropology|cultural]] and [[linguistic anthropology|linguistic]] [[anthropology|anthropologist]] noted for his study of the [[Western Apache]]s, specifically those from the community of [[Cibecue, Arizona]]. Basso was professor emeritus of anthropology at the [[University of New Mexico]] and  earlier taught at the [[University of Arizona]] and [[Yale University]].<ref>{{citation | title = A community of scholars: faculty and members, 1930-1980 | publisher = Princeton University, Institute of Advanced Studies | date = 1980 | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=Kj9tAAAAMAAJ&q=%22Basso,+keith+hamilton%22+1940+born}}</ref><ref>{{citation | title = Supplement to Who's who in America | volume = 44 | date = 1987 | publisher = Marquis Who's Who | isbn = 9780837971001 | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=NVFAAQAAIAAJ&q=%22+Basso+Keith+hamilton%22+1940}}</ref>

After first studying [[Apache]] culture in 1959, Basso completed a bachelor's degree at [[Harvard University]] (B.A., 1962) and then took the doctorate at [[Stanford University]] (Ph.D., 1967).<ref name=obit/> He was the son of novelist [[Hamilton Basso]].

Basso was awarded the [[Victor Turner]] Prize for Ethnographic Writing in 1997 for his [[ethnography]], ''Wisdom Sits in Places: Landscape and Language Among the Western Apache''. The work was also the 1996 [[Western States Book Award]] Winner in Creative Nonfiction.<ref>{{Cite book
