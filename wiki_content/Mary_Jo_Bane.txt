'''Mary Jo Bane''' is an American political scientist who focuses on children and welfare. She is currently the [[Thornton Bradshaw]] Professor at [[Harvard Kennedy School]], and formerly the [[Malcolm H. Wiener|Malcolm Wiener]] Professor of Social Policy and Director of the Malcolm Wiener Center for Social Policy.

==Early life and education==
Bane was born into a Catholic family. While attending [[Georgetown University]], she campaigned for Senator John F. Kennedy’s presidential election. She became inspired by his foreign affairs platform and joined the [[Peace Corps]], where she taught schoolchildren in Liberia.<ref name = "x">{{cite web |title=Their brilliant careers: Bane and Nye reach emeritus status |url=https://www.hks.harvard.edu/more/alumni/hks-magazine/their-brilliant-careers-bane-and-nye-reach-emeritus-status |website=hks.harvard.edu |accessdate=November 18, 2019 |date=Summer 2017}}</ref>

==Career==
Bane joined the faculty at the [[John F. Kennedy School of Government]] in 1981,<ref name = "emeritus">{{cite web |title=Joe Nye and Mary Jo Bane reach emeritus status |url=https://www.hks.harvard.edu/announcements/joe-nye-and-mary-jo-bane-reach-emeritus-status |website=hks.harvard.edu |accessdate=November 18, 2019 |date=August 3, 2017}}</ref> where she would become the first woman to gain tenure at the Kennedy School.<ref>{{cite news |title=HARVARD'S KENNEDY SCHOOL: IS COMPETENCE ENOUGH? |url=https://www.nytimes.com/1989/03/12/magazine/harvard-s-kennedy-school-is-competence-enough.html |accessdate=November 18, 2019 |work=The New York Times |date=March 12, 1989 |quote=says Mary Jo Bane, a professor of public policy who is the first woman to get tenure at the school}}</ref> From 1987 until 1992, Bane served as director of the Malcolm Wiener Center for Social Policy.<ref name = "x"/> The next year,  Bane worked as commissioner of the New York State Department of Social Services and an assistant secretary for children and families at the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services until 1996.<ref name = "dean">{{cite web |title=Bane named academic dean at KSG |url=https://news.harvard.edu/gazette/story/2006/07/bane-named-academic-dean-at-ksg/ |website=harvard.edu |accessdate=November 18, 2019 |date=July 20, 2006}}</ref> A few years later, Bane was elected an 2003 [[American Academy of Political and Social Science]] Fellow.<ref>{{cite web |title=Seven Harvard affiliates honored by AAPSS |url=https://news.harvard.edu/gazette/story/2003/04/seven-harvard-affiliates-honored-by-aapss/ |website=harvard.edu |accessdate=November 18, 2019 |date=April 24, 2003}}</ref> 

A few years later, Bane was elected the Harvard’s Kennedy School of Government Academic Dean, replacing [[Stephen M. Walt]].<ref name = "dean"/> She stayed in this role from 2006 until 2011, when she was succeeded by [[Iris Bohnet]].<ref>{{cite web |title=HKS appoints Bohnet academic dean |url=https://news.harvard.edu/gazette/story/2011/04/hks-appoints-bohnet-academic-dean/ |website=harvard.edu |accessdate=November 18, 2019 |date=April 11, 2011}}</ref>
