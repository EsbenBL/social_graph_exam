'''Alwyn Young''' is a professor of economics and the Leili & Johannes Huth Fellow at the [[London School of Economics and Political Science]] (LSE). He held a named chair at the [[University of Chicago]] and was on the faculty at [[Boston University]] and the [[MIT Sloan School of Management]] before joining the LSE faculty.<ref name="Milton Friedman Institute for Research in Economics">{{cite web|url=http://mfi.uchicago.edu/people/young.shtml|title=Milton Friedman Institute for Research in Economics|access-date=2011-03-02|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110320031837/http://mfi.uchicago.edu/people/young.shtml|archive-date=2011-03-20|url-status=dead}}</ref> A graduate of Cornell University, he holds an MA in law and diplomacy and a PhD in international relations, both from the [[Fletcher School of Law and Diplomacy]] at [[Tufts University]], and a PhD in economics from [[Columbia University]]. Young has taught courses in introductory economics at the LSE to first-year undergraduates, and topics in modern economic growth as a part of advanced macroeconomics course at postgraduate level.

Well known academic papers by Alwyn Young include ''The tyranny of numbers: confronting the statistical realities of the East Asian growth experience<ref name="Tyranny of Numbers">{{cite journal|first=Alwyn|last=Young|journal=The Quarterly Journal of Economics|volume=110|issue=3|pages=641–680|date=August 1995|jstor=2946695|title=The Tyranny of Numbers: Confronting the Statistical Realities of the East Asian Growth Experience|doi=10.2307/2946695|s2cid=115135123|url=http://www.nber.org/papers/w4680.pdf}}</ref>'' and ''A tale of two cities: factor accumulation and technical change in Hong Kong and Singapore<ref name="A Tale of Two Cities">{{cite journal|first=Alwyn|last=Young|journal=NBER Macroeconomics Annual|volume=7|pages=13–54|year=1992|publisher=University of Chicago Press|jstor=3584993|title=A Tale of Two Cities: Factor Accumulation and Technical Change in Hong Kong and Singapore|doi=10.2307/3584993|doi-access=free}}</ref>''.

Professor Young's most recent research has focussed on growth in the African continent<ref>{{citation |last=Young |first=Alwyn |date=September 2009 |title=The African Growth Miracle |url=http://www.econ.yale.edu/seminars/develop/tdw09/young-090924.pdf |publisher=London School of Economics |access-date=2013-03-06 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130520234012/http://www.econ.yale.edu/seminars/develop/tdw09/young-090924.pdf |archive-date=2013-05-20 |url-status=dead }}</ref> as well as the impact of HIV-Aids on GDP figures
