'''Sir George Donald Alastair MacDougall''', {{Post-nominals|country=GBR|CBE|FBA|sep=,|size=100}} (26 October 1912 &ndash; 22 March 2004) was a Scottish economist and [[civil servant]] who influenced UK [[public policy]] during the 1960s. He headed the [[Government Economic Service]] and, between 1969 and 1973, acted as chief economic adviser to [[Chancellor of the Exchequer|Chancellors of the Exchequer]] [[Roy Jenkins]], [[Iain MacLeod]] and [[Anthony Barber]].<ref>{{cite news |url=http://politics.guardian.co.uk/politicsobituaries/story/0,,1177490,00.html |title=Obituary |date=25 March 2004 |work=The Guardian |access-date=15 May 2010 |location=London |first=Michael |last=Posner}}</ref> He predicted in about 2000 that the [[euro]] currency could not work and that the [[EU]] [[Stability and Growth Pact]] would not be enforced.<ref>{{cite news| url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/finance/comment/7664328/Chronicle-of-a-disaster-foretold-plays-out-on-the-streets-of-Athens.html | location=London | work=The Daily Telegraph | first=Liam | last=Halligan | title=Chronicle of a disaster foretold plays out on the streets of Athens | date=1 May 2010}} Interview with MacDougall "about 10 years" before article date.</ref>

MacDougall was born in Glasgow in 1912, the son of a family with a china business, and was educated at [[Kelvinside Academy]], [[Shrewsbury School]] and [[Balliol College, Oxford]].
