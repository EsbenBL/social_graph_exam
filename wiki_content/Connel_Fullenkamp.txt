'''Connel Fullenkamp''' (born May 26, 1965) is an economist and the Director of Undergraduate Studies and [[Professor of the Practice]] in [[economics]] at [[Duke University]], where he teaches core economics and financial economics courses. In addition to his work at Duke University, he consults for the IMF Institute for Capacity Development<ref>{{cite web|title=Authors|url=https://www.weforum.org/agenda/authors/connel-fullenkamp/|website=World Economic Forum}}</ref> at the [[International Monetary Fund]]. As a member of the IMF's finance team, he trains government officials and central bankers in financial market regulation, with a focus on derivatives and emerging financial instruments. He has also collaborated with [[The Great Courses]] to produce several lecture series on economics and investing<ref>{{cite web|last1=Plus|first1=The Great Courses|title=Connel Fullenkamp {{!}} The Great Courses Plus|url=https://www.thegreatcoursesplus.com/professors/connel-fullenkamp|website=The Great Courses Plus|language=en}}</ref> and consults for ''[[The New York Times]]'' as a faculty contributor to the ''NYT in Education'' courses in microeconomics<ref>{{cite web|title=Microeconomics « The New York Times in Education|url=http://nytimesineducation.com/microeconomics/|website=nytimesineducation.com}}</ref> and macroeconomics.<ref>{{cite web|title=Macroeconomics « The New York Times in Education|url=http://nytimesineducation.com/macroeconomics/|website=nytimesineducation.com}}</ref>

==Biography==
Born to a Jewish family in 1965, Fullenkamp earned a B.A. in economics at [[Michigan State University]] (1987), an M.A. in economics at [[Harvard University]] (1989), and a Ph.D. in economics at [[Harvard University]] (1992). He was named a Harry S. Truman Scholar in 1985. Fullenkamp has been published in ''[[The Cato Journal]]'', the ''[[Journal of Banking and Finance]]'', the ''[[Review of Economic Dynamics]]'', and the ''[[Review of Economics and Statistics]]''. In 1998, Fullenkamp received the University of Notre Dame's [[Mendoza College of Business]] Outstanding Teacher Award.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Dame|first1=ENR // AgencyND // University of Notre|title=BP Outstanding Undergraduate Professor Award // Kaneb Center for Teaching and Learning // University of Notre Dame|url=http://kaneb.nd.edu/rsrcs/awards-for-outstanding-teaching/bp-outstanding-undergraduate-professor-award/|website=kaneb.nd.edu|language=en}}</ref> In 2004-2005, he was honored with the Duke University's Alumni Distinguished Undergraduate Teaching Award,<ref>{{cite web|title=Past Award Winners {{!}} Duke|url=https://alumni.duke.edu/past-award-winners|website=alumni.duke.edu|language=en}}</ref> and in 2012 was awarded the ICFR-Financial Times Research Prize for his paper on international financial regulation.
