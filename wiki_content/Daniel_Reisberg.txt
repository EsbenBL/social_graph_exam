'''Daniel Reisberg''' is an American academic who is the Patricia and Clifford Lunneborg Professor of Psychology at [[Reed College]] in [[Portland, Oregon]]. His specialty is [[cognitive psychology]], and he focuses on [[memory]],<ref>[http://www.refinery29.com/2015/02/82074/brian-williams-false-memories "The Science of Fake Memories," Refinery 29, February 10, 2015]</ref> judgment, and [[imagery]], particularly in relation to emotion.<ref>[http://www.heraldextra.com/momclick/health-and-fitness/how-to-create-lasting-holiday-memories/article_4289e27c-04bc-5e5c-8f69-24e637064aef.html "How to Create Lasting Holiday Memories," Herald Extra, December 28, 2014]</ref>

Reisberg earned his B.A. in [[psychology]] and [[philosophy]] from [[Swarthmore College]] in 1975. He then attended the [[University of Pennsylvania]], obtaining an M.A. in 1976 and a Ph.D. in 1980. Reisberg has been teaching at Reed College since 1986.

Reisberg is the author of ''The Science of Perception and Memory: A Pragmatic Guide for the Justice System'' ([[Oxford University Press]]).  Reisberg also consults widely with the justice community, and occasionally appears in court as an [[expert witness]].<ref>[http://www.oregonlive.com/clackamascounty/index.ssf/2015/04/post_184.html "Parents discount daughters' sexual abuse claims against Happy Valley pastor," The Oregonian, April 22, 2015]</ref>
