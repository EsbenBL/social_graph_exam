'''Dina Deborah Pomeranz''' (born 21 February 1977) is a Swiss economist and assistant professor of [[applied economics]] at [[University of Zurich]]. Pomeranz is considered to be one of the most influential Swiss economists.<ref name="graduateinstitute">{{Cite web|title=Alumna Making Waves in Economics {{!}} IHEID|url=https://graduateinstitute.ch/programmes/alumna-making-waves-economics|website=Graduate Institute Geneva|access-date=2 October 2020}}</ref>

== Education ==
Pomeranz grew up in [[Zürich]], where she graduated from high school.<ref>{{cite web|title=Von Zürich nach Harvard und zurück|periodical=|publisher=|url=https://www.econ.uzh.ch/dam/jcr:b22b7dcb-0a6d-44e8-82ec-64109f59656f/37D37603-CEC7-413B-A078-6FD3895AFA6F.pdf|url-status=|format=|access-date=|archive-url=|archive-date=|last=|date=|year=|language=|pages=|quote=}}</ref> She studied [[international relations]] at the [[University of Geneva]] and obtained her master's degree from the [[Graduate Institute of International and Development Studies]] in Geneva in 2004. In 2010 she received her PhD in economics from [[Harvard University]] with the dissertation ''Essays on Tax Evasion and Savings: Evidence from Three Randomized Experiments in Chile.''<ref>{{cite web|title=Dina Pomeranz_CV_UZH|periodical=|publisher=|url=https://www.econ.uzh.ch/dam/jcr:4139f751-8545-4d90-8b3d-6f3edc0ae827/CV%20Pomeranz%2019-10.pdf|url-status=|format=|access-date=|archive-url=|archive-date=|last=|date=|year=|language=|pages=|quote=}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|surname1=Dina Deborah Pomeranz|title=Essays on Tax Evasion and Savings: Evidence from Three Randomized Experiments in Chile|publisher=Harvard University|date=2010|language=German|url=https://books.google.com/books/about/Essays_on_Tax_Evasion_and_Savings.html?id=cK9YewAACAAJ|access-date=30 April 2020
}}</ref>

== Research and career ==
Pomeranz was an assistant professor at [[Harvard Business School]] before she joined the [[University of Zurich]] as an Assistant Professor of Applied Economics. At the University of Zurich, her research interests include development economics, public finance, and impact evaluations. Her research focuses on public policy in developing countries, particularly with regards to taxation and public procurement.<ref name=":0">{{cite web |title=Dina Pomeranz |url=https://www.econ.uzh.ch/en/people/faculty/pomeranz.html |website=UZH Department of Economics |accessdate=2 October 2020 |language=en}}</ref> She is a post-doctoral fellow at the [[Massachusetts Institute of Technology|MIT]]'s [[Abdul Latif Jameel Poverty Action Lab|Poverty Action Lab]] (J-PAL).<ref>{{Cite web|title=Dina Pomeranz|url=https://www.cgdev.org/expert/dina-pomeranz|website=Center For Global Development|language=en|access-date=30 April 2020}}</ref>

Pomeranz is an affiliate professor at the Bureau for Research and Economic Analysis of Development (BREAD) and the Center for Economic Policy Research (CEPR). She is a non-resident fellow at the [[Center for Global Development]] (CGD) and a member of the [[International Growth Centre]] (IGC). She is also a faculty research fellow at the [[National Bureau of Economic Research]] (NBER).<ref name="World Bank">{{Cite web|title=Dina Pomeranz|url=https://live.worldbank.org/experts/dina-pomeranz|date=30 January 2018|website=World Bank Live|language=en|access-date=30 April 2020}}</ref> She was elected to the Council of the European Economic Association for a 5-year term beginning in 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|title=EEA – Fellows of the European Economics Association|url=https://www.eeassoc.org/index.php?site=&page=21&trsz=1|website=www.eeassoc.org|access-date=30 April 2020}}</ref> Pomeranz serves on the boards or advisory boards of Helvetas, Evidence Action, Policy Analytics, TamTam-Together Against Malaria and IDinsight.<ref name=":0" />

== Recognition ==
Pomeranz's work has been published in ''[[The American Economic Review]]'', the ''[[American Economic Journal|American Economic Journal: Applied Economics]]'', and the ''Journal of Economic Development''. In 2017, the [[European Research Council]] awarded her a Starting Grant for research on tax evasion and firm networks.<ref>{{cite web |title=List of Principal Investigators – All domains |url=https://erc.europa.eu/sites/default/files/document/file/erc_2017_stg_results_all_domains.pdf |website=ERC Starting Grants 2017 |accessdate=2 October 2020}}</ref> In 2018, she was rewarded the Excellence Prize in Applied Development Research by [[Verein für Socialpolitik]]. She was ranked as one of the top ten most influential economists in Switzerland by Zurich-based newspaper ''[[Tages-Anzeiger]]''.<ref name="graduateinstitute"/>

''[[Frankfurter Allgemeine Zeitung]]'' ranked Pomeranz the highest in social media influence in its 2019 ranking of economists, with data supported by the economics magazine ''Makronom''.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Bernau |first1=Patrick |title=F.A.Z.-Ökonomenranking: Wer ist der einflussreichste Ökonom? |url=https://www.faz.net/aktuell/wirtschaft/f-a-z-oekonomenranking-2019-ernst-fehr-vor-clemens-fuest-16395138.html |website=Frankfurter Allgemeine Zeitung |language=de |date=21 September 2019}}</ref> As of August 2020, Pomeranz was ranked 50th on [[Research Papers in Economics|RePEc]]'s list of Top Young Economists.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Young Economist Rankings {{!}} IDEAS/RePEc|url=https://ideas.repec.org/top/top.young.html|access-date=2 October 2020|website=ideas.repec.org}}</ref>
