'''Francine Dee Blau''' (born August 29, 1946 in [[New York City]])<ref name="cicarelli">Cicarelli, James and Julianne Cicarelli. [https://books.google.com/books?id=Liu8A8KjDR0C&pg=PA36 ''Distinguished women economists''.] Westport, CT: Greenwood Press, 2003, pp. 36–40, {{ISBN|978-0-313-30331-9}}.</ref> is an [[United States|American]] [[economist]] and professor of economics as well as Industrial and Labor Relations at [[Cornell University]]. In 2010, Blau was the first woman to receive the [[IZA Prize in Labor Economics]] for her "seminal contributions to the economic analysis of labor market inequality."<ref name="iza award">[[Institute for the Study of Labor|IZA]] Press Statement. [http://www.iza.org/files/BlauIZAPrizeEN.pdf ''IZA Prize in Labor Economics goes to Francine D. Blau''.] September 30, 2010.</ref> She was awarded the 2017 [[Jacob Mincer Award]] by the Society of Labor Economists in recognition of lifetime of contributions to the field of labor economics.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://www.sole-jole.org/Mincer2017.html|title=2017 Mincer Prize|website=www.sole-jole.org|access-date=2018-02-07}}</ref>

==Personal life and education==
Blau was born to parents Harold Raymond Blau and Sylvia Blau, née Goldberg, in New York City. Her parents divorced when she was six years old. She and her brother lived with their mother until Sylvia Blau became ill. After that, the children went to live with their father. Harold Blau supported Francine's wish to become an economist.<ref name="cicarelli"/>

Blau graduated from [[Forest Hills High School (New York)|Forest Hills High School]] in [[Queens]] in 1963, after which she entered [[Cornell University]] and received her [[Bachelor of Science|B.S.]] in [[Cornell University School of Industrial and Labor Relations|industrial and labor relations]] in 1966. She received her [[Master of Arts|M.A.]] in [[economics]] from [[Harvard University]] in 1969 and her [[Ph.D.]] in economics from the same university in 1975.<ref name="cicarelli"/>

Blau is married to [[Lawrence M. Kahn]], also an economics professor at [[Cornell University]].<ref name=catt>{{cite news|last=Catt|first=Mary|title=Francine Blau receives top labor economics award|url=http://www.news.cornell.edu/stories/Sept10/BlauIZAprize.html|newspaper=Chronicle Online, [[Cornell University]]|date=Sep 30, 2010}}</ref> Together they have two children.<ref name="cicarelli" />

==Career==
Blau is currently the [[Frances Perkins]] Professor of Industrial and Labor Relations and Professor of Economics at [[Cornell University]].<ref name=catt /><ref name="cornell">Cornell University ILR School. [http://www.ilr.cornell.edu/directory/fdb4/ ''Francine D. Blau''.] Retrieved on May 1, 2011.</ref> Before coming to Cornell in 1994, she was an assistant, associate, and professor of [[Economics]] and [[Labor and Industrial Relations]] at the [[University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign]] where she joined the faculty in 1975. Prior to that, she was a research associate at [[Ohio State University]] and a visiting lecturer at [[Yale University]].<ref name="blaug">Blaug, Mark and Howard R. Vane. [https://books.google.com/books?id=29U9GeQQnSUC&pg=PA98 ''Who's who in economics'.] Cheltenham, UK, Northampton, MA: Edward Elgar Pub., 2003, {{ISBN|978-1-84376-857-9}}.</ref>

Blau is a research associate at the [[National Bureau of Economic Research]],<ref>National Bureau of Economic Research. [http://www.nber.org/people/Francine_Blau ''Francine D. Blau''.] Retrieved on May 1, 2011.</ref> a research fellow at the [[Institute for the Study of Labor]] (IZA),<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=http://legacy.iza.org/en/webcontent/personnel/photos/index_html?key=340|title=IZA - Francine D. Blau|website=legacy.iza.org|access-date=2018-02-07}}</ref> and a research fellow at the [[Center for Economic Studies]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.cesifo-group.de/ifoHome/research/Network/Members.html?p_person_id=52|title=CESifo Group Munich - CESifo Research Network Members|website=www.cesifo-group.de|language=en|access-date=2018-02-07}}</ref> She is a fellow of the [http://www.sole-jole.org/fellows.htm Society of Labor Economists], the [[American Academy of Political and Social Science]], and the [[Labor and Employment Relations Association]].<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=https://www.ilr.cornell.edu/sites/ilr.cornell.edu/files/faculty/vitas/Vita_Blau_July_2017.pdf|title=Curriculum vitae – Francine D. Blau|date=July 2017|publisher=www.ilr.cornell.edu|access-date=February 7, 2018}}</ref> She is also a fellow at the Center for the Study of Poverty and Inequality ([[Stanford University]]) and a research fellow at the Compensation Research Initiative ([[Cornell University]]).<ref name=":2" />

She has served as vice president of the [[American Economic Association]], president of the Society of Labor Economists,<ref name=catt /> the [[Labor and Employment Relations Association]], and the [http://web.grinnell.edu/MEA/ Midwest Economics Association].<ref name="blaug"/> She is an Associate Editor of [[Labour Economics (journal)|Labour Economics]] and was formerly an editor of the [[Journal of Labor Economics]] and an Associate Editor of the [[Journal of Economic Perspectives]].<ref name=":2" /> She serves or has served on numerous Editorial Boards, including the [[American Economic Review]], the [[Journal of Labor Economics]], the [[Journal of Economic Perspectives]], the [[ILR Review]], the [[Journal of Labor Research]], and [[The Annals of the American Academy of Political and Social Science]], among others.<ref name=":2" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ilr.cornell.edu/people/francine-blau|title=Francine Blau {{!}} The ILR School {{!}} Cornell University|website=www.ilr.cornell.edu|language=en|access-date=2018-02-07}}</ref>
