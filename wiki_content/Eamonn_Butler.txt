'''Eamonn Butler''' (born 1953) is a British economist. He is the co-founder and Director of the [[Adam Smith Institute]].

==Early life==
Eamonn Butler was born in 1953. His brother is [[Stuart Butler]].<ref name="ideasandthinktanks">Michael David Kandiah, Anthony Seldon, ''Ideas and Think Tanks in Contemporary Britain'', Routledge, 2014, Volume 1, pp. 73-74 [https://books.google.co.uk/books?id=9y2aAgAAQBAJ&pg=PA73&dq=%22Eamonn+Butler%22&hl=en&sa=X&ei=fR9xVaLDOsav7Aa5nIPACQ&ved=0CFgQ6AEwCTgU#v=onepage&q=%22Eamonn%20Butler%22&f=false]</ref> He graduated from the [[University of St Andrews]].<ref name="ideasandthinktanks"/>

==Career==
Butler worked on pensions and welfare issues for the [[United States House of Representatives]] in [[Washington DC]], before returning to the UK where he served as editor of the ''British Insurance Broker Monthly''.

He co-founded the [[Adam Smith Institute]] in London with his brother Stuart and [[Madsen Pirie]], both graduates from the University of St Andrews.<ref name="ideasandthinktanks"/> He now serves as its Director.<ref>[https://www.theguardian.com/profile/eamonnbutler Profile: Eamonn Butler], ''[[The Guardian]]''</ref>

He is author of books on the work of three economists: ''[[Friedrich von Hayek|Hayek]]: His Contribution to the Economic and Political Thought of Our Time''; ''[[Milton Friedman]]: A Guide to his Economic Thought''; ''[[Ludwig von Mises]]: A Primer''; ''[[Ludwig von Mises]]: Fountainhead of the Modern Microeconomics Revolution''. Additionally, he has contributed extensively to national magazines and newspapers such as ''[[The Times]]'' on subjects ranging from health policy, economic management, taxation and public spending, transport, pensions, and e-government.<ref>[http://www.thetimes.co.uk/tto/public/profile/Eamonn-Butler Eamonn Butler], ''The Times''</ref>

He served as the Secretary on the 2012â€“14 Board of Directors of the [[Mont Pelerin Society]].<ref>[https://www.montpelerin.org/montpelerin/mpsBoard.html Mont Pelerin Society: 2012-2014 Board of Directors]</ref>

He received an Honorary Doctorate from [[Heriot-Watt University]] in 2012.<ref>{{Cite web|url = http://www.hw.ac.uk/news/archive/2012/summer-graduations-2012.htm|title = Summer Graduations 2012|website = Heriot-Watt University|language = en-GB|access-date = 2016-03-29}}</ref>
