'''Robert Herschel Donaldson''' (born June 14, 1943) is an American [[political scientist]].

Donaldson attended [[Harvard University]].  He completed his [[Bachelor of Arts]] ''[[magna cum laude]]'' in 1964, his [[Master of Arts]] in 1966, and his [[Doctor of Philosophy|Ph.D.]] in 1969.  His [[doctoral dissertation]] analyzed the [[Soviet Union]]'s economic policies.  He was admitted to the [[Phi Beta Kappa Society]] as an undergraduate and has remained active in the organization as a professor.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://www.tulsaworld.com/news/article.aspx?subjectid=11&articleid=051016_Ne_A25_Khrus4009 |title=Khrushchev's son to speak at TU forum |work=The Tulsa World |date=October 16, 2005 |accessdate=August 11, 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://orgs.utulsa.edu/phibetakappa/TU_Resident_Members.htm |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2011-08-11 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20111002000122/http://orgs.utulsa.edu/phibetakappa/TU_Resident_Members.htm |archivedate=2011-10-02 }}</ref>

Donaldson began his teaching career at [[Vanderbilt University]], where he rose from assistant professor to full professor and [[associate dean]] of the [[Vanderbilt University College of Arts and Science|College of Arts and Science]].  In 1981 he became the [[Provost (education)|provost]] of [[Lehman College]] at the [[City University of New York]]. In 1984 he became president of [[Fairleigh Dickinson University]] and served until 1990, when he became president of the [[University of Tulsa]]. He worked to raise the university's profile through international conferences in [[Tulsa, Oklahoma|Tulsa]] and exchange programs, particularly with institutions in Russia. His tenure as president was "marked by change and criticism," including a unanimous vote of no confidence by the student government.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://findarticles.com/p/articles/mi_qn4182/is_19950608/ai_n10083776 |title=University of Tulsa President Plans to Step Down Next Year |work=The Journal Record |date=June 8, 1995 |accessdate=August 10, 2011}}{{Dead link|date=May 2012}}</ref> "Citing 'institutional stresses' and conflicting expectations among school constituencies," he resigned as president in 1996.<ref>{{cite news |url=http://newsok.com/university-of-tulsa-president-to-resign/article/2504968 |title=University of Tulsa President to Resign |work=The Oklahoman |date=June 8, 1995 |accessdate=August 9, 2011}}</ref>
