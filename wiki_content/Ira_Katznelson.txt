'''Ira I. Katznelson''' (born 1944) is an American [[political scientist]] and [[historian]], noted for his research on the liberal state, inequality, social knowledge, and institutions, primarily focused on the United States.  His work has been characterized as an "interrogation of political liberalism in the United States and Europe—asking for definition of its many forms, their origins, their strengths and weaknesses, and what kinds there can be".<ref name="Valelly">{{cite journal |last1=Valelly |first1=Richard M. |title=Ira Katznelson: Toward a Useful Historical Political Science of Liberalism |journal=PS: Political Science & Politics |date=October 2005 |volume=38 |issue=04 |pages=797–800 |doi=10.1017/S1049096505210429 |url=https://www.semanticscholar.org/paper/Ira-Katznelson%3A-Toward-a-Useful-Historical-Science-Valelly/a4cd813603ebf81ef970becc535a84f9c925b355 |access-date=12 July 2021}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Katznelson's parents emigrated to the United States after [[World War I]], from [[Belorussia]] and [[Poland]], They lived in New York City, where Katznelson attended school at the [[Yeshivah]] of [[Flatbush, Brooklyn]].
Katznelson earned a Bachelor of Arts degree from [[Columbia University]] in 1966 and completed his [[PhD]] in [[history]] at the [[University of Cambridge]] in 1969. Among his influences he includes [[Richard Hofstadter]], [[Ralph Dahrendorf]], [[Robert Dahl]], and [[Daniel Bell]].<ref name=":0"/>

== Career ==
Katznelson taught at [[Columbia University|Columbia]] from 1969 to 1974, at the [[University of Chicago]] from 1974 to 1983, and at [[The New School for Social Research]] from 1983 to 1994.<ref name=":0">{{Cite journal|last=Katznelson|first=Ira|date=2021-05-11|title=Measuring Liberalism, Confronting Evil: A Retrospective|url=https://www.annualreviews.org/doi/10.1146/annurev-polisci-042219-030219|journal=Annual Review of Political Science|language=en|volume=24|issue=1|pages=1–19|doi=10.1146/annurev-polisci-042219-030219|issn=1094-2939|doi-access=free}}</ref> 
Katznelson was chair of the [[academic department|department]] of [[political science]] at the University of Chicago from 1979 to 1982 and [[Dean (education)|dean]] of [[The New School]] from 1983 to 1989.<ref name="Utter">{{cite book |editor-last1=Utter |editor-first1=Glenn H. |editor-last2=Lockhart |editor-first2=Charles |title=American Political Scientists: A Dictionary |date=2002 |publisher=Greenwich Press|location=Westport, CT; London |edition=2nd |pages=193-196 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=HTXDxjW6OBAC&pg=PA195 |access-date=12 July 2021}}</ref>

In 1994, Katznelson returned to [[Columbia University|Columbia]], where he is the Ruggles [[professor]] of [[political science]] and [[history]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Ira Katznelson: Biography|url=http://polisci.columbia.edu/people/profile/92|work=Columbia University|publisher=Columbia University|access-date=14 December 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131211001815/http://polisci.columbia.edu/people/profile/92|archive-date=11 December 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref> In 2012, he was named president of the [[Social Science Research Council]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Ira Katznelson Named Next SSRC President|url=http://www.ssrc.org/pages/ira-katznelson-named-next-ssrc-president/|publisher=Social Science Research Council|access-date=6 June 2013}}</ref> In 2019, Katznelson was named interim provost at Columbia.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Ira Katznelson, CC '66, to serve as interim provost|url=http://columbiaspectator.com/news/2019/08/16/ira-katznelson-cc-66-to-serve-as-interim-provost/|access-date=2021-03-13|website=Columbia Daily Spectator}}</ref> In that position, he represented management in contested negotiations with the [[Graduate student employee unionization|graduate student union]], a position he noted was "painful" given his "longstanding connections with the labor movement."<ref>{{cite web|last=Bwog Staff|date=8 March 2021|title=Interim Provost Speaks On Upcoming Graduate Workers Strike|url=https://bwog.com/2021/03/interim-provost-speaks-on-upcoming-graduate-workers-strike/|url-status=live|website=Bwog Columbia Student News}}</ref><ref name="Patel">{{cite news |last1=Patel |first1=Vimal |title=A Labor Sympathizer, Now on the Management Side, Calls for ‘Mutual Realism’ |url=https://www.chronicle.com/article/a-labor-sympathizer-now-on-the-management-side-calls-for-mutual-realism?cid=gen_sign_in |access-date=12 July 2021 |work=The Chronicle of Higher Education |date=March 11, 2021}}</ref>
As of July 1, 2021, he was succeeded as provost by [[Mary Cunningham Boyce]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Ira Katznelson Finishes Term as Mary Boyce Becomes Provost |url=https://news.columbia.edu/news/ira-katznelson-finishes-term-mary-boyce-becomes-provost |website=Columbia News |publisher=Columbia University |access-date=12 July 2021|date=June 30, 2021}}</ref>

Katznelson helped to launch the journal ''[[Politics & Society]]'' with Gerald Dorfman and others.  He was lead editor beginning with its first issue, which appeared in 1970. He was succeeded by [[Margaret Levi]].<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{cite web |title=Margaret Levi |url=https://profiles.stanford.edu/margaret-levi |website=Stanford University |access-date=9 August 2021}}</ref>
Katznelson was president of the [[American Political Science Association]] (APSA) in 2005 and 2006.<ref name="APA2007">{{cite journal |last1=Katznelson |first1=Ira |title=APSA Presidential Address: At the Court of Chaos: Political Science in an Age of Perpetual Fear |journal=Perspectives on Politics |date=2007 |volume=5 |issue=1 |pages=3–15 |url=http://www.jstor.org/stable/20446346 |access-date=12 July 2021}}</ref> He previously served as president of APSA's Politics and History Section in 1992 and 1993, and as president of the [[Social Science History Association]] in 1997 and 1998.<ref name="Utter"/> He has also been a [[Guggenheim Fellow]], and was elected as a fellow of the [[American Academy of Arts and Sciences]] in 2000 and the [[American Philosophical Society]] in 2004.<ref>{{Cite web|title=APS Member History|url=https://search.amphilsoc.org/memhist/search?creator=Ira+Katznelson&title=&subject=&subdiv=&mem=&year=&year-max=&dead=&keyword=&smode=advanced|access-date=2021-06-10|website=search.amphilsoc.org}}</ref> 
Katznelson has received [[honorary doctorates]] from the [[New School]] in 1994,<ref>{{cite news |title=Commencements; New School Confers Degrees on 1,433 |url=https://www.nytimes.com/1994/05/25/nyregion/commencements-new-school-confers-degrees-on-1433.html |access-date=12 July 2021 |work=The New York Times |date=May 25, 1994}}</ref>
[[Queens College]] in 2016,<ref>{{cite web |title=I.B.14 – QUEENS COLLEGE - RESOLUTION TO AWARD HONORARY DEGREES |url=http://policy.cuny.edu/policyimport/board_committee_documents/academic_policy,_programs_and_research/agendas/2016/04-04/i-b-14_queens_college_-_resolution_to_award_an_honorary_degree/document.pdf |website=I.B.14 – QUEENS COLLEGE |access-date=12 July 2021}}</ref> 
and the [[University of Cambridge]] in 2018.<ref>{{cite web |title=University of Cambridge honorary degrees 2018 |url=https://www.cam.ac.uk/news/university-of-cambridge-honorary-degrees-2018 |website=Cambridge University |access-date=12 July 2021}}</ref>

===Contributions===
Katznelson has written or co-written ten books, co-edited several others, and published over sixty journal articles. 
He questions "when and why liberal democracies become normatively appealing (less closed and more tolerant) and more effective (less vulnerable and more secure)."<ref name=":0"/> He is particularly interested in the connections and transitions between the political traditions of [[liberalism]] and [[republicanism in the United States]].<ref name="Kurtz">{{cite news |last1=Kurtz |first1=Geoffrey |title=From Liberalism to Social Democracy |journal=Dissent Magazine |date=August 26, 2009 |url=https://www.dissentmagazine.org/online_articles/from-liberalism-to-social-democracy |access-date=9 August 2021}}</ref> His work goes beyond the study of U.S. politics to include [[international relations]], [[political theory]], [[comparative politics]], and [[comparative history]].<ref name="Berman">{{cite journal |last1=Berman |first1=Sheri |title=The New Deal and U.S. Democracy A Discussion of ‘Fear Itself: The New Deal and the Origins of Our Time’ By Ira Katznelson |journal=Perspectives on Politics |date=2014 |volume=12 |issue=3 |pages=704–715 |url=https://www.jstor.org/stable/43281065 |access-date=9 August 2021}}</ref>

His book ''Liberalism’s Crooked Circle: Letters to [[Adam Michnik]]'' (1996) won [[American Political Science Association]]'s (APSA) [[Michael Harrington]] Prize. ''Desolation and Enlightenment'' (2003) won the David and Elaine Spitz Award of the Conference of Political Thought, given to the best book in liberal or democratic theory, and the [[David Easton]] Award of APSA's Foundations of Political Thought Section. In March 2014, Katznelson was awarded the [[Bancroft Prize]] for his book ''Fear Itself: The New Deal and the Origins of Our Time''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://library.columbia.edu/news/libraries/2014/2014-3-13_Bancroft_Winners_Announced.html|title=Winners of the 2014 Bancroft Prize Announced|date=March 13, 2014|publisher=Columbia University Libraries|access-date=2016-01-24}}</ref>
