'''Linda Papadopoulos''' (born May 10, 1971) is a Canadian [[psychologist]] based in [[England]]. {{cn|date=March 2020}}

==Career==
Papadopoulos was born in [[Toronto]], [[Ontario]], [[Canada]], on May 10, 1971. She earned a BA degree in [[Psychology]] from [[York University]] in [[Toronto|Toronto, Ontario]], Canada, an MSc in [[Health psychology]] from the [[University of Surrey]] in the United Kingdom and a [[PhD]] in Psychology from [[City University London|City University]] in [[London]].<ref>* [http://drlinda.live.subhub.com/categories/20070501 My Biography] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100219185325/http://drlinda.live.subhub.com/categories/20070501 |date=2010-02-19 }}, [http://www.drlinda.com/ DrLinda.com].</ref>

In 2010, she was commissioned by the [[Home Office]] to write a review on the effects of sexualisation on young people.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.gov.uk/crime-justice-and-law/crime-prevention|title=Crime prevention - GOV.UK|website=www.gov.uk}}</ref>

Papadopoulos has hosted the TV series ''My Naked Secret'' on the [[Discovery Fit & Health]] channel. The series told the personal stories of people who are hiding physical abnormalities. She is also a regular commentator on many true life crime and history programmes on UKTV channels <ref>[http://www.discoveryfitandhealth.com/tv-shows/questions-for-linda-papadopoulos-of-my-naked-secret.htm Questions for Linda Papadopoulos of My Naked Secret] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140810220739/http://www.discoveryfitandhealth.com/tv-shows/questions-for-linda-papadopoulos-of-my-naked-secret.htm |date=2014-08-10 }}, [[Discovery Fit & Health]], retrieved 29 July 2014</ref>
