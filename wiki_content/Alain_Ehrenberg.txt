'''Alain Ehrenberg''', born in Paris in 1950, is a French [[sociologist]].

Author of a PhD thesis of sociology ''Archangels, warriors, military men and sportsmen. Essay on the education of the strong man'',<ref>{{cite book |url=http://www.sudoc.abes.fr/xslt/DB=2.1/SET=1/TTL=21/SHW?FRST=30 |title=ARCHANGES, GUERRIERS, MILITAIRES ET SPORTIFS - ESSAI SUR L'EDUCATION DE L'HOMME FORT |language=french |author1=Ehrenberg, Alain |author2=Enriquez, Eugène. |year=1978}}</ref> he subsequently became interested in the anxieties of the individual in modern society, faced with the need for achievement and [[Individual autonomy|Autonomy]] and the loss of social signposts and support systems.

After being a researcher at the [[Edgar Morin Centre]], Ehrenberg founded and co-directed the research group "Psychotropics, Politics, Society" at the [[Centre national de la recherche scientifique|CNRS]]. He is currently the director of the "Psychotropics, Mental Health, Society" Research Center (Cesames).
