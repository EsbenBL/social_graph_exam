'''Kent L. Norman''' is an American [[cognitive psychology|cognitive psychologist]] and an expert on [[computer rage]]. He graduated from [[Southern Methodist University]] in 1969 and earned a [[Ph.D.]] in experimental psychology from the [[University of Iowa]] in 1973.<ref>https://nacs.umd.edu/facultyprofile/norman/kent</ref>

Norman was an associate professor of Psychology at the [[University of Maryland, College Park]].<ref name=":0" /> He retired January 2018.

In 1983, Norman co-founded the [[Laboratory for Automation Psychology|Laboratory for Automation Psychology and Decision Processes]] (LAPDP) as an affiliate of the [[University of Maryland Human-Computer Interaction Lab|Human-Computer Interaction Laboratory (HCIL)]][http://www.cs.umd.edu/hcil/members/fac-staff-admin.shtml] and the [[University of Maryland, College Park|University of Maryland]] Institute for Advanced Computer Studies (UMIACS).  The LAPDP studies the cognitive side of the human/[[computer interface]], with an emphasis on the processes of judgment and [[decision making]].  Research in the LAPDP has received corporate support from [[AT&T]], [[Sperry Corporation|Sperry]], and [[IBM]] and United States federal agency support from [[NASA]], [[National Science Foundation|NSF]], [[United States Naval Research Laboratory|NRL]], and the [[U.S. Census Bureau]].

Norman designed and wrote the [[HyperCourseware]][https://web.archive.org/web/20111030032745/http://cognitron.umd.edu/] [[software]] system, in 1990, for the preparation and presentation of materials and the processes of education in a [[virtual learning environment]].  HyperCourseware has been utilized in the [[multimedia]] Teaching Theaters at the [[University of Maryland, College Park]].

In 1997, Norman worked with the Center for the Design of Distance Education Methodology at the [[Open University of Israel]] and collaborated on new methods of [[Internet]] [[distance education]].
