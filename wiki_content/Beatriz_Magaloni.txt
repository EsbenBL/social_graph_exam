'''Beatriz Magaloni''' is a political scientist. She is a professor at the Department of Political Science and a Senior Fellow at the Freeman Spogli Institute for International Studies at [[Stanford University]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Dra. Beatriz Magaloni Kerpel |url=http://carreraaluniverso.itam.mx/muro/2005/semblanza_14.html |publisher=Instituto Tecnológico Autónomo de México |language=es}}</ref>

Magaloni graduated from [[Instituto Tecnológico Autónomo de México]] in law in 1989. She was awarded PhD in political science by [[Duke University]] in 1997. After her time as a visiting professor at [[University of California, Los Angeles]] and professor at Instituto Tecnológico Autónomo de México, she joined Stanford in 2001. In 2010, she founded the Poverty, Violence and Governance Lab at Stanford University, where she was the director as of 2021.<ref name="stanford">{{cite web |title=Beatriz Magaloni |url=https://politicalscience.stanford.edu/people/beatriz-magaloni |publisher=Stanford University}}</ref>

Her research interests include authoritatian regimes, violence, human rights, and distribution of public goods. It mainly concentrates on Latin America.<ref name="stanford"/><ref>{{cite web |title=Beatriz Magaloni |url=https://esoc.princeton.edu/about-us/people/beatriz-magaloni |publisher=[[Princeton University]]}}</ref> In particular, she conducted research in Brazil and Mexico.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Wenner |first1=Alice |title=Police Reform in Brazil and Mexico: What Works, What Doesn’t, and What the U.S. Can Learn |work=Stanford University |date=30 June 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal |title=Experiment in Rio: Pacification Units |journal=Stanford Magazine |date=1 November 2016 |url=https://stanfordmag.org/contents/experiment-in-rio-pacification-units}}</ref>

==Publications==
