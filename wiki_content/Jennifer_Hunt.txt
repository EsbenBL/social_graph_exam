'''Jennifer Hunt''' is a professor of economics at [[Rutgers University]].<ref name=RUT>{{cite web|title=Rutgers University Staff |url=http://economics.rutgers.edu/people/296-hunt-jennifer|accessdate=16 May 2013}}</ref> She previously served as deputy assistant secretary for microeconomic analysis at the [[U.S. Department of the Treasury]]<ref name=Politico>{{cite news|title=Politico Morning Money — April 11, 2014 |url=http://www.politico.com/morningmoney/0414/morningmoney13577.html|accessdate=6 May 2014 | work=Politico}}</ref> after serving a term as [[Chief economist|Chief Economist]] to the [[United States Secretary of Labor|U.S. Secretary of Labor]], serving under acting secretary [[Seth Harris]] and Secretary [[Thomas Perez]].<ref name=DOL>{{cite web|title=DOL Key Personnel |url=http://www.dol.gov/_sec/aboutosec/bio-hunt.htm |accessdate=16 May 2013 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130502023308/http://www.dol.gov/_sec/aboutosec/bio-hunt.htm |archivedate= 2 May 2013 }}</ref> She is a research associate at the [[National Bureau of Economic Research]].<ref name=NBER>{{cite web|title=NBER Research Associates and Faculty Research Fellows in Labor Studies |url=https://www.nber.org/programs/ls/ls-mem.html|accessdate=16 May 2013}}</ref> She has done research in the areas of employment and unemployment policy, immigration, wage inequality, transition economics, crime and corruption.<ref name=WaPo>{{cite news|title=Washington Post blog discussing research related immigration and its impact on wages |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/wonkblog/wp/2013/04/17/immigrations-effect-on-wages-definitely-positive-without-any-doubt-whatsoever/|accessdate=16 May 2013 | work=The Washington Post}}</ref> Her current research focuses on immigration and innovation in the United States, the U.S. science and engineering workforce, and the 2008-2009 recession in Germany. Her research on immigration has been cited by media in the context of [[Border Security, Economic Opportunity, and Immigration Modernization Act (S. 744; 113th Congress)|immigration reform legislation]], currently under consideration by the [[List of bills in the 113th United States Congress|U.S. Congress]].<ref name=NewRep>{{cite news | last1 = Clemens | first1 = Michael | last2 = Lynch | first2 = Robert  | title = Low-Wage Workers Will Benefit From Immigration Reform, Too | newspaper = New Republic | date = 29 April 2013 | url = https://newrepublic.com/article/113065/immigration-reform-low-wage-workers-will-benefit-too# | accessdate = 16 May 2013}}</ref><ref name=NatJo>{{cite news | last = Chokshi| first = Niraj | title = Why Immigration May Not Have a Big Impact on Wages | newspaper = National Journal | date = 2 May 2013 | url = http://www.nationaljournal.com/congress/why-immigration-may-not-have-a-big-impact-on-wages-20130502 | accessdate = 16 May 2013}}</ref>

==Education==

Jennifer Hunt graduated from the [[International School of Geneva]] with an [[International Baccalaureate]] degree in 1983. She then studied [[electrical engineering]] at [[Massachusetts Institute of Technology]], earning a [[Scientiæ Baccalaureus]] in 1987. She then switched fields and studied [[economics]] at [[Harvard University]], earning her [[Ph.D]] in 1992.<ref name=RUT2>{{cite web|title=Rutgers University CV |url=http://www.rci.rutgers.edu/~jah357/Hunt/Home_files/cv0113.pdf |accessdate=16 May 2013 }}{{dead link|date=April 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

==Academic appointments==

Jennifer Hunt began her academic career as an assistant professor at [[Yale University]] in 1992, becoming an associate professor in 1997. She moved to the [[University of Montreal]] in 2001 and [[McGill University]] in 2004. She has been a professor at Rutgers since 2011.<ref name=RUT2 />

Professor Hunt has also held the following visiting positions:
*[[Universitat Autònoma de Barcelona]], Institut d’Anàlisi Econòmica, July 2012<ref name=RUT2 />
*[[University of Milan]], Centro Studi Luco d’Agliano, June 2010<ref name=LDA>{{cite web|title=Centro Studi Luca d'Agliano|url=http://www.dagliano.unimi.it/pages/sus-div/|accessdate=16 May 2013}}</ref>
*[[University of British Columbia]], Department of Economics, 2008-2009<ref name=UBC>{{cite journal | last = Hunt | first = Jennifer | title = Which Immigrants Are Most Innovative and Entrepreneurial? *[[UCLA]], Department of Economics, Spring 2006
*[[Dartmouth College]], Department of Economics, Spring 2000
*[[German Institute for Economic Research]] (DIW-Berlin), SOEP Group, Fall 1999
*[[Stanford University]], Hoover Institution, National Fellow, 1995-1996<ref name=Stanford>{{cite web|title=Stanford University Affiliates |url=http://www.stanford.edu/group/scspi/cgi-bin/scspi_ds_get_affiliate_issue_page.php?affiliate_issue=Crime|accessdate=16 May 2013}}</ref>

Hunt is a Research Associate in Labor Studies at the [[National Bureau of Economic Research]],<ref name=NBER /> a Research Fellow at the [[Centre for Economic Policy Research]] in London,<ref name=CEPR>{{cite web|title=CEPR (the Centre for Economic Policy Research) Research Fellows|url=http://www.cepr.org/research/resfel_long.asp|accessdate=16 May 2013}}</ref> and is on the Scientific Advisory Council of the American Institute for Contemporary German Studies<ref name=AICGS>{{cite web|title=American Institute for Contemporary German Studies Research Fellows|url=http://www.aicgs.org/by-author/jennifer-hunt/|accessdate=16 May 2013}}</ref> in Washington D.C. and the Institut für Arbeitsmarkt- und Berufsforschung in Nuremberg.<ref name=IAB>{{cite web|title=Institut für Arbeitsmarkt- und Berufsforschung der Bundesagentur für Arbeit: Wissenschaftlicher Beirat (Scientific Advisory Council) |url=http://www.iab.de/798/section.aspx |archive-url=https://archive.is/20130701012044/http://www.iab.de/798/section.aspx |url-status=dead |archive-date=1 July 2013 |accessdate=16 May 2013 }}</ref>

Since 2012, Hunt has served as an Associate Editor of the ''[[Journal of Labor Economics]]''.<ref name=JLE>{{cite web|title=Journal of Labor Economics Editorial Board |url=http://www.press.uchicago.edu/journals/jole/board.html?journal=jole|accessdate=16 May 2013}}</ref> She is also an Editor of the ''[[Journal of Comparative Economics]]''.<ref name=IDEAS>{{cite web|title=Citations Database, Federal Reserve Bank of St. Louis |url=https://ideas.repec.org/e/c/phu9.html=jole|accessdate=16 May 2013}}</ref>

==Research awards==
Hunt's paper, "How Much Does Immigration Boost Innovation?" (with coauthor Marjolaine Gauthier-Loiselle) won the [[American Economic Journal]] Best Paper in Macroeconomics Prize in 2013.<ref name=AEJ>{{cite web|title=American Economic Journal Best Papers|url=http://www.aeaweb.org/honors_awards/aej_best_papers.php|accessdate=29 May 2013}}</ref> Hunt was honored in 2012 as the keynote speaker at [[Verein für Socialpolitik]] annual conference, Göttingen. She was also keynote speaker at INSIDE third annual immigration conference, Barcelona, 2009, and Innis Lecturer at the Canadian Economics Association Meetings, 2004. In 2001 she received the DAAD Prize for Distinguished Scholarship in German Studies,<ref name=DAAD>{{cite web|title=DAAD Prize for Distinguished Scholarship in German and European Studies |url=https://www.daad.org/archive07-08 |accessdate=16 May 2013 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150619230357/https://www.daad.org/archive07-08 |archivedate=19 June 2015 }}</ref> and she was awarded "Best Paper Using the German Socio-Economic Panel 1984-99, scholar under 35," in 2000.
