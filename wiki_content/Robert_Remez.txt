'''Robert Remez''',  an American experimental [[psychology|psychologist]] and   [[cognitive science|cognitive scientist]],  is Professor of Psychology at [[Barnard College]], [[Columbia University]] and Chair of the Columbia University Seminar on Language & Cognition (founded in 2000).<ref name=bio>{{Cite web|url=http://www.columbia.edu/~remez/index/BiographicalSketch.html|title=Robert E. Remez Biographical Sketch|website=Columbia University|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180709142040/http://www.columbia.edu/~remez/index/BiographicalSketch.html|archive-date=July 9, 2018|access-date=July 9, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.columbia.edu/~remez/LC/langcog/LanguageCognition.html|title=Language & Cognition|website=Columbia University|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180709142312/http://www.columbia.edu/~remez/LC/langcog/LanguageCognition.html|archive-date=July 9, 2018|access-date=July 9, 2018}}</ref> His teaching focuses on the relationships between cognition, perception and language.<ref name=bio/> He is best known for his theoretical and experimental work on [[perception|perceptual organization]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.blackwellreference.com/public/tocnode?id=g9780631229278_chunk_g97806312292785|title=2. Perceptual Organization of Speech|website=Blackwell Reference Online|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180709142601/http://www.blackwellreference.com/public/tocnode?id=g9780631229278_chunk_g97806312292785|archive-date=July 9, 2018|access-date=July 9, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.sciencedirect.com/science?_ob=ArticleURL&_udi=B6V1C-3VCVPNH-5&_user=10&_rdoc=1&_fmt=&_orig=search&_sort=d&view=c&_acct=C000050221&_version=1&_urlVersion=0&_userid=10&md5=005c143db745cdcfdc646c5513bdcff9|title=Multimodal perceptual organization of speech: Evidence from tone analogs of spoken utterances|website=Science Direct|archive-url=https://archive.today/20130201222355/http://www.sciencedirect.com/science?_ob=ArticleURL&_udi=B6V1C-3VCVPNH-5&_user=10&_rdoc=1&_fmt=&_orig=search&_sort=d&view=c&_acct=C000050221&_version=1&_urlVersion=0&_userid=10&md5=005c143db745cdcfdc646c5513bdcff9|archive-date=2013-02-01|url-status=dead|access-date=July 8, 2018}}</ref> and [[speech perception]].<ref>{{Cite book|title=The Handbook of Speech Perception|last1=Remez|first1=Robert E.|last2=Pisoni|first2=David B.|publisher=Blackwell Publishing|year=2004|isbn=9780631229278}}</ref>

With [[Carol Fowler]], [[Philip Rubin]], and [[Michael Turvey]], he introduced the consideration of speech in terms of a dynamical systems/action theory perspective.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.haskins.yale.edu/Reprints/HL0295.pdf|title=Implications for Speech Production of a General Theory of Action|website=Yale University|access-date=July 9, 2018}}</ref> With Rubin and various other colleagues, he has used the technique of [[sinewave synthesis]] as a unique tool for exploring perceptual organization.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.haskins.yale.edu/MISC/SWS/SWS.html|title=Implications for Speech Production of a General Theory of Action|website=Yale University|access-date=July 9, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.haskins.yale.edu/Reprints/HL0889.pdf|title=On the Perceptual Organization of Speech|website=Yale University|access-date=July 9, 2018}}</ref> He is the co-editor, with David Pisoni, of the ''Handbook of Speech Perception''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.blackwellreference.com/public/book?id=g9780631229278_9780631229278|title=The Handbook of Speech Perception|website=Blackwell Publishing|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180709142850/http://www.blackwellreference.com/public/book?id=g9780631229278_9780631229278|archive-date=July 9, 2018|access-date=July 9, 2018}}</ref> He was the Ann Olin Whitney Professor and former Chair of the Department of Psychology at [[Barnard College]] and is a member of the Board of Directors of [[Haskins Laboratories]].

Remez is a graduate of [[Brandeis University]] and the [[University of Connecticut]].<ref name=bio/>
