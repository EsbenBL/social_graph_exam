'''Hans-Werner Gessmann''' (born 24 March 1950, in [[Duisburg]]) is a German psychologist, founder of humanistic psychodrama and university teacher in Russia, one of the best known psychotherapists worldwide.

== Career ==
Gessmann received a doctorate in 1976 by his work about causation factors of [[Dyslexia]]. He is the founder of humanistic [[psychodrama]]<ref>{{citation |last= Saaman |first=Wolfgang |year=1989 |title=Auf dem Weg zur Organisation von Morgen |work=Poeschel Verlag |volume=15 |page=221 |postscript= .}}</ref><ref>{{citation |last1=Bottenberg |first1=E.H. |last2=Daßler |first2=H. |title=Wege in die Psychologie des Gefühls |work=Roderer Verlag Regensburg |year=1997 |page=164 |postscript= .}}</ref> and adopted in the same year <ref>{{citation |last=Gessmann |first=Hans-Werner |year=1984 |title=Bausteine zur Gruppenpsychotherapie, Band 1 |work=Jungjohann Verlag Neckarsulm |pages=252–4 |postscript= .}}</ref> for the first time psychodrama with [[hypnosis]] in the context of sexual dysfunctions. In the last twenty years he concentrated on diagnosis and therapy of transgender people and is in Germany accepted as a court expert. <ref>Транс-идентичность – стандарты диагностики и лечения. Психологическая наука и образование psyedu.ru. 2014. Т.6. №3. С.160–167. URL:psyjournals.ru/psyedu_ru/2014/n3/71377.shtml (дата обращения: 31.10.2014)</ref> He also established humanistic psychodrama in the field of [[child psychotherapy]]. In the early 80s he began making film-documentations about complete group-psychotherapy-sessions in European countries.<ref>{{citation |last=Buer |first=Ferdinand  |year=1992 |title=Jahrbuch für Psychodrama, psychosoziale Praxis & Gesellschaftspolitik |work=Opladen |pages=252–4 |postscript= .}}</ref> He is the publisher of book series and the International Journal of Humanistic Psychodrama.<ref name="auto">{{citation |last= Benesch |first= Hellmuth |title= Enzyklopädisches Wörterbuch Klinische Psychologie und Psychotherapie |location= Beltz |publisher= Psychologische Verlags Union |year= 1995 |page= 303 |postscript= .}}</ref><ref>{{citation |editor1-last= Grubitzsch |editor1-first= Siegfried |editor2-last= Weber |editor2-first= Klaus |title= Psychologische Grundbegriffe Ein Handbuch |work= Rowohlts Enzyklopädie Rororo |year= 1998 |pages= 451–2 |postscript= .}}</ref> Gessmann is one of the few empirical researchers in the field of psychodrama<ref>{{citation |editor1-last= Fürst |editor1-first= Jutta |editor2-last= Ottomeyer |editor2-first= Klaus |editor3-last= Pruckner |editor3-first= Hildegard |year= 2004 |title= Psychodrama-Therapie - Ein Handbuch |publisher= Facultas Wien |page= 441 |postscript= .}}</ref> and has published over 180 articles on psychological topics.<ref>{{cite web |title= Literature by H-W. Gessmann |url= http://www.pib-zentrum.de/wir-ueber-uns/hans-werner-gessmann/veroeffentlichungen.html |access-date= 2013-01-08 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20130913160940/http://www.pib-zentrum.de/wir-ueber-uns/hans-werner-gessmann/veroeffentlichungen.html |archive-date= 2013-09-13 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Educational films by H-W. Gessmann|url=http://www.pib-zentrum.de/wir-ueber-uns/hans-werner-gessmann/lehrfilm-produktionen.html |access-date=2013-01-08 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130913160856/http://www.pib-zentrum.de/wir-ueber-uns/hans-werner-gessmann/lehrfilm-produktionen.html |archive-date=2013-09-13 |url-status=dead}}</ref> Standards continued his research on the method of doubling from 1996.<ref>Empirische Untersuchung der therapeutischen Wirksamkeit der Doppelmethode im Humanistischen Psychodrama. In: Humanistisches Psychodrama Band 4, (Hrsg.), Publisher of the Psychotherapeutic Institute Bergerhausen, Duisburg, 1996</ref> He is the only author in the German-speaking area, who handled this issue fundamentally and scientifically, including translations of the complete source texts from English.<ref>Humanistisches Psychodrama Band 4, (Hrsg.), Verlag des PIB, Duisburg, 1;1996</ref>

In 1973 he founded the [[Psychotherapeutisches Institut Bergerhausen|Psychotherapeutisches Institut Bergerhausen]] (PIB) in Duisburg and in 1977 nearby [[Kerpen]], Germany<ref>{{citation |last= Meynen |first= Henriette |year= 1980 |title= Wasserburgen, Schlösser und Landsitze im Erftkreis |work= Rheinland Verlag Köln |page= 96 |postscript= .}}</ref> a European training center now for more than 1500 psychotherapists.

In 1986, Gessmann founded Germany's first home-based sleep laboratory with the Department of Sleep Medicine. From 1998 to 2002, under his guidance, the tongue muscle training with the registered trademark ZMT <sup> ® </sup> was developed as an alternative treatment against sleep apnea.<ref>H.-W. Gessmann: ''Das Zungenmuskel-Training.'' Verlag des Psychotherapeutischen Instituts Bergerhausen, Duisburg 2001.</ref><ref>W. Randerath, W. Galetke, K.-H. Rühle, C. Rocholl: ''Zungenmuskel-Training durch intraorale Elektrostimulation bei obstruktiver Schlafapnoe.'' In: ''Am J Respir Critical Care Med.'' 167, Suppl. 1, A327 (2003)</ref><ref>Th. Verse: ''Transkutane Elektrostimulationstherapie bei obstruktiver Schlafapnoe.'' IN: ''MedReport.'' Blackwell Verlag Nr. 32, 26 Jahrgang Berlin, September 2002.</ref>

Gessmann developed and has taught humanistic psychodrama since 1979 as a new form of psychodrama. It became an integral part of [[humanistic psychology]].<ref>{{citation |last=Färber |first=Markus |title=Einleitung in die Humanistische Psychologie und Humanistisches Psychodrama. In: Humanistisches Psychodama Band 4 |publisher=Verlag des PIB Duisburg |year=1996 |pages=13–19 |postscript= .}}</ref>  Gessmann moved the collective action and nature of the people at the center of the therapeutic ethic.<ref>{{citation |last=Gessmann |first=Hans-Werner |year=1996 |title=Humanistische Psychologie und Humanistisches Psychodrama. In: Humanistisches Psychodama Band 4 |publisher=Verlag des PIB Duisburg |pages=27–84 |postscript= .}}</ref>  Faith, love, hope, and the idea of a human community are essential to meaningful humanistic psychodrama.<ref>{{citation |last=Gessmann |first=Hans-Werner |date=2007 |title=Humanistic Psychodrama. In: International Journal of Humanistic Psychodrama |publisher=Verlag des PIB Duisburg |page=8 |postscript= .}}</ref> Intuitive look for detecting the wholeness of a thing, the dialectical conception of polarities, the renunciation of absolute authority, take decisive influence on the image and life of man. The goals and methods of classical psychodrama were necessarily re-evaluated and described.<ref name="auto"/> This led to a fundamental change in international psychodrama practice.<ref name="auto"/> Through his work in many countries and with many different people of different ages, he was able to gain extensive experience, which in turn was very helpful for his future work. He understand new and strange situations and people faster and better. Working with different multicultural people taught him more and more that there is no single type of real life, but that there are an infinite number of individual ways of life and that every person has the right to decide for himself how he would like to shape his life. Good empathy and accepting knowledge of the difference between different people is a crucial prerequisite for successful therapeutic work. The humanistic image of man is guiding him and his work.

Gessmann is a member of the American Society of Group Psychotherapy and Psychodrama as well as the International Association of Group Psychotherapy and Psychodrama since 1977.

Gessmann teaches clinical psychology in the faculty of social psychology of the {{Interlanguage link multi|State University Kostroma|ru|3=Костромской государственный университет}} [[Kostroma]] (KSU) since 2007 and is a professor of general and developmental psychology in the governmental Academy of Social Administration Moscow (ASOU).

After introduction of the test for medical study courses (TMS, colloquially also medicine test) 1986 this was reconstructed for training in the original by Gessmann several times. These training versions are published under the title Ü-TMS.<ref>Übungslehrbuch zum psychologischen Test für das Studium der Medizin, Zahnmedizin und Tiermedizin. (Ü-PTM 14). Jungjohann Verlag, Neckarsulm 1981.</ref>

In April 2011 it was announced he would be the director of the [http://www.iccpp.org/?lang=en International Centre for Clinical Psychology and Psychotherapy (ICCPP)] at the governmental University Kostroma with a focus on psychotherapy training and research, particularly humanistic psychodrama and systemic family therapy. In March 2020 he changed his activity from Kostroma to the State Medical Medicine University in Krasnoyarsk. He started to work here in cooperation together with the International Center of Clinical Psychology and Psychotherapy<ref name="auto2">{{Cite web|url=https://krasgmu.ru/|title=КрасГМУ|website=КрасГМУ}}</ref> The ICCPP <ref name="auto1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.iccpp.org/|title=Startseite - ICCPP}}</ref> was growing fast, working in more than 30 countries with over 200 specialists.<ref name="auto1"/>

Since February 2012, he has been a professor of [[Systemic Therapy|systemic family therapy]] and [[Psychodrama|humanistic psychodrama]] at the {{Interlanguage link multi|Moscow State Pedagogical Psychological University (MGPPU)|ru|3=МГППУ}}. With the beginning of 2013 Gessmann is a visiting professor at the State University Smolensk. Here he teaches Humanistic Psychodrama. In the same year Gessmann receives a call to the Chair for Humanistic Psychodrama at the [[Southeast University|Southeast University in Nanjing]] province Jiansu, one of the oldest universities in China. He was added from Stanford University U.C. in Berkeley to the list of the 30 most influential psychologists working today<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.bestmastersinpsychology.com/30-most-influential-psychologists-working-today/ |title=30 Most Influential Psychologists Working Today |author=Rob |work=bestmastersinpsychology.com}}</ref> and from the Harvard university to the list of 30 most influential living counseling psychologists. <ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.bestcounselingdegrees.net/resources/influential-counseling-psychologists/|title=30 Most Influential Counseling Psychologists Alive Today|date=25 September 2014}}</ref>  In September 2017, Hans-Werner Gessmann introduced group psychotherapy in Azerbaijan for the first time. 141 psychotherapists from the country took part in the event in Baku. 2019 Member of the International Organizing Committee of the VI. International Scientific and practical Conference at the [[Staatliche Medizinische Universität Krasnojarsk|State Krasnoyarsk Medical University Prof. V.F. Volno-Yasenetzky]].<ref name="auto2"/> Working as a longterm visiting professor for Clinical psychology and psychotherapy since March 2020 at the [[Staatliche Medizinische Universität Krasnojarsk|State Krasnoyarsk Medical University Prof. V.F. Volno-Yasenetzky]]. May 2021 Adjunctive Professor at the Madras Social Work Academy for Humanistic Psychology. June 2021 Appointment as head of the department of psychology at DMI - St. John the Baptist University in Malawi / Central Africa. 2021-2022 International Member of the American Psychological Association (APA).
