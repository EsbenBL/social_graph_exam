'''Gregory Clark''' (born 19 September 1957) is an [[economic history|economic historian]] at the [[University of California, Davis]].

==Biography==

Clark, whose grandfathers were migrants to Scotland from Ireland, was born in Bellshill, Scotland. He attended [[Holy Cross High School, Hamilton|Holy Cross High School in Hamilton]]. In 1974 he and a fellow pupil Paul Fitzpatrick won the [[Daily Express|Scottish Daily Express]] schools debating competition. After school he earned his B.A. in economics and philosophy at [[King's College, Cambridge|King's College]], [[University of Cambridge|Cambridge]] in 1979 and his PhD at [[Harvard]] in 1985. He has also taught as an Assistant Professor at [[Stanford]] and the [[University of Michigan]].

Clark is now a professor of economics, and was (until 2013) department chair, at the [[University of California, Davis]]. His areas of research are long term economic growth, the wealth of nations, the [[economic history]] of [[England]] and [[India]], and social mobility.<ref>[http://www.econ.ucdavis.edu/faculty/gclark/index.html Gregory Clark – Professor of Economics Homepage]</ref>

In 2021, a talk by Clark, titled "For Whom the Bell Curve Tolls: A Lineage of 400,000 Individuals 1750-2020 Shows Genetics Determines Most Social Outcomes", was cancelled due to accusations of promoting eugenics.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Horne|first1=Marc|title=Glasgow University in wokeism row for cancelling ‘eugenics’ talk|url=https://www.thetimes.co.uk/article/glasgow-university-in-wokeism-row-for-cancelling-eugenics-talk-38p5lxkcq|work=The Times|issue=25 February 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Haugh|first1=Jack|title=Glasgow University in row over decision to invite guest speaker Gregory Clark|url=https://www.glasgowtimes.co.uk/news/19120844.glasgow-university-row-decision-invite-guest-speaker-gregory-clark/|work=Glasgow Times|issue=26 February 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last1=Horne|first1=Marc|title=Academics led the campaign to silence genetics Professor Gregory Clark on race|url=https://www.thetimes.co.uk/article/glasgow-university-in-wokeism-row-for-cancelling-eugenics-talk-38p5lxkcq|work=The Times|issue=6 March 2021}}</ref>
