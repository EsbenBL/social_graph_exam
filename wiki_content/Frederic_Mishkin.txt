'''Frederic Stanley "Rick" Mishkin''' (born January 11, 1951) is an [[Americans|American]] [[economist]] and Alfred Lerner professor of Banking and Financial Institutions  at the Graduate School of Business, Columbia University. He was a member of the Board of Governors of the [[Federal Reserve System]] from 2006 to 2008.

==Early life and family==
Mishkin was born in [[New York City]] to Sidney Mishkin (b. 1913, d. 1991) and Jeanne Silverstein. His late father endowed the Mishkin Gallery at [[Baruch College]] of the [[City University of New York]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://weissman.baruch.cuny.edu/arts-and-culture-at-weissman/mishkin-gallery/about/ |title=Mishkin Gallery |last= |first=  |date= |website=Baruch College, Weissman School of Arts & Sciences |access-date=29 December 2020}}</ref> He attended [[Ethical Culture Fieldston School|Fieldston School]], then received a B.S. (1973) and Ph.D. (1976), both in [[economics]], from the [[Massachusetts Institute of Technology]]. His doctoral advisor was [[Stanley Fischer]].<ref>[http://library.mit.edu/F/SJ1HDLIQPVFYUSS7GBCL9959IV7DSJ6TMKTKVPU12CLNL1Q91C-00706?func=find-acc&acc_sequence=010962894 Illiquidity, the demand for consumer durables, and monetary policy.]</ref> In 1999, he received an honorary professorship from the [[Renmin University of China|People's (Renmin) University of China]]. He is married to Sally Hammond, a landscape designer. They have a son and a daughter.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.columbia.edu/cu/record/archives/vol19/vol19_iss25/record1925.15 |title=Mishkin named to Federal Reserve |last= |first=  |date=22 April 1884 |website=Columbia University Record |access-date=29 December 2020}}</ref>

==Career==
Mishkin has been a full professor at [[Columbia Business School]] since 1983. He held the A. Barton Hepburn Professorship of Economics from 1991 to 1999, when he was appointed Alfred Lerner Professor of Banking and Financial Institutions. He was also a research associate at the [[National Bureau of Economic Research]] (1980 to 2006) and a senior fellow at the [[Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation]]'s Center for Banking Research (2003 to 2006). Dr. Mishkin was also a professor at the [[University of Chicago]] (1976-1983), a [[visiting professor]] at [[Northwestern University]] (1982-1983), and visiting professor at [[Princeton University]] (1990-1991).<ref name="fedbio">{{cite web|url=http://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/mishkin.htm|title=FRB Biography of Mishkin |publisher=frb.gov|accessdate=2008-07-07 |archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20080223092243/http://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/bios/board/mishkin.htm <!-- Bot retrieved archive --> |archivedate = 2008-02-23}}</ref>

From 1994 to 1997 Mishkin was Executive Vice President and Director of Research at the [[Federal Reserve Bank of New York]] and an Associate Economist of the Federal Open Market Committee of the Federal Reserve System. Dr. Mishkin was the editor of the Federal Reserve Bank of New York's Economic Policy Review and later served on that journal's editorial board. From 1997 to 2006, he also was an academic consultant to and served on the Economic Advisory Panel of the Federal Reserve Bank of New York.  Mishkin has been an academic consultant to the Board of Governors and a visiting scholar at the Board's Division of International Finance.<ref name="fedbio"/>

Mishkin has been a consultant to the [[World Bank]], the [[Inter-American Development Bank]], and the [[International Monetary Fund]], as well as to numerous central banks throughout the world. He was also a member of the International Advisory Board to the Financial Supervisory Service of South Korea and an adviser to the Institute for Monetary and Economic Research at the [[Bank of Korea]].<ref name="fedbio"/>

In 2006 Mishkin co-authored a report called ''Financial Stability in Iceland''.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.chamber.is/news.asp?id=555&news_ID=484&type=one |title=Archived copy |access-date=2007-09-25 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070726131319/http://www.chamber.is/news.asp?id=555&news_ID=484&type=one |archive-date=2007-07-26 |url-status=dead }}</ref> The report maintained that Iceland's economic fundamentals were strong. The report was commissioned by the Icelandic Chamber of Commerce in response to critical coverage of the Icelandic economy and certain Icelandic companies in the international business media.<ref name="fedbio"/> Mishkin was paid $124,000 to co-author the report.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://ftalphaville.ft.com/blog/2010/08/25/325376/mishkins-very-own-icelandic-blow-up/ |title=Mishkin's very own Icelandic blow-up |access-date=2007-09-25 |website=Financial Times|archive-date=}}</ref>

Two and a half years later, Iceland experienced a [[2008–2012 Icelandic financial crisis|spectacular financial collapse]]. According to the documentary film ''[[Inside Job (2010 film)|Inside Job]]'', the title of the report was changed to ''Financial Instability in Iceland'' on Mishkin's ''[[curriculum vitae]]'' (CV). Mishkin's CV was later corrected to list the report with its original title.<ref>Verified on 20 March 2011 at [http://www0.gsb.columbia.edu/faculty/fmishkin/cv.html Mishkin CV online at Columbia University].</ref> Mishkin wrote a note published on October 6, 2010 at the Financial Times' blog <ref>[http://blogs.ft.com/economistsforum/2010/10/the-economists-reply-to-the-inside-job/# The economist’s reply to the ''Inside Job'', October 8, 2010]</ref> explaining his participation in the documentary ''Inside Job''.  The director of ''Inside Job'', [[Charles H. Ferguson|Charles Ferguson]], responded to Mishkin's note at the same blog.<ref>[http://blogs.ft.com/economistsforum/2010/10/the-director-of-inside-job-replies/#axzz1UJ5C49lj The director of 'Inside Job' replies October 14, 2010]</ref>

Mishkin was confirmed as a member of the Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve on September 5, 2006 to fill an unexpired term ending January 31, 2014.<ref name="fedbio"/>  On May 28, 2008, in the middle of the financial collapse, he submitted his resignation from the Board of Governors, effective August 31, 2008, in order to  revise his textbook and resume his teaching duties at [[Columbia Business School]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.federalreserve.gov/newsevents/press/other/other20080528a1.pdf|title=Resignation Letter|publisher=frb.gov|accessdate=2008-07-07}}</ref>

==Authorships and publications==
Mishkin's research focuses on [[monetary policy]] and its impact on [[financial market]]s and the aggregate economy. He is the author of more than fifteen books and has published numerous articles in professional journals and books.  Mishkin has served on the editorial board of the ''[[American Economic Review]]'' and has been an associate editor at the ''Journal of Business and Economic Statistics'', the ''Journal of Applied Econometrics'', and the ''[[Journal of Economic Perspectives]]''. He was an associate editor (member of the editorial board) at the ''Journal of Money, Credit and Banking, Macroeconomics and Monetary Economics Abstracts'', ''Journal of International Money and Finance'', ''International Finance'', and ''Finance India''.

Mishkin is the author of the textbook ''Economics of Money, Banking, and Financial Markets (What's New in Economics)'' (12th edition) {{ISBN|978-0134733821}}
