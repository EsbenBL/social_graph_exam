'''Nancy Bermeo''' is an American political scientist, and senior research fellow at [[Nuffield College]], [[University of Oxford]].  She previously held the position of Nuffield Chair of Comparative Politics at Oxford.

Bermeo won the Stanley Kelley Teaching Prize at [[Princeton University]] in 1998 and the Oxford University Excellence in Teaching Award in 2009.<ref name="Nuffield College Bio">{{cite web|title=Prof Nancy Bermeo|url=https://www.nuffield.ox.ac.uk/People/sites/Nancy.Bermeo/SitePages/Biography.aspx|website=Nuffield College|accessdate=31 May 2017}}</ref><ref name="theigc1">{{cite web|url=http://www.theigc.org/person/nancy-bermeo/ |title=Nancy Bermeo |publisher=IGC |date=2016-06-14 |accessdate=2017-05-31}}</ref>

Bermeo has a PhD from [[Yale University]].<ref name="Nuffield College Bio"/>

She has been Nuffield Chair of Comparative Politics at [[Nuffield College]], [[University of Oxford]], since 2007.<ref name="theigc1"/>

Bermeo is of Ecuadorian, Irish, and Danish heritage.
	 
==Selected publications==
*''Continuity and Crisis: Popular Reactions to the Great Recession'' (ed. with [[Larry Bartels]] 2013)
*''Coping with Crisis: Government Reactions to the Great Recession'' (ed. with [[Jonas Pontusson]] 2012)
*''Ordinary People in Extraordinary Times: The Citizenry and the Collapse of Democracy'', [[Princeton University Press]], 2003.
*''The Revolution within the Revolution: Workers' Control in Rural Portugal'', Princeton University Press, 1986.
