'''Charles Raymond Adrian''' (March 12, 1922 – May 28, 2004) was an American professor of [[political science]] who specialized in municipal politics.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://articles.latimes.com/1988-12-08/news/ve-1479_1_recall-drive|newspaper=[[Los Angeles Times]]|title=Fizzling of Local Political Recall Drives Reflects Trend in State|date=December 8, 1988|first=Tracey|last=Kaplan}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
In December 1924, Adrian's mother sued his father for divorce and custody of Adrian and his sister, Marian.<ref>{{Cite news|title=Legal Notices|newspaper=[[Davenport Democrat and Leader]]|url=https://newspaperarchive.com/us/iowa/davenport/davenport-democrat-and-leader/1924/12-01/page-5|date=December 1, 1924|page=5|via=[[newspaperarchive.com]]}}</ref> After his parents' divorce Adrian relocated with his mother and sister to his mother's hometown of [[Davenport, Iowa]].{{Sfn|Erickson|1998|pp=2–3}} Having been dissuaded against pursuing music while still a high school student, Adrian pursued [[political science]] at [[Cornell College]]. With the US entrance into [[World War II]], Adrian enlisted in the [[United States Army Air Forces]] in 1943 where he served in the [[Air Force Weather Agency|Weather Wing]].{{Sfn|Erickson|1998|pp=5–6}} After his military service ended in 1946, Adrian returned to Cornell College, graduating in 1947 with a Bachelor's degree.<ref name="sun"/> Adrian was elected a member of [[Phi Beta Kappa]] at Cornell.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.cornellcollege.edu/phi-beta-kappa/documents-data/members-of-phi-beta-kappa.shtml|title=Members of Phi Beta Kappa|first=Richard|last=Jacob|website=[[Cornell College]]}}</ref> Adrian met his future wife Audrey Nelson, a [[euphonium]] player, at a concert in Iowa.<ref name="sun"/> Her father was a physics professor at Cornell. They married in 1946.{{Sfn|Erickson|1998|pp=7–8}} Adrian immediately enrolled at the [[University of Minnesota]] to study [[urban politics]] under [[William Anderson (political scientist)|William Anderson]].{{Sfn|Erickson|1998|pp=3–4}} He completed his master's degree in 1948 and his doctorate in 1950. Adrian accepted a post-doctoral fellowship in 1954 from the [[Ford Foundation]] and attended the [[University of Copenhagen]] to study the developing [[welfare state]] there.{{Sfn|Erickson|1998|pp=6–7}}

Adrian started teaching at [[Wayne State University|Wayne University]] in 1949. After returning from his post-doctoral fellowship in the Summer of 1955 he started teaching at [[Michigan State University]] in 1955.{{Sfn|Erickson|1998|pp=10–11}} Through 1956 and 1957 he served as an administrative assistant to [[G. Mennen Williams]], then [[Governor of Michigan]].<ref name="scotmail"/>{{Sfn|Erickson|1998|p=11}} During his time in Michigan Adrian wrote two articles, ''Some General Characteristics of Nonpartisan Elections'' and ''A Typology for Nonpartisan Elections,'' that are considered the seminal works in the study of non-partisanship.<ref>{{Cite journal|doi=10.1177/1065912907311743|journal=[[Political Research Quarterly]]|date=March 2008|volume=61|issue=1|pages=13–16|title=Charles Adrian and the Study of Nonpartisan Elections|first=Gerald C.|last=Wright|s2cid=153918973|quote=While the literature on nonpartisan elections and their effects is not large, virtually all of it acknowledges Charles Adrian's work as the first serious empirical effort to understand its consequences}}</ref><ref>{{Cite journal|title=Electoral Competition and Electoral Systems in Large Cities|author1-first=Charles E.|author1-last=Gilbert|author2-first=Christopher|author2-last=Clague|journal=[[The Journal of Politics]]|volume=24|issue=2|date=May 1962|publisher=[[Southern Political Science Association]]|jstor=2127894|page=330|doi=10.2307/2127894|quote=The starting points in any analysis of nonpartisanship must be the writings of Charles Adrian.}}</ref> Adrian wrote a third, ''The Origin of Minnesota's Nonpartisan Legislature,'' specifically discussing the "political accident" of nonpartisanship in the [[Minnesota Legislature]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://collections.mnhs.org/MNHistoryMagazine/articles/33/v33i04p155-163.pdf|title=The Origin of Minnesota's Nonpartisan Legislature|publisher=[[Minnesota Historical Society]]|date=Winter 1952|access-date=March 2, 2014|last=Adrian|first=Charles R.}}</ref> Adrian was promoted to associate professor in 1957 and again to chair of the political science department in 1963.<ref>{{Cite journal|title=Appointments and Staff Changes|journal=[[The American Political Science Review]]|volume=51|issue=3|pages=905–912|date=September 1957|publisher=[[American Political Science Association]]|jstor=1951893|doi=10.1017/S0003055400071926|doi-access=free}}</ref><ref>{{Cite journal|title=Appointments and Staff Changes|journal=[[The American Political Science Review]]|volume=57|issue=4|pages=1055–1067|date=December 1963|publisher=[[American Political Science Association]]|jstor=1952634|doi=10.1017/S0003055400283512|doi-access=free}}</ref>

Adrian was recruited by fellow University of Minnesota-alum [[Ivan Hinderaker]], the Chancellor of [[University of California, Riverside]] (UCR), to join the faculty.{{Sfn|Erickson|1998|pp=13–14}} Adrian served as chair of the political science department from 1966 until his retirement on June 30, 1988 as the effects of his diabetes hampered his ability to teach.<ref name="scotmail"/>{{Sfn|Erickson|1998|pp=46–47}} Adrian's 1977 book, ''Governing Urban America,'' was considered the most cited textbook in the field of [[Urban politics in the United States|urban politics]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.cornellcollege.edu/cornell_report/issues/summer2004/news.shtml|title=In Memoriam|website=[[Cornell College]]|date=July 15, 2008|access-date=2014-12-24|archive-url=https://archive.is/20141224015001/http://www.cornellcollege.edu/cornell_report/issues/summer2004/news.shtml#|archive-date=2014-12-24|url-status=dead}}</ref>

Adrian was a member of the [[American Political Science Association]], the [[American Society for Public Administration]] and the [[American Association of University Professors]].<ref name="scotmail"/>
