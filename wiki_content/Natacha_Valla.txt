'''Natacha Valla''' (born January 1, 1976) is a French economist and Dean of the [[Sciences Po]] School of Management and Innovation.<ref name=":10">{{Cite news|url=https://www.sciencespo.fr/en/news/news/natacha-valla-new-dean-of-the-school-of-management-and-innovation/4890|title=Natacha Valla, New Dean of the School of Management and Innovation|date=2020-05-12|access-date=2020-02-02|language=en}}</ref> She was Deputy Director General for monetary policy at the [[European Central Bank]] from May 2018 to January 2020. She is also chair of the [[SUERF]], European Money and Finance forum, editorial board.

== Background and education ==
Natacha Valla was born in [[Marseille]], France.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://www.lemonde.fr/economie/article/2018/10/01/natacha-valla-a-la-bce-l-ambition-tranquille_5362780_3234.html|title=Natacha Valla à la BCE : l'ambition tranquille|date=2018-10-01|access-date=2019-03-30|language=fr}}</ref> When she was six, her family moved to [[Gabon]] for her father's career, who was an engineer at [[Électricité de France|EDF]].<ref name=":0" /> After graduating from high school in [[Lyon]], she  was enrolled in a literary [[Higher School Preparatory Classes|Higher School Preparatory Class]] in the Lycée du Parc. She holds a master's degree from [[Georgetown University]].<ref name=":0" /> She is also a graduate from the [[European University Institute]], where she completed her PhD thesis titled ''Learning and distribution in monetary economics.''<ref name=":1">{{cite web|url=https://www.huffingtonpost.fr/author/natacha-valla/|title=Natacha Valla|website=Le Huffington Post|language=fr|access-date=2019-03-30}}</ref>

== Career ==
Valla started her career as an economist at the European Central Bank in 2001.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=https://voxeu.org/article/ecb-conference-bridges-science-and-practice-monetary-policy|title=2018 ECB conference bridges the science and practice of monetary policy|last1=Altavilla|first1=Carlo|last2=Lemke|first2=Wolfgang|date=2019-02-28|website=VoxEU.org|access-date=2019-03-30|last3=Motto|first3=Roberto|last4=Valla|first4=Natacha}}</ref> In 2008, Valla was appointed executive director at [[Goldman Sachs]] Global Economic Research,<ref name=":2" /> focusing on the study of the eurozone. From 2014 to 2016, she was deputy director of CEPII, the main French think tank in international economy according to the World Economic Forum,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.weforum.org/agenda/authors/natacha-valla/|title=Authors|website=World Economic Forum|access-date=2019-03-30}}</ref> before joining the [[European Investment Bank]] in 2016 as head of Policy Strategy division.<ref name=":2" /> During this period, she was also a permanent member of the Conseil d'Analyse Economique, part of the scientific committee of the ACPR, the French Banking Regulatory Body.<ref name=":1" /> Valla was also on the directory boards of several large firms, including [[LVMH]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.lvmh.com/news-documents/press-releases/appointments-to-the-board-of-directors/|title=Appointments to the Board of Directors|website=LVMH|language=en|access-date=2019-03-30}}</ref> Autoroutes SA, and [[AccorHotels]].<ref>{{Cite press release|url=https://www.hotelmanagement.com.au/2016/07/13/new-directors-accorhotels-frhi-acquisition/|title=New directors for AccorHotels with FRHI acquisition|website=Hotel Management|date=13 July 2016|access-date=12 April 2020}}</ref> She was appointed Deputy Director General of the European Central Bank in 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bloombergquint.com/global-economics/ecb-reshuffle-kicks-off-as-states-hold-fire-for-future-posts|title=ECB Reshuffle Kicks Off as States Hold Fire for Future Posts|website=BloombergQuint|language=en|access-date=2019-03-30}}</ref>

== Academic career ==
Natacha Valla has published research on numerous subjects in the field of international finance, monetary and applied macro-economics. She was a fellow at the [[Paris School of Economics]]<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|url=https://www.institutmontaigne.org/macron-les-12-mois-de-jupiter/experts/natacha-valla/|title=Natacha Valla - Macron : Les 12 mois de Jupiter|website=Institut Montaigne|language=fr|access-date=2019-03-30}}</ref> and a member of the French [[Société d'économie politique|Société d'Economie Politique]].<ref name=":1" /> She is also co-author of a textbook of financial macroeconomics and has taught at the [[University of Florence]], the [[Paris Dauphine University|University of Paris Dauphine]], [[HEC Paris|HEC]] and [[Sciences Po]].<ref name=":3" /> She currently teaches at [[New York University Abu Dhabi|New York University]] in Abu Dhabi.<ref name=":3" />

== Awards ==
In 2000, Valla was awarded the SCE Prize in Computational Economics for her graduate research.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://comp-econ.org/graduate_student_contest.html|title=Computational Economics|website=comp-econ.org|access-date=2019-03-30}}</ref>

In 2018, she was recipient of the French [[National Order of Merit (France)|National Order of Merit]].<ref name=":0" />

==Personal life==
Valla is the mother of two sons.<ref name=":0" />
