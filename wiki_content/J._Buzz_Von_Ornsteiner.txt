'''Dr. J. Buzz Von Ornsteiner''' (born August 20, 1967) aka '''Dr. Buzz''' is a licensed [[forensic psychologist]] who provides weekly commentary for the TV show [[CopyCat Killers]] which airs on [[Reelz]] channel.<ref>[https://www.facebook.com/search/top/?q=ReelzChannel%20Dr.%20Buzz] {{dead link|date=June 2019}}</ref>

==Career==
Von Ornsteiner is a licensed [[psychologist]] and has a history working with the mentally ill within the context of the legal system.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://bronx.news12.com/news/new-york-city-s-first-lady-chirlane-mccray-seeks-to-improve-expand-mental-health-care-1.10240912 |title=Archived copy |access-date=2016-06-10 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160822062530/http://bronx.news12.com/news/new-york-city-s-first-lady-chirlane-mccray-seeks-to-improve-expand-mental-health-care-1.10240912 |archive-date=2016-08-22 |url-status=dead }}</ref>
From 2012 till 2019 he was the project director for the Mental Health Court Advocacy Program which was based in Brooklyn Arraignment Court.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://eac-network.org/behavioral-health-criminal-justice/|title=Behavioral Health and Criminal Justice Services|website=Eac-network.org|accessdate=16 June 2019}}</ref> The program, which ended when the city's new bail reform law came into effect <ref>{{cite web|url=https://eac-network.org/mental-health-diversion/}}</ref> was created to divert from further adjudication and confinement, seriously and persistently mentally ill offenders charged with minor, low-level non-violent crimes at the first point of contact within Criminal Court, at Brooklyn Arraignment Court. In addition, Von Ornsteiner also was the project director for EAC NETWORK's Forensic Intensive Case Management Program which provided intensive case management services for severely mentally ill males recently released from New York State prisons. Transitional services included placement within the New York City homeless shelter system.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://eac-network.org/forensic-intensive-case-management/|title=Forensic Intensive Case Management - EAC Network Program|website=Eac-network.org|accessdate=16 June 2019}}</ref> In 2021, Von Ornsteiner began working within the Health and Wellness section of the [[NYPD]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.linkedin.com/in/buzz-von-ornsteiner-03aa7165}}</ref>

Before receiving his doctoral degree, Von Ornsteiner also appeared as a lead actor in several feature films during the late 1980s: [[Robot Holocaust]], [[Slash Dance]]<ref>{{cite web|url=https://horrorpedia.com/2014/12/11/slash-dance-slashdance-1989-horror-thriller-overview-cast-plot-reviews/|title=Slash Dance – USA, 1989 – HORRORPEDIA|website=Horrorpedia.com|accessdate=16 June 2019}}</ref> and [[Zombie Death House]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://bloody-disgusting.com/horrordb/movies/zombie-death-house/|title=Zombie Death House|website=Bloody-disgusting.com|accessdate=16 June 2019}}</ref>

Von Ornsteiner became known to the public as "Dr. Buzz", appearing first on New York City radio station [[WWRL]] with his own show "Ask Dr. Buzz"<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.thebody.com/article/ask-dr-buzz|title="Ask Dr. Buzz" |website=Thebody.com|accessdate=16 June 2019}}</ref> and then on national cable stations, [[CNN International]], [[CNN Headline News]], [[Fox News]], [[E Entertainment]], [[MSNBC]], [[WNYW]], [[Court TV News]], [[Tru TV]], [[WPIX]], [[Investigation Discovery]] and [[Reelz]] as a regular expert analyst commenting on current high-profile criminal cases.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.projectpayitforward.org/in-the-film/buzz-von-ornsteiner|title=Dr. J. Buzz Von Ornsteiner|date=11 March 2015|website=Project Pay it Forward|accessdate=16 June 2019}}</ref>
