'''Richard David Portes''' [[CBE]] is professor of [[Economics]] at [[London Business School]].<ref name=LBSmain>{{cite web|title=Richard Portes|url=http://faculty.london.edu/rportes/|work=Faculty Pages|publisher=[[London Business School]]|accessdate=5 October 2018}}</ref>  He was President of the [[Centre for Economic Policy Research]], which he founded.<ref name=LBSmain/> He also serves as Directeur d'Etudes at the [[Ecole des Hautes Etudes en Sciences Sociales]] in [[Paris]].<ref name=LBSmain/>

He was a [[Rhodes Scholar]] and a Fellow of [[Balliol College, Oxford]].<ref name=NBERCV/> He also taught at [[Princeton University]], [[Harvard University]] (as a [[Guggenheim Fellow]]),<ref name=NBERCV/> was the founder of the Economics Department at [[Birkbeck, University of London|Birkbeck College]] ([[University of London]]) in 1972.<ref name=CEPRCBE/> In 1999–2000, he was the Distinguished Global Visiting Professor at the [[Haas School of Business]], [[University of California, Berkeley]], and in 2003–04 he was [[Joel Stern]] Visiting Professor of International Finance at [[Columbia Business School]].

Professor Portes is a Fellow of the [[Econometric Society]] and a Fellow of the [[British Academy]].  He was the longest serving Secretary-General of the [[Royal Economic Society]] (1992–2008) since [[John Maynard Keynes]]. He is Co-Chairman of the Board of Economic Policy. He is a member of the Group of Economic Policy Advisers to the President of the [[European Commission]]. He is the chair of the Steering Committee of the Euro50 Group, of the [[Bellagio Group]] on the International Economy, and of the Advisory Scientific Committee to the European Systemic Risk Board.<ref name="Richard Portes">{{cite web |title=Richard Portes |url=https://www.london.edu/faculty-and-research/faculty/profiles/p/portes-r |publisher=London Business School |accessdate=24 April 2019}}</ref>

==Writings==
Portes's current research interests include international finance, international macroeconomics, macroprudential regulation, European integration, and European bond markets.<ref name="Richard Portes"/> He has written extensively on sovereign debt, European monetary and financial issues, international capital flows, centrally planned economies and transition, macroeconomic disequilibrium, and European integration.<ref>{{cite web |title=Richard Portes: Citations and Publications |url=https://scholar.google.at/citations?user=zD9KHs4AAAAJ&hl=en |website=scholar.google.com |publisher=Google Scholar |accessdate=5 October 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Richard Portes: Research Output |url=https://ideas.repec.org/e/ppo132.html |website=ideas.repec.org |publisher=IDEAS |accessdate=5 October 2018}}</ref> His study (with [[Richard Baldwin (economist)|Richard Baldwin]] and [[Joseph Francois (economist)|Joseph Francois]]) on the [[2004 enlargement of the European Union|''Eastern Enlargement of the European Union'']] was widely cited in the public policy debate leading up to the 2004 Enlargement.<ref>{{cite web |title=Eastern Promise |url=https://www.economist.com/finance-and-economics/1997/04/10/eastern-promise |website=www.economist.com |publisher=The Economist, April 1997. |accessdate=5 October 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal |last1=Baldwin, R. |first1=J. Francois and R. Portes |title=The costs and benefits of eastern enlargement: the impact on the EU and central Europe |journal=Economic Policy |date=1997 |volume=12 |issue=24 |pages=125–176 |doi=10.1111/1468-0327.00018 |s2cid=14414518 |url=https://academic.oup.com/economicpolicy/article-abstract/12/24/125/2366315 |accessdate=5 October 2018}}</ref> His work on [[collective action clause]]s in sovereign bond contracts, on the international role of the euro, on international financial stability and on European bond markets has been directed towards policy as well as academic publications.<ref>{{cite web |title=Richard Portes at VoxEU |url=https://voxeu.org/users/richardportes |website=www.voxeu.org |publisher=CEPR |accessdate=5 October 2018}}</ref>
