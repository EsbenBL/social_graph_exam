'''Romain Wacziarg''' (born 1970) is an American economist. He is a professor of economics at the [[UCLA Anderson School of Management]] (since 2008) and holds the Hans Hufschmid Chair in Management (since 2015). He is also a research associate at the [[NBER|National Bureau of Economic Research]]. Before joining [[University of California, Los Angeles|UCLA]], he was on the faculty at the [[Stanford Graduate School of Business]]. His research interests span international economics, political economy, economic growth, and development.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://voxeu.org/users/romainwacziarg|title=Romain Wacziarg|last=Wacziarg|first=Romain|website=VoxEU.org|access-date=2017-05-22}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.anderson.ucla.edu/faculty_pages/romain.wacziarg/papersum.html|title=Romain Wacziarg's research website}}</ref>

== Background and professional life ==
Romain Wacziarg grew up in India and France. He holds a bachelor's degree in economics and public policy from [[Sciences Po]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.anderson.ucla.edu/faculty_pages/romain.wacziarg/downloads/cvRW.pdf|title=Romain Wacziarg's CV|access-date=May 23, 2017}}</ref> He earned a Ph.D. in economics at Harvard in 1998 under the supervision of [[Alberto Alesina]], [[Robert Barro]] and [[Dale W. Jorgenson]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www-personal.umich.edu/~alandear/tree/Z339.HTM|title=Alan Deardorff's Academic Family Tree of Trade Economists|access-date=2017-05-22}}</ref> During his Ph.D. he was a consultant for the [[World Bank]] where he conducted research on the links between economic growth and trade policy.

In 1998, he joined the faculty of the [[Stanford Graduate School of Business]] as an assistant professor in the Political Economy Group, where he received tenure in 2006.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://tec.fsi.stanford.edu/people/romain_wacziarg|title=FSI {{!}} The Europe Center - Romain Wacziarg|website=tec.fsi.stanford.edu|language=en|access-date=2017-05-22}}</ref> In 2002–2003 he was a National Fellow at the Hoover Institution.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://news.stanford.edu/news/2002/july24/hoover-724.html|title=Hoover Institution names 13 2002-03 National Fellows}}</ref> In 2008, he moved to the [[UCLA Anderson School of Management]] where he was appointed professor of economics and later chair of the global economics and management area (2011–2015).<ref>Economic Principals, May 20, 2008 (http://www.economicprincipals.com/issues/2008.05.20/318.html)</ref> In 2015, he was awarded the Hans Hufschmid Chair in Management.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.anderson.ucla.edu/faculty-and-research/global-economics-and-management/faculty/wacziarg|title=Wacziarg {{!}} UCLA Anderson School of Management|website=www.anderson.ucla.edu|access-date=2017-05-22}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.anderson.ucla.edu/media-relations/2013/hans-hufschmid-chair|title=Hans Hufschmid Chair {{!}} UCLA Anderson School of Management|website=www.anderson.ucla.edu|access-date=2017-05-23}}</ref>

Wacziarg's research covers a variety of topics, including:
* The relationship between trade liberalization and growth;
* The long-run determinants of growth and development;
* The determinants of the first demographic transition;
* The causes of inter-state conflict;
* The effects of democratization on economic growth;
* The pattern of sectoral diversification in relation to economic development;
* The effect of cultural distance between countries on the diffusion of technology and development.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.economicprincipals.com/issues/2007.08.26/262.html|title=Putting the (Molecular) Clock on Development {{!}} Economic Principals|website=www.economicprincipals.com|language=en-US|access-date=2017-05-23}}</ref>
