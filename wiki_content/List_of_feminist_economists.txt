This is an incomplete alphabetical list by surname of notable '''[[feminist economics|feminist economists]]''', experts in the [[social science]] of feminist economics, past and present. Only economists with biographical articles in Wikipedia are listed here.

== Feminist economists ==

===A===

* [[Bina Agarwal]]{{refn|group=note|name=first|Past president / President / President-elect of the [[International Association for Feminist Economics#Past presidents|International Association for Feminist Economics (IAFFE)]]}} (born 1951), Indian development economist
* [[Randy Albelda]] (born 1955), American labor and welfare economist; her research interests include gender and race, public policies, economics of taxation, and poverty
* [[Sabina Alkire]], welfare economist with an interest in ethics
* [[Iulie Aslaksen]], Norwegian environmental economist
* [[Siobhan Austen]], Australian economist researching gendered aspects of population ageing

* [[Eudine Barriteau]]{{refn|group=note|name=first}} (born 1954), Barbadian professor of gender and public policy, and deputy principal, at the Nita Barrow Unit within the [[Cave Hill, Saint Michael, Barbados#Affiliated Research Units .26 Centres|Institute of Gender and Development Studies]], [[University of the West Indies]], [[Cave Hill, Saint Michael, Barbados|Cave Hill, Barbados]]
* [[Robin L. Bartlett]],{{refn|group=note|name=first}} American professor for the economics department of [[Denison University]], [[Granville, Ohio]]
* [[Amrita Basu]] (born 1953), American academic specializing in [[South Asian]] politics, with a particular interest in women's movements and other social movements
* [[Carolyn Baylies]] (1947–2003), American sociologist, active in [[international development]]
* [[Lourdes Benería]]{{refn|group=note|name=first}} (born 1937), Spanish professor of economics at [[Cornell University]]'s Department of City & Regional Planning
* [[Silvia Berger (economist)|Silvia Berger]], Argentine president of the [[International Association for Feminist Economics|International Association for Feminist Economics (IAFFE)]], 2017–2018
* [[Barbara Bergmann]]{{refn|group=note|name=first}} (1927–2015), American, with an interest in [[social policy]] and [[Egalitarianism|equality]]
* [[Margunn Bjørnholt]] (born 1958), Norwegian [[sociologist]], economist, and [[social psychology (sociology)|social psychologist]]
* [[Anders Borg]], Swedish politician and former Minister for Finance
* [[Heather Boushey]] (born 1970), American senior economist with the [[Center for American Progress]]

* [[Cecilia Conrad]]{{refn|group=note|name=first}} (born 1955), African-American academic with a particular interest in the effects of race and gender on economic status

===D===
* [[Séverine Deneulin]] (born 1974), senior lecturer in International Development at the Department of Social and Policy Sciences, [[University of Bath]]

* [[Diane Elson]] (born 1946), British [[gender and development]] social scientist

* [[Nancy Folbre]]{{refn|group=note|name=first}} (born 1952), focuses on [[family economics]], non-market work and the [[care work|economics of care]]
* [[Sakiko Fukuda-Parr]] (born 1950), Japanese development economist

* [[Jayati Ghosh]] (born 1955), Indian development economist

* [[Alicia Girón]], Mexican president of the [[International Association for Feminist Economics|International Association for Feminist Economics (IAFFE)]], 2014–2015

* [[Heidi Hartmann]] (born 1945), American founder and president of the Washington-based [[Institute for Women's Policy Research|Institute for Women's Policy Research (IWPR)]], a research organization created to conduct women-centered, public policy research
* [[Susan Himmelweit]]{{refn|group=note|name=first}} (born 1948), British emeritus professor of economics for the [[Open University]] in the UK; member of the editorial boards of ''[[Feminist Economics (journal)|Feminist Economics]]'' and  ''[[Journal of Women, Politics & Policy]]''
* [[Jane Humphries]]{{refn|group=note|name=first}} (born 1948), British professor of economic history and Fellow of [[All Souls College]] at the [[University of Oxford]]

* [[Deniz Kandiyoti]] (born 1944), Turkish-British, emeritus professor in development studies at the [[School of Oriental and African Studies|School of Oriental and African Studies, University of London]]
