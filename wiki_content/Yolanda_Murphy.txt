'''Yolanda Murphy''' (born 1925 in [[New York City]]) is a [[cultural anthropologist]] who is the co-author of classic anthropology text ''Women of the Forest'' with her husband, [[Robert F. Murphy (anthropologist)|Robert F. Murphy]]. This text was based on field work done in 1952 among the [[Munduruku|Mundurucu Indians]] of the [[Amazon river|Amazon]] rainforest in [[Brazil]]. She met her husband while they were graduate students in at [[Columbia University]]. They did extensive field work in Brazil in preparation for writing their seminal work.
