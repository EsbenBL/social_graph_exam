She is the author of four books, including two on [[Unmanned combat aerial vehicle|drones]], another on U.S. military interventions after the [[Cold War]], and the most recent on how the [[United States]] pays for its wars.<ref name=":0" />  She is a life member of the [[Council on Foreign Relations]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cfr.org/membership-roster-g-k|title=Membership Roster|website=Council on Foreign Relations|language=en|access-date=2018-01-09}}</ref> and her writing has appeared in [[The Washington Post]], [[International Herald Tribune]], [[The New York Times]], [[USA Today]], [[CNBC]], [[Axios (website)|Axios]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.axios.com/authors/SarahKreps|title=Sarah Kreps on Axios|website=Axios|language=en|access-date=2018-06-21}}</ref> and [[CNN]], among other outlets.<ref name=":0" />

== Military ==
Sarah Kreps was a [[foreign area officer]] for European and Sub-Saharan African affairs from 1999-2003. She worked as a consultant for the [[Ministry of Defence (United Kingdom)]] and [[NATO]] from 2003-2004 and as an analyst for the [[National Reconnaissance Office]] from 2003-2005. Since then, she has worked as an advisor for the [[United States Institute of Peace]] and as an executive consultant for the [[United States Department of Homeland Security]].<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://skgovernment.files.wordpress.com/2017/08/kreps-vitae_august-2017.pdf|title=Sarah E.
