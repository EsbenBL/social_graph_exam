'''Fernand Gobet''' (born February 12, 1962 in [[Switzerland]]) is a [[cognitive scientist]] and a [[cognitive psychologist]], currently Professor of Cognitive Psychology at the [[London School of Economics]]. His research interests focus on the study of cognition, especially in the areas of [[cognitive architectures]], perception, [[Intuition (knowledge)|intuition]], problem solving, learning and decision making. He has developed the [[CHREST]] cognitive architecture, an acronym for Chunk Hierarchy and REtrieval STructures, which is a complete architecture for the processes of learning and perception used by humans.<ref>Gobet, F., Lane, P. C. R., Croker, S., Cheng, P. C-H., Jones, G., Oliver, I. & Pine, J. M. (2001). Chunking mechanisms in human learning. Trends in Cognitive Sciences, 5, 236-243.</ref>
He is a [[chess]] [[International Master]], and played numerous times for the Swiss national team.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.olimpbase.org/players/f1jjnl3e.html|title=Men's Chess Olympiads: Fernand Gobet|last=Bartelski|first=Wojciech|publisher=OlimpBase|accessdate=2011-12-01}}</ref> He was co-editor of the Swiss Chess Review from 1981 to 1989. His [[Elo rating system|Elo rating]] is 2400.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.chessgames.com/player/fernand_gobet.html|title=The chess games of Fernand Gobet|publisher=ChessGames.com|accessdate=1 December 2011}}</ref>
