'''''The Art of the State: Culture, Rhetoric, and Public Management''''' is a book written by [[Christopher Hood]], first published by [[Oxford University Press]] in 1998 with a revised edition published in 2000.   In November 1998 it was awarded the [[W. J. M. Mackenzie]] award of the [[Political Studies Association]].<ref name="WJMMBW">{{cite web |url=https://www.psa.ac.uk/sites/default/files/W%20J%20M%20Mackenzie%20Book%20Prize%20Winners.pdf |title=W J M Mackenzie Book Prize Winners |publisher=[[Political Studies Association]] |accessdate=2016-11-30 }}</ref>

Writing in ''[[Public Administration (journal)|Public Administration]]'' [[Richard J. Stillman II]] described the book as "a timely and important book, by one of Europe’s
most respected administrative scholars".<ref name="StillmanPA">{{cite journal |title=The Art of the State: Culture, Rhetoric, and Public Management |journal=[[Public Administration (journal)|Public Administration]] |year=2000 |last=Stillman II |first=Richard J. |authorlink=Richard J. Stillman II |volume=78 |issue=2 |pages=461–462 |doi=10.1111/1467-9299.00214 }}</ref> In a review in the ''International Public Management Journal'' Eugene Bardach wrote that "For intellectual scope and ambition, ''The Art of the State'' is almost without peer in the recent public management literature".<ref name="BardachIPMJ">{{cite journal |title=The Art of the State: Culture, Rhetoric, and Public Management |journal=International Public Management Journal |year=2000 |last=Bardach |first=Eugene |volume=2 |issue=2 |pages=389–392 |doi=10.1016/S1096-7494(00)89043-1 }}</ref>
