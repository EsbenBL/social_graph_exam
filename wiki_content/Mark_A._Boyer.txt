'''Mark A. Boyer''' (born June 15, 1961) is a Board of Trustees Distinguished Professor in the Department of Geography at the [[University of Connecticut]].<ref>{{URL|1=http://www.yourpublicmedia.org/content/profile/featured/mark-boyer|2=Connecticut Public Broadcasting}}
*{{URL|1=http://provost.uconn.edu/board-of-trustees-distinguished-professor/|2=Office of the Provost, University of Connecticut}}</ref> He is a specialist in [[International Relations]] theory.<ref>{{URL|1=http://www.yourpublicmedia.org/content/wnpr/understanding-global-environmental-politics|2=''Where We Live'', WNPR. "Understanding Global Environmental Politics: Despite Environmental Conditions Worsening, We Have ‘Treaty Congestion.’” Jul 16, 2012.}}</ref> Boyer’s recent scholarship analyzes governmental responses to [[climate change]] at the regional and local level.<ref>{{URL|1=http://wnpr.org/post/what-weve-learned-superstorm-sandy|2=WNPR News. "What We've Learned From Superstorm Sandy," Diane Orson, October 30, 2013.}}
*{{URL|1=http://dailytrojan.com/2013/12/04/professor-discusses-local-policies-on-climate-change/|2=''Daily Trojan''. "Professor Discusses Local Policies on Climate Change," Rebecca Cohen, December 4, 2013.}}
*{{URL|1=http://www.yourpublicmedia.org/node/22628|2=Connecticut Public Broadcasting. "Resilient Cities: With More Severe Storms On The Horizon, How Should Cities And Towns Prepare?" October 31, 2012.}}
*{{URL|1=http://nsglc.olemiss.edu/sglpj/vol5No1/vol5no1.pdf|2=“Adapting to Climate Change: Mapping Connecticut’s Coastal Responses to a Global Problem.” (2013) Mark A. Boyer. ''Sea Grant Law and Policy Journal'', vol. 5, no. 1: 15-40.}}</ref> He has also worked to advance pedagogical scholarship and methods.<ref>{{URL|1=http://sag.sagepub.com/content/42/6/711.abstract|2=“Evolving Beyond Self-Interest? Some Experimental Findings from Simulated International Negotiations.” (2010) Co-author with Anat Niv-Solomon, Laura L. Janik, Mark A. Boyer, Natalie B. Hudson, Brian Urlacher, Scott W. Brown, Donalyn Maneggia. ''Simulation and Games'', vol. 42, no. 6: 711-732.}}
*{{URL|1=http://www.tandfonline.com/doi/abs/10.1080/07380560802688299|2=“Can Multimedia Make Kids Care About Social Studies: The GlobalEd Problem-Based Learning Simulation.” (2009) Co-author with Andri Ioannou, Scott W. Brown, Robert Hannafin. ''Computers in the Schools'', 26: 63-81.}}</ref> As part of his secondary research agenda, he is co-director of the GlobalEd 2 project,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://globaled.education.uconn.edu/|title=Home {{!}} The GlobalEd 2 Project|last=Judd|first=Jamison|date=2013-07-15|website=globaled.education.uconn.edu|language=en-US|access-date=2019-10-18}}</ref> which conducts computer-assisted international studies simulations for middle school and high school students throughout the United States.<ref>{{URL|1=http://today.uconn.edu/blog/2013/05/two-faculty-named-2013-board-of-trustees-distinguished-professors/|2=''UConn Today''. "Two Faculty Named 2013 Board of Trustees Distinguished Professors," Stephanie Reitz, May 13, 2013.}}</ref> Boyer is past co-editor of ''[[International Studies Review]]'' (2008-2012), a journal of the [[International Studies Association]], and former editor of ''[[International Studies Perspectives]]'' (2000-2004); he continues to serve as an editorial board member for both journals.<ref>{{URL|1=http://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/journal/10.1111/(ISSN)1468-2486/homepage/EditorialBoard.html|2=''International Studies Review''}}</ref><ref>{{URL|1=http://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/journal/10.1111/(ISSN)1528-3585/homepage/EditorialBoard.html|2=''International Studies Perspectives''}}</ref>
