'''Clair Wilcox''' (1898 â€“ 1970) was an American economist.<ref>{{Cite news|last=|first=|date=1971-01-01|title=Clair Wilcox, 72, Economic Adviser|language=en-US|work=The New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1971/01/01/archives/clair-wilcox-72-economic-adviser-swarthmore-professor-and-aide-to.html|url-status=live|access-date=2021-01-13|issn=0362-4331}}</ref> He was on the faculty of [[Swarthmore College]] from 1927 to 1968. He chaired the [[International Trade Conference]], which resulted in the [[General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade]].

Wilcox was born in [[Cuba, New York]], and obtained his B.S. and Ph.D. from the [[University of Pennsylvania]].
Before going to Swarthmore, Wilcox taught at [[Lafayette College|Lafayette]], [[Ohio Wesleyan University|Ohio Wesleyan]] and the University of Pennsylvania.  He wrote a monumental book on government regulation of business, ''Public Policies Toward Business''.

Wilcox was married to Florence Ruth Chapman, and had two daughters, Andrea and Carolyn (later known as [[Caroly Wilcox]]). After his first wife died in 1954,<ref>{{Cite news|last=|first=|date=1954-05-19|title=Mrs. Wilcox Dies in Swarthmore|pages=39|work=The Philadelphia Inquirer|url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/67517405/mrs-wilcox-dies-in-swarthmore/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-01-13|via=Newspapers.com}}</ref> he remarried, to Marcia Lincoln Wallace. He died in 1970, aged 72 years, while traveling in Tucson, Arizona.<ref>{{Cite news|last=|first=|date=1971-01-02|title=Dr. Clair Wilcox, dies at 72|pages=4|work=Delaware County Daily Times|url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/11126710/dr-clair-wilcox-dies-at-72/|url-status=live|access-date=2021-01-13|via=Newspapers.com}}</ref>
