'''Menzie David Chinn''' (born 1961) is a professor of [[Public administration|public affairs]] and economics at the [[University of Wisconsin–Madison]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.econ.wisc.edu/faculty.html |title=Department of Economics&nbsp;— Faculty Directory |publisher=Econ.wisc.edu |date=October 10, 2011 |accessdate=October 15, 2011}}</ref> co-editor of the [[Journal of International Money and Finance]], and a Research Associate of the [[National Bureau of Economic Research]] International Finance and Macroeconomics Program.<ref name=NBER>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nber.org/reporter/spring03/profiles.html|title=NBER Profile: Menzie Chinn|website=www.nber.org|access-date=2019-05-28}}</ref>

==Education and career==
Chinn received a BA from [[Harvard College]] in 1984, a PhD in economics from the [[University of California at Berkeley]] in 1991, and was a senior economist with the [[Council of Economic Advisers|White House Council of Economic Advisers]] from 2000 to 2001.{{r|NBER}} He contributes to an economics blog at ''econbrowser.com'' which has been highly ranked by traffic amongst economics blogs,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gongol.com/lists/bizeconsites/ |title=EconDirectory |publisher=Gongol.com |accessdate=October 15, 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://bayesianheresy.blogspot.com/2008/01/bh-economics-blog-awards-2008.html |title=The Bayesian Heresy: BH Economics Blog Awards 2008 |publisher=Bayesianheresy.blogspot.com |date=January 4, 2008 |accessdate=October 15, 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.econolog.net/stats.php?area=blogs |title=The Palgrave Econolog&nbsp;— Zeitgeist |publisher=Econolog.net |accessdate=October 15, 2011 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://archive.is/20120729143849/http://www.econolog.net/stats.php?area=blogs |archivedate=July 29, 2012 |df=mdy-all }}</ref> and is frequently interviewed by economics journalists.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ssc.wisc.edu/~mchinn/cv.html|title=Menzie Chinn's cv page|website=www.ssc.wisc.edu|access-date=2019-05-28}}</ref>

==Works==
By citation, Chinn is ranked among the top 500 authors of economic journals<ref>{{cite web|url=https://ideas.repec.org/top/top.person.all.html |title=Economist Rankings at IDEAS |publisher=Ideas.repec.org |accessdate=October 15, 2011}}</ref> and ranked 521 in terms of downloads on the Social Science Research Network.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/cf_dev/AbsByAuth.cfm?per_id=15131 |title=SSRN Author Page for Menzie David Chinn |publisher=Papers.ssrn.com |accessdate=October 15, 2011}}</ref> He has written extensively on international economics, trade and exchange rates.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://ideas.repec.org/e/pch129.html |title=Menzie Chinn at IDEAS |publisher=Ideas.repec.org |accessdate=October 15, 2011}}</ref>

He describes his teaching material as "the (old) [[neoclassical synthesis]], i.e., [[Keynesian economics|Keynesian]] short run plus [[Classical economics|Classical]] long run — and some [[New Keynesian economics|New Keynesian]]"<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://econbrowser.com/archives/2014/12/keynesian-cassandras-the-sequester-re-assessed|title=Keynesian Cassandras? The Sequester Re-Assessed {{!}} Econbrowser|language=en-US|access-date=2019-05-28}}</ref>

*''Lost Decades'' (with Jeffrey Frieden), W. W. Norton & Company, 2011
