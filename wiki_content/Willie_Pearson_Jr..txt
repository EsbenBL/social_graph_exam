'''Willie Pearson Jr.''' is an American [[sociologist]], who has studied and encouraged the participation of African-Americans and other minorities, as well as women, in science. He has published several books on the experience of African-American scientists with PhDs, including major studies on chemists and engineers. Pearson has had a leading role in many activities and policy development roles in relation to the participation of minorities and women in science, including chairing the Committee on Equal Opportunities in Science and Engineering (CEOSE), a Congressionally mandated committee at the [[National Science Foundation]] (NSF). He served on the President's Board of Advisors on Historically Black Colleges and Universities.

== Early life and education ==

Willie Pearson was born in [[Rusk, Texas]] on 29 June 1945.<ref name="HistoryMakers bio">{{cite web |title=Willie Pearson, Jr.'s Biography |url=https://www.thehistorymakers.org/biography/willie-pearson-jr |website=The HistoryMakers |publisher=The History Makers |accessdate=15 February 2020 |language=en}}</ref> After his parents divorced, he and his older sister, Vassie King, were raised by their mother.<ref name="HistoryMakers bio"/> He attended Emmett J. Scott High School in [[Tyler, Texas]],  going on to study at the small, historically-black, liberal arts college, [[Wiley College]] and graduating with a B.A. in sociology in 1968.<ref name="HistoryMakers bio" /> In 1971, Pearson earned his masters degree in sociology from [[Atlanta University]].<ref name="Wake Forest 1997">{{cite web |title=Four Awarded Wake Forest Professorships |url=https://inside.wfu.edu/1997/04/four-awarded-wake-forest-professorships/ |website=Inside WFU - news for faculty and staff |publisher=Wake Forest University |accessdate=17 February 2020 |language=en |date=18 April 1997}}</ref>

Pearson later undertook doctoral research at the [[Southern Illinois University at Carbondale]], gaining his PhD in 1980/81 with the dissertation, ''One in a hundred: a study of black American science doctorates''.<ref name="Dissertation" /><ref name="CPNAS bio">{{cite web |title=Willie Pearson, Jr. |url=http://www.cpnas.org/aahp/biographies/willie-pearson-jr.html |website=www.cpnas.org |publisher=National Academies of Sciences |accessdate=17 February 2020}}</ref> He undertook postdoctoral studies at the [[Educational Testing Service]] (ETS) and the U.S. Congress' [[Office of Technology Assessment]] (OTA).<ref name="Georgia Tech bio" />

== Career ==

After completing his masters degree, Pearson briefly worked for the [[Department of Health, Education and Welfare]] and the U.S. Army in [[Kansas City, Missouri]].<ref name="HistoryMakers bio" /> In 1972, he was appointed to the sociology faculty of [[Grambling State University]] in Louisiana. In 1980, Pearson joined [[Wake Forest University]] in North Carolina.<ref name="Wake Forest 1997" />

In 2001, he was appointed to Professor and Chair of the Department of History and Sociology at the [[Georgia Institute of Technology]] in Atlanta.<ref name="Georgia Tech bio">{{cite web |title=Willie Pearson, Ph.D. - Ivan Allen College of Liberal Arts |url=https://www.iac.gatech.edu/people/faculty/pearson |website=Ivan Allen College of Liberal Arts at Georgia Tech |publisher=Georgia Institute of Technology |accessdate=17 February 2020 |language=en}}</ref> Soon after, Pearson chaired the U.S. Congress mandated the establishment of the Committee on Equal Opportunities in Science and Engineering (CEOSE),<ref name="CEOSE report">{{cite book |last1=Committee on Equal Opportunities in Science and Engineering |title=2002 Biennial Report to Congress |date=2003 |publisher=National Science Foundation |location=Washington DC |url=https://www.nsf.gov/pubs/2003/ceose/ceose.pdf |accessdate=23 February 2020}}</ref> a congressionally mandated committee in the [[National Science Foundation]] (NSF) which reports biennially to the U.S. Congress.<ref name="James - NSF">{{cite journal |last1=James |first1=SM |last2=Singer |first2=SR |last3=Elgin |first3=Sarah C. R. |title=From the NSF: The National Science Foundation's Investments in Broadening Participation in Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics Education through Research and Capacity Building. |journal=CBE: Life Sciences Education |date=2016 |volume=15 |issue=3 |pages=fe7 |doi=10.1187/cbe.16-01-0059 |pmid=27587853 |pmc=5008905 }}</ref>

In 2010, President [[Barack Obama]] appointed Pearson to his re-established Board of Advisors on Historically Black Colleges and Universities.<ref name="WH 2010 HBCUs">{{cite web |title=President Obama Re-establishes President's Board of Advisors on Historically Black Colleges and Universities, Announces Members |url=https://obamawhitehouse.archives.gov/the-press-office/president-obama-re-establishes-presidents-board-advisors-historically-black-college |website=whitehouse.gov |accessdate=23 February 2020 |language=en |date=26 February 2010}}</ref> He has also served on committees and advisory boards and panels for the [[National Institutes of Health]] (NIH), [[National Science Foundation]] (NSF), the [[American Chemical Society]] (ACS), the [[American Sociological Association]] (ASA), and the [[National Academies of Sciences, Engineering, and Medicine|National Academies of Science, Engineering, and Medicine]] (NASEM).<ref name="CPNAS bio" /><ref name="NAS panel appointment">{{cite web |title=Pearson Invited to Serve on National Academies of Science Panel |url=https://www.iac.gatech.edu/news-events/stories/2016/4/pearson-invited-serve-national-academies-science-panel/527671 |website=Ivan Allen College of Liberal Arts at Georgia Tech |publisher=Georgia Institute of Technology |accessdate=23 February 2020 |language=en |date=21 April 2016}}</ref>
