'''Ralph Braibanti''' (1920 - November 24, 2005<ref>{{cite web |url=http://today.duke.edu/2005/12/braibanti.html |title=Ralph Braibanti, Expert on Islamic-Western Relations, Dies |date=2 December 2005 |website=Duke Today |publisher=Office of News & Communications, [[Duke University]] |access-date=19 March 2018}}</ref>) was the [[James B. Duke Professor]] of [[political science]] at [[Duke University]], and was known for his work in [[Islamic studies]]; his work focused on [[Pakistan]]. He founded the American Institute of Pakistan Studies and served as its President for nine years.<ref>Obituary: Ralph Braibanti. The Washington Post, November 27, 2005</ref>

Braibanti was born 1920 in [[Danbury, Connecticut]] of [[Italian people|Italian]] and [[Polish people|Polish]] heritage and taught at [[Syracuse University]] and [[Kenyon College]] before coming to Duke. He died in 2005. [http://www.dukenews.duke.edu/2005/12/braibanti.html]
