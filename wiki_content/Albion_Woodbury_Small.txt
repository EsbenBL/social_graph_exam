'''Albion Woodbury Small''' (May 11, 1854 – March 24, 1926) founded the first independent Department of [[Sociology]] in the [[United States]] at the [[University of Chicago]] in [[Chicago]], [[Illinois]] in 1892. He was influential in the establishment of sociology as a valid field of academic study.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://homepages.rootsweb.ancestry.com/~smalljd/ri/ri-als-soc.htm|title = Bibliography of Albion Woodbury Small}}</ref>

==Biography==
Albion Woodbury Small was born in [[Buckfield, Maine]] to parents Reverend Albion Keith Parris Small and Thankful Lincoln Woodbury. He lived in [[Bangor, Maine]], and then [[Portland, Maine]] where he attended public schools in both places.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.asanet.org/albion-w-small|title=Albion W. Small|date=2009-06-16|website=American Sociological Association|language=en|access-date=2019-10-10}}</ref>

He attended Colby University, now known as [[Colby College]], from 1872 until he graduated in 1876.<ref name=":0">{{Cite journal|last=Goodspeed|first=Thomas W.|date=1926|title=Albion Woodbury Small|journal=American Journal of Sociology|volume=32|issue=1|pages=1–14|issn=0002-9602|jstor=2765243|doi=10.1086/214021|s2cid=143497701}}</ref> He studied [[theology]] from 1876 to 1879 at the [[Andover Newton Theological School]]. From 1879 to 1881 he studied at the [[University of Leipzig]] and the [[Humboldt University|University of Berlin]] in [[Germany]] [[history]], [[social economics]] and [[politics]].<ref>{{cite journal | jstor = 2765244| last1 = Barnes| first1 = Harry Elmer| title = The Place of Albion Woodbury Small in Modern Sociology| journal = American Journal of Sociology| volume = 32| issue = 1| pages = 15–44| year = 1926| doi = 10.1086/214022| s2cid = 143163925}}</ref> While in Germany, he married Valeria von Massow in June 1881,<ref name=":0" /> with whom he had one child.

From 1888 to 1889 he studied history at the [[Johns Hopkins University]] in [[Baltimore, Maryland]] and was promoted in 1889 with a [[Ph.D.]] thesis (''The Beginnings of American Nationality'') at the same time continuing to teach at [[Colby College]].  From 1889–1892 he was the 10th President of Colby.

In 1892 he founded the first Department of Sociology at the University of Chicago. He chaired this department for over 30 years. In 1894 he, along with George E. Vincent, published the first textbook in sociology: An introduction to the study of society. In 1895 he established the [[American Journal of Sociology]]. From 1905 to 1925 he served as Dean of the Graduate School of Arts and Literature at the University of Chicago.

Albion Woodbury Small was a member of the [[Delta Kappa Epsilon]] fraternity (Xi chapter).

== Influence on sociology ==
Albion Small can be attributed with many "firsts" in the field of sociology. In 1892, he helped to create a Department of Social Science at the University of Chicago, which was the first ever sociology department in the United States.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.anb.org/view/10.1093/anb/9780198606697.001.0001/anb-9780198606697-e-1400569|title=Small, Albion Woodbury (1854–1926), sociologist {{!}} American National Biography|website=www.anb.org|language=en|doi=10.1093/anb/9780198606697.article.1400569|access-date=2019-10-10|year=2000|last1=Laz|first1=Cheryl|isbn=978-0-19-860669-7}}</ref> Then, in 1894, along with colleague  [[George Edgar Vincent|George E. Vincent]], he wrote the first sociology textbook titled ''An Introduction to the Study of Society.''<ref name=":1" /> Lastly, he founded the first Sociology Journal in the United States in 1895, the ''American Journal of Sociology.''<ref name=":1" />
