'''William Hall "Bill" Janeway''' (born May 3, 1943) is an American [[venture capitalist]] and [[economist]]. His work on the [[innovation economy]] emphasizes the strategic role played by the state and by financial speculators.

==Early life and education==
Bill Janeway was born on May 3, 1943 in Manhattan, the second son of [[Elizabeth Janeway]], author and critic, and [[Eliot Janeway]], political economy columnist.  He attended [[Trinity School (New York City)|Trinity School]] in New York, from which he graduated as valedictorian of the class of 1961, and [[Princeton University]], from which he graduated as valedictorian of the class of 1965. He received a [[Marshall Scholarship]] to the [[University of Cambridge]],<ref name="marshall" /> where he matriculated at [[Pembroke College, Cambridge|Pembroke College]] and received a Ph.D. in economics in 1971.<ref>"Dr. William H. Janeway, Hon. CBE". ''Cambridge in America'' website: http://www.cantab.org/component/content/article/1673 (accessed 18 July 2018).</ref> His doctoral thesis, titled "The Economic Policies of the Labour Government of 1929-1931" was supervised by Professor [[Lord Kahn]].<ref>{{cite web|title=The economic policy of the second Labour government 1929-1931 |url=http://ethos.bl.uk/OrderDetails.do?uin=uk.bl.ethos.460697|publisher=British Library|accessdate=29 October 2012|author=W. H. Janeway |location=University of Cambridge |format=Unpublished|year=1971}}</ref>

==Investment career==
===Investment banking===
In 1970, Janeway joined F. Eberstadt & Co., Inc., the investment-banking firm that was founded by [[Ferdinand Eberstadt]]. In 1979, he was promoted to director of corporate finance. In 1985, F. Eberstadt was acquired by [[Robert Fleming & Co.]], the London merchant bank and investment management firm. Janeway served as director of corporate finance of the resulting American subsidiary, Eberstadt Fleming & Co., Inc., until 1988.<ref name="warburgpincus">{{cite web|url=http://www.warburgpincus.com/people/ViewEmployee,employeeid,54.aspx|title=People: William H. Janeway |publisher=[[Warburg Pincus]] |accessdate=2012-10-11}}</ref>

===Venture capital===
Janeway joined [[Warburg Pincus]], the private equity firm, in 1988 as head of its high technology investment team.<ref name="warburgpincus" /> The firm’s high-tech investments through the 1990s centered on information and communications technology, and, after 1991, increasingly focused on enterprise software.

In 1992, the firm funded the launch of OpenVision Technologies, which subsequently merged with [[VERITAS Software]] in 1996.<ref>{{cite web| url=http://investing.businessweek.com/research/stocks/private/person.asp?personId=219665&privcapId=20117&previousCapId=249603&previousTitle=ALCOA%20INC |title=William Janeway: Executive Profile & Biography - Businessweek |publisher=Investing.businessweek.com |accessdate=2012-10-11}}</ref> In 1999, Warburg Pincus also was the founder and sole investor in [[BEA Systems]].<ref>{{cite news|title=BEA-Warburg Venture Formed|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1999/12/14/business/bea-warburg-venture-formed.html|newspaper=New York Times|date=14 December 1999}}</ref> Warburg Pincus eventually distributed its positions in both companies to its limited partners, realizing total returns of $750 million in VERITAS shares and $6.5 billion in BEA shares on investments in each of approximately $50 million.<ref>[http://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/warburg-pincus-closes-53-billion-global-private-equity-fund-77161057.html “Warburg Pincus Closes $5.3 Billion Global Private Equity Fund”]</ref>

In 2006, Janeway retired as Vice Chairman of Warburg Pincus. He remains a special limited partner.<ref name="warburgpincus" />

==Research==
In collaboration with Michael McKenzie of the [[University of Sydney]], Janeway conducted research on venture capital returns.<ref>{{cite journal|last=McKenzie|first=Michael D.|author2=Janeway, William H. |title=Venture capital funds and the public equity market|journal=Accounting & Finance|date=1 September 2011|volume=51|issue=3|pages=764–786|doi=10.1111/j.1467-629X.2010.00373.x|s2cid=154090784}}</ref> He served as a teaching visitor at [[Princeton University]]’s economics department in 2012.

===Work on innovation===
Janeway re-engaged with academic economics through his friendship with [[Hyman Minsky]] that began in the mid-1980s.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ritholtz.com/blog/2008/11/new-hope-for-financial-economics-interview-with-bill-janeway/ |title=New Hope for Financial Economics: Interview with Bill Janeway &#124; The Big Picture |publisher=Ritholtz.com |date=2008-11-17 |accessdate=2012-10-11}}</ref> Janeway’s article, "Doing Capitalism: Notes on the Practice of Venture Capitalism," presented at the annual meeting of the Association for Evolutionary Economics in December 1985,<ref>{{cite journal|last=Janeway|first=William H.|title=Doing Capitalism: Notes on the Practice of Venture Capitalism|journal=Journal of Economic Issues|date=June 1986|volume=20|issue=2|pages=431–441|publisher=Association for Evolutionary Economics|id=4225724|ref=jstor|jstor=4225724|doi=10.1080/00213624.1986.11504514}}</ref> was written at Minsky’s behest.

==Current positions==
Janeway is a special limited partner of Warburg Pincus. Janeway is a director of Magnet Systems and [[O'Reilly Media]]. He is an affiliated member of the faculty of economics at [[Cambridge University]].<ref name="Cam">{{cite web
 | url = http://www.econ.cam.ac.uk/people/crsid.html?crsid=whj20
 | title =Faculty of Economics, University of Cambridge
 | date =February 2017
 | website = www.econ.cam.ac.uk
 | access-date = 2017-04-04
}}</ref>

Janeway is a co-founder and member of the board of governors of the [[Institute for New Economic Thinking]].  He is a member of the board of directors of the [[Social Science Research Council]] and of the Advisory Board of the Princeton [[Bendheim Center for Finance]].  He is a member of the management committee of the Cambridge-INET Institute, University of Cambridge and a member of the board of managers of the Cambridge Endowment for Research in Finance (CERF). He is a member of the board of directors of the [[Fields Institute|Fields Institute for Research in the Mathematical Sciences]].  He is the author of ''Doing Capitalism in the Innovation Economy: Reconfiguring the Three-Player Game between Markets, Speculators, and the State'', published by Cambridge University Press in 2012.

==Philanthropic work==
Janeway and his wife, Weslie Janeway, established the Cambridge Endowment for Research in Finance in 2001<ref>{{cite web|title=$10 million for financial research|url=http://www.admin.cam.ac.uk/univ/newsletter/2001/aug-sep/2.html|publisher=University of Cambridge|accessdate=16 October 2012|year=2001}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|title=Janeways donate $10m to CERF|url=http://www.ft.com/intl/cms/s/0/39902c7c-e6a8-11d9-b6bc-00000e2511c8.html|newspaper=Financial Times|year=2005}}</ref> and funded the annual Princeton-Cambridge Finance Seminars in 2004.

==Personal life==
In 1985, Janeway married Weslie Resnick, a vice president of F. Eberstadt & Company, in a non-denominational ceremony in [[New York City]]. It was his second marriage.<ref>{{Cite web|last=  |first=  |authorlink=  |title= Weslie Resnick Weds W.H. ==Honors==
In September 2012, Janeway was awarded an honorary [[Order of the British Empire|Commander of the Order of the British Empire]] (CBE) "for services to education in support of Cambridge University and to [[United Kingdom–United States relations|UK/US relations]]".<ref>{{cite web|title=Cambridge author William H. Janeway awarded CBE by Her Majesty the Queen|url=http://www.cambridge.org/home/news/article/item7111878/?site_locale=en_US|publisher=Cambridge University Press|accessdate=16 October 2012|year=2012}}</ref> He is the recipient of the 2017 Marshall Medall of the Marshall Aid Commemoration Commission.<ref name="marshall">{{cite web|title=Marshall Aid Commemoration Commission Year ending 30 September 2017 |url=https://www.marshallscholarship.org/media/1211/64th_annual_report.pdf|publisher=Marshall Aid Commemoration Commission|access-date=27 July 2020|year=2017}}</ref>

Janeway's book ''Doing Capitalism'' was included in Foreign Affairs' 2012 list of "Best Books on Economic, Social, and Environmental Subjects", as well as the Financial Times' "Best books of 2012".<ref>{{cite web|title=Best Books on Economic, Social, and Environmental Subjects|url=http://www.foreignaffairs.com/features/collections/the-best-books-of-2012-on-economic-social-and-environmental-subj|publisher=Foreign Affairs|accessdate=27 December 2012}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Best books of 2012|url=http://www.ft.com/cms/s/2/88bdb3c0-37cf-11e2-a97e-00144feabdc0.html#axzz2IBHmgpfN|publisher=Financial Times|accessdate=3 December 2012}}</ref>
