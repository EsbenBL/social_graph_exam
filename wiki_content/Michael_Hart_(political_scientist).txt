'''Michael William Hart''' (born 1956) has been Fellow in Politics at [[Exeter College, Oxford]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.exeter.ox.ac.uk/college/rectorandfellows/hart.html|title=Dr Michael Hart|publisher=[[Exeter College, Oxford|Exeter College]]|accessdate=24 May 2011|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110927034837/http://www.exeter.ox.ac.uk/college/rectorandfellows/hart.html|archivedate=27 September 2011|df=dmy-all}}</ref> since 1982.

His research interests include [[Politics of the United Kingdom|British Politics]] since 1880 and Modern Southern Africa. He lectures on British Politics and Government since 1900.

He took his first degree at Emmanuel College, [[Cambridge|University of Cambridge]], winning a double First, and came to [[Oxford|University of Oxford]] to research his doctorate on the decline of the British Liberal Party. He spent some time at [[Magdalene College, Cambridge]] as a [[Junior Research Fellow]] before taking up his current Fellowship.  Besides academic positions, he has also served as a Junior Proctor in the University of Oxford and as sub-Rector at Exeter.

Hart has been quoted on numerous occasions by [[The Times]] and [[The Daily Telegraph|The Telegraph]] during [[general election]] periods, campaigned against apartheid in South Africa and has been a [[Liberal Party (UK)|Liberal Party]] Councillor on [[Oxford City Council]].  Philosophically, he is a liberal, but combines this with a belief in the need for a strong state.
