'''Ali Babacan''' ({{IPA-tr|aˈli babaˈdʒan}}; born 4 April 1967) is a [[Turkic peoples|Turkish]] politician and economist. He is the founder and current leader of the [[Democracy and Progress Party]] (DEVA). He served 13 years as the [[Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Turkey)|Minister of Foreign Affairs]] and [[Ministry of Trade (Turkey)|Economy of Turkey]] from 2002 to 2015. He was a member of [[Grand National Assembly of Turkey|the parliament]] and former [[Deputy Prime Minister of Turkey]] responsible for the Economy.

He first served as the Minister of State in charge of economic affairs in the [[Cabinet Gül|58th cabinet]] from the [[Justice and Development Party (Turkey)|Justice and Development Party]] (AKP). He retained this position throughout the 58th and 59th Governments of the [[Republic of Turkey]]. On 29 August 2007 he was appointed as the [[Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Turkey)|Minister of Foreign Affairs]] of the 60th Government of the Republic of Turkey.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.mfa.gov.tr/cv-of-minister-of-foreign-affairs-ali-babacan.en.mfa|title=From Rep. of Turkey Ministry of Foreign Affairs|website=Republic of Turkey Ministry of Foreign Affairs}}</ref> During 2009–2015, he served as the Deputy Prime Minister for Economic and Financial Affairs of Turkey.

Ali Babacan had the duty of steering a harsh economic reform program, which was backed by multibillion-dollar [[International Monetary Fund|IMF]] loans; with its help, [[Economy of Turkey|Turkish economy]] achieved a remarkable recovery after two severe crises.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.ft.com/content/b8e492a0-ab76-11d9-893c-00000e2511c8|title=Turkey and IMF reach accord on $10bn loan|work=[[Financial Times]]|date=April 12, 2005}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.ft.com/content/430b9dfe-24c2-11e1-bfb3-00144feabdc0|title=Surging Turkish growth raises fears|work=[[Financial Times]]|date=December 12, 2011}}</ref> He mostly stayed away from the Turkish political arena and focused on economic reforms, acting more like a [[Technocracy (bureaucratic)|technocrat]] and trying to stay away from [[populism]].

In 2019, Babacan left the AKP, citing "deep differences" over the party's direction as a reason and founded the [[Democracy and Progress Party]] (DEVA) in 2020.<ref name="Turkey's Babacan applies to launch new party, calls for more democracy">{{Cite news|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-turkey-politics-babacan-idUSKBN20W0IS|title=Turkey's Babacan applies to launch new party, calls for more democracy|first=Daren|last=Butler|work=[[Reuters]]|date=March 9, 2020}}</ref> Babacan, who also speaks [[English language|English]], is married and has three children.<ref>{{Cite web|title=TÜRKİYE BÜYÜK MİLLET MECLİSİ|url=https://www.tbmm.gov.tr/develop/owa/milletvekillerimiz_sd.bilgi?p_donem=26&p_sicil=6063|website=www.tbmm.gov.tr}}</ref>

== Early life and career ==
[[File:Ali Babacan - World Economic Forum Turkey 2008.jpg|left|thumb|Ali Babacan at the [[World Economic Forum]], 2008]]

===Education===
Ali Babacan graduated from [[TED Ankara College Foundation Schools|TED Ankara College]] ranking first among the class of 1985.<ref name="ntv">{{cite web|url=http://arsiv.ntvmsnbc.com/news/418608.asp|title=Başbakan ve bakanların özgeçmişleri|publisher=Ntvmsnbc.com.|language=tr|date=3 September 2007|access-date=13 January 2011}}</ref> He attended the [[Middle East Technical University]] (METU) in Ankara and in 1989 was awarded a [[Bachelor of Science|BSc]] in Industrial Engineering with the highest marks (''4.00 point out of 4.00'').<ref name="ntv"/>

Babacan went to the [[United States|U.S.]] on a [[Fulbright Scholarship]] to do postgraduate studies and in 1992 received an [[MBA]] from the [[Kellogg School of Management]] at Northwestern University in [[Evanston, Illinois|Evanston]], [[Illinois]], with majors in marketing, organizational behavior and international business.<ref name="ntv" />

===Career in finance===
Babacan worked then for two years as an associate at QRM, Inc. in [[Chicago, Illinois]], a company doing financial consulting to the top executives of major banks in the United States.<ref name="ntv"/> He returned to Turkey in 1994 and, served as chief advisor to the mayor of Ankara the same year. He was the chairman of his family owned textile company between 1994 and 2002.<ref>{{Cite web|title=DEVA - Ali Babacan {{!}} Biography|url=https://devapartisi.org/uyeler/listeleme/ali-babacan|url-status=live|access-date=24 June 2021|website=devapartisi.org|language=tr}}</ref>

==Political life==
=== Ministry of Foreign Affairs & Economy (2002–2015) ===
[[File:Clinton Babacan.JPG|left|thumb|[[Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Turkey)|Minister of Foreign Affairs]] Ali Babacan and his US counterpart [[Hillary Clinton]], 2009]]

He entered politics in 2001 as a co-founder and a Board member of the [[Justice and Development Party (Turkey)|Justice and Development Party]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://arsiv.zaman.com.tr/2001/08/15/politika/politikadevam.htm |title=Meclis'in 6. partisi |publisher=Zaman.com.tr |language=tr |date=15 August 2001 |access-date=13 January 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110721093216/http://arsiv.zaman.com.tr/2001/08/15/politika/politikadevam.htm |archive-date=21 July 2011 }}</ref> and was elected to [[Grand National Assembly of Turkey|parliament]] as deputy for [[Ankara]] on 3 November 2002. He was appointed [[Ministry of Trade (Turkey)|Minister of Economy]] on 18 November 2002 and became the youngest member of the cabinet, then at the age of 35.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://arsiv.ntvmsnbc.com/news/188493.asp|title=58. Hükümet'in profili|publisher=Ntvmsnbc.com|language=tr|date=18 November 2002|access-date=13 January 2011}}</ref>

[[File:Ali Babacan - World Economic Forum Annual Meeting 2012 crop.jpg|left|thumb|Ali Babacan at the Global Economic Outlook session, [[Davos]], 2012]]

On 24 May 2005 Prime Minister [[Recep Tayyip Erdoğan]] announced his appointment as chief negotiator in Turkey's accession talks with the [[European Union]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://arsiv.ntvmsnbc.com/news/325250.asp|title=Başmüzakereci Ali Babacan|publisher=Ntvmsnbc.com|language=tr|date=29 May 2005|access-date=13 January 2011}}</ref> which started on 3 October 2005.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://hurarsiv.hurriyet.com.tr/goster/ShowNew.aspx?id=355050|title=Türkiye-AB müzakereleri resmen başladı|publisher=Hurriyet.com.tr|language=tr|date=4 October 2005|access-date=13 January 2011}}</ref> As government minister Babacan attended several international meetings including the [[World Economic Forum]] in [[Davos]], [[Switzerland]], and the [[Bilderberg Group]].

In 2019, Babacan left the ruling [[Justice and Development Party (Turkey)|AKP]], citing "deep differences" over the party's direction as a reason.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.ft.com/content/14806d84-a179-11e9-974c-ad1c6ab5efd1|title=Ali Babacan resigns from Turkey’s ruling AKP|work=[[Financial Times]]|language=en|access-date=2019-07-19}}</ref>

=== Leader of the DEVA (2020–present) ===
[[File:Ali Babacan 2020 (cropped).jpg|right|300px|thumb|Leader of the [[Democracy and Progress Party|DEVA Party]] Ali Babacan, 2020]]

Babacan confirmed his intent to form this party in a late 2019 interview with journalist Şirin Payzın of T24, and expects his party to be a "mainstream party" with particular focuses on minority rights, a return to Turkey's parliamentary system, fair processes in courts and legislation, and restoring freedom of speech and expression. Babacan is quoted as saying that "the nation will give our party its name".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://haber.sol.org.tr/turkiye/ali-babacan-yeni-partinin-ne-zaman-kurulacagini-acikladi-277073|title=Ali Babacan yeni partinin ne zaman kurulacağını açıkladı|website=soL Haber|language=tr|access-date=2019-12-27}}</ref>

On 9 March 2020, he founded [[Democracy and Progress Party]], abbreviated as "DEVA" or "remedy" in Turkish.<ref name="Turkey's Babacan applies to launch new party, calls for more democracy" /> The DEVA Party held its first meeting of the Board of Founders on 10 March 2020, where he was unanimously elected Chairman.
