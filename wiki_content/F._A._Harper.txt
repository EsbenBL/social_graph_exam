'''Floyd Arthur''' "'''Baldy'''" '''Harper''' (February 7, 1905 – April 1973) was an American [[Academia|academic]], economist and writer who was best known for founding the [[Institute for Humane Studies]] in 1961.<ref name="Wilkinson" /><ref>{{Cite book | publisher=Economics America, Inc.| title=The Right Guide: A Guide to Conservative, Free-Market, and Right-of-Center Organizations | author=Wilcox, Derk Arend | year=2000 | location=Ann Arbor, MI | pages=440 | isbn=978-0-914169-06-2}}{{Cite book | publisher=ABC-CLIO| title=Libertarianism Today | author=Huebert, Jacob H | year=2010 | location=Santa Barbara, California | pages=254 | isbn=978-0-313-37754-9}}{{cite web|author-link1=Jörg Guido Hülsmann|last=Hülsmann|first=Jörg Guido|title=Birth of a Movement|url=https://mises.org/daily/2732|work=Mises Daily|publisher=Ludwig von Mises Institute|access-date=October 10, 2011|date=2007-09-28}}{{Cite book | publisher=Harvard University Press| title=The Road from Mont Pèlerin: The Making of the Neoliberal Thought Collective | last1=Mirowski|first1=Philip| year=2009 | location=Cambridge | pages=469 | isbn=978-0-674-03318-4 | last2=Plehwe|first2=Dieter}}{{Cite book| publisher = Sheed Andrews and McMeel| isbn = 9780836207507| last = Rothbard| first = Murray Newton| title = Power and Market: Government and the Economy| year = 1977}}</ref>

==Personal life==
Baldy Harper was born and raised in [[Middleville, Michigan]] and graduated from [[Michigan State University]].<ref name="Wilkinson" /><ref>{{Cite book | publisher=Economics America, Inc.| title=The Right Guide: A Guide to Conservative, Free-Market, and Right-of-Center Organizations | author=Wilcox, Derk Arend | year=2000 | location=Ann Arbor, MI | pages=440 | isbn=978-0-914169-06-2}}{{Cite book | publisher=ABC-CLIO| title=Libertarianism Today | author=Huebert, Jacob H | year=2010 | location=Santa Barbara, California | pages=254 | isbn=978-0-313-37754-9}}{{cite web|last=Hülsmann|first=Jörg Guido|title=Birth of a Movement|url=https://mises.org/daily/2732|work=Mises Daily|publisher=Ludwig von Mises Institute|access-date=October 10, 2011|date=2007-09-28}}{{Cite book | publisher=Harvard University Press| title=The Road from Mont Pèlerin: The Making of the Neoliberal Thought Collective | last1=Mirowski|first1=Philip| year=2009 | location=Cambridge | pages=469 | isbn=978-0-674-03318-4 | last2=Plehwe|first2=Dieter}}</ref> He went on to obtain a doctorate in [[agricultural economics]] from [[Cornell University]].<ref name="Poirot"/> Economist [[Herbert J. Davenport]] was influential to Harper during his time at Cornell.<ref name="Poirot"/>

In 1930, Harper married Marguerite Kaechele. The couple had four children: Barbara, Harriet, Helen, and Larry.

==Career==
The [[Federal Farm Board]] employed Harper as a research field agent in 1930 and 1931. He worked as a [[business analyst]] for the [[Farm Credit System#Agricultural Credit Associations|Farm Credit Association]] in 1934.<ref>{{Cite book | publisher = Ludwig von Mises Institute | isbn = 978-1-61016-119-0 | last = Sennholz | first = Mary | title = On Freedom and Free Enterprise: Essays in Honor of Ludwig Von Mises | year = 1956 }}</ref> In academia Harper spent 19 years as a professor of marketing at [[Cornell University]] and in 1937 was appointed acting head of the Department of [[Agricultural economics]] at the [[University of Puerto Rico]]. ==Institute for Humane Studies==
[[File:Aristotle's Error.jpg|thumb|left|John Hasnas lecturing at [[Towson University]] during an [[Institute for Humane Studies]] [[seminar]]]]
Harper founded the [[Institute for Humane Studies]] in 1961 in [[Menlo Park, California]].<ref>{{Cite news | last = Rothbard | first = Murray N | title = Floyd Arthur 'Baldy' Harper, RIP | work = Mises Daily | date = August 17, 2007 }}{{cite book | title=The Right Guide: A Guide to Conservative, Free-Market, and Right-of-Center Organizations | publisher=Economics America, Inc. | author=Wilcox, Derk Arend | year=2000 | location=Ann Arbor, MI | pages=440 | isbn=978-0-914169-06-2}}{{cite book | title=Libertarianism Today | publisher=ABC-CLIO | author=Huebert, Jacob H | year=2010 | location=Santa Barbara, California | pages=254 | isbn=978-0-313-37754-9}}</ref>

==Legacy==
