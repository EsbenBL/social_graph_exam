'''Charles Knickerbocker Harley''' is an academic [[economic historian]] who has written on a wide range of topics including the British [[industrial revolution]], the late nineteenth century international economy, and the impact of [[technological change]]. He is a practitioner of the [[Cliometrics|New Economic History]].<ref>Harley, 2002, pp. 16, 20</ref>

At Harvard he studied under [[Alexander Gerschenkron]]. He completed his dissertation, ''Shipbuilding and Shipping in the Late Nineteenth Century'', on the transition from wooden [[sailing ships]] to steel [[Steamship|steamers]], in 1972.<ref>Harley, 2002, p. 17</ref> He took a professorship at the [[University of British Columbia]]. In 1978 he moved to the [[University of Western Ontario]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://economics.uwo.ca/people/faculty/harley.html |title=C. Knick Harley, Professor Emeritus |author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.--> |date=n.d. |website=Department of Economics, Western University, Canada |access-date=17 October 2015}}; Harley, 2005</ref> In 2005 he joined the faculty of [[St. Antony's College, Oxford]],<ref>ibid.</ref> where he stayed until becoming an Emeritus Fellow in 2011.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.sant.ox.ac.uk/people/knick-harley |title=Professor Knick Harley, Emeritus Fellow |author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.--> |date=n.d. |website=St Antony's College, University of Oxford |access-date=17 October 2015}}</ref>

He has been a frequent collaborator with [[Nicholas Crafts|N.F.R. Crafts]].<ref>Harley, 2002, p. 20; e.g. Harley 1992, Crafts & Harley, 1992</ref>

He has been awarded [[The Cliometric Society]]'s ''Clio Can'' in 1999 in recognition of his exceptional support of cliometrics<ref>{{cite journal |last=Lyons |first=John |date=July 1999 |title=Report on the 39th Annual Cliometrics Conference |url=http://cliometrics.org/newsletters/Volume%2014/Volume%2014%20Number%202%20Jul%201999.pdf |journal=The Newsletter of the Cliometric Society |volume=14 |issue=2 |access-date=17 October 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160305035306/http://cliometrics.org/newsletters/Volume%2014/Volume%2014%20Number%202%20Jul%201999.pdf |archive-date=5 March 2016 |url-status=dead }}; {{cite web |url=http://cliometrics.org/awards.htm/ |title=Clio Can Award Winners |author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.--> |date=n.d. |website=Cliometric Society |access-date=17 October 2015 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20151103184352/http://www.cliometrics.org/awards.htm |archivedate=3 November 2015 }}</ref> and the ''Arthur H. Cole Prize'' by the ''Journal of Economic History'', for his essay, "British Industrialization Before 1841: Evidence of Slower Growth During the Industrial Revolution".<ref>{{cite journal |author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.--> |date=December 1982 |title=Editors' Notes: 1983 Annual Meeting of Economic History Association |journal=[[The Journal of Economic History]] |volume=42 |issue=4 |pages=923–928 |jstor=2121117 |doi=10.1017/s0022050700028424|doi-access=free }}; {{cite web |url=http://eh.net/eha/cole-prize-winners-1966-1997/ |title=Cole Prize Winners 1966-1997 |author=<!--Staff writer(s); no by-line.--> |date=n.d. |website=Economic History Association |access-date=17 October 2015}}</ref>
