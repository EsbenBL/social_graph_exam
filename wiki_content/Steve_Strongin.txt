'''Steven Strongin''' (born July 30, 1958) is a Senior Advisor at Goldman Sachs.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.goldmansachs.com/our-firm/leadership/management-committee/steven-h-strongin.html|title=Goldman Sachs {{!}} Management Committee - Steven H. Strongin|website=Goldman Sachs|language=en-US|access-date=2020-03-19}}</ref> Previously, he was the head of Global Investment Research and chair of the Global Markets Institute at [[Goldman Sachs]].<ref name="Market Watch">{{cite web|url=https://www.marketwatch.com/story/goldman-names-steve-strongin-to-run-global-research|title=Goldman names Steve Strongin to run global research|last=|first=|date=April 11, 2007|website=|publisher=Market Watch|archive-url=|archive-date=|accessdate=11 November 2018}}</ref> He was a member of the Management Committee, Firmwide Client and Business Standards Committee and was also co-chair of the Firmwide Technology Risk Committee. Strongin's writing on [[economic policy]], [[Investment|investing]] and financial trends is featured across many news sources including [[The Wall Street Journal]], [[Bloomberg News]], and [[Business Insider]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/feds-tarullo-warns-banks-of-significant-increase-in-capital-in-future-stress-tests-1464870270|title=Fed Governors Signal Bigger Bank Capital Requirements Looming|last=Reilly|first=Ryan Tracy and David|work=WSJ|access-date=2018-11-11|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2018-02-07/get-ready-for-most-cryptocurrencies-to-hit-zero-goldman-says|title=Get Ready for Most Cryptocurrencies to Hit Zero, Goldman Says|last=Nishizawa|first=Nana|date=February 7, 2018|website=Bloomberg|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=November 11, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://markets.businessinsider.com/currencies/news/ripples-xrp-price-biggest-loser-of-the-cryptocurrency-bloodbath-2018-2-1014996762|title=Ripple's XRP is the biggest loser of the cryptocurrency bloodbath {{!}} Markets Insider|last=GmbH|first=finanzen.net|work=markets.businessinsider.com|access-date=2018-11-11}}</ref> His article, “Beating Benchmarks,” won the Second Annual Bernstein Fabozzi/Jacobs Levy Award for Outstanding Article after it was published in the ''[[Journal of Portfolio Management]]''.<ref>{{Cite journal|last1=Strongin|first1=Steve|last2=Petsch|first2=Melanie|last3=Sharenow|first3=Greg|date=2000-07-31|title=Beating Benchmarks|journal=The Journal of Portfolio Management|volume=26|issue=4|pages=11–27|doi=10.3905/jpm.2000.319761|s2cid=219219464|issn=0095-4918}}</ref> He holds three [[patent]]s for [[financial instrument]]s. One of his patents was the Wavefront system, which describes how economic shocks ripple through the economy into company performance, market value and equity returns in the US market.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://patents.google.com/patent/US7949590B2/en?oq=7949590|title=US7949590B2|last=|first=|date=April 16, 2014|website=|archive-url=|archive-date=November 11, 2018|access-date=}}</ref>

== Career ==
He joined Goldman Sachs in 1994, and became a managing director in 1998 and partner in 2002.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://live.ft.com/Events/2017/FT-US-Banking-Forum-2017|title=FT US Banking Forum 2017 organised by FT Live|website=FT Live|language=en-GB|access-date=2018-11-11}}</ref> Prior to joining the firm, Strongin served as director of monetary policy research at the [[Federal Reserve Bank of Chicago]] after a twelve-year career in the Economic Research Department.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://quotes.wsj.com/GS/company-people/executive-profile/102892400|title=GS Company Profile & Executives – Goldman Sachs Group Inc. – Wall Street Journal|website=quotes.wsj.com|access-date=2019-06-24}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.chicagofed.org/people/s/strongin-steven|title=Steven Strongin - Federal Reserve Bank of Chicago|website=www.chicagofed.org|access-date=2019-06-24}}</ref> He also served as an economist at the center for the Study of Economy and State at the [[University of Chicago]] and a researcher at PanHeuristics.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/research/stocks/private/person.asp?personId=26859122&privcapId=25232416&previousCapId=25232416&previousTitle=Goldman%2520Sachs%2520Group%2520Inc.,%2520Research%2520Division|title=Steven Harris Strongin: Executive Profile & Biography – Bloomberg|website=www.bloomberg.com|access-date=2019-06-24}}</ref>

At Goldman Sachs, Strongin serves on the advisory board to the [[RAND Corporation|RAND]] Center for Corporate Ethics and on the visiting committee to the college at the [[University of Chicago]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rand.org/well-being/justice-policy/portfolios/corporate-ethics.html|title=Corporate Ethics and Governance|last1=Monica|first1=1776 Main Street Santa|last2=California 90401-3208|website=www.rand.org|language=en|access-date=2019-06-24}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://visitingdirectory.uchicago.edu/visiting/committee.php?c=VCSA|title=Secure Directory {{!}} Visiting Committees & Councils {{!}} The University of Chicago|website=visitingdirectory.uchicago.edu|access-date=2019-06-24}}</ref> He also serves as a director on the boards for the [[Ocean Conservancy]] and previously served as a director for New York City's Fund for Public Schools.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.sais-jhu.edu/events-calendar/events/conversation-steve-strongin-head-global-investment-research-goldman-sachs|title=Conversation with Steve Strongin, Head of Global Investment Research, Goldman Sachs|last=|first=|date=June 24, 2019|website=Johns Hopkins School of Advanced International Studies|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://oceanconservancy.org/about/leadership/|title=Leadership & Board of Directors|date=2017-02-13|website=Ocean Conservancy|language=en|access-date=2019-06-24}}</ref> He is a distinguished visiting professor at the [[Frederick S. Pardee RAND Graduate School|Pardee RAND Graduate School]]. Strongin is a jury member for the [[World Resources Institute]]'s Ross Prize for Cities<ref>{{Cite web|title=Jury|url=https://prizeforcities.org/jury|access-date=2021-01-20|website=prizeforcities.org|language=en}}</ref> and he is on the advisory council for the [[Becker Friedman Institute for Research in Economics|Becker Friedman Institute]] at the University of Chicago.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Our Team|url=https://bfi.uchicago.edu/about/our-team/|access-date=2021-01-20|website=BFI|language=en-US}}</ref>
