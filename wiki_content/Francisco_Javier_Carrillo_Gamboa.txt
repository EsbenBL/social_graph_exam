'''Francisco Javier Carrillo Gamboa''' is a Mexican professor and researcher in [[knowledge systems]] and knowledge administration with the [[Monterrey Institute of Technology and Higher Education]] (Tec de Monterrey). He introduced the concept to Mexico and has promoted and developed it both inside the country and abroad.<ref name=70anos>{{cite web |url=http://www.itesm.edu/wps/wcm/connect/snc/portal+informativo/por+tema/educacion/70anostecdemonterrey072_28may13 |title= Francisco Javier Carrillo: pionero en México de la Administración del Conocimiento |date=May 28, 2013 |publisher=Tec de Monterrey |location=Mexico |language=Spanish |trans-title=Francisco Javier Carrillo: Pioneer in knowledge administration in Mexico |accessdate=June 21, 2013 }}</ref>

==Biography==
Carrillo has a masters in economics from the [[London School of Economics]] and a doctorate in the psychology of science and technology from [[King's College London]].<ref name=70anos/><ref name=llego>{{cite web |url=http://70tec.milenio.com/perfil/francisco-javier-carrillo-gamboa |title=Francisco Javier Carrillo Gamboa:Llegó por el cambio del Tec y ahora él transforma ideas |publisher=Tec de Monterrey |location=Mexico |language=Spanish |trans-title=Francisco Javier Carrillo Gamboa: Arrived because of the change in Tec and now he transforma ideas |accessdate=June 21, 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140107144138/http://70tec.milenio.com/perfil/francisco-javier-carrillo-gamboa |archive-date=January 7, 2014 |url-status=dead }}</ref><ref name=cronica>{{cite web |url=http://www.itesm.mx/cronicaintercampus/no_14/perfiles.html |title=Fundador del Centro de Sistemas de Conocimiento del Tecnológico de Monterrey preocupado por impulsar la economía basada en el conocimiento |work=Crónica Intercampus |publisher=Tec de Monterrey |location=Mexico |language=Spanish |trans-title=Founder of the Knowledge Systems Center at the Tec de Monterrey worries about developing the economy based on knowledge |accessdate=June 21, 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111205020958/http://www.itesm.mx/cronicaintercampus/no_14/perfiles.html |archive-date=December 5, 2011 |url-status=dead }}</ref> He is an expert in knowledge cities and knowledge based development and introduced to the concept to Mexico.(world capital) .<ref name=70anos/><ref name=llego/> His specialty is the creation and development of knowledge with the aim of stimulating the economy.  This not only includes supporting business ideas with financing, but also making available intellectual capital and sources of knowledge.<ref name=70anos/><ref name=llego/>  He developed a system called the Capital Systems Framework as a model for knowledge-based organizations, which has been adopted in various countries.<ref name=cronica/><ref name=worldcapital>{{cite web |url=http://www.worldcapitalinstitute.org/content/francisco-javier-carrillo-wci-president |title= Francisco Javier Carrillo, WCI President  |date=February 11, 2011 |publisher=World Capital Institute |location=Mexico |accessdate=June 21, 2013 }}</ref> His book Knowledge Cites has been described as successfully challenging “the prevailing urban planning paradigm with a powerful vision of a future based on the critical resource of human capital.” and “the most important new book in urban planning since the publication of Jay Forrester’s “Urban Dynamics.” by Steven A. Cavaleri of [[Central Connecticut State University]].<ref name=gurteen>{{cite web |url=http://www.gurteen.com/gurteen/gurteen.nsf/id/S291535/|title= Knowledge Cities by Francisco Carrillo |publisher=Gurteen Knowledge Community |location=United Kingdom  |accessdate=June 21, 2013 }}</ref>

He believes that growing the economy based on knowledge resources is urgent, with Mexico losing competitiveness due to an outdated industrial base, low productivity of is labor force and the lack of political and social cohesion, making it unable to exploit its natural, social and other resources. He also believes that Mexico is capable of an economic level much higher than is generally imagined.<ref name=cronica/> He also believes that the Tec of Monterrey is one of the main movers of change in Latin America and the world.<ref name=70anos/>

He began working with the Tec de Monterrey in 1989, when the new Centro de Tecnologia Avanzada para la Producción, generally called CETEC opened its doors, precisely to join that institution, billed as a center of innovation.<ref name=70anos/><ref name=llego/> He arrived to the Monterrey campus with his wife is Rosa María Sánchez Cantú, who also is a professor and researcher of the institution.<ref name=70anos/>

In 1992, he established the Centro de Sistemas de Conocimiento (CSC or Center for Knowledge Systems) at the Monterrey campus, which promotes the creation of new knowledge in the Spanish language. With this institution, he has led over 100 contracted projects.<ref name=70anos/><ref name=worldcapital/>

In addition to his activities at the Tec de Monterrey, he is also an international consultant and leads another of other organizations and projects.<ref name=worldcapital/> Carrillo is the founder and president of the World Capital Institute as well as president of the Comunidad Iberoamericana de Sistemas de Conocimiento.<ref name=70anos/> The World Capital Institute sponsors the annual MAKCi (Most Admired Knowledge City) Awards as well as the Knowledge Cities World Summit, which has been held in various parts of the world including [[Melbourne]].<ref name=worldcapital/> He is a founding member of the New Club of Paris and served on the editorial boards of several international journals, as well as founder and editor of MetaSite, a “site of sites” about knowledge management.<ref name=llego/><ref name=worldcapital/> He is part of a program called Monterrey Ciudad Internacional de Conocimiento, which aims to develop the city along knowledge management lines.<ref name=cronica/>

Carrillo is listed in [[Who’s Who in the World]], Who´s Who in Global Banking and Finance and in the Special Edition 2000 of The Barons 500.<ref name=worldcapital/>
