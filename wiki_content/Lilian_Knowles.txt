'''Lilian Charlotte Anne Knowles''' (née '''Tomn'''; 1870–1926) was a British historian and Professor of Economic History at the [[London School of Economics|London School of Economics (LSE)]] in the 1920s. She was the first female Dean of the Economic History Faculty in the University of London. 

== Biography ==
Born in [[St Clement, Cornwall|St Clement]], Cornwall, Knowles attended [[Truro High School]].<ref name=LSE/><ref name=MaxBODNB/> After a tour of the continent with her family she went to [[Girton College, Cambridge]].<ref name=MaxBODNB/> At Girton she read history and law taking both a History [[Tripos]] and a Law Tripos (Part 1) in 1894.<ref name=LSE/> Both were judged to be "first class" and she was the first woman to obtain a first class in the law tripos.<ref name=MaxBODNB/> At the time she took her degree [[University of Cambridge|Cambridge]] did not allow women to receive their degrees.<ref name=Steamboat/><ref name=MBerg/> Knowles, as with many other such female students from both Cambridge and [[University of Oxford|Oxford]] universities, was later, in 1907, to take the steamboat to Ireland to be conferred with an ''[[ad eundem degree|ad eundem]]'' [[University of Dublin]] [[academic degree|degree]] at [[Trinity College, Dublin]].<ref name=Steamboat/> Hers was a DLitt.<ref name=LSE/> Thus she became one of the ''[[steamboat ladies]]''.<ref name=Steamboat/>
[[File:Lilian Knowles House, Spitalfields - East London..jpg|thumb|left|Lilian Knowles House is a student residence reserved for postgraduate students at the LSE<ref name=gen/>]]

Between 1895–99 she was one of the [[London School of Economics|London School of Economic]]'s first research students and taught there as an ''occasional lecturer'' between 1897 and  1898.<ref name=MBerg/><ref name= Bev/> In 1904 she gained a teachership in modern economic history at [[London School of Economics|LSE]] — thus, according to [[Maxine Berg|Berg]], "becoming the first full-time teacher of the subject at any British university".<ref name=MBerg/> In 1907 she was promoted to Reader in economic history.<ref name=MaxBODNB/><ref name= Bev/> In 1921 she was promoted to a professorship in Economic History becoming Britain's second professor in that subject (the first being George Unwin at the [[Victoria University of Manchester|University of Manchester]]).<ref name=MaxBODNB/><ref name=MBerg/> From 1920 to 1924 she served as the [[University of London]]'s Dean of the Faculty of Economics.<ref name=MaxBODNB/><ref name=MBerg/><ref name= Bev/> This made her the first woman to become a Dean of the University of London.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://blogs.lse.ac.uk/lsehistory/2016/07/14/the-hidden-women-of-lse/|title=The 'hidden' women of LSE|last1=July 14th|last2=LSE|first2=2016{{!}}Hidden|date=2016-07-14|website=LSE History|language=en-US|access-date=2019-08-07|last3=People|last4=Comments|first4=Women's history{{!}}0}}</ref>

Outside academia, she was the only female member of the Royal Commission on Income Tax, 1919–1920 and she was especially concerned with the practice, extant at the time, of charging income tax on joint income of married couples.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://blogs.lse.ac.uk/lsehistory/2018/10/24/lilian-knowles-1870-1926-the-pioneer/|title=Lilian Knowles (1870-1926) – the pioneer|last1=October 24th|last2=life|first2=2018{{!}}Academic|date=2018-10-24|website=LSE History|language=en-US|access-date=2019-08-07|last3=History|first3=Department of Economic|last4=People|last5=Comments|first5=Women's history{{!}}0}}</ref> She was also a member of the Council of the [[Royal Economic Society]] and of the Council of the [[Royal Historical Society]].<ref name=LSE/><ref name=MBerg/>
The Cornish community in London led by LSE graduate Tim James are seeking that this Cornish pioneer is commemorated with her own blue plaque. 
==Legacy==
Since 2006, the [[London School of Economics|LSE]] has had a 360 bedroom development reserved for their postgraduate students. The building is based on a Victorian building and it is now called the "Lilian Knowles House".<ref name=gen>[http://www.generationestates.co.uk/projectdetail.php?project_id=19&mode=all&showindex=8 Lilian Knowles House] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150218233931/http://www.generationestates.co.uk/projectdetail.php?project_id=19&mode=all&showindex=8 |date=18 February 2015 }}, GenerationEstates, retrieved 18 February 2015</ref> There are also two student prizes named after her at [[London School of Economics|LSE]]; one for the highest marks in the first year and one for the highest marks in the final year of the Economic History Undergraduate degree programme.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://blogs.lse.ac.uk/lsehistory/2018/10/24/lilian-knowles-1870-1926-the-pioneer/|title=Lilian Knowles (1870-1926) – the pioneer|last1=October 24th|last2=life|first2=2018{{!}}Academic|date=2018-10-24|website=LSE History|language=en-US|access-date=2019-08-07|last3=History|first3=Department of Economic|last4=People|last5=Comments|first5=Women's history{{!}}0}}</ref>
