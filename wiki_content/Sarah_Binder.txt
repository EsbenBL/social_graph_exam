'''Sarah A. Binder''' is an American [[Political Scientist|political scientist]], author, senior fellow with the [[Brookings Institution]] and serves as professor of political science at [[George Washington University]]'s [[Columbian College of Arts and Sciences|Columbian College of Arts and Science]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://politicalscience.columbian.gwu.edu/sarah-binder|title=Sarah A. Binder {{!}} Department of Political Science {{!}} The George Washington University|website=politicalscience.columbian.gwu.edu|language=en|access-date=2018-12-04}}</ref>

== Early life and education ==
Binder graduated with a B.A. in political sciences from [[Yale University]] in 1986 and obtained a Ph.D. from the [[University of Minnesota]] in 1995.

== Career ==
Binder started her career serving as [[Lee H. Hamilton|Lee Hamilton's]] legislative aide and press secretary from 1986 to 1990. In 1995, Binder became a research fellow at the [[Brookings Institution]], where she serves as a senior fellow in Governance Studies.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://www.brookings.edu/experts/sarah-a-binder/|title=Sarah A. Binder|date=2016-02-16|work=Brookings|access-date=2018-12-04|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://thehill.com/capital-living/cover-stories/112741-the-roots-of-august-recess|title=The roots of August recess|last=Ruyle|first=Megan|date=2010-08-05|work=TheHill|access-date=2018-12-04|language=en}}</ref> She also served as Robert Hartley Research Fellow and in 1999, joined [[George Washington University]], where she serves as a professor of political science today. 

Binder is regularly requested as a political commentator and has been featured in different media outlets for more than two decades.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB94262090283103601|title=Congressional Partisanship Creates a Tower of Babel|work=Wall Street Journal|access-date=2018-12-04|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.rollcall.com/news/harry_reid_move_senate_rule_not_big_deal-209380-1.html|title=Reid's Move on Senate Rule Is Not a Big Deal|date=2011-10-11|work=Roll Call|access-date=2018-12-04|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/02/10/opinion/how-to-use-mcconnells-playbook.html|title=Opinion {{!}} How to Use McConnell's Playbook|access-date=2018-12-04|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/SB10001424127887323848804578605840784361484|title=Closed-Door Showdown on Filibuster Fight|last=Boles|first=Corey|work=Wall Street Journal|access-date=2018-12-04|language=en-US}}</ref>

== Works ==
Binder has authored and co-authored different books and various publications.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/people/sarah-binder/|title=Washington Post: Sarah Binder (body of work)}}</ref>

Among her notable works are:

*''Minority Rights, Majority Rule: Partisanship and the Development of Congress'' (1997)<ref>{{Cite book|title=Minority Rights, Majority Rule: Partisanship and the Development of Congress: Sarah A. Binder: 9780521587921: Amazon.com: Books|isbn = 978-0521587921|last1 = Binder|first1 = Sarah A.|date = 1997-06-13}}</ref>
*''Stalemate: Causes and Consequences of Legislative Gridlock'' (2003)<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://thehill.com/blogs/congress-blog/politics/126479-divided-government-is-bad-for-obama|title=Divided government is bad for Obama|last=Kim|first=Barbra|date=2010-10-29|work=TheHill|access-date=2018-12-04|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book|title = Stalemate: Causes and Consequences of Legislative Gridlock: Sarah A. Binder: 9780815709114: Amazon.com: Books|isbn = 978-0815709114|last1 = Binder|first1 = Sarah A.|year = 2003|url-access = registration|url = https://archive.org/details/stalematecausesc00bind}}</ref>
*''Advice and Dissent: The Struggle to Shape the Federal Judiciary'' (together with [[Forrest Maltzman]], 2009)<ref>{{Cite book|title=Advice and Dissent: The Struggle to Shape the Federal Judiciary: Sarah A. Binder, Forrest Maltzman: 9780815703402: Amazon.com: Books|isbn = 978-0815703402|last1 = Binder|first1 = Sarah A.|last2 = Maltzman|first2 = Forrest|year = 2009}}</ref>
*''The Myth of Independence: How Congress Governs the Federal Reserve'' (together with [[Mark Spindel]], 2017)<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://press.princeton.edu/interviews/qa-11123|title=Princeton University Press: Q & A with Sarah Binder & Mark Spindel}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://press.princeton.edu/titles/11123.html|title=The Myth of Independence|website=Princeton University Press|language=en|access-date=2018-12-04}}</ref>

Binder served as co-editor of the [[John Wiley & Sons|Wiley Library]]'s  publication ''[[Legislative Studies Quarterly]]'' and also serves as and editor and contributor of the [[The Washington Post|Washington Post]]'s Monkey Cage blog.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/page/journal/19399162/homepage/editorialboard.html|title=Editorial Board of Legislative Studies Quarterly}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/monkey-cage/wp/2014/01/01/about-the-monkey-cage/|title=About the Monkey Cage Blog - Editor Sarah Binder}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.rollcall.com/news/nuclear-summer-for-the-senate|title='Nuclear' Summer for the Senate?|last=Lesniewski|first=Niels|date=2013-05-20|work=Roll Call|access-date=2018-12-04|language=en}}</ref>

== Additional affiliations ==
Binder is a member of the [[Center for Effective Public Management]] and serves as President of the [[Midwest Political Science Foundation]] for the 2018–2019 term.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.mpsanet.org/About-MPSA/Governance/Current-Officers|title=Current Officers|website=www.mpsanet.org|language=en-US|access-date=2018-12-04}}</ref> She also chairs the MPSA's publishing-ethics committee, which oversees the editorial process at the ''[[American Journal of Political Science]]'' to guard against conflict-of-interest concerns.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://ajps.org/2018/08/10/new-ajps-submission-requirements/ |website=[[American Journal of Political Science]] |date=2018-08-10 |title=Some Details about New AJPS Submission Requirements |access-date=2018-12-06 |last=Heeke |first=Melissa}}</ref>

== Awards and recognitions ==
In 2003, Binder received the [[American Political Science Association]]'s (APSA) [[Richard F. Fenno Jr.|Richard F. Fenno, Jr.]] Prize, recognizing her book "Stalemate" as the best book in legislative politics.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.apsanet.org/section-3-richard-f-fenno-prize|title=American Political Science Association > MEMBERSHIP > Organized Sections > Organized Section 3: Richard F. Fenno Prize|website=www.apsanet.org|language=en-US|access-date=2018-12-04}}</ref>

In 2015, she became an elected member of the [[American Academy of Arts and Sciences]].<ref name=":0" />

In 2018, she was awarded the APSA's [[Gladys M. Kammerer Award]] for the best book published in the field of U.S. national policy in 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://connect.apsanet.org/apsa2018/2018-award-winners/|title=2018 Award Recipients – 2018 APSA Annual Meeting|website=connect.apsanet.org|language=en-US|access-date=2018-12-04}}</ref>
