'''Christopher Cropper Hood''' {{post-nominals|country=GBR|CBE|FBA}} (born 1947) is a visiting Professor of the [[Blavatnik School of Government]] at the [[University of Oxford]], and an Emeritus Fellow of [[All Souls College, Oxford]].<ref name="AllSouls">{{cite web |url=https://www.asc.ox.ac.uk/person/26 |title=Professor Christopher Hood |work=People |publisher=[[All Souls College, Oxford]] |accessdate=31 October 2015 }}</ref> Hood was [[Gladstone Professor of Government]] at [[All Souls College, Oxford]] from 2001 to 2014, and Director of the [[Economic and Social Research Council|ESRC]] Research Programme ''Public Services: Quality, Performance and Delivery'' from 2004 to 2010. His books include ''The Limits of Administration'' (1976), ''The Tools of Government'' (1983) (updated as ''The Tools of Government in the Digital Age'' (2007) with [[Helen Margetts]]), ''[[The Art of the State]]'' (1998 and 2000) and ''[[A Government that Worked Better and Cost Less?]]'' (2015, with Ruth Dixon). He chaired the [[Nuffield Council on Bioethics]]' Working Party on ''medical profiling and online medicine'' from 2008 to 2010.<ref name="NCBE">{{cite web |url=http://nuffieldbioethics.org/project/personalised-healthcare-0/working-party/ |title=Membership of the Working Party |work=Medical profiling and online medicine |publisher=[[Nuffield Council on Bioethics]] |date=October 2010 |accessdate=31 October 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180615103527/http://nuffieldbioethics.org/project/personalised-healthcare-0/working-party |archive-date=15 June 2018 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

He specialises in the study of executive government, regulation and public-sector reform and has written on [[New Public Management]].<ref name=JPART14>{{cite journal | title=The Middle Aging of New Public Management: Into the Age of Paradox? | journal=Journal of Public Administration Research and Theory | first=Christopher | last=Hood |author2=Guy Peters | volume=14 | issue=3 | pages=267â€“282 | year=2004 | doi=10.1093/jopart/muh019}}</ref>

==Education==
Hood obtained a [[Bachelor of Arts|B.A.]] degree ([[first-class honours]]) in Social Sciences from the [[University of York]] in 1968, and a [[Bachelor of Letters|B.Litt.]] degree from the [[University of Glasgow]] in 1971. He was awarded a [[Doctor of Letters|D.Litt.]] degree from the University of York in 1987.
