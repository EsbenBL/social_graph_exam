'''Anu Muhammad Anisur Rahman''', better known as '''Anu Muhammad''' ({{lang-bn|আনু মুহাম্মদ}}; born 1956), is a [[Bangladesh]]i economist, [[public intellectual]], writer, editor, and political activist. He is the member-secretary of the National Committee to Protect Oil, Gas, Mineral Resources, Power and Ports.<ref name="politicalaffairs">{{cite magazine |date=11 September 2009 |title=Oil Companies: Hands Off Bangladesh |url=http://www.politicalaffairs.net/oil-companies-hands-off-bangladesh/ |magazine=Political Affairs |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160827164507/http://www.politicalaffairs.net/oil-companies-hands-off-bangladesh/ |archive-date=27 August 2016 |access-date=24 August 2016}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.thedailystar.net/author/anu-muhammad |title=Anu Muhammad |website=The Daily Star |access-date=24 August 2016}}</ref><ref name=":0" />

== Career ==
Anu Muhammad is a professor at [[Jahangirnagar University]] where he has been teaching [[economics]] since 1982 and taught [[anthropology]] from 1991 to 2005. He was a visiting professor at the [[University of Winnipeg]] and the [[University of Manitoba]] in 2001 and worked at [[Columbia University]] as a visiting scholar in 1993. His research and written work focus on such areas as current economic and political problems in Bangladesh, [[political economy]], [[globalisation]], social transformation, [[gender]] issues, [[non-governmental organisations]], [[Environmental policy|environment]], and energy. He is the member-secretary of the citizen's movement platform called National Committee to Protect Oil-Gas-Mineral Resources-Power and Ports.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://dailyasianage.com/news/33137|title=Rampal power plant to affect 40m people |date=5 October 2016|work=The Asian Age |location=Dhaka |access-date=29 July 2018|language=en}}</ref>

== Political views ==
Anu is involved in a variety of movements in Bangladesh and his appearance on TV [[talk shows]] with his anti-establishment position led to a death threat he received in February 2008.<ref>[http://www.independent-bangladesh.com/200802272432/country/death-threat-to-anu-condemned.html "Death threat to Anu condemned"], Bangladesh News, 27 February 2008.</ref> He was injured by the police in September 2009 during a demonstration protesting exploration deals granted by the government to two international oil companies.<ref>{{cite news |title=Protesters clash with cops: 30 hurt |url=http://www.thedailystar.net/news-detail-104194 |newspaper=The Daily Star |date=2 September 2009}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title=Motia, Imam and Hanif visit Prof Anu Muhammad at hospital |url=http://bdnews24.com/bangladesh/2009/09/03/motia-imam-and-hanif-visit-prof-anu-muhammad-at-hospital |work=bdnews24.com |date=3 September 2009}}</ref> This event led to a protest by the [[Bangladesh Economic Association]]<ref>{{cite news |title=BEA slams police attack on Prof Anu Muhammad |url=http://www.thefinancialexpress-bd.com/2009/09/05/78177.html |newspaper=The Financial Express |location=Dhaka |date=5 September 2009}}</ref> and to an [[open letter]] condemning the event, signed as it was by intellectuals, academics, and activists from around the world, including, among many others, [[Bertell Ollman]], [[E. San Juan, Jr.]], [[Vijay Prashad]], [[Gopal Balakrishnan]], [[Nurul Kabir]], and by [[Azfar Hussain]], who convened this international signature campaign.<ref name="politicalaffairs"/>
