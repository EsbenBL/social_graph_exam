'''Lars Michael Clausen''' (8 April 1935, [[Berlin]] – 20 May 2010, [[Hamburg]]) was a German [[sociologist]] and professor at the [[University of Kiel]].<ref name="knonline">{{cite news|title=Abschied von einem Universalgelehrten|url=http://www.kn-online.de/lokales/kiel/153880-Abschied-von-einem-Universalgelehrten.html|accessdate=16 June 2010|newspaper=Kieler Nachrichten online|date=27 May 2010|language=German|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20100601010102/http://www.kn-online.de/lokales/kiel/153880-Abschied-von-einem-Universalgelehrten.html|archivedate=1 June 2010}}</ref>

==Life and work==

During World War II, the family lived on the [[Darß]] (in Pomerania). 1944 his father Jürgen Clausen, a movie producer, was killed in action; his mother [[Rosemarie Clausen]], a famous photographer, fled with her three children 1945 to Hamburg, where Lars Clausen attended the [[Christianeum]]. 1955, he took up Business, Economics, Sociology, and History at the universities of Berlin (the [[Free University of Berlin|Free University]]), [[University of Cologne|Cologne]], and [[University of Hamburg|Hamburg]]. 1960, he took his first degree in business in [[University of Hamburg|Hamburg]] (''Dipl.-Kfm.''). He got both his doctorate (''Dr.sc.pol.'') and post-doctoral degree (''Habilitation'') at the [[University of Münster]] (1964 resp. 1968) in sociology, having done field work in [[Zambia]]n industries, 1964—65. After academic teaching in [[University of Münster|Münster]], [[Bielefeld University|Bielefeld]], and The Hague, he was called 1970 to the chair of Sociology at [[Kiel University]].

He specialized in the [[sociology of culture]], of labor, and of [[Sociology of disaster|disaster]], and is chief editor of the Complete Works of [[Ferdinand Tönnies]].

1993 to 1994, Clausen was Chairman of the [[German Society for Sociology]]. He served as well as President of the German Africa Society and as Chairman of the [[Schutzkommission]] of the German Ministry of Interior, 2003—2009. Since 1978, he was President of the [[Ferdinand Tönnies Society]].
