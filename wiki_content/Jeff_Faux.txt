'''Jeff Faux''' (born '''Geoffrey Faux''' in New York City) is the principal founder of the [[Economic Policy Institute]] and was its first president, from 1986 to 2002.<ref name=Bio>{{cite web|title=Biography: Jeff Faux|url=http://www.epi.org/people/jeff-faux/|publisher=Economic Policy Institute|accessdate=1 March 2012}}</ref>  He is now the Institute’s Distinguished Fellow and also serves on its board of directors.

Faux was educated at [[Queens College]], [[George Washington University]], and [[Harvard University]].<ref name=Bio /> He is the author of ''The Servant Economy'' (Wiley, 2012), ''The Global Class War'' (Wiley, 2006),  ''The Party’s Not Over'' (Basic Books, 1996) and co-author of ''Reclaiming America'' (M.E. Sharpe, 1996),  ''Rebuilding America'' (Pantheon, 1984) and ''The Star-Spangled Hustle'' (Doubleday, 1972).

''The Global Class War'' was translated into Spanish and Arabic. In 2009, New York Times reporter John Harwood wrote: “’The distribution of income and opportunity is likely to dominate the next stage of American politics,’ Jeff Faux predicted in ‘The Global Class War’ in 2006. …Then last fall, the financial crisis shoved other concerns aside and began to vindicate Mr. Faux’s prediction.”<ref>{{cite news|last=Harwood|first=John|title=For Populism, a Return to Economic Roots|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2009/03/23/us/politics/23caucus.html|accessdate=1 March 2012|newspaper=New York Times|date=23 March 2009}}</ref>

Faux is also a contributing editor to [[Dissent (American magazine)|Dissent Magazine]]<ref>{{cite web|title=Dissent Magazine |url=http://dissentmagazine.org/display.php?id=masthead |accessdate=1 March 2012 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120306031436/http://www.dissentmagazine.org/display.php?id=masthead |archivedate= 6 March 2012 }}</ref>  and [[The American Prospect]]<ref>{{cite web|title=American Prospect staff page|url=http://prospect.org/meet-staff|accessdate=1 March 2012}}</ref>  and serves on the board of directors of [[Campaign for America's Future]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Campaign for America's Future |url=http://www.ourfuture.org/page/2009052122/campaign-americas-future-board-members |accessdate=1 March 2012 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120121161717/http://www.ourfuture.org/page/2009052122/campaign-americas-future-board-members |archivedate=21 January 2012 }}</ref>

Faux has received a Carnegie Scholar Award from the [[Carnegie Corporation of New York]],<ref>{{cite web|title=Carnegie Corporation Of New York Awards $1.5 Million To 16 "Scholars Of Vision" |url=http://carnegie.org/news/press-releases/story/news-action/single/view/carnegie-corporation-of-new-york-awards-15-million-to-16-scholars-of-vision/ |publisher=Carnegie Corporation of New York |accessdate=1 March 2012 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120914184806/http://carnegie.org/news/press-releases/story/news-action/single/view/carnegie-corporation-of-new-york-awards-15-million-to-16-scholars-of-vision/ |archivedate=14 September 2012 }}</ref> the Weinberg Award from [[Wayne State University]], a fellowship at the [[Harvard Institute of Politics]], and an honorary doctorate from the [[University of New England (United States)|University of New England]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Looking Ahead: Where America's Current Economic Path Will Take Us |url=http://www.une.edu/cgh/lectures/lookingahead.cfm |publisher=University of New England |accessdate=1 March 2012 }}{{dead link|date=April 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> 
 
Prior to starting the Economic Policy Institute, he was the co-director of the Center for Economic Alternatives. He is a former economist for the U.S. Departments of [[United States Department of State|State]], [[United States Department of Commerce|Commerce]], and [[United States Department of Labor|Labor]], and former director of economic development for the U.S. [[Office of Economic Opportunity]].
