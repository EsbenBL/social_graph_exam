'''Adriana Kugler''' is a [[Colombian-American]] economist and professor of public policy at [[Georgetown University]].<ref name=AEI>{{cite web|title=Is uncertainty responsible for high unemployment?|url=http://www.aei.org/events/2012/05/11/is-uncertainty-responsible-for-high-unemployment/|date=May 11, 2012|accessdate=January 5, 2019}}</ref><ref>Announcement - Labor Department appoints CLAS Professor Adriana Kugler as Chief Economist {{cite web|url=http://www.georgetown.edu/story/adriana-kugler-appointment.html|title=Labor Department Appoints Professor as Chief Economist|accessdate=September 8, 2012|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120719083434/http://www.georgetown.edu/story/adriana-kugler-appointment.html|archivedate=July 19, 2012|df=dmy-all}}</ref> She served as the [[Chief economist|Chief Economist]] to [[United States Secretary of Labor|U.S. Labor Secretary]] [[Hilda L. Solis]] from September 6, 2011 to January 4, 2013.<ref name="NBC Latino">{{cite web|title=Women's Wage Gap Is Big Economic Roadblock For Latinas, Says New Report|url=http://nbclatino.com/2012/07/19/womens-wage-gap-is-big-economic-roadblock-for-latinas-says-new-report/|author=Sandra Lilley|work=NBC Latino|date=July 19, 2012|accessdate=January 5, 2019}}</ref><ref name="Dept of Labor">U.S. Department of Labor {{cite web|url=http://www.dol.gov/_sec/aboutosec/bio-kugler.htm|title=Chief Economist â€” Adriana Kugler|accessdate=September 8, 2012|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120917020022/http://www.dol.gov/_sec/aboutosec/bio-kugler.htm|archivedate=September 17, 2012|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

==Education==
Adriana Kugler received her Bachelor of Arts degree from [[McGill University]] in 1991, graduating with first class joint honors in economics and political science. In 1997, she was awarded her Ph.D. by the [[University of California at Berkeley]], Ph.D.; her advisors were Nobel laureate [[George Akerlof]], [[Nada Eissa]], and [[David K. Levine]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www18.georgetown.edu/data/people/ak659/cv.pdf |title=Adriana Kugler C.V. posted at Public Policy Institute Website |access-date=2012-09-08 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140611082433/http://www18.georgetown.edu/data/people/ak659/cv.pdf |archive-date=2014-06-11 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

==Academic appointments==
Dr. Adriana Kugler is Vice-Provost for Faculty and a full professor at the McCourt School of Public Policy.<ref name="AEI"/><ref name="Georgetown">{{cite web|url=https://gufaculty360.georgetown.edu/s/contact/00336000014RkLPAA0/adriana-kugler|title=Faculty Page, Adriana Kugler|work=Georgetown University: McCourt School of Public Policy|accessdate=January 5, 2019}}</ref> She was founder and co-director of the International Summer Institute on Policy Evaluation between 2010-2013. She served as chief economist of the U.S. Department of Labor in 2011 and 2012, where she worked actively on developing policies and proposals on unemployment insurance, training programs, retirement benefits, overtime pay and minimum wages, immigration, disability insurance and occupational safety regulations. Prior to coming to Georgetown, she was a full and associate professor at the economics departments at the [[University of Houston]]  and at [[Pompeu Fabra University]]  in [[Barcelona]].<ref name="Georgetown"/>

Kugler is a research associate with the [[National Bureau of Economic Research]] in the Labor Studies program<ref name="Dr. Kugler: Labor Studies Research Associate with NBER">{{cite web|publisher=National Bureau of Economic Research|title=Adriana D. Kugler|url=http://www.nber.org/people/adriana_kugler|accessdate=January 5, 2019}}</ref> and a research fellow of the Center for Economic Policy Research (CEPR) in London,<ref name="Adriana D. Kugler: Centre for Economic Policy Research">{{cite web|publisher=Centre for Economic Policy Research|title=Professor Adriana D. Kugler|url=https://cepr.org/active/researchers/contact.php?IDENT=139732|accessdate=January 5, 2019}}</ref> the [[Institute for the Study of Labor]] (IZA) in Bonn,<ref>[https://www.iza.org/person/157/adriana-kugler Fellows of the Institute for the Study of Labor]</ref> the Centre for Research and Analysis of Migration (CReAM) in London,<ref>[http://www.cream-migration.org/personneldetail.php?id=59 External Fellows of CReAM]</ref> and the Center for the Study of Poverty and Inequality at Stanford University<ref name=AEI/><ref name="Dept of Labor"/><ref>[https://web.stanford.edu/group/scspi/center_search.html Stanford Center on Poverty and Inequality]</ref> and senior fellow at the Center for American Progress.<ref>https://www.americanprogress.org/</ref>

Kugler has served on the editorial boards of the [[Industrial and Labor Relations Review]],<ref>[http://www.ilr.cornell.edu/ilrreview/staff.html Editorial Board of the Industrial and Labor Relations Review]</ref> The Journal of Labor and Development,<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.iza.org/en/research/publications/izajournals/jodm?page=16|title=IZA Journal of Development and Migration|publisher=[[IZA Institute of Labor Economics]]|accessdate=January 5, 2019}}</ref> the British Journal of Industrial Relations,<ref>[https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/page/journal/14678543/homepage/editorialboard.html Editorial Board, British Journal of Industrial Relations]</ref> [[Labour Economics]],<ref>[https://www.journals.elsevier.com/labour-economics/editorial-board/ Editorial Board of Labour Economics]</ref> Applied Economics Quarterly<ref>[https://elibrary.duncker-humblot.com/journals/id/22/ Applied Economics Quarterly Editorial Board]</ref> and Economia.<ref>[http://www.lacea.org/portal/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=130:adriana-kugler&catid=2&Itemid=105 Economia, Journal of the Latin American and Caribbean Economic Association (LACEA), Editorial Board]</ref>

==Research==
Dr. Adriana Kugler continues to research and work with policies that help stimulate youth employment, as well as observing the effect varieties of policies have on worker mobility and job quality.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://gufaculty360.georgetown.edu/s/faculty-research?id=00336000014RkLPAA0|title=Georgetown University Faculty Directory}}</ref> Within these studies, she has found a variety of results in which she has published within the National Bureau of Economic Research (NBER) as Working Papers.

==Research awards==
Kugler was the 2007 recipient of the John T. Dunlop Outstanding Scholar Award from the [[Labor and Employment Relations Association]], in recognition of her research contributions to the field of labor and industrial relations.<ref name="Adriana Kugler 2007 recipient of John T. Dunlop Scholar Award">{{cite web|publisher=Labor and Employment Relations Association|title=John T. Dunlop Scholar Awards|url=https://www.leraweb.org/john-t-dunlop-scholar-awards|accessdate=January 5, 2019}}</ref>

In 2010, one of her papers, "Trade Reforms and Market Selection: Evidence from Manufacturing Firms in Colombia" won first prize for Best Contribution in the area of "Globalization, Regulations and Development" from the [[Global Development Network]].<ref name="Georgetown"/><ref>[http://www.uh.edu/~adkugler/ps_ehkk.pdf Global Development Network Research Papers]</ref>

Her research interests focus in areas like labor markets and policy evaluation. She has been the recipient of numerous research grants for studies in areas which include the role of public policies, unemployment, and immigration on labor markets and policy evaluation in developed and developing countries.<ref name="Georgetown"/>
