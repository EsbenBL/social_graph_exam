'''Amy Gale Mazur''' (born May 5, 1962) is an American political scientist and professor at [[Washington State University]], as well as an associate researcher at the Centre d’Études Européennes at [[Sciences Po|Sciences Po, Paris]].

== Education ==
Mazur attended [[University of Caen Normandy]] in 1982-1983 and attained her B.A. at [[Colby College]] in 1984. She completed graduate work at [[Sciences Po]] in 1986-1987. She obtained her M.A. in 1986 and in 1992 her Ph.D. in Politics and French Studies at [[New York University]].<ref name="school bio"/>

== Career ==
Mazur's research and teaching interests focus on comparative feminist policy, women’s policy agencies/state feminism, feminist policy implementation, conceptualization and mixed methods, and French politics.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pppa.wsu.edu/documents/2018/04/mazur-cv-4-16-18.pdf/|title=CV|last=|first=|date=|website=|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181214064650/https://pppa.wsu.edu/documents/2018/04/mazur-cv-4-16-18.pdf/|archive-date=2018-12-14|access-date=|url-status=dead}}</ref> She is the co-convener of the Gender Equality Policy in Practice Network (GEPP)<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://news.wsu.edu/2016/04/13/wsu-professor-leads-international-study-womens-rights/|title=WSU professor leads international study of women's rights {{!}} WSU Insider {{!}} Washington State University|date=2016-04-13|website=WSU Insider|language=en-US|access-date=2020-02-16}}</ref> and the associate editor of ''French Politics''. She is one of the founders of the Research Network on Gender Politics and the State (RNGS). From 2006 to 2014, Mazur was coeditor of ''[[Political Research Quarterly]]'', with Cornell Clayton. In 2015, she was a fellow at the [[Birkbeck, University of London|Birkbeck Institute]] for the Humanities; in 2009, a visiting fellow at the Institute for Advanced Study and the [[University of Warwick]]; and in Fall 2001 the Marie-Jahoda Professor of International Feminist Studies at [[Ruhr University Bochum]]. In 2005–2006, she was an expert for the [[United Nations]] for the Expert Group Meeting on Equal Participation of Women and Men in Decision-making Processes and rapporteur of the final meeting report. Mazur has also been consulted by the [[European Union]], the [[World Bank]], the [[International Labour Organization]] and the [[Presidency of Barack Obama|Obama Administration]]. She has received research grants from the [[National Science Foundation]], the [[European Science Foundation]], the French Ministry of Social Affairs and the Norwegian National Science Foundation.<ref name="school bio">{{Cite web|url=https://pppa.wsu.edu/amy-mazur/|title=Meet our Faculty {{!}} PPPA {{!}} Washington State University|website=pppa.wsu.edu|access-date=2018-05-26}}</ref>
