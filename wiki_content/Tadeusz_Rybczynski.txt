'''Tadeusz Mieczysław Rybczyński''' (also '''Rybczynski'''; 1923–1998) was a [[Poland|Polish]]-[[English people|English]] [[economist]] who is known for the development of the [[Rybczynski theorem]] (1955).<ref>{{cite book |editor-first=Mark |editor-last=Blaug |title=Who's Who in Economics |edition=3rd |publisher=Edward Elgar |location=London |isbn=1-85898-886-1 |year=1999 |page=971 }}</ref>

He studied at the [[London School of Economics]]. Soon after discovering his famous theorem, he joined [[Lazard]] and spent the rest of his career there as an [[investment banker]].
