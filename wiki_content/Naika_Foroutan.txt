'''Naika Foroutan''' ({{Lang-fa|نایکا فروتن}}; born 24 December 1971<ref name="kurzbiographie">{{cite web|title=Dr. Naika Foroutan|periodical=Heymat.hu-berlin.de|publisher=|url=http://www.heymat.hu-berlin.de/team/foroutan/kurzbio_foroutan|format=|accessdate=2015-02-17|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110319214726/http://www.heymat.hu-berlin.de/team/foroutan/kurzbio_foroutan|archivedate=2011-03-19|last=|date=|year=|language=|pages=|quote=}}</ref> in [[Boppard]]<ref>{{cite web|title=1. Bundesfachkongress Interkultur 2006 in Stuttgart. Referentenliste|periodical=Bundesfachkongress-interkultur.de|publisher=|url=http://www.bundesfachkongress-interkultur.de/2006/referenten.html|accessdate=2015-02-17|archiveurl=|last=|date=|year=|language=|pages=|quote=}}</ref>) is a German social scientist.

She has been head of the research group "Young Islam-related topics in Germany" (JUNITED) at the Humboldt-Universität Berlin (HU) since 2011; in June 2015, she was appointed professor of "integration research and social policy" by Humboldt-Universität.<ref>Berliner Institut für empirische Integrations- und Migrationsforschung (BIM): [http://www.bim.hu-berlin.de/de/personen/prof-dr-naika-foroutan/ ''Prof. '']</ref> In addition, she is secretary of the five-member board of the German Council for Migration;<ref>{{cite web|url=https://rat-fuer-migration.de/ueber-uns/vorstand/|title=Vorstand|date=18 May 2017|publisher=|accessdate=25 July 2018}}</ref> since 2017 she has also been head of the German Centre for Integration and Migration Research (DeZIM).<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.dezim-institut.de/das-dezim-institut/|title=Das DeZIM-Institut – DeZIM-Institut|website=www.dezim-institut.de|accessdate=25 July 2018}}</ref>

Since May 2018, Foroutan has been the director of the Berlin Institute for Empirical Integration and Migration Research (BIM) and has led the "Integration research and social policy" department.<ref>{{cite web|title=Berliner Institut für empirische Integrations- und Migrationsforschung - Prof. Dr. Naika Foroutan|periodical=|publisher=|url=https://www.bim.hu-berlin.de/de/personen/prof-dr-naika-foroutan/|format=|accessdate=2018-06-28|archiveurl=|last=BIM|date=|year=|language=de-DE|pages=|quote=}}</ref>

== Life ==
Foroutan is the daughter of a German mother and an Iranian father. She is the sister of actress [[Melika Foroutan]]. Her father Bahman Foroutan is a football coach. He was from 2010 to 2014, coach of various amateur clubs in Berlin. Naika Foroutan lived for 11 years in Tehran and left Iran in 1983 together with her family.<ref name="books-NBZ2N3mGM74C-PR11">Naika Foroutan: ''Kulturdialoge zwischen dem Westen und der islamischen Welt: eine Strategie zur Regulierung von Zivilisationskonflikten'', Vorwort.</ref> Foroutan studied political science, romance Philology and Islamic Studies at the [[University of Cologne]]. In the years 2000 to 2004 she received a doctorate at the [[University of Göttingen]] at Bassam Tibi in the thematic area "inter-civilisation cultural dialogues between the West and the Islamic world" as a result, she was there as a lecturer in the field International relations. Between 2006 and 2009, Foroutan taught at the Department of Politics of the Middle East at the Otto-Suhr-Institut of the [[Free University of Berlin|Freie Universität Berlin]], until she then switched to the Institute for Social Sciences of the [[Humboldt University Berlin|Humboldt University of Berlin]]. From 2008 to 2013, together with her colleague [[Isabel Schäfer]], she led the research project "hybrid European-Muslim identity models (HEYMAT)". The HEYMAT project was extended to 2013 by the Volkswagen Foundation until the year 2015. Since the extension, Naika Foroutan has led the HEYMAT project. The HEYMAT project was completed at the end of June 2015.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bim.hu-berlin.de/de/projekte/2008/04/01/2015/06/01/hybride-europaeisch-muslimische-identitaetsmodelle-heymat/|title=Berliner Institut für empirische Integrations- und Migrationsforschung - Hybride europäisch-muslimische Identitätsmodelle (HEYMAT)|last=BIM|website=www.bim.hu-berlin.de|accessdate=25 July 2018}}</ref>

In cooperation with the Mercator Foundation, Foroutan organized the "Young Islam conference-Berlin 2011" for the Humboldt University in Berlin.<ref>{{cite web|title=Junge Islam Konferenz — Heymat|periodical=Heymat.hu-berlin.de|publisher=|url=http://www.heymat.hu-berlin.de/junge-islamkonferenz|format=|accessdate=2015-02-17|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150217204303/http://www.heymat.hu-berlin.de/junge-islamkonferenz|archivedate=2015-02-17|last=|date=2013-10-15|language=|pages=|quote=|url-status=dead}}</ref> In the year 2012, another "young Islam conference" was held under her leadership. During the conference it was announced that the "Young Islam Conference" and a corresponding research project "Young Islam-related issues in Germany"  under Foroutans, would continue up to the year 2016, with two million euros funding by the&nbsp;Mercator foundation.<ref>{{cite web|title=Zwei Millionen Euro für die Junge Islam Konferenz|periodical=Hu-berlin.de|publisher=|url=http://www.hu-berlin.de/pr/pressemitteilungen/pm1204/pm_120423_00|format=|accessdate=2015-02-17|archiveurl=|last=|date=2012-04-19|language=|pages=|quote=}}</ref> Since 2012, she is co-head of the research project "Concepts for the development of intelligence, Security and Prevention" (CODISP), sponsored by the Federal Ministry of Education and Research.<ref name="teamforoutan">{{cite web|title=Naika Foroutan — Heymat|periodical=Heymat.hu-berlin.de|publisher=|url=http://www.heymat.hu-berlin.de/team/foroutan|format=|accessdate=2015-02-17|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130705132626/http://www.heymat.hu-berlin.de/team/foroutan|archivedate=2013-07-05|last=|date=|year=|language=|pages=|quote=|url-status=dead}}</ref>
