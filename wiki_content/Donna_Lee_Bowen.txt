'''Donna Lee Bowen''' is an American political scientist who specializes in studies of family policy in the Middle East.  She is a professor of political science at [[Brigham Young University]] (BYU) where she is also an affiliated faculty member of the Women's Studies Program.

Bowen holds a bachelor's degree in political science from the [[University of Utah]] and an MA and PhD in Near Eastern Languages and Civilizations from the [[University of Chicago]].

Bowen edited ''Everyday Life in the Contemporary Middle East'' with [[Evelyn A. Early]].<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://www.iupress.indiana.edu/product_info.php?products_id=807340 | title=Everyday Life in the Muslim Middle East, Third Edition}}</ref>  She has an upcoming book entitled ''Family Planning, Islam and the State''.  Her most cited article is "Abortion, Islam and the 1994 Cairo Population Conference" published in the ''[[International Journal of Middle East Studies]]'' Vol. 29, issue 2, May 1997.  She has written articles such as "What is the relationship of Inequity in Family Law and Violence Against Women" with [[Valerie M. Hudson]] that was published in 2011 in the journal ''[[Politics and Gender]]''.

Bowen has also appeared on the television show "[[Insite with Jon Du Pre]]".<ref>[https://www.imdb.com/name/nm4950845/ IMDb listing for Bowen]</ref>

The [[American Institute for Maghrib Studies]] has a graduate student travel award named after Bowen.<ref>[http://aimsnorthafrica.org/fellowships/travelawards.cfm AIMS page on giving of the Bowen award] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130503104932/http://aimsnorthafrica.org/fellowships/travelawards.cfm |date=2013-05-03 }}</ref>
