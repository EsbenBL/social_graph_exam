'''Scott Lash''' (born December 23, 1945)<ref name="dob">{{cite web | url=http://id.loc.gov/authorities/names/n83155625.html | title=Lash, Scott - From Library of Congress Name Authority File | publisher=Library of Congress | accessdate=14 May 2014}}</ref> is a professor of [[sociology]] and [[cultural studies]] at [[Goldsmiths, University of London]]. Lash obtained a BSc in Psychology from the [[University of Michigan]], an MA in Sociology from [[Northwestern University]], and a PhD from the [[London School of Economics]] (1980). Lash began his teaching career as a lecturer at [[Lancaster University]] and became a professor in 1993. He moved to London in 1998 to take up his present post as Director for the Centre for Cultural Studies and Professor of Sociology at Goldsmiths College.<ref name="virtualsociety">{{cite web|title=Professor Scott Lash|url=http://virtualsociety.sbs.ox.ac.uk/people/lash.htm|accessdate=14 May 2014|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20151208021158/http://virtualsociety.sbs.ox.ac.uk/people/lash.htm|archivedate=8 December 2015}}</ref>

Lash's work has been influential in sociology and cultural studies, he has been interviewed by numerous media outlets and other academics, including the BBC Radio's ''Essay'' and in ''Design and Culture''.<ref name="BBC">{{cite web | url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b01n11qn | title=Scott Lash on 'Liquid Modernity' | publisher=BBC | accessdate=14 May 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.designculture.info/reviews/ArticleStash/GJLashLuryInterview.pdf | title=DIALOGS Value, Relationality and Unfinished Objects: Guy Julier Interview with Scott Lash and Celia Lury | publisher=Design and Culture | accessdate=14 May 2014 | author=Julier, Guy}}</ref> He has been an editor of the journal ''Theory, Culture and Society'' since 1989.<ref name="virtualsociety" /> His work with co-author [[John Urry (sociologist)|John Urry]] has received a great deal of attention in [[cultural geography]] and [[Gibson Burrell]] stated their work "their book (''The End of Organised Capitalism'') was a huge achievement in the ‘assemblage’ of material, and 25 years of ‘events’ have not been too unkind to it."<ref>{{cite web | url=http://wes.sagepub.com/content/27/3/537.full | title=Book review symposium: Scott Lash and John Urry The End of Organised Capitalism | accessdate=14 May 2014 | author=Burrell, Gibson}}</ref> His works have been translated to 15 languages, and he has directed research projects in the domain of technology and media since 1996.<ref>{{cite web|title=Professor Scott Lash BSc MA PhD|url=http://www.gold.ac.uk/cultural-studies/staff/s-lash/|publisher=Centre for Cultural Studies|accessdate=14 May 2014}}</ref>
