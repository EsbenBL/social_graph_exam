'''Michael Haas''' (born 1938, Detroit, Michigan) is an American political scientist.

==Education and career==
After graduating from [[Hollywood High School]] in 1959, he attended [[Los Angeles City College]] for a semester and then enrolled in [[Stanford University]], where he received his cum laude baccalaureate degree in 1959. [[Yale University]] awarded him a master's degree in 1960. He received his Ph.D. at Stanford in 1964, under the direction of Robert C. North. All degrees were in political science.

During his last year of work on his doctoral degree, he taught temporarily at [[San Jose State University]] from 1963 to 1964. He then accepted a permanent position at the [[University of Hawai`i]]’s main campus in Manoa Valley, [[Honolulu]], where he rose from Assistant Professor to Professor from 1964 to 1971 and remained until 1998.

While on the faculty in Honolulu, he also held temporary positions at [[Northwestern University]], [[Purdue University]], the [[University of California]] (Riverside), [[San Francisco State University]], the [[University of the Philippines]], and the [[University of London]].

His research appointments include a [[United Nations Institute for Training and Research]] (UNITAR) consultancy at the [[United Nations Economic and Social Commission for Asia and the Pacific]] in [[Bangkok]] during 1971 and a [[Fulbright Research Fellowship]] at the [[Institute of Southeast Asian Studies]] on the [[University of Singapore]] campus in 1987.

In 1998 he retired to [[Los Angeles]]. During the next decade, he held temporary positions at [[Loyola Marymount University]], [[California State University, Fullerton]], [[California State University, Los Angeles]], [[California State Polytechnic University, Pomona]], [[Rio Hondo College]], [[College of the Canyons]], and [[Occidental College]]. He ended these teaching positions in 2008.

==Contributions==
Haas's 1964 Ph.D. dissertation, ''Some Societal Correlates of International Political Behavior'' demonstrated that countries go to war after serious unemployment results in increased suicide.

He is the author of ''George W. Bush, War Criminal? The Liability of the Bush Administration for 269 War Crimes'' (2009).<ref name="George W. Bush, war criminal?">{{cite book |title=George W. Bush, war criminal? : the Bush administration's liability for 269 war crimes |url=https://searchworks.stanford.edu/view/7763224 |publisher=Praeger |accessdate=18 January 2020 |date=2009 |isbn=9780313364990 }}</ref> As of 2021, he has authored 61 books and many articles in books, journals, and on websites.

He founded the [[Political Film Society]] in Honolulu during 1986.<ref name="Political Film Society">{{cite web |title=About Political Film Society |url=http://polfilms.com/faqs.html |publisher=Political Film Society |accessdate=7 November 2021 |date= }}</ref> He is president of the [[Political Film Society]], which nominates and gives awards to the best political films each year. Winners receive the most votes from members of the [[Political Film Society]].
