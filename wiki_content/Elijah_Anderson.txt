'''Elijah Anderson''' (* [[1943]] in [[Hermondale, Missouri|Hermondale]], [[Missouri]]) is an American [[sociologist]]. He is the Sterling Professor of Sociology and of African American Studies at [[Yale University]],<ref name="Yale">[http://www.yale.edu/sociology/faculty/pages/anderson/ "Elijah Anderson"] at [[Yale University]].</ref> where he teaches and directs the Urban Ethnography Project. Anderson is one of the nation’s leading urban [[ethnographer]]s<ref>[http://opac.yale.edu/news/article.aspx?id=2390 "Distinguished Sociologist Elijah Anderson Joins Yale Faculty"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110724184224/http://opac.yale.edu/news/article.aspx?id=2390 |date=2011-07-24 }}. Yale Office of Public Affairs & Communications (June 27, 2007), accessed October 03, 2011.</ref> and [[cultural theorist]]s. Anderson is known most notably for his book, ''Code of the Street: Decency, Violence, and the Moral Life of the Inner City'' (1999).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://news.yale.edu/2017/02/24/qa-award-winning-urban-ethnographer-elijah-anderson-illuminating-issues-affect-world|title=Q&A: Award-winning urban ethnographer Elijah Anderson on illuminating issues that affect the world|last=Connolly|first=Bess|date=2017-02-24|website=YaleNews|language=en|access-date=2020-04-24}}</ref>

==Early life==
Anderson received his B.A. from [[Indiana University Bloomington]], his M.A. from the [[University of Chicago]] and  his Ph.D from [[Northwestern University]], where he was mentored by [[Howard S. Becker]].

==Career==
Anderson worked as an assistant professor of Sociology at [[Swarthmore College]] (1973–1975). In 1975, he joined the University of Pennsylvania faculty where he rose to associate professor in 1981, and to full professor in 1988. He was appointed to the Max and Heidi Berry Term Chair in the Social Sciences in 1989,<ref name="Yale"/> to the Charles and William L. Day Professorship in 1991, and then to Distinguished Professor in 2001. He has also served as Visiting Professor at Swarthmore College, [[Princeton University]], and [[École des hautes études en sciences sociales|''Ecole des Etudes Hautes en Science Sociales'']] in [[Paris]], [[France]].

Anderson served for many years as the Charles and William L. Day Distinguished Professor of the Social Sciences and Professor of Sociology at the [[University of Pennsylvania]],<ref>[http://sociology.sas.upenn.edu/elijah_anderson "Elijah Anderson, Ph.D."] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100621182933/https://sociology.sas.upenn.edu/elijah_anderson |date=2010-06-21 }} at [[University of Pennsylvania]], School of Arts and Sciences.</ref> with a secondary appointment in the [[Wharton School]]; in 2008, he was awarded the Charles and William L. Day Distinguished Professor Emeritus of the Social Sciences at the University of Pennsylvania.

Anderson has written and edited numerous books, book chapters, articles, and scholarly reports on race in American cities. His most prominent works include ''Code of the Street: Decency, Violence, and the Moral Life of the Inner City'' (1999),<ref>[http://books.wwnorton.com/books/detail.aspx?ID=6797 "Code of the Street"] at W.W. Norton.</ref> winner of the 2000 Komarovsky Award from the [[Eastern Sociological Society]],<ref>[http://www.essnet.org/AwardKomarovsky.aspx "Mirra Komarovsky Book Award"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101202115058/http://www.essnet.org/AwardKomarovsky.aspx |date=2010-12-02 }} at Eastern Sociological Society.</ref> [https://web.archive.org/web/20100615010852/http://www.press.uchicago.edu/presssite/metadata.epl?mode=synopsis&bookkey=3619575 ''Streetwise: Race, Class, and Change in an Urban Community ''(1990)], winner of the [[American Sociological Association]]’s Robert E. Park Award for the best published book in the area of Urban Sociology,<ref>[http://www.commurb.org/Park_Book_Award.htm "The Robert E. Park Book Award"]</ref> and the classic sociological work, ''A Place on the Corner: A Study of Black Street Corner Men''.<ref>[http://www.press.uchicago.edu/presssite/metadata.epl?mode=synopsis&bookkey=3619613 "A Place on the Corner, Second Edition"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100618002559/http://www.press.uchicago.edu/presssite/metadata.epl?mode=synopsis&bookkey=3619613 |date=2010-06-18 }} at the University of Chicago Press.</ref> In 2008, he edited ''Against the Wall: Poor, Young, Black, and Male''],<ref>[http://www.upenn.edu/pennpress/book/14430.html "Against the Wall: Poor, Young, Black, and Male"] at University of Pennsylvania Press.</ref> which is based on a national conference, "Poor, Young, Black, and Male: A Case for National Action?" which he organized at the University of Pennsylvania in 2006. His most recent work is ''The Cosmopolitan Canopy: Race and Civility in Everyday Life''.<ref>[http://books.wwnorton.com/books/detail.aspx?ID=17200 "The Cosmopolitan Canopy: Race and Civility in Everyday Life"] at W.W. Norton.</ref>

In addition, Anderson has won the Lindback Award for Distinguished Teaching at the University of Pennsylvania,<ref>[http://www.archives.upenn.edu/people/notables/awards/lindback.html "Penn Notables"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130324132120/http://www.archives.upenn.edu/people/notables/awards/lindback.html |date=2013-03-24 }}, University of Pennsylvania, University Archives & Records Center.</ref> and he was named the Robin M. Williams, Jr., Distinguished Lecturer for 1999-2000 by the Eastern Sociological Society.<ref>[http://www.asanet.org/about/presidents/Robin_M_Williams_Jr.cfm "Robin Murphy Williams, Jr."] at American Sociological Society.</ref> In 2006, he was awarded an honorary Doctor of Science degree from Northwestern University. Anderson has served on the Board of Directors of the [[American Academy of Political and Social Science]] and as vice-president of the American Sociological Association. He has served in an editorial capacity for a wide range of professional journals and special publications in his field, including ''[[Qualitative Sociology]], ''[http://eth.sagepub.com/'' Ethnography'']'', [[American Journal of Sociology]], [[American Sociological Review]], [http://www.wiley.com/bw/journal.asp?ref=1535-6841 City & Community], [http://ann.sagepub.com/ Annals of the Society of Political and Social Science]'', and the ''[http://www.blackwellpublishing.com/journal.asp?ref=0309-1317 International Journal of Urban and Regional Research].''  He has also served as a consultant to a variety of government agencies, including the [[White House]], the [[United States Congress]], the [[National Academy of Science]], and the [[National Science Foundation]]. Additionally, he was a member of the [https://web.archive.org/web/20101128094308/http://sites.nationalacademies.org/NRC/index.htm National Research Council’s] [http://www.nap.edu/catalog.php?record_id=4422 Panel on the Understanding and Control of Violent Behavior].

==Confederate statues==
In August 2017, in the wake of the [[Unite the Right rally]] in [[Charlottesville, Virginia]], Anderson argued that [[List of Confederate monuments and memorials|Confederate monuments]] "really impact the psyche of black people."<ref name="ftmunshitrumpsaysitisfoolish">{{cite news|last1=Munshi|first1=Neil|title=Trump says it is 'foolish' to remove Confederate symbols|url=https://www.ft.com/content/e7496854-82a1-11e7-a4ce-15b2513cb3ff|access-date=August 17, 2017|work=Financial Times|date=August 17, 2017}}</ref>
