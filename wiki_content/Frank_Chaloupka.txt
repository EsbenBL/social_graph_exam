'''Frank J. Chaloupka''' (born c. 1962) is an American professor of [[economics]] at the [[University of Illinois at Chicago]] (UIC) and an affiliate of the [[National Bureau of Economic Research]]. He is an expert in the economic analysis of [[substance use disorder]]s and on the effect of prices and substance control policies in affecting the demands for [[tobacco]], [[alcohol (drug)|alcohol]], and [[illicit drugs]]. Chaloupka has authored over a hundred articles and several book chapters on topics within this area.

Chaloupka received his [[Bachelor of Arts|B.A.]] in economics from [[John Carroll University]] in Ohio in May 1984, and his [[Ph.D.]] from the [[City University of New York]] in May 1988, specializing in health economics, industrial organization, labor economics, and applied econometrics. Chaloupka directs Impacteen, a collaboration funded by the [[Robert Wood Johnson Foundation]] which investigates common threats to adolescent health, such as [[obesity]], substance use disorders, and [[tobacco use]]. Impacteen is based at the UIC Institute for Health Research and Policy, and collaborators are at [[RAND]], [[Roswell Park Comprehensive Cancer Center]], and other institutions around the country.

In 2009, Chaloupka was named UIC Researcher of the Year.<ref name="auto">{{cite web|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160303171327/http://www.uic.edu/htbin/cgiwrap/bin/uicnews/articledetail.cgi?id=12708 |title=Economic analysis of unhealthy behaviors ‘has impact in real world’|date=January 28, 2009|first1=Christy|last1=Levy|publisher=UIC News}}</ref> He is the "first winner of the UIC Researcher of the Year award for social science and humanities."<ref name="auto"/>

Chaloupka has a wife Eshell, and three children; Benjamin, Jacob, and Frank.
