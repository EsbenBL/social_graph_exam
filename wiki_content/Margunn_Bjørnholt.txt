'''Margunn Bjørnholt''' (born 9 October 1958 in [[Bø, Telemark]]) is a Norwegian [[sociologist]] and [[economics|economist]]. She is a [[Academic ranks in Norway#Research professor|Research Professor]] at the [[Norwegian Centre for Violence and Traumatic Stress Studies]] (NKVTS) and a Professor of Sociology at the [[University of Bergen]].<ref name="NKVTS" /><ref name=UiB />

Her research focuses on [[gender-based violence]], [[Human migration|migrants]] and [[refugee]]s, [[gender equality]], [[Men's studies|men and masculinities]], [[policy studies]], and several other topics. Her most recent research explores questions of gender, violence and power, including [[Sexual violence|sexual]] and gender-based violence against women migrants and refugees and violence in indigenous [[Sámi]] communities. Her earlier research focused on [[ethical banking]], [[money]], [[monetary system]]s, [[management]] and [[organisational change]] in the [[public sector]].

She has also worked as a consultant, a civil servant, served as an expert to the [[European Commission]] and been President of the [[Norwegian Association for Women's Rights]].

==Background and career==

She studied [[economics]], [[regional planning]], [[politics]] and [[contemporary history]]; she holds a [[cand.mag.]] degree from the [[University of Tromsø]] (1981), an MA in [[international economics|European economic studies]] from the [[College of Europe]] (1982), a [[Magister (degree)#Denmark and Norway|mag.art.]] (PhD) in [[economic sociology]] from the [[University of Oslo]] (1995), with a dissertation on [[microfinance]], [[ethical banking|ethical]] and [[interest]]-free [[banking]], and a PhD in the field of [[men's studies]] from [[Örebro University]] (2014), with the dissertation ''Modern Men''. The dissertation, influenced and advised by psychologist [[Margot Bengtsson]], employs social psychological and sociological perspectives on intergenerational [[Cultural learning|transmission]] and [[social change]]. She was awarded full professor competence in 2015.<ref name=UiB /><ref name=kv-presidents>{{cite web |url= http://kvinnesak.no/presidents/margunn-bjornholt/ |title= Margunn Bjørnholt |publisher=[[Norwegian Association for Women's Rights]] |access-date=18 March 2019}}</ref><ref name="mb-bio">{{cite web |url= http://www.margunnbjornholt.no/bio/ |title= Biography |access-date=18 March 2019}}</ref>

In the 1980s and early 1990s she worked at the [[Regional Development Fund]], the [[National Institute of Technology (Norway)|National Institute of Technology]], and as a partner in a consultancy, promoting [[regional development]] and [[female entrepreneur|women's entrepreneurship]] and leadership.<ref>{{cite news |last=Evensen |first=Kjell |date=19 September 1987 |title=Kvinner har de beste idéene |url=http://urn.nb.no/URN:NBN:no-nb_digavis_dagensnaeringsliv_null_null_19870919_98_213_1 |work=[[Dagens Næringsliv]] | page=20 }}</ref> From 1993 she was affiliated with the Project for an Alternative Future, a research program at what is now the [[Centre for Development and the Environment]] at the [[University of Oslo]]. Since 1997 she has worked as a researcher and professor at a number of research institutions including the [[Work Research Institute]], the [[University of Oslo]] and the [[University of Bergen]]. In 2016 she became a [[Academic ranks in Norway#Research professor|Research Professor]] at the [[Norwegian Centre for Violence and Traumatic Stress Studies]] (NKVTS) at the University of Oslo.<ref name="NKVTS">{{cite web |url= https://www.nkvts.no/english/employee/bjornholt-margunn/|title= Bjørnholt, Margunn |publisher=[[Norwegian Centre for Violence and Traumatic Stress Studies]] |access-date=25 February 2016}}</ref> In 2021 she also became a Professor of Sociology at the [[University of Bergen]].<ref name=UiB>{{cite web |title=Margunn Bjørnholt, Professor |url=https://www.uib.no/en/persons/Margunn.Bj%C3%B8rnholt|publisher=[[University of Bergen]]}}</ref><ref name=CNRS>{{cite web |title=Margunn Bjørnholt |url=https://gbvmigration.cnrs.fr/members/ |publisher=[[CNRS]] |access-date=1 July 2021}}</ref>

Fineman]]) at [[Emory University School of Law|Emory Law]],<ref>{{cite web |url= http://web.gs.emory.edu/vulnerability/Affiliated%20Faculty/bjornholt.html |title= Margunn Bjørnholt |publisher= [[Emory ==Research==
Her research interests include [[gender-based violence]], [[Human migration|migration]], [[gender equality]], [[men's studies|men and masculinities]], [[organisation]], [[policy studies]] and several other topics. She has published papers in ''[[The Sociological Review]]'', the ''[[Journal of European Social Policy]]'', ''[[Qualitative Research (journal)|Qualitative Research]]'', ''[[Retfærd]]'', the ''[[Nordic Journal of Criminology]]'', the ''Journal of Gender-Based Violence'', ''[[Norma (journal)|Norma]]'', ''[[Fathering (journal)|Fathering]]'', ''Central and Eastern European Migration Review'', and other journals.

Her research in the 1990s focused on [[ethical banking]], [[money]] and [[monetary system]]s. From the late 1990s she focused on [[management]] and [[organisational change]] in the [[public sector]], particularly organisational and spatial flexibility. Her research on working life led her to work–family issues and [[men's studies]], and from the 2000s she has published widely on changes over time and generations in men's work–family practices and Since the 2010s her research has focused on questions of gender, violence and power. She has led several research projects at [[NKVTS]] funded by the [[Ministry of Justice and Public Security]], including a project on [[intimate partner violence]] with a focus on the importance of gender and power relations<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nkvts.no/english/project/intimate-partner-violence-gender-equality-and-power/|title=Intimate partner violence: gender, equality and power|publisher=NKVTS}}</ref> and a project on violence in indigenous [[Sámi]] communities.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nkvts.no/prosjekt/vold-i-naere-relasjoner-i-samiske-samfunn-2/|title=Vold i nære relasjoner i samiske samfunn II|publisher=NKVTS}}</ref> She currently heads the Norwegian part of an EU-funded research project on [[Sexual violence|sexual]] and [[gender-based violence]] against women [[Human migration|migrants]] and [[refugee]]s, in cooperation with [[Jane Freedman]], [[Ruth Halperin-Kaddari]] and researchers in six other countries in Europe, the Middle East and Canada. The aim of the project is to make policy recommendations for reducing women's vulnerability to sexual and gender-based violence.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nkvts.no/english/project/violence-against-women-migrants-and-refugees-analysing-causes-and-effective-policy-response/|title=Violence against women migrants and refugees: analysing causes and effective policy response|publisher=NKVTS}}</ref> She co-edited a book on ''Men, Masculinities and Intimate Partner Violence'' with [[Lucas Gottzén]] and [[Floretta Boonzaier]], and a book on violence in close relations with [[Kristin Skjørten]] and others.

Other research fields include theories of [[social justice]], the [[welfare state]], [[human rights]], and [[feminist economics]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://kilden.forskningsradet.no/c17223/ekspert/vis.html?tid=88115 |title=Margunn Bjørnholt |work=Expert database for gender research |publisher=[[Research Council of Norway]] |access-date=4 October 2014 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20141006164100/http://kilden.forskningsradet.no/c17223/ekspert/vis.html?tid=88115 |archive-date=6 October 2014 |df=dmy-all }}</ref> She has cooperated with the American legal theorist [[Martha Albertson Fineman]] for a number of years and edited an issue of the journal ''[[Retfærd]]'' on Fineman's vulnerability theory in 2013. She was co-editor, with the Scottish economist [[Ailsa McKay]], of the 2014 book ''Counting on [[Marilyn Waring]]: New Advances in Feminist Economics''.<ref>{{cite web |url= http://www.forskning.no/artikler/2014/mai/391278 |title= – Klart vi kan jobbe mindre |author= Kristin Marie Skaar |date= 24 May 2014 |publisher= [[forskning.no]] |access-date= 8 July 2014 |archive-url= https://web.archive.org/web/20140714162456/http://www.forskning.no/artikler/2014/mai/391278 |archive-date= 14 July 2014 |url-status= dead }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/2013-06-18/women-unaccounted-for-in-global-economy-proves-waring-influence.html |title= Women Unaccounted for in Global Economy Proves Waring Influence |last1= Langeland |first1=Terje |date= 18 June 2013 |publisher=[[Bloomberg L.P.|Bloomberg.com]]}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal |last= Sullivan |first=T.E. |year=2014 |title= Review: Counting on Marilyn Waring: new advances in feminist economics |journal=[[Choice: Current Reviews for Academic Libraries]] |volume=52 |issue=3 |pages= 52–1517|doi= 10.5860/CHOICE.185300 }}</ref><ref>{{cite journal |last= Elson |first=Diane |author-link= Diane Elson |year= 2015 |title= Book Review: Counting on Marilyn Waring: new advances in feminist economics |url=http://www.palgrave-journals.com/fr/journal/v109/n1/full/fr201458a.html |journal= Feminist Review |volume=109 |pages= e9–e11|doi= 10.1057/fr.2014.58 |access-date=25 February 2015 |issue=1|doi-access= free }}</ref> She has also published works on the contemporary use of [[intangible cultural heritage]], the relationship between [[learning]] and [[architecture]], and [[research methods]].

==Civic and political activities==
[[File:NKF Presidents Torild Skard and Margunn Bjørnholt, Palais des Nations, Geneva, 2014.jpg|thumb|NKF President Margunn Bjørnholt (right) and former NKF President [[Torild Skard]] in front of the painting ''The Dream of Peace'', [[Palace of Nations]], Geneva, 2014]]
In the early 1980s she was a journalist for the feminist radio station [[radiOrakel]]. She was involved in the [[ethical banking]] movement in the early 1990s as chair of a working group attempting to start a bank in Norway modelled after, and in cooperation with, [[JAK Members Bank]] in Sweden.<ref>"[https://urn.nb.no/URN:NBN:no-nb_digavis_telemarkarbeiderblad_null_null_19911025_69_247_1 Hundre interesserte til alternativ bank]," ''Telemark Arbeiderblad'' 25 October 1991 p. 5</ref> She has been President of the [[Norwegian Association for Women's Rights]] (2013–2016),<ref name="aftenposten2014">{{cite news |title= Mannsforsker ny leder i Norsk Kvinnesaksforening |url=http://www.aftenposten.no/nyheter/iriks/Mannsforsker-ny-leder-i-Norsk-Kvinnesaksforening-7563258.html |newspaper=[[Aftenposten]] |date=2014-05-11 }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title= Amal Aden tildelt Gina Krog-prisen |url=http://www.adressa.no/nyheter/innenriks/2016/05/21/Amal-Aden-tildelt-Gina-Krogh-prisen-12769416.ece |newspaper=[[Adresseavisen]] |date=2016-05-21 }}</ref> President of the [[Norwegian Women's Lobby]] (2014–2016)<ref name="kv-bio">{{cite web |url= http://www.kvinnelobby.no/en/people/margunn-bjornholt/ |title= Margunn Bjørnholt |publisher=Norwegian Women's Lobby |access-date=18 March 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |title= Norsk kvinnebevegelse slår seg sammen i ny lobby  |url=http://www.kureren.no/artikler/41326 |newspaper=Kureren |date= 29 January 2014 |access-date=4 October 2014}}</ref> and a board member of the [[International Alliance of Women]] (2013–2017).<ref name=kv-presidents /> She was appointed by the Norwegian government as a member of the Norwegian delegation to the 59th and 60th sessions of the UN [[Commission on the Status of Women]] (CSW).<ref name=kv-presidents /> She was a candidate for the [[Green Party (Norway)|Green Party]] in the [[2015 Norwegian local elections|2015 elections]].<ref>[http://www.budstikka.no/nyheter/nyheter/han-topper-barums-gronne/s/2-2.310-1.8666945 Han topper Bærums grønne], ''Budstikka'', 26 November 2014</ref>
