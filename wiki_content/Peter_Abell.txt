'''Peter Abell''' (born 1939) is a British [[social scientist]], currently professor emeritus at the [[London School of Economics]] where he has founded and directed the "Interdisciplinary Institute of Management".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lse.ac.uk/management/documents/cv/PAbell-CV-Jan-2011.pdf|title=Peter Abell's CV 2011|publisher=[[London School of Economics]]|access-date=25 February 2014}}</ref> He has been teaching for many years at LSE's Department of Management,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.lse.ac.uk/management/people/emeriti-visiting-staff/pabell.aspx|title=Professor Peter Abell|last=Science|first=London School of Economics and Political|website=London School of Economics and Political Science|language=en-GB|access-date=2020-02-10}}</ref> managerial economics and strategy group.

==Work==
He is known for his contribution to mathematical social science, both quantitative and qualitative. He is the author of several books on methodology and individual participation and co-operation<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.european-academy-sociology.eu/fellows/abell.html|title=European Academy of Sociology - Fellows|publisher=[[European Academy of Sociology]]|access-date=19 February 2014}}</ref> and currently focuses on an approach he coined [[Narrative#Mathematical sociology approach|''Bayesian narratives'']] and on [[Social network|network analysis]] particularly the role of [[Balance theory|signed structures]]  in group formation and identity change.
