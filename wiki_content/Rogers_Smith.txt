'''Rogers M. Smith''' (born September 20, 1953) is an American [[political scientist]] and [[author]] noted for his research and writing on American [[Constitution of the United States|constitutional]] and political development and [[political theory|political thought]], with a focus on issues of [[citizenship]] and [[Race (classification of human beings)|racial]], [[gender]], and [[Social class|class]] [[Social inequality|inequalities]].<ref name="Bredbenner">{{cite journal |last1=Bredbenner |first1=Candice |last2=Smith |first2=Rogers M. |title=Civic Ideals: Conflicting Visions of Citizenship in U. S. History |journal=The American Journal of Legal History |date=October 1998 |volume=42 |issue=4 |pages=434 |doi=10.2307/846055 |url=https://www.jstor.org/stable/846055 |access-date=27 September 2021}}</ref><ref name="Fredrickson">{{cite book |last1=Fredrickson |first1=George M. |title=The Historical Construction of Race and  Citizenship in the United States |date=October 2003 |publisher= United Nations  Research Institute  for Social Development |location=Geneva 10, Switzerland |url=https://www.unrisd.org/80256b3c005bccf9/(httpauxpages)/8a0ae7eacd11f278c1256dd6004860ea/$file/fredrick.pdf |access-date=28 September 2021}}</ref> His work identifying multiple, competing traditions of national identify including “liberalism, republicanism, and ascriptive forms of Americanism” has been described as "groundbreaking".<ref name="Mylonas">{{cite journal |last1=Mylonas |first1=Harris |last2=Tudor |first2=Maya |title=Nationalism: What We Know and What We Still Need to Know |journal=Annual Review of Political Science |date=11 May 2021 |volume=24 |issue=1 |pages=109–132|doi-access=free |doi=10.1146/annurev-polisci-041719-101841 |url=https://www.annualreviews.org/doi/full/10.1146/annurev-polisci-041719-101841 |access-date=27 September 2021}}</ref><ref name="Smith">{{cite book |last1=Smith |first1=Rogers |title=Civic Ideals: Conflicting Visions of Citizenship in U.S. History |date=1997 |publisher=Yale Univ. Press |location=New Haven, CT |page=550}}</ref><ref name="Bonikowski">{{cite journal |last1=Bonikowski |first1=Bart |title=Nationalism in Settled Times |journal=Annual Review of Sociology |date=30 July 2016 |volume=42 |issue=1 |pages=427–449 |doi=10.1146/annurev-soc-081715-074412 |url=https://scholar.harvard.edu/files/bonikowski/files/bonikowski_-_nationalism_in_settled_times.pdf |access-date=28 September 2021}}</ref> 
Smith is the Christopher H. Browne [[Distinguished Professor]] of [[Political Science]] at the [[University of Pennsylvania]].<ref name="Penn">{{cite web |title=Rogers Smith: Christopher H. Browne Distinguished Professor of Political Science |url=https://web.sas.upenn.edu/endowed-professors/rogers-smith/ |website=University of Pennsylvania School of Arts & Sciences |access-date=27 September 2021}}</ref> He was the  president of the [[American Political Science Association]] (APSA) for 2018–2019.<ref name="Citizens"/>

==Education==
Born in [[Spartanburg, South Carolina]], and raised in [[Springfield, Illinois]],<ref name="Popp">{{cite news |last1=Popp |first1=Trey |title=Who is America? |url=https://thepenngazette.com/who-is-america/ |access-date=28 September 2021 |work=The Pennsylvania Gazette |date=26 Oct 2018}}</ref> Smith graduated with a [[Bachelor of Arts|B.A.]] in [[political science]] from James Madison College, [[Michigan State University]] in 1974, including [[study abroad]] at the [[University of Kent]] in [[England]]. He attended [[graduate school]] at [[Harvard University]],<ref name="Whittington">{{cite journal |last1=Whittington |first1=Keith E. |title=Rogers M. Smith: Stories We Tell Ourselves |journal=PS: Political Science & Politics |date=October 2018 |volume=51 |issue=4 |pages=895–899 |doi=10.1017/S1049096518001348 |url=https://doi.org/10.1017/S1049096518001348 |access-date=28 September 2021|doi-access=free }}</ref> completing his [[Master of Arts|M.A.]] in 1978 and his [[PhD]] degree in [[political science|government]] in 1980.<ref name="GRASPP">{{cite web |title=SMITH, Rogers |url=http://www.pp.u-tokyo.ac.jp/graspp-old/en/faculty/professors/RogersSmith.htm |website=GRASPP |access-date=28 September 2021}}</ref>

==Career==
Smith taught at [[Yale University]]<ref name="Whittington"/> from 1980 to 2001, as the Alfred Cowles Professor of Government and the co-director of the Center for the Study of Race, Inequality, and Politics.
In 2001 he moved to the [[University of Pennsylvania]], where he is the Christopher H. Browne [[Distinguished Professor]] of [[Political Science]].<ref name="Penn"/>

Smith was the founding chair of the Penn Program on Democracy, Citizenship, and Constitutionalism, later the Andrea Mitchell Center for the Study of Democracy, from 2006 to 2017.  Smith was also a co-founder of the Teachers Institute of Philadelphia, a partnership between the university and public schools.<ref name="DiSanto"/> He co-chaired its advisory council from 2006 until 2018.  Smith was associate dean for the social sciences in the School of Arts and Sciences at the University of Pennsylvania from 2014 to 2018.<ref name="Feiner">{{cite news |last1=Feiner |first1=Lauren |title=Political Scientist Rogers Smith to be Associate Dean of Social Sciences |url=https://www.thedp.com/article/2014/04/rogers-smith-associate-dean-social-sciences |access-date=28 September 2021 |work=The Daily Pennsylvanian |date=April 30, 2014}}</ref>  

Smith was president of the Politics and History section of [[American Political Science Association]] (APSA) for 2001–2002 and served on the APSA Council in 2005 and 2006. He was vice president of the American Political Science Association in 2008–2009 and co-president of the Migration and Citizenship section of APSA from 2013 to 2015. <ref name="Elect">{{cite web |title=Rogers Smith – President-Elect |url=https://connect.apsanet.org/s11/new-2017-apsa-council-members/ |website=Religion and Politics  Section 11 of the American Political Science Association|date=September 3, 2017 |access-date=28 September 2021}}</ref>
He served as president of the American Political Science Association in 2018–2019.<ref name="DiSanto">{{cite news |last1=DiSanto |first1=Jill |title=Penn’s Rogers Smith Selected as President-Elect of American Political Science Association |url=https://penntoday.upenn.edu/news/penns-rogers-smith-selected-president-elect-american-political-science-association |access-date=28 September 2021 |work=Penn Today |date=September 8, 2017}}</ref><ref name="Citizens">{{cite web |title=Our Story |url=https://thecitizenscampaign.org/about/our-story/ |website=The Citizens Campaign |access-date=28 September 2021}}</ref>

==Recognition==
Smith's writings have received numerous awards. ''Civic Ideals'' (1997) was a finalist for the 1998 [[Pulitzer Prize]] in [[history]],<ref name="Pulitzer">{{cite web |title=Finalist: Civic Ideals: Conflicting Visions of Citizenship in U.S. History, by Rogers M. Smith (Yale University Press) |url=https://www.pulitzer.org/finalists/rogers-m-smith |website=The Pulitzer Prizes |access-date=28 September 2021}}</ref> and won several awards from the [[American Political Science Association]] (APSA), the [[Organization of American Historians]], and the Social Science History Association.<ref name="AAPSS">{{cite web |title=Rogers Smith2011 Theodore Roosevelt Fellow |url=https://www.aapss.org/fellow/rogers-smith/ |website=AAPSS |access-date=28 September 2021}}</ref>

Smith was elected a [[fellow]] of the [[American Academy of Arts and Sciences]] in 2004; a fellow of the American Academy of Political and Social Science in 2011;<ref name="Marback">{{cite book |last1=Marback |first1=Richard |title=The Meaning of Citizenship |date=Nov 15, 2015 |publisher=Wayne State University Press |location=Detroit, MI |page=359 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=_ivmCgAAQBAJ&pg=PA359 |access-date=28 September 2021}}</ref> and a member of the [[American Philosophical Society]] in 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|title=APS Member History|url=https://search.amphilsoc.org/memhist/search?creator=Rogers+M.+Smith&title=&subject=&subdiv=&mem=&year=&year-max=&dead=&keyword=&smode=advanced|access-date=2021-02-18|website=search.amphilsoc.org}}</ref>
