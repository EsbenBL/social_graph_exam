The '''British Election Study''' is an academic project to analyse the results of British elections<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.britishelectionstudy.com/|title=The British Election Study|website=www.britishelectionstudy.com}}</ref> that has examined every [[general election]] in the United Kingdom since 1964.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://beta.ukdataservice.ac.uk/datacatalogue/series/series?id=200003|title=UK Data Service â€º Series|website=beta.ukdataservice.ac.uk}}</ref> The lead researchers are based at the [[University of Oxford]] and the [[University of Manchester]].

The first study was conducted by [[David Butler (psephologist)|David Butler]] and [[Donald E. Stokes]]. Since then the BES has provided data to help researchers understand changing patterns of party support and election outcomes.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://esrc.ukri.org/research/our-research/british-election-study/|title=British Election Study - Economic and Social Research Council|website=esrc.ukri.org}}</ref> The British Election Study (BES) is one of the longest running election studies world-wide and the longest running social science survey in the UK.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.britishelectionstudy.com/get-started/#.W9di1xNKiqA|title=Get Started - The British Election Study|website=www.britishelectionstudy.com|language=en-GB|access-date=2018-10-29}}</ref>

==Methodology==
Each wave of the study involves online surveys of around 30,000 people.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.britishelectionstudy.com/data-objects/panel-study-data/|title=Data Categories  Panel study data - The British Election Study|website=www.britishelectionstudy.com}}</ref>
