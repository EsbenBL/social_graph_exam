'''Adolphus G. Belk, Jr.''' is a political analyst and professor of political science and African American studies.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.chron.com/CDA/archives/archive.mpl/2007_4442645/campaign-2008-clinton-and-obama-test-black-women-s.html|title=Archives - Houston Chronicle|website=Chron.com|accessdate=19 August 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2007/10/14/us/politics/14carolina.html|title=Clinton-Obama Quandary for Many Black Women|first=Katharine Q.|last=Seelye|date=14 October 2007|accessdate=19 August 2017|website=NYTimes.com}}</ref> He was born in New York to Mrs. Azalia Belk and Mr. Adolphus Belk, Sr. He currently teaches at [[Winthrop University]] with a focus on issues of race and politics.<ref name=autogenerated2>[http://faculty.winthrop.edu/belka/abjr.htm Winthrop University - Dr. Adolphus G. Belk, Jr<!-- Bot generated title -->] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100625225733/http://faculty.winthrop.edu/belka/abjr.htm |date=June 25, 2010 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=18296098|title=Candidates Exchange Jabs at Democratic Debate|website=Npr.org|accessdate=19 August 2017}}</ref> He also specializes in the "[[prison-industrial complex]]."<ref>[http://sentencingproject.org/detail/clearinghouse.cfm?clearinghouse_id=412 The Sentencing Project Clearinghouse Data - A New Generation of Native Sons: Men of Color & the Prison-Industrial Complex<!-- Bot generated title -->] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304095947/http://sentencingproject.org/detail/clearinghouse.cfm?clearinghouse_id=412 |date=March 4, 2016 }}</ref><ref name=autogenerated1>{{cite web|url=http://www.odu.edu/ao/instadv/vol34issue12/news.htm|title=The Courier Online News&Notes - Volume 34, No. 12 - June 10, 2005|website=www.odu.edu|accessdate=19 August 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100610072220/http://www.odu.edu/ao/instadv/vol34issue12/news.htm|archive-date=10 June 2010|url-status=dead}}</ref> He is a political commentator and has published in periodicals such as ''Time'' and ''Democrat''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://thetandd.com/news/article_fba19699-1c2b-5374-a4a7-17464e27d1fc.html|title=Blacks moving away from Clinton to Obama, poll shows|website=Thetandd.com|accessdate=19 August 2017}}</ref> He was a guest editor for a special issue of the ''Journal of Race and Policy'', in which he published twice.<ref name=autogenerated2 />

==Career==
He was a double major, receiving a B.A. in [[Department of African American Studies - Syracuse University|African American Studies]] and a B.A. in Political Science from Syracuse University. He graduated summa cum laude and [[Phi Beta Kappa]]. He received an M.A. and Ph.D. at the [[University of Maryland]].
