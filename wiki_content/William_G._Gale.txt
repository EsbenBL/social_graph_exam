'''William G. "Bill" Gale''' is the Arjay and Frances Miller Chair in Federal Economic Policy and the former vice president and director of the Economic Studies Program at the [[Brookings Institution]].<ref name='brookings-chair'>{{Cite web |url=https://www.brookings.edu/news-releases/ex-ford-motor-company-president-arjay-miller-and-frances-fearing-miller-endow-chair-in-federal-economic-policy-at-the-brookings-institution/ |title=Ex-Ford Motor Company President Arjay Miller and Frances Fearing Miller Endow Chair in Federal Economic Policy at the Brookings Institution |date=2001-10-22 |website=Brookings |access-date=2021-01-16}}</ref> He conducts research on a variety of economic issues, focusing particularly on tax policy, fiscal policy, pensions and saving behavior. He is also co-director of the [[Tax Policy Center]], a joint venture of the Brookings Institution and the [[Urban Institute]].  Gale attended [[Duke University]] and the [[London School of Economics]] and received his Ph.D. from [[Stanford University]] in 1987.

Prior to joining Brookings in 1992, he was an assistant professor in the Department of Economics at the [[University of California]], [[Los Angeles, California|Los Angeles]], and a senior staff economist for the [[Council of Economic Advisers]] under President [[George H.W. Bush]].

==Publications==
Gale is the author of ''Fiscal Therapy: Curing America’s Debt Addiction and Investing in the Future'' (Oxford University Press, 2019).<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://global.oup.com/academic/product/fiscal-therapy-9780190645410?cc=us&lang=en&|title=Fiscal Therapy: Curing America's Debt Addiction and Investing in the Future|publisher=Oxford University Press|isbn=9780190645410|location=Oxford, New York}}</ref>

He has also co-authored or co-editored several books, including ''Automatic: Changing the Way America Saves'' (Brookings, 2011),<ref>{{cite book|first1=William G. |last1=Gale|first2=J. Mark |last2=Iwry|first3=David C. |last3=John|first4=Lina |last4=Walker|title=Automatic: Changing the Way America Saves|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=HzJJqBV0oLYC|date=1 December 2011|publisher=Brookings Institution Press|isbn=978-0-8157-0382-2}}</ref> ''Aging Gracefully: Ideas to Improve Retirement Security in America'' (Century Foundation, 2006); ''The Evolving Pension System: Trends, Effects, and Proposals for Reform'' (Brookings, 2005); ''Private Pensions and Public Policy'' (Brookings, Los Angeles, California 2004); ''Rethinking Estate and Gift Taxation'' (Brookings, 2001), and ''Economic Effects of Fundamental Tax Reform'' (Brookings, 1996).

Gale has written numerous scholarly research articles, including publications in the [[American Economic Review]], [[Journal of Political Economy]], and [[Quarterly Journal of Economics]],<ref>{{cite web|title=William Glenn Gale|url=https://econpapers.repec.org/RAS/pga40.htm|publisher=EconPapers}}</ref> and has served as editor and editorial board member of several academic journals. He has also written extensively in policy-related publications and newspapers.  Gale has served on advisory boards for the [[Government Accountability Office]], the [[Internal Revenue Service]], the [[Joint Committee on Taxation]] and the Board of the [[Center on Federal Financial Institutions]].
