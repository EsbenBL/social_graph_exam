'''Richard A. Brealey''' is a British economist and author. He is an [[Emeritus Professor]] at the [[London Business School]] and a [[British_Academy#Fellowship|Fellow of the British Academy]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.britac.ac.uk/fellowship/directory/ord.cfm?member=3175 |title=Record for: BREALEY, Professor Dick], britac.ac.uk|archive-url=https://archive.today/CCHf5|archive-date=2014-06-19}}</ref> He co-authored ''[[Principles of Corporate Finance]]'' with [[Stewart Myers|Stewart C. Myers]] and [[Franklin Allen]] (now in its thirteenth edition).

He was a full-time faculty member of the London Business School from 1968-1998.{{citation needed|date=January 2011}} He has held the posts of Director of the [[American Finance Association]] and President of the [[European Finance Association]].  He is a member of the editorial boards of the ''[[Journal of Applied Corporate Finance]]'', ''[[Journal of Empirical Finance]]'',<ref>[http://www.journals.elsevier.com/journal-of-empirical-finance/editorial-board/ editorial-board], Journal of Empirical Finance </ref> and ''[[European Finance Review]]''.<ref name="london"/>

Professor Brealey is a director of the Swiss Helvetia Fund and a former director of HSBC Investor Funds, [[Sun Life Financial|Sun Life Assurance Company of Canada UK Holdings plc]], and [[The Bank of Tokyo-Mitsubishi UFJ|Tokai Derivative Products Ltd]].{{citation needed|date=January 2011}} He was formerly a special adviser to the Governor of the [[Bank of England]].<ref name="london">{{cite web|url=http://www.london.edu/facultyandresearch/faculty/search.do?uid=rbrealey|title=Dick Brealey (profile)|publisher=London Business School|access-date=23 January 2011|archive-url=https://archive.today/9htU|archive-date=2012-12-12}}</ref>

He was educated at [[Exeter College, Oxford]], with an [[Master of Arts|M.A.]] in [[Philosophy, Politics and Economics]].<ref name="brattle">{{cite web|url=http://www.brattle.com/experts/richard-a-brealey |title=Richard A. Brealey (biography)|publisher=The Brattle Group|access-date=23 January 2011|archive-url=https://archive.today/1dmEK|archive-date=2014-06-19}}</ref>
