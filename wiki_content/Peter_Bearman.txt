'''Peter Shawn Bearman''' (born 1956)<ref name=AAAS /> is an American [[sociologist]], notable for his contributions to the fields of [[adolescent health]], [[research design]], [[structural analysis]], [[Content analysis|textual analysis]], [[oral history]] and [[social networks]]. He is the Jonathan R. Cole Professor of Social Science in the Department of Sociology at [[Columbia University]], the President of the American Assembly at Columbia University, as well as the director of the Interdisciplinary Center for Innovative Theory and Empirics ([https://www.incite.columbia.edu/ INCITE]). He is also the founding director of the [[Institute for Social and Economic Research and Policy]], and co-founding director of Columbia's [http://oralhistory.columbia.edu/ Oral History Master of Arts Program], the first oral history masters program in the country. He was elected a Fellow of the [[American Academy of Arts and Sciences]] in 2008,<ref name=AAAS>{{cite web|title=Book of Members, 1780-2010: Chapter B|url=http://www.amacad.org/publications/BookofMembers/ChapterB.pdf|publisher=American Academy of Arts and Sciences|access-date=May 29, 2011}}</ref> a member of the [[National Academy of Sciences]] in 2014,<ref name=NAS>{{cite web|title=News from the National Academy of Sciences|url=http://www.nasonline.org/news-and-multimedia/news/april-29-2014-NAS-Election.html|access-date=Apr 29, 2014|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150818062140/http://www.nasonline.org/news-and-multimedia/news/april-29-2014-NAS-Election.html|archive-date=2015-08-18}}</ref> a [[Guggenheim Fellowship|Guggenheim Fellow]] in 2016,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.gf.org/news/fellows-news/fellowship-awards-in-the-united-states-and-canada-2016/|title=John Simon Guggenheim Foundation {{!}} Fellowship Awards in the United States and Canada, 2016|language=en-US|access-date=2019-03-04}}</ref> and a member of the [[National Academy of Medicine]] in 2019.<ref name="NAM">{{cite web|url=https://www.incite.columbia.edu/news/2019/10/21/peter-bearman-elected-to-the-national-academy-of-medicine|title=Peter Bearman elected to the National Academy of Medicine|last=|first=|date=|website=|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref>

==Career==

Bearman received his B.A. in sociology from [[Brown University]] in 1978, magna cum laude, and his  M.A. (1982) and Ph.D. (1985) in sociology from [[Harvard University]].<ref name=CV>{{cite web|title=Peter Bearman CV|url=http://www.sociology.columbia.edu/pdf-files/BearmanCV1118.pdf|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110428122104/http://www.sociology.columbia.edu/pdf-files/BearmanCV1118.pdf|url-status=dead|archive-date=April 28, 2011|work=Columbia University|access-date=May 29, 2011}}</ref>

After receiving his PhD, he was a lecturer at Harvard, before joining the sociology department at [[University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill]]. There he moved from assistant professor to full professor by 1996, before moving to Columbia University in 1997. At Columbia, Bearman was chair of the department of sociology from 2001-2005 and chair of the department of statistics from 2007-2008. Between 2002 and 2003, he was a visiting professor at the [[University of Genoa]], Italy, the [[University of Munich]]. He has chaired 50 doctoral dissertations.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.incite.columbia.edu/peter-bearman|title=Peter Bearman|website=INCITE|language=en-US|access-date=2019-03-20}}</ref>

Bearman was the founding director of the [[Institute for Social and Economic Research and Policy]], and is currently the director of INCITE, the [https://www.incite.columbia.edu/ Interdisciplinary Center for Innovative Theory and Empirics] at Columbia University. He was also co-founding director of Columbia's Oral History Master of Arts program<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://oralhistory.columbia.edu/faculty-and-staff-|title=Faculty and Staff|website=Oral History Master of Arts|language=en-US|access-date=2019-03-04}}</ref> and co-founding director of the [[Global Health Research Center in Central Asia]].

He is currently co-editor of the Oral History Series and the Middle Range Series, both published by [[Columbia University Press]]. He has also been on the editorial board of several scholarly journals, including the [[American Journal of Sociology]], [[Social Forces]], and [[Sociological Theory (journal)|Sociological Theory]].

==Major contributions==

Bearman, along with [[J. Richard Udry]], designed the [[National Longitudinal Study of Adolescent Health]] (Add Health), currently the only nationally-representative study of adolescent sexuality in the United States, which has yielded over a thousand published research articles and received the 2016 [[Golden Goose Award]].

From these data, Bearman has published seminal articles on the [[sexual network]], virginity pledges, same sex attraction, and adolescent suicidality. He is widely credited with bringing [[social network analysis]] methods to the demographic and population research community. He also introduced social network approaches to [[social sequence analysis]] through the concept of narrative networks.<ref>{{cite journal |doi=10.1017/S0145553200021854 |last1=Bearman |first1=Peter |last2=Faris |first2=Robert |last3=Moody |first3=James |title=Blocking the Future: New Solutions for Old Problems in Historical Social Science |journal=Social Science History |volume=23 |issue=4 |year=1999 |pages=501–33 |s2cid=142075647 |url=https://muse.jhu.edu/article/32131 }}</ref><ref>{{cite journal |doi=10.1016/S0304-422X(99)00022-4 |title=Becoming a Nazi: A model for narrative networks |journal=Poetics |volume=27 |issue=2–3 |pages=69–90 |year=2000 |last1=Bearman |first1=Peter S |last2=Stovel |first2=Katherine }}</ref> Bearman currently directs the [[Robert Wood Johnson Foundation|Robert Wood Johnson]] Program in population health at Columbia University.  He has received major grants and contracts from the [[National Science Foundation]], the [[American Legacy Foundation]], the Office of Population Affairs [[National Institutes of Health]], the [[National Institute of Child Health and Development]], [[Andrew W. Mellon Foundation|The Andrew Mellon Foundation]], the [[Russell Sage Foundation]], the [[Robert Wood Johnson Foundation]], and the [[Rockefeller Foundation]], totaling over $20,000,000.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.incite.columbia.edu/research|title=Research|website=INCITE|language=en-US|access-date=2019-03-01}}</ref>

With co-authors [[Katherine Stovel]], and James Moody, Bearman received the A Roger V. Gould Prize in 2004 for his article ''“Chains of Affection: The Structure of Adolescent Romantic and Sexual Networks.”'' The editorial board of the [[American Journal of Sociology]] selects one article published in the journal for a two-year period. They award the prize to an article that is "empirically rigorous, theoretically grounded, and lucidly written."<ref name="About the Gould Prize">{{cite web|title=About the Gould Prize|url=http://sociology.uchicago.edu/ajs/gould_prize.shtml|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120503095104/http://sociology.uchicago.edu/ajs/gould_prize.shtml|url-status=dead|archive-date=3 May 2012|access-date=17 July 2014}}</ref>

In 2007, Bearman was awarded the [[National Institute of Health]] (NIH) Director's Pioneer Award to  investigated the social determinants of the autism epidemic.

Bearman is the author of ''Doormen'' (University of Chicago Press, 2005), an ethnographic study of doormen in New York City, and is the co-author of ''Working for Respect: Community and Conflict at Walmart'' with Adam Reich (Columbia University Press, 2018). He is also co-editor of ''After the Fall'', an oral history documenting New Yorkers' recollections of the [[September 11 attacks]], as well as ''Robert Rauschenberg: An Oral History'', which is to be published in 2019.
