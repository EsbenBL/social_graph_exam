'''Reiner Tom Zuidema''' (May 24, 1927 – March 2, 2016) was professor of [[Anthropology]] and Latin American and Caribbean Studies at [[University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign]].<ref name=renner>{{cite web|title=Reiner "Tom" Zuidema|url=http://www.renner-wikoffchapel.com/fh/obituaries/obituary.cfm?o_id=3623278&fh_id=10562|publisher=Renner-Wikoff Chapel|accessdate=23 March 2016}}</ref><ref>http://www.anthro.illinois.edu/people/rtzuidem. Retrieved July 5, 2011.</ref> He is well known for his seminal contributions on [[Inca civilization|Inca]] social and political organization. His early work consisted of a structural analysis of the ceque system. He later extended this approach, based on French and Dutch [[structuralism]], to other aspects of Andean civilization, notably [[kinship]], the Inca calendar and Incaic understanding of [[astronomy]].<ref>Morris, Craig. 2007. Andean Ethnohistory and the Agenda for Inka Archaeology. In ''Variations in the expression of Inka power: a symposium at Dumbarton Oaks, 18 and 19 October 1997'', ed. [[Richard L. Burger]], Craig Morris, and Ramiro Matos Mendieta, 1-10. Washington, D.C.: Dumbarton Oaks.</ref>

==Biography==
Zuidema was born in [[Haarlem]], the Netherlands. He obtained a PhD in his home country and later moved to the United States.<ref name=renner/> He had started studying Indonesian social sciences but later switched to anthropology of South America.<ref name=renner/> The main reason for this was that Indonesia became independent of the Netherlands, and he could not work in the country anymore.<ref>{{cite news|author=Jacqueline Fowks |url=http://internacional.elpais.com/internacional/2016/03/05/actualidad/1457140702_640465.html |title=Tom Zuidema, 'el Indiana Jones' del Imperio inca |language=Spanish |newspaper=El País |date=5 March 2016 |accessdate=8 April 2016}}</ref> Zuidema studied Spanish in [[Madrid]] and moved to Peru in 1957. He published a study on the {{Interlanguage link multi|Ceque|es}} (lines) of [[Cusco]] in 1962.<ref name=comercio>{{cite news|author=Lilia Córdova Tábori |url=http://elcomercio.pe/huellas-digitales/archivo/fallece-tom-zuidema-destacado-antropologo-holandes-noticia-1883994 |title=Fallece Tom Zuidema, destacado antropólogo holandés |language=Spanish |newspaper=[[El Comercio (Peru)|El Comercio]] |date=4 March 2016 |accessdate=8 April 2016}}</ref> In 1964 Zuidema moved his family to Peru and started teaching at [[San Cristóbal of Huamanga University]]. After three years he returned to the United States and started working at the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign. In 1993 he retired.<ref name=renner/>

In 1977 Zuidema became a correspondent of the [[Royal Netherlands Academy of Arts and Sciences]].<ref>{{cite web|author= |url=https://www.knaw.nl/en/members/correspondents/5063 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304053211/https://www.knaw.nl/en/members/correspondents/5063 |title=R.T. Zuidema |publisher=Royal Netherlands Academy of Arts and Sciences |date= |archive-date=4 March 2016 |accessdate=28 March 2016}}</ref> He was awarded an honorary doctorate by the [[Pontifical Catholic University of Peru]] in 1993.<ref name=comercio/>
