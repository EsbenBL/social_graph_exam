'''Richard Rose''' (born 9 April 1933 in [[St Louis, Missouri]])<ref>{{cite book|chapter-url=https://books.google.com/books?id=sR4Ch1dMe8IC&q=Richard%20Rose%209%20April%201933&pg=PA1437|title=The International Who's Who 2004|chapter=Rose, Richard|page=1437|location=London|publisher=Europa Publication|year=2003|isbn=1857432177|editor-first=Elizabeth|editor-last=Sleeman}}</ref><ref name=Helensburgh>{{cite news|url=http://www.helensburgh-heritage.co.uk/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=646:honours-galore-for-politics-professor&catid=39:people-&Itemid=399|title=Prof marks 80th birthday with new books|first=Donald|last=Fullarton|publisher=Helensburgh Heritage Trust|date=18 November 2013|accessdate=7 August 2015}}</ref><ref name=Debretts>{{cite web|url=http://www.debretts.com/people-of-today/profile/2643/Richard-ROSE|title=Richard ROSE|work=People of Today|publisher=Debrett's|accessdate=7 August 2015}}</ref> is an American [[Political science|political scientist]] who has been a professor of politics in Scotland since 1966. His research has included the [[The Troubles|Northern Ireland conflict]], [[enlargement of the European Union]], [[democratisation]], [[policy transfer]], elections and voting.<ref name=CV/>

==Education==
Rose studied as an undergraduate at [[Johns Hopkins University]]. His doctorate at the [[University of Oxford]], entitled ''The relation of socialist principles to British Labour foreign policy, 1945-51'', was completed in 1960<ref name="CV">{{cite web|url=http://www.abdn.ac.uk/cspp/rrcv.shtml|title=Professor Richard Rose|publisher=Centre for the Study of Public Policy, University of Aberdeen|accessdate=10 April 2008}}</ref><ref>{{cite thesis|first=C. Richard|last=Rose|date=1960|type=DPhil|url=https://ora.ox.ac.uk/objects/uuid:f8075789-095d-45f9-8561-9fadb83ea68c|title=The relation of socialist principles to British Labour foreign policy, 1945-51|publisher=Oxford University Research Archive|accessdate=24 August 2017}}</ref> 

==Academic career==
From 1961 to 1966, Rose was lecturer in government at the [[University of Manchester]].

Since 1966, he has been based in Scotland, mostly as professor of politics at the [[University of Strathclyde]],<ref name=CV/> where in 1976 he founded the Centre for the Study of Public Policy, of which he is director.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.ukwhoswho.com/search?q=Rose+Prof.Richard+born+9+April+1933+Professor+of+Politics+1966+and+since+2012+and+Founder+and+Director+Centre+for+the+Study+of+Public+Policy+since+1976+University+of+Strathclyde |title=Rose, Prof. Richard, (born 9 April 1933), Professor of Politics, 1966–2005 and since 2012, and Founder and Director, Centre for the Study of Public Policy, since 1976, University of Strathclyde |date=1 December 2007 |website=Who's Who 2021 |publisher=Oxford University Press |access-date=7 June 2021 |url-access=subscription }}</ref> However, from 2005 to 2011, he was at the [[University of Aberdeen]] where he held the sixth century chair in politics.<ref name=CV/>

Rose is an honorary vice president of the [[Political Studies Association]], which each year awards a prize in his name to a scholar under 40 years of age who has made a distinctive contribution to the study of British politics.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.psa.ac.uk/about/abt_richard_rose.htm|title=Richard Rose Prize 2008|publisher=Political Studies Association|accessdate=10 April 2008|archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20080103095632/http://www.psa.ac.uk/about/abt_richard_rose.htm <!-- Bot retrieved archive --> |archivedate = 3 January 2008}}</ref> Rose has an [[honorary doctorate]] from [[Örebro University]] in Sweden,<ref name=CV/> a lifetime achievement award from the [[Policy Studies Organization]],<ref name=Debretts/> and a career award from the 
[[:fr:Association_internationale_de_politique_publique|International Public Policy Association]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=IPPA - CAREER AWARD (2019)|url=https://www.ippapublicpolicy.org/award/career-award/10|access-date=2021-02-19|website=www.ippapublicpolicy.org}}</ref> 

He is a fellow, member, or honorary member of the [[American Academy of Arts and Sciences]], the [[British Academy]], the [[Finnish Academy of Science and Letters]], the [[Academy of Learned Societies for the Social Sciences]],<ref name=CV/> and the [[Royal Society of Edinburgh]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.rse.org.uk/fellow/richard-rose/|title=Professor Richard Rose FBA FRSE|publisher=The Royal Society of Edinburgh|language=en-GB|access-date=15 March 2019}}</ref>
