'''Paul Richard Brass''' (born November 8, 1936) is professor emeritus of [[political science]] and [[international relations]] at the [[University of Washington Henry M. Jackson School of International Studies|Henry M. Jackson School of International Studies]], [[University of Washington]], where he taught since 1965.  After his  B.A. in government in 1958 from [[Harvard College]], he did M.A. in political science, [[University of Chicago]] in 1959, followed by Ph.D. in political science, University of Chicago in 1964.<ref>{{cite web |title=Paul R Brass Profile |url=http://www.sagepub.in/authorDetails.nav?contribId=524299 |accessdate=2014-08-16 |work=SAGE}}</ref>

He has studied the [[Indian subcontinent]] since 1961.<ref>{{cite news |title=County may face "communal hatred" |url=https://news.google.com/newspapers?id=0y0oAAAAIBAJ&sjid=XL4EAAAAIBAJ&pg=1365,752717&dq=paul+brass+washington&hl=en |newspaper=Anchorage Daily News |date=November 2, 1984 |accessdate=May 29, 2012}}</ref> He has published numerous books on the [[politics of India]], including ''[[The Production of Hindu-Muslim Violence in Contemporary India]]'' (2004)<ref>[http://www.indianexpress.com/news/the-politics-of-power/840869/ The Politics of Power] [[Indian Express]] - September 3, 2011</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=As bold as Brass |url=http://www.telegraphindia.com/1130804/jsp/7days/17193422.jsp |work=The Telegraph |date=August 4, 2013 |accessdate=2014-08-16 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161113054208/http://www.telegraphindia.com/1130804/jsp/7days/17193422.jsp |archive-date=November 13, 2016 |url-status=dead }}</ref>
