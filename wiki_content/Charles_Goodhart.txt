'''Charles Albert Eric Goodhart''', {{post-nominals|country=GBR|CBE|FBA|size=100%|sep=,}} (born 23 October 1936) is a British [[economist]].  He was a member of the [[Bank of England]]'s [[Monetary Policy Committee (United Kingdom)|Monetary Policy Committee]] from June 1997 to May 2000 and a professor at the [[London School of Economics]] (1985–2002, [[Emeritus Professor]] since 2002). He is the developer of [[Goodhart's law]], an economic law named after him. He is the son of [[Arthur Lehman Goodhart]],<ref name="archivelink">{{cite web |title=Professor Charles Goodhart |url=http://www.lse.ac.uk/RESEARCHANDEXPERTISE/EXPERTS/profile.aspx?KeyValue=c.a.goodhart%40lse.ac.uk |accessdate=19 November 2020 |date=23 November 2010|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20101123222940/http://www.lse.ac.uk/RESEARCHANDEXPERTISE/EXPERTS/profile.aspx?KeyValue=c.a.goodhart%40lse.ac.uk|archive-date = 2010-11-23}}</ref><ref name=economics>{{cite web|title=Charles Goodhart|url=http://ineteconomics.org/people/charles-goodhart|publisher=ineteconomics.org|accessdate=14 November 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131110034211/http://ineteconomics.org/people/charles-goodhart|archive-date=10 November 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref name=goodhart>{{cite web|title=Charles A.E. Goodhart|url=http://www.voxeu.org/person/charles-ae-goodhart|publisher=voxeu.org|accessdate=14 November 2013|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20131226145650/http://www.voxeu.org/person/charles-ae-goodhart|archivedate=26 December 2013}}</ref><ref name=bloomberg>{{cite web|title=Charles Goodhart|url=http://topics.bloomberg.com/charles-goodhart/|publisher=topics.bloomberg.com|accessdate=14 November 2013|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130726100306/http://topics.bloomberg.com/charles-goodhart/|archive-date=26 July 2013|url-status=dead}}</ref> and the brother of [[William Goodhart]] and [[Philip Goodhart|Sir Philip Goodhart]].<ref name="Modern Law Review">{{cite news|title=Economics and the Law: Too Much One-Way Traffic?|author=Charles Goodhart| volume=60|journal=Modern Law Review| date=1997|doi=10.1111/1468-2230.00063}}</ref>

==Education==
Goodhart attended [[Eton College]], followed by [[Trinity College, Cambridge]], at which he was a scholar with First Class Honours in Economics Tripos. He went on to [[Harvard University|Harvard]] Graduate School of Arts and Sciences, where he gained a PhD in 1963.

==Career==
From 1955 to 1957, Goodhart completed his [[National Service in the United Kingdom|National Service]], in which he was a 2nd Lieutenant with the [[King's Royal Rifle Corps]]. From 1963 to 1964, he was an Assistant Lecturer in Economics at [[Cambridge University]]. This was followed by a stint as an Economic Adviser at the [[Department of Economic Affairs (UK)]] from 1965 to 1967, under the [[Labour Party (UK)|Labour]] [[First Wilson ministry|government of Harold Wilson]]. In 1967, Goodhart became a lecturer in Monetary Economics at the [[London School of Economics]], staying until 1969.

He was an adviser at the [[Bank of England]] from 1969 to 1980, with particular reference to monetary policy. He was subsequently a chief adviser from 1980 to 1985. In 1997, he became an External Member of the [[Monetary Policy Committee (United Kingdom)|Monetary Policy Committee]] in 1997, staying in the role until 2000.

From 1990 to 1997, Goodhart was a member of the Advisory Committee at the [[Hong Kong Monetary Authority|Hong Kong Exchange Fund]]. Meanwhile, he was Norman Sosnow Professor of Banking and Finance at the [[London School of Economics]] from 1985 to 2002, and has been an [[Emeritus Professor]] since then. He has also been a member of the Financial Markets Group at the LSE since 1987.<ref name="lse2020">{{cite web |title=The Great Demographic Reversal |url=https://www.lse.ac.uk/Events/Open/202010261700/the-great-demographic-reversal |website=London School of Economics and Political Science |accessdate=19 November 2020}}</ref>

He is a Fellow of the [[British Academy]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Professor Charles Goodhart FBA |url=https://www.thebritishacademy.ac.uk/fellows/charles-goodhart-FBA/ |website=The British Academy |accessdate=19 November 2020 |language=en}}</ref>
