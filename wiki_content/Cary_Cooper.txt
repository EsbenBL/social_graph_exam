'''Sir Cary Lynn Cooper''' {{post-nominals|country=GBR|CBE|FAcSS}} (born 28 April 1940), is an American-born British [[psychologist]] and 50th Anniversary Professor of [[Organizational Psychology]] and [[occupational health|Health]] at the [[Manchester Business School]], [[University of Manchester]].

Before moving to Manchester he was Distinguished Professor at [[Lancaster University]]. Cooper was Head of the Manchester School of Management (within [[UMIST]]) from the early 1980s. In 1995 he became Pro-Vice-Chancellor and then Deputy Vice-Chancellor of UMIST until 2002. From 1979 to 1980 he was chairman of the Management Education and Development Division of the [[Academy of Management]] and was elected as Founding President of the [[British Academy of Management]].<ref name=aston>{{cite web |url=http://www.aston.ac.uk/aston-business-school/staff/professor-cary-cooper/ |title=Professor Cary Cooper |accessdate=17 June 2015 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161201213106/http://www.aston.ac.uk/aston-business-school/staff/professor-cary-cooper/ |archive-date=1 December 2016 |url-status=dead }}</ref> In June 2005 he was appointed head of the [[Sunningdale Institute]], which, managed by the United Kingdom [[National School of Government]], brings international academics and industry figures together to advise on issues facing UK public sector organisations.<ref>{{cite news | url=http://news.lancs.ac.uk/Web/News/Pages/EBC6865D03E024D88025702A0040C15B.aspx |title=Professor Cary Cooper to lead new Sunningdale Institute |accessdate=30 April 2015}}</ref> He was Chair of the [[Academy of Social Sciences]],<ref name=personally>{{cite web |url=http://www.personallyspeakingbureau.com/speaker/cary-cooper-cbe-prof/ |title=Cary Cooper CBE, Prof. - Personally Speaking Bureau |accessdate=17 June 2015 |archive-date=3 May 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190503171101/http://www.personallyspeakingbureau.com/speaker/cary-cooper-cbe-prof/ |url-status=dead }}</ref> a body representing over 88,000 social scientists and 46 learned societies in the social sciences (including the [[Royal Geographical Society]], [[British Psychological Society]], the [[Political Studies Association]], and the [[Royal Statistical Society]]).  In 2010 he was Chair of the Chronic Diseases and Mental Health Global Agenda Council of the World Economic Forum. He is immediate past President of [[Relate]],<ref name=relate>{{cite web |url=http://www.relate.org.uk/about-us/media-centre/press-releases/2013/12/11/prof-cary-cooper-announced-new-president-relate |title=Prof Cary Cooper announced as the new President of Relate |accessdate=17 June 2015}}</ref> Clinical Advisor to [[Anxiety UK]],<ref name=anxiety>{{cite web|url=http://www.anxietyuk.org.uk/about-us/whos-who/medical/ |title=Clinical Advisors Anxiety UK |accessdate=17 June 2015 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20150617185402/http://www.anxietyuk.org.uk/about-us/whos-who/medical/ |archivedate=17 June 2015}}</ref> President of the Institute of Welfare,<ref name=welfare>{{cite web |url=http://www.instituteofwelfare.com/the-institute/our-leadership/ |title=Our Leadership Institute of Welfare |accessdate=17 June 2015}}</ref> President of the Chartered Institute of Personnel and Development 2016-, Co-Chair of the National Forum for Health & Wellbeing at Work (2016-) and past President of the [[British Association for Counselling and Psychotherapy]].<ref name=bacp>{{cite web|url=http://www.bacp.co.uk/about_bacp/history_officers.php |title=BACP About BACP - History of Officers and Chief Executive |accessdate=17 June 2015}}</ref> Cooper is also a director of well-being specialists and business psychologists at Robertson Cooper Limited, a university spin off company which he set up in 1999 with Ivan Robertson.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.robertsoncooper.com |title=Business psychology company founded by Cary Cooper and Ivan Robertson, based Manchester and London, offering well-being, stress and resilience, leadership development and talent management consultancy and tools |publisher=Robertson Cooper |date=17 August 2012 |accessdate=22 August 2012}}</ref> He was the founding editor-in-chief of the ''[[Journal of Organizational Behavior]]''.

Cooper's parents were Jews from Romania and Ukraine who settled in [[West Hollywood]].<ref>[http://evolution.skf.com/cary-cooper/ "Cary Cooper"] in ''Evolution Online'', 15 March 2004</ref><ref>[http://www.lancaster.ac.uk/news/articles/2014/knighthood-and-mbe-for-lancaster-professors/ "Knighthood and MBE for two Lancaster professors"], Lancaster University, 16 June 2014</ref> He became a British citizen in 1993.

== Education ==
Cooper has obtained a BSc (UCLA), an M.B.A. (UCLA), and a PhD (Leeds University, UK).

== Queen's Birthday Honours ==
Cooper was appointed [[Commander of the Order of the British Empire]] (CBE) in the 2001 Birthday Honours for his contribution to occupational/organizational health and was [[Knight Bachelor|Knighted]] in the [[2014 Birthday Honours]] for services to social science.<ref>{{London Gazette |issue=60895 |date=14 June 2014 |page=b2 |supp=y}}</ref>
