'''Bruce Kingma''' (born October 4th, 1961) is an American [[economist]] and academic [[entrepreneur]], who since 1988 has taught and worked in the [[United States]], [[Canada]], and [[New Zealand]]. Kingma is a pioneer in experiential entrepreneurship education and [[community engagement]] and his work cover topics ranging from academic entrepreneurship, entrepreneurship and religion, [[information economics]], online education, community engagement, [[library science]], and [[nonprofit]] management.

He is currently working as a [[Professor]] of [[Entrepreneurship]] at [[Syracuse University]], with joint appointments in the [[Martin J. Whitman School of Management]] and [[Syracuse University School of Information Studies|School of Information Studies]]. He is the former Associate [[Provost (education)|Provost]] for Entrepreneurship and Innovation (2007-2013) <ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.asis.org/kingma_bruce.html/|title=Bruce Kingma has been Appointed Associate Provost for Entrepreneurship and Innovation|last=|first=|date=2007-07-13|website=archive.is|url-status=live|archive-url=https://archive.today/20070713064920/http://www.asis.org/kingma_bruce.html/|archive-date=2007-07-13|access-date=2020-04-23}}</ref> and the former [[Associate Dean]] of the iSchool (2000-2007), [[Syracuse University]].

== Early life and education ==

Kingma attended [[Thornton Fractional South High School]]  in [[Lansing, Illinois]]. In 1983, Kingma graduated from the [[University of Chicago]] with a [[Bachelor of Arts|Bachelors of Arts]] degree in [[Economics|Economics with honors]]. Kingma then moved to [[Rochester, New York|Rochester]] to pursue a [[Doctor of Philosophy|PhD]] in economics from the [[University of Rochester]] and graduated in 1989.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://ischool.syr.edu/people/directories/view/brkingma/|title=Bruce Kingma|last=|first=|date=|website=School of Information Studies {{!}} Syracuse University|language=en|access-date=2020-04-23}}</ref>

== Career ==
=== Academic ===
'''Bruce Kingma''' is currently working as a professor at [[Syracuse University]] in the [[Syracuse University School of Information Studies|iSchool]] and [[Martin J. Whitman School of Management|School of Management]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ischool.syr.edu/people/directories/view/brkingma/|title=Bruce Kingma|date=4 June 2020}}</ref>

Kingma started his career as an [[Assistant professor|Assistant Professor]] at the [[Texas A&M University]] in the Department of Economics. He then moved to [[Albany, New York|Albany]] to work as [[Associate professor|Associate Professor]] for the [[University at Albany, SUNY|School of Information Science and Policy]] at the [[University of Albany]]. While at the University of Albany, he also worked as an [[Interim management|Interim Director]] of Interdisciplinary [[Doctor of Philosophy|Ph.D]]. Program in [[Information science|Information Science]] (1996-1997) and as a [[Academic ranks in the United States|Director]] of Interdisciplinary Undergraduate Program in [[Information science|Information Science]] (1997-2000). As the inaugural director of the [[Bachelor's degree|bachelor’s program]] in [[information science]], Kingma increased course enrollments from 20 to 1600 students within a period of 3 years.

Bruce Kingma joined [[Syracuse University]] in the year 2000, as the [[Associate Dean]] of the [[Syracuse University School of Information Studies|School of Information Studies]]. He also founded the WISE education consortium<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://wiseeducation.org/|title=WISE Education Program|last=|first=|date=|website=wiseeducation.org|access-date=2020-04-23}}</ref> - a partnership of 15 universities worldwide committed to quality [[Distance education|online education]].

Under his leadership, programs and initiatives like The Web-based Information Science Education Consortium (WISE).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://news.syr.edu/blog/2008/05/14/wise-receives-adec-national-award-for-excellence-in-distance-education/|title=WISE receives ADEC National Award for Excellence in Distance Education|website=SU News|language=en-US|access-date=2020-04-23}}</ref>

In 2007, Kingma served as the Provost for Entrepreneurship, Innovation, and Community Engagement at Syracuse University, a title which he held for 6 years.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ischool.syr.edu/people/directories/view/brkingma/|title=Bruce Kingma|date=4 June 2020}}</ref>

Major programs started under Provost Kingma’s leadership raised over $350m in external funding.  These include [https://www.eda.gov/files/programs/university-centers/uc-tools/archive/29-Syracuse-U_UC.pdf The Raymond von Dran Innovation and Disruptive Entrepreneurship Accelerator], [https://news.syr.edu/blog/2012/08/01/raymond-von-dran-idea-student-sandbox-named-no-3-college-business-incubator/ RvD Student Sandbox Incubator], IDS Lean-Startup courses, [[Institute for Veterans and Military Families]], The Near West Side Initiative, The Center for Digital Media Entrepreneurship, [https://ebv.vets.syr.edu/veterans/ The Entrepreneurship Bootcamp for Veterans with Disabilities], StartUp New York, Engagement Scholars and Kauffman Entrepreneurship Engagement Fellows, The Connective Corridor, [https://whitman.syr.edu/programs-and-academics/centers-and-institutes/falcone/programs/ssic.aspx The Janklow Arts Leadership Program], [https://whitman.syr.edu/programs-and-academics/centers-and-institutes/falcone/programs/ssic.aspx The South Side Innovation Center], The South Side Initiative, The New York State Business Plan Competition,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://news.syr.edu/blog/2012/04/27/su-and-suny-esf-student-entrepreneurs-shine-in-nys-business-plan-competition/|title=SU and SUNY-ESF student entrepreneurs shine in NYS Business Plan Competition|website=SU News|language=en-US|access-date=2020-04-23}}</ref> Women Igniting the Spirit of Entrepreneurship,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://wisecenter.org/|title=Women Igniting the Spirit of Entrepreneurship – Women Changing the World, One Venture at a Time|language=en-US|access-date=2020-04-23}}</ref> The Syracuse University Entrepreneurship Club, and The Creativity, Innovation, and Entrepreneurship Learning Community.

From 2010 to 2013, Kingma was a contributor to the [[Institute of Museum and Library Services]]-funded Lib-Value project measuring the return on investment of [[Academic library|academic libraries]].<ref>{{Citation|title=Lib-Value Workshop - Bruce Kingma|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=5AzpvJTtyTY|language=en|access-date=2020-04-23}}</ref>

=== Entrepreneurial ===
Bruce Kingma is the founder of the award-winning WISE Consortium<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://news.syr.edu/blog/2008/05/14/wise-receives-adec-national-award-for-excellence-in-distance-education/|title=WISE receives ADEC National Award for Excellence in Distance Education|website=SU News|language=en-US|access-date=2020-04-23}}</ref> in 2002 which was the first international consortium of universities in online education.

=== Research  ===

(joint proposal with the [[University of Illinois at Urbana–Champaign|University of Illinois at Champaign]]-Urbana and the [[University of Pittsburgh]]), [[Institute of Museum and Library Services|Institute for Museum and Library Services]], [[Washington, D.C.|Washington, D.C]]. ($936,338). From 2004-2006, Kingma produced the “WISE: Web-based Information Science Education. An Online Consortial Initiative to Build Multi-Institutional Capacity for Library and Information Science Education.” Principal Investigator (joint proposal with the U[[University of Illinois at Urbana–Champaign|niversity of Illinois at Champaign-Urbana]]), [[Institute of Museum and Library Services|Institute for Museum  and Library Services]], [[Washington, D.C.|Washington, D.C]]. ($713,492).
