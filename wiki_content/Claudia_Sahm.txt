'''Claudia R. Sahm''' (''[[Given name#Name at birth|née]]'' '''Foster''') is an American [[economist]]. She was formerly director of macroeconomic policy at the [[Center for American Progress#Washington Center for Equitable Growth|Washington Center for Equitable Growth]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://equitablegrowth.org/press/equitable-growth-announces-claudia-sahm-as-new-director-of-macroeconomic-policy/|title=Equitable Growth Announces Claudia Sahm as New Director of Macroeconomic Policy|website=Equitable Growth|language=en-US|access-date=2019-12-02}}</ref> and a Section Chief at the [[Board of Governors of the Federal Reserve|Board of Governors]] of the [[Federal Reserve System]], where she worked in various capacities from 2007 to 2019.<ref name="FRB-Bio-2018">{{cite web|date=13 August 2018|title=Meet the Economists: Claudia R. Sahm|url=https://www.federalreserve.gov/econres/claudia-r-sahm.htm|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190818154533/https://www.federalreserve.gov/econres/claudia-r-sahm.htm|archive-date=August 18, 2019|website=[[Federal Reserve Board of Governors]]}}</ref> Sahm specializes in [[macroeconomics]] and household finance.<ref name=Denison-WhoWillLead-Snippet>{{cite news|title=Who Will Lead?: Claudia Foster Sahm '98|url=https://denisonmagazine.com/article/uncommon-ground/|accessdate=7 October 2018|work=Denison Magazine|publisher=[[Denison University]]}}</ref> She is best known for the development of the [[Sahm Rule]], a [[Federal Reserve Economic Data]] (FRED) indicator for identifying recessions in real-time.<ref>{{Cite news|last=Schneider|first=Howard|date=2019-10-04|title='Sahm Rule' enters Fed lexicon as fast, real-time recession flag|language=en|work=Reuters|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-usa-fed-sahm-idUSKBN1WJ12J|access-date=2020-12-21}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=2020-12-04|title=Real-time Sahm Rule Recession Indicator|url=https://fred.stlouisfed.org/series/SAHMREALTIME|access-date=2020-12-21|website=fred.stlouisfed.org|language=en}}</ref>

== Education and career ==
In 1998, Sahm received a bachelor's degree in economics from [[Denison University]], where she also studied political science and German.<ref name=FRB-Bio-2018 /> From 1998 to 1999, Sahm was a [[Fulbright Program|Fulbright Scholar]] at the [[Technical University of Dresden]], where she studied economic transitions in post-socialist countries.<ref name=Denison-WhoWillLead-Snippet /> From 1999 to 2001, Sahm worked at the [[Brookings Institution]] as a research assistant.<ref name=Denison-WhoWillLead-Snippet /> Sahm began her PhD at the [[University of Michigan]] in 2001, guided by Matthew D. Shapiro and [[Miles Kimball|Miles Spencer Kimball]], completing her thesis ''Risk, Tolerance and Asset Allocation'' in 2007.<ref name="UMI-Thesis-2007">{{cite thesis|last=Sahm|first=Claudia R.|date=2007|title=Risk Tolerance and Asset Allocation|type=PhD|publisher=[[University of Michigan]]|url=https://deepblue.lib.umich.edu/bitstream/handle/2027.42/55677/csahm_1.pdf|oclc=894382254}}</ref>

In 2007, Sahm began working at the [[Federal Reserve Board of Governors]] as an economist, where her work was focused on examining consumer spending. Her first position was studying the impact of tax rebates.<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=PlDF3uvBTa0C&q=claudia+sahm+economics&pg=PT152|title=The Little Book of Economics: How the Economy Works in the Real World|last=Ip|first=Greg|date=August 26, 2010|publisher=[[John Wiley & Sons]]|location=Hoboken, New Jersey|isbn=9780470929407}}</ref> In 2013 she was promoted to Senior Economist, and in 2015 to Principal Economist, at the [[Federal Reserve Board of Governors]].<ref name=FRB-Bio-2018 />

Sahm was a Senior Economist at the [[Council of Economic Advisers]] for the [[Obama Administration]], where she worked on macroeconomic developments and housing policy from 2015 to 2016.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://denison.edu/academics/economics/feature/103202|title=Claudia Sahm '98 Headliner at Women in Economics Forum|website=denison.edu|publisher=[[Denison University]]|location=Granville, Ohio|access-date=October 4, 2018}}</ref> She studied the impact of unusual weather on economic data.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.brookings.edu/bpea-articles/weather-adjusting-economic-data/|title=Weather adjusting economic data|first1=Michael|last1=Boldin|first2=Jonathan H.|last2=Wright|date=September 10, 2015|work=[[Brookings Institution|Brookings]]|access-date=October 4, 2018}}</ref> She showed that people were less willing to take economic risks as they age.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.forbes.com/sites/modeledbehavior/2016/08/14/the-five-biggest-open-economics-questions-to-me/#49afc3cc3afd|title=The Five Biggest Open Economics Questions To Me|last=Ozimek|first=Adam|work=[[Forbes]]|date=August 4, 2016|access-date=October 4, 2018}}</ref><ref>{{Cite journal|last=Sahm|first=Claudia R.|date=December 2012|title=How Much Does Risk Tolerance Change?|journal=[[Quarterly Journal of Finance]]|volume=02|issue=4|pages=1250020|doi=10.1142/s2010139212500206|pmid=25544881|pmc=4276321|issn=2010-1392}}</ref>

In September 2017 Sahm was promoted to Section Chief of the [[Federal Reserve Board of Governors]] in the Division of Consumer and Community Affairs.<ref name=FRB-Bio-2018 /> From October 2019 to September 2020, she was director of macroeconomic policy at the Washington Center for Equitable Growth.<ref>{{Cite news|first=Catarina|last=Saraiva|date=September 24, 2020|title=Economist Claudia Sahm Leaves Center for Equitable Growth|language=en|work=[[Bloomberg News|Bloomberg]]|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2020-09-24/economist-claudia-sahm-leaves-center-for-equitable-growth|access-date=October 15, 2020}}</ref> In September 2020, Sahm joined [[Bloomberg Opinion]] as a contributor<ref>{{Cite web|last=Sahm|first=Claudia|date=|title=hey, look who’s a @bloomberg Opinion contributor now: me|url=https://twitter.com/Claudia_Sahm/status/1304391062342053890|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=December 8, 2020|website=Twitter}}</ref> and in October 2020, she joined [[The New York Times]] as a Contributing Opinion Writer.<ref>{{Cite web|title=The New York Times - Claudia Sahm|url=https://www.nytimes.com/search?query=claudia+sahm|access-date=2020-12-22|website=www.nytimes.com|language=en}}</ref>

As of 2021, Sahm is a senior fellow researching [[guaranteed income]] at the Jain Family Institute.<ref>Lynch, David J., [https://www.washingtonpost.com/us-policy/2021/03/29/summers-biden-economy-inflation/ "How Larry Summers went from Obama’s top economic adviser to one of Biden’s loudest critics"], ''Washington Post'', March 29, 2021. Retrieved 2021-04-04.</ref><ref>[https://www.jainfamilyinstitute.org/about-us/ "About Us"], Jain Family Institute. Retrieved 2021-04-04.</ref> She is also a member of the [[National Bureau of Economic Research]] Conference on Research in Income and Wealth.<ref>{{Cite web|title=CRIW - Members|url=https://www.nber.org/programs-projects/projects-and-centers/conference-research-income-and-wealth/criw-members|access-date=2021-09-30|website=NBER|language=en}}</ref>

=== Research ===
Sahm conducts research in macroeconomics with a focus on business cycles, fiscal stimulus and consumer behavior.

Her research has appeared in numerous FEDS notes,<ref>{{Cite web|title=FRB: Search Results|url=https://www.fedsearch.org/board_public/search?source=board_pub_discussion_series&text=sahm&submit=Search|access-date=July 30, 2020|website=www.fedsearch.org|language=en}}</ref> books, and major academic journals.<ref>{{Cite journal|last1=Slemrod|first1=Joel|last2=Shapiro|first2=Matthew D.|last3=Sahm|first3=Claudia R.|date=April 2012|title=Check in the Mail or More in the Paycheck: Does the Effectiveness of Fiscal Stimulus Depend on How It Is Delivered?|journal=[[American Economic Journal]]|volume=4|issue=3|pages=216–250|doi=10.1257/pol.4.3.216|issn=1945-7731|pmc=3747574|pmid=23970951}}</ref><ref>{{Cite journal|last1=Kimball|first1=Miles S.|last2=Sahm|first2=Claudia R.|last3=Shapiro|first3=Matthew D.|date=September 1, 2008|title=Imputing Risk Tolerance From Survey Responses|journal=[[Journal of the American Statistical Association]]|volume=103|issue=483|pages=1028–1038|doi=10.1198/016214508000000139|issn=0162-1459|pmc=2856097|pmid=20407599}}</ref><ref>{{Cite journal|last1=Shapiro|first1=Matthew D.|last2=Sahm|first2=Claudia R.|last3=Kimball|first3=Miles S.|date=May 2009|title=Risk Preferences in the PSID: Individual Imputations and Family Covariation|journal=[[American Economic Review]]|volume=99|issue=2|pages=363–368|doi=10.1257/aer.99.2.363|issn=0002-8282|pmc=2995549|pmid=21132079}}</ref>

Outside the academic audience Sahm is known for the ''[[Sahm Rule]]'', also known as the "Sahm Recession Indicator":<ref>{{Cite magazine|url=https://www.economist.com/graphic-detail/2019/06/11/how-to-spot-a-recession|title=How to spot a recession|magazine=[[The Economist]]|date=June 11, 2019|access-date=September 14, 2019}}</ref><blockquote>One reliable early signal of recession is the Sahm Rule: A downturn is probably occurring if the three-month average of the unemployment rate has risen by at least 0.5 percentage point above its low point in the previous 12 months. This rule [...] has signaled every recession since 1970 with virtually no false positives.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.wsj.com/articles/launch-a-pre-emptive-strike-against-recession-11567723004|title=Opinion {{!}} Launch a Pre-Emptive Strike Against Recession|last=Furman|first=Jason|website=[[Wall Street Journal]]|access-date=September 14, 2019}}</ref></blockquote>The Sahm Rule tracks the unemployment rate to detect upcoming and current recessions.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.hamiltonproject.org/papers/direct_stimulus_payments_to_individuals?_ga=2.66506413.2082832713.1568458560-1495596607.1568458560|title=Direct Stimulus Payments to Individuals {{!}} The Hamilton Project|website=www.hamiltonproject.org|access-date=2019-09-14}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ft.com/content/1b55e40a-d571-11e9-a0bd-ab8ec6435630|title=Sahm Rule is a poor predictor of recessions|website=Financial Times|date=September 13, 2019}}</ref>

=== Other work ===
Sahm maintains the economics blog ''MacroMom''.<ref name=MacroMom-Blog>{{cite web|last1=Sahm|first1=Claudia|url=http://macromomblog.com/|title=MacroMom|website=MacroMom Blog|access-date=4 October 2018}}</ref>

Sahm campaigns for more diversity in economics.<ref name="WomenInEcon-StLouis-2018">{{cite web|last1=Sahm|first1=Claudia|url=http://calwomenofecon.weebly.com/uploads/9/6/1/0/96100906/women_econ_csahm.pdf|title=Women in Economics Symposium|date=22 February 2018|website=St Louis Fed|format=presentation}}</ref><ref name="StLouisFed-TimelyTopics-Podcast-2018">{{cite news|last1=Suiter|first1=Mary|last2=Sahm|first2=Claudia|title=Women in Economics: Claudia Sahm|url=https://www.stlouisfed.org/timely-topics/women-in-economics/claudia-sahm|work=Timely Topics|publisher=[[Federal Reserve Bank of St. Louis]]|date=28 March 2018|format=Podcast}}</ref> She petitioned the [[American Economic Association]] to remove sexist comments on the website Economics Job Market Rumors (EJMR).<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.iaffe.org/petition-aea-ejmr/|title=IAFFE - Petition to the AEA re the EJMR website|website=www.iaffe.org|access-date=2018-10-04}}</ref> She is also known for her critiques of the culture of the economics profession, citing many specific examples of bullying and harassment she experienced, observed, or had reported to her by others, often directed at female and minority economists.<ref>{{Cite web|first=Megan|last=Cerullo|title=Former Fed economist blasts her profession as racist, sexist, elitist|url=https://www.cbsnews.com/news/claudia-sahm-former-federal-reserve-economist-field-racist-elitist-sexist/|date=July 30, 2020|access-date=December 2, 2020|website=[[CBS News]]|language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|first=Lynn|last=Parramore|title=What Happens When a Noted Female Economist Fights Toxic Culture in the Field?|url=https://www.ineteconomics.org/perspectives/blog/what-happens-when-a-noted-female-economist-fights-toxic-culture-in-the-field|date=September 9, 2020|access-date=December 2, 2020|website=[[Institute for New Economic Thinking]]|language=en}}</ref>
