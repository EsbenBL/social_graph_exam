'''Clifford Collier Clogg''' (October 16, 1949 â€“ May 7, 1995) was an [[Americans|American]] [[sociologist]], [[demographer]], and [[statistician]].  He is best known for his contributions to [[population statistics]], [[categorical data analysis]], and [[latent class analysis]].

==Biography==
Clogg was born in 1949 in [[Oberlin, Ohio|Oberlin]], [[Ohio]].  He earned a [[B.A.]] in sociology in 1971 from [[Ohio University]], and continued his studies at the [[University of Chicago]], where he earned an [[M.A.]] in sociology and an [[M.Sc.]] in statistics in 1974, and a [[Ph.D.]] in sociology in 1977. Starting in 1976 he served at the [[Pennsylvania State University]] faculty, rising to the rank of Distinguished Professor of Sociology and Professor of Statistics.<ref name="obit">{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1995/05/10/obituaries/clifford-c-clogg-45-authority-on-demographics.html|work=[[The New York Times]]|date= May 10, 1995|title= Clifford C. Clogg, 45, Authority on Demographics|author=Wolfgang Saxon}}</ref><ref>[https://groups.google.com/forum/#!topic/bit.listserv.sos-data/ggbjibUlUy0 ''Center Daily Times'', May 9, 1995]</ref>

He served as an editor of several journals, including the ''[[Journal of the American Statistical Association]]'', ''[[Demography (journal)|Demography]]'', ''[[Sociological Methodology]]'', and the ''[[Journal of Educational and Behavioral Statistics]]''.<ref name="obit"/>
