'''Samuel Merrill III''' (born 1939) is an American [[mathematician]] and [[political scientist]] best known for his work on alternative [[voting systems]], voter behavior, party competition, and [[arbitration]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://pqasb.pqarchiver.com/washingtonpost/access/19415981.html?dids=19415981:19415981&FMT=ABS&FMTS=ABS:FT&type=current&date=Oct+09%2C+1995&author=Kathy+Sawyer&pub=The+Washington+Post+(pre-1997+Fulltext)&desc=A+Paradox+Of+Majority+Politics&pqatl=google|title=A Paradox Of Majority Politics|last=Sawyer|first=Kathy|date=October 9, 1995|page=A3|accessdate=28 April 2011}}</ref><ref name="Haskell1996">{{cite book|last=Haskell|first=John|title=Fundamentally flawed: understanding and reforming presidential primaries|url=https://archive.org/details/fundamentallyfla0000hask|url-access=registration|accessdate=28 April 2011|date=May 1996|publisher=Rowman & Littlefield|isbn=978-0-8476-8241-6|pages=[https://archive.org/details/fundamentallyfla0000hask/page/92 92]–}}</ref><ref name="FarberO'Connell2010">{{cite book|last1=Farber|first1=Daniel A.|last2=O'Connell|first2=Anne Joseph|title=Research handbook on public choice and public law|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=H0t6-2UpqOAC&pg=PA130|accessdate=28 April 2011|date=May 2010|publisher=Edward Elgar Publishing|isbn=978-1-84720-674-9|pages=130–}}</ref>

Merrill was raised in [[Bogalusa]], [[Louisiana]].  He received his bachelor's degree from [[Tulane University]] and his Ph.D. in [[mathematics]] in 1965 from [[Yale University]] under [[Charles Earl Rickart|C. E. Rickart]] with thesis ''Banach Spaces of Analytic Functions''.<ref>{{MathGenealogy|id=96103|title=Samuel Merrill, III}}</ref>  Merrill was a professor of mathematics and [[statistics]] at [[Wilkes University]] until he retired in 2004.

Samuel Merrill is the author of three books on political science:
*''Making Multicandidate Elections More Democratic'' (1988, Princeton University Press)
*''A Unified Theory of Voting'' with [[Bernard Grofman]] (1999, Cambridge University Press)
*''A Unified Theory of Party Competition'' with James Adams and Bernard Grofman (2005, Cambridge University Press)
