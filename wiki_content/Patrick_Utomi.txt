'''Patrick Okedinachi Utomi''' (born February 6, 1956) is a Nigerian professor of political economy and management expert.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.tribune.com.ng/sun/news/8185-nigerias-economy-is-in-crisis-utomi-/|author=Suzy Oruya|place=[[Onitsha]]|title=Nigeria's economy is in crisis - Utomi|publisher=Tribune|date=12 August 2012|accessdate=25 November 2012|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120815181833/http://tribune.com.ng/sun/news/8185-nigerias-economy-is-in-crisis-utomi-|archivedate=15 August 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref> He is a Fellow of the Institute of Management Consultants of Nigeria<ref name=cop>{{cite web|url=http://corpgovnigeria.org/board%206.htm|title=:: Our Activities|website=Corpgovnigeria.org|accessdate=25 November 2012|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://archive.is/20130414131313/http://corpgovnigeria.org/board%206.htm|archivedate=14 April 2013}}</ref> and a former presidential candidate.<ref name="fin">{{cite web|url=http://www.financialfreedominspiration.com/resources/success-stories/pat-utomi/|title=Pat Utomi – Entrepreneur & Social Activist|publisher=Financial Freedom Inspiration|date=6 February 1956|accessdate=25 November 2012|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://archive.is/20130123133637/http://www.financialfreedominspiration.com/resources/success-stories/pat-utomi/|archivedate=23 January 2013}}</ref> He is the founder of Centre for Value in Leadership  (CVL)<ref name="CVLFounder">{{cite web|url=https://www.channelstv.com/2012/11/10/flood-victims-utomi-calls-for-focus-on-post-camp-strategy/|title=Channels Television - Nigeria's Award Winning Television Station|date=10 November 2012|accessdate=31 March 2017|quote=The challenging health conditions in areas affected by the flood disaster should be the critical emphasis of policy makers as the floods begin to recede. This was the call made by the founder of the Centre for Values in Leadership (CVL), Prof. Pat Utomi, at the presentation of relief materials to officials of Delta State Government at St Patrick’s College, at the Asaba camp for displaced persons.}}</ref> and the [[African Democratic Congress]]. He is a professor at [[Lagos Business School]],<ref name=lbs/> and has served in senior positions in government, as an adviser to the president of [[Nigeria]], the private sector, as Chief Operating Officer of [[Volkswagen]] Nigeria.<ref name=fin/>

==Background==
Patrick Utomi was born in [[Kaduna]],<ref name="who is who">{{cite web|url=http://www.africa-confidential.com/whos-who-profile/id/2705/|title=Mr Patrick Utomi &#124; Who's Who Profile|author=profile|publisher=Africa Confidential|accessdate=25 November 2012}}</ref> he is a descent of [[Igbuzo]] in Oshimili North Local Government Area of [[Delta State]].<ref name="who is who"/> He had his [[primary education]] at St. Thomas, [[Kano State|Kano]] 1960 - 1962;<ref name="who is who"/> Our Lady of Fatima, [[Gusau]] 1962 - 1966;<ref name="who is who"/> [[Christ the King College, Onitsha]]<ref name="who is who"/> and Loyola College, [[Ibadan]],<ref name="who is who"/> finishing his secondary education at the age of 15. The entry age for university education was 17, so he enrolled at the Federal School of Arts and Science in the interim.<ref name="who is who"/> He later got admission into the [[University of Nigeria, Nsukka]] in 1973 to study [[Mass Communication]].<ref name="who is who"/> Patrick Utomi graduated from university of [[Nigeria]] Nsuka in 1977,<ref name="who is who"/> He attended [[Indiana University]], Bloomington, Indiana, USA, where he got his Ph.D. MPA, MA.<ref name="who is who"/> He was appointed professor of Social and Political Economy Environment of Business and [[Entrepreneurship]]<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.guardiannewsngr.com/index.php?option=com_content&id=99824%3Afashola-utomi-others-seek-action-on-entrepreneurship-education&Itemid=488/|title=Fashola, Utomi, others seek action on entrepreneurship education |website=Guardiannewsngr.com|date=27 September 2012|accessdate=25 November 2012}}</ref> at [[Lagos Business School]]<ref name="lbs">{{cite web|url=http://lbs.edu.ng/celebrating-20-years/milestones/|title=Lagos Business School - Milestones|website=Lbs.edu.ng|publisher=[[Lagos Business School]]|accessdate=24 November 2012|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20121015201928/http://www.lbs.edu.ng/celebrating-20-years/milestones/|archivedate=15 October 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref> in 2003. he has been a scholar-in-residence at the [[Harvard Business School]] and the [[American University]] in [[Washington, D.C.]]<ref>{{Cite web|title=Prof. PAT UTOMI* to be honoured with other Top Nigerian Leaders as one of the *PATRONs* of Arise O Nigeria at the 3rd Annual *Arise O Nigeria Leadership Symposium & PAN Nigerian Humanitarian Awards.* – BMTV NIGERIA|url=https://bmtvnigeria.com.ng/prof-pat-utomi-to-be-honoured-with-other-top-nigerian-leaders-as-one-of-the-patrons-of-arise-o-nigeria-at-the-3rd-annual-arise-o-nigeria-leadership-symposium-pan-nigerian-humanitarian-awards/|language=en-US|access-date=2020-05-29}}</ref>

==Career==
[[File:Pat utomi economic.jpg|200px|thumb|left|South South Economic Summit 2009 - Gov. Imoke, President [[Goodluck Jonathan]], Pat Utomi Gov. Oshiomole]]
Within a few months of his return to Nigeria from Bloomington, Indiana, United States, Pat got enlisted by the former vice-president [[Alex Ifeanyichukwu Ekwueme|Alex Ekwueme]] to provide consultancy services on some public policy papers. Soon after, he was appointed Special Assistant by former president [[Shehu Shagari]]<ref name=fin/> a position he lost following the military coup of December 1983, which terminated the Second Republic .

Following the end of the Second Republic, he became chairman/chief executive officer, Utomapp Holdings Limited, 1984 – 1986, and assistant general manager, corporate affairs, Volkswagen Nigeria [[Lagos]], 1983 - 1993.<ref name=cop/>

Utomi was scholar-in-residence, American University, Washington DC, USA, 1996 and research associate, the [[Harvard Business School]], Boston, Massachusetts, USA, 1996.<ref><nowiki>https://thepointernewsonline.com/?p=18348</nowiki></ref> (Lagos Business school started operating in 1991.)

Utomi also belongs to many professional bodies. He is a member, Nigerian Institute of Public Relations, NIPR, [[Institute of Directors]], IOD, Nigeria Economic Summit Group and Nigeria Economic Society.

Utomi coordinated the establishment of several civil society groups on good governance and accountability such as Transparency in Nigeria, the Centre for Values in Leadership, the Concerned Professionals, and the Restoration Group. In the business sphere, Utomi was the vice-chairman, Platinum–Habib Bank. He is a columnist, and chairman.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://thepointernewsonline.com/?p=18348|title=Pat Utomi: A Distinguished Professor Of Many Parts|date=2013-03-02|website=The Pointer News Online|language=en-US|access-date=2020-01-23}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Save Our Needy: Prof. PAT UTOMI Becomes Patron of Save Our Needy|url=http://saveourneedy.blogspot.com/2016/08/prof-pat-utomi-becomes-patron-of-save.html|last=Needy|first=Save Our|date=2016-08-04|website=Save Our Needy|access-date=2020-05-25}}</ref>

Utomi has served in various private sector associations including the [[Lagos chamber of commerce and industry|Lagos Chamber of Commerce and Industry]] (LCCI), the National Council of the Manufacturers Association of Nigeria, he is also the chairman of Poise Nigeria Limited, and the Nigeria Employers Consultative Association (NECA). He has spoken on issues in the following areas on various international platforms; growth economics, comparative development, leadership, oil and China’s economic surge and growing influence in Africa.  He has collaborated frequently with the center for strategic and international studies (CSIS) in Washington, and Chatham House, in the UK.  He has also written commissioned papers for the UK's DFID including the latest collaboration with Colleagues from [[Oxford]] on the Political Economy of growth in Nigeria. (2006).<ref>{{Cite web|title=Prof. PAT UTOMI* to be honoured with other Top Nigerian Leaders as one of the *PATRONs* of Arise O Nigeria at the 3rd Annual *Arise O Nigeria Leadership Symposium & PAN Nigerian Humanitarian Awards.* – BMTV NIGERIA|url=https://bmtvnigeria.com.ng/prof-pat-utomi-to-be-honoured-with-other-top-nigerian-leaders-as-one-of-the-patrons-of-arise-o-nigeria-at-the-3rd-annual-arise-o-nigeria-leadership-symposium-pan-nigerian-humanitarian-awards/|language=en-US|access-date=2020-05-29}}</ref>
 
Utomi also ran a failed attempt for presidency of Nigeria in 2011.<ref>{{Cite web|title=SDMP picks Utomi as presidential candidate|url=https://www.vanguardngr.com/2011/01/sdmp-picks-utomi-as-presidential-candidate/|date=2011-01-14|website=Vanguard News|language=en-US|access-date=2020-05-25}}</ref>
In 2021, Pat and other political stakeholders pioneered the formation of a new political opposition ahead of the 2023 Nigerian general elections.<ref name="Olatunji 2021">{{cite web | last=Olatunji | first=Kehinde | title=Jega, Utomi, Duke, others float new political party | website=The Guardian Nigeria News | date=2021-09-22 | url=https://guardian.ng/politics/jega-utomi-duke-others-float-new-political-party/ | access-date=2021-09-24}}</ref>

==Governorship candidacy==
Pat Utomi was declared as the governorship candidate of the [[All Progressive Congress]] for the 2019 general election in Delta State by a faction of the All Progressive Congress.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.punchng.com/breaking-delta-apc-faction-declares-pat-utomi-gov-candidate/amp/|title = Delta APC faction declares Pat Utomi gov candidate|date = October 2018}}</ref>
The national working Committee of the All Progressive Congress overturned the factional declaration and declared Chief [[Great Ogboru]] as its Delta state gubernatorial Candidate for the 2019 general Elections.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.guardian.ng/news/apc-denies-magnus-abe-pat-utomi-governorship-tickets/amp|title = APC denies Magnus Abe, Pat Utomi governorship tickets}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vanguardngr.com/2018/10/utomi-explodes-says-apc-will-lose-delta-governorship-if/amp/|title=Utomi explodes, says APC will lose Delta governorship if|date=7 October 2018}}</ref>
