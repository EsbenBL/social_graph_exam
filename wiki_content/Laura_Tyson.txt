'''Laura D'Andrea Tyson''' (born June 28, 1947) is an [[United States|American]] [[economist]] and former Chair of the US President's [[Council of Economic Advisers]] during the [[Presidency of Bill Clinton|Clinton Administration]]. She also served as Director of the [[National Economic Council (United States)|National Economic Council]]. She is currently a professor at the [[Haas School of Business]] of the [[University of California, Berkeley]], and a senior fellow at the [[Berggruen Institute]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Laura D. Tyson - People|url=https://www.berggruen.org/people/laura-d-tyson/|access-date=2021-01-02|website=Berggruen Institute|language=en-US}}</ref>

== Early life and education ==
Tyson was born '''Laura D'Andrea''' in New Jersey.<ref>{{cite news| url=https://pqasb.pqarchiver.com/USAToday/access/19348806.html?dids=19348806:19348806&FMT=ABS&FMTS=ABS:FT&type=current&date=May+03%2C+1995&author=Janet+L.+Fix&pub=USA+TODAY+(pre-1997+Fulltext)&desc=LAURA+TYSON+%2F%2F+Even+critics+give+her+high+marks&pqatl=google | work=[[USA Today]] | title=LAURA TYSON // Even critics give her high marks | first=Janet L. | last=Fix | date=May 3, 1995}}</ref> Her father was [[Italian American]] and her mother was of Swedish and Dutch descent.<ref>http://www.managementtoday.co.uk/search/article/407205/the-andrew-davidson-interview-laura-tyson/{{dead link|date=December 2017 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> Tyson graduated ''[[summa cum laude]]'' with a B.A. in [[Economics]] from [[Smith College]] in 1969 and earned her Ph.D. in [[Economics]] from the [[Massachusetts Institute of Technology]] in 1974.<ref>{{cite document|title=Inflation in Yugoslavia, 1962-1972; an empirical analysis.|hdl = 1721.1/13931}}</ref> Her doctoral advisor was [[Evsey Domar]].<ref>[http://library.mit.edu/F/2C5XDBG1ISI933RATQYQ5VV53YNUTVR1YTGP4KGY4QR8Y5K2PS-04955?func=find-acc&acc_sequence=003671580 Inflation in Yugoslavia, 1962-1972; an empirical analysis.]</ref> She joined the faculty of the economics department at [[Princeton University]] in 1974 and remained in the position until 1977 when she became a professor of economics at the [[University of California, Berkeley]]. She was appointed a professor of [[business administration]] in 1990.

== Career ==
From 2002 to 2006, Tyson was the first female Dean of [[London Business School]]. From 1998 to 2001, she was Dean of the [[Haas School of Business]]. She served in the [[Bill Clinton|Clinton Administration]] as Chairman of the President's [[Council of Economic Advisers]] from 1993 to 1995. She was a spokesperson in favour of [[General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade|GATT]], arguing with [[Sir James Goldsmith]] on [[Charlie Rose]] that American jobs will be increased by the trade agreement.  Tyson was Director of the [[National Economic Council (United States)|National Economic Council]] from 1995 to 1996. Tyson has been a member of the [[Council on Foreign Relations]] since 1987, a board director of [[Morgan Stanley]] since 1997, a board director of [[AT&T Inc.]] since 1999, a board director of [[Eastman Kodak]] and is a member of the [[Committee on Capital Markets Regulation]]. In December, 2009 it was announced that Tyson will join [[CB Richard Ellis]] Board of Directors on March 4, 2010.<ref>{{Cite press release|title=Laura D. Tyson Joins CB Richard Ellis Group Inc. Board of Directors|publisher=CB Richard Ellis|date=December 8, 2009|url=http://www.cbre.com/EN/AboutUs/MediaCentre/2009/Pages/120809.aspx|access-date=December 10, 2009}}</ref> Tyson also sits on the [[QFINANCE]] Strategic Advisory Board.

[[Image:Laura Tyson.jpg|thumb|right|Tyson at the [[World Economic Forum]] in [[Davos]], [[Switzerland]], 2007]]

Tyson has published a number of books and articles on industrial competitiveness, [[trade]], and also on the economies of [[Central Europe]] and their transitions to [[market system]]s.<ref name="tyson_">{{cite book |title=Who's Bashing Whom: Trade Conflict in High Technology Industries|url=https://archive.org/details/whosbashingwhomt00tyso|url-access=registration|author=Laura Tyson|publisher=Washington, D.C.: Institute for International Economics}}</ref><ref name="AJE-Corporate">{{cite news |title=The corporate tax conundrum  |author=Laura Tyson |url=http://www.aljazeera.com/indepth/opinion/2012/05/20125512854705939.html |publisher=Al Jazeera English |access-date=May 12, 2012 |quote=The country's relatively high rate encourages US companies to locate their investment, production and employment in foreign countries, and discourages foreign companies from locating in the US, which means slower growth, fewer jobs, smaller productivity gains and lower real wages.
 }}
</ref>

An Economic Viewpoint columnist for [[BusinessWeek]] magazine, Tyson writes regularly about domestic and international economic policy matters in [[The Washington Post]], [[The New York Times]] and other nationally and internationally syndicated newspapers and magazines.

In addition to her professorship at UC Berkeley, Tyson is also a member of the Board of Trustees at UC Berkeley's Blum Center for Developing Economies.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blumcenter.berkeley.edu/blum-center-developing-economies/blum-center-trustees |title=Archived copy |access-date=2011-10-26 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111110154336/http://blumcenter.berkeley.edu/blum-center-developing-economies/blum-center-trustees |archive-date=2011-11-10 }}</ref> The Center is focused on finding solutions to address the crisis of [[extreme poverty]] and disease in the developing world.<ref>blumcenter.berkeley.edu</ref>

Tyson joined [[Berkeley Research Group, LLC]], an expert services advisory firm co-founded by [[David Teece]], as a special advisor in 2010.<ref>{{Cite press release|title=Dr. Laura D'Andrea Tyson Joins Berkeley Research Group as a Special Advisor|publisher=Berkeley Research Group|date=July 13, 2010|url=http://www.businesswire.com/portal/site/home/permalink/?ndmViewId=news_view&newsId=20100713005699&newsLang=en|access-date=March 28, 2012}}</ref> Tyson had consulted for [[LECG]], another expert advisory firm founded by Teece, from 1997 to 2001.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.haas.berkeley.edu/faculty/pdf/tyson.pdf |title=Archived copy |access-date=2012-03-28 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100613053227/http://www.haas.berkeley.edu/faculty/pdf/tyson.pdf |archive-date=2010-06-13 }}</ref>

Since 2012, Tyson has written monthly columns for international media organization [[Project Syndicate]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.project-syndicate.org/columnist/laura-tyson|title=Laura Tyson - Project Syndicate|website=Project Syndicate|language=en|access-date=2017-10-20}}</ref>

In November 2013, Tyson founded the [[Institute for Business and Social Impact]] at the University of California, Berkeley, Haas School of Business.
