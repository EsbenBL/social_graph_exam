{{nihongo|'''Kazuhide Uekusa'''|植草 一秀|Uekusa Kazuhide|born December 18, 1960}} is a Japanese [[economist]], economic analyst, former senior economist at [[Nomura Research Institute]], and chairman of the [[Three-Nations Research Institute]].<ref name="uekusa20101113"/><ref name=uekusa>{{cite book|author=Kazuhide Uekusa|script-title=ja:知られざる真実 勾留地にて|trans-title=Untold Truth: At the Detention Center|publisher=Epushiron Publishing Project|language=Japanese|location=Tokyo|year=2007|pages=209–269|isbn=978-4903145280}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|author=Kazuhide Uekusa |script-title=ja:国家は「有罪(えんざい)」をこうして創る 「植草事件」裁判記録 |trans-title=Miscarriage of Justice: How the Government Makes the Innocent Guilty |author2=Takahiko Soejima |author3=Hirohiko Takahashi  |publisher=[[Shodensha]] |location=Tokyo |year=2012 |pages=195–216 |isbn=978-4396614256|language=Japanese}}</ref> He was arrested in 2004 and 2006 for [[sex and the law|sex offences]].<ref>{{cite book|author=Anil Aggrawal|author-link=Anil Aggrawal|title=Forensic and Medico-legal Aspects of Sexual Crimes and Unusual Sexual Practices|url=https://archive.org/details/forensicmedicole00aggr_764|url-access=limited|publisher=CRC Press|year=2009|pages=[https://archive.org/details/forensicmedicole00aggr_764/page/n162 134], 185|isbn=9781420043082}}</ref>

==Life and career==
In 1979, he entered the [[University of Tokyo]] and majored in economics. Uekusa joined [[Nomura Research Institute]] in April 1983 after graduating from University of Tokyo in March 1983. He obtained positions as a researcher of Fiscal and Monetary Policy Institute at the [[Ministry of Finance (Japan)|Ministry of Finance]] in July 1985, an [[assistant professor]] at the Economic Research Institute at [[Kyoto University]] in June 1991, an honorary fellow of the [[Hoover Institution]] at [[Stanford University]] in October 1993, a senior economist at Nomura Research Institute in April 2002, and became a professor at the graduate school in [[Waseda University]] from April 2003, until he was dismissed in April 2004. Afterwards, he established the Three-Nations Research Institute, and became a chairman of the company on April 1, 2005. In April 2006, he made a career comeback in an academic position as a [[visiting professor]] at [[Nagoya University of Commerce & Business]] graduate school in [[Nagoya]]. He lectured on "national economic strategy" until the arrest in September of the year.<ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20060324060106/http://japundit.com/archives/2006/01/27/1921/ Japundit » Nagoya U hires perv prof]</ref>

He appeared as a commentator on television programs such as [[Fuji TV]]'s morning ''[[Tokudane!]]''.<ref name="asiax200407">{{cite web|url=http://www.asiax.biz/column/musashino/001.php |script-title=ja:植草教授の奇妙な行動 |trans-title=The strange behaviour of Professor Uekusa |publisher=Media Japan PTE Ltd. |work=Asia X |date=July 2004 |accessdate=27 June 2014 |author=Kamijo, Masashi |language=Japanese |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20140820022608/http://www.asiax.biz/column/musashino/001.php |archivedate=20 August 2014 }}</ref><ref name="jt20060915"/>

===Criminal charges===
On April 8, 2004, Uekusa was arrested for trying to peep under a high school girl's skirt using his hand mirror on an escalator at [[Shinagawa Station]] of [[East Japan Railway Company]] in [[Minato, Tokyo]].<ref>{{cite news|language=Japanese|title=植草一秀教授、のぞきの疑い JR品川駅で現行犯逮捕|trans-title=Professor Kazuhide Uekusa arrested at JR Shinagawa Station on suspicion of peeping |date=2004-04-12|newspaper=Asahi Shimbun}}</ref> Immediately after the arrest, Uekusa admitted his guilt,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.japantimes.co.jp/2004/06/18/announcements/economist-denies-looking-up-skirt/|title=Economist denies looking up skirt|work=The Japan Times|date=2004-06-18}}</ref> and he apologized.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.japantimes.co.jp/news/2004/04/13/national/tv-economist-held-over-peek-up-dress/|title=TV economist held over peek up dress|work=The Japan Times|date=2004-04-13}}</ref> He was dismissed from being a professor on May 7, 2004 because of the arrest, despite denying the charges.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.japantimes.co.jp/news/2004/08/31/national/scholar-denies-peeping-up-skirt/|title=Scholar denies peeping up skirt|work=The Japan Times|date=2004-08-31}}</ref>

His trial opened at the [[Tokyo District Court]] on March 23, 2005, and the [[Chief Justice]] fined  him 500,000 yen and confiscated his hand mirror. He insisted that it was an "unfair judgement" and intended to appeal.<ref>{{cite news|script-title=ja:植草元教授に有罪判決 手鏡事件|trans-title=Guilty verdict for Professor Kazuhide Uekusa hand mirror case|date=2005-03-23|newspaper=Asahi Shimbun|language=Japanese}}</ref> The ruling was established however as he did not make the appeal by the deadline on April 6, 2005.<ref>{{cite news|title=植草被告の有罪確定 手鏡事件 |trans-title=Uekusa's guilty verdict confirmed in hand mirror case |date=2005-04-07 |newspaper=Asahi Shimbun}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.japantimes.co.jp/news/2005/04/08/national/peeping-tom-to-pay-fine-lose-mirror/|title=Peeping Tom to pay fine, lose mirror|work=The Japan Times|date=2005-04-08}}</ref>

Uekusa was arrested again at [[Keikyū Kamata Station]] for molesting a girl in a [[Keikyu]] train on September 13, 2006, at around 10:10&nbsp;p.m.<ref name="jt20060915">{{cite web|url=http://www.japantimes.co.jp/news/2006/09/15/national/peeping-economist-nabbed-in-groping/|title=Peeping economist nabbed in groping|work=The Japan Times|date=2006-09-15}}</ref> On September 27, 2006, [[Nagoya University of Commerce & Business]] announced that they had dismissed him from his duties as a visiting professor.<ref>{{cite web|author=Ryann Connell |url=http://mdn.mainichi.jp/culture/waiwai/archive/news/2006/09/20060922p2g00m0dm024000c.html |title=Third strike for naughty number cruncher Kazuhide Uekusa |work=Mainichi Daily News |date=2006-09-22 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20070514030407/http://mdn.mainichi.jp/culture/waiwai/archive/news/2006/09/20060922p2g00m0dm024000c.html |archivedate=May 14, 2007 }}</ref> Uekusa claimed to be innocent, saying that he was drunk when he was arrested, so he does not remember what happened that night.<ref>{{cite news|script-title=ja:植草教授、また逮捕 女子高生に痴漢の疑い |trans-title=Professor Uekusa arrested again - on suspicion of groping high-school girl |date=2006-09-14|newspaper=Asahi Shimbun|language=Japanese}}</ref> He was indicted for ''[[groping#Japan|chikan]]'' on October 4, 2006.<ref>{{cite news|script-title=ja:女子高生に痴漢、植草容疑者起訴 |trans-title=Uekusa prosecuted for groping high-school girl |language=Japanese|date=2006-10-05|newspaper=Asahi Shimbun}}</ref>

On January 22, 2007, Uekusa was released from the Tokyo Detention Center on bail of 6 million yen.<ref>{{cite news |script-title=ja:植草元教授に6か月求刑 電車痴漢事件 東京地裁 |trans-title=6-month sentence requested for former professor Uekusa in Tokyo groping case at Tokyo District Court |date=2007-07-18 |newspaper=Asahi Shimbun|language=Japanese }}</ref>

On October 16, 2007, Uekusa was sentenced to four months in prison by Presiding Judge Sho Kamisaka of the Tokyo District Court.<ref>{{cite news|script-title=ja:植草被告、実刑4カ月 痴漢の罪で東京地裁判決 |trans-title=Uekusa receives 4-month prison sentence at Tokyo District Court in groping case |language=Japanese|date=2007-10-16|newspaper=Asahi Shimbun}}</ref> He repeated his earlier denials,<ref name=uekusa/> but fabric traces on his suit in addition to witness testimony were enough to convince the court that he had groped under the victim's skirt.<ref name=japantimes20071017/> In handing down the sentence, Kamisaka noted that "The court cannot anticipate that the accused will be rehabilitated" on his own recognizance.<ref name=japantimes20071017/> Uekusa vowed to appeal, in a statement saying "the truth will always prevail."<ref name=japantimes20071017>{{cite web|author=Jun Hongo|url=http://www.japantimes.co.jp/news/2007/10/17/news/groping-economist-gets-four-month-prison-term/|title=Groping economist gets four-month prison term|work=The Japan Times|date=2007-10-17}}</ref> On April 16, 2008, The Tokyo High Court upheld a four-month prison term for Uekusa.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.japantimes.co.jp/news/2008/04/17/national/man-sentenced-to-life-for-28-rapes-attempted-sexual-assaults/|title=Man sentenced to life for 28 rapes, attempted sexual assaults|work=The Japan Times|date=2008-04-17}}</ref> On June 25, 2009, The Supreme Court turned down Uekusa's final appeal.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.japantimes.co.jp/news/2009/06/28/national/uekusa-prison-term-upheld-by-top-court/|title=Uekusa prison term upheld by top court|work=The Japan Times|date=2009-06-28}}</ref>

In an interview with journalist [[Benjamin Fulford]] on June 19, 2008, he stated that he had been arrested in the national policy investigation after criticizing an economic policy by [[Junichiro Koizumi]] and [[Heizō Takenaka]] on a television program.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1w1zqMhxZMU |title=植草一秀ＶＳベンジャミン・フルフォード対談 |trans-title=Kazuhide Uekusa talks with Benjamin Fulford |publisher=Youtube.com |date=2008-07-20 |accessdate=2013-01-04}}</ref>
