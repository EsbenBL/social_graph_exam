'''Amy Singer''' (born September 8, 1953) is a Florida trial consultant and research [[psychologist]].<ref>Posey, Amy J. and Wrightsman, Lawrence S., [https://www.amazon.com/Trial-Consulting-American-Psychology-Law-Society/dp/0195183096/ref=sr_1_2?s=books&ie=UTF8&qid=1309109027&sr=1-2  Trial Consulting], Oxford University Press, 2005, pp. 17, 18, 24, 81, 82, 85, 90, 254, 263, 270.</ref> Singer’s firm, Trial Consultants, Inc., which she founded in [[Miami]] in 1979, is one of the first [[trial consulting]] firms in the United States.<ref>[http://directory.westlaw.com/scope/default.asp?db=TRDELIB&RS=W&VR=2.0  Westlaw Database Directory], "[http://articles.sun-sentinel.com/2003-01-05/news/0301040494_1_jury-s-racial-makeup-black-officers-four-questionable-shootings/3  Race May Be Key In Police Trial Jury Selection],” Diana Marrero, SunSentinel.com, January 5, 2003, p. 3., and "[http://articles.sun-sentinel.com/1985-12-01/news/8502230947_1_mock-jury-trial-consultants-case  Attorneys Use Mock Juries To Predict Cases]," Patricia Elich, SunSentinel.com, December 1, 1985.</ref> Singer is an acknowledged authority in the field of litigation psychology, a discipline she helped pioneer.<ref>“[http://info.courtroomview.com/Blog/bid/42329/Jury-Research-Expert-Dr-Amy-Singer-Interviewed-By-CVN  Jury Research Expert Dr. Amy Singer Interviewed By CVN] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110713031650/http://info.courtroomview.com/Blog/bid/42329/Jury-Research-Expert-Dr-Amy-Singer-Interviewed-By-CVN |date=2011-07-13 }},” Courtroom Views – The CVN News Blog, June 29, 2010, and “[http://articles.sun-sentinel.com/1985-12-01/news/8502230947_1_mock-jury-trial-consultants-case  Attorneys Use Mock Juries To Predict Cases],” Patricia Elich, SunSentinel.com, December 1, 1985.</ref> Her revolutionary approach, which consists of applying principles of psychology and using open-ended questions to elicit jurors’ value beliefs regarding key trial issues, changed the way that attorneys around the United States conduct [[voir dire]]. Largely through Singer’s influence, this became a juror de-selection, not selection, process.<ref>“[https://archive.is/20120910210817/http://www.nj-justice.org/NJ/index.cfm?event=showPage&pg=bwtrialstrategies  Creating a Fungible Judge and Jury: Breaking Trial Strategies and Kabbalah Techniques],” Boardwalk Seminar 2011, April 15, 2011, New Jersey Association for Justice Education Foundation, Inc.; and [http://www.trialconsultants.com/Library/RTJ-Defense.html Singer Litigation Library].</ref>

Singer is a leader in the application of [[neuro-linguistic programming]] (NLP) by attorneys to influence jurors.<ref>“[http://www.howardnations.com/persuasivejuryarguments/secretsway.html Secret Sway] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120214133715/http://www.howardnations.com/persuasivejuryarguments/secretsway.html |date=February 14, 2012 }},” Terry Carter, ABA Journal, p. 63, September 2001, see webpage: The Law Offices of Howard L. Nations.</ref>  Singer also is believed to be the first person to conduct a shadow jury on television, which she did for A Current Affair in its coverage of the [[William Kennedy Smith]] rape trial in Palm Beach, Florida, in 1991.<ref>"[http://www.people.com/people/archive/article/0,,20111523,00.html Hot on the Trial],” Pope Brock, People, Vol. 36, No. 23, December 16, 1991.</ref>

Singer is a 1975 graduate of [[Hofstra]] University, [[Hempstead, New York|Hempstead]], [[New York (state)|New York]]. She received her [[Master of Arts]] (1977) and her [[doctorate]] (1978) at Hofstra in applied research psychology. Singer is licensed in [[clinical psychology]] in New York. She is a past instructor in psychology at [[Nova Southeastern University]], [[Fort Lauderdale]], and [[Florida International University]], [[Miami-Dade County]], Florida.

Singer continues to serve as a popular and prolific author, trial consultant, and speaker contributing her expertise to national publications, broadcast media, legal associations and high-profile cases such as the [[Death of Caylee Anthony|Casey Anthony]], [[Jack Kevorkian|Dr. Kavorkian]], [[Michael Jackson]], William Kennedy Smith and [[O. J. Simpson]] trials.

In her most recent innovative strategy, Singer analyzed over 40,000 blog posts and tweets<ref>Blog Posts and Tweets Helped Defense Team Win Acquittal for Casey Anthony in Capital Murder Case [http://www.abajournal.com/news/article/blog_posts_and_tweets_helped_defense_team_win_acquittal/ Blog Posts and Tweets Helped Defense Team Win Acquittal for Casey Anthony in Capital Murder Case]</ref> relating to the Casey Anthony trial. Singer was the first trial consultant to use social media as a strategy.
