'''Christopher Peterson''' (February 18, 1950 – October 9, 2012)<ref>{{cite journal|title= Obituaries: Christopher Peterson "Other People Matter": 1950–2012|date= 2013-03-04|doi=10.1111/aphw.12007|pmid= 23457083|volume=5|issue= 1|pages=1–4|journal=Applied Psychology: Health and Well-Being|last1 = Park|first1 = Nansook|last2= Oates|first2= Sarah|last3= Schwarzer|first3= Ralf|url= https://deepblue.lib.umich.edu/bitstream/2027.42/96673/1/aphw12007.pdf|hdl= 2027.42/96673|hdl-access= free}}</ref> was the Arthur F. Thurnau professor of [[psychology]] and organizational studies at the [[University of Michigan]] in [[Ann Arbor]], [[Michigan]] and the former chair of the clinical psychology area. He was science director of the [[VIA Institute on Character]], and co-author of ''[[Character Strengths and Virtues (book)|Character Strengths and Virtues]]'' for the classification of character strengths.<ref>Peterson, C., & Seligman, M. E. P. (2004). Character strengths and virtues: A handbook and classification. New York: Oxford University Press/Washington, DC: American Psychological Association.</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Measuring Your Character Strengths|url=https://psychcentral.com/blog/measuring-your-character-strengths/|last=Tartakovsky|first=Margarita|date=2011-01-05|website=psychcentral.com|language=en-US|access-date=2020-05-19}}</ref> He was a member of the Positive Psychology Steering Committee and the International Positive Psychology Association board of directors, a senior fellow at the Positive Psychology Center and a lecturer for the Master of Applied Positive Psychology program at the [[University of Pennsylvania]]. He was a co-editor of Applied Psychology: Health and Well-being and the Positive Psychology Book Series Editor for [[Oxford University Press]].<ref>{{Cite book|title=Applied positive psychology: improving everyday life, health, schools, work, and society|date=2011|publisher=Psychology Press|others=Stewart Ian Donaldson, Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi, Jeanne Nakamura|isbn=978-0-203-81890-9|location=New York, NY|oclc=713015173}}</ref>

He is noted for his work in the study of [[optimism]], [[health]], [[character structure|character]], [[well-being]] and one of the founders of positive psychology.<ref>{{cite book|last=Peterson|first=Christopher|title=A Primer in Positive Psychology|publisher=Oxford University Press|location=Oxford|year=2006}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://viacharacter.org |title=VIA Institute on Character|access-date=2008-04-12}}</ref> He has published over 300 academic publications. In 2003 the [[Institute for Scientific Information]] (ISI) named him among the 100 most frequently cited psychologists in the past 20 years.<ref>{{Cite book|last=Wong|first=Paul T. P.|title=The human quest for meaning: theories, research, and applications|isbn=1-136-50810-4|edition=2nd|location=New York|oclc=787851351}}</ref> In 2010, Dr. Peterson won the 2010 Golden Apple Award for Outstanding Teaching – the most prestigious teaching award at the [[University of Michigan]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Psychology Prof. Christopher Peterson receives Golden Apple Award|url=https://www.michigandaily.com/content/psychology-prof-peterson-gets-golden-apple-award|website=The Michigan Daily|language=en|access-date=2020-05-19}}</ref>
