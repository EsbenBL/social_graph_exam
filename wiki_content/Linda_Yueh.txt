'''Linda Yueh''' is a British/American economist, broadcaster, and author, born in [[Taiwan]] and of dual British and American citizenship.<ref>{{cite web|title=BBC World News names Linda Yueh as Chief Business Correspondent|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/mediacentre/worldnews/linda-yueh|website=BBC Media Centre|publisher=[[BBC]]|accessdate=22 December 2015}}</ref> Yueh is an [[Adjunct Professor]] of Economics at [[London Business School]],<ref name="Faculty Profile, Linda Yueh"/> and a [[Fellow]] in Economics at [[St Edmund Hall]], [[Oxford University]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Academic profile – Linda Yueh|url=http://www.economics.ox.ac.uk/index.php/academic/linda-yueh|publisher=Oxford University|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20131029225802/http://www.economics.ox.ac.uk/index.php/academic/linda-yueh|archivedate=29 October 2013|df=dmy-all}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.seh.ox.ac.uk/discover/research/china-growth-centre-cgc|title=China Growth Centre}}</ref> She was also a Visiting Professor at [[Peking University]] and associated with both the Centre for Economic Performance and [[LSE IDEAS|IDEAS]] research centres at the [[London School of Economics]] (LSE).<ref>{{cite web|title=Speakers archive – Linda Yueh |url=http://www.thersa.org/events/speakers-archive/y/dr-linda-yueh |publisher=RSA – Royal Society for the encouragement of Arts, Manufactures and Commerce |accessdate=8 June 2013 |url-status=dead for [[British Broadcasting Corporation|BBC]] programmes such as Radio 4 Analysis, Business Daily on [[BBC World Service]], and [[Today (BBC Radio 4)|Radio 4 Today]] programme.<ref ==Background==
Yueh was born in [[Taipei]] and emigrated to the United States around 1982 when she was five years old. She has a [[Bachelor of Arts|BA]] from [[Yale University]], a [[Master of Public Policy]] from [[Harvard University]], a [[Juris Doctor|JD]] from [[New York University]] School of Law and a [[Doctor of Philosophy|D.Phil]] in Economics and MA from [[St Edmund Hall, Oxford]].<ref name="Faculty Profile, Linda Yueh">{{cite web|title=Faculty Profile, Linda Yueh|url=http://www.london.edu/facultyandresearch/faculty/search.do?uid=lyueh|publisher=London Business School|accessdate=8 June 2013}}</ref>

==Career==
Yueh has been a [[corporate lawyer]] at [[Paul, Weiss, Rifkind, Wharton & Garrison]] resident in New York City, Beijing, and Hong Kong.<ref name="Correspondents" />

She was previously a [[non-executive director]] of two FTSE-listed companies, a board member of [[London & Partners]], the official promotion agency of London, and an adviser to the British Chambers of Commerce (BCC). Yueh is currently a non-executive director at [[Rentokil Initial]], she has held this role since November 2017.{{cn|date=June 2021}}

She is an External Trustee of the Coutts Foundation and also on the advisory board of [[OMFIF]]. She serves on the supervisory Policy Committee of the Centre for Economic Performance (CEP) at the [[London School of Economics]] (LSE). She is also the Chair of the [[Royal Commonwealth Society]] and [[Malaria No More UK]].<ref name="london.edu">{{Cite web|url=https://www.london.edu/faculty-and-research/faculty-profiles/y/yueh-l|title=Linda Yueh|website=London Business School}}</ref>

In September 2020, she was named an advisor to the British [[Board of Trade]].<ref name="https://www.gov.uk/government/groups/board-of-trade#advisers-to-the-board-september-2020">{{Cite web|url=https://www.gov.uk/government/groups/board-of-trade|title=Board of Trade|website=GOV.UK}}</ref>

==International activities==
Yueh has been an advisor to the [[World Bank]], the [[European Commission]], the [[Asian Development Bank]], and the [[World Economic Forum]] at [[Davos]].<ref name="london.edu"/>

==Academia==
Yueh has had several dozen scholarly articles published<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.lindayueh.com/Pages/contactus.aspx |title=Personal website |access-date=8 June 2013 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130722174036/http://www.lindayueh.com/Pages/contactus.aspx |archive-date=22 July 2013 |url-status=dead }}</ref>{{Obsolete source|date=August 2020}}{{Self-published inline|date=August 2020}}{{unreliable source?|date=August 2020}} while teaching at Oxford University, London Business School, London School of Economics and Political Science and Peking University where she holds a visiting professorship in economics.
