'''Jane Green''' is a British [[political scientist]] and academic. She is Professor of Political Science and British Politics at the [[University of Oxford]] and a professorial fellow of [[Nuffield College]]. She is a specialist in [[public opinion]] and [[electoral behaviour]], and has co-directed the [[British Election Study]].

==Academic work==
Green received her PhD from Nuffield College, Oxford. She then joined the faculty at the [[University of Manchester]], before moving to the University of Oxford.<ref name=fas>{{cite web |url=https://ces.fas.harvard.edu/people/jane-elizabeth-green |title=Jane Elizabeth Green |publisher=Harvard University |year=2017 |access-date=28 October 2020}}</ref>

In 2017, Green coauthored the book ''The Politics of Competence: Parties, Public Opinion and Voters'' with political scientist Will Jennings. In ''The Politics of Competence'', Green and Jennings study how voters evaluate the competence of political parties on specific issues.<ref name=pederson20>{{cite journal |first=Christoffer |last=Green-Pedersen |title=Review The Politics of Competence: Parties, Public Opinion and Voters |journal=Perspectives on Politics |volume=18 |issue=2 |pages=648–649 |date=June 2020 |doi=10.1017/S1537592720000626|s2cid=225826071 }}</ref> They use data from the United Kingdom, the United States, Canada, Australia, and Germany to study three components of party issue competence: the reputation of parties on particular issues, voters' evaluations of parties on a particular issue, and how well voters think a party is performing overall.<ref name=pederson20/>

Green was a coauthor, together with the other British Election Study team members, of the 2020 book ''Electoral Shocks: Understanding the Volatile Voter in a Turbulent World''. The book uses data from the British Election Study to examine the state of British politics, and is organised around five shocks to British elections in recent years: the economic crisis, [[Brexit]], immigration following the [[2004 enlargement of the European Union]], the [[Conservative–Liberal Democrat coalition agreement]] and the [[2014 Scottish independence referendum]].<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.ft.com/content/f9be4482-392e-11ea-a6d3-9a26f8c3cba4 |title=Electoral Shocks: the Volatile Voter in a Turbulent World, by Edward Fieldhouse, Jane Green and others |work=Financial Times |first=Miranda |last=Green |date=January 20, 2020 |access-date=28 October 2020}}</ref>

Green has served on the editorial boards of prominent political science journals including ''[[Comparative Political Studies]]'' and ''Political Science Research and Methods''. She has been the co-director of the British Election Study,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.britishelectionstudy.com/meet-the-bes-team/ |title=Meet the BES team: Professor Jane Green |publisher=British Election Study |access-date=October 28, 2020}}</ref> and she was a member of the inquiry established by the [[British Polling Council]] and the [[Market Research Society]] into the failings of the polling relating to the British 2015 general election.<ref name=fas/>

Green has provided election analysis for ''[[ITV News]]'', including as part of their election night coverage.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.politics.ox.ac.uk/news/jane-green-part-of-itv-news-election-coverage.html |title=Jane Green part of ITV News election coverage |publisher=University of Oxford |year=2019 |access-date=28 October 2020}}</ref> She has also done political analysis on [[BBC]] television and radio programs.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/p02mfd71 |title=What does a 'majority' mean in a general election? |work=BBC |date=March 20, 2015 |access-date=28 October 2020}}</ref> She received the Research Communicator of the Year award from the [[Political Studies Association]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.manchester.ac.uk/discover/news/the-bes-jane-green-is-research-communicator-of-the-year/ |title=The BES' Jane Green is 'Research Communicator of the Year' |publisher=University of Manchester |date=December 1, 2015 |access-date=28 October 2020}}</ref>
