'''Yair Tauman''' ({{lang-he|יאיר טאומן}}; born January 20, 1948) is a Professor of [[Economics]] at [[State University of New York, Stony Brook]] and the Director of the Stony Brook Center for Game Theory.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.gtcenter.org|title=Stony Brook Center for Game Theory|website=www.gtcenter.org}}</ref> He studied at the [[Hebrew University of Jerusalem]] where he obtained his B.Sc. in [[Mathematics]] and [[Statistics]] and M.Sc. and Ph.D. in Mathematics, the latter two under the supervision of [[Robert Aumann]]. His areas of research interests are [[game theory]] and [[industrial organization]]. He has published, among others, in ''[[Econometrica]]'', ''[[Games and Economic Behavior]]'', ''[[Journal of Economic Theory]]'', ''[[Quarterly Journal of Economics]]'' and ''[[The RAND Journal of Economics|RAND Journal of Economics]]''.

Tauman has been the organizer of the longest and most established series of [[International Conference on Game Theory at Stony Brook|International Summer Conferences in Game Theory]] (for over 25 years)<ref name="festival">[http://gtcenter.org/?page=Archive.html The Summer Festival in Game Theory], held annually at Stony Brook.</ref> and has been on the Faculty at the Kellogg Graduate School of Management and served as the dean of the business school at the Interdisciplinary Center in Hertzliya, Israel. Since 2009 he has served as the academic director of the Zell entrepreneurship program in the Interdisciplinary Center in Hertzliya.

== Biography (Business) ==

In 2005, Tauman led a small group of Israeli investors to interfere with a takeover of online auction company [[Tradus|QXL]]<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.haaretz.com/1.4844252|title=TA Professor Leads Way to $37m Profit on QXL Trading|date=13 September 2005|newspaper=Haaretz}}</ref> which was then sold for $1.9 Billion dollars.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.haaretz.com/1.4967840|title=Izaki Group Makes $270m on QXL Sale|date=19 December 2007|newspaper=Haaretz}}</ref>

Tauman co-founded [[Bidorbuy]].com and has served as a member of the board of directors for the following companies: ADFVN,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.rttnews.com/1223792/advfn-names-yair-tauman-non-executive-director-quick-facts.aspx|title=ADVFN Names Yair Tauman Non-Executive Director - Quick Facts}}</ref> Digi-block, Radware, and Expo-bee.

Since 2011, Tauman has served as a director at [[Bank Hapoalim]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.duedil.com/director/916948610/yair-tauman|title=DueDil|website=www.duedil.com}}</ref>

== Biography (Film) ==
Tauman produced a film about his sister, entitled, ''My Sister Tikva: A Holocaust Journey''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.go2films.com/Family-Social-issues/My-Sister-Tikva|title=My Sister Tikva Go 2 Films}}</ref>  It was named best international film at the [[Chagrin Documentary Film Festival]].<ref>[http://www.clevelandjewishnews.com/features/leisure/arts/article_999604b2-0964-11e1-a9fd-001cc4c002e0.html `Tikvah' tops at festival], Clevland Jewish News, Nov 7, 2011.</ref>

Tauman also stars in a [[TEDx]] talk on game theory.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.tedxtelaviv.com/2010/08/06/yair-tauman-about-serious-games|title=TEDx Talk By Yair Tauman}}</ref>
