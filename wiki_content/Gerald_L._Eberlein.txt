'''Gerald L. Eberlein''' (5 May 1930 &ndash; 12 August 2010) was a German [[sociology]] educator.

==Biography==
Eberlein was born in [[Berlin|Berlin, Germany]] to Kurt Karl and Alice Caroline (b. Seiffermann) Eberlein.
Gerald L. Eberlein became Ph.D at the [[University of Technology|Technical University]] [[Stuttgart|Stuttgart, Germany]], in 1962. He was [[Professor|assistant professor]] at the University of Münster and at the [[Free University of Berlin]], Germany, 1963-1970. Between 1971 and 1972 he was visiting professor at the [[Technical University of Berlin]]. 1972-1975 he was associate professor of [[Philosophy of social science|philosophy of the social sciences]], economy and politics at the University of the Saarland, Germany. Eberlein was professor of sociology at the [[Technical University of Munich]], Germany, 1975-1998. In 1998 he became professor emeritus.

Gerald L. Eberlein has one child, Viviane Caroline. He was living in [[Pöcking, Germany]] at the time of his death.
