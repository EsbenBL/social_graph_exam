'''Barbara L. Bonner''' is a [[Clinical psychology|clinical psychologist]] and expert on juvenile sex offenders.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://centerforchildpolicy.org/experts.html|title=Center for Child Policy - Experts|website=centerforchildpolicy.org|language=en-US|access-date=2018-12-03}}</ref> She is known for her research on the assessment and treatment of [[Child abuse|abused children]], prevention of child fatalities due to neglect, and treatment of children and adolescents with problematic sexual behavior.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://ashonors.okstate.edu/barbara-bonner/|title=BARBARA BONNER, Ph.D.|date=2016-09-02|work=College of Arts and Sciences|access-date=2018-10-02|language=en-US}}</ref>  Bonner is the CMRI/Jean Gumerson Endowed Chair and Professor of Pediatrics at the [[University of Oklahoma College of Medicine]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.oumedicine.com/department-of-pediatrics/department-sections/devbehav/faculty|title=OU Medicine Department of Pediatrics}}</ref> She serves as the Director of the Child Abuse and Neglect at the [[University of Oklahoma Health Sciences Center]].<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=http://www.ncsby.org/content/about-us|title=About us {{!}} National Center on the Sexual Behavior of Youth|website=www.ncsby.org|language=en|access-date=2018-12-03}}</ref>

Bonner was recipient of the 2014 [[American Psychological Association]] Award for Distinguished Contributions of Applications of Psychology to Education and Training.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.apa.org/about/awards/appsyc-edutrain.aspx?tab=3|title=Distinguished Contributions of Applications of Psychology to Education and Training|website=www.apa.org|language=en|access-date=2018-11-06}}</ref> Her award cited "her passionate determination to improve the lives of children throughout the world by training countless professionals to be leaders in the field of child maltreatment, for her leadership in establishing one of the most highly respected centers for the study of child maltreatment in the nation, and for directing an interdisciplinary training program on child maltreatment".<ref name=":0">{{Cite journal|date=2014|title=Barbara L. Bonner: Award for Distinguished Contributions of Applications of Psychology to Education and Training.|journal=American Psychologist|language=en|volume=69|issue=8|pages=789–791|doi=10.1037/a0037854|pmid=25486157|issn=1935-990X}}</ref>  Bonner was previously awarded the 2001 APA [[Nicholas Hobbs]] Award for Child Advocacy from the Society for Child and Family Policy and Practice (APA Division 37).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.apadivisions.org/division-37/awards/hobbs.aspx|title=Nicholas Hobbs Award|website=www.apadivisions.org|language=en|access-date=2018-12-03}}</ref>

== Biography ==
Bonner received her B.S. degree in [[Special education|Special Education]] at [[Oklahoma State University–Stillwater|Oklahoma State University]].<ref name=":0" />  She obtained a master's degree in [[Educational assessment|Educational Assessment]] and a PhD in [[Clinical psychology|Clinical Psychology]] at Oklahoma State University (1984).

Bonner's career has focused on developing interventions for children with problematic sexual behavior and the treatment and prevention of child abuse. In 1985, while working as a post-doctoral fellow at the University of Oklahoma Health Sciences Center (supervised by C. Eugene Walker),  Bonner established the Adolescents with Illegal Sexual Behavior Treatment Program. In 1987, she joined the faculty of the University of Oklahoma Health Sciences Center and established its Interdisciplinary Training Program in Child Abuse and Neglect.<ref name=":0" />  In 1992, she established the Center on Child Abuse and Neglect in the Department of Pediatrics of the University of Oklahoma Health Sciences Center, and a treatment program for children with sexual behavior problems.<ref name=":1" />

Bonner served terms as President of the [[International Society for the Prevention of Child Abuse and Neglect|International Society for Prevention of Child Abuse]]<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.ispcan.org/who-we-are-2/history/|title=History|work=ISPCAN|access-date=2018-12-03|language=en-US}}</ref> and the American Professional Society on the Abuse of Children (APSAC). The APSAC awarded Bonner the Outstanding Service award in 1997 and the Ronald C. Laney Distinguished Service Award in 2012 in recognition of her contributions through leadership and service to the Society.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://apsac.memberclicks.net/apsac-awards|title=APSAC Awards|website=apsac.memberclicks.net|language=en-us|access-date=2018-12-03|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180114184723/https://apsac.memberclicks.net/apsac-awards|archive-date=2018-01-14|url-status=dead}}</ref> Her other awards include the 2011 Byliner Award for Child Advocacy from the [[Association for Women in Communications]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.awcokc.org/application/files/7214/4667/1321/PastBylinerHonorees_1.pdf|title=The Association for Women in Communications Past Byliner Award Winners}}</ref>

Bonner's work has been funded by the [[National Center on Child Abuse and Neglect]]<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://newsok.com/article/2418704/ou-to-study-treatment-of-child-sex-offenders|title=OU to Study Treatment Of Child Sex Offenders|date=1993-01-17|work=NewsOK.com|access-date=2018-12-03|language=en-US}}</ref> and by the [[United States Department of Health and Human Services|U.S. Department of Health and Human Services]]. Bonner is co-author (with C. Eugene Walker and Keith Kaufman) of the practitioner guide ''The Physically and Sexually Abused Child: Evaluation and Treatment.''<ref>{{Cite book|title=The physically and sexually abused child: Evaluation and treatment|last1=Walker|first1=C. Eugene|last2=Bonner|first2=Barbara L.|last3=Kaufman|first3=Keith L.|date=1988|publisher=Pergamon Press|isbn=978-0080327693|edition= 1st|location=New York|oclc=15518385}}</ref> She is author of the booklet ''Taking Action: Support for Families of Adolescents with Illegal Sexual Behavior'', distributed by the Safer Society Foundation to provide information and support for parents and caregivers of juvenile sex offenders.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.safersociety.org/foundation/taking-action-booklets-help-for-parents/|title=Taking Action booklets – Help for Families & Caregivers|date=2015|work=Safer Society Foundation|access-date=2018-12-03|language=en-US}}</ref> Her research paper "Family preservation and family support programs: Child maltreatment outcomes across client risk levels and program types" (co-authored with Mark Chaffin and Robert Hill) received the Pro Humanitate Literary Award from the North American Resource Center for Child Welfare in 2002.<ref>{{Cite journal|last1=Chaffin|first1=Mark|last2=Bonner|first2=Barbara L|last3=Hill|first3=Robert F|date=2001|title=Family preservation and family support programs: child maltreatment outcomes across client risk levels and program types|journal=Child Abuse & Neglect|volume=25|issue=10|pages=1269–1289|doi=10.1016/s0145-2134(01)00275-7|pmid=11720379|issn=0145-2134}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://youthtoday.org/2002/11/awards-for-november-2002/|title=Awards for November 2002 - Youth Today|website=youthtoday.org|language=en-US|access-date=2018-12-03}}</ref> This large-scale study failed to find evidence that statewide Family Preservation and Family Support programs were effective in decreasing child maltreatment.
