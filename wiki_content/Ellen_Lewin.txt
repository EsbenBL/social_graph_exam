'''Ellen Lewin''' is an American author, anthropologist, and academic.  Lewin, a lesbian,<ref>{{Cite book |last=Lewin |first=Ellen |chapter=Confessions of a Reformed Grant Hustler |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=9sK2x1ZZ9TUC&pg=pa113# |title=Out in the Field: Reflections of Lesbian and Gay Anthropologists |publisher=University of Illinois Press |year=1996 |editor-first=Ellen |editor-last=Lewin |editor2-first=William L. |editor2-last=Leap}}</ref> focuses her work on areas of motherhood, sexuality, and reproduction. She received the [[Ruth Benedict Prize]] in 1992 for her [[monograph]], ''Lesbian Mothers: Accounts of Gender in American Culture.''. Lewin is a professor of anthropology at the [[University of Iowa]].

== Biography ==
Ellen Lewin received her A.B. (Bachelors of Arts) in 1967 for linguistics from the [[College of the University of Chicago]]. She went on to obtain her [[Master of Arts|AM]] (Masters of Arts) in Anthropology in 1968 and later her Ph.D. in Anthropology in 1975 at [[Stanford University]]. She concluded her studies at Stanford University with one of her earlier works, ''Mothers and Children: Latin American Immigrants in San Francisco.''<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://clas.uiowa.edu/gwss/people/ellen-lewin|title=Ellen Lewin {{!}} Gender, Women's & Sexuality Studies {{!}} College of Liberal Arts & Sciences {{!}} The University of Iowa|website=clas.uiowa.edu|language=en|access-date=2017-05-15}}</ref>

Lewin conducted research on Latin American women in San Francisco for her dissertation. She also completed another study comparing aspects of motherhood among lesbians and heterosexual single women. Her research has focused on gender, sexuality, identity, reproduction, medical anthropology and lesbian/gay anthropology.<ref name=":0" />

==Awards==
Lewin was recipient of the [[Ruth Benedict Prize]] in 1992 for her [[monograph]], ''Lesbian Mothers: Accounts of Gender in American Culture.''.<ref name="Ruth Benedict Prize">{{cite web |title=The Ruth Benedict Prize |url=http://queeranthro.org/awards/the-ruth-benedict-prize/ |website=The Association for Queer Anthropology |accessdate=9 June 2019}}</ref> :3
