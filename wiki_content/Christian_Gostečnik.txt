'''Christian Gostečnik''', [[Ordo Fratrum Minorum|OFM]] (born 1 May 1955 in [[Šmihel nad Mozirjem]], [[Slovenia]]) is a Slovenian [[Roman Catholic]] [[priest]] of the [[Franciscan]] [[Order of Friars Minor]], theologian, clinical psychologist and marriage-and-family therapist.

He was [[Dean (education)|dean]] of [[Faculty of Theology, Ljubljana|Faculty of Theology]] ([[University of Ljubljana]]), while currently he is a director of [[Franciscan Family Institute]] in [[Ljubljana]] ({{lang-sl|Frančiškanski družinski inštitut}}; ''FDI'').

==Biography==
After he entered the Franciscan order, he started studying at Faculty of Theology in Ljubljana, where he graduated in 1983.<ref name="MDDSZ">{{Cite web | url = http://www.mddsz.gov.si/si/delovna_podrocja/sociala/nagrade_za_delo_na_podrocju_socialnega_varstva/nagrada_za_izstopajoce_kvalitetno_in_strokovno_delo_2007_cg/ | title = NAGRADA ZA IZSTOPAJOČE KVALITETNO IN STROKOVNO DELO 2007 (CG) | accessdate =23 January 2012 | publisher=Ministry of Work, Family and Social Affairs|language=sl}}</ref>

He was then sent to [[United States]] for further studies and also as a member of Slovenian-American Parish in [[Lemont, Illinois]].<ref name="MDDSZ"/> During his stay in US he completed [[Master of Arts|M.A.]] on [[Loyola University Chicago]] (1988), while at [[Illinois School of Professional Psychology]] ([[Argosy University]]) he completed specialization in Diagnostics (1991), specialization in marriage and family therapy (1993), internship in marriage and family therapy (1994) and PhD in clinical psychology (1995).<ref name="MDDSZ"/><ref name="TEOF">{{Cite web | url = http://www.teof.uni-lj.si/?viewPage=153&action=view&id=27 | title = Gostečnik Christian OFM | accessdate =23 January 2012 | publisher=Faculty of Theology, Ljubljana|language=sl}}</ref> Gostečnik was also instrumental in establishment of [http://www.lemont-svs.org Slovenian Catholic Mission] in Lemont in 1994.<ref name="Lemont">{{Cite web | url = http://www.lemont-svs.org/aboutus.htm | title = Mission Statement | accessdate = 23 January 2012 | publisher = Slovenian Catholic Mission | url-status = dead | archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20120605013526/http://www.lemont-svs.org/aboutus.htm | archivedate = 5 June 2012 }}</ref>

In 1995 he returned to Ljubljana, Slovenia<ref name="TEOF"/> and became assistant professor (religious-educational psychology and medicine) at Faculty of Theology; in the same year he also established '''Franciscan Family Center''' ({{lang-sl|Frančiškanski družinski center}}) (later renamed to current '''Franciscan Family Institute''').<ref name="MDDSZ"/>

In two years, he finished his postdoctoral study and received [[doctorate of theology]].<ref name="TEOF"/> He then completed his third PhD study, this time from psychology at [[Faculty of Arts, Ljubljana]].<ref name="MDDSZ"/><ref name="TEOF"/> In 2004 he was elected to associate professor and in 2009 to full professor.<ref name="MDDSZ"/><ref name="UniLJ">{{Cite web | url=http://www.uni-lj.si/aktualno_na_univerzi/novice_po_kategorijah/tu2009/novi_redni_profesorji_2009.aspx | title = Novi redni profesorji 2009 | accessdate =23 January 2012 | publisher=University of Ljubljana|language=sl}}</ref>

During his academic career he "developed an innovative approach to marriage and family therapy called: [[Relational family therapy]]".<ref name="TEOF"/> In 2007 he was co-recipient of award for work on social services from Ministry of Work, Family and Social Affairs.<ref name="MDDSZ"/>

Between 2012 and 2016 he was dean of his [[alma mater]]: Faculty of Theology in Ljubljana.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.druzina.si/icd/spletnastran.nsf/all/E261CE7DE3732611C1257AAF0038D85E?OpenDocument | title= Nova številka Družine vabi k prebiranju | accessdate =23 January 2012 | publisher=Druzina.si|language=sl}}</ref>
