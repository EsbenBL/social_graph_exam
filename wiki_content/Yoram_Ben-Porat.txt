'''Yoram Ben-Porat''' (also, '''Ben-Porath'''; Hebrew: '''יורם בן-פורת'''; born 1937; died October 18, 1992) was an Israeli academic and economist. He served as president of the [[Hebrew University of Jerusalem]] from 1990 until his death in 1992 in an automobile accident at the age of 55.

==Biography==
Ben-Porat was born in [[Ramat Gan]], Israel.<ref name="auto2">{{Cite web|url=http://www.independent.co.uk/news/people/obituary-professor-yoram-ben-porath-1559687.html|title=Obituary: Professor Yoram Ben-Porath|date=October 26, 1992|website=The Independent}}</ref>

He was an economist.<ref name="auto2"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1984/11/03/business/israel-reaches-accord-to-help-cut-inflation.html|title=Israel Reaches Accord to Help Cut Inflation|first1=Thomas L.|last1=Friedman|date=November 3, 1984|work=The New York Times}}</ref> Ben-Porat specialized in the problems of the Israeli economy.<ref name="auto2"/><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://newspaperarchive.com/madison-wisconsin-state-journal-feb-03-1985-p-16/|title= Archives, p. 16|date=February 3, 1985|work=Madison Wisconsin State Journal}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=csMeAQAAMAAJ&q=%22Yoram+Ben-Porat%22+%22hebrew+university%22|title=The Israel Economist; Volume 43|date=January 22, 1987|publisher=Kollek.}}</ref>

Ben-Porat obtained both a bachelor's degree and a master's degrees at the [[Hebrew University of Jerusalem]].<ref name="auto2"/> He then obtained a Ph.D. at [[Harvard University]] in 1967, studying with [[Simon Kuznets]].<ref name="auto2"/><ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=EO40DAAAQBAJ&q=%22Yoram+Ben-Porath%22+hebrew+university&pg=PA460|title=The New Palgrave Dictionary of Economics|date=2016|publisher=Springer|isbn=9781349588022}}</ref>

He became a member of the Hebrew University of Jerusalem Department of Economics faculty in 1967, where he was the William Haber Professor of Economics.<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://www.jta.org/1992/10/22/archive/thousands-mourn-death-in-israel-of-hebrew-universitys-president|title=Thousands Mourn Death in Israel of Hebrew University's President|publisher=Jewish Telegraphic Agency|date=October 22, 1992}}</ref><ref name="auto2"/><ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=YHPaAAAAMAAJ&q=%22Yoram+Ben-Porat%22+%22hebrew+university%22|title=Communications and Political Attitudes in West Bank Refugee Camps|first=Shimon|last=Shamir|date=January 22, 1974|publisher=Tel Aviv University, Shiloah Center for Middle Eastern and African Studies}}</ref> In 1987, Ben-Porat became Rector of the university.<ref name="auto2"/> 

Ben-Porat was the president of the [[Hebrew University of Jerusalem]] from 1990 to 1992, following [[Amnon Pazy]] and succeeded by [[Hanoch Gutfreund]].<ref name="auto1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1992/10/19/obituaries/yoram-ben-porath-55-a-top-israeli-academic.html|title=Yoram Ben-Porath, 55; A Top Israeli Academic|date=October 19, 1992|work=The New York Times}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://new.huji.ac.il/en/page/454|title=Office of the President &#124; האוניברסיטה העברית בירושלים &#124; The Hebrew University of Jerusalem|website=new.huji.ac.il}}</ref>

He was also President of the Israel Economic Association, Director of the Maurice Falk Institute for Economic Research in Israel from 1979–84, and a consultant to the [[Rand Corporation]].<ref name="auto"/>

He died on October 18, 1992, in an automobile accident in [[Eilat]], Israel, at the age of 55, along with his 42-year-old wife Dr. Yael Cohen Ben-Porath (a lecturer in logic in the Hebrew University of Jerusalem Philosophy Department) and their five-year-old son, Yahali.<ref name="auto1"/><ref name="auto2"/><ref name="auto3">[https://www.washingtonpost.com/archive/politics/1992/10/31/in-israel-roads-are-deadly-but-is-anyone-paying-attention/776d7cc4-791d-4343-884b-afd2dc7a7f04/ "IN ISRAEL, ROADS ARE DEADLY, BUT IS ANYONE PAYING ATTENTION?"], ''The Washington Post'', October 31, 1992.</ref> They were buried at the [[Har Hamenuhot]] cemetery in Jerusalem.<ref name="auto"/>

He was succeeded as President of the university by [[Hanoch Gutfreund]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://apnews.com/f56c852db078b85c9c504e3dcf234f39|title=Hebrew University Names Physics Professor As President|date=June 7, 1993|website=AP News}}</ref>
