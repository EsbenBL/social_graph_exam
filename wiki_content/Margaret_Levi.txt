'''Margaret Levi''' (born 1947) is an [[United States|American]] [[political scientist]] and [[author]], noted for her work in [[Comparative politics|comparative]] [[political economy]], [[Trade union|labor politics]], and democratic theory, notably on the origins and effects of [[Trust (social sciences)|trustworthy]] [[government]].

==Education==
Margaret Levi earned her BA from Bryn Mawr College in 1968, in political science.<ref name="Kata">{{cite news |last1=Kata |first1=Anassa |title=Margaret Levi '68 |url=https://www.brynmawr.edu/bulletin/margaret-levi |access-date=14 October 2021 |work=Bryn Mawr Bulletin |issue=Spring |date=2019}}</ref> At Bryn Mawr, she was influenced by Alice Frey Emerson, [[Paul Brass]] and Peter Bachrach to pursue political science.<ref name=":0">{{Cite journal|last=Levi|first=Margaret|date=2022-05-11|title=The Power of Beliefs|url=https://www.annualreviews.org/doi/10.1146/annurev-polisci-051120-013517|journal=Annual Review of Political Science|language=en|volume=25|issue=1|pages=annurev–polisci–051120-013517|doi=10.1146/annurev-polisci-051120-013517|issn=1094-2939}}</ref> In 1967, she took a class at [[Swarthmore College]] alongside fellow students [[Peter J. Katzenstein|Peter Katzenstein]] and [[David D. Laitin|David Laitin]], who would both go on to become prominent political scientists.<ref>{{Citation|last=Prize|first=The Johan Skytte|title=2020/2021 Johan Skytte Prize Award Ceremony|date=2021-10-15|url=https://vimeo.com/632806575|access-date=2021-10-15}}</ref><ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-08-11|title=Swarthmore Meets Skytte: A Conversation|url=https://immigrationlab.org/2021/08/11/swarthmore-meets-skytte/|access-date=2021-11-16|website=Immigration Policy Lab|language=en-US}}</ref>

She began her PhD studies on urban and regional planning at the Harvard Graduate School of Design.<ref name=":0" /> However, she abandoned her studies before returning to Harvard University to do a PhD in political science.<ref name=":0" /> During her political science PhD studies, she was influenced by Michael Lipsky, Robert Fogelson and Edward Banfield.

She earned her PhD from Harvard University in 1974,<ref name="NAS">{{cite web |title=Margaret Levi |url=http://www.nasonline.org/member-directory/members/59618.html |website=National Academy of Sciences |access-date=14 October 2021}}</ref> 
the year she joined the faculty of the [[University of Washington]].<ref name="UW">{{cite web |title=Margaret Levi - New Leadership Role |url=https://www.polisci.washington.edu/news/2014/05/14/margaret-levi-new-leadership-role |website=Department of Political Science · University of Washington |access-date=14 October 2021|date=May 14, 2014}}</ref><ref name=":0" /> In her early work, she focused on urban politics.<ref name=":0" /> At the University of Washington, she co-taught classes with Douglass North for several years.<ref name=":0" />

==Career==
Levi is the Sara Miller McCune Director<ref name="Ethics">{{cite web |title=Margaret Levi, Co-Director |url=https://ethicsinsociety.stanford.edu/ethics-society-technology-hub/about/people/margaret-levi-co-director |website=Stanford University |access-date=14 October 2021}}</ref> of the [[Center for Advanced Study in the Behavioral Sciences]] (CASBS) at Stanford and Professor of Political Science, Stanford University. She succeeded [[Iris F. Litt]] as director as of March 31, 2014.<ref name="Rezendes">{{cite news |last1=Rezendes |first1=Diane |title=New Director Named at Stanford’s Center for Advanced Study in the Behavioral Sciences |url=https://watson.brown.edu/files/watson/imce/news/2013/Release-Levi%20to%20Direct%20CASBS.pdf |access-date=15 October 2021 |work=Center for Advanced Study in the Behavioral Sciences News |date=December 12, 2013}}</ref> She is the Jere L. Bacharach Professor Emerita of International Studies in the Department of Political Science of the University of Washington.<ref name="Kata"/>

Levi was a Senior Fellow at the [[Watson Institute for International Studies]], [[Brown University]] for 2013-14.<ref name="AAPSS"/> She held the Chair in Politics of [[United States Studies Centre]] at the [[University of Sydney]] from 2009–13.<ref>{{cite web |title=US Studies Centre appoints leading academics |date=27 October 2008 |url=https://www.ussc.edu.au/analysis/us-studies-centre-appoints-leading-academics |website=United States Studies Centre h |access-date=15 October 2021}}</ref> At the [[University of Washington]] she was director of the CHAOS (Comparative Historical Analysis of Organizations and States) Center.  She previously served as the [[Harry Bridges]] Chair and Director of the Harry Bridges Center for Labor Studies at the University of Washington.<ref name="papers">{{cite web |title=John Ahlquist and Margaret Levi research materials for the book In the Interest of Others : Organizations and Social Activism, 2006-2012 |url=http://archiveswest.orbiscascade.org/ark:/80444/xv64431 |website=University of Washington Libraries, Special Collections |access-date=15 October 2021}}</ref>

Levi's book ''Of Rule and Revenue'' (1988), a study of the institutions of state [[Taxation|revenue]] production, helped pioneer [[Rational choice theory|rational choice]] approaches in [[comparative politics]]. She has since "pushed rational choice analysis into new substantive areas", for example, in examining people's acceptance of military [[conscription]] in ''Consent, Dissent, and Patriotism'' (1997).<ref name="Hanson"/>{{rp|897}}

She is also the co-author of ''[[Analytic narrative|Analytic Narratives]]'' (Princeton University Press, 1998)<ref name="PNAS">{{cite web |title=About the PNAS Member Editor |url=https://nrc88.nas.edu/pnas_search/memberDetails.aspx?ctID=59618 |website=Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences of the United States of America |access-date=15 October 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal |last1=Elster |first1=Jon |title=Rational Choice History: A Case of Excessive Ambition - Analytic Narratives. ''Cooperation Without Trust?'' (Russell Sage, 2005),<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Molm |first1=Linda D. |title=Cooperation without Trust? |journal=Administrative Science Quarterly |date=June 2006 |volume=51 |issue=2 |pages=305–307 |doi=10.2189/asqu.51.2.305 |url=https://doi.org/10.2189/asqu.51.2.305 |access-date=15 October 2021 |language=en |issn=0001-8392}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal |last1=Macy |first1=M. |title=Cooperation Without Trust? and  ''Labor Standards in International Supply Chains'' (Edward Elgar, 2015).<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Lauriano |first1=Lucas Amaral |title=Book Review: Labor Standards in International Supply Chains: Aligning Rights and Incentives . By Daniel Berliner, Anne Regan Greenleaf, Milli Lake, Margaret Levi, and Jennifer Noveck |journal=ILR Review |date=August 2020 |volume=73 |issue=4 |pages=1024–1025 |doi=10.1177/0019793920912924 |url=https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=3585179 |access-date=15 October 2021}}</ref> ''In the Interest of Others'' (Princeton, 2013), co-authored with John Ahlquist, explores how organizations provoke member willingness to act beyond material interest.<ref name="Scherer">{{cite journal |last1=Scherer |first1=Jennifer |title=Book Review: In the Interest of Others: Organizations and Social Activism, by John S. In other work, Levi investigates the conditions under which people come to believe their governments are legitimate and the consequences of those beliefs for compliance, consent, and the rule of law. Her research continues to focus on how to improve the quality of government.<ref name="Knight">{{cite book |last1=Knight |first1=Jack |last2=Schwartzberg |first2=Melissa |title=Political Legitimacy: NOMOS LXI |date=6 August 2019 |publisher=NYU Press |isbn=978-1-4798-7142-1 |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=3PZ0DwAAQBAJ&pg=PT17 |access-date=15 October 2021 |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal |last1=Levi |first1=Margaret |last2=Sacks |first2=Audrey |last3=Tyler |first3=Tom |title=Conceptualizing Legitimacy, Measuring Legitimating Beliefs |journal=American Behavioral Scientist |date=1 November 2009 |volume=53 |issue=3 |pages=354–375 |doi=10.1177/0002764209338797 |url=https://doi.org/10.1177/0002764209338797 |access-date=15 October 2021 |language=en |issn=0002-7642}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal |last1=Levi |first1=Margaret |last2=Sacks |first2=Audrey |title=Legitimating beliefs: Sources and indicators |journal=Regulation & Governance |date=2009 |volume=3 |issue=4 |pages=311–333 |doi=10.1111/j.1748-5991.2009.01066.x |url=https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1748-5991.2009.01066.x |access-date=15 October 2021 |language=en |issn=1748-5991}}</ref> She is also committed to understanding and improving supply chains so that the goods we consume are produced in a manner that sustains both the workers and the environment.<ref name="Berliner">{{cite journal |last1=Berliner |first1=Daniel |last2=Greenleaf |first2=Anne Regan |last3=Lake |first3=Milli |last4=Levi |first4=Margaret |last5=Noveck |first5=Jennifer |title=Governing Global Supply Chains: What We Know (and Don't) About Improving Labor Rights and Working Conditions |journal=Annual Review of Law and Social Science |date=3 November 2015 |volume=11 |issue=1 |pages=193–209 |doi=10.1146/annurev-lawsocsci-120814-121322 |url=https://doi.org/10.1146/annurev-lawsocsci-120814-121322 |access-date=15 October 2021 |issn=1550-3585}}</ref>

Levi started The Brand Responsibility Project—a research project to document the campaign and dispute settlement between [[Nike, Inc.]] and the [[General Workers Central|Central General de Trabajadores of Honduras (CGT)]]. CGT claimed that Nike was responsible for providing terminal compensation, benefits and priority rehiring for 1,800 factory employees following the 2009 bankruptcy and closure of two Honduran factories (Hugger and VisionTex) that were part of Nike's supply chain.<ref>{{cite web |title=Brand Responsibility Project records, 2004-2012 |url=http://archiveswest.orbiscascade.org/ark:/80444/xv62799 |website=University of Washington Libraries, Special Collections |access-date=15 October 2021}}</ref>

{{blockquote|"Margaret Levi's research program addresses fundamental issues concerning the bases for and effects of legitimacy, compliance, and consent in democratic regimes. Levi's scholarship has made pioneering contributions to understanding enduring questions about the conditions for and consequences of trust and distrust, compliance and resistance, and individual versus collective action."<ref name="Hanson">{{cite journal |last1=Hanson |first1=Stephen E. |last2=Jupille |first2=Joseph |last3=Olson |first3=David J. |last4=Weingast |first4=Barry R. |title=Margaret Levi: Institutions, Individuals, Organizations, and Trust in Democratic Regimes |journal=PS: Political Science & Politics |date=2004 |volume=37 |issue=4 |pages=895–898 |doi=10.1017/S1049096504045391 |url=https://doi.org/10.1017/S1049096504045391 |access-date=14 October 2021}}</ref> }}

Levi was general editor of the series Cambridge Studies in Comparative Politics.<ref name="Stanford">{{cite web |title=Margaret Levi |url=https://politicalscience.stanford.edu/people/margaret-levi |website=Stanford |access-date=15 October 2021}}</ref> She is a member editor of the ''[[Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences of the United States of America]]'' (PNAS)<ref name="PNAS"/> and  co-[[editor-in-chief|editor]] of the ''[[Annual Review of Political Science]]''.<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Levi |first1=Margaret |title=Nelson W. Polsby (1934–2007) |journal=Annual Review of Political Science |date=1 June 2007 |volume=10 |issue=1 |doi=10.1146/annurev.pl.10.041807.100001 |url=https://www.annualreviews.org/doi/10.1146/annurev.pl.10.041807.100001 |access-date=14 October 2021 |issn=1094-2939}}</ref> 
Levi serves on the boards of the: [[Social Science Research Council]] (SSRC);<ref>{{cite web |title=SOCIAL SCIENCE RESEARCH COUNCIL |url=https://nonprofitlight.com/ny/brooklyn/social-science-research-council |website=Non Profit Light |access-date=15 October 2021}}</ref> 
[[Institute for Advanced Study]] in Princeton;<ref name="Altman">{{cite news |last1=Altman |first1=Alexandra |title=Institute for Advanced Study Appoints Two New Trustees |url=https://www.ias.edu/press-releases/institute-advanced-study-appoints-two-new-trustees-0 |access-date=15 October 2021 |work=Institute for Advanced Study |date=June 10, 2014}}</ref> Center for Advanced Studies in the Social Sciences (CEACS) in Madrid; Scholar and Research Group of the [[World Justice Project]],<ref>{{cite web |title=Announcing the WJP Rule of Law Research Consortium  |date=May 7, 2014 |url=https://worldjusticeproject.org/news/announcing-wjp-rule-law-research-consortium |website=World Justice Project |access-date=15 October 2021}}</ref> and the [[Berggruen Institute]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Margaret Levi |url=https://www.berggruen.org/people/margaret-levi/ |website=Berggruen Institute |date=2018 |access-date=15 October 2021}}</ref>

Her fellowships include the Woodrow Wilson in 1968, German Marshall in 1988–1989, and the Center for Advanced Study of the Behavioral Sciences in 1993–1994. She has been a visiting fellow at the [[Australian National University]], [[Brown University]], the [[European University Institute]], the [[Max Planck Institute for the Study of Societies]] in Cologne, the [[Juan March institute]], the Budapest Collegium, [[Cardiff University]], the [[University of Oxford]], [[Bergen University]], and [[Peking University]].<ref name="Stanford"/>

==Awards and honors==
She became a fellow of the [[American Academy of Arts and Sciences]] in 2001, a [[John Simon Guggenheim Fellow]] in 2002, and a member of the [[National Academy of Sciences]] in 2015. She served as president of the [[American Political Science Association]] from 2004 to 2005. In 2014 she received the [[William H. Riker]] Prize in Political Science.  She was elected as a Fellow of the [[American Academy of Political and Social Science]] as of 2017.<ref name="AAPSS">{{cite news |title=Former APSA President, Margaret Levi, Elected Fellow of the AAPSS |url=https://politicalsciencenow.com/former-apsa-president-margaret-levi-elected-fellow-of-the-aapss/ |access-date=14 October 2021 |work=Political Science Now |date=January 20, 2017}}</ref>  She was elected to the [[American Philosophical Society]] in 2018.<ref name="APS">{{Cite web|url=https://www.amphilsoc.org/blog/election-new-members-2018-spring-meeting|title=Election of New Members at the 2018 Spring Meeting|website=American Philosophical Society|accessdate=Apr 8, 2021}}</ref> She is the 2019 winner of the [[Johan Skytte Prize in Political Science|Johan Skytte Prize]].<ref name="Kata"/>

==Personal life==
Levi and her husband, attorney Robert Kaplan, are avid collectors of Australian Aboriginal art,<ref name="NAS"/><ref name="Davila">{{cite news |last1=Davila |first1=Florangela |title=At the Seattle Art Museum: Australian Aboriginal art |url=https://www.knkx.org/artscape/2012-07-02/at-the-seattle-art-museum-australian-aboriginal-art |access-date=15 October 2021 |work=KNKX Public Radio |date=July 2, 2012}}</ref> ''Ancestral Modern'', an exhibition drawn from their collection, was on view at the [[Seattle Art Museum]] (SAM) in 2012.<ref name="McClusky">{{cite web |last1=McClusky |first1=Pam |title=Ancestral Modern: Australian Aboriginal Art from the Kaplan & Levi Collection |url=https://www.seattleartmuseum.org/Pages/Australian-Aboriginal-Art-from-the-Kaplan--Levi-Collection.aspx |website=Seattle Art Museum |access-date=14 October 2021}}</ref><ref>{{cite book |last1=McClusky |first1=Pamela |last2=Caruana |first2=Wally |last3=Corrin |first3=Lisa Graziose |last4=Gilchrist |first4=Stephen |title=Ancestral Modern: Australian Aboriginal Art |date=2012 |publisher=Seattle Art Museum |location=Seattle, WA |url=https://seattleartmuseum.org/Documents/Ancestral-Modern-Exhibition-Catalogue.pdf}}</ref>
It afterward travelled to the [[Frist Art Museum|Frist Center for the Arts]] in [[Nashville]], the [[Chazen Museum of Art]] in [[Madison, Wisconsin|Madison]], the [[Blanton Museum of Art]] in [[Austin]], and the [[Audain Art Museum]] in [[Whistler, British Columbia|Whistler]].<ref name="SAMBlog">{{cite web |last1=McClusky |first1=Pam |title=Welcome Home, Ancestral Modern!   |date=May 22, 2019 |url=https://samblog.seattleartmuseum.org/2019/05/ancestral-modern/ |website=SAMBlog |access-date=14 October 2021}}</ref>
