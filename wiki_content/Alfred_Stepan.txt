'''Alfred C. Stepan''' (July 22, 1936 – September 27, 2017)<ref>{{Cite web|title=In Memory of Professor and Former SIPA Dean Alfred C. Stepan {{!}} Columbia SIPA|url=https://www.sipa.columbia.edu/news/memory-professor-and-former-sipa-dean-alfred-c-stepan|access-date=2021-05-31|website=www.sipa.columbia.edu}}</ref> was an American [[political scientist]] specializing in [[comparative politics]]. He was the Wallace S. Sayre [[Professor]] of [[Political science|Government]] at [[Columbia University]], where he was also director of the Center for the Study of Democracy, Toleration and Religion. He is known for his comparative politics research on the military, state institutions, democratization, and democracy.<ref name=":0">{{Cite book|last1=Munck|first1=Gerardo L.|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=itGOAAAAMAAJ|title=Passion, Craft, and Method in Comparative Politics|last2=Snyder|first2=Richard|date=2007|publisher=Johns Hopkins University Press|isbn=978-0-8018-8464-1|pages=392–393|language=en}}</ref>

== Life and Academic Career ==
Stepan was born in Chicago, Illinois in 1936.<ref name=":0" /> He graduated from the [[University of Notre Dame]] with a [[B.A.]] in 1958. He gained his [[PhD]] from [[Columbia University]] in 1969.<ref name=":0" /> 

He subsequently taught at [[Yale University]], before being appointed [[Dean (education)|Dean]] of the School of International and Public Affairs at Columbia in 1983. He became the first [[Rector (academia)|Rector]] of [[Central European University]] (CEU) in 1993,<ref>Stepan discusses his experience as Rector of the CEU in Alfred Stepan, "The Early Years of Central European University as a Network: A Memoir," ''Social Research'' 76, 2(2009): 687-710.</ref> and in 1996 was appointed [[Gladstone Professor of Government]] at [[All Souls College, Oxford|All Souls College]], [[Oxford University]]. He returned to [[Columbia University]] in 1999.<ref name=CV>{{cite web|url=http://www.columbia.edu/~as48/|title=Alfred C. Stepan|publisher=Columbia University|accessdate=17 May 2008|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080319103834/http://www.columbia.edu/~as48/|archive-date=19 March 2008|url-status=dead}}</ref> He has been a [[Fellow]] of the [[American Academy of Arts and Sciences]] since 1991 and a member of the [[British Academy]] since 1997.<ref name=CV/> 

According to the editors of a collection of essays published in honour of Stepan, he was one of the few academics to be a member of both national social science academies.<ref name=Chalmers>{{cite book|editor-first1=Douglas|editor-last1=Chalmers|editor-first2=Scott|editor-last2=Mainwaring|chapter=Preface and Acknowledgements|title=Problems Confronting Contemporary Democracies: Essays in Honor of Alfred Stepan|year=2012|pages=ix-xii|publisher=University of Notre Dame Press|location=Notre Dame, Indiana|url=http://www3.nd.edu/~undpress/excerpts/P01520-ex.pdf}}</ref> 
Stepan is a member of the [[National Endowment for Democracy]]'s International Forum for Democratic Studies Research Council.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ned.org/experts/alfred-stepan/|title=Alfred Stepan|accessdate=20 November 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20151120220349/http://www.ned.org/experts/alfred-stepan/|archive-date=20 November 2015|url-status=dead}}</ref>

== Awards ==

in 1991, Stepan was elected to the American Academy of Arts and Sciences.<ref>Gerardo L. Munck and Richard Snyder, "Alfred Stepan: Democratic Governance and the Craft of Case-Based Research,” pp. 392-455, in Gerardo L. Munck and Richard Snyder, ''Passion, Craft, and Method in Comparative Politics''. Baltimore, Md.: The Johns Hopkins University Press, 2007, p. 393.</ref>

In 2002, Stepan was awarded the [[Order of Rio Branco]] by the Brazilian government. He has also been awarded the Kalman Silvert Award for his lifetime contribution to Latin American studies. 

In 2007, he was included on a list of the 400 most highly cited US-based political scientists.<ref name=Chalmers/> 

In 2012, he was awarded the [[Karl Deutsch]] Award by the [[International Political Science Association]]. The award is intended "to honour a prominent scholar engaged in the cross-disciplinary research of which Karl Deutsch was a master".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.ipsa.org/awards/karl-deutsch|title=Awards - Karl Deutsch Award|publisher=International Political Science Association|accessdate=20 November 2015}}</ref>

== Academic Research ==
He authored and edited a number of books, including ''Arguing Comparative Politics'' (Oxford University Press, 2001), ''Crafting State-Nations. India and Other Multinational Democracies'' (co-authored with [[Juan Linz]] and [[Yogendra Yadav]]; 2011), ''Problems of Democratic Transition and Consolidation: Southern Europe, South America, and Post Communist Europe'' (with [[Juan Linz]]; 1996) and ''Democracies in Danger'' (2009).<ref>Gerardo L. Munck and Richard Snyder, "Alfred Stepan: Democratic Governance and the Craft of Case-Based Research,” pp. 392-455, in Gerardo L. Munck and Richard Snyder, ''Passion, Craft, and Method in Comparative Politics''. Baltimore, Md.: The Johns Hopkins University Press, 2007, p. 392-93.</ref>

===''The Military in Politics''===

The ''The Military in Politics'',<ref>Alfred Stepan, ''The Military in Politics. Changing Patterns in Brazil''. Princeton, NJ: Princeton University Press, 1971.</ref> Stepan analyzes the breakdown of democracy in Brazil in 1964. Stepan "challenged the prevailing view in comparative research that saw the military as a force for achieving modernization and national integration." He also showed that "understanding the military’s political behavior ... required a focus on the broader context of civil-military relations."<ref>Gerardo L. Munck and Richard Snyder, "Alfred Stepan: Democratic Governance and the Craft of Case-Based Research,” pp. 392-455, in Gerardo L. Munck and Richard Snyder, ''Passion, Craft, and Method in Comparative Politics''. Baltimore, Md.: The Johns Hopkins University Press, 2007, p. 392.</ref>

===''The State and Society. Peru in Comparative Perspective''===
In this book,<ref>Alfred Stepan, ''The State and Society. Peru in Comparative Perspective''. Princeton, NJ: Princeton University Press, 1978.</ref> Stepan "critiqued both pluralist and Marxist approaches for focusing insufficient attention on state elites." This work defined Stepan "as an early and key contributor to the ‘‘new institutional’’ research that emphasized the potential autonomy of political institutions from economic, societal, and cultural forces."<ref>Gerardo L. Munck and Richard Snyder, "Alfred Stepan: Democratic Governance and the Craft of Case-Based Research,” pp. 392-455, in Gerardo L. Munck and Richard Snyder, ''Passion, Craft, and Method in Comparative Politics''. Baltimore, Md.: The Johns Hopkins University Press, 2007, p. 392-93.</ref>

===''The Breakdown of Democratic Regimes''===
''The Breakdown of Democratic Regimes'' is a four volume coedited work with [[Juan José Linz|Juan Linz]].<ref>Juan Linz and Alfred Stepan (eds.), ''The Breakdown of Democratic Regimes.'' Baltimore, Md.: The Johns Hopkins University Press, 1978.</ref> It emphasizes "how the actions of democratically elected incumbents contributed to the demise of democracy."<ref>Gerardo L. Munck and Richard Snyder, "Juan J. Linz: Political Regimes and the Quest for Knowledge,” pp. 150-209, in Gerardo L. Munck and Richard Snyder, ''Passion, Craft, and Method in Comparative Politics''. Baltimore, Md.: The Johns Hopkins University Press, 2007, p. 392.</ref>

===''Rethinking Military Politics. Brazil and the Southern Cone''===

In ''Rethinking Military Politics'',<ref>Alfred Stepan, ''Rethinking Military Politics. Brazil and the Southern Cone''. Princeton, NJ: Princeton University Press, 1988.</ref> Stepan "argued that to achieve stable democracy, civilian elites in newly democratic regimes had to find ways to handle the problem of establishing civilian control over the armed forces."<ref>Gerardo L. Munck and Richard Snyder, "Alfred Stepan: Democratic Governance and the Craft of Case-Based Research,” pp. 392-455, in Gerardo L. Munck and Richard Snyder, ''Passion, Craft, and Method in Comparative Politics''. Baltimore, Md.: The Johns Hopkins University Press, 2007, p. 393.</ref>

===''Problems of Democratic Transition and Consolidation''===
This work on [[democratization]], with [[Juan José Linz|Juan Linz]], provides a cross-regional comparison of thirteen countries in South America, Southern Europe, and postcommunist Europe. It introducing a novel focus on ‘‘stateness’’ problems stemming from nationalist conflicts. It also argues the type of old non-democratic regime affected subsequent trajectories of democratization.<ref>Gerardo L. Munck and Richard Snyder, Juan J. Linz: Political Regimes and the Quest for Knowledge,” pp. 150-209, in Gerardo L. Munck and Richard Snyder, ''Passion, Craft, and Method in Comparative Politics''. Baltimore, Md.: The Johns Hopkins University Press, 2007, p 151.</ref>
