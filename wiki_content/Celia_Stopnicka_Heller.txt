'''Celia Heller''' (20 November 1922 â€“ 15 April 2011)  was an [[Sociology|American sociologist]]. She was born in [[Poland]].

==Biography==
After graduating from [[Brooklyn College]], in 1950, she entered a graduate program in sociology at [[Columbia University]], earning a Masters in 1952, and a Ph.D. in 1962.<ref name="ContemporaryAuthors">"Celia S(topnicka) Heller." ''Contemporary Authors Online''. Detroit: Gale, 2002. Retrieved from ''Biography in Context'' database.</ref> Subsequently, she taught at [[Hunter College]], beginning as an assistant professor, in 1964.<ref name="ContemporaryAuthors"/> She became a full professor there in 1972, and retired with emeritus status in 1984. In the course of her career at Hunter College she also taught at the [[Graduate Center, CUNY]]. From 1970 to 1971 she was a visiting professor at [[Tel Aviv University]] and [[Bar-Ilan University|Bar Ilan University]].<ref name="ContemporaryAuthors"/>

Her first book was ''Mexican American Youth: Forgotten Youth at the Crossroads'' ([[Random House]], 1966).<ref>{{Cite book |title=Mexican American Youth: Forgotten Youth at the Crossroads |publisher=Random House |year=1966 |via=[[Internet Archive]] |url=https://archive.org/details/mexicanamericany0000hell |url-access=registration |quote=Celia Stopnicka Heller. |last1=Heller |first1=Celia Stopnicka }}</ref>  This was researched while she was at [[UCLA]] and represents the first of three phases of her writing.

The second phase of Heller's writing is represented by ''Structured social inequality: a reader in comparative social stratification'' (Macmillan, 1968),<ref>{{cite book | url=https://archive.org/details/structuredsocial0000unse | url-access=registration |title = Structured social inequality: A reader in comparative social stratification| publisher=Macmillan |last1 = Heller|first1 = Celia Stopnicka|year = 1968}}</ref> which a collection of articles some of which she wrote.

Her last phase of writing includes ''On the Edge of Destruction: Jews in Poland Between the Two World Wars'' ([[Columbia University Press]] 1977). The book is dedicated to her grandfather, the Rabbi Shaul ben Yitzhak Yosef ha-Kohen Rosenman.{{fact|date=April 2020}} It won a [[National Jewish Book Award]] in the Jewish History category.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.jewishbookcouncil.org/awards/national-jewish-book-awards/past-winners?category=30762|title=Past Winners|last=|first=|date=|website=Jewish Book Council|language=en|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-01-23}}</ref>

She served as president of the [[Association for the Social Scientific Study of Jewry]] 1977-1979.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://hirr.hartsem.edu/ency/heller.htm |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2012-09-22 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://archive.is/20121212030416/http://hirr.hartsem.edu/ency/heller.htm |archivedate=2012-12-12 }}</ref>

According to the late Irving Piliavin, Professor and Director Emeritus at the University of Wisconsin School of Social Welfare, as Celia Stopnicka Rosenthal did, she wrote a [[Mimeograph|mimeographed manuscript]], ''Toward the Conceptualization of 'Needs''', about which there is no further information.<ref>Council on Social Work Education., Mendes, R. H. P., Piliavin, I., Romanyshyn, J. M., & Bisno, H. (1963). Social welfare as a social institution; illustrative syllabi for the basic course in undergraduate social welfare. New York: Council on Social Work Education.</ref>

She published twice in the ''[[American Journal of Sociology]]'' and once in the ''[[British Journal of Sociology]]''.<ref>Rosenthal, C. S. (1955). Functions of Polish trade unions: their progression toward the Soviet pattern. British Journal of Sociology, 6, 264-276. doi: 10.2307/586951; Rosenthal, C. S. (1953). Social stratification of the Jewish community in a small Polish town. American Journal of Sociology, 59, 1-10; Rosenthal, C. S. (1954). Deviation and social change in the Jewish community of a small Polish town. American Journal of Sociology, 60, 177-181.</ref>
