'''Pierangelo Garegnani''' (1930–2011) was an [[Italy|Italian]] [[economist]] and [[professor]] of the [[Roma Tre University|University of Rome III]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://host.uniroma3.it/dipartimenti/economia/it/index.asp?se=Personale&id=1012&incd=docenti|title=Docenti e Ricercatori: Pierangelo Garegnani|publisher=Università degli Studi Roma Tre|language=it|accessdate=28 June 2010}} {{Dead link|date=April 2019}}</ref> He was the Director of the Fondazione Centro Piero Sraffa di Studi e Documenti at the Federico Caffè School of Economics, and also the [[literary executor]] of the works, documents and papers left by the Italian economist [[Piero Sraffa]] to the [[University of Cambridge|University of Cambridge's]] [[Wren Library, Cambridge|Wren Library]].

Professor Garegnani was one of the leading theoretical critics of [[neoclassical economics]]. He published several books and articles concerning the classical economic theory, from [[David Ricardo|Ricardo]] to Sraffa, as an alternative theoretical foundation to analyse the [[capitalist economy]]. An account of his contributions was published by the [[Royal Economic Society]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Pierangelo Garegnani|accessdate=16 March 2011|publisher=Royal Economic Society|url=http://www.res.org.uk/view/obituariesGaregnani.html}}</ref> During the 1980s, Garegnani worked as a visiting professor at [[The New School]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Pierangelo Garegnani, 1930–2011|url=https://www.hetwebsite.net/het/profiles/garegnani.htm}}</ref>
