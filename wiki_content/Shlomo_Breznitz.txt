'''Shlomo Breznitz''' ({{lang-he|שלמה ברזניץ}}, born 3 August 1936) is an [[Israel]]i author, psychologist, former professor of psychology, former [[Rector (academia)|rector]] and president of the [[University of Haifa]],<ref>[https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2007/09/10/AR2007091001853_2.html Cross-Training Your Brain to Maintain Its Strength] Washington Post, 12 September 2007</ref> and previous member of the [[Knesset]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://knesset.gov.il/mk/eng/mk_eng.asp?mk_individual_id_t=769 |title=Shlomo Breznitz Profile Page |author=<!--Not stated--> |website=Knesset |access-date=September 23, 2020}}</ref> He is the founder and currently one of the members of the board of directors<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.cognifit.com/about-team |title=Management Team |author=<!--Not stated--> |website=CogniFit |access-date=September 23, 2020}}</ref> of CogniFit, a [[brain fitness]] software company.

==Early life==
Breznitz was born in [[Bratislava]] in [[Czechoslovakia]] (today [[Slovakia]]). During [[the Holocaust]] he and his sister were hidden in a [[Roman Catholicism|Roman Catholic]] orphanage,<ref>{{cite news |last=Reardon |first=Patrick T. |date=January 8, 1993 |title=A Holocaust Survivor Faces The Pain, The Questions |url=https://webcache.googleusercontent.com/search?q=cache:8yr2CIz0qP8J:https://www.chicagotribune.com/news/ct-xpm-1993-01-08-9303151659-story.html+&cd=1&hl=en&ct=clnk&gl=us |work=Chicago Tribune |access-date=September 23, 2020}}</ref> an experience detailed in his memoirs, "Memory Fields".<ref>{{cite book |last=Breznitz |first=Shlomo |date=December 12, 1992 |title=Memory Fields |url=https://www.publishersweekly.com/978-0-679-40403-3 |location= New York |publisher=Alfred A. Knopf |page=179 |isbn=978-0-679-40403-3}}</ref> His father was killed in [[Auschwitz]],<ref>[http://www.secretlives.org/home/film/shlomo.html Hidden Children: Shlomo Breznitz and his sister, Yehudit Karban, Czechoslovakia] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081121192340/http://www.secretlives.org/home/film/shlomo.html |date=2008-11-21 }} Secret Lives</ref> but his mother survived and they made [[aliyah]] to Israel in 1949. He studied [[psychology]] at the [[Hebrew University of Jerusalem]], gaining a BA in 1960, an MA in 1962 and a PhD in 1965, the first person to receive a doctorate in the new field of psychology in Israel.

==Later career==
From 1969 until 1971, Breznitz served as a consultant to the [[Israeli Air Force]] on problems of stress. He was the founding director of the Ray D. Wolfe Center for Study of Psychological Stress at the [[University of Haifa]] in 1979. Breznitz also has served as the Lady Davis Professor of Psychology and was the visiting professor at the [[London School of Economics]], [[University of California, Berkeley|Berkeley]], [[Stanford University|Stanford]], National Institutes of Health and [[Rockefeller University]]. He has also been a visiting scientist at the National Institute of Mental Health in [[Washington D.C.]].

He has written eleven books and has contributed chapters to over 20 other books in addition to numerous professional articles and research reports.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.tnis.eu/files/documents/shlomo_breznitz_curriculum%20vitae_bibliography%20books_professional%20articles_and%20research%20reports.pdf |title=Shlomo Breznitz’s Curriculum Vitae |author=<!--Not stated--> |website=TNIS-Club General M.R. Stefanik |access-date=September 23, 2020}}</ref> In 1999 he retired from Haifa University to found brain fitness software company, CogniFit. He has been in the forefront of cognitive training using a personal computer and has developed patented technology that turns the personal computer into a tool for providing individualized training programs for a wide range of cognitive skills needed for everyday function and cognitive skills specific to particular fields of interest.

==Politics==
After [[Ehud Olmert]], a personal friend, convinced him to enter politics, Breznitz was elected to the [[Knesset]] on the [[Kadima]] list in 2006,<ref>{{cite web |url=http://knesset.gov.il/mk/eng/mk_eng.asp?mk_individual_id_t=769 |title=Shlomo Breznitz Knesset Actitivities |author=<!--Not stated--> |website=Knesset |access-date=September 23, 2020}}</ref> the first [[Slovak people|Slovak]] to become an MK.<ref>[http://www.spectator.sk/articles/view/27319/2/ 'We need friends'] The Slovak Spectator, 9 April 2007</ref> He was the founder of the India-Israel Parliamentary Friendship Group in the Knesset.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/apps/news?pid=newsarchive&sid=a12JnKt1Pwlc |title=Politics |publisher=Bloomberg |date=2019-02-07 |accessdate=2020-02-19}}</ref> However, he retired from politics and left the Knesset on September 28, 2007.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://knesset.gov.il/mk/eng/mk_eng.asp?mk_individual_id_t=769 |title=Shlomo Breznitz Knesset Actitivities |author=<!--Not stated--> |website=Knesset |access-date=September 23, 2020}}</ref>
