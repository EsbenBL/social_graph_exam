'''Thomas Diez''' (born 2 February 1970) is a German professor of Political Science and International Relations at the Institute for Political Science, [[University of Tübingen]].<ref name="CV">{{cite web|url=https://uni-tuebingen.de/uploads/media/CVWeb0907.pdf|title=Thomas Diez Curriculum Vitae|date=July 2009|publisher=University of Tübingen|accessdate=7 September 2009|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110607004708/http://www.uni-tuebingen.de/uploads/media/CVWeb0907.pdf|archivedate=7 June 2011}}</ref> He was formerly Professor of International Relations Theory in the Department of Political Science and International Studies at the [[University of Birmingham]], where he was Head of Department from 2005-2008. Diez earned his PhD at the [[University of Mannheim]].<ref name="profile">{{cite web|url=http://www.cceia.org/people/data/thomas_diez.html|title=Thomas Diez|publisher=Carnegie Council for Ethics in International Affairs|date=2007-12-19|accessdate=7 September 2009}}</ref> He was formerly a Research Fellow at the [[Copenhagen Peace Research Institute]] where he worked with [[Barry Buzan]] and [[Ole Waever]]. He studies [[international relations theory]], [[European integration]] and [[conflict transformation]] and is best known for his contributions to the debate on the [[European Union]]'s [[normative]] [[Power in international relations|power]]. Books he has edited or co-edited include ''The EU and the Cyprus Conflict: Modern Conflict, Postmodern Union'' (Manchester University Press, 2002), ''European Integration Theory'' (Oxford University Press, 2004, 2009) and ''The European Union and Border Conflicts'' (Cambridge University Press, 2008), ''Cyprus: A Conflict at the Crossroads'' (Manchester University Press, 2009) and ''An Introduction to International Relations Theory: Perspectives and Themes'' (Pearson, 2010).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wiso.uni-tuebingen.de/faecher/ifp/lehrende/internationale-beziehungen-friedens-und-konfliktforschung-professuren-diez-und-hasenclever/mitarbeiterinnen/professor-dr-thomas-diez/publikationen.html|title=Publikationen|publisher=University of Tübingen|accessdate=18 November 2010|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110719111450/http://www.wiso.uni-tuebingen.de/faecher/ifp/lehrende/internationale-beziehungen-friedens-und-konfliktforschung-professuren-diez-und-hasenclever/mitarbeiterinnen/professor-dr-thomas-diez/publikationen.html|archivedate=19 July 2011}}</ref>

25 September 2009 Diez was awarded the 2009 [[Anna Lindh]] Award for contributions to the analysis of European foreign and security policy.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://uni-tuebingen.de/en/news/pressemitteilungen/archiv/archivfullview-pressemitteilungen/browse/2/select_category/1/article/anna-lindh-preis-fuer-politikwissenschaftler-thomas-diez/1449.html|title=Anna Lindh Preis|publisher=University of Tübingen|language=German|accessdate=18 November 2010}}</ref>
