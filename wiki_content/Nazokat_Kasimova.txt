'''Nazokat Anvarovna Kasimova''' ({{lang-uz|'''Nazokat Anvarovna Qosimova'''}}) (born 1969) is an [[Uzbekistan]]i political scientist, also noted for her work within the field of [[higher education]] reform.<ref name="archive1">{{cite web |url=http://www.tempus.uz/index_ru.php?tnth |title=Tempus in Uzbekistan |date=2016-01-27 |access-date=2017-11-12 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160127104958/http://www.tempus.uz/index_ru.php?tnth |archive-date=2016-01-27 }}</ref>

Kasimova was born in [[Tashkent]], and is the daughter of noted Indologist and Turkologist [[Anvar Kasimov]]; she has one sister, Mumtoz, and two brothers, Mahmod and Muzaffar. Their grandfather was the military leader [[Mahmud Kasimov]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://libmonster.ru/m/person/view/%D0%9A%D0%9E%D0%A1%D0%98%D0%9C%D0%9E%D0%92-%D0%9C%D0%B0%D1%85%D0%BC%D1%83%D0%B4-%D0%A2%D0%BE%D0%B6%D0%B8%D0%BA%D1%83%D0%BB%D0%BE%D0%B2%D0%B8%D1%87 |title=КОСИМОВ Махмуд Тожикулович |publisher=Libmonster.ru |date= |access-date=2017-11-12}}</ref> She graduated from the faculty of history of [[Tashkent State University]] in 1991. She attended graduate school at the [[University of Washington]] from 1993 to 1994, and in 1995, at the [[University of World Economy and Diplomacy]], she defended her thesis, on the subject of diplomatic relations between the [[United States]], [[Nazi Germany]], the [[United Kingdom]], and the [[Soviet Union]] in the buildup to [[World War II]]; she received her doctoral degree from that institution in 2002. She has worked at the [[Woodrow Wilson International Center for Scholars]], in 2000, and at [[Tsukuba University]], in 2008.<ref name="ceasia1">{{cite web |url=http://ceasia.ru/uzbekistan/kasimova-nazokat-anvarovna.html |title=Касымова Назокат Анваровна - Центральная Евразия - Авторский проект Владимира Парамонова. Информационные, аналитические и прогнозные статьи / Политические, экономические и иные информационно-аналитические и аналитико-прогнозные доклады и книги |publisher=Ceasia.ru |date= |access-date=2017-11-12 |archive-url=https://archive.today/20160116174547/http://ceasia.ru/uzbekistan/kasimova-nazokat-anvarovna.html |archive-date=2016-01-16 |url-status=dead }}</ref> In 2011 she was a [[Fulbright Scholar]] at [[American University]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.cies.org/grantee/nazokat-kasimova |title=Nazokat Kasimova &#124; Fulbright Scholar Program |publisher=Cies.org |date= |access-date=2017-11-12}}</ref> She has published over 40 papers during her career, and is active with numerous international organizations. She currently teaches at the University of World Economy and Diplomacy.<ref name="ceasia1"/> Her area of specialization is the [[Bologna Process]]; within that subject she is concerned with such issues as promoting mobility, introducing a system of credits, and promoting cooperation with Europe in the field of [[quality assurance]].<ref name="archive1"/>
