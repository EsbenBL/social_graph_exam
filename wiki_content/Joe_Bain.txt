'''Joe Staten Bain''' (4 July 1912, [[Spokane, Washington]] – 7 September 1991, [[Columbus, Ohio]]) was an American economist associated with the [[University of California, Berkeley]].  Bain was designated a [[American Economic Association#Distinguished Fellows|Distinguished Fellow]] by the [[American Economic Association]] in 1982.<ref>Along with [[Gérard Debreu]] that year per [http://www.aeaweb.org/honors_awards/disting_fellows.php AEAweb: Distinguished Fellows.]</ref> An accompanying statement referred to him as "the undisputed father of modern [[Industrial Organization]] Economics."<ref name="DistinguishedFellow1982" /><ref name= "WilliamGShepherd2008">William G. Shepherd. "Bain, Joe Staten (1912–1991)," ''[[The New Palgrave Dictionary of Economics]]'', 2nd Edition.  [https://link.springer.com/referenceworkentry/10.1057/978-1-349-95121-5_684-2 Extract]</ref>

==Background and career==
Bain received an [[Bachelor of Arts|A.B.]] from UCLA in 1935. He studied at [[Harvard]], where he has also an instructor in Economics from 1936 to 1939.  There he received an [[Master of Arts|M.A.]] in 1939 and [[Ph.D.]] in 1940,<ref>Joe S. Bain, 1986. "Structure Versus Conduct as Indicators of Market Performance," ''Antitrust Law and Economics Review''. Thumbnail [http://heinonline.org/HOL/LandingPage?collection=journals&handle=hein.journals/antlervi18&div=18&id=&page= bio.]</ref><ref name = "BalderstonBreak">Frederick E. Balderston and George F. Break. [http://texts.cdlib.org/view?docId=hb7c6007sj&doc.view=frames&chunk.id=div00002&toc.depth=1&toc.id= Joe S. Bain Jr., Economics: Berkeley]. [[California Digital Library]]</ref> under the direction of [[Joseph Schumpeter]],<ref name= "WilliamGShepherd2008"/> [[Edward Chamberlin]] and [[Edward Sagendorph Mason|Edward Mason]]. The title of his dissertation was "The Value, Depreciation and Replacement of Durable Capital Goods".

Bain was appointed Lecturer at the University of California, Berkeley in 1939 and served there until his retirement in 1975.<ref name= "BalderstonBreak" /> He also served in 1960 as director of studies at the California Water Industry.<ref name= "WilliamGShepherd2008"/>

==Contributions==
Bain was a prolific author, at both the theoretical and [[applied economics|applied]] level. Major works included ''The Economics of the Pacific Coast Petroleum Industry'' (1944-1947)<ref>Joe S. Bain, ''The Economics of the Pacific Coast Petroleum Industry'', University of California Press:<br/>&nbsp;&nbsp; • 1944, v.1. ''Market Structure'', chapter-preview [https://books.google.com/books?hl=en&lr=&id=T141FkWTP7wC&oi=fnd&pg=PR11&dq=false#v=onepage&q&f=false links]<br/>&nbsp;&nbsp; • 1945. v. 2, ''Price Behavior and Competition''<br/>&nbsp;&nbsp; • 1947. v. 3. ''Public Policy toward Competition and Pricing''</ref> described as "a landmark in the application and empirical testing of the hypotheses of [[microeconomic theory]]."<ref name="DistinguishedFellow1982" >• 1983. "Joe S. Bain: Distinguished Fellow 1982," ''American Economic Review'', 73(3) [2 unnumbered front pages.]<br/>• Frederick E. Balderston and George F. Break. [http://texts.cdlib.org/view?docId=hb7c6007sj&doc.view=frames&chunk.id=div00002&toc.depth=1&toc.id= Joe S. Bain Jr., Economics: Berkeley - California Digital Library.]</ref>  His work in the 1950s culminated in ''Barriers to New Competition'' (1956),<ref name="Bain1956" >Joe S. Bain, 1956. ''Barriers to New Competition: Their Character and Consequences in Manufacturing'', Harvard University Press.  Review extracts [https://www.jstor.org/discover/10.2307/1811245?uid=3739936&uid=2&uid=4&uid=3739256&sid=21101828169287].</ref> and ''Industrial Organization: A [[Treatise]]'' (1959).<ref name="Bain1959" >Joe S. Bain, 1959, 2nd ed., 1968. ''Industrial Organization: A Treatise'', John Wiley.</ref>

Bain developed the [[paradigm]] of [[Structure-conduct-performance paradigm|structure, conduct, and performance]] as related to the industry, as distinct from individual firms or industries.<ref name="Bain1959" /><ref>Leonard W. Weiss, 1979. "The Structure-Conduct-Performance Paradigm and Antitrust," ''[[University of Pennsylvania Law Review]]'',127(4) p. [https://www.jstor.org/discover/10.2307/3311794?uid=3739936&uid=2&uid=4&uid=3739256&sid=21101827642097 1104].</ref> It postulated and tested [[barriers to entry]] as a determinant of industry performance.<ref name="Bain1956" />  [[Market concentration]] was analyzed in relation to [[market power]] and [[rate of profit]].<ref name= "WilliamGShepherd2008"/>
