'''Mark P. Taylor''' is the dean of the [[Olin Business School]] at [[Washington University in St. Louis|Washington University]] in St. Louis, USA, where he is also the Donald Danforth, Jr. Distinguished Professor of Finance.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Dean Mark P. Taylor {{!}} WashU Olin Business School|url=https://olin.wustl.edu/EN-US/about-olin/Pages/dean.aspx|access-date=2021-05-26|website=olin.wustl.edu}}</ref>  Previously, he was the dean of [[Warwick Business School]] (WBS) at the [[University of Warwick]] and, before that, a managing director of BlackRock.<ref name=":1">{{cite web|last=Bradshaw|first=Della|title=Meet The Dean|url=http://www.ft.com/cms/s/2/f3ded4f6-c0ab-11df-94f9-00144feab49a.html#axzz1ngWNe1GB|publisher=Financial Times}}</ref><ref name=":2">{{cite web|title=Mark P Taylor, Dean of Warwick Business School|url=http://www.wbs.ac.uk/about/governance/dean.cfm|work=Profile of The Dean|accessdate=21 November 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|last=Rane|first=Gauri|title=Implementing Innovations|url=http://educationtimes.com/educationTimes/CMSP/STUDY-ABROAD/102/UK/95/20120319201203191402596233da2a531/Implementing-Innovations.html|publisher=Times of India|accessdate=29 March 2012|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://archive.is/20120711174735/http://educationtimes.com/educationTimes/CMSP/STUDY-ABROAD/102/UK/95/20120319201203191402596233da2a531/Implementing-Innovations.html|archivedate=11 July 2012|df=dmy-all}}</ref>

==Education==
Taylor completed his undergraduate degree in [[philosophy, politics and economics]] (PPE) at the [[University of Oxford]] and holds [[MSc]] and [[PhD]] degrees in economics and finance from [[London University]], as well as an [[MBA]] in Higher Education from the [[UCL Institute of Education|Institute of Education]] at [[University College London|University College, London]] and an [[Master of Arts|MA]] in English literature from [[University of Liverpool|Liverpool University]]. In 2012, Taylor was awarded a higher doctorate, the degree of Doctor of Science ([[DSc]]), by the [[University of Warwick]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.wbs.ac.uk/news/releases/2012/07/27/Dean/of/Warwick|title=Dean of Warwick Business School awarded higher doctorate|accessdate=12 November 2012}}</ref>

==Academic and business career==
Taylor began his career as a foreign exchange trader in the City of [[London]].   From 2006 until 2010 he worked as a managing director at [[BlackRock]].   In 2010, Taylor left BlackRock to become dean of [[Warwick Business School]].  In December 2016, he became dean of Olin Business School.  Taylor has also been a fellow of [[University College, Oxford|University College]], [[University of Oxford]], has held professorships at the [[University of Dundee]], [[Cass Business School]], [[London]] and at [[Liverpool University]] and has been a visiting professor at the [[Stern School of Business]], [[New York University]],  the [[Bordeaux University|University of Bordeaux]] and at the [[University of Aix-Marseille]]. Taylor was a senior economist at the [[Imf|International Monetary Fund]], Washington, D.C., for five years and an economist at the [[Bank of England]].<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1" /><ref name=":2" />

==Research background==
Taylor is a Nonresident Senior Fellow of the [[Brookings Institution]],<ref>{{Cite web|date=2018-05-02|title=Mark Taylor|url=https://www.brookings.edu/experts/mark-taylor/|access-date=2021-05-26|website=Brookings|language=en-US}}</ref> a Research Fellow of the [[Centre for Economic Policy Research]],<ref>{{Cite web|title=Centre for Economic Policy Research {{!}} A network of over 1300 economists based across Europe|url=https://cepr.org/|access-date=2021-05-26|website=cepr.org}}</ref> a Fellow of the [[Royal Statistical Society]] (FRSS),<ref>{{Cite web|title=Fellows Archive|url=https://www.acss.org.uk/fellows/|access-date=2021-05-26|website=Academy of Social Sciences|language=en-GB}}</ref> a Fellow of the [[Academy of Social Sciences]] (FAcSS)<ref>{{Cite web|title=RSS - Home|url=https://www.rss.org.uk/|access-date=2021-05-26|website=RSS}}</ref> and a Fellow of the [[Royal Society of the Arts]] (FRSA).<ref>{{Cite web|date=2018-05-02|title=Mark Taylor|url=https://www.brookings.edu/experts/mark-taylor/|access-date=2021-05-26|website=Brookings|language=en-US}}</ref> 

==Editorial activities==
Taylor is the [[editor-in-chief]] of both ''[[Applied Economics (journal)|Applied Economics]]''<ref>{{Cite web|title=Applied Economics Editorial Board|url=https://www.tandfonline.com/action/journalInformation?show=editorialBoard&journalCode=raec20&|access-date=2021-05-26|website=www.tandfonline.com}}</ref> and its sister journal ''[[Applied Economics Letters]]''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Applied Economics Series|url=https://www.tandfonline.com/action/journalInformation?show=editorialBoard&journalCode=rael20|url-status=live|work=Taylor & Francis|accessdate=28 January 2016}}</ref> He is also the editor of the ''International Journal of Finance & Economics''.<ref>{{Cite web|title=International Journal of Finance & Economics|url=https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/journal/10991158|access-date=2021-05-26|website=Wiley Online Library|language=en}}</ref>
