'''Jason Johnson''' is an American political scientist, commentator and writer. He is the author of the book ''Political Consultants and Campaigns: One Day to Sell.'' Johnson is an [[associate professor]]<ref>https://www.morgan.edu/school_of_global_journalism_and_communication/academics/multimedia_journalism/our_faculty/staff_/jason_johnson.html</ref> of communication and journalism at [[Morgan State University]]. He is a regular political contributor to [[MSNBC]] and [[CNN]].

== Education ==
Johnson earned his [[B.A.]] in [[political science|government]] from the [[University of Virginia]], followed by an [[M.A.]] and [[Ph.D.]] in political science from the [[University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2016-11-04|title=Jason Johnson|url=https://www.igs.berkeley.edu/people/jason-johnson|access-date=2020-08-18|website=Institute of Governmental Studies - UC Berkeley|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Jason Johnson|url=http://www.morgan.edu/school_of_global_journalism_and_communication/academics/multimedia_journalism/our_faculty/staff_/jason_johnson.html|access-date=2020-08-18|website=Morgan State University|language=en}}</ref>

==Career==
Johnson is currently a tenured associate professor in the School of Global Journalism and Communication at [[Morgan State University]] in [[Baltimore, Maryland]], where he teaches courses focused on political and international journalism.<ref>{{cite web|last1=Watson|first1=Jamal Eric|title=Morgan State Bolsters Journalism School with Jason Johnson|date=16 August 2016|url=http://diverseeducation.com/article/86064/|publisher=diverseeducation.com|access-date=16 August 2016}}</ref> Johnson formerly was a professor of [[political science]] and communications at [[Hiram College]] in [[Hiram, Ohio]], where he taught American politics, comparative politics, campaign management and communications.<ref>{{cite web |publisher=Hiram College |title=Biography, Jason Johnson |url=http://www.hiram.edu/jasonjohnson/}}</ref> In October 2010, Johnson was named the politics editor for ''[[The Source]]''.<ref>{{cite magazine|magazine=The Source |title=Getting Brand New |date=2010-10-27 |url=http://www.thesource.com/articles/29434/Getting-Brand-New/ |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110724180804/http://www.thesource.com/articles/29434/Getting-Brand-New/ |archive-date=2011-07-24 }}</ref> He also served as politics editor of [[The Root (magazine)|''The Root'']] until early 2020.<ref name="root">{{Cite web|url=https://www.essence.com/news/politics/jason-johnson-the-root-misfit-black-girls-msnbc/|title = MSNBC Contributor Jason Johnson 'No Longer Employed' at the Root}}</ref>

Johnson is the author of ''Political Consultants and Campaigns: One Day to Sell''.

Johnson served as campaign manager on legislative races in [[Virginia]], [[South Carolina]] and [[Maryland]]. In the field of international and comparative politics, Johnson worked on the [[2000 London mayoral election]], and as an international election monitor in [[Mexico]] and [[South Africa]].

Vote for a black man?  |date=2008-07-13 |url=http://news.cincinnati.com/article/20080713/NEWS0108/307130024/Will-Ohio--Ky.-vote-for-a-black-man}}</ref> ''[[Akron Beacon Journal]]'',<ref>{{cite news |last=Warsmith |first=Stephanie |newspaper=Akron Beacon Journal |title=Brunner seeks election overhaul |date=2007-12-15 |url=http://www.ohio.com/news/12528241.html?page=1&c=y}}</ref> and ''[[The Plain Dealer]]''.<ref>{{cite news |last=Kroll |first=John |newspaper=[[The Plain Dealer]] |title=Race mattered more in Ohio primary than in any other state |date=2008-03-08 |url=http://blog.cleveland.com/openers/2008/03/race_mattered_more_in_ohio_pri.html#post}}</ref> He has also appeared in the online edition of [[Essence (magazine)|''Essence'']] and ''[[Black Enterprise]]''.<ref>{{cite web |publisher=Hiram College |title=Biography, Jason Johnson |url=http://www.hiram.edu/jasonjohnson/}}</ref>

Johnson is a paid contributor on [[MSNBC]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://politicon.com/speaker/jason-johnson/|title=Dr. Jason Johnson|website=Politicon|access-date=2020-02-04}}</ref> He was temporarily "benched" in February 2020 after referring to female African American supporters of 2020 Democratic presidential candidate [[Bernie Sanders]] as being from “the Island of Misfit Black Girls”.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.thedailybeast.com/msnbc-benches-contributor-jason-johnson-who-said-bernie-sanders-staffers-are-island-of-misfit-black-girls|title=MSNBC Benches Contributor Jason Johnson, Who Said Bernie Sanders Staffers Are 'Island of Misfit Black Girls'|website=The Daily Beast|date=26 February 2020|access-date=2020-02-27|last1=Tani|first1=Justin Baragona}}</ref> He was also fired from his position at [[The Root (magazine)|''The Root'']] because of the comment.<ref name="root"/> Johnson has made several other noteworthy statements regarding the [[2020 United States presidential election]] during his time on MSNBC. In January 2019, Johnson stated that Sanders's presidential campaign was done and that he would drop out by August.<ref>{{cite web | url =https://www.vanityfair.com/news/2020/02/fox-has-been-more-fair-why-bernies-team-has-had-it-with-msnbc | title =Fox has been "More Fair": Why Bernie's Team has had it with MSNBC | last =Kludt | first =Tom | date =February 18, 2020 | website =Vanity Fair | publisher =Conde Nast | access-date =February 27, 2020}}</ref> Additionally, in February 2020, Johnson asserted that billionaire presidential candidate [[Michael Bloomberg]] is not an [[oligarchy|oligarch]], prompting [[Saagar Enjeti]] of ''The Hill'' to characterize Johnson as a "professional wrong person".<ref>{{cite AV media | people =Saagar Enjeti and Krystal Ball | title =Nina Turner calls Bloomberg an oligarch, MSNBC melts down | medium =Video | publisher =The Hill | location =Washington DC | date = February 4, 2020 | url =https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nwa4VodV0tk}}</ref><ref>{{cite AV media | people =Krystal Ball and Saagar Enjeti  | title =Krystal Ball: The Establishment starts to bend the knee to Bernie | medium =Video | publisher =The Hill | location =Washington D.C. | date =February 27, 2020 | url =https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=psBfVarP8CM }}</ref> Johnson returned to MSNBC in July 2020 and remains a regular political panelist.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Baragona |first1=Justin |title=Jason Johnson Returns to MSNBC After Months-Long Benching for Smearing Bernie Sanders Staffers |url=https://www.thedailybeast.com/jason-johnson-returns-to-msnbc-after-months-long-benching-for-smearing-bernie-sanders-staffers |work=The Daily Beast |date=15 July 2020 |language=en}}</ref>
