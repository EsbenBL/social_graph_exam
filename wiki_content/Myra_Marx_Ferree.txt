'''Myra Marx Ferree''' (born October 10, 1949) is a former professor of [[sociology]] and director of the Center for German and European Studies at the [[University of Wisconsin–Madison]],<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://www.americanacademy.de/person/myra-marx-ferree/|title=Myra Marx Ferree|website=American Academy|language=en-US|access-date=2019-04-13}}</ref> where she was also a member of the [[Women's Studies]] Program. In 2005 she was a [[Berlin Prize|Berlin Prize Fellow]] at the [[American Academy in Berlin]] and in 2004 the Maria-Jahoda Visiting Professor at the Ruhr University Bochum.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://www.sowi.rub.de/jahoda/profs/s2004_ferree.html.en|title=Prof. Myra Marx Ferree|website=www.sowi.rub.de|access-date=2019-04-13}}</ref><ref name=":0" />  Ferree retired in 2018.<ref>{{Cite web|date=768|title=CELEBRATING THE RETIREMENT OF MYRA MARX FERREE WITH "SUPERFEMSEM"|url=https://daadcenter.wisc.edu/celebrating-the-retirement-of-myra-marx-ferree-with-superfemsem/|access-date=2020-12-20|website=CGES|language=en-US}}</ref>

== Life ==
Ferree attended [[Harvard University]] where she obtained a PhD in social psychology and social relations in 1976.<ref name=":12">{{Cite web|url=https://www.sowi.rub.de/jahoda/profs/s2004_ferree.html.en|title=Prof. Myra Marx Ferree|website=www.sowi.rub.de|access-date=2019-04-13}}</ref>

She has written numerous articles about [[feminist organizations]] and politics in the US, Germany and internationally, as well as about [[gender inequality]] in families, the inclusion of gender in [[sociological theory]] and practice, and the intersections of [[gender]] with [[Race (classification of human beings)|race]] and [[Social class|class]].  Her current work focuses on comparisons between US and German feminist movements and gender policy developments since the 1960s as well as the development of feminist identities in [[transnational women's organizations]].

She has been the recipient of the [[Jessie Bernard]] Award (sociology's highest honor for work in gender),<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.asanet.org/news-and-events/member-awards/jessie-bernard-award/myra-marx-ferree-award-statement|title=Myra Marx Ferree Award Statement|date=2009-06-03|website=American Sociological Association|language=en|access-date=2019-04-13}}</ref> vice-president of the [[American Sociological Association]] and deputy editor of its leading journal, ''[[American Sociological Review]]'', president of [[Sociologists for Women in Society]] and recipient of its mentoring and feminist scholarship awards.<ref name=":1" />
