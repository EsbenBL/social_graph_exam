'''Darnell Felix Hawkins''' (born November 24, 1946)<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://id.loc.gov/authorities/names/n77014164.html |title=Darnell Felix Hawkins |website=Name Authority File}}</ref> is an American sociologist and criminologist. He is emeritus professor of [[African-American studies]], [[sociology]], and [[criminal justice]] at the [[University of Illinois at Chicago]]. He is known for his research on [[racial differences in crime]], the ways in which the law is applied differently across races, and violence prevention.<ref name=umc>{{Cite web |url=https://www2.umc.edu/administration/centers_and_institutes/evers-williams_institute/darnell_f__hawkins,_phd,_jd.aspx |title=Darnell F. Hawkins |website=University of Mississippi Medical Center}}</ref> This includes research on [[urban violence]]
and [[racial profiling]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://articles.latimes.com/1992-06-16/news/mn-511_1_chicago |title=Police Action Questioned in Chicago's Violence; 1,000 Arrested |last=Secter |first=Bob |date=16 June 1992 |website=Los Angeles Times}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=http://articles.baltimoresun.com/1998-08-15/news/1998227080_1_racial-profiling-driving-while-black-bias |title='Driving While Black' bill targets police bias Data on traffic stops, searches stipulated in Senate legislation |last=Texeira |first=Erin |date=15 August 1998 |website=Baltimore Sun}}</ref>

==Biography==
Hawkins received his B.A. from [[Kansas State University]] in 1968, his M.A.T. from [[Wayne State University]] in 1970, his A.M. and Ph.D. from the [[University of Michigan]] in 1974 and 1976, respectively, and his J.D. from the [[University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill]] in 1981.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://uic.academia.edu/DarnellHawkins/CurriculumVitae |title=Darnell Hawkins CV}}</ref> He was a professor at UIC from 1987 until his retirement from there in 2002.<ref name=umc/>
