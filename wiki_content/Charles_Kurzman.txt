'''Charles Kurzman''' is a [[Professor]] of [[Sociology]] at [[University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill]] who specializes in [[Middle East]] and [[Islamic studies]].<ref name=SmithExtremist>{{cite news|last1=Smith|first1=Joy Lukachick|title=Terrorist or extremist, was Abdulazeez a man with a plan?|url=http://www.timesfreepress.com/news/local/story/2015/jul/23/fbi-abdulazeez-homegrown-extremist/316023/|accessdate=23 July 2015|publisher=[[Chattanooga Times Free Press]] |date=23 July 2015}}</ref> 

==Education and employment==
After completing his B.A. at [[Harvard University]] in 1986, he completed his M.A. and PhD. at [[University of California, Berkeley]] in 1997 and 1992 respectively. He has been affiliated with [[University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill]] since 1998. <ref>[http://kurzman.unc.edu/cv/ Official CV] </ref>

==Books==
* ''The Missing Martyrs: Why There Are So Few Muslim Terrorists'' (2011)
* ''Democracy Denied, 1905-1915: Intellectuals and the Fate of Democracy'' (2008)
* ''The Unthinkable Revolution in Iran'' (2004)
* ''Modernist Islam, 1840-1940: A Sourcebook'' (2002)
* ''Liberal Islam: A Sourcebook'' (1998)
