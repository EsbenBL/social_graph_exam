{{Short description|British diplomat, economist and author (1864-1950)}}
'''Alfred Mitchell-Innes''' (30 June 1864 &ndash; 13 February 1950) was a [[United Kingdom|British]] diplomat, [[economist]] and author. He had the Grand Cross of the [[Order of Medjidie]]h conferred upon him by [[Abbas II of Egypt|Abbas II]], [[Khedive of Egypt]].

==Family==
[[File:Cypriot ambassador's residence.JPG|thumb|Former residence of Alfred Mitchell-Innes in [[Washington, D.C.]]]]
The youngest child of Alexander Mitchell-Innes (1811–1886) of [[Ayton, Scottish Borders|Ayton]], and Whitehall (near [[Chirnside]]), [[Berwickshire]], by his second spouse Fanny Augusta (1821–1902), daughter of James Vine, in [[Puckaster]], [[Isle of Wight]], Alfred was born at 2 Forres Street, [[Edinburgh]]. He married (her second marriage) in 1919, Eveline (d. 28 December 1946), daughter of [[Sir William Miller, 1st Baronet]] of [[Manderston]], [[Berwickshire]]. In 1934, Mitchell-Innes and his wife contributed 25 Egyptian and oriental antiquities he had acquired from Egypt to the British Museum. <ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.britishmuseum.org/collection/term/BIOG54597/|title=A Mitchell-Innes|last=The British Museum}}</ref>

==Career==
Educated privately, he entered the [[British Diplomatic Service]] in 1890 and was appointed to [[Cairo]] the next year. In 1896 he became financial advisor to [[Chulalongkorn|Chulalongkorn the Great]] (Rama V), King of [[Siam]]. In 1899, he was appointed Under-Secretary of State for Finance in [[Egypt]], where he founded [[Al Ahly SC]] on 24 April 1907.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://alahlyegypt.com/announcement/%D8%A3%D9%88%D9%84-%D8%B1%D8%A6%D9%8A%D8%B3-%D9%88%D8%A3%D9%88%D9%84-%D8%A7%D8%AC%D8%AA%D9%85%D8%A7%D8%B9/|title=أول مجلس في تاريخ الأهلي|last=alahlyegypt|website=alahlyegypt.com|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200702154909/http://alahlyegypt.com/announcement/%D8%A3%D9%88%D9%84-%D8%B1%D8%A6%D9%8A%D8%B3-%D9%88%D8%A3%D9%88%D9%84-%D8%A7%D8%AC%D8%AA%D9%85%D8%A7%D8%B9/|archive-date=2 July 2020}}</ref>

He later worked as a Counselor at the [[Embassy of the United Kingdom in Washington, D.C.|British Embassy]] in [[Washington, D.C.]] from 1908 to 1913. He was Minister to [[Uruguay]] from 1913 to 1919, after which he retired.

While in Washington, he wrote two articles on money and credit for ''The Banking Law Journal''. The first, 'What is Money?', received an approving review from [[John Maynard Keynes]],<ref>Keynes, John Maynard, 'What is money?', ''[[Economic Journal]]'', 24:95 (September 1914), pp.419-21</ref> which led to the publication of the second, '[[Credit theory of money]]'.<ref>[[David Graeber]]: ''Debt: The First 5,000 Years'', Publisher: Melville House; Reprint edition (November 27, 2012, {{ISBN|1612191290}}</ref> Long forgotten and rediscovered decades later, the articles have been praised as "the best pair of articles on the nature of money written in the twentieth century".<ref>L. Randall Wray, ''Credit and state theories of money: the contributions of A. Mitchell Innes'', p.223</ref>

In retirement he joined [[Bedford, England|Bedford]] Town Council, serving twice: from 1921 to 1931 and from 1934 to 1947.
