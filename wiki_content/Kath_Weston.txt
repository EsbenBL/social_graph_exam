'''Kath Weston''' (born 2 November 1958) is an American [[anthropology|anthropologist]], author and academic. She is a [[Guggenheim Fellowship|Guggenheim Fellow]] and has twice received the [[Ruth Benedict Prize]] for anthropological works.

==Biography==

Kath Weston was born in Illinois on 2 November 1958. She studied at the [[University of Chicago]] (B.A., 1978; M.A., 1981) and at [[Stanford University]] (M.A., 1984; Ph.D., 1988).<ref name="EC">{{cite web|url=https://www.encyclopedia.com/arts/educational-magazines/weston-kath-1958|title=Kath Weston 1958-|website=Encyclopedia.com|access-date=5 December 2018}}</ref>

She first worked as a fellow at the Center for Advanced Feminist Studies at the [[University of Minnesota]] from 1989 to 1990. She was then appointed assistant and later associate professor of anthropology at [[Arizona State University]] from 1990 to 1998. She became director of studies on women, gender, and sexuality at [[Harvard University]] in 2001.<ref name="EC"/> She is currently Professor of Anthropology at the [[University of Virginia]]<ref name="UV">{{cite web|url=https://anthropology.virginia.edu/faculty/profile/kmw7j|title=Kath Weston|website=University of Virginia|access-date=5 December 2018}}</ref> and [[British Academy]] Global Professor in the Department of [[Social anthropology|Social Anthropology]] at the [[University of Edinburgh]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Kath Weston {{!}} School of Social and Political Science|url=https://www.sps.ed.ac.uk/staff/kath-weston|access-date=2021-07-10|website=www.sps.ed.ac.uk}}</ref>

Weston was appointed a Guggenheim Fellowship in 2011 in the fields of Anthropology and Cultural Studies.<ref name="GF">{{cite web|url=https://www.gf.org/fellows/all-fellows/kath-weston/|title=Kath Weston|website=John Simon Guggenheim Memorial Foundation|access-date=5 December 2018}}</ref> Weston, a lesbian,<ref>{{Cite book |title=Families We Choose: Lesbians, Gays, Kinship |last=Weston |first=Kath |url=https://archive.org/details/familieswechoose00west |url-access=registration |page=[https://archive.org/details/familieswechoose00west/page/104 104] |year=1997 |publisher=Columbia University Press}}</ref> was awarded a Ruth Benedict Prize in 1990 for her monograph ''Families We Choose: Lesbians, Gays, Kinship'', and a second such award in 1997 for her monograph ''Render Me, Gender Me: Lesbians Talk Sex, Class, Color, Nation, Studmuffins''.<ref name="RB">{{cite web|url=http://queeranthro.org/awards/the-ruth-benedict-prize/|title=The Ruth Benedict Prize|website=Association for Queer Anthropology|access-date=5 December 2018}}</ref>
