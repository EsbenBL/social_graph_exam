'''Dara Strolovitch''' is an [[America]]n [[political scientist]], currently a professor of [[Gender studies|gender and sexuality studies]], [[African American studies]], and political science at [[Princeton University]]. She studies the politics of race, class, gender, and sexuality in the context of intersectional societal inequality, and the representation of those who are marginalized in multiple overlapping ways.

==Education and early career==
Strolovitch attended [[Vassar College]], earning a BA in political science with a minor in women's studies in 1992.<ref name=gssprofile>{{cite web |url=https://gss.princeton.edu/dara-z-strolovitch |title=Dara Z. Strolovitch Profile |access-date=11 January 2020 |publisher=Princeton University}}</ref> She attended [[Yale University]] for graduate school, earning an MA in political science in 1998, an MPhil in political science in 2002, and a PhD in political science also in 2002.<ref name=gssprofile/> Between 2000 and 2001, she was a research fellow at [[The Brookings Institution]].<ref name=gssprofile/> In 2001, Strolovitch became a professor of political science at the [[University of Minnesota]], before moving to Princeton University in 2013.<ref name=gssprofile/> She has been a visiting scholar at [[Georgetown University]], [[Stanford University]], and The [[Russell Sage Foundation]].<ref name=gssprofile/>

==Career==
Strolovitch's first book, published in 2007, is called ''Affirmative Advocacy: Race, Class, and Gender in Interest Group Politics''.<ref name=meetapsr>{{cite web |url=https://politicalsciencenow.com/meet-2020-apsr-editor-dara-z-strolovitch-of-princeton-university/ |title=Meet 2020 APSR Editor, Dara Z. Strolovitch of Princeton University |date=25 August 2019 |access-date=11 January 2020 |work=Political Science Now |publisher=American Political Science Association}}</ref> Strolovitch uses a survey and interviews to study the [[political representation]] of [[interest group]]s, with a theory of interest group effectiveness that builds on the idea of [[intersectionality]].<ref name=jones09>{{cite journal |title=Affirmative Advocacy: Race, Class, and Gender in Interest Group Politics |last=Jones |first=Bryan D |journal=Perspectives on Politics |volume=7 |issue=3 |year=2009 |pages=631–633 |doi=10.1017/S1537592709990090}}</ref> The findings of the book include evidence that already advantaged groups are better represented by interest group politics than disadvantaged groups are, and that often interest groups focus more on the legislative and executive branches of the US government than they do on the judicial branch.<ref name=jones09/> In reviewing the book, [[Bryan D. Jones]] wrote that "the empiricism is as strong or stronger than the best of the existing interest group studies".<ref name=jones09/> ''Affirmative Advocacy'' received multiple awards from caucuses of the [[American Political Science Association]],<ref>{{cite web |url=http://web.mnstate.edu/schwartz/AmerPoliSciAssocKammerer.html |title=Gladys M. Kammerer Award Winners |last=Schwartz |first=Larry |publisher=Minnesota State University Moorhead}}</ref> as well as an award from the [[American Sociological Association]],<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.asanet.org/asa-communities/asa-sections/current-sections/race-gender-and-class/section-race-gender-and-class-award-recipients |title=The Section on Race, Gender, and Class' Distinguished Contribution to Scholarship Book Award |publisher=American Sociological Association |access-date=11 January 2020}}</ref> and excerpts have been used in American politics textbooks.<ref>{{cite book |first=Ken |last=Kollman |title=Readings in American Politics |url=https://archive.org/details/readingsinameric0000koll |url-access=registration |publisher=WW Norton |year=2010}}</ref>

Strolovitch also co-edited the ''CQ Guide to Interest Groups and Lobbying'' with Burdett Loomis and Peter Francia, and she has a forthcoming book called ''When Bad Things Happen to Privileged People: Race, Gender, and the Political Construction of Crisis & Non-Crisis''.<ref name=gssprofile/><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.russellsage.org/visiting-scholars/dara-strolovitch |title=Dara Strolovitch |year=2018 |access-date=11 January 2020 |publisher=Russell Sage Foundation}}</ref>

Strolovitch is a member of the 2020-2024 editorial leadership of the [[American Political Science Review]],<ref>{{cite web |url=https://politicalsciencenow.com/apsa-announces-the-new-editorial-team-for-the-american-political-science-review/ |title=APSA Announces the New Editorial Team for the American Political Science Review |publisher=American Political Science Association |date=26 July 2019 |access-date=31 December 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last=Marshall |first=Jenna |url=https://www.santafe.edu/news-center/news/righting-balance-new-apsr-editors-meet-sfi-discuss-gender-and-race-scientific-publishing |date=2019-07-26 |title=Righting the balance: New APSR editors meet at SFI to discuss gender and race in scientific publishing | publisher=Santa Fe Institute |access-date=25 December 2019}}</ref> which is the most selective political science journal.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://polisci.utk.edu/newsletter/fall2019/austin.php |title=Cutting-Edge Research Agenda | publisher=University of Tennessee Knoxville |access-date=25 December 2019}}</ref> She was also a founding associate editor of the ''Journal of Race, Ethnicity, and Politics''.<ref name=meetapsr/>
