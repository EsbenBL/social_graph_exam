'''Elihu Katz''' ([[Hebrew]]: אליהוא כ"ץ, born in 1926 in [[New York City]]) is an American and Israeli [[sociologist]] and [[communication scientist]], usually associated with [[uses and gratifications theory]]. He is known for his work with [[Paul Lazarsfeld]] in the field of mass communication, most notably for developing the theory of the [[two-step flow of communication]]. He is currently Emeritus Professor of Communication at the [[Annenberg School for Communication at the University of Pennsylvania|Annenberg School for Communication]] of the [[University of Pennsylvania]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.asc.upenn.edu/people/faculty/elihu-katz-phd|title=Elihu Katz, Ph.D. {{!}} Annenberg School for Communication|website=www.asc.upenn.edu|language=en|access-date=2018-05-14}}</ref>

In 1989, he was awarded the prestigious [[Israel Prize]], for social sciences.<ref name=prize>{{Cite web| title = Israel Prize Official Site - Recipients in 1989 (in Hebrew)| url = http://cms.education.gov.il/EducationCMS/Units/PrasIsrael/Tashmag/Tashnab_Tashmag_Rikuz.htm?DictionaryKey=Tashmat|work=cms.education.gov.il}}</ref>

In 2005, he received the Marshall Sklare Award, given annually by the Association for the Social Scientific Study of Jewry<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.assj.org/|title=assj.org}}</ref> to a senior scholar who has made a significant scholarly contribution to the social scientific study of Jewry.

In 2013, he received an honorary degree from Northwestern University.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.asc.upenn.edu/news-events/news/prof-katz-receive-honorary-degree-northwestern-university|title=Katz to Receive Honorary Degree from Northwestern {{!}} Annenberg School for Communication|website=www.asc.upenn.edu|language=en|access-date=2018-05-14}}</ref>

In 2018, he received an honorary doctorate from the University of Pennsylvania.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.asc.upenn.edu/news-events/news/professor-emeritus-elihu-katz-receive-honorary-doctorate-penn|title=Professor Emeritus Elihu Katz to Receive Honorary Doctorate from Penn {{!}} Annenberg School for Communication|website=www.asc.upenn.edu|language=en|access-date=2018-05-14}}</ref>
