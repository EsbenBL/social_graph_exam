'''Pınar Selek''' (born October 8, 1971) is a [[Turkey|Turkish]] [[sociologist]], [[feminist]], and [[author]].<ref>Kerem Öktem (15 September 2008), [http://www.merip.org/mero/mero091508 "Another Struggle: Sexual Identity Politics in Unsettled Turkey"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171017203124/http://www.merip.org/mero/mero091508 |date=2017-10-17 }}. ''Middle East Research and Information Project''. Retrieved January 26, 2013.</ref> She is known for her work on the rights of vulnerable communities in [[Turkey]], including women, the poor, street children, sexual minorities, and [[Kurdish people|Kurdish]] communities. She is the author of several books published in [[Turkish people|Turkish]], [[German People|German]], and [[French people|French]], and is one of the founding editors of ''Amargi'', a Turkish feminist journal.<ref>[http://amargigroupistanbul.wordpress.com/about-amargi/pinar-selek/ Amargi Istanbul - Pınar Selek]. Retrieved 25 January 2013.</ref> She currently resides in [[France]] and became a French citizen in 2017.<ref>{{citation|year=2019|title=Alet-les-Bains: Pinar Selek guest of the Friends of François de Ravignan|url=https://www.lindependant.fr/2019/10/27/alet-les-bains-pinar-selek-invitee-des-amis-de-francois-de-ravignan,8508146.php|publisher=[[L'Indépendant (Pyrénées-Orientales)|L'Indépendant]]|access-date=23 November 2020}}</ref> 

Selek has been prosecuted over a 15-year period in Turkey in connection to an explosion that occurred at the [[Spice Bazaar, Istanbul]] in 1998. Tried and acquitted of all charges on three occasions (in 2006, 2008, and 2011), her most recent acquittal was amended in November 2012 by the Istanbul Heavy Penal Court No. 12, which sentenced her to life in prison on January 24, 2013.<ref>(25 January 2013), [http://english.sabah.com.tr/national/2013/01/25/pinar-selek-sentenced-to-life "PıNAR SELEK SENTENCED TO LIFE"]. [http://www.sabah.com.tr ''Sabah'']. Retrieved 2 February 2013.</ref><ref>The Observatory (27 November 2012), [http://www.fidh.org/TURKEY-Judicial-harassment-of-12503 "TURKEY: Judicial harassment of Pinar Selek continues as Istanbul Heavy Penal Court decides to amend her acquittal and request her conviction"]. [http://www.fidh.org/-english- International Federation for Human Rights] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130605045312/http://www.fidh.org/-english- |date=2013-06-05 }}. Paris-Geneva. Retrieved 2 February 2013.</ref> Selek's lawyers have appealed the verdict and announced plans to bring her case before the [[European Court of Human Rights]].<ref>Kaya Genc (1 February 2013), [http://uncut.indexoncensorship.org/2013/02/pinar-selek-turkey-sociologist-kurdistan-workers-party/ "Why was this Turkish sociologist given a life sentence?"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130603075323/http://uncut.indexoncensorship.org/2013/02/pinar-selek-turkey-sociologist-kurdistan-workers-party/ |date=2013-06-03 }}. [http://uncut.indexoncensorship.org/ UNCUT] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160826222220/http://uncut.indexoncensorship.org/ |date=2016-08-26 }}. Retrieved 2 February 2013.</ref>

==Education==

Selek attended the French-language high school [[Lycée Notre Dame de Sion Istanbul]] and completed her undergraduate and graduate studies in the sociology department at [[Mimar Sinan Fine Arts University]]. She is currently pursuing doctoral studies in [[political science]] at the [[University of Strasbourg]].

==Arrest, imprisonment, and release, 1998-2000==

Pınar Selek was arrested on July 11, 1998, in connection to an explosion that had occurred two days prior at the [[Spice Bazaar, Istanbul]], which had killed seven people and wounded approximately 100 others.<ref>Baran Mavzer (27 January 2013), [http://globalvoicesonline.org/2013/01/27/pinar-selek-issused-a-lifetime-imprisonment-after-15-year-long-trial/ "Pınar Selek Issued a Life Sentence after 15 Year Trial"]. [http://globalvoicesonline.org/ ''Global Voices'']. Retrieved 27 January 2013.</ref> The arrest is widely considered to have been motivated by her contact with Kurds as part of her academic research.<ref>{{cite journal |author = Editorial |title = Rights for all |journal = Nature |volume = 470 |issue = 7335 |pages = 436 |year = 2011 |doi = 10.1038/470436a |pmid = 21350442 |bibcode = 2011Natur.470..436. |doi-access = free }}</ref><ref>{{cite journal |author = Maureen Freely |title = Crossing the Line |journal = Index on Censorship |volume = 41 |issue = 3 |pages = 56–65 |year = 2012 |doi = 10.1177/0306422012456477|s2cid = 148073684 }}</ref> Her work was confiscated, and she refused to name the individuals she had interviewed during the course of her research.<ref>{{cite news |title=Sociologist Selek handed life in prison for alleged bombing |author=Erdem Güneş |url=http://www.hurriyetdailynews.com/sociologist-selek-issued-lifetime-imprisonment-for-alleged-bombing.aspx?pageID=238&nID=39789&NewsCatID=339 |newspaper=Hürriyet Daily News |date=24 January 2013 |access-date=27 January 2013}}</ref> Another suspect, Abdülmecit Öztürk, was arrested two weeks after Selek, and confessed to police that the two had carried out the bombing together, although he later recanted his statement and claimed that he had been tortured in police custody.<ref>Baran Mavzer (27 January 2013), [http://globalvoicesonline.org/2013/01/27/pinar-selek-issused-a-lifetime-imprisonment-after-15-year-long-trial/ "Pınar Selek Issued a Life Sentence after 15 Year Trial"]. [http://globalvoicesonline.org/ ''Global Voices'']. Retrieved 27 January 2013.</ref><ref>[http://www.fidh.org/-english- International Federation for Human Rights] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130605045312/http://www.fidh.org/-english- |date=2013-06-05 }} (25 January 2013), [http://www.fidh.org/TURKEY-Outrage-following-the-12799 "TURKEY: Outrage following the conviction of Pinar Selek to life imprisonment"]. Paris-Geneva. Retrieved 25 January 2013.</ref> Öztürk was later acquitted of all charges, and his statement against Selek was ruled as inadmissible.<ref>{{cite news |title=Selek Case a Travesty For Turkish Judiciary |author=Cengiz Çandar |url=http://www.al-monitor.com/pulse/originals/2013/01/pnar-selek-dreyfuss-affair-egyptian-bazaar-bombing.html|newspaper=[[Al-Monitor]] |date=28 January 2013 |access-date=28 January 2013}}</ref><ref>[http://www.hwr.org Human Rights Watch] (8 February 2011), [https://www.hrw.org/news/2011/02/08/turkey-activist-s-trial-travesty-justice "Turkey: Activist’s Trial a Travesty of Justice: Drop All Charges Against Pınar Selek"]. Istanbul. Retrieved 3 February 2013.</ref>

After spending two and a half years in prison, during which time she was subject to torture and ill-treatment,<ref>[http://www.fidh.org/-english- International Federation for Human Rights] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130605045312/http://www.fidh.org/-english- |date=2013-06-05 }} (25 January 2013), [http://www.fidh.org/TURKEY-Outrage-following-the-12799 "TURKEY: Outrage following the conviction of Pinar Selek to life imprisonment"]. Paris-Geneva. Retrieved 25 January 2013.</ref> Selek was released on December 22, 2000, when a team of experts, including faculty from [[Istanbul University]]'s Analytic Chemistry Department and Cerrahpaşa Medical Faculty’s Forensic Department,<ref>[http://www.nationalturk.com/en/sociologist-pinar-selek-sentenced-lifetime-prison-for-bombing-claims-turkey-news-32813 "Sociologist Pinar Selek sentenced lifetime prison for bombing claims"]. [http://www.nationalturk.com/en/ ''National Turk'']. Retrieved 25 January 2013.</ref> issued reports concluding that the explosion had been caused by the accidental ignition of a gas cylinder.<ref>[http://www.pen-international.org/ PEN International] (12 December 2012), [http://www.pen-international.org/newsitems/turkey-pen-international-vice-president-to-attend-selek-hearing/ "News: TURKEY – PEN International Concerned About Pinar Selek Trial"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130503150406/http://www.pen-international.org/newsitems/turkey-pen-international-vice-president-to-attend-selek-hearing/ |date=2013-05-03 }}. Retrieved 25 January 2013.</ref><ref>[http://www.focus-fen.net/?id=n297740 "Turkish sociologist sentenced to life for 'terrorism'"]. Istanbul. [http://www.focus-fen.net/ ''FOCUS Information Agency'']. Retrieved 25 January 2013.</ref> Three expert witnesses assigned by the court also testified that the explosion was caused by a gas leak.<ref>{{cite news |title=Sociologist Selek handed life in prison for alleged bombing |author=Erdem Güneş |url=http://www.hurriyetdailynews.com/sociologist-selek-issued-lifetime-imprisonment-for-alleged-bombing.aspx?pageID=238&nID=39789&NewsCatID=339 |newspaper=Hürriyet Daily News |date=24 January 2013 |access-date=27 January 2013}}</ref>

==Acquittals, retrial, and sentencing, 2006-present==

[[File:Pinar Selek et Günter Wallraff conférence de presse Strasbourg 25 janvier 2013.jpg|thumb|left|Pınar Selek and [[Günter Wallraff]] at a press conference in Strasbourg following her sentencing on January 25, 2013.]]

The Istanbul High Criminal Court No. 12 acquitted Selek of wrongdoing on three occasions (in 2006, 2008, and 2011), citing a lack of any evidence linking her to the blast.<ref>[http://www.omct.org/ World Organisation Against Torture], [http://www.omct.org/human-rights-defenders/urgent-interventions/turkey/2013/01/d22129/ "Turkey: Continued judicial harassment faced by Ms. Pinar Selek"]. Retrieved 25 January 2013.</ref> Nonetheless, the court decided on November 22, 2012, to amend its own prior acquittal decisions and reopen her trial, a move which her defense lawyers labeled as "unprecedented in Turkish legal history."<ref>[http://www.fidh.org/-english- International Federation for Human Rights] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130605045312/http://www.fidh.org/-english- |date=2013-06-05 }} (27 November 2012), [http://www.fidh.org/TURKEY-Judicial-harassment-of-12503 "TURKEY: Judicial harassment of Pinar Selek continues as Istanbul Heavy Penal Court decides to amend her acquittal and request her conviction"]. Retrieved 25 January 2013.</ref>

On January 24, 2013, after just over an hour of deliberation, the court sentenced her to life in prison for the 1998 spice bazaar bombing.<ref>[http://www.focus-fen.net/?id=n297740 "Turkish sociologist sentenced to life for 'terrorism'"]. Istanbul. [http://www.focus-fen.net/ ''FOCUS Information Agency'']. Retrieved 25 January 2013.</ref> The decision was reached by majority of two to one, with the head judge in the case issuing a dissenting opinion.<ref>Çiçek Tahaoğlu (24 January 2013), [http://www.bianet.org/english/other/143848-pinar-selek-receives-life-sentence-again "Pinar Selek Receives Life Sentence"]. [http://www.bianet.org/english ''Bianet'']. Retrieved 25 January 2013.</ref> While Selek was tried in absentia, more than 30 nongovernmental organizations and political party representatives from [[France]], [[Germany]], [[Italy]], and [[Austria]] attended the hearings, and nearly 150 people protested during the trial.<ref>[http://www.nationalturk.com/en/sociologist-pinar-selek-sentenced-lifetime-prison-for-bombing-claims-turkey-news-32813 "Sociologist Pinar Selek sentenced lifetime prison for bombing claims"]. [http://www.nationalturk.com/en/ ''National Turk'']. Retrieved 25 January 2013.</ref> Four observers from the [[University of Strasbourg]], including the vice rector, also attended the trial.<ref>Erdem Güneş (24 January 2013), [http://www.hurriyetdailynews.com/pinar-selek-back-on-trial-again-in-absentia.aspx?pageID=238&nID=39730&NewsCatID=339 "Pınar Selek back on trial again in absentia"]. Istanbul. ''[[Hurriyet Daily News]]''. Retrieved 25 January 2013.</ref>

==Support from academic institutions and international organizations==
[[File:Pinar Selek conférence de presse 01 Strasbourg 25 janvier 2013.jpg|thumb|Pınar Selek (center) at a press conference in Strasbourg on January 25, 2013. The banner reads: "Freedom for research. Freedom for Pinar!!!"]]
The [[International Federation for Human Rights]] (FIDH) and [[World Organisation Against Torture]] (OMCT) have called on Turkish authorities to end the 14-year "judicial harassment" of Selek, calling her actions a "legitimate exercise of the freedom of opinion and expression."<ref>[http://www.fidh.org/-english- International Federation for Human Rights] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130605045312/http://www.fidh.org/-english- |date=2013-06-05 }} (27 November 2012), [http://www.fidh.org/TURKEY-Judicial-harassment-of-12503 "TURKEY: Judicial harassment of Pinar Selek continues as Istanbul Heavy Penal Court decides to amend her acquittal and request her conviction"]. Retrieved 25 January 2013.</ref> [[PEN International]] has expressed outrage and concern and argued that the judicial campaign against Selek seeks to penalize her for her "long standing support for and work on minority groups in Turkey."<ref>[http://www.pen-international.org/ PEN International] (12 December 2012), [http://www.pen-international.org/newsitems/turkey-pen-international-vice-president-to-attend-selek-hearing/ "News: TURKEY – PEN International Concerned About Pinar Selek Trial"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130503150406/http://www.pen-international.org/newsitems/turkey-pen-international-vice-president-to-attend-selek-hearing/ |date=2013-05-03 }}. Retrieved 25 January 2013.</ref> The [[Middle East Studies Association of North America]] (MESA) has expressed its support for Selek and dismay at the prolonged denial of justice she has been subject to.<ref>Peter Sluglett, [http://www.mesa.arizona.edu/committees/academic-freedom/index.html Middle East Studies Association Committee on Academic Freedom] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130111004844/http://www.mesa.arizona.edu/committees/academic-freedom/index.html |date=2013-01-11 }} (25 December 2012), [http://www.jadaliyya.com/pages/index/9011/letter-concerning-prolonged-denial-of-justice-for- "Letter Concerning Prolonged Denial of Justice for Pinar Selek"]. Retrieved 25 January 2013.</ref> In a letter addressed to Turkish Prime Minister [[Recep Tayyip Erdoğan]], MESA's Committee on Academic Freedom asserted that: "all of the circumstances attendant to her case suggest that Selek has been on trial for the last fourteen years for her research on the PKK in violation of her right to academic freedom."<ref>[http://www.mesa.arizona.edu/ Middle East Studies Association] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140326082905/http://www.mesa.arizona.edu/ |date=2014-03-26 }} (12 December 2012). [http://www.mesa.arizona.edu/committees/academic-freedom/intervention/letters-turkey.html "Letters on Turkey"]. Retrieved 3 February 2013.</ref> [[Human Rights Watch]] has called her prosecution a "perversion of the criminal justice system and abuse of due process," and insisted that the "baseless charges should be dropped once and for all."<ref>[https://www.hrw.org/ Human Rights Watch] (8 February 2011). [https://www.hrw.org/news/2011/02/08/turkey-activist-s-trial-travesty-justice "Turkey: Activist’s Trial a Travesty of Justice"]. Retrieved 27 January 2013.</ref> Turkish journalist [[Cengiz Çandar]] called the January 2013 ruling a "travesty" and a "disgraceful judgment."<ref>{{cite news |title=Selek Case a Travesty For Turkish Judiciary |author=Cengiz Çandar |url=http://www.al-monitor.com/pulse/originals/2013/01/pnar-selek-dreyfuss-affair-egyptian-bazaar-bombing.html|newspaper=[[Al-Monitor]] |date=28 January 2013 |access-date=28 January 2013}}</ref>

Other organizations that have voiced solidarity with Selek include [[Amnesty International]],<ref>[http://humanrightsturkey.org/ Amnesty International] (22 January 2013). [http://humanrightsturkey.org/2013/01/22/kafka-in-turkish-the-pinar-selek-trial/ "Kafka in Turkish: The Pınar Selek Trial"]. Retrieved 28 July 2013.</ref> the Transnational Work Group on Academic Liberty and Freedom of Research in Turkey,<ref>[http://gitamerica.blogspot.com/ GIT-North America] (27 November 2012). [http://gitamerica.blogspot.com/2012/11/on-case-of-pnar-selek.html "On the Case of Pınar Selek"]. Retrieved 28 July 2013.</ref> the French Sociological Association <ref>[http://www.afs-socio.fr/node/276]</ref> and the [[Committee of Concerned Scientists]].<ref>[http://concernedscientists.org/ Committee of Concerned Scientists] (05 April 2013). [http://concernedscientists.org/2013/04/ccs-joins-amnestys-campaign-on-turkeys-lack-of-due-process-and-free-expression-for-academics/#pinar-selek "CCS Joins Amnesty’s Campaign on Turkey’s Lack of Due Process and Free Expression for Academics"]. Retrieved 28 July 2013.</ref>

[[:fr:Alain Beretz|Alain Beretz]], president of the [[University of Strasbourg]], has espoused the university's solidarity with Selek, calling her life imprisonment conviction "unjust and revolting."<ref>Alain Beretz (24 January 2013), [http://www.unistra.fr/uploads/media/declarationAB_jugementpinarselek.pdf "Un jugement injuste et révoltant : Pinar Selek doit vivre en chercheuse libre!"]. (in French). [http://www.unistra.fr/index.php?id=homepage University of Strasbourg]. Retrieved 25 January 2013.</ref><ref>[http://www.pinarselek.com/public/Default.aspx Pinar Selek website] (in Turkish), [http://www.pinarselek.com/public/page_item.aspx?id=1791 "Declaration by the President of the University of Strasbourg Raising support for PhD. student Pinar Selek"]. Retrieved 25 January 2013.</ref> At a press conference following the verdict, Selek vowed to continue her struggle for justice.<ref>[http://www.pinarselek.com/public/default.aspx Pinar Selek website] (in Turkish) (25 January 2013), [http://www.pinarselek.com/public/page_item.aspx?id=1891 "Freedom to Research, Freedom to Pınar!"]. Retrieved 26 January 2013.</ref>
