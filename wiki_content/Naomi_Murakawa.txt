'''Naomi Murakawa''' is an American [[political scientist]] and associate professor of [[African-American studies]] at [[Princeton University]]. Along with [[Kent Eaton]], she is also the co-chair of the 2017 [[American Political Science Association]] (APSA) Section 24 meeting.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.apsanet.org/section24 |title=Politics and History (Section 24) |website=[[American Political Science Association]]}}</ref> Murakawa received her B.A. in women’s studies from [[Columbia University]], her M.Sc. in social policy from the [[London School of Economics]], and her Ph.D. in political science from [[Yale University]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://aas.princeton.edu/author/murakawa/ |title=Naomi Murakawa |website=Department of African American Studies |publisher=[[Princeton University]]}}</ref> She is known for her 2014 book, ''[[The First Civil Right]]'', which contends that American liberals are just as responsible for [[mass incarceration]] in the United States as conservatives are.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.themarshallproject.org/2015/01/15/blame-liberals#.UpAGZVnJd |title=‘Blame Liberals’ |last=Goldstein |first=Dana |website=[[The Marshall Project]] |publication-date=2015-01-15}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.thenation.com/article/how-white-liberals-used-civil-rights-create-more-prisons/ |title=How White Liberals Used Civil Rights to Create More Prisons |last=Osterweil |first=Willie |website=[[The Nation]] |publication-date=2015-01-06}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Did Liberals Put Black America Behind Bars?|url=https://www.newsweek.com/did-liberals-put-black-america-behind-bars-297096|date=2015-01-06|website=Newsweek|language=en|access-date=2020-05-01}}</ref> In 2015, Murakawa won the [[Michael Harrington]] Book Award from APSA for this book.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.apsanet.org/section-27-Michael-Harrington-Book-Award |title=Michael Harrington Book Award Winners |website=American Political Science Association}}</ref>
