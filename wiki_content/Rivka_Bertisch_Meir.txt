'''Rivka Bertisch Meir''' was an [[United States|American]] [[psychologist]], licensed mental health counselor and [[psychotherapist]].  She is known for the creation of [[personal development]] programs in the  United States, Israel, and [[Argentina]] in the 1970s and 1980s and for establishing global partnerships in [[international psychology]].

Meir was a Chairperson and International Liaison of International Psychology ([[international psychology|Division 52]]) of the [[American Psychological Association]] from 2005 to 2012.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://internationalpsychology.files.wordpress.com/2013/01/ipb_summerfall_2009-1.pdf |title=Data |date= 2009|website= internationalpsychology.files.wordpress.com|access-date=2020-06-11}}</ref> As International Liaison, she recruited international psychologists to establish partnerships with the [[American Psychological Association]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.apa.org/international/pi/2010/04/issue.pdf |title=Data |date= 2010|website=www.apa.org|access-date=2020-06-11}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.apa.org/international/pi/2010/04/div-52.aspx|title=Division 52 Update: April 2010}}</ref> Meir was founder of the [[American Psychological Association|APA]] "Adopt a Psychologist" initiative which established international alliances between [[psychologists]].<ref name="apa">{{cite web|url=http://www.apa.org/monitor/may07/closer.aspx |title=International alliances |publisher=Apa.org |date= |accessdate=2013-07-03}}</ref> Meir was co-founder of the [[American Psychological Association|APA]] National Speakers Network, a directory of psychologists willing to share their expertise with others.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.apadivisions.org/division-1/news-events/speakers/index.aspx |title=APA Speakers Network |publisher=Apadivisions.org |date= |accessdate=2013-07-10}}</ref>

==Background and education==
Rivka Bertisch was born in [[Argentina]] to European Jewish parents.<ref name="saybrook_a">{{Cite web|url=http://www.saybrook.edu/sites/default/files/alumni/newsletters/200906.pdf|title = Accredited, Online College in Pasadena}}</ref>  Most of her relatives were killed by the Nazis in Poland during [[World War II]].<ref name="saybrook_a" />  She attended the [[University of Hawaii]] where she received dual bachelor's degrees in Communications and Psychology and a Masters in Public Health.<ref name="saybrook_a" />  Meir pursued and completed all but one graduate level course at the [[University of Hawaii]] and then received a PhD from the University for Humanistic Studies (presently [[Alliant International University]]). Her dissertation centered on: Life Pattern Counseling—Theory and Practice. Her dissertation work used [[cell memory]] techniques to access and modify traumatic memories.<ref name="search.proquest">{{Cite document|title=Changing one's life story: A retrospective multiple case study - ProQuest|id={{ProQuest|305372711}}}}</ref>

In the 1970s, Meir was a program planner and researcher at the School of Community Medicine of the [[Hadassah Medical Center]] at [[Hebrew University]] in [[Jerusalem]], Israel, and then at the [[Jerusalem Municipality]], Department of Public Health where Rivka (as Rivka Danziger) co-directed [[Project KIDUM]] in which she developed clinic and community-based programs in counseling and how to improve cognitive and intelligence development,  implementing it in the 30 Well Baby Clinics in Jerusalem, Israel (Tipat Chalav).<ref>{{cite news|title=Rivka Danziger, a new immigrant from Hawaii and KIDUM Specialist|newspaper=Jerusalem Post Magazine|date=June 22, 1979}}</ref> Meir also introduced and directed "EST" ([[Erhard Seminar Training]]—[[Landmark Education]]) to Israel from 1977 to 1979. In 1986, she moved back to [[Argentina]], where she pioneered using large group formats to teach human development and the Integrative Rivka’s Method<ref>{{Cite book | url = https://books.google.com/books?id=v9SDiX6pOZQC&q=%22rivka's+integrative+technique%22&pg=PA150 | title = Stop Beliefs That Stop Your Life: Fixed Beliefs and Life Pattern Theory | isbn = 9781463472467 | last1 = Rivka Bertisch Meir | first1 = Ph. D. M. P. H | date = December 2009}}</ref> that she developed, which included relationship improvement skills, developed under the auspices of the R. Bertisch Foundation from 1990 to 1996.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.stjohns.edu/academics/graduate/liberalarts/professional/professional/upcoming/bullying_/speakers.stj |title=About the Invited Speakers |publisher=Stjohns.edu |date=2011-04-13 |accessdate=2013-06-30}}</ref> This is an approach to therapy that integrates body-mind connection, health promotion, spiritual and psychological techniques, including [[firewalking]] (mind over matter).

In 1996, she moved to the New York City area.<ref name="saybrook_a" /> Her work has continued at the corporate level centering on organizational productivity, life and business management coaching, team building and serving as a consultant, trainer, and resource person for top executives, staff, individuals, and groups.

In 2004, Meir founded the [[American Psychological Association|APA]] "Adopt a Psychologist" initiative, a mentoring program to foster alliances between psychologists worldwide.<ref name="apa" />
