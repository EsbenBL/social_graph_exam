'''Price V. Fishback''' (born c. 1955) is an economic historian.  He is a professor of economics at the [[University of Arizona]] and a research associate at the [[National Bureau of Economic Research]]. His research on [[American economic history]] has included employment and labor in the nineteenth and early twentieth centuries especially in the [[History of coal mining in the United States|coal industry]], and government programs of the [[New Deal]]. His work has been recognised by the [[Cliometric Society]] via their awarding him a ''Clio Can'' in recognition of his "exceptional support of [[cliometrics]]".<ref>[https://econ.arizona.edu/people/price-v-fishback University of Arizona: Price V. Fishback] (Accessed Jan 2016)</ref><ref>[http://www.nber.org/people/price_fishback National Bureau of Economic Research: Price V. Fishback] (Accessed Dec 2011)</ref> Prior to arriving to the University of Arizona, Fishback was an Assistant and later Associate Professor at the [[University of Georgia]].

==Education==
Fishback received a B.A. with honors in Mathematics and Economics from [[Butler University]] in 1977. He then received his M.A. and Ph.D. from the [[University of Washington]] in 1979 and 1983, respectively. His Ph.D. Thesis was entitled "Employment Conditions of Blacks in the Coal Industry, 1900-1930." His advisor was [[Robert Higgs]].<ref>{{cite web |title=PRICE VANMETER FISHBACK |url=https://econ.arizona.edu/sites/default/files/persons/fishback.vita_.online2017.09.22.pdf |website=University of Arizona: Department of Economics}}</ref>
