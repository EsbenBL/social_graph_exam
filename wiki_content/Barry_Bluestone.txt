'''Barry Alan Bluestone''' (born December 27, 1944)<!--sources are LCCN, GND--> is an American academic who is the Stearns Trustee Professor of [[Political economy|Political Economy]], founding director of the Kitty and Michael Dukakis Center for Urban and Regional Policy, and the founding dean of the School of [[Public policy|Public Policy]] & Urban Affairs at [[Northeastern University]] in [[Boston, Massachusetts]].

Previously he was Professor of Political Economy at the University of Massachusetts Boston. He also taught economics at [[Boston College]] where he served as director of the University's Social Welfare Research Institute.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://wfnetwork.bc.edu/leaders_entry.php?id=116&area=All |title=Welcome &#124; Work and Family Researchers Network |publisher=Wfnetwork.bc.edu |date= |accessdate=September 28, 2013}}</ref> He is also one of five co-founders of the Economic Policy Institute.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.epi.org/content.cfm/about |title=About &#124; Economic Policy Institute |publisher=Epi.org |date= |accessdate=September 28, 2013}}</ref> In the 1980s and the 1990s he collaborated with the late economist [[Bennett Harrison]] on several publications.

==Education==
Bluestone attended [[University of Michigan]] where he received his Ph.D (1974).<ref>[http://www.socant.neu.edu/faculty/bluestone/ ] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20080919125739/http://www.socant.neu.edu/faculty/bluestone/ |date=September 19, 2008 }}</ref>

==Writings==
Bluestone has written extensively and has authored or co-authored a number of books focused on political economy and society including: ''The Deindustrialization of America (1982)'', ''The Great U-Turn: Corporate Restructuring and the Polarizing of America (1988)'', ''Negotiating the Future: A Labor Perspective on American Business (1992)'', ''Growing Prosperity: The Battle for Growth with Equity in the 21st Century (2000)'',''The Prosperity Gap: A Chartbook of American Living Standards (2000)'', ''The Boston Renaissance: Race, Space, and Economic Change in an American Metropolis (2001)'',  ''Beyond the Ruins: The Meanings of Deindustrialization (2003)'',
''The Urban Experience: Economics, Society, and Public Policy (2008)''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.bookfinder.com/author/barry-bluestone/ |title=Barry Bluestone: new books, used books, rare books by Bluestone, Barry @ |publisher=Bookfinder.com |date= |accessdate=September 28, 2013}}</ref>

==Government service==
In 1995, Bluestone served as policy staff member for Congressman [[Dick Gephardt|Richard Gephardt]] Democratic Leader of the [[United States House of Representatives|U.S. House of Representatives]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://govinfo.library.unt.edu/tdrc/hearings/13mar00/bbluestone.pdf |title=Barry Bluestone : CV |publisher=Govinfo.library.unt.edu |accessdate=September 28, 2013}}</ref>

==Trump statement controversy==
On February 5, 2018, a clip of a January 2018 lecture given by Bluestone was posted online in which he criticized President [[Donald Trump]]. In the clip, Bluestone said that "Sometimes I want to just see [Trump] impeached other times, quite honestly -- I hope there are no FBI agents here -- I wouldn’t mind seeing him dead." In a subsequent interview with ''[[the Boston Globe]]'', Bluestone explained that his remark was "offhand" and that he does not condone violence or want President Trump assassinated.<ref>{{Cite web |url=https://www.insidehighered.com/quicktakes/2018/02/08/professor-criticized-comment-trump |title=Professor Criticized for Comment on Trump  |last=Jaschik |first=Scott |date=February 8, 2018 |website=Inside Higher Ed |language=en |access-date=February 8, 2018}}</ref> He later apologized for his remarks, saying that he meant that he would have liked to see Trump "disappear from the White House."<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.foxnews.com/us/2018/02/08/northeastern-professor-caught-saying-wouldnt-mind-seeing-trump-dead-apologizes-following-backlash.html |title=Northeastern professor caught saying he wouldn't mind seeing Trump 'dead'; apologizes following backlash |last=Norman |first=Greg |date=February 8, 2018 |website=Fox News |language=en-US |access-date=February 8, 2018}}</ref>
