'''Albert Benschop''' (10 May 1949, [[Rijswijk]] - 27 February 2018<ref>http://familieberichten.nl/nl/memorial/Albert-Benschop/435634/1</ref>) was a Dutch [[sociologist]] with the [[University of Amsterdam]]'s faculty of Social and Behavioral Sciences. He was the chief editor of the university's SocioSite project.<ref>[http://www.sociosite.net SocioSite] - Social Science Information System</ref> His internet studies are published in the Peculiarities of Cyberspace.<ref>[http://www.sociosite.org Peculiarities of Cyberspace] - Building blocks of an internet sociologie</ref>

== Biography ==
Benschop received a master's degree in sociology and psychology at the [[Vrije Universiteit]] Amsterdam early 1970s. In 1972 he made name as leader of the SRVU [[Students' union]] of the [[Vrije Universiteit]] Amsterdam, which occupied the main building of the Vrije Universiteit during the student protests that year.<ref>Arie Theodorus van Deursen, Herbert Donald Morton (2008) ''The distinctive character of the Free University in Amsterdam, 1880-2005''. Wm. B. Eerdmans Publishing p. 323.</ref>

Since 1973 Benschop has been a lecturer and researcher at the department of [[Sociology]] at the Universiteit van Amsterdam. In the late 1990s he founded the SocioSite website, which is considered to be one of the world's most consulted social-science websites.<ref>Jim Millhorn, Jim Millhorn (1999). ''Student's companion to the World Wide Web: social sciences and humanities resources''. p.49</ref><ref>Stuart Stein (2002). ''Sociology on the web: a student guide''. p.139</ref><ref>David Fisher, Sandra P. Price, Terry Hanstock (2002). ''Information sources in the social sciences''. K.G. Saur,</ref><ref>Jorinde Seijdel, Oliver Marchart, Geert Lovink (2007). ''The rise of the informal media: how search engines, weblogs and YouTube change public opinion''. p.170</ref>
