'''Philip N. Cohen''' is an [[United States|American]] [[sociology|sociologist]]. He is a Professor of Sociology at the [[University of Maryland]], College Park,<ref>[https://www.philipncohen.com/ Philip N. Cohen website]</ref> and director of [[SocArXiv]], an open archive of the social sciences.<ref>[http://www.socarxiv.org/ SocArXiv]</ref>

==Early life==

Cohen grew up in [[Ithaca, New York]] and attended the [[Lehman Alternative Community School]].<ref>[https://fourfromfour.wordpress.com/2017/11/22/how-acs-inspired-philip-cohens-crusade-for-equality/ "How ACS Inspired Philip Cohen's Crusade for Equality," Four from Four: Lehman Alternative Community School's Alumni Journal.]</ref>

==Career==

Cohen graduated from the [[University of Michigan]] with a B.A. in American Culture, from the [[University of Massachusetts]] with an M.A. in Sociology, and from the [[University of Maryland]] with a Ph.D. in Sociology. His previous faculty positions were at the [[University of North Carolina]] and the [[University of California, Irvine]].<ref>[http://www.terpconnect.umd.edu/~pnc/PNC-CV.pdf Philip N. Cohen]</ref>  

He is a sociologist and demographer who works in the areas of families and inequality, [[social demography]], and social 
inequality. His concerns include gender and race/ethnic segregation in occupations, gender and authority, unpaid housework and [[care work]], [[Health equity|health disparities]], and demographic measurement.<ref>[https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=paVll7oAAAAJ Google Scholar list of publications]</ref>

He is a former member of the [[American Sociological Association]] (ASA) Committee on Publications,<ref>[https://www.asanet.org/about-asa/governance/committees-and-task-forces/committee-publications ASA Committee on Publications webpage]</ref> and chair of the ASA's section on Sociology of the Family.<ref>[http://www.asafamilysection.com/leadership.html ASA Family Section webpage]</ref> He also is an Associate of the Maryland Population Research Center,<ref>[http://www.popcenter.umd.edu/mprc-associates Maryland Population Research Center]</ref> and was formerly secretary-treasurer of the ASA Population Section.<ref>[http://www.asanet.org/sections/population.cfm ASA Population Section]</ref>  He was co-editor, with [http://myweb.brooklyn.liu.edu/sfali/Welcome.html Syed Ali], of [[Contexts]], the quarterly magazine of the ASA, from 2014 to 2017.

==Books==

Cohen has written two books:

* ''The Family: Diversity, Inequality, and Social Change'', first published in 2014 by [[W. W. Norton & Company]]; the third edition was published in 2020.<ref>[https://wwnorton.com/books/9780393537314/ ''The Family: Diversity, Inequality, and Social Change'' at Norton]</ref>
* ''Enduring Bonds: Inequality, Marriage, Parenting, and Everything Else That Makes Families Great and Terrible'', published in 2018 by the [[University of California Press]].<ref>[https://www.ucpress.edu/book.php?isbn=9780520292390 ''Enduring Bonds'' at University of California Press]</ref>

He is co-editor, with [http://myweb.brooklyn.liu.edu/sfali/Welcome.html Syed Ali], of ''The Contexts Reader'', a collection of essays from the magazine Contexts, the quarterly magazine of the [[American Sociological Association]].<ref>[http://books.wwnorton.com/books/webad-detail-editions.aspx?id=4294994901 ''The Contexts Reader'' at W. W. Norton.]</ref>

==Research==

Cohen's paper on [[divorce]], "The Coming Divorce Decline,"<ref>{{cite journal | last1 = Cohen | first1 = Philip N. | year = 2018 | title = The Coming Divorce Decline | journal = SocArXiv | doi=10.31235/osf.io/h2sk6 | url = http://osf.io/h2sk6/ | doi-access = free }}</ref> reported a drop in U.S. divorce rates from 2008 to 2017, and predicted further declines in the coming years. It was reported by [[Bloomberg News]],<ref>{{cite news|title=Millennials Are Causing the U.S. Divorce Rate to Plummet|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2018-09-25/millennials-are-causing-the-u-s-divorce-rate-to-plummet|access-date=October 7, 2018|newspaper=[[Bloomberg News]]|date=September 25, 2018}}</ref> the [[Today (U.S. TV program)|Today]] show,<ref>{{cite news|title=Why millennials are being credited with dropping divorce rate|url=https://www.today.com/video/why-millennials-are-being-credited-with-dropping-divorce-rate-1329626179778|access-date=October 7, 2018|newspaper=[[Today (U.S. TV program)|Today]]|date=September 26, 2018}}</ref> [[Buzzfeed]],<ref>{{cite news|title=Millennials Are Being Blamed For "Killing" Divorce, And The Jokes Basically Wrote Themselves|url=https://www.buzzfeed.com/sydrobinson1/millennials-killing-divorce|access-date=October 7, 2018|newspaper=[[Buzzfeed]]|date=September 26, 2018}}</ref> and [[the Atlantic]].<ref>{{cite news|title=The Not-So-Great Reason Divorce Rates Are Declining|url=https://www.theatlantic.com/family/archive/2018/09/millennials-divorce-baby-boomers/571282/|access-date=October 7, 2018|newspaper=[[The Atlantic]]|date=September 25, 2018}}</ref>

His work on labor market inequality has focused on race/ethnic and [[gender inequality in the United States]]. On race, he has published in the ''American Journal of Sociology''<ref>{{cite journal | last1 = Huffman | first1 = Matt L. | last2 = Cohen | first2 = Philip N. | year = 2004 | title = Racial Wage Inequality: Job Segregation and Devaluation Across U.S. Labor Markets | jstor = 10.1086/378928 | journal = American Journal of Sociology | volume = 109 | issue = 4| pages = 902–936 | doi=10.1086/378928| citeseerx = 10.1.1.560.2662 | s2cid = 154979334 }}</ref> (with Matt Huffman) and ''Social Forces'',<ref>{{cite journal | last1 = Cohen | first1 = Philip N | year = 1998 | title = Black Concentration Effects on Black-White and Gender Inequality: Multilevel Analysis for U.S. Metropolitan Areas | journal = Social Forces | volume = 77 | issue = 1| pages = 207–229 | jstor=3006015 | doi=10.1093/sf/77.1.207}}</ref> assessing the relationship between demographic composition of labor markets and patterns of inequality.

In the area of gender inequality, his research (with Matt Huffman) has addressed [[occupational segregation]] and gender devaluation<ref>{{cite journal | last1 = Cohen | first1 = Philip N. | last2 = Huffman | first2 = Matt L. | year = 2003 | title = Individuals, Jobs, and Labor Markets: The Devaluation of Women's Work | journal = American Sociological Review | volume = 68 | issue = 3| pages = 443–63 | jstor=1519732 | doi=10.2307/1519732 }}</ref> and the effects of women in workplace management positions.<ref>{{cite journal | last1 = Cohen | first1 = Philip N. | last2 = Huffman | first2 = Matt L. | year = 2007 | title = Working for the Woman? Female Managers and the Gender Wage Gap | journal = American Sociological Review | volume = 72 | issue = 5| pages = 681–704 | doi=10.1177/000312240707200502| s2cid = 37110842 }}</ref><ref>{{cite journal | last1 = Huffman | first1 = Matt L. | last2 = Cohen | first2 = Philip N. | last3 = Pearlman | first3 = Jessica | year = 2010 | title = Engendering Change: Organizational Dynamics and Workplace Gender Segregation, 1975-2005 | journal = Administrative Science Quarterly | volume = 55 | issue = 2| pages = 255–277 | doi=10.2189/asqu.2010.55.2.255| s2cid = 1880151 | url = https://semanticscholar.org/paper/b100365d4e2b2b5fabbd9d9706ba752eb80ef84a }}</ref> Alone as well as with a number of different co-authors, he has published research on the gender division of household labor.<ref>{{cite journal | last1 = Cohen | first1 = Philip N | year = 2004 | title = The Gender Division of Labor: 'Keeping House' and Occupational Segregation in the United States | journal = Gender and Society | volume = 18 | issue = 2| pages = 239–252 | jstor=4149435 | doi=10.1177/0891243203262037| s2cid = 18231053 | url = https://semanticscholar.org/paper/a36b0f860b64500b21760fe8a5c058baf27b206a }}</ref><ref>{{cite journal|doi=10.1111/j.1741-3737.2002.00743.x | volume=64 | issue=3 | title=Premarital Cohabitation and Housework: Couples in Cross-National Perspective | year=2002 | journal=Journal of Marriage and Family | pages=743–755 | last1 = Batalova | first1 = Jeanne A.| citeseerx=10.1.1.470.7312 }}</ref><ref>{{cite journal| doi=10.1016/j.ssresearch.2006.04.005 | volume=36 | issue=2 | title=Housework and social policy | year=2007 | journal=Social Science Research | pages=512–530 | last1 = Fuwa | first1 = Makiko}}</ref><ref>{{cite journal|doi=10.1111/j.1741-3737.2011.00850.x | volume=73 | issue=4 | title=Headed Toward Equality? Housework Change in Comparative Perspective | year=2011 | journal=Journal of Marriage and Family | pages=832–844 | last1 = Geist | first1 = Claudia| pmid=24465055 | url=https://semanticscholar.org/paper/8ba32fe88afff4db74d2b1c59e2ff0e8506aee81 | pmc=3898932 | s2cid=6682719 }}</ref>

On family structure, he has addressed issues of measurement, including how to identify cohabiting couples in [[U.S. Census]] data.,<ref>{{cite journal | last1 = Casper | first1 = Lynne M. | last2 = Cohen | first2 = Philip N. | year = 2000 | title = How Does POSSLQ Measure Up? Historical Estimates of Cohabitation | journal = Demography | volume = 37 | issue = 2| pages = 237–45 | jstor=2648125 | doi = 10.2307/2648125 | pmid = 10836181 | s2cid = 31769045 | doi-access = free }}</ref> and the language used for marriage ([[Homogamy (sociology)|homogamy]] and heterogamy).<ref>{{cite journal|doi=10.1111/j.1756-2589.2010.00080.x | volume=3 | title=Homogamy Unmodified | year=2011 | journal=Journal of Family Theory & Review | pages=47–51 | last1 = Cohen | first1 = Philip N.| url=http://osf.io/r9rm7/ }}</ref>

On health disparities, he has studied the [[COVID-19 pandemic]] in rural U.S. counties,<ref>{{cite journal | last1 = Cohen | first1 = Philip N. | year = 2020 | title = The COVID-19 Epidemic in Rural U.S. Counties | journal = SocArXiv | doi=10.31235/osf.io/pnqrd | url = https://osf.io/preprints/socarxiv/pnqrd/ }}</ref> marriage and mortality,<ref>{{cite journal | last1 = Cohen | first1 = Philip N. | year = 2019 | title = The rising marriage mortality gap among Whites | journal = SocArXiv | doi=10.31235/osf.io/8374m | url = https://osf.io/preprints/socarxiv/8374m/ }}</ref> disability rates among adopted children,<ref>{{cite journal | last1 = Kreider | first1 = Rose | last2 = Cohen | first2 = Philip N. | year = 2009 | title = Disability Among Internationally Adopted Children in the United States | url = http://pediatrics.aappublications.org/content/124/5/1311 | journal = Pediatrics | volume = 124 | issue = 5| pages = 1311–1318 | doi=10.1542/peds.2008-3206| pmid = 19858154 | s2cid = 16891927 }}</ref> the living arrangements of children with disabilities,<ref>{{cite journal|doi=10.1111/j.1741-3737.2006.00279.x | volume=68 | issue=3 | title=Gendered Living Arrangements Among Children With Disabilities | year=2006 | journal=Journal of Marriage and Family | pages=630–638 | last1 = Cohen | first1 = Philip N.}}</ref> the relationship between parental age and childhood disability,<ref>{{cite journal | last1 = Cohen | first1 = Philip N | year = 2014 | title = Parental Age and Cognitive Disability among Children in the United States | journal = Sociological Science | volume = 1 | pages = 102–110 | doi=10.15195/v1.a8| doi-access = free }}</ref> and race/ethnic disparities in infant mortality.<ref>{{cite journal | last1 = Cohen | first1 = Philip N | year = 2016 | title = Maternal Age and Infant Mortality for White, Black, and Mexican Mothers in the United States | journal = Sociological Science | volume = 3 | pages = 32–38 | doi=10.15195/v3.a2| doi-access = free }}</ref>
 
Some of Cohen's research is part of the tradition of [[intersectionality]], including his work on the American [[women's suffrage]] movement;<ref>{{cite journal | last1 = Cohen | first1 = Philip N. | year = 1996 | title = Nationalism and Suffrage: Gender Struggle in Nation-Building America | journal = Signs | volume = 21 | issue = 3| pages = 707–727 | jstor=3175176 | doi=10.1086/495103| s2cid = 145659343 | url = https://semanticscholar.org/paper/f2be3a8dabc80ac676889fe756307084a0df83bd }}</ref> and on the relationship between population composition and inequality by race, class and gender.<ref>{{cite journal| doi=10.1006/ssre.2000.0693 | volume=30 | title=Race, Class, and Labor Markets: The White Working Class and Racial Composition of U.S. Metropolitan Areas | year=2001 | journal=Social Science Research | pages=146–169 | last1 = Cohen | first1 = Philip N.|citeseerx = 10.1.1.497.3381}}</ref>

==Congressional Testimony==

In 2007, Cohen testified before the U.S. Senate Committee on Health, Education, Labor, and Pensions, on equal pay for women workers.<ref>[http://www.gpo.gov/fdsys/pkg/CHRG-110shrg34993/html/CHRG-110shrg34993.htm Senate HELP Committee testimony]</ref> The legislation under consideration at that hearing eventually became the [[Lilly Ledbetter Fair Pay Act of 2009]].
