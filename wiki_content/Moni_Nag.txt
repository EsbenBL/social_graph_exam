{{Use Indian English|date=December 2018}}'''Moni Nag''' (1925 – 7 December 2015) was an Indian [[Anthropology|anthropologist]] specialising in the politics of sexuality.

==Education and career==
Born in India, Nag earned a Master's degree in statistics from the [[University of Calcutta]] in 1946 and a PhD in anthropology from [[Yale University]] in 1961.<ref name=obit>{{cite news |title=Anthropologist Moni Nag no more |newspaper=[[The Times of India]] |date=11 December 2015 |url=https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/kolkata/Anthropologist-Moni-Nag-no-more/articleshow/50131018.cms |agency=TNN |access-date=13 December 2018 }}</ref><ref name=bio>{{cite book |editor=Moni Nag |title=Population and Social Organization |location=The Hague / Paris |publisher=Mouton |year=1975 |isbn=9789027975898 |contribution=Moni Nag |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=ejUVGnqnKiEC&pg=PA353 |page=353 }}</ref> He started his career in the [[Indian Statistical Institute]]{{citation needed|date=October 2017}} and worked on the [[Anthropological Survey of India]] before joining the Department of Anthropology at [[Columbia University]] in New York in 1966; he was a lecturer and later an adjunct professor and headed the social demography section in the International Institute for the Study of Human Reproduction.<ref name=obit/><ref name=bio/> He was also a senior associate in the [[Population Council]] in New York<ref name=anthro>{{cite web |url=https://anthropologyworks.com/2015/12/14/anthro-in-the-news-12142015-7/ |title=Anthro in the news 12/14/2015: in memoriam |website=Anthropologyworks |date=14 December 2015 |access-date=11 October 2017 }}</ref> and a patron and vice president of the Elmhirst Institute of Community Studies at [[Santiniketan]],<ref name=obit/> and served as chair of the population commission in the [[International Union of Anthropological and Ethnological Sciences]].<ref name=bio/>

==Research and publications==
Nag was a pioneer of demographic anthropology.<ref name=anthro/> He researched and published in the fields of human sexuality, fertility, family planning, [[HIV]] prevention, and [[sex work]], with a focus on India, and both studied and worked for the rights of prostitutes in the [[Kolkata]] [[red-light district]] of [[Sonagachi]];<ref name=obit/><ref name=anthro/><ref>{{cite book |author1=Shalina Mehta |author2=Suninder K.
