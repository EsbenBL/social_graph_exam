'''Loch K. Johnson''' is Regents Professor of Political Science at the [[University of Georgia]].<ref name="whoswho">{{cite web
 | url = http://www.worldwhoswho.com/public/views/entry.html?id=sl2175074
 | title = Biography at World Who's Who
}}</ref> He is editor of the journal ''[[Intelligence and National Security]]''<ref>{{cite web
 | url = http://www.tandf.co.uk/journals/journal.asp?issn=0268-4527&linktype=145
 | title = Intelligence and National Security: Editorial Board
}}</ref> and has written numerous books<ref>{{cite web
 | url = http://www.google.com/search?q=Loch+K.+Johnson&tbs=bks:1
 | title = Query Google books for Loch K. Johnson
}}</ref> on American foreign policy. Johnson was special assistant to the chair of the [[United States Senate Select Committee on Intelligence|Senate Select Committee House Subcommittee on Intelligence]] from 1975 to 1976. He also served as staff director of the [[House Permanent Select Committee on Intelligence|House Subcommittee on Intelligence Oversight]] from 1977 to 1979. In 1995 and 1996, Johnson worked with the chair of the [[Aspin-Brown Commission]] on Intelligence.

==Career==
Johnson earned a PhD in Political Science from the [[University of California at Riverside]] in 1969.<ref name="whoswho" /> He has won the [[Josiah Meigs#Legacy|Josiah Meigs Prize]], the highest teaching honor at the [[University of Georgia]] in addition to the Owens Award, its highest honor for research. The [[Southeastern Conference]] named him its inaugural Professor of the Year in 2012.<ref>{{cite web|title=SEC Professor of the Year |url=http://www.secdigitalnetwork.com/NEWS/tabid/473/Article/234539/southeastern-conference-announces-2012-professor-of-the-year-award-recipient.aspx|access-date=May 8, 2012}}</ref>  The award recognizes a faculty member from one the conference's member institutions whose achievement in scholarship, research and service puts them in the elite of higher education. He was a visiting scholar at [[Yale University]] over fall semester 2005. Currently,{{when|date=May 2018}} he is back at UGA continuing his research on intelligence.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://spia.uga.edu/faculty-member/loch-k-johnson/ |title=Loch K. Johnson |publisher=Spia.uga.edu |access-date=2018-05-24}}</ref>
