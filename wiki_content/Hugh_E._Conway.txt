'''Hugh Edward Conway''' was a [[college]] [[professor]] and expert on [[labor economics]] and the [[construction]] industry in the [[United States]].  He held the Department of Labor Chair and taught economics at the [[Industrial College of the Armed Forces]].  He traveled extensively with military and academic groups studying construction project across the United States and around the world.  Conway wrote numerous articles on a wide range of labor and economics-related subjects.  He also compiled and edited several books.

== Early life ==

Hugh Conway was born in [[New York City]], [[New York (state)|New York]] on 9 October 1942.<ref name="IC1">"ICAF Staff and Faculty", ''Industrial College of the Armed Forces Biography Book – Academic Year 1997'', National Defense University, Fort Lesley J. McNair, Washington, District of Columbia, August 1996, p. 17.</ref>  Conway attended [[Cornell University]], receiving a [[Bachelor of Science]] degree in industrial and labor relations in 1964.  He then went on to attend graduate school at the [[London School of Economics]], where he earned a [[Master of Economics|Master of Science in economics]] degree in 1965.  The next step for Conway was doctoral level education at [[University of London]].  He did his doctoral work at the university's [[SOAS, University of London|School of Oriental and African Studies]].  In 1968, the University of London awarded him a [[doctor of philosophy]] degree in economics and labor relations.<ref name="IC1" /><ref name="BL">Gropman, Alan (editor), [http://www.ibiblio.org/hyperwar/USA/BigL/BigL-Author.html "The Editors and Authors"], ''The Big 'L' American Logistics in World War II'', National Defense University Press, Washington, District of Columbia, 1997, p. 446.</ref>  An article based on his thesis was published in an [[Australia]]n academic journal.<ref>Conway, H. E., "Labour Protest Activity in Sierra Leone", ''Labour History'', Australia, Number 15, November 1968, pp. 49-63.</ref>

Conway served in the [[United States Army]] between 1968 and 1972.  He received a [[Bronze Star Medal]] and several campaign medals for his service during the [[Vietnam War]].<ref name="IC1"/>

== Labor economist ==

Conway was a career employee of the [[United States Department of Labor]].  He began his service as an economist in the department's Pay Board in [[Washington, District of Columbia|Washington]],  [[District of Columbia]].  In 1973, he was selected to serve as an executive assistant in the office of the [[Secretary of Labor]].  In 1975, he was appointed head of a research division in the labor department's [[Employment Standards Administration]]. In 1978, Conway became assistant director of the construction industry office in the Labor Management Services Administration, another Department of Labor agency.<ref name="IC1" />

In 1984, Conway was promoted to director of the office of regulatory analysis in the [[Occupational Safety and Health Administration]].  In that position, he was responsible for preparing [[Economic impact analysis|economic studies]], [[cost-benefit analysis]], and [[Cost-effectiveness analysis|cost effectiveness reports]] for the Department of Labor.  This work supported a wide range of occupational health and safety regulations developed by [[Federal government of the United States|Federal government]].<ref name="IC1" /><ref name="BL" /><ref name="RCC">Roukis, George S., Hugh Conway, and Bruce Charnov, [https://books.google.com/books?id=Bp8v9Kj_BWsC&pg=PA337&lpg=PA337&dq#v=onepage&q&f=false "About the editors"], ''Global Corporate Intelligence, Opportunities, Technologies, and Threats 1990's''], Quorum Books, Greenwood Publishing Group, Westport, Connecticut, 1990.</ref><ref name="NDU">"Dr. Hugh Conway", National Defense University faculty biographies, National Defense University, Fort McNair, Washington, District of Columbia, ca. 2001.</ref>

While working for the Department of Labor, Conway wrote articles for academic journals and edited several books.  In 1986, he co-authored an article on Kuwait's economic environment.<ref>Conway, Hugh and Ewan Anderson, "Kuwait's Labor Force: An Historical Perspective, Industrial Development, and Measures to Stabilize the Expatriate Labor Force", published in ''Workforce Management in the Arabian Peninsula'' (George S. Roukis and Patrick J. Montana editors), Greenwood Press, Westport, Connecticut, 1986, pp. 139-156.</ref>  In 1990, his book entitled ''Defense Economic Issues'' was published by the [[National Defense University Press]].<ref name="BL" /><ref>Conway, Hugh, [https://books.google.com/books/about/Defense_Economic_Issues.html?id=eg6TMQEACAAJ ''Defense Economic Issues''], National Defense University Press, Washington, District of Columbia, 1990.</ref>  That same year, he co-edited ''Global Corporate Intelligence, Opportunities, Technologies, and Threats 1990's'', which was published by [[Greenwood Publishing Group|Greenwood-Quoram Books]].<ref name="RCC" />  His article entitled "Medical Surveillance in United States Industry" was published in the ''Journal of Occupational Medicine'' in July 1993.<ref name="BL" />

== Industrial College of the Armed Forces ==

In the spring of 1994, Conway joined the Industrial College of the Armed Forces faculty as a professor of economics and the college's Department of Labor Chair.<ref name="IC1" /><ref name="BL" />  In 1999, Conway accepted a permanent appointment as a professor of economics at the college.  He taught core courses in both [[Macroeconomics|macro-economic]] and [[Microeconomics|micro-economics]] plus several economics-related elective courses including "Economics and Information Technology".<ref name="NDU" />  He also led the faculty team that taught the college's construction industry studies seminar.  This program included extensive travel across the United States and a two-week oversees study tour each year.  During these trips, Conway met with senior government officials, construction industry executives, and local labor leaders.<ref name="CI1">Conway, Hugh, Colin Dunn, and Gary Khalil, "Construction, A Report on the Industry"], Industrial College of the Armed Forces, National Defense University, Fort McNair, Washington, District of Columbia, June 2004.</ref><ref name="CI2">Conway, Hugh, Maureen Crandall, Ken Ryan, and Gary Khalil,  [http://s3.amazonaws.com/zanran_storage/www.ndu.edu/ContentPages/11151428.pdf "Construction, A Report on the Industry"], Industrial College of the Armed Forces, National Defense University, Fort McNair, Washington, District of Columbia, June 2005.</ref><ref name="ITI">Abbott, Gerald (editor), "Construction" (by Gary Berry, Hugh Conway, et al), [https://www.hsdl.org/?abstract&did=1699 ''In Touch with Industry – ICAF Industry Studies 1999'', Industrial College of the Armed Forces, National University Press, Washington, District of Columbia, pp. 5.1-5.22.]</ref><ref>Abbott, Gerald (editor), "Construction Industry Report 1996" (by Hugh Conway, David Price, et al), ''In Touch with Industry – ICAF Industry Studies 1996''], Industrial College of the Armed Forces, National University Press, Washington, District of Columbia, pp. 6.1-6.22.</ref>

In 1997, Conway wrote the section on World War II infrastructure construction projects for ''The Big L- American Logistics in World War II''.  The book was published by National Defense University Press.<ref name="BL" /><ref>Gropman, Alan, [http://www.ibiblio.org/hyperwar/USA/BigL/BigL-4.html "Building Victory's Foundation: Infrastructure"], ''The Big 'L' American Logistics in World War II'', National Defense University Press, Washington, District of Columbia, 1997, pp. 193-264.</ref>  He published academic articles in ''Monthly Labor Review''<ref>Conway, Hugh and Jens Svenson, [http://www.bls.gov/opub/mlr/1998/11/art3full.pdf "Occupational Injury and Illness Rates, 199296: Why They Fell"], ''Monthly Labor Review'', United States Bureau of Labor Statistics, Washington, District of Columbia, November 1998, pp. 38-58.</ref> and the ''Journal of Labor Research''.<ref>{{Cite journal | doi=10.1007/s12122-001-1002-2|title = Musculoskeletal disorders and productivity| journal=Journal of Labor Research| volume=22| pages=29–54|year = 2001|last1 = Conway|first1 = Hugh| last2=Svenson| first2=Jens| url=https://zenodo.org/record/1232884/files/article.pdf}}</ref>  Conway also edited the construction industry section for the ''In Touch with Industry'' report, published annually by the Industrial College of the Armed Forces.<ref name="CI1" /><ref name="CI2" /><ref name="ITI" />

Conway retired from his teaching post at the Industrial College of the Armed Forces in 2005.  Upon his retirement, he received the ''Joint Civilian Service Commendation Award'' from the [[United States Department of Defense]].  Today, he lives in [[Alexandria Virginia|Alexandria]], [[Virginia]].<ref>United States Office of Personnel Management, Notification of Personnel Action (Re: Conway, Hugh E.), Industrial College of the Armed Forces, National Defense University, Fort McNair, Washington, District of Columbia, 1 November 2005.</ref><ref>''Joint Civilian Service Commendation Award'' (presented to Hugh E. Conway), award citation, National Defense University, United States Department of Defense, Washington, District of Columbia, 1 October 2005.</ref>
