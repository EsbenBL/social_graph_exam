'''Bernardo Sorj''' (born September 1948, [[Montevideo]], [[Uruguay]]) is a [[Brazil]]ian [[social scientist]], retired [[professor]] of [[Sociology]] at the [[Federal University of Rio de Janeiro]]. He is Director of [[The Edelstein Center for Social Research]]<ref>[http://www.centroedelstein.org.br/English/ Edelstein Center for Social Research Website.]</ref> and of the ''Plataforma Democrática''  Project.<ref>[http://www.plataformademocratica.org/English/ Plataforma Democrática] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090426080534/http://www.plataformademocratica.org/English/ |date=April 26, 2009 }} {{in lang|en}}</ref>  He has published 30 books and more than 100 articles, on [[Latin America]]n political development, international relations, the social impact of new [[technologies]], [[social theory]] and [[Judaism]].

== Biography ==

Sorj begun his studies in Social Sciences in Uruguay and completed his B.A. and M.A. in [[History]] and [[Sociology]] from the [[University of Haifa]], [[Israel]], and received his Ph.D. in Sociology from the [[University of Manchester]] in [[England]].

He is a naturalized Brazilian, living in [[Brazil]] since 1976 . He was professor at the Department of [[Political Science]] at the [[Federal University of Minas Gerais]] and at the [[:pt:Instituto de Relações Internacionais da Pontifícia Universidade Católica do Rio de Janeiro|Institute for International Relations]] - [[Pontifical Catholic University of Rio de Janeiro]] and visiting professor and chair at many [[Europe]]an and [[North America]]n universities, including the ''Chaire Sérgio Buarque de Hollanda,'' at the [[:fr:Fondation Maison des sciences de l'homme|Fondation Maison des sciences de l’homme]],<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.chairesergiobuarque.msh-paris.fr/spip.php?article11 |title=Maison des sciences de l'homme. Chaire Sergio Buarque de Hollanda. Bernardo Sorj |access-date=2009-04-21 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110721013003/http://www.chairesergiobuarque.msh-paris.fr/spip.php?article11 |archive-date=2011-07-21 |url-status=dead }}</ref> in [[Paris]],  and the ''Cátedra Simón Bolívar'' of the [[:fr:Institut des hautes études d'Amérique latine|Institut des hautes études d'Amérique Latine]] - [[University of Paris III: Sorbonne Nouvelle]].<ref>[http://www.iheal.univ-paris3.fr/spip.php?rubrique606 ''Liste des professeurs invités sur cette chaire.'']</ref>

He is member of the board of several academic journals, advisor to scientific institutions and consultant to international organizations. He is also the coordinator of ''[[SciELO]] Latin American Social Sciences Journals English Edition'',<ref>[http://socialsciences.scielo.org/scielo.php/lng_es SciELO - Scientific Electronic Library Online Website ]{{failed verification|date=December 2020}}</ref> a site offering free access to 30 [[Latin American]] journals translated into [[English language|English]].  In 2005 he was elected Brazil's Man of Ideas.<ref>[http://jbonline.terra.com.br/jb/papel/cadernos/ideias/2005/12/30/joride20051230002.html [[Jornal do Brasil|Jornal do Brasil Online]], Dec 31, 2005.]{{dead link|date=October 2016 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>
