'''Mao Yushi''' ({{zh|c=茅于轼}}; born 14 January 1929 in [[Nanjing]], [[Jiangsu]]) is a Chinese [[economist]]. Mao graduated from [[Shanghai Jiao Tong University]] in 1950 and was [[Anti-Rightist Campaign|labeled a 'rightist']] in 1958. In 1986 Mao was a visiting scholar at [[Harvard University]] and in 1990 Mao was a senior lecturer at [[Queensland University]].<ref>http://finance.ifeng.com/people/detail/economist/maoyushi.shtml</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.macrochina.com.cn/prize/brt/maoyushi.shtml |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2011-06-25 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110616034346/http://www.macrochina.com.cn/prize/brt/maoyushi.shtml |archivedate=2011-06-16 }}</ref>

==Career==

He co-founded the Unirule Institute of Economics, which educated new and old generations of Chinese on the importance of private property, freedom of choice, voluntary exchange, rule of law, and other aspects of the free market economy, teaching how to transition away from central planning. On 4 May 2012 Mao Yushi was awarded the [[Cato Institute]]'s Milton Friedman Prize for Advancing Liberty for his work in [[classical liberalism]] and [[free-market economics]].<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://www.thechinatimes.com/online/2012/05/3350.html |title = Economist Mao Yushi Awarded $250,000 Milton Friedman Liberty Prize &#124; the China Times}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thechinatimes.com/chinese/online/2012/05/1437.html |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2012-05-05 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120508021316/http://www.thechinatimes.com/chinese/online/2012/05/1437.html |archivedate=2012-05-08 }}</ref> In October 2014 Beijing began a "crackdown on dissent" by banning the publication of his works.<ref>{{cite news|last1=Wee|first1=Sui-Lee|last2=Rajagopalan|first2=Megha|title=China detains scholar, bans books in crackdown on moderate voices|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-china-rights-idUSKCN0I20NU20141013|accessdate=14 October 2014|publisher=[[Reuters]]|date=13 October 2014}}</ref> In January 2017 they also shut down his Web site.<ref>[https://www.cato.org/blog/china-shuts-down-website-mao-yushi-friedman-prize-winner Cato Institute, Jan. 24, 2017]</ref> 

===Criticism of Mao Zedong===

Mao Yushi wrote an online column criticizing the [[Communism|communist]] and [[Totalitarianism|totalitarian]] policies of [[Mao Zedong]] in China. He was attacked by [[Maoism|Maoists]] in the country, who called for his arrest.<ref>[https://www.economist.com/node/18744533?story_id=18744533 The Economist]</ref><ref>[https://archive.today/20130127143648/http://poettpourri.jimdo.com/2012/07/01/ein-sadist-und-l%C3%BCstling-mao-yushi-entbl%C3%A4ttert-mao-zedong-und-kocht-chinas-ultralinke/ The Pöttpourri (in German)]</ref>
