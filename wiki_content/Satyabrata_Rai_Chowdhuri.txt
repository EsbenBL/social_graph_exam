'''Satyabrata Rai Chowdhuri''', [[Royal Asiatic Society|FRAS]] (1935â€“2016) was a [[political scientist]], [[Political history|political historian]] and [[international relations]] expert. 

He was a senior research fellow in international relations at the [[Institute of Commonwealth Studies]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://commonwealth.sas.ac.uk/fellowships/current-fellows.html|title=Current Fellows|publisher=Institute of Commonwealth Studies|accessdate=15 January 2011|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20110305052508/http://commonwealth.sas.ac.uk/fellowships/current-fellows.html|archivedate=5 March 2011}}</ref> [[University of London]], [[United Kingdom]], and an Emeritus Professor of Political Science at India's [[University Grants Commission (India)|University Grants Commission]]. Earlier he taught at the universities of [[University of Calcutta|Calcutta]], [[Rabindra Bharati University|Rabindra Bharati]], [[University of London|London]] and [[University of Oxford|Oxford]].

Rai Chowdhuri was a prolific writer with primary interests in nuclear issues and South Asian Politics. He was a columnist at [[Project Syndicate]], and [[Asia Times Online]], besides many news dailies across the globe. He was also known for his books [https://www.amazon.com/dp/1932705023 ''Nuclear Politics''] and [http://us.macmillan.com/leftisminindia19171947 ''Leftism in India''] among others.

His scholarship in [[South Asian Studies]] was honored in 2006 with the [[Royal Asiatic Society of Great Britain and Ireland|Fellowship of the Royal Asiatic Society]], the UK's highest society in [[Asian studies]].
