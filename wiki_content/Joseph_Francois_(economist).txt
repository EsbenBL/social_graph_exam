'''Joseph Francois''' is a professor of international economics at the [[University of Bern]], where he has taught since 2013.<ref>Prof. Dr. Joseph Francois [http://www.vwi.unibe.ch/about_us/people/prof_dr_francois_joseph/index_eng.html], www.vwi.unibe.ch. Retrieved 2018-02-06.</ref><ref>“Joseph Francois: Citations” [https://scholar.google.ch/citations?user=7cMgunAAAAAJ&hl=en&oi=sra], retrieved 2018-02-21.</ref> 
<ref>{{cite web |title=Joseph Francis Francois: Research Output |url=https://ideas.repec.org/e/pfr26.html |website=ideas.repec.org |publisher=Research Papers in Economics (REPEC) |access-date=6 July 2018}}</ref> Since 2015, he is also the Managing Director of the [[World Trade Institute]]. He is co-director of the European Trade Study Group,<ref>{{cite web |title=European Trade Study Group |url=http://www.etsg.org |website=etsg.org |publisher=European Trade Study Group |access-date=5 October 2018}}</ref> which he co-founded in 1999. He is a research fellow of the [[Centre for Economic Policy Research]] (CEPR)<ref name="Professor Joseph Francois">{{cite web |title=Professor Joseph Francois |url=https://cepr.org/active/researchers/contact.php?IDENT=117038 |website=www.cepr.org |publisher=Centre for Economic Policy Research |access-date=6 July 2018}}</ref> and an at-large board member of the [[GTAP|Global Trade Analysis Project (GTAP)]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Joseph Francois: Member Profile |url=https://www.gtap.agecon.purdue.edu/network/member_display.asp?UserID=115 |website=www.gtap.org |publisher=Global Trade Analysis Project |access-date=6 July 2018}}</ref>

== Research ==
===Areas of Research===
He has published on: the role of the service sector in trade, growth and development; regional economic integration;<ref name=EPROMISE>{{Cite news |title=Eastern Promise |url=https://www.economist.com/finance-and-economics/1997/04/10/eastern-promise |newspaper=The Economist |access-date=6 July 2018|date=1997-04-10 }}</ref> the [[WTO]]; and the social and environmental effects ([[Sustainable development|sustainability impacts]]) of globalization. His research on the economics of services has emphasized linkages between rising complexity in production and the rising share of producer services with growth, a distinct mechanism from the consumption-side productivity trap with consumer services known as ''[[Baumol's cost disease]]''.<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Francois |first1=J. |title=Producer services, scale, and the division of labor |journal=Oxford Economic Papers |date=1990 |volume=42 |issue=4 |pages=715–729 |doi=10.1093/oxfordjournals.oep.a041973 }}</ref><ref>{{cite journal |last1=Francois, J. |first1=and B. Hoekman |title=Services trade and policy |journal=Journal of Economic Literature |date=2010 |volume=48 |issue=4 |pages=642–92 |doi=10.1257/jel.48.3.642 |hdl=10419/73561 |s2cid=153378987 |url=https://wiiw.ac.at/services-trade-and-policy-dlp-2030.pdf }}</ref> His research also includes [[computational economics]] (including [[Computable general equilibrium|quantitative trade policy modeling]])<ref>{{cite book |last1=Francois, J.F. |first1=and K. Reinert (eds) |title=Applied methods for trade policy analysis: a handbook. |date=1997 |publisher=Cambridge University Press |isbn=9780521589970 |url=https://www.cambridge.org/gb/academic/subjects/economics/history-economic-thought-and-methodology/applied-methods-trade-policy-analysis-handbook?format=PB&isbn=9780521589970 |access-date=9 October 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite book |last1=Dixon, P.B. |first1= and D.W. Jorgensen (eds) |title=Handbook of Computable General Equilibrium Modeling SET, Vols. 1A and 1B |date=2013 |publisher=Elsevier |isbn=978-0-444-59568-3 |url=https://www.sciencedirect.com/handbook/handbook-of-computable-general-equilibrium-modeling/vol/1/suppl/C |access-date=9 October 2018}}</ref> as well as the role of [[Regionalism (international relations)|regional agreements]] and the [[World Trade Organization|multilateral system]] in reducing economic [[policy uncertainty]]{{refn|group=nb|His study (with [[Richard Baldwin (economist)|Richard Baldwin]] and [[Richard Portes]]) on the [[2004 enlargement of the European Union|''Eastern Enlargement of the European Union'']] emphasized the role of adopting the [[Acquis communautaire]] in reducing economic policy uncertainty in the East European countries joining the European Union. == Early life and education ==
He was born in [[Washington DC]] in 1961, and is the oldest of 5 children (Joseph, Marie, Michael, Monica, and [[Sister Susan Rose Francois|Susan]]). He is the son of [[Francis B. Francois|Francis Francois]] and Eileen Schmelzer. He was raised in [[Bowie, Maryland|Bowie]], [[Maryland]] and attended [[St. John's College High School]].<ref name=Iowa>{{cite book |last1=Francois |first1=Francis B. |title=Me? I'm from Iowa |date=2011 |publisher=Lulu Press |pages=87–88|isbn=978-1105258800 }}</ref> He received his [[Doctor of Philosophy|PhD]] from the [[University of Maryland]] in 1988, after receiving a [[Bachelor of Arts|B.A.]] with distinction in both economics and in history from the [[University of Virginia]] (UVA) in 1982 and an [[Master of Arts|M.A.]] in economics from UVA in 1983.<ref name=wiiwCV/>

== Career ==
He worked as economist at the U.S. [[Bureau of Labor Statistics]] from 1983-87.<ref name=wiiwCV>{{cite web |title=staff CV: Joseph F. Francois |url=https://wiiw.ac.at/files/staff/cv/CV-joseph-f-francois.pdf |website=wiiw.ac.at |publisher=Vienna Institute for International Economic Studies |access-date=9 October 2018}}</ref> He then moved to the [[USITC|US International Trade Commission]], where from 1987-1993 he started as a research economist and later became Chief of Research (at the age of 28), and then Acting Director of Economics with the USITC.<ref name=wiiwCV/> While at the USITC he was co-director of the 1992 USITC assessment of the [[NAFTA]],<ref>{{cite web |last1=Francois, J |first1=and C.R. Shiells |title=A FTA with Mexico and a NAFTA with Canada and Mexico |url=https://www.usitc.gov/publications/332/pub2508.pdf |website=www.usict.gov |publisher=US International Trade Commission, publication 2508, May 1992. |access-date=6 July 2018}}</ref> He was a research economist with the [[General Agreement on Tariffs and Trade|GATT]]/[[World Trade Organization|WTO]] from 1993-1996,<ref name=wiiwCV/> where he coordinated the GATT secretariat economic assessment of the [[Uruguay Round]] at the end of the Round.<ref>{{cite web |last1=GATT Secretariat |title=THE RESULTS OF THE URUGUAY ROUND: Market Access for Goods and Services: Overview of the Results |url=http://www.ub.edu/prometheus21/articulos/archivos/gatt.PDF |website=www.ub.edu |publisher=GATT Secretariat, Geneva, November 1994. |access-date=6 July 2018}}</ref> From 1996-2007 he was full Professor of International Economics at [[Erasmus University Rotterdam]] and research fellow of the [[Tinbergen Institute]].<ref name=wiiwCV/> He moved to Austria in 2007 as Professor of Economics at the [[Johannes Kepler University Linz]] from 2007-2013.<ref name=wiiwCV/> He took a chair in international economics at the [[University of Bern]] in 2013, and became managing director of the [[World Trade Institute]] in 2015. He consults for governments and international organisations, and periodically advises on the negotiation and impact of trade and investment agreements.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Ana Brito |first1=en Luís Villalobos |title=Portugal ganha mais de mil milhões com acordo comercial entre UE e EUA |url=https://www.publico.pt/2014/07/31/economia/noticia/portugal-ganha-mais-de-mil-milhoes-com-acordo-comercial-entre-ue-e-eua-1664868 |website=www.publico.pt |publisher=Público. 31 de Julho de 2014. |access-date=6 July 2018}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |last1=Francois |first1=Joseph|display-authors=etal |title=Reducing Transatlantic Barriers to Trade and Investment: An Economic Assessment |url=https://voxeu.org/content/reducing-transatlantic-barriers-trade-and-investment-economic-assessment |website=voxeu.org |publisher=Centre for Economic Policy Research. Report for the European Commission's Directorate General for Trade, 2013. |access-date=6 July 2018|date=2013-03-12 }}</ref>
