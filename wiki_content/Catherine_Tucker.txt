'''Catherine Tucker''' (born May 16, 1977) is the Sloan Distinguished Professor of Management at [[MIT Sloan]], where she is also chair of the PhD program. She is known for her research into the consequences of digital data for privacy, [[algorithmic bias]], digital health and online advertising. She is also a Research Associate at the [[NBER]] and cofounder of the Cryptoeconomics lab at MIT with [[Christian Catalini]].

== Biography ==
Catherine Tucker was born in [[Oxford]], and attended [[Merton College]] at the [[University of Oxford]],<ref name="Oxford">{{cite web | url=http://www.ox.ac.uk/gazette/1998-9/weekly/291098/coll.htm | title=Elections | publisher=Oxford University | work=Oxford University Gazette | accessdate=May 7, 2013 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081028093333/http://www.ox.ac.uk/gazette/1998-9/weekly/291098/coll.htm | archive-date=October 28, 2008 | url-status=dead }}</ref> studying [[Philosophy, Politics and Economics]]. She emigrated to the United States in 1999, and obtained a PhD in Economics from [[Stanford]], advised by [[Susan Athey]], [[Tim Bresnahan]] and Liran Einav. She is married and has four children.<ref name="Wicked Local">{{cite news | url=http://www.wickedlocal.com/medford/news/x165091159/Fairfield-St-family-celebrates-a-happy-Mother-s-Day | title=Fairfield St. Family Celebrates A Happy Mother's Day | date=2008-05-14 | accessdate=May 4, 2013| last=Escobar Coakley | first=Nell | newspaper=Medford Daily Transcript}}</ref> She is the niece of pioneering special effects make-up artist [[Christopher Tucker]].

== Research field ==
Tucker's research focuses on [[algorithmic bias]], the economics of [[privacy]], the [[economics of digitization]], and [[online advertising]]. Much of her publications have studied social media and its interactions with consumers. Tucker's research has evolved with the development of social media; her earlier publications focused on privacy<ref>{{Cite web | url=https://www.elsevier.com/books/handbook-of-media-economics-vol-1a/anderson/978-0-444-62721-6 |title = Handbook of Media Economics, vol 1A - 1st Edition}}</ref> and advertising,<ref>{{Cite journal | doi=10.1287/mksc.2017.1062| title=Advertising to Early Trend Propagators: Evidence from Twitter| journal=Marketing Science| volume=37| issue=2| pages=177–199| year=2018| last1=Lambrecht| first1=Anja| last2=Tucker| first2=Catherine| last3=Wiertz| first3=Caroline| url=http://lbsresearch.london.edu/822/1/twitter_combined.pdf| hdl=1721.1/122363| hdl-access=free}}</ref> and now also focus in the direction of algorithmic influence and consumer interactions with the blockchain.<ref>{{Cite journal |ssrn = 3199453|title = Antitrust and Costless Verification: An Optimistic and a Pessimistic View of the Implications of Blockchain Technology|date = 2018-06-19|last1 = Tucker|first1 = Catherine E.|last2 = Catalini|first2 = Christian}}</ref> She has published 67 scholarly papers in these related fields. Common outlets for Tucker's publications include Marketing Science,<ref>{{Cite journal |doi = 10.1287/mksc.1100.0583|title = Online Display Advertising: Targeting and Obtrusiveness|journal = Marketing Science|volume = 30|issue = 3|pages = 389–404|year = 2011|last1 = Goldfarb|first1 = Avi|last2 = Tucker|first2 = Catherine|hdl = 1721.1/67901|hdl-access = free}}</ref> Quantitative Marketing and Economics,<ref>{{Cite journal |doi = 10.1007/s11129-011-9109-0|title = Heterogeneity and the dynamics of technology adoption|journal = Quantitative Marketing and Economics|volume = 10|pages = 63–109|year = 2012|last1 = Ryan|first1 = Stephen P.|last2 = Tucker|first2 = Catherine|s2cid = 189953091|hdl = 1721.1/73195|hdl-access = free}}</ref> and the Journal of Marketing Research.<ref>{{Cite journal |jstor = 43832316|title = Social Networks, Personalized Advertising, and Privacy Controls|journal = Journal of Marketing Research|volume = 51|issue = 5|pages = 546–562|last1 = Tucker|first1 = Catherine E.|year = 2014|doi = 10.1509/jmr.10.0355|hdl = 1721.1/99170|s2cid = 11518615|hdl-access = free}}</ref><ref>https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/cf_dev/AbsByAuth.cfm?per_id=512675</ref> She has expertise in studying how can firms use digital data and machine learning to improve performance. <ref name="Methodology Link"<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://mitmgmtfaculty.mit.edu/cetucker/about/ | title=MIT Sloan Faculty: Catherine Tucker &#124; Methodology}}</ref>

== Career ==
Tucker teaches at the [[MIT Sloan School of Management]] and is chair of the MIT Sloan PhD Program.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://mitmgmtfaculty.mit.edu/cetucker/|title=MIT Sloan Faculty: Catherine Tucker &#124; Sloan Distinguished Professor of Management Science and Professor of Marketing}}</ref> Tucker is also a cofounder of the MIT Cryptoeconomics lab which analyzes blockchain applications.<ref name="Methodology Link" />

Tucker has given talks to the [[European Union]] Future of Privacy Forum Roundtable and the [[OECD]] Roundtable on Economics of Privacy. She has testified to the US Congress on [https://docs.house.gov/meetings/IF/IF17/20171129/106659/HHRG-115-IF17-Wstate-TuckerC-20171129.pdf algorithmic bias] and online [https://www.govinfo.gov/content/pkg/CHRG-112hhrg73961/pdf/CHRG-112hhrg73961.pdf privacy].<ref name="NYT">{{cite news|url=http://bits.blogs.nytimes.com/2011/09/15/less-web-tracking-means-less-effective-ads-researcher-says/|title=Less Web Tracking Means Less Effective Ads, Researcher Says|last=Sengupta|first=Somini|date=2011-09-15|newspaper=The New York Times|accessdate=May 7, 2013}}</ref> In 2011, she received a CAREER Award from the [[National Science Foundation]].<ref name="NBER">{{cite web | url=http://www.nber.org/reporter/2012number1/news.html | title=2011 Awards and Honors | publisher=National Bureau of Economic Research | work=NBER Reporter | accessdate=May 7, 2013}}</ref> In 2012, she was admitted as a Research Associate of the [[National Bureau of Economic Research]], and became the first woman to be awarded [[tenure]] in the MIT Marketing Department. In 2015, she received the Erin Anderson Award, honoring an "emerging research star...who has exceeded the normal expectations for someone of her rank in mentoring doctoral students or junior faculty members."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://executive.mit.edu/blog/mit-sloan-professor-catherine-tucker-receives-top-marketing-award|title=MIT Sloan Professor Catherine Tucker Receives Top Marketing Award {{!}} MIT Sloan Executive Education|website=executive.mit.edu|access-date=2019-02-12}}</ref> In 2018, she received the William O'Dell Award, for an "article ... that has made a significant contribution to marketing theory, practice or methods".<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://ama-academics.communityzero.com/elmar?go=6140530|title=AMA Academic Community - "ELMAR"|website=ama-academics.communityzero.com|access-date=2019-02-12}}</ref> Later that year Tucker went on to receive the 2018 INFORMS Society for Marketing Science Long Term Impact Award.<ref>17</ref> She then continued on with Duncan Simester to win the 2020 Weitz-Weiner-O'Dell Award from the American Marketing Association.<ref>{{cite web |title=Catherine Tucker |url=https://mitsloan.mit.edu/faculty/directory/catherine-tucker |website=MIT Management Sloan School |publisher=2020 MIT Sloan School of Management |access-date=10 December 2020}}</ref> Additionally, she was appointed a visiting fellow at [[All Souls College, Oxford|All Souls College]] at the [[University of Oxford]];<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.asc.ox.ac.uk/people/5/0/24|title=All Souls College Oxford|website=www.asc.ox.ac.uk|access-date=2019-02-12}}</ref> and cofounded the [https://ce.mit.edu/ Cryptoeconomics Lab at MIT.]
