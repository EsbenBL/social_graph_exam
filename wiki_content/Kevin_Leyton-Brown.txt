'''Kevin Leyton-Brown''' (born May 12, 1975) is a Professor of [[Computer Science]] at the [[University of British Columbia]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://cs.ubc.ca/~kevinlb |title=Kevin Leyton-Brown's Home Page |publisher=Cs.ubc.ca |date=2013-01-19 |accessdate=2013-02-20}}</ref>  He received his Ph.D. at [[Stanford University]] in 2003.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://genealogy.math.ndsu.nodak.edu/id.php?id=101069 |title=The Mathematics Genealogy Project - Kevin Leyton-Brown |publisher=Genealogy.math.ndsu.nodak.edu |date= |accessdate=2013-02-20}}</ref> He was the recipient of a 2014 [[NSERC]] E.W.R. Steacie Memorial Fellowship,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nserc-crsng.gc.ca/Prizes-Prix/Steacie-Steacie/Profiles-Profils/LeytonBrown-LeytonBrown_eng.asp |title=NSERC - E.W.R. Steacie Memorial Fellowships - Dr. Kevin Leyton-Brown |publisher=Nserc-crsng.gc.ca |date=2014-02-03 |accessdate=2014-08-17}}</ref> a 2013/14 Killam Teaching Prize,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://vpacademic.ubc.ca/killam-awards/killam-teaching-prize/award-winners/#20132014-0 |title=Killam Teaching Prize Winners - Office of the Provost and Vice-President Academic |publisher=Vpacademic.ubc.ca |date=2014-05-06 |accessdate=2014-08-17}}</ref> and a 2013 Outstanding Young Computer Science Researcher Prize from the Canadian Association of Computer Science.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://cacsaic.org/awards_2013 |title=Awards 2013 - CACS/AIC - Canadian Association of Computer Science |publisher=Cacsaic.ca |accessdate=2014-08-17}}</ref> 
Leyton-Brown co-teaches a popular game theory course on Coursera.org,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://game-theory-class.org |title=Game Theory Online |publisher=Game-theory-class.org |date= |accessdate=2013-02-20}}</ref> along with [[Matthew O. Jackson]] and [[Yoav Shoham]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://science.ubc.ca/news/680 |title=UBC's first MOOC attracts 130,000 registrants &#124 |publisher=Science.ubc.ca |date=2013-01-31 |accessdate=2013-02-20 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130214042038/http://science.ubc.ca/news/680 |archivedate=2013-02-14 }}</ref> Leyton-Brown serves as an associate editor for the ''Journal of Artificial Intelligence Research'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.jair.org/vol/mast46.html |title=JAIR Editorial Board |publisher=JAIR |date= |accessdate=2013-02-20}}</ref> the ''Artificial Intelligence'' journal,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.journals.elsevier.com/artificial-intelligence/editorial-board/ |title=Artificial Intelligence Editorial Board |publisher=Journals.elsevier.com |date= |accessdate=2013-02-20}}</ref> and ''ACM Transactions on Economics and Computation',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://teac.acm.org/editors.shtml |title=ACM Transactions on Economics and Computation Editorial Board |publisher=Teac.acm.org |accessdate=2013-02-20 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20130215061654/http://teac.acm.org/editors.shtml |archivedate=2013-02-15 }}</ref> and was program chair for the ACM Conference on Electronic Commerce in 2012.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.sigecom.org/ec12/chairs.html |title=13th ACM Conference on Electronic Commerce |publisher=Sigecom.org |date= |accessdate=2013-02-20}}</ref>
Leyton-Brown and coauthors have received the IJCAI-JAIR Best Paper Prize<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.jair.org/bestpaper.html |title=IJCAI-JAIR Best Paper Award Recipients |publisher=JAIR |date=2012-07-24 |accessdate=2013-02-20}}</ref> and numerous medals in international SAT competitions (2003â€“12).<ref>{{cite web|author=SATComp Organizing committee |url=http://www.satcompetition.org |title=International SAT Competitions |publisher=Satcompetition.org |date= |accessdate=2013-02-20}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://baldur.iti.kit.edu/SAT-Challenge-2012/results.html |title=SAT Challenge 2012 |date= |accessdate=2013-02-20}}</ref>

Leyton-Brown's research is at the intersection of [[computer science]] and [[microeconomics]], addressing computational problems in economic contexts and incentive issues in [[multiagent systems]]. He also studies the application of [[machine learning]] to the automated design and analysis of algorithms for solving hard computational problems.
