'''Gershon Ben-Shakhar''' ('''גרשון בן שחר'''; born May 25, 1942) is an Israeli [[psychologist]]. He served as President of the [[Open University of Israel]].<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://www.openu.ac.il/personal_sites/gershon-ben-shakhar/|title=Prof. Gershon Ben-Shakhar|website=www.openu.ac.il}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=vgEoAQAAIAAJ&q=Gershon+Ben-Shakhar&dq=Gershon+Ben-Shakhar&hl=en&newbks=1&newbks_redir=0&sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwiQ2qHWmMvnAhVC1VkKHb84DzsQ6AEwGnoECB8QAg|title=Yearbook of Science and the Future|date= 1989|work=Encyclopaedia Britannica|via=Google Books}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://reason.com/2018/10/03/lie-detectors-arent/|title='Lie Detectors' Do Not Detect Lies|date=October 3, 2018|work=Reason}}</ref>

==Academic career==
Gershon Ben-Shakhar earned a B.A. in Psychology and Statistics (1966), an M.A. in Psychology (1970), and a Ph.D. in Psychology (1975) from [[The Hebrew University of Jerusalem]].<ref name="auto"/><ref name="auto1">{{Cite web|url=http://education.academy.ac.il/Index2/Entry.aspx?nodeId=1109&entryId=20878|title=היזמה למחקר יישומי בחינוך - Members|website=education.academy.ac.il}}</ref> He was a Post-Doctoral Fellow at [[Northwestern University]] in [[Evanston, Illinois]], from 1975 to 1976.<ref name="auto"/>

Ben-Shakhar taught in the Department of Psychology at The Hebrew University of Jerusalem from 1981 on, ultimately as a Professor and for a time as the Chair of the Department and the Dean of the Faculty of Social Sciences.<ref name="auto"/><ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=LV4vAQAAIAAJ&q=Gershon+Ben-Shakhar&dq=Gershon+Ben-Shakhar&hl=en&newbks=1&newbks_redir=0&sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwiQ2qHWmMvnAhVC1VkKHb84DzsQ6AEwEHoECBQQAg|title=Polygraph|date=February 12, 1997|publisher=American Polygraph Association|via=Google Books}}</ref><ref name="auto1"/>

In 2003 he became President of the [[Open University of Israel]], succeeding [[Eliahu Nissim]].<ref name="auto"/><ref name="auto1"/> 
==Published works==
Along with [[John J. Furedy]] he wrote the book ''Theories and Applications in the Detection of Deception: A psychophysiological and international perspective'' (New York: [[Springer-Verlag]], 1990).<ref name="auto"/><ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=GGAcBQAAQBAJ&printsec=frontcover&dq=Gershon+Ben-Shakhar&hl=en&newbks=1&newbks_redir=0&sa=X&ved=2ahUKEwiQ2qHWmMvnAhVC1VkKHb84DzsQ6AEwAHoECAEQAg#v=onepage&q=Gershon+Ben-Shakhar&f=false|title=Theories and Applications in the Detection of Deception: A Psychophysiological and International Perspective|first1=Gershon|last1=Ben-Shakhar|first2=John J.|last2=Furedy|date=2012|publisher=Springer Science & Business Media|via=Google Books}}</ref>

==Awards and recognition==
In 2011 he was an [[EMET Prize]] Laureate.<ref name="auto1"/>
