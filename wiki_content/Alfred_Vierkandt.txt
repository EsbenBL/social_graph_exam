'''Alfred Vierkandt''' (4 June 1867 &ndash; 24 April 1953) was a [[Germany|German]] [[sociologist]], [[ethnographer]],  [[social psychologist]], [[social philosopher]] and [[philosopher of history]]. He is known for a broad and [[Phenomenology (psychology)|phenomenological]] ''Gesellschaftslehre'' promulgated in the 1920s, and for his [[formal sociology]].

Vierkandt was born in [[Hamburg]].  He first studied science and philosophy at [[Leipzig University]]. He habilitated at [[Braunschweig|Brunswick]]. He was first at ''[[Dozent]]'' in ethnology, becoming eventually in 1913 Professor of Sociology at the [[University of Berlin]]. He was one of the founders of the [[Deutsche Gesellschaft für Soziologie]], in 1909. He was made to retire in 1934. He died, aged 85, in [[Berlin]].
