'''Abdellah Hammoudi''' (born in 1945) is a Moroccan anthropologist, ethnographer, and emeritus professor of anthropology at [[Princeton University]].

== Biography ==
Abdellah was born in [[Kalaat Sraghna]] in 1945.<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=Am3OCgAAQBAJ|title=The Hajj: Pilgrimage in Islam|last=Tagliacozzo|first=Eric|last2=Toorawa|first2=Shawkat M.|publisher=Cambridge University Press|year=2015|isbn=9781107030510|pages=253|language=en}}</ref> He received a bachelor's degree in philosophy from the Faculty of Letters at [[University of Mohammed V]], and, at the same time, a degree in sociology from the Institute of Sociology.<ref>{{Cite magazine|last=El Mansour|first=Mohamed|last2=Monjib|first2=Maâti|title=Abdellah Hammoudi : "Le printemps arabe reviendra"|url=https://zamane.ma/fr/abdellah-hammoudi-%E2%80%89le-printemps-arabe-reviendra%E2%80%89/|magazine=[[Zamane]]|issue=57-58}}</ref> He obtained his doctorate from the [[University of Paris 1 Pantheon-Sorbonne|Pantheon-Sorbonne University]] in 1977. From 1972 to 1989, he worked as a Professor at the Agronomic Institute of the Mohammed V University in Rabat. Before moving to the United States of America as a Faisal Visiting Professor for Anthropology at Princeton University in 1989, and he joined permanently faculty in 1991, a post which he held until his retirement on 1 July 2016 when he was given the title emeritus professor. He was the Founding Director and served for over ten years as Director of the University's Institute for the Transregional Study of the Contemporary Middle East, North Africa, and Central Asia.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://dof.princeton.edu/about/clerk-faculty/emeritus/abdellah-hammoudi|title=Abdellah Hammoudi {{!}} Dean of the Faculty|website=dof.princeton.edu|access-date=2019-05-26}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.iea-nantes.fr/en/abdellah-hammoudi/|title=Abdellah Hammoudi - Nantes Institute for Advanced Study Foundation|website=www.iea-nantes.fr|access-date=2019-05-26}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://anthropology.princeton.edu/people/emeritus-faculty/abdellah-hammoudi|title=Abdellah Hammoudi {{!}} Anthropology@Princeton|website=anthropology.princeton.edu|access-date=2019-05-26}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.lettre-ulysses-award.org/authors05/hammoudi.html|title=Abdellah Hammoudi, Morocco|website=Lettre ulysses Award for the Art of Reportage|access-date=2019-05-26}}</ref>

== Awards ==
In 1998, he was awarded a [[Guggenheim Fellowship]] from the [[John Simon Guggenheim Memorial Foundation]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.gf.org/fellows/all-fellows/abdellah-hammoudi/|title=Abdellah Hammoudi|website=John Simon Guggenheim Memorial Foundation|language=en-US|access-date=2019-05-26}}</ref>

In 2005, he won the second Prize [[Lettre Ulysses Award]] for the "Art of Reportage", for his non-fiction work entitled ''Une saison à la Mecque: récit de pélerinage''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.lettre-ulysses-award.org/authors05/winners2005.html|title=Winners 2005|website=Lettre ulysses Award for the Art of Reportage|access-date=2019-05-26}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://eng.babelmed.net/article/6198-moroccan-writer-abdellah-hammoudi-second-prize-of-the-lettre-ulysses-award-2005/|title=Moroccan Writer Abdellah Hammoudi, Second Prize of the Lettre Ulysses Award 2005|date=2005-10-20|website=[[Babelmed]]|language=en|access-date=2019-05-27}}</ref>
