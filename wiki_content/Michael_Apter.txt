'''Michael J. Apter''' (born 1939) is a British [[psychologist]] who was born in England and grew up in Bristol.<ref>{{cite book|last1=Chapman|first1=A.J.|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=KzA6s7KZo-MC|title=Biographical Dictionary of Psychology|last2=Conroy|first2=W.|last3=Sheehy|first3=N.|date=2002|publisher=Routledge|isbn=9780415285612|location=|pages=18|access-date=2020-12-30}}</ref> He was educated at [[Clifton College]] (1965) and at [[Bristol University]] where he gained both his Bachelor of Science degree and his Doctorate in Psychology in 1965,<ref>{{cite web |last1=Apter |first1=Michael |title=Curriculum Vitae |url=http://michaelapter.net/wp-content/uploads/2016/04/Curriculum-Vitae-2016.pdf}}</ref> having also spent a doctoral year at [[Princeton University]]. He taught for twenty years at [[Cardiff University]] in [[Wales]] and has since held invited positions at [[Purdue University]], the [[University of Chicago]], [[Yale University]], [[University of Toulouse]],<ref>{{cite web |title=Michael J. Apter |url=https://oneworld-publications.com/michael-j-apter.html |website=Oneworld Publications |access-date=December 18, 2020}}</ref> and [[Georgetown University]].<ref>{{cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=Human Resources|url=https://hr.georgetown.edu/apter/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-11-15|website=Georgetown University}}</ref> He also taught at [[Northwestern University]] where he received a teaching award.<ref>{{cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=About|url=http://michaelapter.net/about-mjapter/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2020-12-03|website=Michael Apter}}</ref> He has held visiting positions at several additional universities and is a chartered psychologist and fellow of the [[British Psychological Society]].<ref>{{cite web|last1=Perks|first1=Julie|date=|title=Oral History Project|url=https://archives.bps.org.uk/Record.aspx?src=CalmView.Catalog&id=AUD%2F002%2FOHP+58|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2021-01-06|website=History of Psychology Centre|publisher=The British Psychological Society}}</ref>
