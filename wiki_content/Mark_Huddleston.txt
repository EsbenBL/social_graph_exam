'''Mark W. Huddleston''' was the 19th President of the [[University of New Hampshire]] (UNH), having been elected to that position on April 18, 2007.<ref>"Dr. Mark Huddleston Elected the 19th President of the University of New Hampshire by the USNH Board of Trustees," [http://www.unh.edu/news/cj_nr/2007/apr/mc18huddleston.cfm] accessed 1/1/2010.</ref>

==Biography==
Huddleston received his bachelor's degree in [[political science]] from the [[University at Buffalo, The State University of New York]] in 1972. He earned his master's and doctoral degrees in political science from the [[University of Wisconsin–Madison]] in 1973 and 1978 respectively. He has worked extensively in [[Bosnia and Herzegovina|Bosnia]] and [[Kazakhstan]] for [[USAID]] and the [[Organization for Security and Co-operation in Europe]] in Europe. He also has managed programs for the [[United States Information Agency]] in [[Slovenia]], [[Mexico]], [[Botswana]], [[South Africa]], and [[Zimbabwe]]. He has published several articles on consensus building in higher education, and several books on the American [[civil service]].  Huddleston took up the position at UNH after stepping away from his post as the 15th president of [[Ohio Wesleyan University]].

Huddleston specializes in [[public administration]], a field in which he has published widely, focusing on the senior [[Federal government of the United States|federal]] career service and a variety of [[international relations|international issues]]. Before going to Ohio Wesleyan, Huddleston served in the faculty of the [[University of Delaware]] for 24 years, ultimately as the Dean of the College of Arts and Sciences.<ref name=Huddleston>[http://www.udel.edu/PR/UDaily/2004/huddleston061204.html Mark Huddleston named president of Ohio Wesleyan University]. ''University of Delaware Daily'' (June 12, 2004). Retrieved on 2006-12-02.</ref> Huddleston has been an active consultant for both the [[U.S. government]] and international organizations in the past. He worked previously in the [[Balkans]], southern [[Africa]], and central and southeast [[Asia]]. He gained [[international development]] experience in [[Bosnia and Herzegovina|Bosnia]] as an advisor on rebuilding financial and administrative infrastructures following the [[Dayton Accords]]. Prior to assuming his current position, Huddleston served as the President of [[Ohio Wesleyan University]], located in [[Delaware, Ohio]], from 2004 to 2007.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www1.udel.edu/PR/UDaily/2004/huddleston061204.html|title=Mark Huddleston named president of Ohio Wesleyan University|website=www1.udel.edu|access-date=2018-11-19}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.dispatch.com/article/20070406/NEWS/304069894|title=Ohio Wesleyan president might leave for New Hampshire college|work=The Columbus Dispatch|access-date=2018-11-19|language=en}}</ref>

After taking the role in 2007, Huddleston received a vote of no confidence by the faculty in 2011. Huddleston was the highest paid public employee in the state of New Hampshire in 2016 with a salary of $492,800. Huddleston earned $385,000 in base pay during the 2013-14 year — plus an additional $94,710 in bonus pay, his total compensation was $479,710.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.nhpr.org/post/unh-names-jim-dean-jr-next-president#stream/0|title=UNH Names Jim Dean Jr. as Next President|last=Staff|first=NHPR|access-date=2018-11-19|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://nhpr.org/post/unh-president-mark-huddleston-defends-salary-rates-performance-based-pay#stream/0]|title=UNH President Mark Huddleston Defends Salary Rates,Performance-Based Pay|last=Staff|first=NHPR}}</ref>

On May 3, 2017, the university announced that Huddleston would retire from his position at the end of the 2017-2018 school year.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.unh.edu/unhtoday/news/release/2017/05/03/unh-president-mark-w-huddleston-retire-2018|title=UNH President Mark W. Huddleston to Retire in 2018|date=2017-05-03|work=UNH Today|access-date=2018-11-19|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.fosters.com/news/20180220/huddleston-gives-last-state-of-unh-address|title=Huddleston gives last State of UNH address|last=Doyle|first=John|work=fosters.com|access-date=2018-11-19|language=en}}</ref> It was announced in the spring of 2018 that James W. Dean Jr. would succeed Huddleston as President, having previously served as executive vice chancellor and provost at [[University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill]], where he is also a professor of [[organizational behavior]].
