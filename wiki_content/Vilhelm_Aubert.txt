'''Johan Vilhelm Aubert''' (7 June 1922 – 19 July 1988) was an influential [[Norway|Norwegian]] [[sociologist]]. He was a professor at the [[Faculty of Law, University of Oslo]] from 1963 to 1971 and at the [[Department of Sociology and Human Geography, University of Oslo|Department of Sociology]] from 1971 to 1988. He co-founded the [[Norwegian Institute for Social Research]] already in 1950, and has been labelled the "[[List of persons considered father or mother of a field|father of]] Norwegian sociology". In his early life he was a member of the [[Norwegian resistance movement|anti-Nazi resistance]] group [[XU]], and while later involved on the radical wing of the [[Norwegian Labour Party|Labour Party]], he edited the newspaper ''[[Orientering]]''.

==Early career==
Vilhelm Aubert was born in [[Oslo|Kristiania]] in 1922.<ref name=kalleberg>{{cite journal | last=Kalleberg | first=Ragnvald | author-link=Ragnvald Kalleberg |year=2000 |title='The Most Important Task of Sociology is to Strengthen and Defend Rationality in Public Discourse': On the Sociology of Vilhelm Aubert |journal=Acta Sociologica |volume=43 |issue=4 |pages=399–411 |url=http://www.iss.uio.no/instituttet/ansatte/ragnvald_pdfs/2000_most_important_task_of_sociology.pdf |access-date=2 December 2008 | doi=10.1177/000169930004300412| s2cid=143116226 }} {{Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot}}</ref> He was the older brother of mathematician [[Karl Egil Aubert]], born 1924.<ref name=snl>{{cite encyclopedia |year=2007 |title=Aubert, (Johan) Vilhelm |encyclopedia=[[Store norske leksikon]] |publisher=Kunnskapsforlaget |url=http://www.snl.no/article.html?id=451989 }}{{Dead link|date=July 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref> Vilhelm Aubert enrolled at the [[University of Oslo]] in 1940, the same year as Norway was [[Operation Weserübung|invaded by Germany]] as a part of the [[Second World War]]. Aubert became a member of the [[Norwegian resistance movement|illegal]] intelligence organization [[XU]].<ref name=kalleberg/>

Aubert finally graduated with the [[cand.jur.]] degree in 1946. He then lived in the United States for two years, studying [[sociology]] and [[psychology]] at [[Columbia University|Columbia]] and [[University of Berkeley|Berkeley]].<ref name=kalleberg/><ref name=obit/> After returning to Norway, he was instrumental in the foundation and consolidation of [[social science]] research in Norway, a still fledgling field.<ref name=obit>{{cite news|title=Vilhelm Aubert er død (obituary)|last=Eckhoff|first=Torstein|date=22 July 1988|work=Aftenposten}}</ref> He was a joint founder of the [[Norwegian Institute for Social Research]] (ISF, or ''Institutt for samfunnsforsking''), an [[independent research institute|independent]] [[research institute]] in [[Oslo]], along with [[Arne Næss]], [[Eirik Rinde]], and [[Stein Rokkan]] in 1950.<ref>{{cite web | url = http://www.samfunnsforskning.no/page/About_ISF/Menu_About_ISF/7979/8038 | title = History of the ISF | access-date = 28 April 2007 | last = Beer | first = Nicolai | work = Institute for Social Research (official site) |archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20070803230017/http://www.samfunnsforskning.no/page/About_ISF/Menu_About_ISF/7979/8038 <!-- Bot retrieved archive --> |archive-date = 3 August 2007}}</ref>

Aubert was a member of the [[Norwegian Labour Party|Labour Party]] in his younger days. Situated on its left wing, he co-published the pamphlet ''Tenk en gang til. Tanker om fred og nedrustning'' in 1952,<ref name=pax>{{cite encyclopedia |year=1976 |title=Aubert, Vilhelm |encyclopedia=[[Pax leksikon]] |publisher=Kunnskapsforlaget |url=http://mediabase1.uib.no/paxlex/alfabetet/a/a18.html |access-date=2009-12-29 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120315115330/http://mediabase1.uib.no/paxlex/alfabetet/a/a18.html |archive-date=2012-03-15 |url-status=dead }}</ref> and was among the founders of the newspaper ''[[Orientering]]''. He was the editor-in-chief of the newspaper for some time.<ref name=obit2>{{cite news|title=Vilhelm Aubert er død (obituary)|author=Norwegian News Agency|author-link=Norwegian News Agency|date=20 July 1988}}</ref> The persons in and around this newspaper were excluded from the Labour Party in 1960,<ref name=snl/> following a turbulent existence as an internal party opposition, especially in foreign policy issues. Some of the excluded members went on to found the [[Socialist People's Party (Norway)|Socialist People's Party]],{{Citation needed|date=December 2008}} whereas Aubert left partisan politics to concentrate on an academic career.<ref name=snl/> However, he continued his opposition towards [[nuclear arms]].<ref name=snl/><ref name=obit/>

==Later career==
He took the doctor's degree in 1954, with the thesis ''Straffens sosiale funksjon'' (The Social Function of Punishment), which was also selected for the [[Norwegian Sociology Canon]] in 2009–2011. In it, he discussed the preventive nature of laws and punishment.<ref name=snl/> In the same year he was hired as a lecturer at the [[University of Oslo]].<ref name=obit2/> He was promoted to professor of the [[sociology of law]] in 1963.<ref name=snl/> This institution was, and still is, a part of the Faculty of Law.<ref name=obit/> Books in the field of sociology of law include ''Likhet og rett'' (1963), ''Rettssosiologi'' (1968) and ''Rettens sosiale funksjon'' (1976).<ref name=snl/> ''Continuity and Development in Law and Society'' was published posthumously in 1989.<ref name=kalleberg/>

He later moved to the Department of Sociology at the Faculty of Social Sciences at [[Blindern]], as a professorship in general sociology was established in 1971. His textbook ''Sosiologi'', published in 1964, was the authoritative introduction to sociology in Norway for many years. His book ''The Hidden Society'' from 1965 was inspired by [[symbolic interactionism]] and the [[Chicago school (sociology)|Chicago school]].<ref name=snl/>

==Death and legacy==
Aubert died in July 1988 during a hiking trip.<ref name=obit2/>

He held an [[honorary degree]] at the Faculty of Law of the [[University of Edinburgh]], issued in 1971.<ref name=obit2/> He has been credited for his contributions to the sociology of law, and some have labelled him the "[[List of persons considered father or mother of a field|father of]] Norwegian sociology" in general.<ref name=obit2/>

Later researchers have noted his tendency to stress the importance of [[Norm (sociology)|norms]].<ref>{{cite book|last=Aakvaag|first=Gunnar C.|title=Moderne sosiologisk teori|publisher=Abstrakt forlag|location=Oslo|year=2008|pages=39|isbn=978-82-7935-219-8}}</ref> He has been criticized for maintaining a thin line between research and activism in some of his works.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.klassekampen.no/kontakt_oss/51031/article/item/null|title=Fra forskere til aksjonister|last=Haugen|first=Karin|date=11 February 2008|work=Klassekampen|access-date=2 December 2008|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110717134851/http://www.klassekampen.no/kontakt_oss/51031/article/item/null|archive-date=17 July 2011|url-status=dead|df=dmy-all}}</ref> Aubert's employment of [[action research]] has been attributed to his left-wing political stance.<ref name=pax/>
