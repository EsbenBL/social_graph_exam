'''Zeine Ould Zeidane''' ({{lang-ar|الزين ولد زيدان}}) (born 1966)<ref name=Prend>[http://www.ami.mr/fr/articles/2007/avril/21/3.html "Le nouveau Premier ministre prend fonction"], Agence Mauritanienne d'Information, April 21, 2007 {{in lang|fr}}.</ref> is a [[Mauritania]]n [[economist]] and [[politician]]. He placed third as a candidate in the [[2007 Mauritanian presidential election|March 2007 presidential election]], and he subsequently served as Prime Minister from April 2007 to May 2008.

== Biography ==
Born in [[Tamchekett]], Zeidane studied in [[Nouakchott]], then at the [[University of Nice]], in France. He taught briefly at the University of Nice before returning to Mauritania, where he taught at the [[University of Nouakchott]] before entering banking.<ref name=Prend/><ref name=Palais>[http://www.apanews.net/spip.php?page=show_article&id_article=21990 "Zeine Ould Zeidane veut troquer le fauteuil de la Banque centrale contre celui du Palais présidentiel"], African Press Agency, February 24, 2007 {{in lang|fr}}.</ref> In 2000, he was posted to the [[World Bank]],<ref name=Palais/> while he later served as economic Counsel to the [[President of Mauritania]], [[Maaouya Ould Sid'Ahmed Taya]].<ref name=Prend/><ref name=Palais/> On July 26, 2004, he was appointed by [[Maaouya Ould Sid'Ahmed Taya|Ould Taya]] as Governor of the [[Central Bank of Mauritania]].<ref>[http://www.ami.mr/fr/Archives2004/7/26/2.htm "Nomination d'un nouveau gouverneur de la Banque Centrale de Mauritanie"], AMI, June 26, 2004 {{in lang|fr}}.</ref> He was virtually the only minister to maintain his post following the coup of August 2005,<ref name=Palais/><ref name=BBC>[https://www.bbc.co.uk/french/specials/164_mauritania_elex/page5.shtml Zeine Ould Zeidane], BBCAfrique.com {{in lang|fr}}.</ref> leaving the position in September 2006 because of his presidential candidacy.<ref name=Palais/>

Zeidane announced his candidacy on December 18, 2006.<ref>[http://www.ami.mr/fr/articles/2006/decembre/18/35.html "M. Zein Ould Zeidane annonce sa candidature aux élections présidentielles"], Agence Mauritanienne d'Information, December 18, 2006 {{in lang|fr}}.</ref> In the presidential election, which was held on March 11, 2007, he was the youngest candidate,<ref name=Palais/><ref name=BBC/> standing as an [[independent (politician)|independent]].<ref name=BBC/> He took third place in the election with 15.28% of the votes cast.<ref>[http://www.ami.mr/fr/articles/2007/mars/15/5.html "Le conseil constitutionnel proclame les résultats du premier tour de l'élection présidentielles du 11 mars 2007"], Agence Mauritanienne d'Information, March 15, 2007 {{in lang|fr}}.</ref> In the election, Zeidane was considered by some to be the candidate favored by exiled former President Ould Taya.<ref name=Taya>[http://www.jeuneafrique.com/jeune_afrique/article_jeune_afrique.asp?art_cle=LIN11037ouldtenadie0 "Ould Taya vote Ould Zeidane"], Jeuneafrique.com, March 11, 2007 {{in lang|fr}}.</ref> On March 17, Zeidane announced his support for [[Sidi Ould Cheikh Abdallahi]] in the second round of the election.<ref>Ibrahima Sylla, [http://www.int.iol.co.za/index.php?set_id=1&click_id=86&art_id=nw20070318235001735C417182 "Abdallahi receives boost ahead of polls"], Reuters (''IOL''), March 19, 2007.</ref>

Abdallahi won the second round, held on March 25, and on April 20, one day after he took office as President, Abdallahi named Zeidane as Prime Minister.<ref>[http://www.jeuneafrique.com/fluxafp/fil_info.asp?art_cle=36374 "Mauritanie: Zeine Ould Zeidane nommé Premier ministre"], AFP (Jeuneafrique.com), April 20, 2007 {{in lang|fr}}.</ref> Zeidane took office on April 21.<ref name=Prend/> Zeidane's new government was named on April 28; it included 28 members<ref>[http://apanews.net/spip.php?page=show_article&id_article=32298 "New Mauritania govt forms first post-transition cabinet"], African Press Agency, April 28, 2007.</ref> and was composed of technocrats who were largely considered obscure figures. Three of the ministers appointed were members of the [[People's Progressive Alliance (Mauritania)|People's Progressive Alliance]] (led by [[Messaoud Ould Boulkheir]], another presidential candidate who backed Abdallahi in the second round).<ref>[http://www.jeuneafrique.com/fluxafp/fil_info.asp?reg_id=0&art_cle=36531 "Mauritanie: formation d'un nouveau gouvernement"], AFP (Jeuneafrique.com), April 28, 2007 {{in lang|fr}}.</ref>

Zeidane presented the resignation of his government on May 6, 2008; Abdallahi accepted the resignation<ref>[http://www.ami.mr/fr/articles/2008/Mai/06/16.html "Le Premier ministre Zeine ould Zeidane présente la démission du gouvernement"], AMI, May 6, 2008 {{in lang|fr}}.</ref> and appointed [[Yahya Ould Ahmed El Waghef]] to succeed him on the same day.<ref>[http://www.jeuneafrique.com/fluxafp/fil_info.asp?art_cle=45534 "Mauritanie: démission du Premier ministre Zeine Ould Zeidane"], AFP (Jeuneafrique.com), May 6, 2008 {{in lang|fr}}.</ref><ref>[http://apanews.net/apa.php?page=show_article_eng&id_article=62816 "Mauritania: Ould Ahmed El Waghev becomes new Premier"], African Press Agency, May 6, 2008.</ref>
