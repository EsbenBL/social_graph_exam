'''Lara M. Brown''' is an American [[Political science|political scientist]] and the director of the [[The Graduate School of Political Management|Graduate School of Political Management]] (GSPM) at  [[George Washington University]] since 2016.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://gspm.gwu.edu/dr-lara-brown|title=Dr. Lara Brown {{!}} Graduate School of Political Management {{!}} The George Washington University|website=gspm.gwu.edu|access-date=2018-11-26}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.abcactionnews.com/news/national/after-midterms-2020-presidential-electiontop-of-mind-especially-for-democrats|title=2020 president election top of mind post-midterm|date=2018-11-08|publisher=WFTS|access-date=2018-12-13}}</ref>

== Early life and education ==
Brown was born and grew up in [[California]]. She obtained a BA, MA and PhD in political sciences from the [[University of California, Los Angeles]]. Subsequently she also obtained an MA in American politics and public policy from the [[University of Arizona]].<ref name=":0" />

== Career ==
Brown started her career in academics and eventually served as an assistant professor at Villanova University.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www1.villanova.edu/villanova/artsci/psc/facstaff/biodetail.html|title=Faculty and Staff {{!}} Villanova University|website=www1.villanova.edu|access-date=2018-11-26}}</ref> She then served as an education policy and public affairs consultant in Silicon Valley and Los Angeles for some time and later returned into academics, also serving in the Department of Education in President Bill Clinton's administration.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://gwtoday.gwu.edu/lara-brown-named-interim-director-gspm|title=Lara Brown Named Interim Director of GSPM|website=GWTODAY|date=2 May 2016|access-date=2018-11-26}}</ref>

=== George Washington University ===
In 2013, Brown joined the [[The Graduate School of Political Management|Graduate School of Political Management]] as an associate professor and director for the Political Management Program.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.pbs.org/to-the-contrary/panelists/35/brown|title=To The Contrary Panelists {{!}} To The Contrary|publisher=PBS|access-date=2018-11-26}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.politico.com/story/2015/05/social-media-hillary-clinton-ted-cruz-study-118378|title=Study finds Clinton, Cruz dominate social media race|work=Politico|access-date=2018-11-26}}</ref> In 2016, she was appointed as Interim Director of GSPM, then, in 2017, named as its new Director.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.cbsnews.com/news/wrapping-our-heads-around-trumps-use-of-executive-actions/|title=What are executive actions?|last1=January 26|first1=Tim Perry CBS News|last2=2017|publisher=CBS News|access-date=2018-12-13|last3=Pm|first3=4:42}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://gwtoday.gwu.edu/lara-brown-named-interim-director-gspm|title={{!}} GW Today {{!}} The George Washington University|website=gwtoday.gwu.edu|access-date=2018-11-26}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://gwtoday.gwu.edu/gspm-names-new-director|title={{!}} GW Today {{!}} The George Washington University|website=gwtoday.gwu.edu|access-date=2018-11-26}}</ref>

Brown served on the board of directors of [[The New Agenda]], an organization focused on advancing women rights.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://vimeo.com/75405719|title=Lara M Brown Media Reel|via=Vimeo|access-date=2018-11-26}}</ref>
