'''Rogelio Ramírez de la O''' is an [[economist]] based in [[Mexico City]]. He is designated as finance secretary of Mexico.{{Citation needed|date=June 2021}} Director and sole partner of Ecanal S.A.,<ref>{{cite news |title=The Mexico Question |url=https://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9E0CE7DA103CF935A2575BC0A964958260 |work=[[The New York Times]] |date=1992-08-16 |accessdate=2008-04-03 | first=Joel | last=Kurtzman}}</ref> a private company which provides [[macroeconomics|macroeconomic analysis]] and forecasts on Mexico to business, including some of the largest [[multinational companies]] with interests in Mexico.<ref>{{cite news |title=I.B.M. to Pay Mexico City Millions for Failed System |url=https://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9D06E3DB1239F937A15754C0A96E958260 |work=The New York Times |date=1998-07-24 |accessdate=2008-04-03 | first=Sam | last=Dillon}}</ref> During 2006 he headed on an honorary basis the economic policy team of presidential candidate Andrés Manuel López Obrador,<ref>{{cite news|title=Ramírez de la O señala la necesidad de mejorar competitividad de la IP |url=http://www.jornada.unam.mx/2006/01/24/010n2pol.php |work=[[La Jornada]] |date=2006-03-24 |accessdate=2008-04-03 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20070821113718/http://www.jornada.unam.mx/2006/01/24/010n2pol.php |archivedate=August 21, 2007 }}</ref> backed by a coalition of parties including the PRD (Party of the Democratic Revolution). Ramírez de la O received a bachelor's degree from the National Autonomous [[University of Mexico]] and an Economics PhD from [[Cambridge University]], [[United Kingdom]]. He has published works in Mexico, the [[United States]] and [[Europe]] on [[NAFTA]], Mexico’s macroeconomic problems and the Mexican auto industry, among others. In the frame of the 2012 campaign for presidential elections, [[Andrés Manuel López Obrador]] included him as Finance Minister in his cabinet proposal, but the party did not win the elections.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.lopezobrador.org.mx/gabinete2012/index.html|title=Propuesta de gabinete de Andrés Manuel López Obrador|accessdate=4 February 2012|url-status=dead|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120131213325/http://www.lopezobrador.org.mx/gabinete2012/index.html|archivedate=31 January 2012}}</ref>
