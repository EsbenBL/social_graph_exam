{{Short description|American sociologist}}
'''John Keith Irwin''' (May 21, 1929 – January 3, 2010) was an [[People of the United States|American]] [[sociology|sociologist]] and  [[criminologist]] who was known internationally as an expert on the [[Incarceration in the United States|American prison system]].<ref name="SFGate">[https://www.sfgate.com/bayarea/article/Criminal-turned-criminologist-John-Irwin-dies-3205069.php San Francisco Chronicle. "Irwin, John." Wednesday, January 6, 2010].</ref> He published dozens of scholarly articles and seven books on the topic.

==Early life and education==
Irwin was raised in [[Los Angeles]]. In 1952, he robbed a [[gas station]] and served a five-year prison term for [[armed robbery]] mostly at the [[Correctional Training Facility|Soledad Prison]] in the [[Salinas Valley]]. He finished a semester in college via correspondence. After his release he earned a bachelor's degree at the [[University of California, Los Angeles]]. He went on to receive his Ph.D. in Sociology from the [[University of California, Berkeley]]. Irwin later became a professor and chaired the Meagan Coveney sociology department at [[San Francisco State University]], where he taught for 27 years until his retirement.<ref name="sfgate.com">[http://www.sfgate.com/bayarea/article/Criminal-turned-criminologist-John-Irwin-dies-3205069.php Fagan "Criminal Turned Criminologist John Irwin Dies], ''[[San Francisco Chronicle]]'', January 7, 2010. Retrieved 18 November 2016.</ref><ref name="sentencingproject.org">[http://www.sentencingproject.org/detail/news.cfm?news_id=838&id=167 The Sentencing Project. "John Irwin: Scholar, Activist, Convict Criminologist." January 5, 2010.] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110718181211/http://www.sentencingproject.org/detail/news.cfm?news_id=838&id=167 |date=July 18, 2011 }}</ref>

==Career==
In 1967 Irwin founded Project Rebound, a program which helps those coming out of prison go to college.<ref name="sentencingproject.org"/> Irwin co-founded the Prisoners Union in 1971, which organized inmates to push for their civil rights and worked closely with the California legislature on the Uniform Sentencing Act passed in 1976.<ref name="SFGate"/><ref name="convictcriminology.org">[http://www.convictcriminology.org/ Richards, Stephen C., et al. "John Irwin   1929 - 2010." Convict Criminology. January 5, 2010.]</ref> At a panel presentation at the American Society of Criminology’s annual conference in 1997, Irwin helped officially establish the Convict Criminology movement, in which convicts who became professors critically examine the [[criminal justice system]].<ref name="sfgate.com"/><ref name="sentencingproject.org"/> In 1985 he received the August Vollmer award from the [[American Society of Criminology]].<ref name="convictcriminology.org"/>

==Death==
Irwin died on January 3, 2010, at the age of 80.<ref name="SFGate" />

==Personal==
Irwing was survived by his daughters Professor Katy Irwin, Anne, Jeanette, son Johnny and wife of 38 years, researcher Marsha Rosenbaum.<ref name="SFGate" />
