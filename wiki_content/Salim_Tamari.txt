'''Salim Tamari'''  ({{lang-ar|سليم تماري}}; born 1945), is a Palestinian sociologist who is the director of the [[Institute of Palestine Studies]] and an adjunct professor at the [[Center for Contemporary Arab Studies]] at [[Georgetown University]].<ref name="Palestine: Information with Provenance (PIWP database)">{{cite web|title=Salim Tamari|url=http://cosmos.ucc.ie/cs1064/jabowen/IPSC/php/authors.php?auid=68|work=Palestine: Information with Provenance (PIWP database)|accessdate=11 April 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=CCAS Faculty Page|url=http://ccas.georgetown.edu/about/people/faculty/|accessdate=1 February 2012}}</ref> [[Rashid Khalidi]], the Edward Said Professor of Modern Arab Studies at [[Columbia University]], has called Tamari "the preeminent [[Palestinian people|Palestinian]] historical [[Sociology|sociologist]]."<ref>{{cite web|title=Review of Mountain Against The Sea: Essays On Palestinian Society And Culture|url=http://www.flipkart.com/mountain-against-sea-salim-tamari-book-0520251296|work=FlipKart.com|accessdate=11 April 2011}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Tamari was born in the ancient port city of [[Jaffa]], Palestine, in 1945. When he was three years old, in April 1948, his family fled Jaffa when it was attacked by Jewish paramilitary groups as part of the [[1948 Arab–Israeli War]]. Tamari studied at Birzeit College (later renamed [[Birzeit University]]) in the [[West Bank]] and then received a B.A. in politics from [[Drew University]] in New Jersey, United States. He later received an M.A. in sociology from the [[University of New Hampshire]] and a Ph.D. in sociology from the [[University of Manchester]].

==Career==
Tamari has been a sociologist at Birzeit University since 1971. In 1994, he was appointed director of the Institute of Jerusalem Studies, an affiliate of the Beirut-based [[Institute for Palestine Studies]] that publishes the Arabic journal [[Institute for Palestine Studies#Publications|Jerusalem Quarterly]] (formerly Jerusalem Quarterly File). He has also served on the refugee committee in the multilateral peace talks held following the [[Madrid Conference of 1991]]. He has been a visiting fellow at the [[Aga Khan]] Program for Islamic Architecture at [[MIT]],<ref>{{cite web|title=Hauser Center Brown Bag Lunch Series Presents The First Intifada and the Transformation of Palestinian Civil Society Presented by Dr. Salim Tamari|url=http://www.hks.harvard.edu/hauser/learn/seminarslectures/brownbaglunchtalks/archived/salim-tamari/|work=The Hauser Center for Nonprofit Organizations at Harvard University|accessdate=11 April 2011}}</ref> and has been a visiting professor at the University of California Berkeley (2005, 2007), New York University (2001–03); Cornell University (1997), and the University of Chicago (1991–92).<ref>{{cite web|title=Salim Tamari: End of the Ottoman Era in Palestine|url=http://www.watsoninstitute.org/events_detail.cfm?id=1303|work=The Watson Institute for International Studies|accessdate=11 April 2011}}</ref> He was a 2008 Eric Lane Fellow at the [[University of Cambridge]] and is a lecturer in Mediterranean Studies at the [[Ca' Foscari University of Venice]].<ref>{{cite web|title=IPS Fellow Salim Tamari|url=http://www.palestine-studies.org/ipsfellows.aspx?id=9|work=Institute for Palestinian Studies|accessdate=11 April 2011}}</ref>

==Works==
Drawing upon archival materials and personal diaries, Tamari has produced numerous studies documenting and analysing Palestinian society.<ref name=memo>{{cite web |last= Tamari |first= Salim |title= Treacherous memories: electronic return to Jaffa |work= The Electronic Jaffa Forum |url= http://www.palestineremembered.com/Jaffa/Jaffa/Story152.html |access-date=11 April 2011}}</ref> Books by Tamari include:
*''Jerusalem 1948: The Arab Neighborhoods and Their Fate in the War''<ref name=memo/>
*''Palestinian Refugee Negotiations: From Madrid to Oslo II''<ref name=memo/>
*''Mountain against the Sea: Essays on Palestinian Society and Culture''<ref name=memo/>
*''Year of the Locust: A Soldier's Diary and the Erasure of Palestine's Ottoman Past'', a biography of a young Palestinian soldier stationed in Jerusalem during World War I and referred to on worldcat.org as "the first ordinary recruit to describe the World War I from the Arab side". Berkeley: University of California Press, 2011.<ref name=memo/><ref>[https://www.worldcat.org/title/year-of-the-locust-a-soldiers-diary-and-the-erasure-of-palestines-ottoman-past/oclc/750183116 Year of the locust] entry on worldcat.org. Accessed August 2021.</ref>
