'''Varadarajan Venkata Chari''' (born c. 1952) is an [[Indian-American]] [[economist]] and [[professor]] of [[economics]] at the [[University of Minnesota]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.minnpost.com/politics-policy/2009/03/um-economist-vv-chari-government-bailout-policy-cross-your-fingers-and-hope|title=UM economist V.V. Chari: Government bailout policy is "cross your fingers and hope"|last=Perry|first=Steve|work=MinnPost|publisher=March 19, 2009|access-date=10 July 2012}}</ref>

Chari received a [[Bachelor of Technology]] in [[Chemical Engineering]] from the [[Indian Institute of Technology, Bombay]] in 1974, and was a [[production engineer]] at [[Union Carbide|Union Carbide (India) Limited]] from 1974 to 1976.

Chari received his Ph.D. from [[Carnegie Mellon University]] in 1980. His dissertation won him the [[Alexander Henderson Award]] for excellence in economics, an award also won by [[Nobel Memorial Prize in Economics|Nobel Laureates]] [[Oliver E. Williamson]], [[Dale T. Mortensen]], [[Finn E. Kydland]] and [[Edward C. Prescott]]. Chari joined the [[Kellogg School of Management]], [[Northwestern University]] as an [[assistant professor]] of [[managerial economics]]. In 1986 he moved to the [[Federal Reserve Bank of Minneapolis]]. In 1992 he returned to the Kellogg Graduate School of Management and then moved back to [[Minneapolis]] and the University of Minnesota in the fall of 1994.

His research interests are in banking, fiscal and monetary policy and in issues of economic development. He has written extensively on banking crises, exchange rate fluctuations and international capital flows.
