'''Ronald J. Deibert''' {{Post-nominals|country=CAN|OOnt}} (born 1964)<ref>{{cite web|last1=Scott Anderson says|accessdate=2019-03-16|title=The Troublemaker|url=https://magazine.utoronto.ca/people/faculty-staff/profile-of-u-of-t-citizen-lab-founder-professor-ron-deibert/|website=University of Toronto}}</ref> is a Canadian [[professor]] of [[political science]], philosopher, and director of the [[Citizen Lab]] at the [[Munk School of Global Affairs]], [[University of Toronto]].<ref name="Bpr2012-10-21" /> The Citizen Lab is an interdisciplinary laboratory focusing on research, development, and high-level strategic policy and legal engagement at the intersection of information and communication technologies, human rights, and global security.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://citizenlab.ca/about/|title=About the Citizen Lab|website=The Citizen Lab|language=en-US|access-date=2019-01-07}}</ref> He is a co-founder and a principal investigator of the [[OpenNet Initiative]] and [[Information Warfare Monitor]] projects.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2008/08/26/AR2008082603128.html|title=A new breed of hackers tracks online acts of war|work=The Washington Post|accessdate=2011-09-06|date=2008-08-27|first=Kim|last=Hart}}</ref> Deibert was one of the founders and former VP of global policy and outreach for [[Psiphon]].

He is a co-editor of three major volumes with ''MIT Press: Access Denied: The practice and policy of Internet Filtering'' (2008),<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://mitpress.mit.edu/books/access-denied|title=Access Denied|last=Press|first=The MIT|website=The MIT Press|language=en|access-date=2020-01-13}}</ref> ''Access Controlled: The shaping of power, rights, and rule in cyberspace'' (2010),<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://mitpress.mit.edu/books/access-controlled|title=Access Controlled|last=Press|first=The MIT|website=The MIT Press|language=en|access-date=2020-01-13}}</ref> and ''Access Contested: Security, Identity, and Resistance in Asian Cyberspace (''2011).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://mitpress.mit.edu/books/access-contested|title=Access Contested|last=Press|first=The MIT|website=The MIT Press|language=en|access-date=2020-01-13}}</ref> He is the author of ''Parchment, Printing, and Hypermedia: Communications in World Order Transformation'' (New York: Columbia University Press, 1997)<ref>{{Cite book|url=http://cup.columbia.edu/book/parchment-printing-and-hypermedia/9780231107136|title=Parchment, Printing, and Hypermedia: Communication and World Order Transformation|last=Deibert|first=Ronald|date=September 1997|publisher=Columbia University Press|isbn=978-0-231-10713-6}}</ref> ''and Black Code: Surveillance, Privacy, and the Dark Side of the Internet'',<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.blackcodebook.com/index.html#.UZVQ6II1Z2c|title=Black Code|last=|first=|date=|website=|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref> published in May 2013 by [[Penguin Random House]] and turned into a feature-length documentary by Nick De Pencier in 2017.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.mongrelmedia.com/index.php/filmlink?id=64c3a8d2-38f8-42db-bca9-f5377a11264d|title=Mongrel Media|last=|first=|date=|website=|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref>

As director of the Citizen Lab, Deibert has overseen and been a contributing author to more than 120 reports<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://citizenlab.ca/publications/|title=Publications|website=The Citizen Lab|language=en-US|access-date=2020-01-13}}</ref> covering path breaking research on cyber espionage, commercial spyware, Internet censorship, and human rights. These reports include the landmark Tracking Ghostnet<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://citizenlab.ca/wp-content/uploads/2017/05/ghostnet.pdf|title=Tracking Ghostnet: Investigating a Cyber Espionage Network|last=|first=|date=|website=|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref> report (which uncovered an espionage operation that infiltrated the computer networks of hundreds of government offices, NGOs, and other organizations, including those of the Dalai Lama), China’s Great Cannon<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://citizenlab.ca/2015/04/chinas-great-cannon/|title=China's Great Cannon|date=2015-04-10|website=The Citizen Lab|language=en-US|access-date=2020-01-13}}</ref> (an offensive tool used to hijack digital traffic through Distributed Denial of Service attacks), the Kingdom Came to Canada<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://citizenlab.ca/2018/10/the-kingdom-came-to-canada-how-saudi-linked-digital-espionage-reached-canadian-soil/|title=The Kingdom Came to Canada: How Saudi-Linked Digital Espionage Reached Canadian Soil|date=2018-10-01|website=The Citizen Lab|language=en-US|access-date=2020-01-13}}</ref> (an investigation of a Canadian permanent resident, Saudi dissident, and Khashoggi colleague who was targeted with commercial spyware), and the Reckless Series<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://citizenlab.ca/2017/02/bittersweet-nso-mexico-spyware/|title=Bitter Sweet: Supporters of Mexico's Soda Tax Targeted With NSO Exploit Links|date=2017-02-11|website=The Citizen Lab|language=en-US|access-date=2020-01-13}}</ref> (an investigation into the abuse of commercial spyware to target journalists, anti-corruption advocates, and public health officials in Mexico). These reports have been cited widely in global media, garnering 25 front page exclusives in the New York Times, Washington Post, and other leading outlets, and have been cited by policymakers, academics, and civil society as foundational to the understanding of digital technologies, human rights, and global security.

Deibert presently serves on the editorial boards of the journals International Political Sociology,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://academic.oup.com/ips/pages/Editorial_Board|title=Editorial Board|website=Oxford Academic|language=en|access-date=2020-01-13}}</ref> Explorations in Media Ecology,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://media-ecology.wildapricot.org/eme|title=Media Ecology Association - Explorations in Media Ecology|website=media-ecology.wildapricot.org|access-date=2020-01-13}}</ref> Review of Policy Research,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://onlinelibrary.wiley.com/page/journal/15411338/homepage/editorialboard.html|title=Review of Policy Research|website=Wiley Online Library|language=en|access-date=2020-01-13}}</ref> Journal of Global Security Studies,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://academic.oup.com/jogss/pages/Editorial_Board|title=Editorial Board|website=Oxford Academic|language=en|access-date=2020-01-13}}</ref> and Astropolitics.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.tandfonline.com/action/journalInformation?show=editorialBoard&journalCode=fast20&|title=Astropolitics|website=www.tandfonline.com|access-date=2020-01-13}}</ref> He has served on the advisory boards of [[AccessNow.org|Access Now]], [[Privacy International]], the technical advisory groups of [[Amnesty International]] and [[Human Rights Watch]], and is currently on the advisory boards of [[PEN Canada]] and the Design4Democracy Coalition,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://d4dcoalition.org/advisory-board/ronald-deibert.html|title=Ronald Deibert|date=2019-12-10|website=D4D Coalition|language=en-US|access-date=2020-01-13}}</ref> the steering committee of the [[World Movement for Democracy]], the advisory board for the Citizen Clinic at the [[University of California, Berkeley]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://cltc.berkeley.edu/about-us/citizen-clinic/|title=Citizen Clinic - CLTC UC Berkeley Center for Long-Term Cybersecurity|website=CLTC|language=en-US|access-date=2020-01-13}}</ref> and co-chair of the [[University of Toronto]]'s Information Security Council.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://main.its.utoronto.ca/news/newly-formed-information-security-council/|title=Newly formed Information Security Council at U of T {{!}} ITS {{!}} University of Toronto|website=main.its.utoronto.ca|access-date=2019-01-17}}</ref>  In 2020, he gave the annual [[Massey Lectures]] for the [[Canadian Broadcasting Corporation]], and they were published in book form under the title ''Reset: Reclaiming the Internet for Civil Society''.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.kobo.com/ca/en/ebook/reset-74|title=Kobo catalog listing: Reset by Ronald J. Diebert|access-date=2020-10-18}}</ref> The book won the 2021 [[Shaughnessy Cohen Prize for Political Writing]].<ref>[https://www.piquenewsmagazine.com/the-mix/citizen-labs-ronald-deibert-wins-25k-shaughnessy-cohen-prize-for-political-writing-4358059 "Citizen Lab's Ronald Deibert wins $25K Shaughnessy Cohen Prize for Political Writing"]. ''[[Pique Newsmagazine]]'', September 22, 2021.</ref>
