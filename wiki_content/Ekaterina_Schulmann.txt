'''Ekaterina Schulmann''' née '''Zaslavskaya''' ({{lang-ru|Екатерина Михайловна Шульман}}, {{Transliteration|ru|BGN/PCGN|Yekaterina Mikhaylovna Shulman}}; born 19 August 1978, [[Tula, Russia|Tula]], Russia) is a Russian [[political science|political scientist]] specializing on the studies of lawmaking.

== Life ==
Schulmann was born in Tula and graduated from high school there. She studied political science at the Russian Presidential Academy of National Economy and Public Administration. After graduation, she worked at the [[State Duma]] of the Russian Federation, subsequently in a consulting company. In 2013, she obtained a [[Candidate of Sciences]] degree in political science and was hired by the Academy. Her research interest is lawmaking, in particular, in Russia.<ref name="ou">{{cite web |title=Екатерина Шульман |url=https://openuni.io/expert/48/ |publisher=Open University |language=ru |access-date=2018-12-31 |archive-date=2019-01-01 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190101002722/https://openuni.io/expert/48/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

She is an associate professor of the [[Russian Presidential Academy of National Economy and Public Administration]].<ref name="hrights">{{cite web |url=http://president-sovet.ru/members/constitution/read/97/ |title=Шульман Екатерина Михайловна |publisher=[[Presidential Council for Civil Society and Human Rights]] |access-date=31 December 2018 |language=ru |archive-date=31 December 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181231194149/http://president-sovet.ru/members/constitution/read/97/ |url-status=dead }}</ref> In 2018, she became a member of the [[Presidential Council for Civil Society and Human Rights]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Политолог Екатерина Шульман получила предложение войти в СПЧ |url=https://www.rbc.ru/politics/29/11/2018/5bffff0e9a7947363e3247f5 |access-date=31 December 2018 |agency=[[RBK Group]] |language=ru |archive-date=31 December 2018 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181231194643/https://www.rbc.ru/politics/29/11/2018/5bffff0e9a7947363e3247f5 |url-status=live }}</ref><ref name="hrights" /> On 21 October 2019 she was dismissed from the Council.<ref>{{cite news |title=Обновленный состав СПЧ: без Федотова и Чикова, но с Фадеевым и Вышинским |url=https://www.bbc.com/russian/news-50124082 |agency=BBC |date=21 October 2019 |language=ru |access-date=22 October 2019 |archive-date=22 October 2019 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191022171654/https://www.bbc.com/russian/news-50124082 |url-status=live }}</ref>

Schulmann is popular within Russian media and is often invited as a columnist or a commentator. In particular, she writes columns for ''[[Vedomosti]]'', [[The New Times (magazine)|''The New Times'']] and comments weekly at ''[[Echo of Moscow]]''.<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=vKd6DwAAQBAJ&pg=PA64|title=Developments in Russian Politics 9|last=White|first=Richard Sakwa, Henry E. Hale and Stephen|publisher=Macmillan International Higher Education|isbn=9781352004687|language=en|access-date=2019-04-25|archive-date=2020-08-23|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200823092842/https://books.google.com/books?id=vKd6DwAAQBAJ&pg=PA64|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{Cite book|title=The new autocracy : information, politics, and policy in Putin's Russia|others=Treisman, Daniel|year=2018|isbn=978-0815732433|location=Washington, D.C.|pages=292|chapter=About the contributors|oclc=978604475|chapter-url=https://books.google.com/books?id=PcZZDgAAQBAJ&pg=PT245|last1=Treisman|first1=Daniel|access-date=2019-04-25|archive-date=2020-08-23|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200823092845/https://books.google.com/books?id=PcZZDgAAQBAJ&pg=PT245|url-status=live}}</ref>

She is married to [[Mikhail Schulmann]], a literary critic and an expert on the life and work of [[Vladimir Nabokov]]. The Schulmanns have three children (two daughters and a son) together.
