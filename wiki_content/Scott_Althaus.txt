'''Scott Althaus''' is a professor of [[political science]] and of [[communication]] at the [[University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign]] and the director of the Cline Center for Advanced Social Research at the University.<ref name=about>{{cite web|url=http://faculty.las.illinois.edu/salthaus/|title = Scott Althaus|publisher = [[University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign]]|accessdate = June 4, 2014}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.pol.illinois.edu/people/salthaus|title = Scott Althaus|publisher = Department of Political Science, [[University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign]]|accessdate = June 4, 2014}}</ref>

==Academic biography==

Althaus completed his doctoral work in political science at [[Northwestern University]]. After that, he joined UIUC in 1996 and has stayed there ever since.<ref name=about/>

==Academic work==

===Journals===

Althaus serves on the editorial boards of ''[[Critical Review (scholarly journal)|Critical Review]]'', ''[[Human Communication Research]]'', ''[[Journal of Communication]]'', ''[[Political Communication]]'', and ''[[Public Opinion Quarterly]]''. His research has appeared in the ''[[American Political Science Review]]'', the ''[[American Journal of Political Science]]'', ''[[Communication Research]]'', ''[[Journalism and Mass Communication Quarterly]]'', ''[[Journal of Broadcasting & Electronic Media]]'', ''[[Journal of Conflict Resolution]]'', ''[[Journal of Politics]]'', ''[[Public Opinion Quarterly]]'', and ''[[Political Communication]]''.<ref name=about/>

===Books===

Althaus authored a book on the political uses of opinion surveys in democratic societies titled ''Collective Preferences in Democratic Politics: Opinion Surveys and the Will of the People''.<ref>{{cite book|title = Collective Preferences in Democratic Politics: Opinion Surveys and the Will of the People|last = Althaus|first = Scott|date = September 2003|accessdate = June 4, 2014|url = http://www.cambridge.org/us/academic/subjects/politics-international-relations/american-government-politics-and-policy/collective-preferences-democratic-politics-opinion-surveys-and-will-people}}</ref>
