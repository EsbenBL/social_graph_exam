'''Douglas Vakoch''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|v|ɑː|ˌ|k|oʊ|tʃ}} {{Respell|VAH|kohtch}};<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=98v1yOV0-5s|title=How to Design an Interstellar Message for Extraterrestrials|accessdate=25 January 2021}}</ref> born June 16, 1961) is an American astrobiologist, search for extraterrestrial intelligence (SETI) researcher, psychologist, and president of [[METI (Messaging Extraterrestrial Intelligence)|METI International]], a nonprofit research and educational organization devoted to transmitting intentional signals to extraterrestrial civilizations. Vakoch led METI's participation in Sónar Calling GJ 273b, which transmitted a series of interstellar messages to Luyten's Star, located 12.4 light years from Earth. Vakoch advocates ongoing transmission projects, arguing that this does not increase risks of an alien invasion as suggested by British cosmologist Stephen Hawking. He has participated in several SETI observation programs, and after sixteen years at the SETI Institute, where he was director of Interstellar Message Composition, Vakoch founded METI International. He has edited over a dozen books in SETI, astrobiology, the psychology of space exploration, and ecocriticism. He is general editor of two book series in ecocriticism and in the intersection of space and society. Vakoch has appeared widely on television and radio as a commentator on SETI and astrobiology. He is an emeritus professor of clinical psychology at the California Institute of Integral Studies (CIIS).

== Early life and education ==
Douglas Vakoch grew up in rural Minnesota.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2002/03/05/science/scientist-work-douglas-vakoch-when-it-s-not-enough-say-take-me-your-leader.html|title=SCIENTIST AT WORK: DOUGLAS VAKOCH; When It's Not Enough to Say 'Take Me to Your Leader'|last=Overbye|first=Dennis|date=2002-03-05|work=The New York Times|access-date=2017-04-09|issn=0362-4331}}</ref> He created his first interstellar message as a high school student—a series of two-dimensional pictures that built upon a [[Arecibo message|message transmitted from Arecibo Observatory in 1974]].<ref name=":29">{{Cite news|url=https://www.wired.com/2016/07/meet-guy-left-seti-institute-talk-aliens/|title=Meet the Guy Who Left the SETI Institute to Talk to Aliens|last=Scoles|first=Sarah|date=2016-07-15|work=WIRED|access-date=2017-04-16|language=en-US}}</ref><ref name=":64">{{Cite news|url=https://utopia.forbes.at/artikel/anyone-there.html|title=Anyone there?|last=Hintermayer|first=Niklas|date=2018-03-09|work=Forbes Utopia|access-date=2018-03-09}}</ref> "The issue that really hit me early on, and that has stayed with me, is just the challenge of creating a message that would be understandable," he told ''[[The New York Times Magazine]]''.<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Johnson|first=Steven|date=2017-07-02|title=To Whom It May Concern|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2017/06/28/magazine/greetings-et-please-dont-murder-us.html|journal=The New York Times Magazine|pages=36}}</ref> Vakoch earned a bachelor's degree in comparative religion from [[Carleton College]], a master's degree in history and philosophy of science from the [[University of Notre Dame]], and a PhD in psychology from [[Stony Brook University]].<ref name=":0" /> He completed a postdoctoral fellowship at [[Vanderbilt University]] before he accepted a position at the [[SETI Institute]] in Mountain View, California.<ref name=":0" />

== Active Search for Extraterrestrial Intelligence (Active SETI) ==
Vakoch argues that in order to make contact, humankind may need to take the initiative in transmitting, a project called [[active SETI]].<ref name=":11">{{Cite journal|last=Winerman|first=Lea|date=October 2015|title=Hello? Anyone out there?|url=http://www.apa.org/monitor/2015/10/extraterrestrial.aspx|journal=Monitor on Psychology|volume=46|issue=9|pages=44}}</ref> He has been called "a prominent voice in favor of active SETI,"<ref name=":11" /> "the most prominent METI [messaging to extraterrestrial intelligence] proponent,"<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.nbcnews.com/mach/space/why-these-scientists-fear-contact-space-aliens-n717271|title=Why these scientists fear contact with space aliens|last=Boyle|first=Rebecca|date=2017-02-08|work=NBC News|access-date=2017-04-09|language=en}}</ref> and "the man who speaks for Earth."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.newyorker.com/books/joshua-rothman/the-man-who-speaks-for-earth|title=The Man Who Speaks for Earth|last=Rothman|first=Joshua|date=2014-06-10|website=The New Yorker|access-date=2017-04-17}}</ref> In [[Discover (magazine)|''Discover'']]'s ranking of scientists either in favor of or opposed to transmitting, Vakoch was cited as "super pro," at the extreme of those advocating messaging.<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Scoles|first=Sarah|date=2017|title=Alien contact|url=http://discovermagazine.com/2017/jul-aug/alien-contact|journal=Discover|volume=38|issue=6|pages=60|oclc=557197122}}</ref> After sixteen years at the SETI Institute,<ref name=":49">{{Cite web|url=https://astronomerswithoutborders.org/gam2017-programs/astroarts/3690-search-for-extraterrestrial-intelligence-an-earthling-s-quest.html|title=Search for Extraterrestrial Intelligence: An Earthling's Quest|last=McMonigal|first=Christie|date=2017-04-12|website=astronomerswithoutborders.org|language=en-us|access-date=2017-04-23}}</ref> where he was director of Interstellar Message Composition,<ref name=":29" /><ref name=":49" /> Vakoch founded [[METI (Messaging Extraterrestrial Intelligence)]],<ref name=":12" /> a nonprofit research and educational organization.<ref name=":29" />

== Passive<nowiki/> SETI ==
In 2010, Vakoch was one of the leaders of Project Dorothy, a multinational effort launched by Japanese astronomer Shin-ya Narusawa to observe several stars for signals from other civilizations to commemorate the fiftieth anniversary of [[Project Ozma]], the first modern-day [[search for extraterrestrial intelligence]] (SETI).<ref name=":19">{{Cite news|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2010/11/06/AR2010110604127.html?sid=ST2010110604135|title=Observatories on 5 continents to scan skies for extraterrestrial life|last=Kaufman|first=Marc|date=2010-11-07|work=The Washington Post|access-date=2017-04-12|language=en-US|issn=0190-8286}}</ref> Telling ''[[The Washington Post]]'' about the Project Dorothy observations, Vakoch said "[w]hat this weekend really does is begin the process of making it possible to track a possible SETI signal around the globe," and he added "[i]f a signal is detected, it has to be confirmed and followed, and now we're setting up a network to do that."<ref name=":19" />

Vakoch contends that it is essential to expand an understanding of SETI beyond the technology needed to search by also re-examining assumptions about the nature of intelligence, which was the motivation for the METI workshop, “The Intelligence of SETI: Cognition and Communication in Extraterrestrial Intelligence,”<ref name=":12">{{Cite news|url=http://www.airspacemag.com/daily-planet/conversing-et-180959306/|title=Conversing With E.T.|last=Schulze-Makuch|first=Dirk|date=2016-06-03|work=Air & Space Magazine|access-date=2017-04-09|language=en}}</ref> held in San Juan, Puerto Rico on May 18, 2016.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.universetoday.com/129092/alien-minds-extraterrestrial-civilizations-likely/|title=Alien Minds I: Are Extraterrestrial Civilizations Likely to Evolve?|last=Patton|first=Paul|date=2016-05-24|work=Universe Today|access-date=2017-04-19|language=en-US}}</ref> "By studying the variety of intelligence found on Earth," Vakoch said, "we can gain new insights into sending messages to life on other planets."<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.inverse.com/article/15804-how-do-we-talk-to-aliens-scientists-meet-in-puerto-rico-to-hash-it-out|title=How Do We Talk to Aliens? Scientists Meet in Puerto Rico to Hash It Out|last=Patel|first=Neel V.|date=2016-05-18|work=Inverse|access-date=2017-04-19}}</ref> He called for "rethinking what SETI means," saying "what we haven't caught up with is the real understanding of intelligence."<ref name=":38">{{Cite news|url=http://www.ibtimes.co.uk/alien-intelligence-life-universe-may-be-abundant-will-it-ever-communicate-us-1561088|title=Alien life in the universe may well be abundant, but will it ever communicate with us?|last=Osborne|first=Hannah|date=2016-05-20|work=International Business Times UK|access-date=2017-04-19}}</ref> Vakoch told the [[International Business Times]] that "[i]n this new approach, we're putting the intelligence back into SETI."<ref name=":38" /> He argues that the fact that extraterrestrial intelligence may rely on different senses than humans adds to the complexity of interspecies communication.<ref name=":10">{{Cite web|url=http://www.dailymotion.com/video/xb23qg_cosmic-connexion-interviews_tech|title=Cosmic Connexion : Interviews - vidéo|date=2009-11-06|website=Dailymotion|language=fr|access-date=2017-04-09}}</ref><ref name=":14">{{Cite journal|last=Sutton|first=Jon|date=October 2015|title=What would you say to an alien?|url=https://thepsychologist.bps.org.uk/volume-28/october-2015/interview-what-would-you-say-alien|journal=The Psychologist|volume=28|pages=800–803}}</ref><ref name=":13" />

interstellar space."<ref name=":16">{{Cite news|url=https://www.ncronline.org/blogs/eco-catholic/how-do-we-craft-message-stars|title=How do we craft a message to the it is possible that extraterrestrial mathematics and science may vary significantly from human mathematics and science.<ref name=":3">{{Cite consistent frameworks for understanding the world, but they vary in their foundational assumptions.<ref name=":13">{{Cite news|url=https://www.wired.com/2013/11/enders-game-alien-communication/|title=What Ender's Game Gets Right About Communicating With In contrast to the images included on the [[Voyager Golden Record]] that emphasized the positive aspects of life on Earth, Vakoch proposes that we should be honest about human frailties.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.newscientist.com/article/dn16981-why-we-shouldnt-hide-our-problems-from-et/|title=Why we shouldn't hide our problems from ET|last=Vakoch|first=Douglas|date=2009-04-18|work=New Vakoch calls for increasing the range of people participating in interstellar message design, and he led a workshop in Paris in 2002 on the interface of art and science in interstellar messages.<ref name=":17">{{Cite news|url=http://www.cbsnews.com/news/what-would-iyou-i-say-to-et/|title=What Would ''You '' Say To E.T.?|last=Collins|first=Dan|date=2002-03-26|work=CBS News|access-date=2017-04-10|publisher=Reuters|language=en}}</ref> Speaking to ''[[Reuters]]'' on the day of the meeting, he said "Today the focus has been on whether we can explain something about our aesthetic sensibilities. == Space exploration ==
Vakoch has contributed to the study of space exploration, most notably through books examining psychological dimensions of space travel. [[The Journal of Military History|''The'' ''Journal of Military History'']] noted about Vakoch's edited book ''Psychology of Space Exploration: Contemporary Research in Historical Perspective'' that "[f]or those interested in an overview and synthesis of some of the key issues in the psychology of space exploration, this book provides a great introduction," adding that "[m]ost interestingly to those whose primary interest lies in history, many of the chapters engage the ''history of the psychology'' of space exploration quite well, most notably in the book’s first two chapters."<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Hansen|first=James R.|date=January 2012|title=Psychology of Space Exploration: Contemporary Research in Historical Perspective|url=http://connection.ebscohost.com/c/book-reviews/70050833/psychology-space-exploration-contemporary-research-historical-perspective|journal=Journal of Military History|volume=76|issue=1|pages=301}}{{dead link|date=November 2020|bot=medic}}{{cbignore|bot=medic}}</ref><ref name=":52" /> ''[[Aviation, Space, and Environmental Medicine]]'' observed that "[t]his book is unique in that it places much of the ongoing research and interest in the topic of human behavior and performance in space in historical perspective" and concluded that "[i]t is certainly worthwhile reading for those directly involved in the next phase of human exploration of space as well as those who will witness this phase from the confines of Earth."<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Palinkas|first=Lawrence A.|date=2012-07-01|title=Psychology of space exploration|journal=Aviation, Space, and Environmental Medicine|volume=83|issue=7|pages=711|doi=10.3357/ASEM.3231.2012}}</ref> ''[[Isis (journal)|Isis]]'' wrote that "[t]his diverse and thought-provoking collection represents an important departure for the NASA History Series, a turn from works focused on machines, missions, and management structures to a concern with the smaller group of space sciences interested in human subjects, like space medicine and human factors engineering" and noted that the book "represents an important step in bringing the human-focused space sciences to the attention of a wider audience."<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Bimm|first=Jordan|date=2012-01-01|title=Review of Psychology of Space Exploration: Contemporary Research in Historical Perspective, Douglas A. Vakoch|jstor=10.1086/670120|journal=Isis|volume=103|issue=4|pages=812|doi=10.1086/670120}}</ref>

''[[Journal of Mind and Behavior|The Journal of Mind and Behavior]]'' noted that Vakoch's follow-up book ''On Orbit and Beyond: Psychological Perspectives on Human Spaceflight'' includes several chapters that address the implications of the increased autonomy that astronauts would have on missions to Mars and Saturn, as compared to orbiting Earth or travelling to the Moon.<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Michael|first=George|date=2013-01-01|title=Review of On Orbit and Beyond: Psychological Perspectives on Human Spaceflight|jstor=43854335|journal=The Journal of Mind and Behavior|volume=34|issue=2|pages=197–204}}</ref><ref name=":53" /> In an interview Vakoch explained the implications of this increased autonomy: "On missions to Mars, where greater autonomy will be expected of astronauts because of the greater distances, ground personnel should expect that their own roles will change over the course of the mission. Unless ground personnel are prepared, they may feel like they are no longer playing as central a role as they did shortly after liftoff because astronauts are now making more decisions."<ref name=":54">{{Cite web|url=http://www.nightsky.ws/interview-with-prof-douglas-vakoch.html|title=Interview with Prof. Douglas Vakoch|last=Sanaee|first=Ehsan|website=Nightsky|access-date=2017-05-01}}</ref>

At the 2008 annual convention of the [[American Psychological Association]], Vakoch chaired the symposium "To the Moon and Mars: Psychology of Long-Duration Space Exploration,"<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.apa.org/news/press/releases/2008/08/space-flights.aspx|title=To the Moon and Mars: Psychologists Show New Ways to Combat Behavioral Health Challenges During Long Space Flights|date=2008-08-14|website=American Psychological Association|language=en|access-date=2017-05-01}}</ref> which was identified as a "highlight" of the convention.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.apa.org/news/press/releases/2008/05/convention.aspx|title=American Psychological Association 116th Annual Convention in Boston|date=2008-05-12|website=American Psychological Association|language=en|access-date=2017-05-01}}</ref>

Vakoch has also examined unmanned space exploration. Commenting on plans to send miniature spacecraft to a nearby star, he told the ''[[International Business Times]]'' that "[b]y sending hundreds or thousands of space probes the size of postage stamps, [[Breakthrough Initiatives|Breakthrough Starshot]] gets around the hazards of spaceflight that could easily end a mission relying on a single spacecraft. Only one nanocraft needs to make its way to [[Alpha Centauri]] and send back a signal for the mission to be successful. When that happens, Starshot will make history."<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.ibtimes.co.uk/alpha-centauri-mission-stephen-hawking-yuri-milner-plan-send-tiny-spacecrafts-stars-1554511|title=Alpha Centauri mission: Stephen Hawking and Yuri Milner plan to send tiny spacecrafts &#91;sic&#93; to the stars|last=Osborne|first=Hannah|date=2016-04-12|work=International Business Times UK|access-date=2017-05-01}}</ref> Focusing within the solar system, he commented on NASA's plans to send a lander to Jupiter's moon [[Europa (moon)|Europa]], telling ''[[Gizmodo]]'' that "[t]he top priority of this lander mission will be to search for evidence of life on Europa," adding that "even if that main goal isn’t met, we will learn a great deal about the potential habitability of this icy moon, which will be essential for future, even more ambitious missions."<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://gizmodo.com/nasa-plans-to-drill-toward-europas-ocean-in-search-of-l-1792233535|title=NASA Plans to Drill Into Europa's Crust In Search of Life|last=Paoletta|first=Rae|date=2017-02-10|work=Gizmodo|access-date=2017-05-01|language=en-US}}</ref>
