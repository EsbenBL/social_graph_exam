'''Fathi Mohammed Baja''' ({{lang-ar|فتحي محمد البعجة}}), also called '''Fatih Baja''', is a [[Libya]]n academic and was a member of the [[National Transitional Council]] in charge of political affairs and representing [[Benghazi]].<ref name=list>{{cite web|title=National Transitional Council|url=http://ntclibya.com/InnerPage.aspx?SSID=7&ParentID=3&LangID=1|publisher=[[National Transitional Council]]|accessdate=25 August 2011|archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/61DEzFbAb?url=http://ntclibya.com/InnerPage.aspx?SSID=7&ParentID=3&LangID=1|archivedate=25 August 2011|location=[[Benghazi]]|year=2011|url-status=dead}}</ref> Today he is Libya's Ambassador to Canada.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.almasryalyoum.com/news/details/540862 |title=سفير ليبيا في كندا: "الإخوان" يسعون للانقضاض على السلطة في مصر عبر ليبيا (حوار) |last= |first= |date=10 October 2014 |access-date=23 January 2016|language= Arabic|trans-title= }}</ref>

Baja attended [[Cairo University]], [[Northeastern University]], then [[Mohammed V University]] in [[Morocco]], where he earned a [[PhD]] in [[political science]] at [[Mohammed V University]].<ref>Ro'ya newspaper, June 27; 2011, no. 9, p.5.</ref> He taught at [[Garyounis University]].<ref name=list/> He wrote the manifesto adopted by leaders to outline the basic goals of the [[2011 Libyan civil war]]: democracy and national unity.<ref>{{cite news|title=Wer kommt, wenn Gaddafi geht? |url=http://derstandard.at/1313025120790/Die-Zeit-danach-Wer-kommt-wenn-Gaddafi-geht |accessdate=26 August 2011 |newspaper=[[Der Standard]] |date=25 August 2011 |archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/61F0dNHc1?url=http://derstandard.at/1313025120790/Die-Zeit-danach-Wer-kommt-wenn-Gaddafi-geht |archivedate=27 August 2011 |location=[[Vienna]] |url-status=dead }}</ref> Baja represents the city of [[Benghazi]] on the [[National Transitional Council]] of [[Libya]]. He is also the member of the council in charge of political affairs, and as such heads the Political Affairs Advisory Committee.<ref name=list/> In this role, he has had direct contact with leaders
