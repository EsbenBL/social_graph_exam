'''Philippe Mario Aghion''' [[Fellow of the British Academy|FBA]] (born 17 August 1956) is a French [[economist]] who is a Professor at [[Collège de France|College de France]], at [[INSEAD]], and at the [[London School of Economics]]. He is also teaching at the Paris School of Economics. Philippe Aghion was formerly the Robert C. Waggoner Professor of Economics at [[Harvard University]]. Prior to that, he was a Professor at [[University College London]], an Official Fellow at [[Nuffield College, Oxford]], and an Assistant Professor at the [[Massachusetts Institute of Technology]] (MIT).

==Early life and education==
Aghion was born in Paris, the son of [[Gaby Aghion|Gaby]] and Raymond Aghion.<ref>Aghion, Philippe et Banerjee, Abhijit [https://books.google.com/books?id=G-FfKX_h-W0C&pg=PR5 ''Volatility And Growth'']. [[Oxford University Press]], 2005 (See [[dedication (publishing)|dedication]]: « ''To our parents Gaby and Raymond Aghion... »).</ref><ref>[http://www.accessmylibrary.com/article-1G1-304699298/then-and-now-gaby.html Then And Now. Gaby Aghion founder and Clare Waight Keller designer of Chloe SA.]  By Diderich, Joelle. WWD, September 28, 2012.</ref>

Aghion is a graduate of the [[École Normale Supérieure de Cachan]] ([[ENS Cachan]], Mathematics Section), has a [[Diplôme d'études approfondies]] (DEA) in Mathematical Economics from [[University of Paris 1 Pantheon-Sorbonne]], and a [[Doctor of Philosophy|Ph.D.]] from Harvard University (1987).

==Career==
His main research work is on growth and innovation. With [[Peter Howitt (economist)|Peter Howitt]], he developed the so-called "[[Schumpeter]]ian [[paradigm]]", and extended the paradigm in several directions; much of the resulting work is summarised in his joint book with Howitt entitled ''Endogenous Growth Theory''. and more recently in The Power of Creative Destruction. In 2019 he received the [[BBVA Foundation Frontiers of Knowledge Awards|BBVA Foundation Frontiers of Knowledge Award]] in Economics.{{citation needed|date=September 2021}}

He is a member of the American Academy of Arts and Science, and was elected as a [[Corresponding Fellow of the British Academy|Corresponding Fellow]] of the [[British Academy]] in 2015. He is also a fellow of the [[European Economic Association]].  He has been an editor of the ''[[Annual Review of Economics]]'' since 2018.<ref>{{cite web |title=Annual Review of Economics, Planning Editorial Committee - Volume 10, 2018 |url=https://www.annualreviews.org/db/directory?2018,economics |website=Annual Reviews Directory |access-date=15 September 2021}}</ref>

==Other activities==
Aghion was elected a Fellow of the [[American Academy of Arts and Sciences]] in 2009 and he is a member of the Executive and Supervisory Committee (ESC)] of [[CERGE-EI]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.cerge-ei.cz/governance/executive-and-supervisory-committee|title=CERGE-EI &#124; Executive and Supervisory Committee &#124; Governance Bodies &#124; People|website=www.cerge-ei.cz}}</ref><ref name=AAAS>{{cite web|title=Book of Members, 1780–2010: Chapter A|url=http://www.amacad.org/publications/BookofMembers/ChapterA.pdf|publisher=American Academy of Arts and Sciences|access-date=6 April 2011}}</ref> He also serves on the Scientific Advisory Board of the [[Max Planck Institute for Research on Collective Goods]].

Ahead of the [[2012 French presidential election]], Aghion co-signed an appeal of several economists in support of candidate [[François Hollande]].<ref>[https://www.lemonde.fr/idees/article/2012/04/17/nous-economistes-soutenons-hollande_1686249_3232.html Nous, économistes, soutenons Hollande] ''[[Le Monde]]'', April 17, 2012.</ref>

In 2016, Aghion was appointed by [[United Nations Secretary-General]] [[Ban Ki-moon]] to an expert group advising the High-Level Commission on Health Employment and Economic Growth, which was co-chaired by presidents [[François Hollande]] of [[France]] and [[Jacob Zuma]] of [[South Africa]].<ref>[https://www.who.int/hrh/com-heeg/hrh_heeg_bios/en/ High-Level Commission on Health Employment and Economic Growth – Expert Group] [[World Health Organization]].</ref> In 2021, he was appointed to the [[World Bank]]–[[International Monetary Fund]] High-Level Advisory Group (HLAG) on Sustainable and Inclusive Recovery and Growth, co-chaired by [[Mari Pangestu]], [[Ceyla Pazarbasioglu]], and [[Nicholas Stern]].<ref>[https://www.imf.org/en/News/Articles/2021/06/14/pr21175-world-bank-imf-launch-hlag-sustainable-inclusive-recovery-growth World Bank, IMF Launch High-Level Advisory Group on Sustainable and Inclusive Recovery and Growth] [[International Monetary Fund]], press release of June 15, 2021.</ref>
