The '''Narodowy Bank Polski''' ({{IPA-pl|narɔdɔvɨ bank pɔlski|pron}}; the '''National Bank of Poland'''), often abbreviated to '''NBP''', is the [[central bank]] of [[Poland]], founded in 1945. It controls the issuing of Poland's currency, the [[Polish złoty]]. The Bank is headquartered in [[Warsaw]], and has branches in 16 major Polish cities. The NBP represents Poland in the [[European System of Central Banks]], an [[European Union|EU]] organization.
[[File:Gmach Narodowego Banku Polskiego w Warszawie.jpg|thumb|right|260px|Headquarters of the National Bank of Poland in central [[Warsaw]], 2015]]

==History==
Although the bank has existed under its current name since 1945,<ref name="history">{{cite web|url=http://www.nbp.pl/homen.aspx?f=/en/onbp/informacje/historia_bankowosci.html|title = The history of central banking in Poland|date = 22 September 2014|publisher = Narodowy Bank Polski|access-date = 11 April 2016}}</ref> it is a continuation of two previous central banks, both named simply [[Bank of Poland]] (''Bank Polski''). The first one was founded in Warsaw in 1828 by Prince [[Franciszek Ksawery Drucki-Lubecki]]. An institution of the government of the [[Congress Poland|Kingdom of Poland]], it was entitled with issuance of the Polish currency as well as control over the credit rates. It was also entitled with a concession to operate foreign currencies and buy off credits issued by foreign companies and banks.

During the [[Second World War]] the gold reserves of Poland were transferred to [[Romania]], [[Great Britain]] and [[Canada]]. In November 1946, following the cancellation of Poland's wartime debt, the larger part of prewar gold reserves were returned to new [[Polish People's Republic|communist authorities]]. In 1947, the gold deposited in Romania in 1939 was returned, while the former [[Bank of Poland]] itself was closed down and finally in 1952 absorbed by the newly created Narodowy Bank Polski.

The latter was one of two banks allowed to operate in Poland's postwar [[planned economy]]. It had a monopoly for currency, credits and accumulation of savings. The other bank, [[PKO Bank Polski]], was responsible for private accounts. After the fall of the communist system in 1989, the [[market economy]] was reintroduced and the NBP limited its functions to currency control and supervision of other, privately controlled banks.

==Structure==
The structure and functioning of the Narodowy Bank Polski are regulated by article 227<ref>Art. 227 Konstytucji Rzeczypospolitej Polskiej z dnia 2 kwietnia 1997 r. ([http://isap.sejm.gov.pl/isap.nsf/DocDetails.xsp?id=WDU19970780483 Dz.U. z&nbsp;1997&nbsp;r. nr&nbsp;78,&#32;poz.&nbsp;483]).</ref> of the [[Constitution of Poland]] of 1997 and the Narodowy Bank Polski Act<ref>{{cite journal|title=POWOŁANIE I ODWOŁANIE PREZESA NARODOWEGO BANKU POLSKIEGO W KONSTYTUCJI RZECZYPOSPOLITEJ POLSKIEJ I USTAWIE O NARODOWYM BANKU POLSKIM|author=Piotr Stanisławiszyn|journal=Ekonomia i Prawo|volume=7|date=2011-12-31|issn=1898-2255|doi=10.12775/eip.2011.009|doi-access=free}}</ref> of the same year.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nbp.pl/akty_prawne/ustawa_o_nbp/ustawa_o_nbp.pdf|title = Organizacja NBP|date = 29 August 1997|language=pl|work = Ustawa o Narodowym Banku Polskim|publisher = Narodowy Bank Polski|access-date = 18 April 2013}}</ref> The President of the NBP is appointed by the [[Sejm]], at the request of the [[President of the Republic of Poland]], for a term of six years. The NBP President is responsible for the organisation and functioning of the Polish's central bank. The same person cannot serve as President of the NBP for more than two terms of office. Apart from his function as the superior of the NBP staff, he is also the chairman of the Monetary Policy Council, the NBP Management Board and the Commission for Banking Supervision. He is also responsible for representing Poland in international banking and financial institutions.

In 2019, the average employment at the National Bank of Poland on full-time amounts was 3312 people.<ref>{{cite web|title=Sprawozdanie z działalności Narodowego Banku Polskiego w 2019 roku. Druk nr 394|url=http://www.sejm.gov.pl/Sejm9.nsf/druk.xsp?documentId=F6A5FD74FD26C1D6C12585770048FB49|agency=sejm.gov.pl|pages=10|date=28 May 2020|access-date=2020-08-30}}</ref>
