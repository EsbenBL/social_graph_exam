'''Edward Beiser''' (March 10, 1942 in [[New York City]] &ndash; September 4, 2009 in [[Warwick, Rhode Island]]) was an [[United States|American]] [[political scientist]], constitutional scholar, law professor and [[medical ethics|medical ethicist]] who taught at [[Brown University]] from 1968 to 2003.<ref name="jvhri">{{cite web |url=http://www.jvhri.org/detail/1739.html |title=Dr. Edward Beiser |work=The Jewish Voice and Herald |date=Oct 2, 2009 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20091017030021/http://www.jvhri.org/detail/1739.html |archivedate=Oct 17, 2009 }}</ref> He also served as associate dean of Brown's medical school.<ref>''Providence Journal'', Jan 10, 1998</ref>

Beiser was widely regarded as an authority on the [[Rhode Island Supreme Court]].<ref>''Providence Journal'', May 3, 1989</ref>  He was a court-appointed special master during the trial of [[Klaus von Bulow]].

Beiser was a graduate of the [[City College of New York]] and held a Ph.D. in political science from [[Princeton University]] and a law degree from [[Harvard Law School]].<ref name="jvhri"/>  He taught at [[Williams College]] from 1965 to 1968 prior to joining the Brown faculty.<ref name="jvhri"/>

==Select publications==
