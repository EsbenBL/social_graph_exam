'''Manuel Velez Pangilinan''', {{post-nominals|post-noms=[[Order of Lakandula|CL]]}} {{post-nominals|post-noms=[[Order of Lakandula|GCrL]]}} (born July 14, 1946), also known by his initials '''MVP''', is a [[Filipino people|Filipino]] businessman and sports patron. He is the managing director and CEO of [[First Pacific|First Pacific Company Limited]], a Hong Kong-based investment management and holding company with operations in the Asia-Pacific region. Pangilinan is also First Pacific's chairman for the group's investments in the Philippines, such as [[Metro Pacific Investments Corporation]], [[PLDT]], and Philex Mining Corporation. He is chairman Emeritus of the [[Samahang Basketbol ng Pilipinas]] (SBP) and was its first president, having served two consecutive terms from 2007 to 2018.

==Education==

Pangilinan completed his primary and secondary [[Formal_learning|education]] at [[San Beda College]]. He graduated ''[[Latin honors#Types|cum laude]]'' from the [[Ateneo de Manila University]] with a Bachelor of Arts degree in Economics. Pangilinan won a competition sponsored by [[Procter & Gamble]] for a scholarship to the [[Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania]] and graduated in 1968 with a [[Master of Business Administration]] degree.<ref name="businesstip">{{cite web |last1=Sabornido |first1=Lyza |title=Manny Pangilinan: A Filipino Entrepreneur's Success Story |url=https://businesstips.ph/manny-pangilinan-a-filipino-entrepreneurs-success-story/ |website=BusinessTips.com.ph |access-date=September 23, 2019 |date=July 22, 2012}}{{better source needed|date=September 2019}}</ref>{{better source needed|date=September 2019}}

==Career==
Pangilinan's first job was as the Executive Assistant to the President of the Philippine Investment Management Consultants, Inc. (PHINMA) where he served for six years.<ref name=journeytosuccess>{{cite web |title=Manny Pangilinan's journey to success |url=https://primer.com.ph/business/2017/03/19/manny-pangilinans-journey-to-success/ |website=Philippine Primer |access-date=September 23, 2019 |date=March 19, 2017}}</ref>

In 1976, Pangilinan relocated to Hong Kong as executive director at Bancom International, an investment bank, where he gained experience in international finance. He then went on to the [[American Express]] in Hong Kong as an investment banker.<ref name=journeytosuccess/>

In Hong Kong, Pangilinan co-founded [[First Pacific]] in 1981 with [[Sudono Salim]] and Salim's son, Anthoni Salim. In 1987, Pangilinan established [[Metro Pacific]] as First Pacific's investment arm in the Philippines.<ref name=journeytosuccess/> As chairman of Metro Pacific, Pangilinan contributed to nation building through major investments in power distribution and energy innovations ([[Meralco]]), urban water concession ([[Maynilad Water Services]]), tollways (Metro Pacific Tollways Corporation), upgrading hospitals and health services (Metro Pacific Investments Corporation). He is also Chairman of [[5 (TV channel)|TV5]] and Philex Mining Corporation.{{citation needed|date=September 2019}}

In 1998, First Pacific acquired [[PLDT]], the largest telecommunications firm in the Philippines. The company, which had once been troubled by debt and technological issues,<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.chicagotribune.com/news/ct-xpm-1993-02-14-9303181972-story.html|title=TELEPHONE MONOPOLY HANGS UP PHILIPPINES|first=Uli Schmetzer, Chicago|last=Tribune|website=chicagotribune.com}}</ref> improved under First Pacific's management to become the nation's leading provider of digital communications.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://pldthome.com/news-media/2018/04/06/pldt-is-undisputed-leader-in-home-broadband|title=PLDT is undisputed leader in home broadband|website=pldthome.com}}</ref>

Pangilinan is also a major patron of Philippine sports. He owns three [[Philippine Basketball Association|PBA]] teams – [[TNT KaTropa]], the [[Meralco Bolts]] and the [[NLEX Road Warriors]]. He founded the [[Samahang Basketbol ng Pilipinas]] (SBP), the national sport association for basketball in the Philippines. He served as the first president of SBP for two consecutive terms (2007 to 2016). He is currently the chairman emeritus of SBP.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.sbp.ph/a-message-from-the-sbps-chairman-emeritus-manuel-v-pangilinan/|title=A message from SBP Chairman Emeritus, Manuel V. Pangilinan – SAMAHANG BASKETBOL NG PILIPINAS INC.}}</ref> He was elected to the Central Board of [[FIBA]] in 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.rappler.com/sports/by-sport/basketball/fiba/69031-manny-v-pangilina-fiba-board|title=Manny V. Pangilinan elected to FIBA board|first=Jane|last=Bracher|work=SPIN.PH|date=September 14, 2014|access-date=March 1, 2019}}</ref> Moreover, his MVP Sports Foundation has supported other Philippine athletes, some of whom won gold medals in the [[2018 Asian Games]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.foxsports.ph/offbeat/905326/mvp-sports-foundation-seeks-to-further-hone-pinoy-athletes-for-international-tourneys/|title=MVP Sports Foundation seeks to further hone Pinoy athletes for international tourneys|date=September 6, 2018}}</ref>

==Involvement in sports==
Pangilinan is a sports patron having been raised from a basketball loving family. His mother was a fan of the San Beda Lions basketball team and was best friends with [[Caloy Loyzaga]]. His father played the sport along with baseball and tennis. Pangilinan himself plays badminton. Through the MVP Foundation, Pangilinan funds initiatives on badminton, boxing, golf and taekwondo.<ref name="mvpnotgivingup">{{cite news |last1=Catacutan |first1=Dodo |title=Why MVP isn't giving up on the country's basketball dream |url=https://www.spin.ph/life/guide/why-manny-pangilinan-refuses-to-give-up-on-the-country-s-basketball-dream-a791-20190922 |access-date=September 23, 2019 |work=Sports Interactive Network Philippines |date=September 22, 2019}}</ref>
