'''Eszter Hargittai''' (born 1973<ref>{{cite LAF|id=no2003-73054}}</ref> in [[Budapest]], [[Hungary]]) is a [[communication studies]] scholar and Professor at the [[University of Zurich]].

==Biography==
She holds a [[Bachelor's degree|B.A.]] in [[Sociology]] from [[Smith College]] and a [[Doctor of Philosophy|Ph.D.]] in Sociology from [[Princeton University]] where she was a Wilson Scholar.

Before moving to Zurich, she was Delaney Family Professor of Communication Studies and Faculty Associate of the Institute for Policy Research (IPR) at Northwestern University where she is still affiliated as Adjunct Professor and Fellow at IPR.

She was a fellow at the [[Center for Advanced Study in the Behavioral Sciences]] at Stanford (2006–07), a fellow at the Institute for International Integration Studies, Trinity College Dublin (2007), and a fellow at [[Harvard]]'s [[Berkman Klein Center for Internet & Society]] (2008–09) where she was on the Faculty Advisory Board until 2020.<ref>[http://cyber.law.harvard.edu/people/EHargittai Entry at the Berkman Center for Internet & Society page]</ref> 
She is a member of the group blog [[Crooked Timber]] (since 2003).<ref>[http://crookedtimber.org/2003/09/25/say-hello-to-eszter/ Say Hello to Eszter] entry on Crooked Timber</ref>

Her research focuses on the social and policy implications of information technologies with a particular interest in how IT may contribute to or alleviate social inequalities.<ref>Copies of Eszter Hargittai's [http://www.webuse.org/pubs research papers]</ref> She has studied the differences in people's Web-use skills, the evolution of search engines <ref>[http://jcmc.indiana.edu/vol12/issue3/ The Social, Political, Economic, and Cultural Dimensions of Search Engines] Special Theme of the Journal of Computer-Mediated Communication</ref> and the organization and presentation of online content, political uses of information technologies, how IT are influencing the types of cultural products people consume, and geocaching.

Her work is regularly featured in the media.<ref>[http://www.webuse.org/press Web Use Project Press Coverage]</ref> She was interviewed about the Internet and its social implications on [[CNNfn]]'s ''The Flip Side'' on April 29, 2004.
