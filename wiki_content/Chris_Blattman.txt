{{Short description|American economist and political scientist}}
'''Christopher Blattman''' is an economist and political scientist working on international development and policy, as well as a blogger on international economics and politics.<ref name=about>{{cite web|url=http://chrisblattman.com/about/|title = About me|last = Blattman|first = Chris}}</ref> He is the Ramalee E. Pearson Professor of Global Conflict Studies at the [[University of Chicago]]'s [[Harris School of Public Policy Studies]] and [http://www.thepearsoninstitute.org/ The Pearson Institute for the Study and Resolution of Global Conflicts].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://news.uchicago.edu/article/2016/05/31/pearson-institute-names-inaugural-faculty-members|publisher=University of Chicago|accessdate=9 June 2016|title = The Pearson Institute names inaugural faculty members}}</ref> He is also a research associate of the [[National Bureau of Economic Research]], a non-resident fellow with the [[Center for Global Development]],<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.cgdev.org/expert/chris-blattman|title=CGD Chris Blattman Profile}}</ref> and a Board Member and academic lead of the Crime and Violence Section of the [[Abdul Latif Jameel Poverty Action Lab]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.povertyactionlab.org/blattman|title=J-PAL Chris Blattman Profile}}</ref>

==Education==

Blattman received a BA in Economics from the [[University of Waterloo]]. He completed a Master's in Public Administration and International Development (MPA/ID) from the [[Kennedy School of Government]] at [[Harvard University]], and a Ph.D. in [[economics]] from the [[University of California, Berkeley]].

==Career==

Blattman was a resident fellow at the [[Center for Global Development]] in 2007-2008 and a faculty member at [[Yale University]] from 2008 to 2012 before moving to [[Columbia University]] in 2012, where he became an associate professor of international affairs and political science in July 2014.<ref name=about/> He moved to The [[University of Chicago]] in 2016. He blogs on his personal website<ref name=blog>{{cite web|url=http://chrisblattman.com |title=Chris Blattman: International development, economics, politics, and policy}}</ref> and for the Washington Post's Monkey Cage.<ref name=monkey>{{cite web|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/people/chris-blattman|work=[[The Washington Post]]|title=Chris Blattman: Contributor, Monkey Cage }}</ref>

===Research and writing===

Blattman has used field experiments to argue that poor and unemployed young people in low-income countries tend to invest cash in small enterprises and thus raise their incomes. He advocated for cash transfers to the poor in a 2014 op-ed in ''[[The New York Times]]''<ref name=nyt>{{cite news | url=https://www.nytimes.com/2014/06/30/opinion/let-them-eat-cash.html?_r=0 | title = Let them eat cash | newspaper = The New York Times | date = June 29, 2014 | last1 = Blattman | first1 = Christopher }}</ref> as well as a 2014 ''[[Foreign Affairs]]'' magazine article.<ref name=foreign>{{cite magazine | url=http://www.foreignaffairs.com/articles/141214/christopher-blattman-and-paul-niehaus/show-them-the-money | title = Show Them the Money | date = May–June 2014 | last1 = Blattman | first1 = Christopher | last2 = Niehaus | first2 = Paul }}</ref> This work has also been covered by [[National Public Radio]],<ref name=npr>{{cite web|url=https://www.npr.org/blogs/money/2013/11/08/243967328/episode-494-what-happens-when-you-just-give-money-to-poor-peoplethe |title = What Happens When You Just Give Money To Poor People? |date = November 8, 2013 |publisher = NPR Planet Money}}</ref> ''[[The New York Times]]'',<ref name=economix>{{cite web|url=https://economix.blogs.nytimes.com/2013/06/20/ending-poverty-by-giving-the-poor-money/|title = Ending Poverty by Giving the Poor Money|last = Lowrey|first = Annie|date = June 20, 2013 |work=The New York Times Economix blog}}</ref> [[Slate (magazine)|Slate]],<ref name=slate>{{cite web | url=http://www.slate.com/articles/business/moneybox/2013/05/unconditional_cash_transfers_giving_money_to_the_poor_may_be_the_best_tool.html | title =The Best and Simplest Way to Fight Global Poverty |last = Yglesias|first = Mathew|date = May 29, 2013}}</ref> and the ''[[Financial Times]]'',<ref name=ft>{{cite web|url=http://www.ft.com/intl/cms/s/2/a1101f00-e8fd-11e2-aead-00144feabdc0.html#axzz2YrNfbJGn | title =The Undercover Economist: How to give money away |last = Harford|first = Tim|date = July 12, 2013}}</ref>.

Together with economist [[Stefan Dercon]], Blattman ran a [[randomized controlled trial]] in Ethiopia that investigated the impact of low-skill industrial jobs.<ref>{{Cite journal|last1=Blattman|first1=C.|last2=Dercon|first2=S.|date=2016|title=Occupational choice in early industrializing societies: Experimental evidence on the income and health effects of industrial and entrepreneurial work|url=https://ora.ox.ac.uk/objects/uuid:2caf91e5-0a07-4125-ac1a-4d494b4af256|language=en}}</ref> The research was covered by [[Our World in Data]] and the ''[[Financial Times]]''.<ref>{{Cite journal|last1=Roser|first1=Max|last2=Ortiz-Ospina|first2=Esteban|date=2013-05-25|title=Global Extreme Poverty|url=https://ourworldindata.org/extreme-poverty|journal=Our World in Data}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ft.com/content/2d9d8c2a-958d-11e6-a80e-bcd69f323a8b|title=The sweatshop dilemma|last=Harford|first=Tim|date=|website=|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref>

==Personal==
Blattman grew up in [[Ontario|Ontario, Canada]], in a family of bank managers.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Blattman|first=Chris|date=2010-06-04|title=Should you be an accountant?|url=https://chrisblattman.com/2010/06/04/should-you-be-an-accountant/|access-date=2021-10-13|website=Chris Blattman|language=en-US}}</ref> He is married to Jeannie Annan,<ref>{{Cite web|title=Best wishes to the Blattman/Annan family|url=https://blogs.worldbank.org/impactevaluations/best-wishes-to-the-blattmanannan-family|access-date=2021-10-13|website=blogs.worldbank.org|language=en}}</ref> with whom he has two children.
