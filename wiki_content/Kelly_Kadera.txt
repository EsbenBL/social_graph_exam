'''Kelly M. Kadera''' is an [[America]]n [[political scientist]], currently a professor at the [[University of Iowa]]. She studies [[Peace and conflict studies|international conflict]], [[Democratic backsliding|democratic survival]], and gender in academia using [[Positive political theory|formal theory]], [[Dynamical system|dynamic modeling]], and empirical methods.

==Early career and education==
Kadera attended [[Wells College]] in [[Aurora, Cayuga County, New York|Aurora, New York]], receiving a BA in government in 1987. In 1988 she completed an MA in political science at the [[University of Illinois, Urbana]], and in 1995 received a PhD there, also in political science.<ref name=profile>{{cite web |url=https://www.kellykadera.com/ |author=Kelly Kadera |title=Kelly Kadera Profile |access-date=8 January 2020}}</ref> After finishing her PhD, Kadera became a professor at the [[University of Iowa]] in 1995.<ref name=profile/>

==Career==
In 2001, Kadera published the book ''The Power-Conflict Story: A Dynamic Model of Interstate Rivalry''. The book uses dynamic modeling to capture patterns in the behavior of major world powers since 1816, focusing specifically on the rivalries between those powers.<ref name=lemke03>{{cite journal |last=Lemke |first=Douglas |title=Review of The Power-Conflict Story: A Dynamic Model of Interstate Rivalry |journal=The Journal of Politics |volume=65 |number=1 |date=February 2003 |pages=257–259|doi=10.1111/1468-2508.00002 |s2cid=153165209 }}</ref> The book combines theories about balance of power and power transitions into a formal model which, through numerical simulations,<ref name=mitchell03>{{cite journal |last=McLaughlin Mitchell |first=Sara |journal=Perspectives on Politics |title=Review of The Power-Conflict Story: A Dynamic Model of Interstate Rivalry |volume=1 |number=1 |date=March 2003 |pages=235–236}}</ref> produces more than a dozen specific hypotheses.<ref name=lemke03/> Kadera then validates these predictions using empirical data.<ref name=mitchell03/> For this book, Kadera won the 2001 Best Book in Conflict Processes Award from the [[American Political Science Association]].<ref name=bestbook>{{cite web |url=https://www.librarything.com/bookaward/American+Political+Science+Association%27s+Conflict+Processes+Section%27s+Best+Book+Award |title=Book awards: American Political Science Association's Conflict Processes Section's Best Book Award |year=2014 |access-date=8 January 2020 |publisher=LibraryThing}}</ref> The book arose from Kadera's dissertation, for which she had previously won the 1996 Peace Science Society’s Walter Isard Dissertation Award.<ref name=lemke03/>

Kadera's research on topics like the survival of democratic regimes, international conflict management, and the evolution of war and peace over time<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.ft.com/content/c88ff51d-4cce-3c18-8653-7e555eef2f0e |title=No, Russia is not in decline – at least not any more and not yet |work=Financial Times |date=28 April 2016 |last=Saradzhyan |first=Simon |access-date=8 January 2020}}</ref> has been published in journals like the [[American Journal of Political Science]], [[International Studies Quarterly]], and [[Conflict Management and Peace Science]].<ref name=meetapsr/>

In addition to research on international conflict and the survival of democratic regimes, including pieces on gender and conflict studies,<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/monkey-cage/wp/2017/09/29/how-do-women-affect-conflict-peace-and-security-the-answer-depends-on-exactly-what-youre-asking/ |last=Tucker |first=Joshua |title=How do women affect conflict, peace, and security? That depends on exactly what you're asking |work=The Washington Post |date=29 September 2017 |access-date=8 January 2020}}</ref> Kadera has notably worked on equity issues within world politics. She has published on the undervaluation of research done by women in the study of world politics,<ref>{{cite journal |journal=International Studies Perspectives |title=The Social Underpinnings of Women's Worth in the Study of World Politics: Culture, Leader Emergence, and Coauthorship |last=Kadera |first=Kelly M. |date=18 November 2013 |volume=14 |number=4 |pages=463–475|doi=10.1111/insp.12028 }}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.insidehighered.com/blogs/university-venus/addressing-syllabus-gap |title=Addressing the Syllabus Gap |last=Leanna Henshaw |first=Alexis |work=Inside Higher Education |date=17 October 2013 |access-date=8 January 2020}}</ref> and has promoted the mentoring of women who are junior scholars in international relations. She was the first organizer of the Pay It Forward program at the [[International Studies Association]], which organizes a workshop that connects women who are already established researchers in international studies as mentors for women who are junior scholars in the field.<ref name=meetapsr/> Kadera is also a founder and organizer of the Journeys in World Politics program, which has a similar mandate.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.saramitchell.org/journeys.html |title=Journeys in World Politics |last1=Kadera |first1=Kelly M. |last2=McLaughlin Mitchell |first2=Sara |access-date=8 January 2020}}</ref> For this work she received the 2016 Susan Northcutt Award from the International Studies Association, which "recognizes a person who actively works towards recruiting and advancing women and other minorities in the profession, and whose spirit is inclusive, generous and conscientious."<ref name=northcutt>{{cite web |url=https://www.isanet.org/Programs/Awards/Susan-Stoudinger-Northcutt/Past-Recipients |title=Susan Northcutt Award Winners |year=2019 |access-date=8 January 2020}}</ref>

Kadera is a member of the 2020-2024 editorial leadership of the [[American Political Science Review]],<ref>{{cite web |url=https://politicalsciencenow.com/apsa-announces-the-new-editorial-team-for-the-american-political-science-review/ |title=APSA Announces the New Editorial Team for the American Political Science Review |publisher=American Political Science Association |date=26 July 2019 |access-date=31 December 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |last=Marshall |first=Jenna |url=https://www.santafe.edu/news-center/news/righting-balance-new-apsr-editors-meet-sfi-discuss-gender-and-race-scientific-publishing |date=2019-07-26 |title=Righting the balance: New APSR editors meet at SFI to discuss gender and race in scientific publishing | publisher=Santa Fe Institute |access-date=25 December 2019}}</ref> which is the most selective political science journal.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://polisci.utk.edu/newsletter/fall2019/austin.php |title=Cutting-Edge Research Agenda | publisher=University of Tennessee Knoxville |access-date=25 December 2019}}</ref> She has also been an editor at the [[International Studies Review]].<ref name=meetapsr>{{cite web |url=https://politicalsciencenow.com/meet-2020-apsr-editor-kelly-m-kadera-of-university-of-iowa/ |publisher=American Political Science Association |work=Political Science Now |title=Meet 2020 APSR Editor, Kelly M. Kadera of University of Iowa |date=29 August 2019 |access-date=8 January 2020}}</ref>
