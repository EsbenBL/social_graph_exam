'''Julia R. Azari''' is an American [[political scientist]]. She is a professor of political science at [[Marquette University]], where she is also the Assistant Chair of the Department of Political Science. Azari studies the [[President of the United States|American Presidency]] and [[political parties in the United States]], with particular attention to [[Partisan (politics)#United States|partisanship in the United States]] and how these systems have changed since the early 20th century. Azari also engages in public communication about political science, including as a frequent contributor to the politics coverage of the data journalism website ''[[FiveThirtyEight]]'', a writer of the blog ''Mischiefs of Faction'', and a host of the political science podcast ''Politics in Question''.

==Education and early work==
Azari attended the [[University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign]].<ref name=profile>{{cite web |url=https://www.marquette.edu/political-science/directory/julia-azari.php |title=Julia Azari, PhD |publisher=Marquette University |access-date=April 9, 2020}}</ref> She originally intended to become a [[Political journalism|political journalist]], but realized that she enjoyed academic political science.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://pol.illinois.edu/spotlight/alumni/julia-azari |title=Julia Azari |publisher=University of Illinois |access-date=April 9, 2020}}</ref> She graduated with a B.A. degree in political science in 2002.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://uofi150.news-gazette.com/people/julia-azari |title=Julia Azari Profile |publisher=University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign |work=University of Illinois News-Gazette |access-date=April 9, 2020}}</ref> She then received an M.Phil, an M.A., and a Ph.D. in political science at [[Yale University]].<ref name=profile/>

==Career==
===Research===
In 2014 Azari published the book ''Delivering the People's Message: The Changing Politics of the Presidential Mandate'', which studies the history of the idea of a [[Mandate (politics)|political mandate]] for the President of the United States, from [[Herbert Hoover]] in 1928 to the election of [[Barack Obama]] in 2008.<ref>{{cite journal |first=Gemma |last=Horton |title=Delivering the People's Message: The Changing Politics of the Presidential Mandate |journal=Information, Communication & Society |volume=21 |issue=12 |date=February 23, 2018 |pages=1836–1838 |doi=10.1080/1369118X.2018.1442490|s2cid=149010227 }}</ref> Azari uses [[content analysis]] on presidents' communications to analyze how presidents have used the notion of a presidential mandate, showing that over the last several decades presidents have increasingly invoked their mandate to defend the legitimacy of the office.<ref>{{cite journal |first=R.P |last=Seyb |title=Azari, Julia R. Delivering the people's message: the changing politics of the presidential mandate. Cornell, 2014. 206p index afp ISBN 9780801452246 cloth, $39.95 |journal=CHOICE: Current Reviews for Academic Libraries |volume=52 |issue=4 |date=December 1, 2014 |pages=701}}</ref>

Together with William D. Adler, Azari won the 2019 Founder's Best Paper Award from the [[American Political Science Association]] for their working paper "The Party Decides (Who the Vice President Will Be)". The Founder's Award "is given for the best paper on executive politics authored by a PhD-holding scholar at the previous year's" meeting of the American Political Science Association.<ref name=found>{{cite journal |url=https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/ps-political-science-and-politics/article/2019-apsa-organized-section-awards/0AB2943B3604C9CAD07DB2B194BC3B4C/core-reader |title=Founder's Best Paper Award |journal=PS: Political Science & Politics |publisher=American Political Science Association |year=2019 |volume=52 |issue=4 |pages=838–858 |doi=10.1017/S1049096519001550 |access-date=April 9, 2020|doi-access=free }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.marquette.edu/political-science/news-archive.php |title=2019-20 Academic Year News |publisher=Marquette University |year=2020 |access-date=April 9, 2020}}</ref>

Some of Azari's work in science communication, including posts on the ''Mischiefs of Faction'' blog, have also in turn been cited in academic literature.<ref>{{cite journal |author1=Christopher M Federico |author2=Agnieszka Golec de Zavala |title=Collective Narcissism and the 2016 US Presidential Vote |journal=Public Opinion Quarterly |volume=82 |issue=1 |date=February 21, 2018 |pages=110–121 |doi=10.1093/poq/nfx048|url=https://research.gold.ac.uk/21878/1/Federico%20%26%20Golec%20POQ%202017.pdf }}</ref><ref>{{cite journal |author1=Peter Buisseret |author2=Richard Van Weelden |title=Crashing the Party? Elites, Outsiders, and Elections |journal=American Journal of Political Science |year=2020 |volume=64 |issue=2 |pages=356–370 |doi=10.1111/ajps.12457}}</ref><ref>{{cite book |editor1=Frances E. Lee |editor2=Nolan McCarty |author1=Daniel Schlozman |author2=Sam Rosenfeld |title=The Hollow Parties |page=353}}</ref>

===Science communication===
Azari has been a frequent contributor to the data journalism website ''FiveThirtyEight'' since 2016,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://fivethirtyeight.com/contributors/julia-azari/page/11/ |title=All posts by Julia Azari |publisher=FiveThirthyEight |year=2020 |access-date=April 9, 2020}}</ref> both as an author of articles and as a guest on the ''FiveThirtyEight Politics Podcast''.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://fivethirtyeight.com/features/politics-podcast-will-trump-face-a-primary-challenge/ |title=Politics Podcast: Will Trump Face A Primary Challenge? |publisher=FiveThirtyEight |date=January 10, 2019 |access-date=April 9, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://fivethirtyeight.com/features/politics-podcast-what-does-it-mean-to-be-electable/ |title=Politics Podcast: What Does It Mean To Be 'Electable'? |publisher=FiveThirtyEight |date=January 10, 2019 |access-date=April 9, 2020}}</ref> Azari regularly writes for ''Mischiefs of Faction'', which was founded as an independent political science blog in 2012, and for a period after 2015 was incorporated into ''[[Vox (website)|Vox]]''.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.vox.com/mischiefs-of-faction/2015/9/9/9280495/mischiefs-of-faction |title=Mischiefs of Faction, now on Vox |date=September 9, 2015 |work=Vox |last=Azari |first=Julia |access-date=April 8, 2020}}</ref> Azari has also appeared as an expert on television panels and interviews, including appearances on [[C-SPAN]] dating back to 2009,<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.c-span.org/search/?searchtype=Videos&sort=Newest&personid[]=9266357 |title=Julia Azari |publisher=C-SPAN |year=2018 |access-date=April 9, 2020}}</ref> as well as on radio stations like [[Wisconsin Public Radio]]<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.wpr.org/people/julia-azari |title=Julia Azari |publisher=Wisconsin Public Radio |year=2020 |access-date=April 9, 2020}}</ref> and [[KCRW]].<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.kcrw.com/people/julia-azari |title=Julia Azari |publisher=KCRW Radio |year=2020 |access-date=April 9, 2020}}</ref> She has also directed or engaged in public events about politics.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://resources.twc.edu/articles/introducing-campaign-2020-dnc-faculty-director-julia-azari |title=Introducing Campaign 2020 Faculty Director for the DNC, Dr. Julia Azari |publisher=The Washington Center |date=June 13, 2019 |access-date=April 9, 2020}}</ref>



In 2019, Azari was the recipient of the inaugural Best Public-Facing Scholarship Award from the American Political Science Association, which recognizes the "best public-facing scholarship published in the previous calendar year", including "blog posts and popular press publications intended for a broad public audience".<ref>{{cite journal |url=https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/ps-political-science-and-politics/article/2019-apsa-organized-section-awards/0AB2943B3604C9CAD07DB2B194BC3B4C/core-reader |title=Best Public-Facing Scholarship |journal=PS: Political Science & Politics |publisher=American Political Science Association |year=2019 |volume=52 |issue=4 |pages=838–858 |doi=10.1017/S1049096519001550 |access-date=April 9, 2020|doi-access=free }}</ref> Azari has also been recognized for her public discussions of the role and value of political science communication, and the undervaluing of communication work by some of the evaluation criteria commonly used by universities.<ref name=bps>{{cite web |url=https://medium.com/@nberlat/we-need-more-support-for-public-facing-scholarship-b706b85862b7 |title=We Need More Support For Public Facing Scholarship |last=Berlatsky |first=Noah |date=March 25, 2019 |access-date=April 9, 2020}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://marquettewire.org/4014420/news/mu-omits-woman-professors-title-but-not-mens/ |title=MU omits woman professor's title, but not men's |date=July 17, 2019 |work=Marquette Wire |author1=Sydney Czyzon |author2=Sarah Lipo |access-date=April 9, 2020}}</ref>
