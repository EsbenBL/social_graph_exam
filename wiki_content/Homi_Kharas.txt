'''Homi Kharas''' is a British economist who has been a Senior Fellow and Deputy Director for the Global Economy and Development program at the [[Brookings Institution]] since 2005.<ref>Brookings Institution, Fellows, URL: http://www.brookings.edu/experts/kharash</ref>

==Early life and education==
Kharas holds a PhD in economics from [[Harvard University]] and has an M.A. and B.A. from King's College, [[University of Cambridge]].

==Career==
===World Bank===
Kharas spent 26 years at the [[World Bank]], serving for seven years as chief economist for the [[World Bank]]’s East Asia and Pacific region<ref>[https://www.nytimes.com/2005/04/28/business/worldbusiness/world-bank-raises-east-asia-growth-outlook.html World Bank raises East Asia growth outlook] ''[[New York Times]]'', April 28, 2005.</ref> and as Director for Poverty Reduction and Economic Management, Finance and Private Sector Development. Along with Indermit Gill, he developed the novel concept of [http://documents.worldbank.org/curated/en/2015/08/24954519/middle-income-trap-turns-ten 'middle income traps'.]

From 1990-1991, Kharas was a Senior Partner with [[Jeffrey Sachs|Jeff Sachs]] and Associates, advising governments in [[Eastern Europe]] and the [[Soviet Union]] on transition.

===Brookings Institution===
Kharas began his career at the [[Brookings Institution]] in 2005 at the [https://web.archive.org/web/20150920232027/http://www.brookings.edu/about/centers/wolfensohn/wolfensohn-center-for-development-working-paper Wolfensohn Center for Development], where he pioneered foreign aid reform analyses, measuring the volatility of aid,<ref>Kharas (2006), Measuring the volatility of aid, Brookings Working Paper, available at: http://www.brookings.edu/research/papers/2008/07/aid-volatility-kharas</ref> quality of foreign aid,<ref>Kharas et al, Quality of Official Development Assistance (QuODA), available at: {{cite web |url=http://www.brookings.edu/about/programs/global/quoda |title=Archived copy |accessdate=2015-09-16 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304083120/http://www.brookings.edu/about/programs/global/quoda |archivedate=2016-03-04 }}</ref> new players in development, including individuals and foundations, and helped develop the Center's strategy along that of the Global Economy and Development Program. Kharas is a recognized foreign aid expert and has advised donors, foundations and emerging/developing country governments on best practices in the area.

Kharas is principal investigator of a number of grants to Brookings, including the [[Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation]] sponsored, Ending Rural Hunger and [http://www.brookings.edu/about/projects/development-assistance/brookings-blum-roundtable Brookings Blum Roundtable], to name a few.

He has served as the lead author and [http://www.post2015hlp.org/category/secretariat/ executive secretary of the secretariat supporting the High Level Panel], co-chaired by [[Ellen Johnson Sirleaf]], [[Susilo Bambang Yudhoyono]] and [[David Cameron]], advising [[United Nations Secretary General]] [[Ban Ki-moon]] on the [[Post-2015 Development Agenda]] (2012-2013). The report, “[http://www.post2015hlp.org/the-report/ A New Global Partnership: Eradicate Poverty and Transform Economies through Sustainable Development]”<ref>United Nations (2013), Post 2015 Development HLPE Report, available at: http://www.post2015hlp.org/the-report/</ref> was presented on May 30, 2013, and served as the basis for [[Sustainable Development Goals]] discussions.

He has recently served as a member on the International Panel Review Committee on [[Malaysia]]’s economic and governance transformation programs (2012); the post-Busan Advisory Group to the [[OECD|Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development]]'s [[Development Assistance Committee]] co-chairs (2011); the National Economic Advisory Council to [[Prime Minister of Malaysia|Prime Minister]] [[Najib Razak]] of [[Malaysia]] (2009–10); and a member of the Working Group for the [http://go.worldbank.org/LS3NIIIJF0 Commission on Growth and Development], chaired by Professor A. [[Michael Spence]] (2007–10).<ref>Commission on Growth and Development, available at: http://go.worldbank.org/LS3NIIIJF0</ref> He was a Non-Resident Fellow of the [[OECD Development Centre]] (2009). In June 2021, he was appointed to the [[World Bank]]–[[International Monetary Fund]] High-Level Advisory Group (HLAG) on Sustainable and Inclusive Recovery and Growth, co-chaired by [[Mari Pangestu]], [[Ceyla Pazarbasioglu]], and [[Nicholas Stern]].<ref>[https://www.imf.org/en/News/Articles/2021/06/14/pr21175-world-bank-imf-launch-hlag-sustainable-inclusive-recovery-growth World Bank, IMF Launch High-Level Advisory Group on Sustainable and Inclusive Recovery and Growth] [[International Monetary Fund]], press release of June 15, 2021.</ref>

==Publications==
Kharas' most recent co-authored books are: ''[http://www.brookings.edu/research/books/2014/the-last-mile-in-ending-extreme-poverty The Last Mile in Ending Extreme Poverty]'' (Brookings Press, 2015);  ''[http://www.brookings.edu/research/books/2013/gettingtoscale Getting to Scale: How to Bring Development Solutions to Millions of Poor People]'' (Brookings Press, 2013); ''After the Spring: Economic Transitions in the Arab World'' (Oxford University Press, 2012); ''[http://www.brookings.edu/research/books/2011/catalyzingdevelopment Catalyzing Development: A New Vision for Aid]'' (Brookings Press, 2011); and ''[http://www.brookings.edu/research/books/2010/deliveringaiddifferently Delivering Aid Differently]'' (Brookings Institution Press, 2010) . He has published articles, book [http://www.brookings.edu/experts/kharash chapters and opinion pieces on global development policy, global trends, the global food crisis, international organizations, the G20, the DAC and private philanthropy].
