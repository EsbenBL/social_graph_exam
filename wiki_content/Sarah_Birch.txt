'''Sarah Birch''', {{Post-nominals|country=GBR|size=100%|sep=,|FRSE|FBA}} (born 5 December 1963) is an American [[political scientist]] and academic, specialising in [[comparative politics]]. Since 2016, she has been [[Professor (highest academic rank)|Professor]] of Political Science at [[King's College London]]. She had taught at the [[University of Essex]] between 1996 and 2013, and held the Chair of Comparative Politics at the [[University of Glasgow]] between 2013 and 2016.<ref name="WW 2017">'BIRCH, Prof. Sarah', ''[[Who's Who 2017]]'', A & C Black, an imprint of Bloomsbury Publishing plc, 2017; online edn, Oxford University Press, 2016; online edn, Nov 2016 [http://www.ukwhoswho.com/view/article/oupww/whoswho/U258532 accessed 24 Sept 2017]</ref><ref name="FBA">{{cite web|title=Professor Sarah Birch|url=https://www.britac.ac.uk/users/professor-sarah-birch|website=The British Academy|access-date=25 September 2017}}</ref><ref name="FRSE">{{cite web|title=Professor Sarah Birch FBA FRSE|url=https://www.rse.org.uk/fellow/sarah-birch/|website=The Royal Society of Edinburgh|access-date=25 September 2017}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Birch was born on 5 December 1963 in [[Boston]], [[Massachusetts]], United States.<ref name="WW 2017" /> She was educated at [[Dartmouth College]], and, having [[Major (academic)|majored]] in [[comparative literature]], graduated with a [[Bachelor of Arts]] (BA) degree in 1985.<ref name="CV">{{cite web|title=CURRICULUM VITAE for Sarah Birch|url=http://www.europarl.europa.eu/meetdocs/2009_2014/documents/afet/dv/2012/2012101/20121011_cv_birch_en.pdf|website=European Parliament|access-date=27 September 2017|date=October 2012}}</ref> She then moved to the United Kingdom to study at the [[Faculty of Medieval and Modern Languages, University of Oxford|Faculty of Medieval and Modern Languages]], [[University of Oxford]].<ref name="WW 2017" /> She graduated with a [[Master of Philosophy]] (MPhil) degree in [[European literature]] in 1988, and completed a [[Doctor of Philosophy]] (DPhil) degree in 1992.<ref name="WW 2017" /><ref name="CV" /> Her [[doctoral thesis]] was titled "[[Christine Brooke-Rose]] and post-war writing in France".<ref>{{cite web|last1=Birch|first1=Sarah|title=Christine Brooke-Rose and post-war writing in France|url=http://ethos.bl.uk/OrderDetails.do?uin=uk.bl.ethos.314887|website=E-Thesis Online Service|publisher=The British Library Board|access-date=27 September 2017|date=1991}}</ref>

Having earned three degrees in literature, Birch turned to politics. She studied [[political behaviour]] at the [[University of Essex]], graduating with a [[Master of Arts]] (MA) degree in 1994.<ref name="CV" /> She remained at Essex to gain a further Doctor of Philosophy (PhD) degree, which she completed in 1998.<ref name="WW 2017" /> Her doctoral thesis was tiled "The social determinants of electoral behaviour in Ukraine, 1989-1994".<ref>{{cite web|last1=Birch|first1=Sarah|title=The social determinants of electoral behaviour in Ukraine, 1989-1994|url=http://ethos.bl.uk/OrderDetails.do?uin=uk.bl.ethos.242231|website=E-Thesis Online Service|publisher=The British Library Board|access-date=27 September 2017|date=1998}}</ref>

==Academic career==
In 1996, while still studying for her second doctorate, Birch was appointed a [[lecturer]] at the [[University of Essex]].<ref name="WW 2017" /> She was promoted to [[Reader (academic rank)|Reader]] in 2003, and appointed [[Professor (highest academic rank)|Professor]] of Politics in 2012.<ref name="WW 2017" /><ref name="FRSE" /> From 2002 to 2011, she served as a co-editor of the ''[[British Journal of Political Science]]''.<ref name="New">{{cite web|title=New staff member: Sarah Birch|url=https://www.gla.ac.uk/schools/socialpolitical/news/archive/2012-13/headline_282463_en.html|website=School of Social & Political Sciences|publisher=University of Glasgow|access-date=27 September 2017|date=4 July 2013}}</ref> In July 2013, she joined the [[University of Glasgow]] where she had been appointed to the Chair of Comparative Politics.<ref name="FBA" /><ref name="New" /><ref>{{cite web|title=Professor Sarah Birch|url=http://www.universitystory.gla.ac.uk/biography/?id=WH25615&type=P|website=The University of Glasgow Story|publisher=University of Glasgow|access-date=27 September 2017}}</ref> In 2016, she joined [[King's College London]] as Professor of Political Science and Director of Research of its Department of Political Economy.<ref name="FBA" /><ref name="bio Department">{{cite web|title=Sarah Birch|url=https://www.kcl.ac.uk/sspp/departments/politicaleconomy/People/academic/birch.aspx|website=Department of Political Economy|publisher=King's College London|access-date=27 September 2017}}</ref>

==Honours==
In 2013, Birch was elected a [[Fellow of the British Academy]] (FBA), the United Kingdom's [[national academy]] for the humanities and social sciences.<ref name="FBA" /> In 2016, she was elected a [[Fellow of the Royal Society of Edinburgh]] (FRSE), Scotland's national academy of science and letters.<ref name="FRSE" />
