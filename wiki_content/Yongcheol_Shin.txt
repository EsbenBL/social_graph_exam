'''Yongcheol Shin''' (born 24 December 1960) is a [[Koreans in the United Kingdom|South Korean-born British]] economist at the [[University of York]]. He has previously held positions at leading academic institutions such as [[University of Cambridge]], [[University of Edinburgh]] and [[University of Leeds]]. His notable contributions to econometrics include asymmetric autoregressive distributed lag model, [[unit root test]]s in ESTAR framework, and the long-run structural [[vector autoregression|VAR]] modelling approach.

==Education and career==
Yongcheol Shin received his bachelor's degree in [[English Literature]] in 1983 and [[Master of Economics|Master's in Economics]] at the [[Hankuk University of Foreign Studies]], [[South Korea]] in 1985.<ref name="leeds">{{cite web|url=http://lubswww.leeds.ac.uk/MKB/ys100t/cv.htm|website=lubswww.leeds.ac.uk|title=MKB/ys100t/cv|access-date=24 November 2021}}</ref> He received his PhD in economics from [[Michigan State University]] in 1992. Currently, Shin is a Professor at the Department of Economics and Related Studies at the [[University of York]].<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://www.york.ac.uk/economics/our-people/staff-profiles/yongcheol-shin/ |title = Shin, Yongcheol - Economics, the University of York}}</ref> Before he has worked as Professor at the Department of Economics at the [[Leeds University Business School]] (University of Leeds) for seven years.<ref name="Archived copy">{{cite web|url=http://business.leeds.ac.uk/about-us/faculty-staff/member/profile/yongcheol-shin/ |title=Archived copy |access-date=5 September 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110820191723/http://business.leeds.ac.uk/about-us/faculty-staff/member/profile/yongcheol-shin/ |archive-date=20 August 2011 }}</ref>

Previously, he has worked as Senior Research Officer from 1995 to 1998 and Research Officer at the Department of Applied Economics, [[University of Cambridge]]. In 1995–1996, Shin served as short term consultant at the International Economics Department of the [[World Bank]] and at the [[Citibank]] International plc. London. He was a visiting professor at the SungKunKwan University, [[Seoul]], and Wits University, [[Johannesburg]]. From 1995 to 1997, he worked on the econometric software project, Working with [[Microfit (software)|Microfit]], at the Cambridge University as hands-on session supervisions.<ref>94.76.226.154/Files/microfit_programme_and_booking_form.sflb.ashx</ref>

Shin has served as [[Reader (academic rank)|Reader]] from 2000 to 2004<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://www.ed.ac.uk/schools-departments/economics/about-us/history/age-of-expansion |title = The age of expansion}}</ref> and a lecturer at the [[University of Edinburgh School of Economics]] from 1998 to 2000.<ref name="leeds"/> He was a Professor at the Economics Division at the [[University of Leeds]] from 2004 to 2011.<ref name="Archived copy"/>

==Academic merits==
Shin has published and written more than 46 articles in leading scientific journals in the areas of [[econometrics]], [[macroeconomics]], [[asset pricing]] and [[empirical finance]].<ref name="leeds2">{{cite web|url=http://lubswww.leeds.ac.uk/MKB/ys100t/pub.htm|website=lubswww.leeds.ac.uk|title=MKB/ys100t/pub|access-date=24 November 2021}}</ref> His contribution to the ARDL model for the cointegration analysis with [[Mohammad Hashem Pesaran]] was introduced in the Cambridge University book, "Econometrics and Economic Theory in the 20th Century" (Ed. Steinar Strøm).<ref name="cambridge">{{cite web|url=http://www.cambridge.org/aus/catalogue/catalogue.asp?isbn=9780521633239|title=Econometrics and economic theory 20th century ragnar frisch centennial symposium &#124; Econometrics, statistics and mathematical economics &#124; Cambridge University Press|website=cambridge.org|access-date=24 November 2021}}</ref>

He was awarded Best Paper Award 2002–2004 by [[Econometric Reviews]] with Pesaran in 2005 for their research paper Long Run Structural Modelling.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://dornsife.usc.edu/IEPR/News/Best_Paper.shtml |title=Archived copy |access-date=8 September 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110827082503/http://dornsife.usc.edu/IEPR/News/Best_Paper.shtml |archive-date=27 August 2011 }}</ref>
