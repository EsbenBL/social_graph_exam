'''Fahmida Khatun''' is a Bangladeshi [[Rabindra Sangeet]] singer.<ref name="conv">{{cite news | url=http://archive.thedailystar.net/2007/08/06/d708061402130.htm | title=Rabindra Sangeet: Awakening Bengali nationalism - In conversation with Fahmida Khatun | work=The Daily Star | date=6 August 2007 | accessdate=15 August 2016 | author=Ahsan Habib}}</ref> She was awarded [[Shilpakala Padak]] in 2014.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.thedailystar.net/arts-entertainment/event/shilpakala-padak-2014-conferred-3039|title=Shilpakala Padak 2014 conferred|date=21 February 2015|accessdate=15 August 2016|newspaper=The Daily Star}}</ref>

==Early life==
Khatun was born to [[Qazi Motahar Hossain]], a scientist and [[National Professor]] of Bangladesh. Her siblings include [[Sanjida Khatun]], [[Qazi Mahbub Hussain]] and [[Qazi Anwar Hussain]].

Khatun started off as a dancer. She later turned to singing. As a school student, she took lessons in classical music at home.<ref name=conv/> Her mentors were Ustad Munir Hussain in classical and [[Sanjida Khatun]], [[Kalim Sharafi]], [[Abdul Ahad (music director)|Abdul Ahad]] in Tagore songs.<ref name=conv/>

==Career==
In 1956, Khatun rendered songs of the ''mother'' character in ''Chandalika'', a dance drama. She rendered ''"Ami Tomar Shongey Bedhechhi Amar Praan"'' in the film ''Dharapat'', the first [[Rabindra Sangeet]] song used in a film made in the then [[East Pakistan]].<ref>{{cite news | url=http://www.thedailystar.net/melodies-on-a-monsoon-evening-28829 | title=Melodies on a monsoon evening | work=The Daily Star | date=17 June 2014 | accessdate=15 August 2016 | author=Fahmim Ferdous}}</ref><ref name=conv/>

On December 26, 1964, the second day of the inauguration of [[Bangladesh Television]], Khatun and Zahedur Rahim sang the first Rabindra Sangeet on the television screen. The song was titled ''"Tori Amar Hothat Dube Jai"''.<ref>{{cite news | url=http://www.thedailystar.net/the-first-days-of-television-34703 | title=The First Days of Television | work=The Daily Star | date=26 July 2014 | accessdate=15 August 2016 | author=Faridur Reza Sagor}}</ref>
