'''Sir Edward Austin Gossage Robinson''', {{post-nominals|country=GBR|commas=on|CMG|OBE|FBA|size=100%}} (20 November 1897 – 1 June 1993, [[Cambridge, England]])<ref name="obit">{{cite news |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/news/people/obituary-sir-austin-robinson-1489686.html |title=Obituary: Sir Austin Robinson |newspaper=[[The Independent]] |date=5 June 1993 |last=Harcourt |first=G. C. |authorlink=Geoff Harcourt |accessdate=31 December 2010}}</ref> was a [[University of Cambridge]] [[economist]]. He was an undergraduate at [[Christ's College, Cambridge]], and a [[Fellow#Oxford, Cambridge and Dublin|fellow]] of [[Sidney Sussex College, Cambridge]].

A close associate of [[John Maynard Keynes]],<ref name="obit"/> Robinson served as assistant editor during Keynes's time as editor of [[The Economic Journal]]; following Keynes's retirement in 1944, Robinson took over the joint [[Editing#Scholarly books and journals|editorship]] with [[Roy Harrod]]. He was at the centre of [[Economic policy|economic policy-making]] during and after the [[Second World War]], holding posts in the [[Cabinet Office]], the [[Ministry of Production]] and the [[Board of Trade]].<ref name="obit" /> Robinson spent the postwar years working as a professor, editor, and economic adviser.<ref name=Cairncross1994>{{Citation

In the course of his life, Robinson also served as a [[seaplane]] pilot during the [[First World War]], and spent two years in the 1920s tutoring a [[Maharajah]] in [[India]]. Disillusioned after his service in the war, Robinson found Keynes's lectures on his ''[[The Economic Consequences of the Peace]]'' to be "a revelation," influencing Robinson's entry into the study of economics.<ref name="obit"/>

He was president of the [[International Economic Association]] from 1959 to 1962.

The Faculty of Economics at the University of Cambridge is located in the Austin Robinson Building, a tribute to Robinson's contributions to the subject.<ref name="obit"/><ref name="building">{{cite web |url=http://www.econ.cam.ac.uk/about |title=University of Cambridge Faculty of Economics: Contacts |publisher=[[University of Cambridge]] |accessdate=31 December 2010}}</ref>

Robinson was the husband of economist [[Joan Robinson]]. They had two daughters.<ref name="obit"/> Austin's brother was the Rt Revd [[Christopher Robinson (bishop)|Christopher Robinson]], an Anglican bishop (firstly [[Anglican Bishop of Lucknow|Bishop of Lucknow]] and later [[Anglican Diocese of Bombay|Bishop of Bombay]]).

==Major works==
* ''The Structure of Competitive Industry'' (1931)
* ''Monopoly'' (1934)
