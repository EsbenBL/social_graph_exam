'''American politics''' (or '''American government''') is a field of study within the academic discipline of [[political science]]. It is primarily, but not exclusively, studied by researchers in the [[United States]]. Along with [[comparative politics]], [[international relations]], and [[political theory]], it is one of the major fields of political science that are studied in American academic institutions.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.insidehighered.com/news/2008/08/29/ampol|title=Should American Politics Be Abolished (as a Field)?|website=www.insidehighered.com|access-date=2016-06-16}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.polisci.washington.edu/what-political-science|title=What is Political Science? {{!}} Department of Political Science {{!}} University of Washington|website=www.polisci.washington.edu|access-date=2016-06-16}}</ref>

Political scientists studying American politics are sometimes referred to within the discipline as "Americanists".<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://themonkeycage.org/2008/10/americanists_and_political_eco/|title=Americanists and political economy - The Monkey Cage|date=2008-10-08|website=The Monkey Cage|language=en-US|access-date=2016-06-16}}</ref><ref>{{Cite journal|last=Bird|first=Stephen|last2=Yesnowitz|first2=Joshua C.|title=Choosing the One for 101: A Review of Primary Textbooks for Introductory Political Science Survey Courses|journal=Journal of Political Science Education|volume=4|issue=2|pages=253–259|doi=10.1080/15512160801998171|year=2008}}</ref> The field is conventionally divided into the sub-fields of [[political behavior]] and [[political institutions]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.princeton.edu/politics/fields/american/|title=American Politics - Department of Politics at Princeton University|last=University|first=Princeton|website=www.princeton.edu|access-date=2016-06-16}}</ref> It also consists of other major sub-fields, such as [[American political development]] (APD), which do not fit neatly into either category.

Research areas within the American political behavior sub-field include [[voting behavior]], [[public opinion]], [[partisanship]], and the politics of [[Race (human classification)|race]], [[gender]], and [[ethnicity]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://www.du.edu/ahss/polisci/areasofstudy/subfields/american.html|title=American Politics {{!}} Sub-fields {{!}} Areas of Study {{!}} Political Science Department  {{!}} University of Denver|last=Denver|first=University of|website=www.du.edu|access-date=2016-06-24}}</ref> Questions within the study of American political institutions include the legislative behavior and [[United States Congress]], [[POTUS|the presidency]], [[American judiciary|courts and the legal process]], [[bureaucracy]], [[public law]], state and local politics, and [[Foreign policy of the United States|foreign policy]].<ref name=":0" /> Scholars in American political development focus on determining how American politics has changed over time and what factors (institutional and behavioral) led to these changes.<ref>{{Cite journal|url=http://oxfordhandbooks.com/view/10.1093/oxfordhb/9780199697915.001.0001/oxfordhb-9780199697915-e-12|title=American Political Development and Political History|journal=The Oxford Handbook of American Political Development|volume=1|last=John|first=Richard R.|date=2014-08-04|doi=10.1093/oxfordhb/9780199697915.013.12}}</ref> [[Public policy]] is also widely studied by Americanists.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.northeastern.edu/cssh/polisci/research/public-policy/|title=Public Policy Studies - Department of Political Science|language=en-US|access-date=2016-06-24}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.mcla.edu/Academics/undergraduate/political-science-and-public-policy/|title=Political Science and Public Policy|website=www.mcla.edu|access-date=2016-06-24}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.bu.edu/polisci/courses/public-policy/|title=Public Policy »  Department of Political Science  {{!}} Boston University|website=www.bu.edu|access-date=2016-06-24}}</ref>

In universities outside of the United States, American Politics generally refers to a course in comparative politics or a survey course in American domestics politics for International Relations within political science.{{Citation needed|date=June 2016}}
