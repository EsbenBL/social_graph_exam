'''Tijjani Muhammad-Bande''' [[Order of the Federal Republic|OFR]] (born 7 December 1957) is a Nigerian [[diplomat]], [[Academy|academic]] and [[Political Scientist|political scientist]]<ref name="UN profile">{{cite web |title=Profile of Professor Tijjani Muhammad-Bande |url=https://www.un.org/pga/73/wp-content/uploads/sites/53/2019/04/Profile_Ambassador-Tijjani-Muhammad-Bande_Rev2.pdf |website=un.org |publisher=United Nations |accessdate=7 May 2019}}</ref> who was the [[President of the United Nations General Assembly]] of [[Seventy-fourth session of the United Nations General Assembly|74th session]] from 17 September 2019 to 15 September 2020 . He previously served as Vice President from [[Seventy-first session of the United Nations General Assembly|September 2016]].<ref name="DT2">{{cite web|url=https://www.dailytrust.com.ng/breaking-muhammad-bande-emerges-unga-president.html|title=Muhammad Bande emerges UNGA President|publisher=Daily Trust|accessdate=4 June 2019}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title=Tijjani Muhammad-Bande|url=https://www.aljazeera.com/profile/tijjani-muhammad-bande.html|access-date=2020-09-20|website=www.aljazeera.com}}</ref> 

He is also the [[Nigeria and the United Nations|Nigerian Ambassador to the United Nations]]. From 2010 to 2016, he was the [[director general]] of the [[National Institute of Policy and Strategic Studies]] in [[Kuru, Nigeria]], and was the Vice Chancellor of the [[Usmanu Danfodiyo University|Usmanu Danfodio University]] from 2004 to 2009.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2019-06-04|title=Tijjani Muhammad-Bande: 7 things you need to know about Nigerian diplomatic appointed as new President of United Nations General Assembly|url=https://www.pulse.ng/bi/politics/tijjani-muhammad-bande-7-things-you-need-to-know-about-nigerian-diplomatic-appointed/ryh9krb|access-date=2020-09-20|website=www.pulse.ng|language=en-US}}</ref><ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Admin|first=Web|title=Ambassador/Permanent Representative|url=http://nigeriaunmission.org/ambassadorpermanent-representative/|access-date=2020-09-20|website=Permanent Mission of Nigeria to the United Nations, New York|language=en-US}}</ref> In 2021, he was re-listed by [[Muhammadu Buhari|President Buhari]] as the Permanent Representative to the [[United Nations]], New York.<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-01-19|title=President assigns portfolios to new ambassadors|url=https://guardian.ng/news/president-assigns-portfolios-to-new-ambassadors/|access-date=2021-01-29|website=The Guardian Nigeria News - Nigeria and World News|language=en-US}}</ref>

==Early life==
Muhammad-Bande was born and educated in Zagga, a town in present-day [[Kebbi State]].<ref name="SR1">{{cite web |title=Quick Fact about New Nigeria’s Ambassador to UN, Bande |url=http://saharareporters.com/2017/03/31/quick-fact-about-new-nigeria%E2%80%99s-ambassador-un-bande |publisher=[[Sahara Reporters]] |accessdate=7 May 2019}}</ref> 

== Education ==
He attended [[Ahmadu Bello University]] in [[Zaria]] where he received a bachelors degree in [[Political Science|political science]] in 1979 before proceeding to [[Boston University]], where he graduated with a [[Political science|Master of Arts in Political Science]] in 1981. Muhammad-Bande received a Ph.D in [[Political science|Political Science]] from the [[University of Toronto]] in 1987.<ref name="UN profile" /><ref name=":0" />

==Career==
In the 1980s, he taught at the [[Usman Danfodio University]] in [[Sokoto]] and rose to the rank of professor in 1998.<ref name="UN profile" /> Between 2000 to 2004, Bande served as the Director-General of the African Training and Research Centre in Administration for Development in [[Tangier]], [[Morocco]].<ref name="ICIR">{{cite web|title=Nigerian professor, Muhammad-Bande, appointed president of UN General Assembly|url=https://www.icirnigeria.org/nigerian-professor-muhammad-bande-appointed-president-of-un-general-assembly/|accessdate=8 May 2019|publisher=ICIR Nigeria}}</ref> Between 2004 and 2009, he served as Vice [[Chancellor (education)|Chancellor]] of [[Usmanu Danfodiyo University|Usman Danfodio University]] before being appointed Director-General of Nigeria's [[National Institute for Policy and Strategic Studies]], a position he held from 2010 to 2016.<ref name="SR2">{{cite web |title=Professor Tijjani Bande Announced As President, UN General Assembly |url=http://saharareporters.com/2019/05/07/professor-tijjani-bande-announced-president-un-general-assembly |publisher=Sahara Reporters |accessdate=7 May 2019}}</ref>
