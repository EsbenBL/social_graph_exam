'''T. N. Srinivasan''', in full '''Thirukodikaval Nilakanta Srinivasan''' (27 March 1933 – 11 November 2018),<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.econ.yale.edu/~srinivas/TNS%20LONG%20CV%200905.pdf |title=Archived copy |access-date=2014-04-04 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150923233450/http://www.econ.yale.edu/~srinivas/TNS%20LONG%20CV%200905.pdf |archive-date=2015-09-23 }}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=Economist TN Srinivasan is no more |url=https://www.thehindubusinessline.com/news/economist-tn-srinivasan-is-no-more/article25469155.ece |website=The Hindu BusinessLine |access-date=12 November 2018}}</ref> was an [[India]]n economist who since 1980 had taught and worked in the United States.  He was the [[Professor#Retired_faculty_2|Emeritus]] Samuel C. Park, Jr. Professor of [[Economics]] at [[Yale University]].<ref>{{cite web|title=T. N. Srinivasan|publisher=Department of Economics, Emeritus Faculty, Yale University|url=http://economics.yale.edu/people/t-n-srinivasan|access-date=5 November 2013}}</ref>  He was formerly chairman of the department of economics. He was a special adviser to the Development Research Center at the [[World Bank Group|World Bank]] from 1977 to 1980, and has taught at numerous academic institutions over the past four decades, including [[Massachusetts Institute of Technology|MIT]], [[Stanford University]], and the [[Indian Statistical Institute]].  In 2007, he received a [[Padma Bhushan]] decoration from the President of India for his contributions to Literature and Education.<ref name="Padma Awards">{{cite web | url=http://mha.nic.in/sites/upload_files/mha/files/LST-PDAWD-2013.pdf | title=Padma Awards | publisher=Ministry of Home Affairs, Government of India | date=2015 | access-date=21 July 2015}}</ref>

He earned his Ph.D. in Economics (1962) from Yale University, M.A. in Mathematics (1954) from [[University of Madras]], India and B.A. (Honors) Mathematics 1953 from University of Madras, India. He did his Professional Training in Statistics (1953-1955) at Indian Statistical Institute, [[Calcutta]]. He has made important contributions in the fields of economic growth and development economics, and international trade. He had been very active in policy debates concerning India. He was founding co-editor of the ''[[Journal of Development Economics]]''. 

He was visiting fellow at the Center for Research on Economic Development and Policy Reform, [[Stanford University]]; fellow of the [[Econometric Society]], [[American Academy of Arts and Sciences]], and [[American Philosophical Society]]; and a foreign associate of the [[United States National Academy of Sciences|National Academy of Sciences]] of the US.  He has authored a prolific collection of books and articles on [[econometrics]], [[International trade|world trade]], and developing country economics.'''<ref>[http://www.thehindubusinessline.com/bline/2008/02/05/stories/2008020550060900.htm `Let us take a leaf out of China's book in WTO']</ref><ref>[http://www.econ.yale.edu/~srinivas/ T.N. Srinivasan Yale University Faculty page] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20091224152605/http://www.econ.yale.edu/~srinivas/ |date=2009-12-24 }}</ref><ref>[http://www.thehindubusinessline.in/bline/2009/03/23/stories/2009032351630300.htm ‘India’s response to slowdown seems misdirected, may stoke inflation’]</ref><ref>[http://economictimes.indiatimes.com/opinion/interviews/India-must-speed-up-reforms-for-fast-growth/articleshow/4742393.cms 'India must speed up reforms for fast growth']</ref>'''
