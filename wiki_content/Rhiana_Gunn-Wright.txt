'''Rhiana Gunn-Wright''' (born 1988) is the Climate Policy Director at the [[Roosevelt Institute]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Rhiana Gunn-Wright|url=http://rooseveltwec.wpengine.com/authors/rhiana-gunn-wright/|access-date=2020-09-28|website=Roosevelt Institute|language=en-US}}</ref> She has worked with [[Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez]] as an author of the [[Green New Deal]]. Gunn-Wright was educated at [[Yale University|Yale]], before becoming a [[Rhodes Scholarship|Rhodes Scholar]] at the [[University of Oxford]] in 2013.

== Early life and education ==
Gunn-Wright grew up in [[Englewood, Chicago|Englewood]] in the [[South Side, Chicago|South Side of Chicago]], where the local population are more likely to suffer from asthma because of their proximity to pollution.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.essence.com/feature/she-the-people-rhiana-gunn-wright-green-new-deal/|title=She, The People: Meet Rhiana Gunn-Wright, An Architect Behind The Green New Deal|last=Owens|first=Donna M.|website=Essence|language=en-US|access-date=2019-08-11}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.americanrhodes.org/news-768.html|title=The Association of American Rhodes Scholars: Rhiana Gunn-Wright, Rhodes Scholar, Aims To Shine Light From Her Bright Future On To Englewood|website=www.americanrhodes.org|access-date=2019-11-20}}</ref> She was raised by her mother and her grandmother. At the age of fourteen Gunn-Wright moved to the [[Illinois Mathematics and Science Academy]] in [[Aurora, Illinois]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://iwpr.org/rhodes-scholar-rhiana-gunn-wright-grateful-november-19-2012/|title=Rhodes Scholar Rhiana Gunn-Wright 'Grateful'|date=2012-11-27|website=Institute for Women's Policy Research|access-date=2019-11-20}}</ref> Her asthma disappeared after a short amount of time. Gunn-Wright attended [[Yale University]] for her undergraduate degree, majored in [[African-American studies]] and graduated with honours in 2011.<ref name=":0" /><ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=https://news.yale.edu/2012/12/10/rhiana-gunn-wright-11-shaping-policy-break-cycle-disadvantage|title=Rhiana Gunn-Wright '11: Shaping policy to break the cycle of disadvantage|last=Baker|first=Dorie|date=2012-12-10|website=YaleNews|language=en|access-date=2019-11-20}}</ref> During her studies she worked with the community in [[New Haven, Connecticut|New Haven]], helping at the Polly McCabe Centre for pregnant adolescents. The centre offers parenting classes and health care to young mothers. She was inspired by the ''New Haven Promise'' initiative as a way to break the cycle of disadvantage. After graduating she joined the [[Institute for Women's Policy Research]] as a research fellow. Here she worked alongside [[Heidi Hartmann]] on paid maternity leave.<ref name=":3" /> In 2013 Gunn-Wright was selected as a [[Rhodes Scholarship|Rhodes Scholar]] at the [[University of Oxford]] where she studied social policy.<ref name=":1" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://news.tulane.edu/pr/green-new-deal-visionary-rhiana-gunn-wright-speak-tulane|title=Green New Deal visionary Rhiana Gunn-Wright to speak at Tulane|website=Tulane News|language=en|access-date=2019-11-20}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.jbhe.com/2012/11/three-african-american-women-win-rhodes-scholarship/|title=Three African American Women Win Rhodes Scholarships|date=2012-11-19|website=The Journal of Blacks in Higher Education|access-date=2019-11-20}}</ref>

== Research and career ==
After graduating, Gunn-Wright served as an intern to [[Michelle Obama]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.aspenideas.org/speakers/rhiana-gunn-wright|title=Rhiana Gunn-Wright {{!}} Aspen Ideas|website=Aspen Ideas Festival|language=en-US|access-date=2019-11-20}}</ref> Gunn-Wright was appointed policy lead for [[Abdul El-Sayed]]'s campaign for governor of Michigan.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://newconsensus.com/people/|title=People|date=2019-01-20|website=New Consensus|language=en-US|access-date=2019-11-20}}</ref> When he asked her to join his campaign, she asked if he was certain: "Are you sure? Because you’re very Muslim and I’m very black, and this is Michigan." For El-Sayed, Gunn-Wright set out a bold policy agenda, including state-funded access to the internet and a shift to all renewable energy by 2030.<ref name=":2">{{Cite web|url=https://www.eenews.net/stories/1060107485|title=CLIMATE: Meet the scholar crafting the 'Green New Deal'|website=www.eenews.net|language=en|access-date=2019-08-11}}</ref>

Gunn-Wright was head hunted by the [[Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez]] campaign to join New Consensus, a think-tank championed by Ocasio-Cortez in [[Washington, D.C.]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.marieclaire.com/politics/a29326190/green-new-deal-architect-rhiana-gunn-wright/|title=For the Architect Behind Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez's Green New Deal, Policy Is Personal|last=Smith-Janssen|first=Karen L.|date=2019-10-04|website=Marie Claire|language=en-US|access-date=2019-11-20}}</ref><ref name=":3">{{Cite web|url=http://nymag.com/intelligencer/2019/09/rhiana-gunn-wright-on-the-rapid-evolution-of-climate-policy.html|title=A Green New Deal Co-Author on the Rapid Evolution of Climate Policy|last=Wallace-Wells|first=David|date=2019-09-20|website=Intelligencer|language=en-us|access-date=2019-11-20}}</ref> For some time New Consensus had been working with the [[Sunrise Movement]] on the development of an environmental policy program and with the [[Justice Democrats]] on identifying new progressive candidates. The [[Justice Democrats]] helped [[Ocasio-Cortez]] run her campaign,  New Consensus appointed Gunn-Wright policy director in which capacity she has worked with Desmond Drummer on the [[Green New Deal]].<ref name=":2" /><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ecosystemmarketplace.com/articles/land-use-markets-green-new-deal/|title=Land Use, Markets, and the Green New Deal: a Conversation With Rhiana Gunn-Wright|website=Ecosystem Marketplace|language=en-US|access-date=2019-11-20}}</ref> To Gunn-Wright, climate policy has always been connected to social justice.<ref name=":3" /> For example, over one million African-American people live half a mile from an oil and gas facility, and six million live in the same county as a refinery.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://ibw21.org/editors-choice/meet-rhiana-gunn-wright-an-architect-behind-the-green-new-deal/|title=Meet Rhiana Gunn-Wright, An Architect Behind The Green New Deal|last=IBW21|date=2019-08-13|website=Institute of the Black World 21st Century|language=en-US|access-date=2019-11-20}}</ref> She has established a "climate mobilisation office", through which she works on climate change policy.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://newconsensus.com/wp-content/uploads/2019/02/new_consensus_gnd_14_pager.pdf|title=The Green New Deal|last=|first=|date=|website=New Consensus|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2019-11-20}}</ref> Gunn-Wright has called for Advanced Research Projects Agency–Energy ([[ARPA-E]]) to be as generously funded as DARPA, and the creation of a green bank that will offer financing to communities that do not have access to clean water or transportation.<ref name=":2" /> The [[Green New Deal]] includes a plan for 100% renewable energy, [[Carbon neutrality|net zero]] greenhouse gas emissions, sustainable jobs and high-quality healthcare.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://energypolicy.columbia.edu/green-new-deal-rhiana-gunn-wright|title=Columbia {{!}} SIPA Center on Global Energy Policy {{!}} The Green New Deal: Rhiana Gunn-Wright|website=energypolicy.columbia.edu|access-date=2019-08-11}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.msnbc.com/ali-velshi/watch/green-new-deal-architect-details-on-cost-policy-are-coming-1457532995890|title=Green New Deal architect: Details on cost, policy are coming|website=MSNBC.com|language=en|access-date=2019-08-11}}</ref> She has since been involved with the [[Ocasio-Cortez]] town halls and the [[CNN]] climate town hall.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.msnbc.com/transcripts/all-in/2019-03-29|title=Town Hall with Alexandria Ocasio-Cortez. TRANSCRIPT: 3/29/19, All In w/ Chris Hayes.|date=2019-03-29|website=MSNBC|access-date=2019-11-20}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.vox.com/2019/9/5/20850009/cnn-climate-town-hall-2020-presidential-democrats-winners-and-losers|title=6 winners and 3 losers from CNN's climate town hall|last=Irfan|first=Umair|date=2019-09-05|website=Vox|language=en|access-date=2019-11-20}}</ref>

She has written for ''[[The Guardian]]'' and was included in [[Time (magazine)|''Time'' magazine]]'s list of the top women fighting to end [[climate change]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.theguardian.com/profile/rhiana-gunn-wright|title=Rhiana Gunn-Wright {{!}} The Guardian|website=the Guardian|language=en|access-date=2019-11-20}}</ref> Gunn-Wright was a signatory on the ''Women Lead Climate'' campaign.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://womenleadclimate.org/|title=Declaration|website=Women Lead Climate|language=en|access-date=2019-11-20}}</ref>
