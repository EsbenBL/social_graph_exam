'''Willem Hendrik Buiter''' [[Order of the British Empire|CBE]] (born 26 September 1949) is an [[United States|American]]-[[United Kingdom|British]] economist.<ref name=auto>{{cite web|url=http://www.willembuiter.com/personal.htm |title=Willem Hendrik Buiter |publisher=Willembuiter.com |access-date=2014-04-18}}</ref> He spent most of his career as an academic, teaching at various universities. More recently, he was Chief Economist at [[Citigroup]].<ref name=lse>{{cite web|url=http://www2.lse.ac.uk/europeanInstitute/staff/academicStaff/buiter/Home.aspx |title=Professor Willem H Buiter - Professor Willem H Buiter - Academic Staff - Staff - European Institute - Home |publisher=.lse.ac.uk |date=2010-02-05 |access-date=2014-04-18}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Buiter was born in [[The Hague]], [[Netherlands]] on 26 September 1949. He is a national of the [[United States]] and the [[United Kingdom]].<ref>http://www.nber.org/~wbuiter/personal.htm</ref> Willem's father, [[Harm Buiter]], was a [[Netherlands|Dutch]] economist, international trades union official and politician of the [[Labour Party (Netherlands)|Labour Party]] (PvdA), who had served as [[Mayor]] of [[Groningen]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.elsevier.nl/web/Nieuws/Nederland/290152/Oudburgemeester-Groningen-Harm-Buiter-overleden.htm |title=Oud-burgemeester Groningen Harm Buiter overleden ("Former mayor of Groningen, Harm Buiter, deceased" |publisher= Elsevier.nl |date=23 February 2011|language= nl}}</ref>

Buiter went to the [[European School]] in [[Brussels]], [[Belgium]] from 1962 to 1967, where he obtained his [[European Baccalaureate]].<ref name=auto /> After studying Political and Social Science for one year at the [[University of Amsterdam]] from 1967 to 1968, Buiter went to [[Emmanuel College, Cambridge]], to study Economics and received his [[Bachelor of Arts|B.A.]] with [[First Class Honours#First-class honours|First-Class Honours]] in 1971.  He was awarded his [[Master of Arts|M.A.]] in Economics in 1972 and his [[M.Phil.]] in Economics in 1973, his fields of concentration being International Economics, and Economic Development.

Buiter was awarded his [[Ph.D.]] in Economics from [[Yale University]] in [[New Haven]], [[Connecticut]], [[United States]], in 1975. His thesis, "Temporary Equilibrium and Long-Run Equilibrium," was subsequently published in 1979.

==Career==
From 1975 to 1976 and 1977 to 1979, Buiter was an Assistant Professor of Economics and International Affairs at the [[Woodrow Wilson School]] of [[Princeton University]] in [[Princeton, New Jersey]]. From 1976 to 1977, he was lecturer in Economics at the [[London School of Economics and Political Science]]. From 1980 until 1982, he was Professor of Economics at the [[University of Bristol]].

From 1977 until 2011, he was  a Research Associate in the Financial Markets and Monetary Economics Program of the [[National Bureau of Economic Research]].<ref name=lse2>[[LSE Magazine]], November 1982, No 42, p.4</ref>

On 1 April 1982, Buiter was appointed Cassel Professor of Economics at the [[London School of Economics]] with special reference to money and banking. He left LSE in 1985 to teach Economics at [[Yale University]] in New Haven, Connecticut from 1985 until 1994.<ref name=auto /> In 1989 Buiter became a correspondent of the [[Royal Netherlands Academy of Arts and Sciences]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://knaw.nl/en/members/correspondents/3962 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170904201119/http://knaw.nl/en/members/correspondents/3962 |title=Willem Buiter |publisher=Royal Netherlands Academy of Arts and Sciences |archive-date=4 September 2017}}</ref>

Buiter left the United States in 1994 when he was appointed as Fellow of [[Trinity College, Cambridge]], and professor of International Macroeconomics at the [[University of Cambridge]], positions he held until May 2000. He was an external member of the [[Bank of England]]'s [[Monetary Policy Committee (United Kingdom)|Monetary Policy Committee]] from June 1997 to May 2000. In June 2000, he became Chief Economist and Special Advisor to the President at the [[European Bank for Reconstruction and Development]], a position he held until August 2005. From September 2005 until May 2009, Buiter was professor of European Political Economy at the European Institute of the [[London School of Economics and Political Science]]. From 2005 to 2010, Buiter was an International Advisor of [[Goldman Sachs|Goldman Sachs International]].<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.economonitor.com/blog/author/wbuiter3/ |title=Willem Buiter |date=26 April 2010 |publisher=[[Roubini Global Economics|EconoMonitor]] |access-date=4 October 2013}}</ref>

Buiter is a contributor to the ''[[Financial Times]]'', where until December 2009 he wrote a blog entitled " Maverecon".<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blogs.ft.com/maverecon/ |title=willem buiter's maverecon |publisher=Blogs.ft.com |access-date=2014-04-18}}</ref> In April 2008, he wrote a paper about the [[2008–2012 Icelandic financial crisis|situation of Icelandic banks]] for [[Landsbanki]], together with his wife Anne Sibert.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://blogs.ft.com/maverecon/2009/03/all-in-the-family/ |title=All in the family &#124; willem buiter's maverecon |publisher=Blogs.ft.com |date=2009-03-08 |access-date=2014-04-18}}</ref> In mid-July 2008, an updated version was presented to the government of Iceland.<ref name="paper">{{cite web |url=http://www.cepr.org/pubs/PolicyInsights/CEPR_Policy_Insight_026.asp |title=The Icelandic banking crisis and what to do about it |access-date=2009-03-19 |url-status=bot: unknown |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081108095037/http://www.cepr.org/pubs/PolicyInsights/CEPR_Policy_Insight_026.asp |archive-date=8 November 2008 |df=dmy-all }}</ref> The Icelandic interlocutors considered the paper to be too market-sensitive and it was agreed to be kept confidential.<ref name="paper" />

From June 2009 till August 2011, he was professor of Political Economy at the Centre for Economic Performance of the [[London School of Economics|LSE]]. In January 2010, Buiter joined [[Citigroup]] as Chief Economist, replacing Lewis Alexander who vacated the position to work with the [[United States Treasury]] eight months prior. In an April 2009 blog post, Buiter had earlier described Citigroup as "a conglomeration of worst practice from across the financial spectrum."<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bloomberg.com/apps/news?pid=newsarchive&sid=aQ4gD_50jmjA|title=Willem Buiter Will Join Citigroup as Chief Economist|authors=Craig Stirling and Bradley Keoun|work=Bloomberg L.P.|date=30 November 2009|access-date=27 January 2011}}</ref>

Buiter was elected fellow of the [[European Economic Association]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Fellows {{!}} EEA|url=https://www.eeassoc.org/fellows|access-date=2021-02-26|website=www.eeassoc.org}}</ref> He is currently an adjunct professor at [[Columbia University]]'s [[School of International and Public Affairs, Columbia University|School of International and Public Affairs]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=2021-07-31|title=Is the US economy running out of slack?|url=http://www.jordantimes.com/opinion/willem-h-buiter/us-economy-running-out-slack|access-date=2021-08-01|website=Jordan Times|language=en}}</ref>

==Personal life==
In 1973 Buiter was married to Jean Archer. The marriage was dissolved in 1997. They had two children, David Michael Alejandro, born on 22 February 1991 in [[Callao]], [[Peru]], and Elizabeth Lorca, born 6 August 1993 in [[Cochabamba]], [[Bolivia]].

Buiter, since 5 June 1998<ref name=auto /> is married to Anne Sibert, professor of Economics at [[Birkbeck, University of London]], who was also an External Member of the Monetary Policy Committee of the [[Central Bank of Iceland]] from 2009 until 2012. Sibert, on account of her criticism on the banking system and European finances for the [[Centre for Economic Policy Research]],<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.voxeu.org/index.php?q=node/6980 |title=The damaged ECB legitimacy &#124; vox |publisher=Voxeu.org |access-date=2014-04-18}}</ref> has been called "a commentator who cannot easily be ignored."<ref name=auto /><ref name=naked>{{cite web|last=Smith |first=Yves |url=http://www.nakedcapitalism.com/2011/09/anne-siebert-the-damaged-ecb-legitimacy.html |title=Anne Sibert: The damaged ECB legitimacy |publisher=naked capitalism |date=2011-09-15 |access-date=2014-04-18}}</ref>
