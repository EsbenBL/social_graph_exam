'''Keith Fitzgerald''' is a former [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democratic]] member of the [[Florida House of Representatives]], representing the 69th District for two terms from 2007 to 2010.

==Academic career==
Keith Fitzgerald is also a [[political scientist]] specializing in American politics with a focus on institutions and public policy. He is the author of ''Face of the Nation: Immigration, the State, and the National Identity.'' Following a stint at [[Grinnell College]], since 1994 Fitzgerald has been a professor of political science at the [[New College of Florida]]. He received his B.A. from the [[University of Louisville]] and his Ph.D. from [[Indiana University]].

==Florida legislature==
In December 2005, Keith Fitzgerald announced his candidacy for the [[Florida State House]], running as a [[Democratic Party (United States)|Democrat]] in District 69, which contains the city of [[Sarasota, Florida]] and part of [[Manatee County, FL|Manatee county]].<ref>[http://www.gofitzgo.com/ Keith Fitzgerald for State House - District 69<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> Fitzgerald narrowly defeated Republican Laura Benson 51% to 49% in the November 2006 election.<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.bradenton.com/mld/bradenton/15102304.htm |title=Archived copy |access-date=2006-08-15 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070311032546/http://www.bradenton.com/mld/bradenton/15102304.htm |archive-date=2007-03-11 |url-status=dead }}</ref>

In 2008 Fitzgerald was re-elected, again running against Laura Benson. In 2010 however, he was defeated by [[Ray Pilon]].<ref>http://www.heraldtribune.com/article/20101115/COLUMNIST/11151035</ref>

==2012 U.S. House campaign==
{{Main|United States House of Representatives elections in Florida, 2012#District 16}}
Fitzgerald won the Democratic nomination to challenge [[Vern Buchanan]] in [[Florida's 16th congressional district]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://floridaindependent.com/50887/keith-fitzgerald-vern-buchanan|title=Keith Fitzgerald officially announces he's challenging Vern Buchanan|date=October 6, 2011|accessdate=October 6, 2011|work=[[American Independent News Network|The Florida Independent]]|first=Virginia|last=Chamlee|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111009032722/http://floridaindependent.com/50887/keith-fitzgerald-vern-buchanan|archive-date=October 9, 2011|url-status=dead}}</ref>

;Polling
A mid-July 2012 poll showed the incumbent US Rep. [[Vern Buchanan]] leading Keith Fitzgerald by a margin of 54%-32%. The poll had a margin of error of +/-4.9%.<ref>{{cite news|title=Vern Buchanan poll: Buchanan 54%, Keith Fitzgerald 32%|url=http://www.tampabay.com/blogs/the-buzz-florida-politics/content/vern-buchanan-poll-buchanan-54-keith-fitzgerald-32|accessdate=4 September 2012|newspaper=Tampa Bay Times|date=18 July 2012}}</ref>

An independent poll released on September 5, 2012 showed the incumbent US Rep. [[Vern Buchanan]] leading Keith Fitzgerald by a margin of 56%-37%. The poll had a margin of error of 4%-5%.<ref>{{cite news|last=Schorsch|first=Peter|title=New independent poll shows Vern Buchanan up twenty points over Keith Fitzgerald|url=http://saintpetersblog.com/2012/09/new-independent-poll-shows-vern-buchanan-up-twenty-points-over-keith-fitzgerald/|accessdate=5 September 2012|newspaper=Saint PetersBlog|date=5 September 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121024073444/http://saintpetersblog.com/2012/09/new-independent-poll-shows-vern-buchanan-up-twenty-points-over-keith-fitzgerald/|archive-date=24 October 2012|url-status=dead}}</ref>

;Finance
As of August 3, 2012, Fitzgerald's campaign had $670,000 in the bank, compared to Buchanan's $1.4 million.<ref>{{cite news|last=Wallace|first=Jeremy|title=For third straight period, challenger Fitzgerald again outraises incumbent Buchanan|url=http://politics.heraldtribune.com/2012/08/03/fitzgerald-again-outraises-buchanan/|accessdate=4 September 2012|newspaper=Sarasota Herald-Tribune|date=3 August 2012}}</ref> Democratic Minority Leader [[Nancy Pelosi]] and other House Democrats have supported Fitzgerald with at least $34,000 in campaign contributions.<ref>{{cite news|last=Wallace|first=Jeremy|title=John Boehner and Nancy Pelosi give money to area congressional race|url=http://politics.heraldtribune.com/2012/07/22/john-boehner-and-nancy-pelosi-give-money-to-area-congressional-race/|accessdate=4 September 2012|newspaper=Sarasota Herald-Tribune|date=22 July 2012}}</ref>

The Democratic Congressional Campaign Committee reserved $2.5 million of ad time in the Tampa Bay media market, some or all of which may be used to help Fitzgerald.<ref>{{cite news|last=Masferrer|first=Marc|title=Report: DCCC reserves $2.5 million in TV ads for Tampa, maybe to help Keith Fitzgerald (UPDATED)|url=http://manateepolitics.blogspot.com/2012/04/dccc-reserves-25-million-in-tv-ads-for.html|accessdate=4 September 2012|newspaper=Bradenton Herald Online|date=18 April 2012}}</ref> Meanwhile, Buchanan's campaign reserved $4 million in the same market.<ref>{{cite news|last=Masferrrer|first=Marc|title=Rep. Vern Buchanan plans to spend $4 million on TV campaign ads|url=http://www.bradenton.com/2012/06/01/4060207/rep-vern-buchanan-plans-to-spend.html#storylink=misearch#storylink=cpy|accessdate=4 September 2012|newspaper=Bradenton Herald|date=1 June 2012}}</ref> As of early September 2012, there has been no public announcement of an ad buy from Fitzgerald's campaign.

Fitzgerald won 46.4% of the vote, but failed to defeat Rep. Buchanan.
