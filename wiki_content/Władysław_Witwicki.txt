'''Władysław Witwicki''' (30 April 1878,{{sfnp|Nowicki |1982 |p=117}} [[Lubaczów]] – 21 December 1948,{{sfnp|Nowicki |1982 |p=120}} [[Konstancin-Jeziorna|Konstancin]]){{sfnp|Chodakiewicz |Radzilowski |Tolczyk |2009 |p=174}} was a [[Poles|Polish]] [[psychologist]], [[philosopher]], [[Translation|translator]], [[historian]] (of philosophy and art) and artist. He is seen as one of the fathers of [[psychology]] in Poland.{{sfnp|Nowicki |1982 |p=61}}{{sfnp|Citlak |2015 |p=156}}

Witwicki was also the creator of the theory of cratism,{{sfnp|Rzepa |1991 |pp=68–81}}{{sfnp|Citlak |2015 |pp=155–184}} theory of feelings,{{sfnp|Rzepa |1992 |pp=215–216}}{{sfnp|Nowicki |1982 |pp=73–74}} and he dealt with the issues of the [[psychology of religion]],{{sfnp|Nowicki |1982 |pp=79–90}} and the creation of [[secular ethics]].{{sfnp|Nowicki |1982 |p=91}} He was one of the initiators and co-founders of [[Polish Philosophical Society]].{{sfnp|Nowicki |1982 |p=7}} He is one of the thinkers associated with the [[Lwów–Warsaw school]].{{sfnp|Rzepa |1991 |pp=16–17}}

== Background ==
Władysław Witwicki was the fifth child of Urszula Witwicka, born Woińska (niece of the Metropolitan Archbishop of the [[Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Lviv]], [[Łukasz Baraniecki]]), and Ludwik–Filip Wasylkowicz Witwicki,{{sfnp|Rzepa |1991 |p=233}} as well as father of [[Janusz Witwicki]], the creators of the [[Plastic Panorama of Old Lviv]].{{sfnp|Rzepa |1991 |p=234}}

He graduated from the [[University of Lviv]], was a student of [[Kazimierz Twardowski]].{{sfnp|Nowicki |1982 |p=117}}{{sfnp|Rzepa |1991 |pp=233–234}} He also studied at the [[University of Vienna]] (under the direction of [[Alois Höfler]]) and at the [[Leipzig University]] (under the direction of [[Wilhelm Wundt]]).{{sfnp|Płotka |2020|pp=141–167}} He lectured at the University of Lviv and became a professor at the [[University of Warsaw]] (1919–1948).{{sfnp|Nowicki |1982 |pp=13–23}}{{sfnp|Rzepa |1991 |pp=233–236}}

==Works==
Witwicki is the author of the first Polish textbooks on psychology.{{sfnp|Rzepa |1991 |p=120}} He also collaborated with other philosophers. For instance, he worked with [[Bronisław Bandrowski]] to develop a model of psychology based on [[Franz Brentano]]'s theory on [[Phenomenology (philosophy)|phenomenology]]. It included an analysis of [[Edmund Husserl]]'s Theory of Content and the Phenomenon of Thinking.{{sfnp|Płotka |2020 |pp=141–167}}

In the comments to his own translation of the Gospels of [[Gospel of Matthew|Matthew]] and [[Gospel of Mark|Mark]] – ''Dobra Nowina według Mateusza i Marka''{{sfnp|Witwicki|1958|p=}} (''{{ill|The Good News according to Matthew and Mark|pl|Dobra Nowina według Mateusza i Marka}}'') – Witwicki challenges [[Mental health of Jesus|the mental health of Jesus]]. He attributed to Jesus [[subjectivism]], increased sense of his own power and superiority over others, [[egocentrism]] and the tendency to subjugate other people,{{sfnp|Szmyd|1996|p=197}} as well as difficulties communicating with the outside world and [[Dissociative identity disorder|multiple personality disorder]], which made him a [[Schizothymia|schizothymic]] or even [[Schizophrenia|schizophrenic]] type (according to the [[Ernst Kretschmer]]'s [[Personality type|typology]]).{{sfnp|Citlak|2015|pp=155–184}}<ref>{{cite web |url=http://www.racjonalista.pl/kk.php/s,5828 |title=Jezus jako egocentryczny schizotymik |trans-title=Jesus as an egocentric schizotymic |last=Jarzyńska |first=Karina |date=2008-04-10 |website=Racjonalista |publisher=Fundacja Wolnej Myśli |language=pl |access-date=2020-07-28}}</ref>
