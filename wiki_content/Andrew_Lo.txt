'''Andrew Wen-Chuan Lo''' ({{zh|羅聞全}}) (born 1960) is the Charles E. and Susan T. Harris [[Professor]] of Finance at the [[MIT Sloan School of Management]]. Lo is the author of many academic articles in [[finance]] and [[financial economics]].<ref>[https://archive.today/20120715004024/http://scholar.google.com/scholar?num=20&hl=en&lr=&q=author:lo+aw Google Scholar search on A.W. Lo]</ref> He founded AlphaSimplex Group in 1999 and served as chairman and chief investment strategist until 2018 when he transitioned to his current role as chairman emeritus and senior advisor.<ref>{{Citation|last=Founder Biography|title=AlphaSimplex Group|url=https://www.alphasimplex.com/our-team/andrew-w-lo-ph-d/?filter=founder|access-date=2020-01-07}}</ref>

==Career==
Lo is a professor of finance at the MIT Sloan School of Management, the director of MIT's Laboratory for Financial Engineering, a principal investigator at [[MIT Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory|MIT's Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory]], an affiliated faculty of the MIT Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science, an external faculty member of the [[Santa Fe Institute]], and a research associate of the [[National Bureau of Economic Research]].<ref>[https://www.nber.org/authors/andrew_lo NBER Publications by Andrew W. Lo] – NBER</ref>

He is the founder of AlphaSimplex Group,<ref name="ALPHASIMPLEX">[http://www.alphasimplex.com/index.php?p=people Profile: Andrew W. Lo] – AlphaSimplex Group</ref> a [[quantitative investment]] management company based in Cambridge, Massachusetts, and served as its chairman and chief investment strategist until 2018 when he transitioned to his current role as chairman emeritus and senior advisor. AlphaSimplex specializes in quantitative [[global macro]] and [[global tactical asset allocation]] strategies, [[Beta (finance)|beta]]-replication products, and [[Absolute return|absolute-return]] risk analytics.

Lo is a founding co-editor of the ''[[Annual Review of Financial Economics]]''<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Lo |first1=Andrew W. |last2=Merton |first2=Robert C. |title=Preface to the Annual Review of Financial Economics |journal=Annual Review of Financial Economics |date=5 December 2009 |volume=1 |issue=1 |pages=01–17 |doi=10.1146/annurev-financial-071808-145225 |url=https://www.annualreviews.org/doi/10.1146/annurev-financial-071808-145225 |access-date=16 September 2021|hdl=1721.1/66557 |hdl-access=free }}</ref> and advisor to the ''[[Journal of Investment Management]]'' and ''[[The Journal of Portfolio Management]]''. He is also a director of Roivant Sciences.

He previously taught at the [[University of Pennsylvania]]'s [[Wharton School]] from 1984 to 1988.

He has testified before the [[United States House Committee on Oversight and Government Reform|Committee on Oversight and Government Reform]] and the [[United States House Committee on Financial Services|Committee on Financial Services]] of the [[U.S. House of Representatives]].<ref>Lo, Andrew W., [http://web.mit.edu/alo/www/Papers/JFEP2009.pdf "Hedge Funds, Systemic Risk, and the Financial Crisis of 2007–2008: Written Testimony for the House Oversight Committee Hearing on Hedge Funds"], November 13, 2008</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://financialservices.house.gov/calendar/eventsingle.aspx?EventID=404232|title=Examining Private Market Exemptions as a Barrier to IPOs and Retail Investment {{!}} Financial Services Committee|website=financialservices.house.gov|access-date=2020-01-07}}</ref>

With [[Lars Peter Hansen]], Lo co-directed the Macro Financial Modeling project at the [[Becker Friedman Institute for Research in Economics|Becker Friedman Institute]], a network of [[Macroeconomics|macroeconomists]] working to develop improved models of the linkages between the financial and real sectors of the economy in the wake of the [[Financial crisis of 2007–08|2008 financial crisis]].

==Awards==
His awards include Batterymarch, [[Guggenheim Fellowship|Guggenheim]], and Sloan fellowships; the Paul A. Samuelson Award; the Eugene Fama Prize;<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://news.chicagobooth.edu/about/newsroom/press-releases/2014/2014-10-23|title=The Econometrics of Financial Markets awarded first Eugene Fama Prize|website=news.chicagobooth.edu|language=en|access-date=2020-01-07}}</ref> the [[International Association of Financial Engineers#Financial Engineer of the Year .28FEOY.29|IAFE-SunGard Financial Engineer of the Year]] award; the Global Association of Risk Professionals' Risk Manager of the Year award;<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/garp-names-andrew-lo-risk-manager-of-the-year-300601498.html|title=GARP Names Andrew Lo Risk Manager of the Year|last=Professionals (GARP)|first=Global Association of Risk|website=www.prnewswire.com|language=en|access-date=2020-01-07}}</ref> the Harry M. Markowitz Award; the Managed Futures Pinnacle Achievement Award;<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/cme-group-and-barclayhedge-honor-managed-futures-leaders-at-seventh-annual-managed-futures-pinnacle-awards-300669276.html|title=CME Group and BarclayHedge Honor Managed Futures Leaders at Seventh Annual Managed Futures Pinnacle Awards|last=CME Group|website=www.prnewswire.com|language=en|access-date=2020-01-07}}</ref> one of ''[[Time (magazine)|Time]]'' magazine's "100 most influential people in the world";<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://content.time.com/time/specials/packages/article/0,28804,2111975_2111976_2112160,00.html|title=Andrew Lo - The World's 100 Most Influential People: 2012|last=Foroohar|first=Rana|date=2012-04-18|work=[[Time (magazine)|Time]]|access-date=2020-01-07|language=en-US|issn=0040-781X}}</ref> and awards for teaching excellence from both Wharton and MIT. His most recent book, ''Adaptive Markets: Financial Evolution at the Speed of Thought'', has also received a number of awards.<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://press.princeton.edu/books/hardcover/9780691135144/adaptive-markets|title=Adaptive Markets|date=2017-05-02|isbn=978-0-691-13514-4|language=en|last1=Lo|first1=Andrew W.}}</ref> Lo is a fellow of Academia Sinica, the American Academy of Arts and Sciences, the Econometric Society, and the Society of Financial Econometrics.

==Personal life==
Lo received a [[Bachelor of Arts]] in [[economics]] from [[Yale University]] where he graduated in 1980 and a [[Ph.D.]] in economics from [[Harvard University]] graduating in 1984.<ref name="ALPHASIMPLEX"/> He was raised by his mother in a [[single parent]] household.<ref>Speech to CFA Institute Global Conference, [http://video.cfainstitute.org/services/player/bcpid3577743869001?bckey=AQ~~,AAABE5oc3_E~,Leu10fA0D1sc9Dh9wz3oyrstQJ-PkzpJ&bctid=1774059313001 Alpha and Beta in the New Financial Order (38:02)], , Orlando, Florida, 2009</ref> He speaks fluent [[Cantonese]].<ref>{{MathGenealogy|id=63754}}</ref>
