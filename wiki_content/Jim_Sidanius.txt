{{short description|Psychologist and academic}}
'''James H. Sidanius''',<ref name=Obituary/> known as '''Jim Sidanius''' (born '''James Brown''' on December 11, 1945 - June 29, 2021)<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=Sidanius|first=Jim|date=2016|title=Vita|url=https://projects.iq.harvard.edu/files/sidaniuslab/files/sidanius_vita.pdf|access-date=December 5, 2020|website=Harvard University|archive-date=April 14, 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20190414215446/http://projects.iq.harvard.edu/files/sidaniuslab/files/sidanius_vita.pdf|url-status=live}}</ref> was an American psychologist and academic. He served as John Lindsley Professor of [[Psychology]] in memory of [[William James]] and of [[African studies|African]] and [[African American Studies]] at [[Harvard University]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Jim Sidanius|url=http://psychology.fas.harvard.edu/people/james-sidanius|website=psychology.fas.harvard.edu|publisher=Harvard University|access-date=4 April 2017|language=en|archive-date=4 April 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170404220808/https://psychology.fas.harvard.edu/people/james-sidanius|url-status=live}}</ref> He won the 2006 Harold Lasswell Award for “Distinguished Scientific Contribution in the Field of Political Psychology” from the [[International Society of Political Psychology]]<ref>{{cite web|title=Harold Lasswell Award - ISPP.org|url=http://www.ispp.org/awards/lasswell|website=www.ispp.org|publisher=International Society of Political Psychology|access-date=4 April 2017|language=en|archive-date=8 May 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170508075900/http://www.ispp.org/awards/lasswell|url-status=live}}</ref> and the [[Society for Personality and Social Psychology]] 2013 Career Contribution Award.<ref>{{cite web|title=Career Contribution Award {{!}} SPSP|url=http://www.spsp.org/awards/annualawards/seniorcareer/career-contribution-award|website=www.spsp.org|publisher=Society for Personality and Social Psychology|access-date=4 April 2017|archive-date=4 April 2017|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170404060208/http://www.spsp.org/awards/annualawards/seniorcareer/career-contribution-award|url-status=live}}</ref> He was inducted into the [[American Academy of Arts and Sciences]] in 2007.<ref>{{cite web|title=Academy Announces 2007 Class of Fellows|url=https://2m.com/wp-content/uploads/2M_AAAS-Announces-2007-Class-of-Fellows.pdf|publisher=American Academy of Arts and Sciences|access-date=4 April 2017|archive-date=23 October 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161023012850/https://2m.com/wp-content/uploads/2M_AAAS-Announces-2007-Class-of-Fellows.pdf|url-status=dead}}</ref> The [[Society of Experimental Social Psychology]] awarded Sidanius the ''Scientific Impact Award'' in 2019.<ref>{{cite web|title=Sidanius Wins The Society of Experimental Social Psychology's Scientific Impact Award|url=https://psychology.fas.harvard.edu/news/sidanius-wins-society-experimental-social-psychology%E2%80%99s-scientific-impact-award|publisher=Harvard University|access-date=14 June 2019|archive-date=12 November 2019|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20191112150818/https://psychology.fas.harvard.edu/news/sidanius-wins-society-experimental-social-psychology%E2%80%99s-scientific-impact-award|url-status=live}}</ref>

==Life==
Sidanius, who was of [[African Americans|African American]] heritage, grew up in [[New York City]]. He earned a bachelor’s degree in psychology from [[City College of New York]] in 1968.<ref name=":0" /> He went on to pursue a Ph.D. in psychology at the [[Stockholm University|University of Stockholm]], Sweden.<ref name=":0" /> His dissertation, passed in 1977, was titled: "Cognitive functioning and Socio-political Ideology: Studies in political psychology."<ref name=":0" /> He changed his last name from Brown to Sidanius upon moving to Sweden (where he also obtained citizenship), but returned to the United States in the 1980s as a permanent resident. He was married twice.<ref name=Obituary>{{Cite web|url=https://news.harvard.edu/gazette/story/2021/07/psychology-professor-james-sidanius-dies-at-75/|title = Psychology professor James Sidanius dies at 75|date = 7 July 2021}}</ref>
