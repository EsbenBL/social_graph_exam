'''Pratima (Tima) Bansal''' {{post-nominals|list=[[Royal Society of Canada|FRSC]]}} is a Canadian economist and management professor. She is a Professor of Strategy at the [[Ivey Business School]] and Director of Ivey's Centre on Building Sustainable Value. She is the Founder and Executive Director of the [https://www.nbs.net Network for Business Sustainability], a vehicle aimed at sharing academic research on business sustainability with managers. In April 2020, she was appointed to chair the expert panel on the Circular Economy by the [[Council of Canadian Academies]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=CCA Appoints Expert Panel on the Circular Economy in Canada|url=https://cca-reports.ca/cca-appoints-expert-panel-on-the-circular-economy-in-canada/|access-date=2020-08-29|language=en}}</ref>

Before entering academia, Bansal worked as a government economist and management consultant for [[Nicholls Applied Management]]. She was elected a [[Fellow of the Royal Society of Canada]] in 2018. 

==Education==
After graduating from the [[University of Calgary]] with a [[Bachelor of Arts]], Bansal attended the [[University of Western Ontario]] and the [[University of Oxford]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Tima Bansal |url=https://www.ivey.uwo.ca/faculty/directory/tima-bansal/ |website=ivey.uwo.ca |accessdate=October 3, 2019}}</ref>

==Career and work==
Before entering academics, Bansal worked as a government economist and management consultant for [[Nicholls Applied Management]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Tima Bansal, Richard Ivey School of Business |url=https://www.ft.com/content/2eecbadc-d235-11df-8fbe-00144feabdc0 |website=ft.com |accessdate=October 3, 2019 |date=October 7, 2010}}</ref>

In 2005, Bansal founded and directed the Centre on Building Sustainable Value at the University of Western Ontario's Richard Ivey School of Business.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Anderson |first1=Mark |title=The future is in sustainability |url=http://www.calgaryherald.com/sports/future+sustainability/1959508/story.html |accessdate=October 3, 2019 |publisher=Calgary Herald |date=September 21, 2009}}</ref> She also founded the Network for Business Sustainability, which links researchers and managers to challenge current ideas of sustainability.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Pearson |first1=Glen |title=Pearson: Good idea; we should use it |url=https://lfpress.com/2014/03/13/pearson-good-idea-we-should-use-it/wcm/c8e46d93-eec8-0831-4711-dff477959a06 |accessdate=October 3, 2019 |publisher=The London Free Press |date=March 13, 2014}}</ref> As a result, in 2007 she was nominated for the YMCA Women of Distinction Award<ref name = "x">{{cite web |title=Sustainability and CSR Sessions |url=https://tavaresgroupconsulting.com/learning/speaker-bios/ |website=tavaresgroupconsulting.com |accessdate=October 3, 2019 |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20160927234016/http://tavaresgroupconsulting.com/learning/speaker-bios/ |archivedate=November 6, 2016}}</ref> and was the recipient of the Faculty Pioneer Award in Academic Leadership by the [[Aspen Institute]] the following year.<ref>{{cite web |title=Aspen Institute Names Baron Winner of Lifetime Achievement Award |url=https://www.gsb.stanford.edu/newsroom/school-news/aspen-institute-names-baron-winner-lifetime-achievement-award |website=gsb.stanford.edu |accessdate=October 3, 2019 |date=November 1, 2008}}</ref>

From 2016 until 2019, Bansal served as a Deputy Editor for the Academy of Management Journal.<ref>{{cite web |title=Prospering Over the Long-Term |url=https://www.ideasworthteachingawards.com/prospering-over-the-long-term |website=ideasworthteachingawards.com |accessdate=October 3, 2019}}</ref> While in this position, Bansal received accolades for her contributions to improving sustainability. In 2017, a year after her promotion, Bansal was selected to lead a group of researchers from the Ivey Business School, University of Alberta and the University of Quebec at Montreal to collaborate with Canada's Oil Sands Innovation Alliance (COSIA).<ref>{{cite web |title=Teaming up to accelerate sustainability-focused innovation in Canada's oil sands |url=https://www.ivey.uwo.ca/news/news-ivey/2017/9/teaming-up-to-accelerate-sustainability-focused-innovation-in-canadas-oil-sands/ |website=ivey.uwo.ca |accessdate=October 3, 2019 |date=September 19, 2017}}</ref> In August, Bansal was honoured with the Distinguished Scholar award by the Organizations and Natural Environment division of the Academy of Management.<ref>{{cite tweet|user=IveyResearch|number=912325883448446976|date=September 25, 2017|title=Congratulations to Professor Tima Bansal for receiving the Distinguished Scholar Award at #AOM2017}}</ref> The next year, Bansal was elected a [[Fellow of the Royal Society of Canada]]<ref>{{cite web |last1=Mayne |first1=Paul |title=Western elite among Royal Society Fellows |url=https://news.westernu.ca/2018/08/western-elite-among-royal-society-fellows-2/ |website=news.westernu.ca |accessdate=October 3, 2019 |date=August 31, 2018}}</ref> and was one of two Canadians to receive the Ideas Worth Teaching Awards from the Aspen Institute Business & Society Program.<ref>{{cite web |title=Ivey Professor Tima Bansal honoured for innovative teaching |url=https://www.ivey.uwo.ca/news/news-ivey/2018/5/ivey-professor-tima-bansal-honoured-for-innovative-teaching/ |website=ivey.uwo.ca |accessdate=October 4, 2019 |date=May 8, 2018}}</ref>

In her last year, Bansal received the 2019 Best Article Award in the California Management Review for her article "''CSR Needs CPR: Corporate Sustainability and Politics.''"<ref>{{cite web |title=Professor Tima Bansal and co-authors win 2019 best article award |url=https://www.ivey.uwo.ca/sustainability/news/2019/06/professor-tima-bansal-and-co-authors-win-2019-best-article-award/ |website=ivey.uwo.ca |accessdate=October 3, 2019 |date=June 14, 2019}}</ref> She also received funding from the [[Social Sciences and Humanities Research Council of Canada]] (SSHRC) for her research with [[Diane-Laure ArjaliÃ¨s]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Mine |first1=Dawn |title=Ivey receives funding for social sciences and humanities research |url=https://www.ivey.uwo.ca/news/news-ivey/2019/9/ivey-receives-funding-for-social-sciences-and-humanities-research/ |website=ivey.uwo.ca |accessdate=October 3, 2019 |date=September 10, 2019}}</ref>

Bansal's research focuses on how organizations can manage sustainability issues and challenges such as climate change.
