'''Christopher John Hill''' {{post-nominals|country=GBR|FBA}} (born 20 November 1948) 
is an emeritus 
Professor of [[international relations]] at the [[University of Cambridge]].<ref name=whoswho>{{Who's Who | surname = HILL | othernames = Prof. Christopher John | id = | volume = 2014 | edition = online [[Oxford University Press]]|location=Oxford}} {{subscription required}}</ref><ref>[https://web.archive.org/web/20090907012504/http://www.polis.cam.ac.uk/contacts/staff/hill-christopher.html Staff page at the Cambridge Centre of International Studies]</ref>

==Education==
Hill was educated at the [[University of Oxford]] where he received both his [[Master of Arts (Oxbridge and Dublin)|Master of Arts]] and [[Doctor of Philosophy]] degrees.<ref>{{cite thesis|degree=PhD|publisher=University of Oxford|title=The decision-making process in relation to British foreign policy, 1938-1941|first= Christopher|last=Hill|date=1979|url=http://ora.ox.ac.uk/objects/uuid%3A285853fe-e71f-4c01-8088-c26aa0b55506|id={{EThOS|uk.bl.ethos.459127}}|website=ora.ox.ac.uk|oclc=}} {{free access}}</ref>

==Career and research==
From 1974 to 2004 he taught in the Department of International Relations at the [[London School of Economics and Political Science]] where he was the [[Montague Burton Professor of International Relations]] from 1991. He was head of the [[Faculty of Human, Social, and Political Science, University of Cambridge|Department of Politics and International Studies]] (POLIS) at Cambridge, 2012–14.

He has published widely in the areas of foreign policy analysis, European politics and general international relations. He is a past Chair of the British International Studies Association, and was elected a Fellow of the British Academy in 2007. He was a member of the RAE2008 Panel for Politics and International Studies.<ref>{{cite web|title=College Fellows and Staff - Sidney Sussex College, Cambridge University|url=http://www.sid.cam.ac.uk/aboutus/people/person.html?crsid=cjh68|accessdate=25 January 2014}}</ref> From 2016 to 2019 he has been Wilson E. Schmidt Distinguished Professor of International Relations at the School of Advanced International Studies,  Johns Hopkins University, Bologna.
