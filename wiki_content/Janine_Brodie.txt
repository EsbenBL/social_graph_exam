'''Marion Janine Brodie''' {{post-nominals|list=[[Order of Canada|OC]]}} {{post-nominals|list=[[Royal Society of Canada|FRSC]]}} (born 1952) is a Canadian political scientist. She is a [[Distinguished University Professor]] and a [[Canada Research Chair]] in Political Economy and Social Governance at the [[University of Alberta]]. Brodie was elected a [[Fellow of the Royal Society of Canada]] in 2002 and honoured with the [[Order of Canada]] in 2017.

==Education==
Brodie became involved in politics while [[John Diefenbaker]] and [[Lester B. Pearson]] were in positions of power. She studied political science at the [[University of Windsor]] with the intent of continuing her education in law school. However, she was encouraged by one of her professors to pursue a [[Master's degree]] in political science. Her Master's, and eventual PhD, focused on women's role in politics.<ref>{{cite web |title=Janine Brodie |url=https://carleton.ca/fpa/story/janine-brodie/ |website=carleton.ca |accessdate=June 4, 2019}}</ref>

==Career==
While earning her PhD in Political Science from Carleton University in 1981, Brodie began teaching at [[Queen's University at Kingston|Queen's University]].<ref name = "about">{{cite web |title=About Janine Brodie |url=http://neoliberalismcrises.com/about-janine-brodie/ |website=neoliberalismcrises.com |accessdate=June 4, 2019}}</ref> She then taught at [[York University]] where she held the John Robarts Chair in Canadian Studies and was the Inaugural Director of the York Centre for Feminist Research.<ref name = "Order of Canada">{{cite web |author1=Donna McKinnon |title=Social justice champion named to Order of Canada |url=https://www.ualberta.ca/arts/faculty-news/2018/january/social-justice-champion-named-to-order-of-canada |website=ualberta.ca |accessdate=June 4, 2019 |date=January 2, 2018}}</ref> While at the university, she published a book titled ''Women and politics in Canada'' in 1985 which focused on gender-based differences in political parties and legislative offices.<ref>{{cite journal |author1=Donald E. Blake |title=Women and Politics in Canada |journal=Canadian Journal of Political Science |date=June 1986 |volume=19 |issue=2 |pages=384–385 |doi=10.1017/S0008423900054147 }}</ref> This book is regarded as the first to focus on women in politics between 1945 and 1975.<ref>{{cite news |last1=Cook |first1=Stephen |title=Three Edmontonians receive Order of Canada |url=https://edmontonjournal.com/news/local-news/three-edmontonians-to-receive-order-of-canada |accessdate=June 5, 2019 |publisher=Edmonton Journal |date=December 29, 2017}}</ref> Later, she also published ''The politics of abortion'' with  Shelley A. M. Gavigan and Jane Jenson.<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Bashevkin |first1=Sylvia |title=Reviewed Work: The Politics of Abortion by Janine Brodie, Shelley A. M. Gavigan, Jane Jenson |journal=Canadian Journal of Political Science |date=1992 |volume=25 |issue=4 |pages=770–771 |jstor=3229693 |doi=10.1017/S0008423900004558 }}</ref> Brodie was then hired as Chair of the Department of Political Science at the [[University of Alberta]] in 1997.<ref name = "Distinguished Professor">{{cite web |title=Distinguished Professor Janine Brodie |url=https://sites.ualberta.ca/~jbrodie/ |website=/sites.ualberta.ca |accessdate=June 4, 2019}}</ref> From 1997 to 2004, she served in this role.<ref name = "Order of Canada"/> During her time as Chair of the Department of Political Science, Brodie was a lead investigator in a major collaborative research initiatives program (MCRI) project entitled Globalization and its Challengers.<ref name = "about"/>

Brodie was elected as a [[Fellow of the Royal Society of Canada]] in 2002 and appointed as a Tier 1 [[Canada Research Chair]] in 2004 as she stepped down from her position as Chair of the Department of Political Science.<ref name = "about"/><ref name = "folio">{{cite web |title=Political science professor honoured by Royal Society |url=https://www.folio.ca/political-science-professor-honoured-by-royal-society/ |website=folio.ca |accessdate=June 4, 2019 |date=September 24, 2014}}</ref> Two years later, Brodie published ''Reinventing Canada politics of the 21st century'' with Linda Trimble. In 2008, Brodie edited a policy research paper with professor [[Isabella C. Bakker]] after consultation with the Policy Research Fund of Status of Women Canada. The paper, titled "Where Are the Women?", focused on gender within contemporary Canadian public policy and was published through the [[Canadian Centre for Policy Alternatives]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Where Are the Women? |url=https://www.policyalternatives.ca/publications/reports/where-are-women |website=policyalternatives.ca |accessdate=May 1, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |author1=Janine Brodie |author2=Isabella Bakke |title=Where Are the Women? |url=https://www.policyalternatives.ca/sites/default/files/uploads/publications/National_Office_Pubs/2008/Where_Are_the_Women_Contents_Intro.pdf |website=policyalternatives.ca |accessdate=May 1, 2019}}</ref> As a result of her scholarly research on poverty and inequality, Brodie was named a 2010 [[Pierre Elliott Trudeau Foundation#Trudeau Fellowships|Trudeau Fellow]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Trudeau Foundation Announces Latest $225,000 Fellowship Prize Winners |url=https://www.newswire.ca/news-releases/trudeau-foundation-announces-latest-225000-fellowship-prize-winners-545622012.html |website=newswire.ca |accessdate=June 4, 2019 |date=September 28, 2010}}</ref> The following year, Brodie became a Distinguished University Professor at the University of Alberta<ref name = "Order of Canada"/> and was renewed as a Canada Research Chair.<ref>{{cite web |last1=Brown |first1=Michael |title=U of A hits a CRC high |url=https://www.ualberta.ca/chemistry/news-and-events/news/2011/october/uofahitsacrchigh |website=ualberta.ca |accessdate=June 4, 2019 |date=October 12, 2011}}</ref> While simultaneously serving in these academic roles, Brodie served a five-year term as the Director of Social Science Division of the [[Royal Society of Canada]].<ref name = "about"/><ref>{{cite web |title=Janine Brodie |url=https://www.broadbentinstitute.ca/janinebrodie |website=broadbentinstitute.ca |accessdate=June 5, 2019}}</ref>

She received the [[Queen Elizabeth II Diamond Jubilee Medal]] in 2013<ref>{{cite web |title=2013 Arts Awards Night |url=https://www.ualberta.ca/arts/faculty-news/2013/may/2013artsawardsnight |website=ualberta.ca |accessdate=June 5, 2019 |date=May 9, 2013}}</ref> and the Royal Society of Canada's Innis-Gérin Medal in 2014.<ref name = "folio"/> She also edited the fifth edition of ''Critical concepts: an introduction to politics'' with Sandra Rein and [[Malinda S. Smith|Malinda Smith]]. The book focused on political issues and ethical dilemmas as they related to current political upheaval and global tensions.<ref>{{cite web |title=CRITICAL CONCEPTS: AN INTRODUCTION TO POLITICS, 5/E |url=http://neoliberalismcrises.com/about-janine-brodie/scholarship/ |website=neoliberalismcrises.com |accessdate=June 5, 2019}}</ref> In September 2017, Brodie was the recipient of the University of Alberta's University Cup, an award given to their academic staff on the basis of outstanding scholarly research, teaching and service to the university and the community.<ref>{{cite web |author1=Donna McKinnon |title=University of Alberta honours outstanding faculty, students, and staff |url=https://www.ualberta.ca/arts/faculty-news/2017/september/university-of-alberta-honours-outstanding-faculty-students-and-staff |website=ualberta.ca |accessdate=June 4, 2019 |date=September 28, 2017}}</ref> She was also the winner of the Academic Women's Association Woman of the Year award.<ref name = "AWA">{{cite web |title=Dr. Janine Brodie |url=https://uofaawa.wordpress.com/awa-woman-of-the-year/dr-janine-brodie/ |website=uofaawa.ca |accessdate=June 5, 2019|date=September 2017 }}</ref> The following year, Brodie was named a member of the [[Order of Canada]] for her research in Canadian politics, public policy, social governance, and gender politics.<ref>{{cite web |title=DR. JANINE BRODIE, C.M. |url=https://www.gg.ca/en/honours/recipients/146-3172 |website=gg.ca |accessdate=June 4, 2019}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |title=UWindsor alumni receive one of the country's highest civilian honours |url=https://www.uwindsor.ca/viewmagazine/node/178 |website=uwindsor.ca |accessdate=June 4, 2019 |date=March 13, 2018}}</ref> She also edited a book titled ''Contemporary inequalities and social justice in Canada'' through the [[University of Toronto Press]]. The book was compiled of works from Alexa Degagné, [[Judy Fudge]], Grace-Edward Galabuzi, Hayden King, Judy Rebick, David Robichaud, Meenal Shrivastava, and Malinda Smith which focused on the changing landscapes of Canadian society.<ref>{{cite web |title=JANINE BRODIE: CONTEMPORARY INEQUALITIES AND SOCIAL JUSTICE IN CANADA |url=http://www.fondationtrudeau.ca/en/activities/publications/janine-brodie-contemporary-inequalities-and-social-justice-canada |website=fondationtrudeau.ca |accessdate=June 5, 2019 |date=April 30, 2018}}</ref>
