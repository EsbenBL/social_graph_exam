'''Rogers Brubaker''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|b|r|uː|b|eɪ|k|ər}}; born 1956) is professor of [[sociology]] at [[University of California, Los Angeles]] and UCLA Foundation Chair.<ref>[http://www.sscnet.ucla.edu/soc/faculty/brubaker/ "Rogers Brubaker"], UCLA.</ref> He has written academic works on [[social theory]], [[immigration]], [[citizenship]], [[nationalism]], [[Ethnic group|ethnicity]], [[religion]], [[gender]], [[populism]], and digital hyperconnectivity.  <ref>Emma Green (October 2, 2016). [https://www.theatlantic.com/politics/archive/2016/10/trans-brubaker-race-gender/502397/ "If Americans Can Be Transgender, Can They Be Transracial?"], ''The Atlantic''.</ref>

Born in [[Evanston, Illinois]], Brubaker attended [[Harvard University]] and the [[University of Sussex]] before receiving a PhD from [[Columbia University]] in 1990.<ref>[https://brubaker.scholar.ss.ucla.edu/wp-content/uploads/sites/6/2019/05/vita.pdf "Rogers Brubaker"], </ref>
