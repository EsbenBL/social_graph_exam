'''Alice J. Kang''' is Associate Professor of Political Science at [[University of Nebraska-Lincoln]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=https://polisci.unl.edu/alice-kang|title=Alice Kang {{!}} Department of Political Science {{!}} Nebraska|website=polisci.unl.edu|access-date=2019-02-19}}</ref>

== Education ==
Kang received her B.A. in Economics from [[Brown University]]  in 2000 and her Ph.D in Political Science from [[University of Wisconsin–Madison|University of Wisconsin-Madison]] in 2010.<ref name=":0" />

== Career ==
Kang holds a joint appointment with the University of Nebraska-Lincoln’s Institute for Ethnic Studies (African and African American Studies), and is affiliated with the university’s Women's and Gender Studies Program.<ref name=":0" />

Before becoming an academic, Kang was a [[Peace Corps]] volunteer in [[Burkina Faso]] from 2000 to 2002, specializing in community health. She also served as a consultant for the [[International Foundation for Electoral Systems|International Foundation for Election Systems]] and as an intern for [[AllAfrica.com]], both in Washington, D.C.<ref name=":0" />

== Research ==
Kang teaches and conducts research on comparative politics, with a regional focus on Africa and especially [[Niger]].<ref name=":0" /> Her scholarly contributions are in the areas of women's activism, [[Women in government#Quotas|gender quotas]], and women's representation in politics. Kang's research is multidisciplinary, merging qualitative and quantitative methods and borrowing approaches from sociology and anthropology. Her publications include the 2015 book ''Bargaining for Women's Rights: Activism in an Aspiring Muslim Democracy'' ({{ISBN| 978-0-8166-9218-7}}), which is known for developing a theory of how conservative ''counter''-activism, and not just progressive activism, influences why some women's movements succeed more than others.<ref>{{Cite book|last=Alidou|first=Ousseina|url=https://www.worldcat.org/oclc/862381524|title=Muslim women in postcolonial Kenya: leadership, representation, and social change|isbn=1-306-04668-8|location=Madison, Wisconsin|oclc=862381524}}</ref> Her work is rooted in immersive field research, which Kang has conducted since 2006 in Niger, [[Benin]], Burkina Faso, and [[South Africa]]. Her field work in Niger during 2007-2008 was funded by a [[Fulbright-Hays|Fulbright Hays]] Doctoral Dissertation Research Award.
