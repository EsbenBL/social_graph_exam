'''Eric Dunning''' (27 December 1936 – 10 February 2019<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://norbert-elias.com/death-of-eric-dunning/ |title=Norbert Elias : Death of Eric Dunning |language=en-US |access-date=2019-02-21}}</ref>) was a British sociologist who was [[Emeritus Professor]] of [[sociology]] at the [[University of Leicester]].

==Career==
Eric Dunning was a pioneer in the sociology of sport and the founder, with Patrick Murphy and John Williams, of the [https://web.archive.org/web/20110115163640/http://www.le.ac.uk/snccfr/ Sir Norman Chester Centre for Football Research]. He was the author of a number of books and articles on sport and the figurational sociology of [[Norbert Elias]]. Although officially retired, Dunning remained an Emeritus Professor of the University of Leicester as well as a Visiting Professor at the [[Chester Centre for Research into Sport and Society]]. Dunning was also a member of the editorial board of the Jornal de Ciências do Exercicio e Esporte, Paraná Federal University, Brazil.<ref>[http://www2.le.ac.uk/departments/sociology/people/edunning Dunning's faculty page] at the University of Leicester website. Web accessed July 2013.</ref>

Dunning had edited and co-authored numerous books in the sociology of sport. In 1999 he published his first sole-authored book, ''Sport Matters''.<ref>{{cite book | last = Dunning | first = Eric | title = Sport matters: sociological studies of sport, violence, and civilization | publisher = Routledge | location = London New York | year = 1999 | isbn = 9780415093781 }}</ref> In October 2000, he co-edited the comprehensive ''Handbook of Sports Studies'' with Jay Coakley.<ref>{{cite book | last1 = Dunning | first1 = Eric | last2 = Coakley | first2 = Jay | title = Handbook of sports studies | publisher = SAGE | location = London | year = 2000 | isbn = 9780761949497 }}</ref> His book about [[Norbert Elias]], ''Norbert Elias and Modern Sociology'' is based partly on Dunning's personal experience working with Elias for more than three decades. The book, co-authored with [[Jason Hughes (sociologist)|Jason Hughes]], was published in 2012.<ref>{{cite book | last1 = Dunning | first1 = Eric | last2 = Hughes | first2 = Jason | author-link2 = Jason Hughes (sociologist) | title = Norbert Elias and modern sociology knowledge, interdependence, power, process | publisher = Bloomsbury Academic | location = London | year = 2012 | isbn = 9781780932255 }}</ref>

==Research interests==
* sociological theory, especially the functional sociology and theory of civilizing processes of Norbert Elias and its place within the wider field of classical and modern sociology
* the sociology of sport and leisure
* problems of violence and civilization, especially sports-related violence such as football hooliganism
* the use of Elias's theory in understanding the Holocaust and genocide more generally.
