'''Francis T. "Frank" McAndrew''' (b. January 27, 1953 – Augsburg, Germany) is an American [[social psychologist]] and the Cornelia H. Dudley Professor of Psychology at [[Knox College (Illinois)|Knox College]] in [[Galesburg, Illinois]].<ref name=McAndrew>[https://www.knox.edu/academics/majors-and-minors/psychology/faculty/mcandrew-frank Biography of Frank McAndrew at Knox College]</ref> At Knox, he founded the environmental studies program and chaired the psychology department for a decade. McAndrew is an elected fellow of numerous professional organizations, including the Association for Psychological Science, the Society for Personality and Social Psychology, the Society of Experimental Social Psychology, and the Midwestern Psychological Association (Charter Fellow). He received a B.S. in psychology from [[King's College (Pennsylvania)|King’s College]] in Pennsylvania and also a Ph.D. in Experimental Psychology from the [[University of Maine]].<ref name=McAndrew/>

Early in his career, McAndrew specialized in the study of [[environmental psychology]] and [[nonverbal communication]]. He is the author of one of the classic texts in the field, ''Environmental Psychology''.<ref>{{cite book |last=McAndrew|first=Francis T.|authorlink=Francis T. McAndrew |date=1992 |title=Environmental Psychology}}</ref> In mid-career, he moved into the study of [[evolutionary psychology]] where he became best known for his pioneering work on gossip,<ref>[http://blogs.wgbh.org/innovation-hub/2016/6/2/mcandrew-gossip/ Sollinger, M. (2016). Pssssst, I Hear This Article Is About the Science of Gossip]</ref> creepiness,<ref>[http://www.today.com/health/what-makes-person-creepy-these-behaviors-physical-traits-study-finds-t85401 Holohan, M. (2016). What makes a person creepy?]</ref> and the psychology of mass shootings.<ref>[https://www.sfchronicle.com/crime/article/YouTube-shooting-Attacker-bought-pistol-in-12810479.php Fimrite, P. & Lyons, J. (2018). YouTube shooting: Attacker reloaded handgun during shooting, police say]</ref><ref>[https://time.com/5476998/risk-of-guns-america/?xid=tcoshare Chan, M. (2019). How Likely Is the Risk of Being Shot in America? It Depends]</ref>

In recent years, McAndrew has become an essayist and purveyor of psychological science to lay audiences. He has published in dozens of well-known popular media outlets such as ''Time'',<ref>[http://time.com/4450356/why-you-shouldnt-want-to-always-be-happy/?xid=time_socialflow_twitter Why You Shouldn’t Want to Always Be Happy]</ref> CNN,<ref>[https://www.cnn.com/2016/07/08/health/psychology-mass-shootings/index.html The evolutionary psychology behind mass shootings]</ref> ''The Guardian'',<ref>[https://www.theguardian.com/commentisfree/2016/aug/17/psychology-happiness-contentment-humans-aspire-goals-accomplish-evolution Don't try to be happy. We're programmed to be dissatisfied]</ref> and ''Scientific American''.<ref>[https://www.scientificamerican.com/author/frank-t-mcandrew/ Stories by Frank T. McAndrew]</ref> He is also a blogger for ''Psychology Today'' Magazine.<ref>[https://www.psychologytoday.com/experts/frank-t-mcandrew-phd Frank T. McAndrew at ''Psychology Today'']</ref>

McAndrew grew up in the [[Northeastern Pennsylvania]] towns of [[Scranton]] and [[Dallas, Pennsylvania|Dallas]]. He is married and has a son, a daughter, and a granddaughter. He was a wrestler in high school and college, and he coached the Knox College wrestling team for almost 30 years, with twelve years as the head coach.<ref>[http://www.theknoxstudent.com/news/2015/09/23/knox-wrestling-becomes-knox-history/#.WDN2DnowCMk Watkins, Sam (23 September 2015). Article in ''The Knox Student'']</ref>
