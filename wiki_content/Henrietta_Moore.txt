'''Dame Henrietta Louise Moore''', {{post-nominals|country=GBR|DBE|FBA|FAcSS|sep=,|size=100%}} (born 18 May 1957) is a British [[social anthropology|social anthropologist]]. She is the director of the Institute for Global Prosperity at [[University College, London]] (UCL), part of the [[The Bartlett|Bartlett]], UCL's Faculty of the Built Environment.

==Early life==

Moore graduated from [[Durham University]] with an [[British undergraduate degree classification#Upper second-class honours|upper second]] in Archaeology and Anthropology in 1979.<ref>{{cite web |title=XXV (ns) no. 1 including supplement |url=http://reed.dur.ac.uk/xtf/view?docId=bookreader/DU_Gazettes/DUGazette25_1/dg251METS.xml#page/58/mode/2up |website=Durham University Gazette |access-date=4 October 2018 |language=en}}</ref> She continued her studies at [[Newnham College, Cambridge]], completing a PhD in 1983.<ref name="henriettamoore">{{cite book |title=Moore, Prof. Dame Henrietta (Louise), (born 18 May 1957) |url=http://www.ukwhoswho.com/view/10.1093/ww/9780199540884.001.0001/ww-9780199540884-e-246563 |website=UK Who's Who |doi = 10.1093/ww/9780199540884.013.U246563|isbn = 978-0-19-954088-4|access-date=4 October 2018 |language=en }}</ref>

==Career==

After leaving university Moore spent one year working for the [[United Nations]] in [[Burkina Faso]] as a Field Director.<ref name=henriettamoore /> She then became a Curatorial Assistant at the [[Museum of Archaeology and Anthropology, University of Cambridge]] before joining the [[University of Kent]] as a Lecturer in [[Social Anthropology]] in 1985.<ref name=henriettamoore /> Moore eventually rejoined Cambridge as a lecturer, where she became Director of Studies in Anthropology at [[Girton College, Cambridge|Girton College]] and then a [[Fellow]] of [[Pembroke College, Cambridge|Pembroke College]] in 1989.<ref name=henriettamoore />

After a series of academic appointments in Social Anthropology at the [[London School of Economics]] Moore took up the William Wyse Chair of Social Anthropology at [[Cambridge University]]. In 2009 Moore was made a Professorial Fellow of [[Jesus College, Cambridge]].<ref name=henriettamoore />

Moore has been critical of proposed restrictions in immigration, as proposed by [[Leave.EU]] in the run-up to the [[2016 United Kingdom European Union membership referendum]].<ref>{{cite web |last1=Moore |first1=Henrietta |title=Migration is a part of today's world. We can't just shut the borders, whatever the Leave campaign tells you |url=https://www.independent.co.uk/voices/migration-is-a-part-of-today-s-world-we-can-t-just-shut-the-borders-whatever-the-leave-campaign-a7089936.html |website=The Independent |date=19 June 2016 |access-date=4 October 2018}}</ref> She is the Chair and Co-Founder of SHM Group, a consulting firm specialising in [[change management]].<ref>{{cite web |title=SHM Group |url=https://www.shm-group.net/henrietta.php |website=www.shm-group.net |access-date=4 October 2018}}</ref>

==Honours==
She was appointed [[Dame Commander of the Order of the British Empire]] (DBE) in the [[2016 New Year Honours]] for services to the social sciences.<ref>{{London Gazette|issue=61450 |supp=y|page=N8|date=30 December 2015}}</ref>

She was elected a Fellow of the [[British Academy]] in 2007.<ref name="FBA">{{cite web|url=https://www.britac.ac.uk/fellowship/directory/archive.asp?fellowsID=4253|title=MOORE, Professor H L (born 18 May 1957)|work=British Academy Fellows Archive|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110606095640/https://www.britac.ac.uk/fellowship/directory/archive.asp?fellowsID=4253|archive-date=6 June 2011}}</ref> In 1995, Moore and [[Megan Vaughan]] were awarded the [[Herskovits Prize]] by the [[African Studies Association]] for their book ''Cutting Down Trees: Gender, Nutrition, and Agricultural Change in the Northern Province of Zambia, 1890-1990''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Melville J. Herskovits Prize|url=http://www.africanstudies.org/awards-prizes/herskovits-prize|website=African Studies Association|access-date=20 February 2017|date=24 January 2017}}</ref>

In 2014 Moore received an honorary degree from [[Queen's University Belfast]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Social anthropologist recognised by Queen's University Belfast |url=https://www.cam.ac.uk/research/news/social-anthropologist-recognised-by-queens-university-belfast |website=University of Cambridge |access-date=4 October 2018 |language=en |date=24 June 2014}}</ref>
