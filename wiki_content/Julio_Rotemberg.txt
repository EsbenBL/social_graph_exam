'''Julio Jacobo Rotemberg''' was an Argentine/American [[economist]] at [[Harvard Business School]]. He was known for his collaboration with [[Michael Woodford (economist)|Michael Woodford]] on the first [[New Keynesian economics|New Keynesian]] [[Dynamic stochastic general equilibrium|DSGE model]], especially on monopolistic competition.<ref name="Rotemberg1993">{{Citation |last1=Rotemberg |first1=Julio |last2=Woodford |first2=Michael |year=1993 |title=Dynamic General Equilibrium Models with Imperfectly Competitive Product Markets |work=NBER Working Paper No. 4502 |doi=10.3386/w4502 |doi-access=free }}</ref> He was also known for an alternative model of sticky prices.<ref name="Rotemberg1982">{{Citation |last=Rotemberg |first=Julio J. |year=1982 |title=Sticky Prices in the United States |journal=Journal of Political Economy |volume=90 |issue=6 |pages=1187–1211 |jstor=1830944|doi=10.1086/261117 |citeseerx=10.1.1.675.8591 |s2cid=7965196 }}</ref>

Rotemberg held a [[Bachelor of Arts|B.A.]] in economics (1975) from the [[University of California, Berkeley]], and a [[Doctor of Philosophy|Ph.D.]] in economics (1981) from [[Princeton University]].
