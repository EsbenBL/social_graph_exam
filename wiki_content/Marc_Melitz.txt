'''Marc J. Melitz''' (born January 1, 1968)<ref>''U.S. Public Records Index'' Vol 1 (Provo, UT: Ancestry.com Operations, Inc.), 2010.</ref> is an American [[economist]]. He is currently a professor of [[economics]] at [[Harvard University]].

Melitz has published a number of highly cited articles in the area of [[international economics]] and [[international trade]],<ref>{{As of|2010|02}}, Melitz is the most highly cited young economists according to the IDEAS repository: [https://ideas.repec.org/top/top.young.html Rankings at IDEAS:Top Young Economists, as of February 2010]</ref> most notably "The Impact of Trade on Intra-Industry Reallocations and Aggregate Industry Productivity" in ''[[Econometrica]]'' which explores the effects of international trade on the competition within domestic industries.<ref>{{Citation |first=Marc J. |last=Melitz |author-mask=——— |year=2003 |title=The Impact of Trade on Intra-Industry Reallocations and Aggregate Industry Productivity |journal=Econometrica |volume=71 |issue=6 |pages=1695–1725 |doi=10.1111/1468-0262.00467 |citeseerx=10.1.1.563.6294 }}.</ref>

In addition to his Harvard position, Melitz is also a research fellow of the [[Centre for Economic Policy Research]] and a research associate of the [[National Bureau of Economic Research]] since 2000. Before joining Harvard, Melitz was a professor at [[Princeton University]] .<ref name=cv/>

Melitz holds a  BA in Mathematics from [[Haverford College]] (1989), an MSBA in Operations Research from the [[University of Maryland, College Park]] (1992)<ref name=cv>According to his [http://scholar.harvard.edu/files/melitz/files/cv_1.pdf academic CV] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140308092639/http://scholar.harvard.edu/files/melitz/files/cv_1.pdf |date=2014-03-08 }}.</ref> a M.A.(1997), and a Ph.D. (2000) in Economics from the [[University of Michigan]].

In 2008, ''[[The Economist]]'' listed Melitz as one of the top 8 young economists in the world.<ref name="TheEconomist">{{Citation |date=December 30, 2008 |title=International bright young things |newspaper=The Economist |url=http://www.economist.com/businessfinance/displayStory.cfm?story_id=12851150 }}</ref>
