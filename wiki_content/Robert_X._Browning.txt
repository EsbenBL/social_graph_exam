'''Robert Xavier Browning''' is a professor at [[Purdue University]] and head of the [[C-SPAN]] Archives in [[West Lafayette, Indiana]].

==Education and academic career==
Browning graduated from [[Marquette University]] with a [[Bachelor of Science]] degree in 1972. He received a [[master's degree]] in [[public administration]] in 1977 and a master's degree in [[political science]] in 1978, both from the [[University of Wisconsin–Madison]]. In 1981, he was awarded his Ph.D., also from the University of Wisconsin–Madison, under the direction of [[Ira Sharkansky]] with the thesis "Political and economic predictors of policy outcomes: U.S. social welfare expenditures, 1947-1977".<ref>{{cite book |title=Political and economic predictors of policy outcomes: U.S. social welfare expenditures, 1947-1977 |last1=Browning |first1=Robert X |year=1981 |work=WorldCat |publisher=University of Wisconsin-Madison |oclc = 008354624}}</ref><ref name=Purdue>{{cite web |url=http://www.cla.purdue.edu/polsci/directory/index.cfm?p=Robert_Browning |title=Robert Browning |work=Purdue.edu |publisher=Purdue University |accessdate=29 November 2011}}</ref><ref name=CV>{{cite web |url=http://www.cla.purdue.edu/CV/1143/rbrowning_vita_2010.pdf |title=Robert Browning Curriculum Vitae |work=Purdue.edu |publisher=Purdue University |accessdate=29 November 2011 }}{{dead link|date=April 2018 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

In 1981, he became an assistant professor at [[Purdue University]],<ref name=CV/> where he is currently associate professor of communication/political science.<ref name=Weddle>{{cite news |title=C-SPAN Archive puts history on the Internet |author=Eric Weddle |newspaper=The Associated Press State & Local Wire |date=28 March 2010}}</ref><ref name=University>{{cite news |title=Purdue names School of Communication for C-SPAN founder |url=http://www.purdue.edu/newsroom/general/2011/story-print-deploy-layout_1_12151_12151.html |newspaper=University News Service |date=8 April 2011 |accessdate=29 November 2011 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20120314102157/http://www.purdue.edu/newsroom/general/2011/story-print-deploy-layout_1_12151_12151.html |archivedate=14 March 2012 }}</ref> At Purdue, Browning teaches courses related to American politics and the [[United States Congress]].<ref name=Purdue/>

==C-SPAN Archives==
Browning is the founding director of the [[C-SPAN]] Archives.<ref name=Cullen>{{cite news |title=15 Years of History in C-SPAN Archives Housed at Purdue |author=Kevin Cullen |newspaper=The Associated Press State & Local Wire |date=9 November 2002}}</ref> In 1986, with other [[Purdue University|Purdue]] professors, he developed the idea for an educational C-SPAN archive, which was approved in 1987 by David Caputo, then dean of Purdue's School of Humanities, Social Science and Education.<ref name=Lamb>{{cite book |title=America's Town Hall |author=Brian Lamb |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=KwiIAAAAMAAJ&q=America%27s+Town+Hall |year=1988 |publisher=Acropolis Books |pages=47–50|isbn=9780874918892 }}</ref> In September 1987, the archive began cataloguing recordings of C-SPAN's coverage of Congress and other public affairs programming.<ref name=Cullen/><ref name=Lamb/>

Browning assumed the title of Director of C-SPAN Archives in 1998, when the archives were transferred to C-SPAN's management.<ref name=CV/> He facilitates educational access to C-SPAN's collection and oversees the archives, which are located in [[Purdue Research Park]] in [[West Lafayette, Indiana]].<ref name=Purdue/> By the time of  the launch of the [[C-SPAN Video Library]] in 2010, Browning's  team converted over 120,000 hours of recordings of C-SPAN programming from analog to digital.<ref name=Raasch>{{cite news |title=Changing the way we view history |author=Chuck Raasch |url=https://www.usatoday.com/news/opinion/columnist/raasch/2010-03-26-common-ground_N.htm |newspaper=USA Today |date=26 March 2010}}</ref> In 2011, the C-SPAN Video Library was awarded a [[Peabody Award]].<ref>{{cite news |title=Morning Prize: C-SPAN lands Peabody for Library |author= Betsy Rothstein  |url=http://www.mediabistro.com/fishbowldc/morning-prize-c-span-lands-peabody-for-library_b39948 |work=Fishbowl DC |date=24 May 2011 |accessdate=3 January 2012}}</ref>
