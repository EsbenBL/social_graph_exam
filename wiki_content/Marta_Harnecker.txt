'''Marta Harnecker''' ([[Santiago de Chile]], 1937 – [[Vancouver]], Canada 14 June 2019)<ref name="Ciudadano">{{Cite web |url=https://www.elciudadano.com/chile/fallecio-marta-harnecker-una-de-las-mas-grandes-figuras-de-la-izquierda-latinoamericana/06/15/ |title=Falleció Marta Harnecker, una de las más grandes figuras de la izquierda latinoamericana |date=15 June 2019 |website=El Ciudadano |language=es |access-date=17 June 2019}}</ref> was a [[Chilean literature|Chilean]] journalist, author, [[psychologist]], [[sociologist]] and  [[Marxist]] intellectual. She worked in the analysis of the labor movement and elaborated many pieces of formative documentation. She was also an advisor to the socialist [[Marxist]] government of Cuba, as well as a collaborator with left-wing political movements in Latin America. She was active in the government of [[Salvador Allende]] between 1970 and 1973, and a [[Counselor to the President|counselor]] of [[Hugo Chávez]] between 2004 and 2011.

She married Canadian economist Michael A. Lebowitz.<ref name="Ciudadano" />

==Early life==
Harnecker's family had Austrian roots. She studied psychology at the [[Pontifical Catholic University of Chile|Catholic University of Chile]] in 1962, and did postgraduate studies in Paris with [[Paul Ricœur|Paul Ricoeur]] and [[Louis Althusser]]. Upon her return to Chile in 1968, she taught Historical Materialism and Political Economy in Sociology at the [[University of Chile]] and was director of the political weekly ''Chile Hoy''.

In 1968 she joined the [[Socialist Party of Chile]]. In 1973, she got caught up in the fall of president [[Salvador Allende]] and the [[1973 Chilean coup d'état|coup d'état]] led by General [[Augusto Pinochet]] and was forced into [[exile]] in Cuba. There, she founded the research institute Memoria Popular Latinoamerica (MEPLA) and continued to write.<ref name=bio>[http://www.avizora.com/atajo/colaboradores/textos_jorge_serrano_torres/0002_sistema_inteligencia_venezolano.htm  "El sistema de inteligencia venezolano y la guerra asimétrica"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150402143843/http://www.avizora.com/atajo/colaboradores/textos_jorge_serrano_torres/0002_sistema_inteligencia_venezolano.htm |date=2 April 2015 }} (visited 18/08/08)</ref>

==Publications==
Harnecker wrote over 80 books<ref name=biblio>[http://www.asturbulla.org/index.php/varios/libros-libres/8153-bibliografia-integra-de-marta-harnecker-82-libros-al-cumplirse-40-anos-de-la-primera-edicion-de-qlos-conceptos-elementales-del-materialismo-historicoq] (Retrieved 27 June 2019)</ref> including ''The Basic Concepts of Historical Materialism'' and ''The Left after Seattle''. Recent books include ''[[Hugo Chávez Frias]]: un hombre, un pueblo'', ''Venezuela: Militares junto al pueblo'' and ''Venezuela: una revolución sui generis'' which reflect her support of the [[Bolivarian revolution]]. (Source: Zed Books, publisher of ''Rebuilding the Left'' (London, 2007)).<ref name=que>[http://www.quepasa.cl/medio/articulo/0,0,38039290_101111581_308006675_6,00.html Chavez según Harnecker] en el periódico chileno ''Qué pasa'' (visited 18 August 2008)</ref><ref>http://pueblounido-peru.blogspot.com/2008/12/martha-harnecker-opina.html</ref> Her latest book is entitled ''A World to Build'' and it was published in English in January 2015. On 15 August 2014, Marta Harnecker accepted the 2013 Liberator's Prize for Critical Thought, awarded for this book.
