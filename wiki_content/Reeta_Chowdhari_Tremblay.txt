'''Reeta Chowdhari Tremblay''' (born 1950) is a Canadian [[political scientist]] and former [[academic administrator|senior academic administrator]]. She is an internationally recognized expert<ref name="auto">{{Cite web|url=https://www.sagepub.com/hi/nam/author/reeta-chowdhari-tremblay|title=Tremblay, Reeta Chowdhari|date=2019-05-12|website=SAGE Publications Inc|language=hi|access-date=2019-05-18}}</ref> on [[Kashmir]] and [[India]]-[[Pakistan]].

== Biography ==
Tremblay is Professor of [[Comparative Politics]] and [[Global South]] in the Department of [[Political Science]] at the [[University of Victoria]], [[British Columbia]]. She was born in [[Jammu and Kashmir (princely state)|Jammu and Kashmir]], [[India]], obtained an MPhil from [[Jawaharlal Nehru University]], New Delhi, and MA and PhD from the [[University of Chicago]] (1990).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.uvic.ca/socialsciences/politicalscience/people/directory/tremblayreeta.php|title=Reeta Tremblay - University of Victoria|website=UVic.ca|language=en|access-date=2019-05-18}}</ref> She is among the 50 prominent [[Indo-Canadians]] whose biographies appear in the [[Indian diaspora]] publication ''the A-List''.<ref>The A-List, Ed. Ajit Jain, The Society for Policy Studies, New Delhi</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.southasianpost.com/article/6606-indo-canadian-listers-excel-many-ways.html|title=Indo-Canadian A-Listers excel in many ways {{!}} South Asian Post {{!}} Indo Canadian newspaper - Vancouver, Surrey, Calgary, Toronto, Brampton, Edmonton, Winnipeg, Montreal|website=Southasianpost.com|access-date=2019-05-18}}</ref>

== Work ==
Her writings on [[Kashmir conflict|Kashmir]] and [[India–Pakistan relations]], a subject on which she is considered to be a leading North American expert, are widely reviewed and cited.<ref name="auto"/><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cpac.ca/en/programs/podium/episodes/15570963|title=Podium: Reeta Chowdhari Tremblay|website=Cpac.ca|accessdate=21 May 2019}}</ref> Her research deals with identity-based politics, [[secessionist movements]], the politics of [[subaltern (postcolonialism)|subaltern]] resistance and accommodation in [[post-colonial]] societies, [[democracy]] and [[governance]], primarily in the [[South Asia]]n context.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.researchgate.net/profile/Reeta_Tremblay|title=Reeta Tremblay {{!}} University of Victoria, Victoria {{!}} UVIC {{!}} Political Science|website=ResearchGate.net|language=en|access-date=2019-05-18}}</ref> She has authored or co-authored six books and over 60 articles and reviews.

Tremblay has held senior [[academic administrator|academic administrative positions]] in Canada: Vice-President Academic and [[Provost (education)|Provost]] at the University of Victoria,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.uvic.ca/news/topics/2011+indian-federalism-expert-to-be-vpac+ring|title=Indian federalism expert to be VPAC - University of Victoria|website=UVic.ca|accessdate=21 May 2019}}</ref>  Vice-President (Academic) and Vice-Chancellor (Pro Tem) at [[Memorial University]] of [[Newfoundland]]; earlier, she was Dean of the Faculty of Arts at Memorial University,<ref>''Nfld. school charges political interference; Memorial's board breaks its silence in controversy over government's role in search for new president''. Toronto Star ; Toronto, Ont. 07 Aug 2008: A.17.</ref> and Chair of the Department of Political Science at [[Concordia University, Montreal]] which she is credited with having re-built and raised to the ranks of the stronger such departments in Canada.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ctr.concordia.ca/2002-03/Sept_26/02-PoliSci/index.shtml|title=Concordia's Thursday Report|website=Ctr.concordia.ca|accessdate=21 May 2019}}</ref>  While at Memorial University, she helped found what has become the highly successful<ref>{{cite web|url=https://theindependent.ca/2012/01/20/sparks-ready-to-fly-at-mun-this-weekend/|title=SPARKS ready to fly at MUN this weekend|date=20 January 2012|publisher=|accessdate=21 May 2019}}</ref> SPARKS Literary Festival with noted Canadian poet [[Mary Dalton]] in 2009.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mun.ca/gazette/issues/vol43no8/sparks.php|title=SPARKS fly in January|website=Mun.ca|accessdate=21 May 2019}}</ref>

She is past president of the [[Canadian Political Science Association]] (CPSA),<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.mun.ca/gazette/issues/vol42no13/cpsa.php|title=Memorial VP named president-elect of CPSA|website=Mun.ca|accessdate=21 May 2019}}</ref> of the Canadian Asian Studies Association (CASA),<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://uia.org/s/or/en/1100066874|title = Canadian Asian Studies Association &#124; UIA Yearbook Profile &#124; Union of International Associations}}</ref> and the former Canadian Council Area Studies Learned Societies (CCASLS).<ref>https://www.idrc.ca/en/project/canadian-council-area-studies-learned-societies-2007-2008</ref> She has also been on editorial boards of ''PS:Political Science & Politics''<ref>{{cite journal|title=Committees of the American Political Science Association|journal=PS:Political Science & Politics|year=2005|volume=38|issue=2|pages=345–353|doi=10.1017/S1049096505056702|s2cid=233339642}}</ref> [[American Political Science Association]], ''[[Pacific Affairs]]'', ''[[Canadian Journal of Law and Society]]'' and ''Politics and Governance''.

Tremblay is also associated with the Centre d’études et de recherche sur l'Inde, l'Asie du Sud et sa diaspora (CERIAS)<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cerias.uqam.ca/|title=Bienvenue - Centre d'études et de recherche sur l'Inde, l'Asie du Sud et sa diaspora (CERIAS) - UQAM|website=Cerias.uqam.ca|accessdate=21 May 2019}}</ref> at the [[Université du Québec à Montréal]], a CAPI Associate at the Centre for Asia-Pacific Initiatives, a founding member of the Global South Asia Forum at the University of Victoria and a non-resident fellow at the Society for Policy Studies (SPS), New Delhi.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://spsindia.in/|title=Society for Policy Studies|last=|first=|date=|website=Society for Policy Studies|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=}}</ref> In 2015, she was a visiting fellow at the [[Netherlands Institute for Advanced Study |Netherlands Institute for Advanced Study at Wassenaar]]. In 2016 and 2017 summer terms, she was a visiting scholar at SOAS ([[School of Oriental and African Studies]]), [[University of London]].
