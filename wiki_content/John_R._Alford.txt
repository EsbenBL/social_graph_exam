'''John R. Alford''' is a [[political science]] professor at [[Rice University]], known for his research with [[John R. Hibbing]] in the field of [[genopolitics]].<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://journalstar.com/news/local/unl-political-scientist-says-genetics-politics-linked/article_3fcadae7-2547-5236-ae3b-058ce4ac86ec.html |title=UNL political scientist says genetics, politics linked |last=Hansen |first=Matthew |date=2005-07-25 |work=Lincoln Journal-Star |access-date=2019-03-29 |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.newscientist.com/article/mg19726411-800-are-political-leanings-all-in-the-genes/ |title=Are political leanings all in the genes? |last=Giles |first=Jim |date=2008-01-30 |work=New Scientist |access-date=2019-03-29 |language=en-US}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.chronicle.com/article/The-Biology-of-Voting/31258 |title=The Biology of Voting |last=Monastersky |first=Richard |date=2008-10-03 |work=The Chronicle of Higher Education |access-date=2019-03-29 |language=en-US |issn=0009-5982}}</ref> He has also testified as an [[expert witness]] in several court cases pertaining to [[Congressional redistricting]] in [[Texas]].<ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2003/07/01/us/across-us-redistricting-as-a-never-ending-battle.html |title=Across U.S., Redistricting as a Never-Ending Battle |last=Halbfinger |first=David M. |date=2003-07-01 |work=The New York Times |access-date=2019-03-29 |language=en-US |issn=0362-4331}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news |url=https://www.texastribune.org/2017/07/14/redistricting-trial-texas-refutes-discrimination-claims-while-blocking/ |title=On day 5 of redistricting trial, Texas rebuts claim that current political maps discriminate |last=Malewitz |first=Jim |date=2017-07-14 |work=The Texas Tribune |access-date=2019-03-29 |last2=Malewitz |first2=Jim |language=en}}</ref>
