'''Luc Coene''' (11 March 1947 – 5 January 2017)<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://www.lesoir.be/1409723/article/economie/2017-01-06/luc-coene-ex-gouverneur-banque-nationale-belgique-est-decede|title=Luc Coene, ex-gouverneur de la Banque nationale de Belgique, est décédé|last=Counasse|first=Xavier|date=6 January 2017|newspaper=lesoir.be|language=FR|access-date=2017-01-07}}</ref> was a [[Belgium|Belgian]] economist. He was Governor of the [[National Bank of Belgium]] (NBB) from April 2011 until March 2015 .

==Education==
Luc Coene was born in Ghent and graduated in economics at [[Ghent University]] in 1970 ([[Ghent]], [[Belgium]]) and obtained a [[postgraduate]] diploma in European economic integration at the [[College of Europe]] in 1971 ([[Bruges]]).<ref>{{cite web|url=http://investing.businessweek.com/research/stocks/people/person.asp?personId=23955061&ticker=BNB:BB&previousCapId=874182&previousTitle=GROUPE%20BRUXELLES%20LAMBERT%20SA|title=Profile: Luc Coene|website=Businessweek|accessdate=2017-01-07}}</ref>

==Career==
He started his career at the NBB in July 1973, where he worked at the Research Department (Information division) until May 1976. From May 1976 until November 1979 he worked at the Foreign Department (International Agreements division) of the NBB. From November 1979 until February 1985, worked as Assistant to the Belgian Executive Director at the [[International Monetary Fund]] (IMF). He worked as Deputy chef de cabinet to the Minister of Finance from February 1985 until November 1985 and Chef de cabinet to the Vice-Premier and Minister for the Budget from November 1985 until May 1988.

He was a Visiting Scholar at the IMF from June 1988 until November 1988 and an Economic adviser at the [[Directorate-General for Economic and Financial Affairs (European Commission)|ECFIN]] Directorate of the [[European Commission]] from January 1989 until January 1992. From February 1992 until October 1995 he worked for the NBB at the Foreign Department as Adviser to the head of department.

From November 1995 until July 1999, he was a [[Senator]] for the [[Flemish Liberals and Democrats|VLD]] in the [[Belgian Senate]] and subsequently Chef de cabinet to the Prime Minister and Secretary to the Council of Ministers from July 1999 until September 2001. From September 2001 until August 2003 he was Chairman of the Board of Directors of the Chancellery of, Prime Minister [[Guy Verhofstadt]] and Secretary to the Council of Ministers.

From August 2003, he became a Director and Deputy Governor of the NBB for a 6-year term. On 1 April 2011 he was appointed Governor until 11 March 2015.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nbb.be/fr/articles/fin-mandat-du-gouverneur-coene-et-nomination-du-gouverneur-smets|title=Fin mandat du gouverneur Coene et nomination du gouverneur Smets|date=6 March 2015|website=nbb.be|language=FR|access-date=2017-01-07}}</ref> He was succeeded by [[Jan Smets]]. In March 2015 he was appointed Member of the Supervisory Board of the ECB for a period of 5 years.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nbb.be/doc/ts/enterprise/press/2015/ecbpressrelease%20150219.pdf|title=Press Release {{ndash}} Supervisory Board Member appointed|date=19 February 2015|website=nbb.be|publisher=European Central Bank|access-date=2017-01-07}}</ref>

He was a member of the Advisory Board of the [[Itinera Institute]] [[think-tank]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.itinerainstitute.org/en/about-itinera/advisory-board/|title=Advisory Board|website=Itinera Institute|access-date=2017-01-07|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160816192942/http://www.itinerainstitute.org/en/about-itinera/advisory-board|archive-date=16 August 2016|url-status=dead}}</ref>

In March 2015 he became a member of the Supervisory Board of the [[Single Supervisory Mechanism]], the [[European Central Bank|ECB]]'s banking supervision mechanism directly supervising large banks.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.demorgen.be/binnenland/luc-coene-naar-europese-bankentoezichthouder-a2224349/|title=Luc Coene naar Europese bankentoezichthouder|date=19 February 2015|work=[[De Morgen]]|language=Dutch|accessdate=2015-02-19}}</ref>
