'''Sara Binzer Hobolt''', [[Fellow of the British Academy|FBA]] (born 1977) is a Danish [[political scientist]], who specialises in [[European politics]] and [[electoral behaviour]]. She holds the Sutherland Chair in European Institutions at the [[London School of Economics and Political Science]].<ref name="bio EI">{{cite web|title=Professor Sara B Hobolt|url=http://www.lse.ac.uk/europeanInstitute/staff/academicStaff/Hobolt/Home.aspx|website=European Institute|publisher=The London School of Economics and Political Science|accessdate=6 August 2017}}</ref><ref name="FBA">{{cite web|title=Professor Sara Hobolt|url=http://www.britac.ac.uk/user/3199|website=British Academy|accessdate=6 August 2017}}</ref>

==Early life and education==
Hobolt was born in 1977.<ref name="Nils" /> She undertook [[Postgraduate education|postgraduate studies]] in [[political science]] at the [[University of Cambridge]], completing her [[Doctor of Philosophy]] (PhD) degree in 2005.<ref name="Nils" /> Her [[doctoral thesis]] was titled "Europe in question: the role of political information in referendums on European integration".<ref>{{cite web|last1=Sara Binzer|first1=Hobolt|title=Europe in question: the role of political information in referendums on European integration|url=http://ethos.bl.uk/OrderDetails.do?uin=uk.bl.ethos.613655|website=E-Thesis Online Service|publisher=The British Library Board|accessdate=6 August 2017|date=2006}}</ref> While at Cambridge, she was a member of [[St John's College, Cambridge|St John's College]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Professor Ulinka Rublack announced as Fellow of the British Academy|url=http://www.joh.cam.ac.uk/professor-ulinka-rublack-announced-fellow-british-academy|website=St John's College|publisher=University of Cambridge|accessdate=6 August 2017|date=24 July 2017}}</ref>

==Honours==
In July 2017, Hobolt was elected a [[Fellow of the British Academy]] (FBA), the United Kingdom's [[national academy]] for the humanities and social sciences.<ref>{{cite web|title=Elections to the British Academy celebrate the diversity of UK research|url=http://www.britac.ac.uk/news/elections-british-academy-celebrate-diversity-uk-research|website=British Academy|accessdate=6 August 2017|date=21 July 2017}}</ref>

In 2011, Hobolt was awarded the [[European Union Studies Association|EUSA]] "Award for Best Book Published in 2009 or 2010" for her book ''Europe in Question: Referendums on European Integration''.<ref>{{cite web|title=Dr Sara Hobolt awarded EUSA prize|url=https://www.politics.ox.ac.uk/news/dr-sara-hobolt-awarded-eusa-prize.html|website=Department of Politics and International Relations|publisher=University of Oxford|accessdate=6 August 2017}}</ref> In 2012, she was awarded the Nils Klim Prize;<ref name="Nils">{{cite web|title=Sara Hobolt|url=http://www.holbergprisen.no/en/sarah-hobolt.html|website=Holberg Prize|publisher=University of Bergen|accessdate=6 August 2017}}</ref> this prize is awarded "to Nordic scholars under the age of 35, for outstanding contributions within the arts and humanities, social sciences, law or theology".<ref>{{cite web|title=About the Nils Klim Prize|url=http://www.holbergprisen.no/en/nils-klim-prize.html|website=Holberg Prize|publisher=University of Bergen|accessdate=6 August 2017}}</ref>
