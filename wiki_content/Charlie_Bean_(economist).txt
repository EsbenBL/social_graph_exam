'''Sir Charles Richard Bean''' (born 16 September 1953) is a British economist and Professor of Economics at the [[London School of Economics]]. He was previously [[Deputy Governor of the Bank of England|Deputy Governor for Monetary Policy]] at the [[Bank of England]] from 1 July 2008 until 30 June 2014.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/finance/bank-of-england/10704883/Broadbent-to-replace-Bean-as-BoE-deputy-governor-and-Nemat-Shafik-joins-MPC-first-woman-in-four-years.html|title=Broadbent to replace Bean as BoE deputy governor and Nemat Shafik joins MPC – first woman in four years|author=Szu Ping Chan|date=14 March 2014|access-date=30 March 2015}}</ref> From 2000 to 2008, he served as Chief Economist at the Bank.

Bean attended [[Brentwood School (Brentwood, England)|Brentwood School]] and [[Emmanuel College, Cambridge]], and was a contemporary of the comedian [[Griff Rhys Jones]] at both and the writer, [[Douglas Adams]], and the [[Member of Parliament (United Kingdom)|Member of Parliament]] (MP) [[Fabian Hamilton]] at Brentwood School. He gained his PhD at the [[Massachusetts Institute of Technology]] in 1981 with a thesis titled ''Essays in unemployment and economic activity'' under the supervision of [[Robert Solow]].<ref>{{cite web|url = http://library.mit.edu/item/000124484| title = Essays in unemployment and economic activity / by Charles Richard Bean | publisher = MIT Library | access-date = 4 December 2012}}</ref> In 1990 he was visiting Professor at [[Stanford University]] in 1990, and then a lecturer at the [[London School of Economics]], becoming a professor in 1990 and head of the Economics Department in 1999.

He has published articles on European unemployment, the [[Economic and Monetary Union of the European Union|Economic and Monetary Union]], and on [[macroeconomics]] generally. He was Managing Editor of the ''[[Review of Economic Studies]]'' from 1986 to 1990. Bean has also served in a variety of public policy roles, such as consultant to [[Her Majesty's Treasury]] and as special adviser to both the [[Treasury Select Committee|Treasury Committee of the House of Commons]] and to the [[European Parliament Committee on Economic and Monetary Affairs|Economic and Monetary Affairs Committee of the European Parliament]]. He was a special adviser to the [[House of Lords]] enquiry into the [[European Central Bank]].

He was [[Knight Bachelor|knighted]] in the [[2014 Birthday Honours]] for services to monetary policy and central banking.<ref>{{London Gazette |issue=60895 |date=14 June 2014 |page=b2 |supp=y}}</ref><ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/uknews/honours-list/10899033/Queens-Birthday-Honours-List-deputy-Bank-of-England-governor-Charlie-Bean-knighted.html |title=Queen's Birthday Honours List: deputy Bank of England governor Charlie Bean knighted |date=13 June 2014 |access-date=15 June 2014}}</ref> He is a fellow of the [[European Economic Association]].<ref>{{Cite web|title=Fellows {{!}} EEA|url=https://www.eeassoc.org/fellows|access-date=2021-02-26|website=www.eeassoc.org}}</ref>
