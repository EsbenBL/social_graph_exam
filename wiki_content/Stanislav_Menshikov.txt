'''Stanislav Mikhaĭlovich Menʹshikov''' ({{lang-ru|Станисла́в Миха́йлович Ме́ньшиков}}; 12 May 1927, in [[Moscow]] – 13 November 2014, in [[Amsterdam]]), was a [[Russians|Russian]] [[economist]] and former [[Soviet Union|Soviet]] [[diplomat]].<ref>{{cite journal |last=Douglas |first=Rachel |date=12 December 2014 |title=In Memoriam: Stanislav Mikhailovich Menshikov |journal=[[Executive Intelligence Review]] |volume=41 |issue=49 |pages=30–36 |issn= 0273-6314 |url=http://www.larouchepub.com/other/2014/4149menshikov_obit.html |accessdate=22 June 2015}}</ref> He is the author of numerous publications in Russian and English including ''The Anatomy of Russian Capitalism'', and a chapter in ''Capitalism, Communism and Coexistence''. He was a [[Professor]] at the [[Central Economic Mathematical Institute]] at the [[Russian Academy of Sciences]] in Moscow.<ref name=jkgfut>[http://www.palgraveconnect.com/pc/doifinder/10.1057/9780230523708 John Kenneth Galbraith and the Future of Economics]</ref> Menshikov was married to fellow economist Larissa Klimenko-Menshikova.

==Soviet career==
He was formerly employed at the [[Institute of World Economy and International Relations]] of the [[Soviet Academy of Sciences]] during the attempted economic reforms of [[Nikita Khrushchev]].<ref>Menshikov, Catastrophe or Catharsis? The Soviet Economy Today, p 8.</ref> 
In the late 1970s, Menshikov spent six years on the Secretariat of the [[United Nations]] in [[New York City|New York]], during which time he was often asked to comment upon the U.S. economy by ''[[The New York Times]]''.<ref>[http://findarticles.com/p/articles/mi_m1132/is_n8_v44/ai_13370528 "Catastrophe or Catharsis? The Soviet Economy Today"] ''[[Monthly Review]]'',  Jan, 1993 book review by Lynn Turgeon</ref>  During the 1980s, he served as a spokesman for the Soviet government on U.S. television.<ref>''The Philadelphia Inquirer'', March 18, 1985, "SHULTZ SAYS REAGAN THINKS TIME IS RIGHT FOR A SUMMIT"</ref><ref>''St Petersburg Times'', February 15, 1982, "Haig: Chemical Weapons Used By Moscow"</ref><ref>''The Philadelphia Inquirer'', December 30, 1983, SOVIET PARLIAMENT FINISHES UP, PRAISES ANDROPOV"</ref>

==Post-Soviet career==
Menshikov has published and commented on questions of [[economic]] [[Transition economy|transition]]. He co-authored ''Capitalism, Communism and Coexistence'' with [[John Kenneth Galbraith]]<ref>[http://post.economics.harvard.edu/faculty/galbraith/bio.html Bio: John Kenneth Galbraith] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20070205012522/http://post.economics.harvard.edu/faculty/galbraith/bio.html |date=2007-02-05 }}</ref><ref>''[[Toronto Star]]'', 4 May 2006, [https://www.thestar.com/obituary/atog/article/108168 John Kenneth Galbraith, 97: Economist, visionary]</ref> and wrote ''The Anatomy of Russian Capitalism''. He has been active with the [[LaRouche Movement]]'s [[Schiller Institute]] and has spoken at their conferences,<ref>[http://www.schillerinstitute.org/conf-iclc/2001/conf_prog_may_2001.html Schiller Institute Conference program, May 2001]</ref> along with Russian politician and author [[Sergey Glazyev]]. The English translation of Menshikov's book, ''The Anatomy of Russian Capitalism'', was published by LaRouche's ''Executive Intelligence Review''.<ref>[http://www.larouchepub.com/pr/2007/070323menshikov_bk.html Press Release: Anatomy of Russian Capitalism]</ref> In addition, he took part in panel discussion along with the [[Dalai Lama]], [[David Bohm]] and [[Robert Rauschenberg]] on the topic of "From Fragmentation to Wholeness" in a video released by the Forum on Religion and Ecology.<ref>{{cite web |url=http://fore.research.yale.edu/education/resources/videolist/video_3.html |title=Archived copy |access-date=2009-06-01 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100615005951/http://fore.research.yale.edu/education/resources/videolist/video_3.html |archive-date=2010-06-15 }}</ref> Menshikov also authored a Russian-language book on the subject of a "[[compassionate]] [[economy]]" in response to request from the Dalai Lama.<ref>http://www.louwrienwijers.nl/compassionateeconomy.html</ref>
