'''Tracy R. Lewis''', is a Martin L. Black professor of business administration within the [[Fuqua School of Business]] at [[Duke University]]. Before arriving at Duke University in 2003, he was the James Walter Eminent Scholar in Economics at the [[University of Florida]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Tracy R. Lewis|url=http://econ.duke.edu/people?Gurl=/aas/Economics&Uil=tracy.lewis&subpage=profile|work=Duke Economics|publisher=Duke University|accessdate=22 January 2011}}</ref>

His articles include "An Incentive Approach to Banking Regulation," [[Journal of Finance]], with [[Ronald Giammarino]] and [[David Sappington]], 1993. The following papers were also co-authored with Sappington; "Selecting an Agentâ€™s Ability," [[International Economic Review]],  1993; "Ignorance in Agency Problems," [[Journal of Economic Theory]], 1993; and "Incentives for Conservation and Quality-Improvement by Public Utilities," [[American Economic Review]], 1992.

Lewis is perhaps best known as co-author of the [[Brander-Lewis model]] of [[oligopolistic competition]], with [[James A. Brander]] -- ''Oligopoly and Financial Structure'' American Economic Review, 1986.
