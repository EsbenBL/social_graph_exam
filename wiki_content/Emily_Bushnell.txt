'''Emily W. Bushnell''' (born 1950) is an [[emeritus professor]] of [[psychology]] at [[Tufts University]] in [[Medford, Massachusetts|Medford]], [[Massachusetts]], USA. Her areas of professional interest include [[child development]], infant [[perception]], [[haptic perception]] and acquisition of perceptual-motor skills.<ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=3nYWDAAAQBAJ&pg=PT67|title=Learning Through Touch: Supporting Children with Visual Impairments and Additional Difficulties|last=McLinden|first=Mike|last2=McCall|first2=Stephen|date=2016-04-29|publisher=Routledge|isbn=9781136609541|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=9rwmg-maFdYC&pg=PA364|title=Language, Memory, and Cognition in Infancy and Early Childhood|last=Benson|first=Janette B.|last2=Haith|first2=Marshall M.|date=2010-05-22|publisher=Academic Press|isbn=9780123785763|language=en}}</ref> Professor Bushnell received her [[Doctor of Philosophy|Ph.D.]] in Developmental Psychology from the [[University of Minnesota]] in 1979 and a BA in Psychology from Swarthmore College in 1972.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://ase.tufts.edu/faculty/pdfs/retiredFaculty/bushnell.pdf|title=Resolution on the Retirement of Professor Emily Bushnell|date=2013|website=Tufts University|access-date=2017-10-04}}</ref> She served as chair of the Tufts Psychology Department from 1993 to 1996 and sat on the editorial board of the journal [[Child Development (journal)|''Child Development'']].<ref>{{cite web | url=https://ase.tufts.edu/psychology/people/bushnell/index.htm | title=Tufts University, Department of Psychology: People | publisher=[[Tufts University]] | accessdate=2017-10-04 }}</ref>

Bushnell, E. W., and Baxt, C. (1999) "Children’s haptic and cross-modal recognition with familiar and unfamiliar objects", ''Journal of Experimental Psychology: Human Perception and Performance'', '''25''', pp.&nbsp;1867–1881.

Roder, B. J., Bushnell, E. W., and Sasseville, A. M. (2000). "Infants preferences for familiarity and novelty during the course of visual processing", ''Infancy'', '''1''', pp.&nbsp;491–507.
