'''Daniel Levy''' (born 1962) is a [[Germany|German]]&ndash;[[United States|American]] [[Political sociology|political sociologist]] and an Associate Professor of Sociology at the [[Stony Brook University|State University of New York at Stony Brook]]. Levy earned a Bachelor of Arts in [[sociology]] and [[political science]] (1986) and a Master of Arts in sociology (1990) from [[Tel Aviv University]], as well as a Doctorate of sociology from [[Columbia University]] in 1999.<ref name="CV">{{cite web|url=http://www.stonybrook.edu/sociol/faculty/Levy/LEVY%20CV%20SEPT%2010.pdf |title=Curriculum Vitae - Daniel Levy |publisher=State University of New York, Stony Brook website |accessdate=May 17, 2011 |author=Levy, Daniel |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20111219232239/http://www.stonybrook.edu/sociol/faculty/Levy/LEVY%20CV%20SEPT%2010.pdf |archivedate=December 19, 2011 }}</ref> He is a specialist on issues relating to [[globalization]], collective [[memory studies]], and comparative [[historical sociology]].<ref name="ISHR">{{cite web | url=http://hrcolumbia.org/historical/bio.php?n=Daniel_Levy | title=Daniel Levy | publisher=Columbia University, Institute for the Study of Human Rights website | date=March 2010 | accessdate=May 16, 2011 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20131008220310/http://hrcolumbia.org/historical/bio.php?n=Daniel_Levy | archive-date=October 8, 2013 | url-status=dead }}</ref>  Levy, along with the historians [[Paul Gootenberg]] and [[Herman Lebovics]], is a founder and organizer of the Initiative for Historical Social Science, a program that is run out of Stony Brook with the goal of promoting the "New Historical Social Sciences".<ref name="IHSS">{{cite web|url=http://www.stonybrook.edu/sociology/ihss/ |title=Initiative for Historical Social Sciences |publisher=State University of New York, Stony Brook website |accessdate=May 17, 2011 |url-status=dead |archiveurl=https://www.webcitation.org/5yWCkrSiV?url=http://www.stonybrook.edu/sociology/ihss/ |archivedate=May 8, 2011 }}</ref> He also, along with the [[Human Rights]] scholar and historian [[Elazar Barkan]], is the founder of the "History, Redress, and Reconciliation" Seminar series at Columbia University. The seminars are an attempt to provide "a forum for interdisciplinary work on issues at the intersection of history, memory, and contemporary politics" focusing particularly on the "redressing [of] past wrongs and gross violations of [[human rights]]."<ref name="seminar">{{cite web | url=http://www.columbia.edu/cu/seminars/seminars/politics/seminar-folder/Redress.html | title=Current Seminars: History, Redress, and Reconciliation | publisher=Columbia University Seminars website | accessdate=May 17, 2011}}</ref>

Levy serves on the editorial boards of the [[American Sociological Association]]'s ''Rose Series in Sociology'',<ref name="CV" /><ref name="Rose">{{cite web | url=http://www.asanet.org/journals/rose/index.cfm | title=Rose Series in Sociology Editorial Board Members | publisher=American Sociological Association website | accessdate=May 17, 2011}}</ref> the ''[[European Journal of Social Theory]]'',<ref name="CV" /><ref name="social theory">{{cite web | url=http://www.sagepub.com/editorDetails.nav?contribId=652614 | title=Editor - Daniel Levy | publisher=[[SAGE Publications]] website | accessdate=May 17, 2011}}</ref> and for ''[[Memory Studies (journal)|Memory Studies]]''.<ref name="Memory">{{cite web | url=http://www.sagepub.com/journalsProdDesc.nav?ct_p=boards&prodId=Journal201801 | title=Memory Studies Editorial Board | publisher=SAGE Publications website | accessdate=May 17, 2011}}</ref>
