'''Timothy Douglas Harford''' {{Post-nominals|country=GBR|OBE}} (born September 1973) is an English [[economist]], [[radio personality|broadcaster]] and [[journalist]] who lives in [[Oxford]].<ref name="TimHarfordWebSiteBio">{{cite web|title=Tim Harford - The Undercover Economist - Biography|url=http://timharford.com/etc/biography/|website=Tim Harford - The Undercover Economist|access-date=2 December 2017}}</ref> 

Harford is the author of four economics books<ref name="undercover"/><ref>{{cite book|author=Tim Harford|title=Adapt: Why Success Always Starts with Failure|publisher=Abacus Software|year=2012|isbn=0-349-12151-6}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|author1=Tim Harford|author2=Michael Klein|title=The market for aid|publisher=International Finance Corp.|location=Washington, D.C.|year=2005|isbn=0-8213-6228-3}}</ref><ref>{{cite book|author=Tim Harford|title=The Logic of Life: The Undercover Economist|publisher=Abacus|location=London|year=2009|isbn=0-349-12041-2|url=https://archive.org/details/logicoflifeuncov0000harf}}</ref> and writes his long-running ''[[Financial Times]]'' column, "[[The Undercover Economist]]", syndicated in [[Slate (magazine)|''Slate'' magazine]], which explores the economic ideas behind everyday experiences. His column in the ''[[Financial Times]]'', "Since You Asked", ran between 2011 and 2014 and offered a [[sceptic|sceptical]] look at the news of the week.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://timharford.com/articles/sya/|title=Since You Asked|website=Tim Harford|access-date=5 October 2017}}</ref>

In October 2007 Harford assumed the presenter role at the BBC Radio 4 broadcast segment ''[[More or Less (radio programme)|More or Less]].'' The series segments are also available as podcasts. Subsequently Harford launched his own podcast on the podcast production network Pushkin Industries, called ''[[Cautionary Tales (podcast)|Cautionary Tales]]''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://timharford.com/etc/more-or-less/|work=Tim Harford - Podcasts|title=''Cautionary Tales''|access-date=17 April 2021}}</ref>

==Education==
Harford was educated at [[Aylesbury Grammar School]] and then at [[Brasenose College, Oxford]]. He obtained a Bachelor of Arts degree in [[Philosophy, Politics and Economics|philosophy, politics and economics]]<ref name="passedfailed"/> and then a [[Master of Philosophy]] in economics, in 1998.<ref name="harfordmphil">{{cite thesis|degree=MPhil|first=Tim|last=Harford|title=Sequential auctions with financially constrained bidders|publisher=University of Oxford|date=1998|url=http://solo.bodleian.ox.ac.uk/primo_library/libweb/action/dlDisplay.do?docId=oxfaleph013697676}}</ref>

==Personal life==
He lives in Oxford with his wife [[Fran Monks]], a photographer, and their 3 children.

==Career==
Harford joined the ''Financial Times'' in 2003 on a fellowship in commemoration of business columnist Peter Martin. He continued to write his financial column after joining [[International Finance Corporation]] in 2004, and he rejoined the ''Financial Times'' as economics lead writer in April 2006. He is also a member of the newspaper's editorial board.

Tim has spoken at [[TED (conference)|TED]], PopTech and the [[Sydney Opera House]]. He is a [[visiting scholar|visiting fellow]] at [[Nuffield College, Oxford]] and an honorary fellow of the [[Royal Statistical Society]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Mr Tim Harford, Visiting Fellow, Nuffield College, Oxford|url=http://www.nuffield.ox.ac.uk/People/sites/Tim.Harford/SitePages/Biography.aspx|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150129103543/http://www.nuffield.ox.ac.uk/People/sites/Tim.Harford/SitePages/Biography.aspx|archive-date=29 January 2015}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://timharford.com/etc/biography/|title=Biography|last=Chiappella|first=Wolf|date=17 November 2007|work=Tim Harford|access-date=30 November 2017}}</ref>

In August 2007, he presented a television series on the [[BBC]], ''Trust Me, I'm an Economist''.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://timharford.com/2006/08/trust-me-im-an-economist/|work=Tim Harford|title=Trust Me, I'm an Economist|date=5 August 2006|access-date=17 April 2021}}</ref> In October 2007, Harford replaced [[Andrew Dilnot]] on the BBC Radio 4 series ''[[More or Less (radio programme)|More or Less]]''. From November 2016, he presented an economic history documentary radio and podcast series ''[[50 Things That Made the Modern Economy]].'' Since November 2019, he has been presenting the podcast series ''[[Cautionary Tales (podcast)|Cautionary Tales]]''. On 13 November 2020 he started a new podcast series on [[COVID-19 vaccine|COVID-19 Vaccination]] called ''How to Vaccinate the World''.<ref>{{cite web|title=How to vaccinate the world|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/p08ycnc5|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201125214017/https://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/p08ycnc5|archive-date=25 November 2020}}</ref>

==Awards==
* ''More or Less'' won the [[Royal Statistical Society]]'s 2010 award for statistical excellence in broadcast journalism.<ref>[http://membership.rss.org.uk/main.asp?page=2838 Royal Statistical Society awards] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110821182651/http://membership.rss.org.uk/main.asp?page=2838|date=21 August 2011}} Accessed 5 June 2010</ref> In 2017 Harford was made an Honorary Fellow of the society.<ref>{{cite web|title=RSS announces honours for 2017|url=https://www.statslife.org.uk/news/3204-rss-announces-honours-for-2017|website=Royal Statistical Society|access-date=24 September 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200804032405/https://www.statslife.org.uk/news/3204-rss-announces-honours-for-2017|archive-date=4 August 2020|date=21 February 2017|url-status=dead}}</ref>
* ''More or Less'' won [[Mensa International|Mensa]]'s award for promoting intelligence in public life.<ref>[http://www.mensa.org.uk/cgi-bin/item.cgi?id=1363&d=23&h=5&f=3 More or Less Honoured] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100529200919/http://www.mensa.org.uk/cgi-bin/item.cgi?id=1363&d=23&h=5&f=3|date=29 May 2010}} Accessed 5 June 2010</ref>
* Harford was awarded the [[Bastiat Prize]] for economic journalism in 2007 (shared with [[Jamie Whyte]]).<ref>[http://www.policynetwork.net/bastiat-prize/media/fifth-annual-bastiat-prize-journalism-awarded-jointly-tim-harford-and-jamie-whyt Fifth Annual Bastiat Prize awarded jointly to Tim Harford and Jamie Whyte] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101127230053/http://policynetwork.net/bastiat-prize/media/fifth-annual-bastiat-prize-journalism-awarded-jointly-tim-harford-and-jamie-whyt|date=27 November 2010}} Accessed 5 June 2010</ref> In 2010 he again drew with Whyte, in second place.
* He was awarded the [[Order of the British Empire|OBE]] in the [[2019 New Year Honours]] ''for services to Improving Economic Understanding'' <ref>{{cite web|url=https://assets.publishing.service.gov.uk/government/uploads/system/uploads/attachment_data/file/768288/NY19_Queens_List_31_December_2018.pdf|title=New Year Honours list 2019|publisher=UK Government|date=31 December 2018|access-date=12 February 2019}}</ref>
