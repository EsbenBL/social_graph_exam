'''Virginia Sapiro''' (born February 28, 1951) is an American [[Political science|political scientist]] and [[Political psychology|political psychologist]].

A native of [[East Orange, New Jersey]], Sapiro graduated from [[Clark University]] in 1972, and obtained her doctorate from the [[University of Michigan]] in 1976, after which she began teaching at [[University of Wisconsin–Madison]]. She was appointed Sophonisba P. Breckinridge Professor of Political Science and Women's Studies there in 1995, and served through 2007, when she joined the [[Boston University]] faculty. Sapiro has served the [[American Political Science Association]] as secretary, vice president, and president of the APSA Organized Section on Women and Politics Research and as well as the APSA Organized Section on Elections, Public Opinion, and Voting Behavior.<ref>{{cite news |title=Virginia Sapiro |url=https://www.bu.edu/polisci/people/faculty/virginiasapiro/ |accessdate=January 22, 2019 |publisher=Boston University}}</ref>
