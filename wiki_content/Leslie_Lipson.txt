'''Leslie M. Lipson''' (November 14, 1912 – August 11, 2000) was an [[United States|American]] [[political scientist]] who was an expert in [[democracy]] and [[comparative government]], and worked as a [[professor]] at universities in [[New Zealand]] and the [[United States]].<ref name=gilmore>{{cite web |url=http://www.berkeley.edu/news/media/releases/2000/08/15_lipson.html |title=Leslie Lipson, political scientist and UC Berkeley professor for more than 30 years, dies at 87  |last=Gilmore |first=Janet |date=August 15, 2000 |website=Berkeley Campus News |publisher=UC Berkeley |access-date=September 17, 2017}}</ref> He was also a regular commentator on politics for media outlets such as [[PBS]] and the ''[[San Francisco Chronicle]]''.<ref name=Oliver>{{cite news |last=Oliver |first=Myrna |date=August 17, 2000 |title=Obituaries: Leslie M. Lipson; Writer, Political Science Professor |url=http://articles.latimes.com/2000/aug/17/local/me-5963 |work= Los Angeles Times|access-date=September 17, 2017 }}</ref> 

Lipson was born in [[United Kingdom|Britain]] in 1912, and was a student at [[St Paul's School, London]].<ref name=Bancroft>{{cite web |url=http://pdf.oac.cdlib.org/pdf/berkeley/bancroft/m2002_205_cubanc.pdf |title=Finding Aid to the Leslie Lipson Papers, 1941-2000 |date=July 2003 |website=Online Archive of California |publisher=Bancroft Library |access-date=September 18, 2017 }}</ref> He attended [[Balliol College]], [[University of Oxford|Oxford]], and graduated with [[British undergraduate degree classification#First-class honours|First-class honors]] in [[Classics]] and [[Literae Humaniores]] ("Greats").<ref name=Bancroft/> Lipson moved to America after receiving a [[Commonwealth Fund Fellowship]] to complete a [[PhD]] in [[political science]] at the [[University of Chicago]].<ref name=gilmore/><ref name=Bancroft/>

After finishing his doctorate, Lipson moved to [[Wellington]], [[New Zealand]], in 1939, to found a Department of [[Political Science]] at [[Victoria University of Wellington]].<ref name=Oliver/> He taught at Victoria for seven years, and in 1948 published a landmark book on [[New Zealand politics]] called ''The Politics of Equality: Adventures in New Zealand’s Democracy''.<ref name=johansson>{{cite book |last= Johansson|first=Jon |date=2011 |title=The Politics of Equality: Adventures in New Zealand’s Democracy |chapter=Introduction to this Edition |location=Wellington |publisher=Victoria University Press |page=vii }}</ref> Lipson argued that political and economic equality were the defining features of the country, and, in comparison to the [[Statue of Liberty]] in [[New York City|New York]], suggested that "In New Zealand, if any sculptured allegory were to be placed at the approaches of [[Auckland]] or [[Wellington]] harbor, it would assuredly be a statue of Equality."<ref name=lipson>{{cite book |last= Lipson|first=Leslie |date=1948 |title=The Politics of Equality: Adventures in New Zealand’s Democracy |location=Chicago |publisher=University of Chicago Press |page=8 }}</ref> ''The Politics of Equality'' was republished in 2011, and is still considered an essential work on [[New Zealand]]’s early [[Dominion of New Zealand|political development]].<ref name=Gustafson>{{cite web |url=https://nzbooks.org.nz/2011/non-fiction/as-we-were-barry-gustafson/ |title=As We Were |last=Gustafson |first=Barry |date=December 1, 2011 |website=New Zealand Books: A Quarterly Review |access-date=September 17, 2017}}</ref> 

Lipson moved back to the [[United States]] in 1947, taught at [[Swarthmore College]] for two years, and then was a professor at [[UC Berkeley]] from 1950 until his retirement in 1984.<ref name=gilmore/> He received several teaching awards from students while at [[UC Berkeley]], and was given the Berkeley Citation in 1980 for his service to the university.<ref name=gilmore/>

Lipson’s published works included ''The American Governor: From Figurehead to Leader'' (1939), ''The Great Issues of Politics'' (1954), which became a standard introductory text, ''The Democratic Civilization'' (1964), and ''The Ethical Crises of Civilization: Moral Meltdown or Advance?'' (1993).<ref name=Oliver/> He also published articles in ''[[American Political Science Review]]'', ''[[The Political Quarterly]]'' and ''[[The Journal of Politics]]''.<ref name=Bancroft/> Lipson’s papers are held at the [[Bancroft Library]] at [[UC Berkeley]].<ref name=Bancroft/>
