'''Susan Baker''' (born 9 October 1955) is a [[Emeritus|Professor Emerita]] in the School of Social Sciences and former co-director of the Sustainable Places Research Institute at [[Cardiff University]].<ref name=Newbook>{{cite news|url=https://www.cardiff.ac.uk/news/view/143801-new-book-from-sustainable-places-co-director-susan-baker|title=New Book from Sustainable Places Co-director Susan Baker|publisher=Cardiff University|date=30 September 2015|accessdate=31 May 2017}}</ref><ref name=Profile/> Her research concerns environmental governance in the European Union and [[ecofeminism]], gender and the environment.<ref name=Profile>{{cite web|url=https://www.cardiff.ac.uk/people/view/38140-baker-susan#tab-research|title=Professor Susan Baker: Research|publisher=Cardiff University|accessdate=31 May 2017}}</ref>

==Biography==
Baker was born in [[Loughlinstown]], Ireland and moved to [[Cork (city)|Cork]] as a child. She studied philosophy and economics at [[University College Cork]]. After completing an MA and working in the United States and [[Galway]], she won a government scholarship to study for a PhD in political science at the [[European University Institute]] (EUI) in Florence.<ref name=IrishTimes/> Baker completed her PhD thesis, on "Dependency, ideology and the industrial policy of Fianna Fail in Ireland, 1958&ndash;1972", in 1987, and it was examined by [[Philippe C. Schmitter|Philippe Schmitter]], [[Paul Bew]], [[Jean Blondel]] and James Wickam.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://cadmus.eui.eu/handle/1814/5203|title=Dependency, ideology and the industrial policy of Fianna Fail in Ireland, 1958&ndash;1972|publisher=European University Institute|accessdate=1 June 2017}}</ref> She subsequently lectured at [[Ulster University]] in Belfast for six years, and then took up a post at [[Erasmus University Rotterdam]], before moving to Cardiff. In 2001{{ndash}}02, she held a Jean Monnet Fellowship at the EUI's Robert Schuman Centre.<ref name=IrishTimes>{{cite news|url=http://www.irishtimes.com/news/taking-her-palce-at-the-swedish-royal-court-1.353660|title=Taking her palce <nowiki>[sic]</nowiki> at the Swedish Royal Court|work=Irish Times|date=27 March 2003|accessdate=31 May 2017}}</ref><ref name=Forskning>{{cite news|url=http://www.forskning.se/2002/12/03/kunglig-miljoprofessur-till-umea-universitet/|title=Kunglig miljöprofessur till Umeå universitet|publisher=Forskning.se|date=3 December 2002|accessdate=5 June 2017}}</ref> Baker became the first woman to be appointed to a [[King Carl XVI Gustaf Professorship in Environmental Science]] in 2003, spending the 2003{{ndash}}04 academic year visiting [[Umeå University]] while she held the award.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.timeshighereducation.com/news/appointments/174595.article|title=Appointments|work=Times Higher Education|date=7 February 2003|accessdate=3 June 2017}}</ref><ref name=IrishTimes/><ref name=Forskning/><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.umu.se/english/about-umu/news-events/news/newsdetailpage/royal-guest-professors-invited-to-unique-environmental-symposium-in-stockholm.cid185612|title=Royal guest professors invited to unique environmental symposium in Stockholm|publisher=Umeå University|date=21 March 2012|accessdate=1 June 2017}}</ref> In January 2013, she was elected as a Fellow of the [[Royal Swedish Academy of Agriculture and Forestry]].<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.cardiff.ac.uk/news/view/46709-international-honour-for-advancing-environmental-social-science-research|title=International honour for advancing environmental social science research|publisher=Cardiff University|date=25 February 2013|accessdate=31 May 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite Agriculture and Forestry|date=6 December 2012|accessdate=31 May 2017}}</ref>

Baker co-edited the book ''In Pursuit of Sustainable Development: New Governance Practices at the Sub-national Level in Europe'' (Routledge, 2008) with Katarina Eckerberg,<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.stockholmresilience.org/publications/artiklar/2009-12-22-in-pursuit-of-sustainable-development-new-governance-at-the-sub-national-level-in-europe.html|title=In pursuit of sustainable development: New governance at the sub-national level in Europe|publisher=Stockholm Resilience Centre, Stockholm University|accessdate=31 May 2017}}</ref> and is the author of ''Sustainable Development'', which is part of the Routledge Introductions to Environment Series and was published in its second edition in 2015.<ref name=Newbook/> She delivered [[Keynote|keynote lectures]] at the Nordic Environmental Social Science Conference in 1999 and 2007.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://ness2013.ku.dk/history/|title=History of the NESS Conferences|publisher=Nordic Environmental Social Science Conference 2013, University of Copenhagen|accessdate=5 June 2017}}</ref>

Baker is married to Sean Loughlin, an Emeritus Professor at Cardiff University. The couple met while studying at the EUI and have a daughter.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://life.eui.eu/sean-loughlin-sps-82-looks-back.html|title=Sean Loughlin (SPS '82) looks back|first=Jacqueline|last=Gordon|publisher=European University Institute|date=22 June 2016|accessdate=31 May 2017}}</ref>
