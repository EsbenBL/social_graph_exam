'''Sara McLaughlin Mitchell''' (born June 28, 1969) is an American [[political scientist]] and the  F. Wendell Miller Professor of Political Science at [[University of Iowa]]. She is known for her expertise on [[international relations]] and [[political methodology]].<ref>{{cite journal|last1=Owsiak|first1=Andrew P.|last2=Mitchell|first2=Sara McLaughlin|title=Conflict Management in Land, River, and Maritime Claims*|journal=[[Political Science Research and Methods]]|date=2017|volume=7|pages=43â€“61|doi=10.1017/psrm.2016.56|url=https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/political-science-research-and-methods/article/conflict-management-in-land-river-and-maritime-claims/4F008E814F5DA3660580B75152283DD3|access-date=13 May 2018|language=en|issn=2049-8470}}</ref>

== Education ==
Mitchell received her [[Bachelor of Science|B.S.]] in economics and political science from [[Iowa State University]] in 1991 and her [[Ph.D]] in political science from [[Michigan State University]] in 1997.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.saramitchell.org/mitchellcv.pdf|title=CV: Sara McLaughlin Mitchell|access-date=May 16, 2018}}</ref>   Her areas of expertise include international conflict, political methodology, and gender issues in academia. She is co-founder of the Journeys in World Politics workshop, a mentoring workshop for junior women studying international relations.

==Books==

* ''The Triumph of Democracy and the Eclipse of the West'' (Palgrave Macmillan 2013)
