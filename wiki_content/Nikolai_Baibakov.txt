'''Nikolai Konstantinovich Baibakov''' ({{lang-ru|Никола́й Константи́нович Байбако́в}}; 6 March 1911 &ndash; 31 March 2008) was a [[Soviet Union|Soviet]] statesman and economist who served as [[Ministry of Oil Industry (Soviet Union)|Minister of Oil Industry]] from 1944 to 1946 and 1948 to 1955 and Chairman of the [[Gosplan|State Planning Committee]] from 1955 to 1957 and 1965 to 1985. He was awarded a [[Hero of Socialist Labour]] in 1981.

== Biography ==
Born in [[Sabunçu, Baku|Sabunchu]], near [[Baku]], [[Russian Empire]], Baibakov finished [[secondary school]] in 1928 and entered the [[Azerbaijan Oil and Chemistry Institute]], from which he graduated in 1931 as a [[Mining engineering|mining engineer]]. In 1935, he was drafted into the [[Soviet Armed Forces|armed forces]]. After completing his military service, he was appointed chief of the oilfield production department in an industrial complex in the [[USSR]]. Later he was promoted to chief engineer, then general director. He was responsible for evacuating oil industry facilities to the eastern regions during the [[Nazi Germany|Nazi]] [[Operation Barbarossa|invasion]].<ref name=":0" />

Baibakov was appointed to the [[People's Commissariat|Narkomat]] as [[Ministry of Oil Industry (Soviet Union)|Minister of Oil Industry]] in 1944, serving until 1946. Because of his success in the planning of the oil industry sector and experience in economics, he was appointed as Chairman of the State Planning Committee, commonly known as [[Gosplan]], in 1955. He left the post in 1957 but returned to it in 1965. After stepping down in 1985, he continued to work as a state councillor in the [[Presidium of the Supreme Soviet|Presidium]] of the [[Council of Ministers of the Soviet Union|Council of Ministers]] until 1988. Then he was appointed head of the oil and gas section of the Academic Board of the Oil and Gas Institute with the [[Russian Academy of Science]]s.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title=Nikolai Baibakov|url=https://www.gazprom.com/about/history/people/baybakov/|access-date=2020-07-10|website=www.gazprom.com|language=en}}</ref> He died in 2008 in [[Moscow]].<ref>{{Cite news|last=Martin|first=Douglas|date=2008-04-02|title=Nikolai K. Baibakov, a Top Soviet Economic Official, Dies at 97|language=en-US|work=The New York Times|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2008/04/02/world/europe/02baibakov.html|access-date=2020-07-10|issn=0362-4331}}</ref>

==Honours and awards==
* [[Hero of Socialist Labour]] (1981)
* [[Order "For Merit to the Fatherland"]], 2nd class (March 7, 2006) - for outstanding services to the state
* Six [[Order of Lenin|Orders of Lenin]]
* [[Order of the October Revolution]]
* [[Order of the Red Banner of Labour]], twice
* [[Lenin Prize]] (1963) - for the discovery and development of gas-condensate fields
* Honorary Member of the [[Russian Academy of Natural Sciences]] (RANS, established in 1996 was awarded the Medal of the Academy)
* Academician of the [[Academy of Cosmonautics]]
* Laureate of the "Russian National Olympus"
* Honorary Citizen of Ishimbai (Bashkortostan) - as an active participant in the development of Bashkir oil industry (made with such an initiative in 1940)
* [[Istiglal Order]] (Azerbaijan)
