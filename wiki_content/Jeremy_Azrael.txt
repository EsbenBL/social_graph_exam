'''Jeremy Richard Azrael''' (March 23, 1935 – March 19, 2009) was an American political scientist known for his expertise on the [[economy of the Soviet Union]].<ref>{{Cite news |url=http://articles.latimes.com/2009/mar/27/local/me-passings27.S2 |title=PASSINGS / Jeremy R. Azrael |last=Staff |first=Times |date=2009-03-27 |work=Los Angeles Times |access-date=2018-07-22 |last2=Reports |first2=Wire |language=en-US |issn=0458-3035}}</ref>

Azrael was born on March 23, 1935, in [[Baltimore]], [[Maryland]].<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://id.loc.gov/authorities/names/n50029832.html |title=Azrael, Jeremy R., 1935-2009 |website=Library of Congress Name Authority File |access-date=2018-07-22}}</ref> He was educated at [[Harvard University]] and taught at the [[University of Chicago]] from 1961 to 1980. He began working at the [[RAND Corporation]] in 1974 before leaving to take several government positions. These included being a guest analyst at the [[CIA]] and a "charter member" of the [[Nationalities Working Group]], of the [[History of the United States National Security Council 1977–81|National Security Council]].<ref name="Kalinovsky">{{cite journal |last1=Kalinovsky |first1=Artemy M. |title=Encouraging Resistance: Paul Henze, the Bennigsen school, and the crisis of détente |journal=Reassessing Orientalism: Interlocking Orientologies during the Cold War |date=2015 |url=https://www.academia.edu/10633971/Encouraging_Resistance_Paul_Henze_the_Bennigsen_School_and_the_Crisis_of_D%C3%A9tente |accessdate=16 October 2018}}</ref>

He rejoined RAND in 1985, serving as a professor at the [[Pardee RAND Graduate School]] from then until 1990. Beginning in 1993, he was the director of the RAND Center for Russia and Eurasia. He became director of the RAND Business Leaders Forum in 1996, a year before it was formally launched.<ref name="pr" /><ref name=prgs>{{Cite web |url=https://www.prgs.edu/admissions/funding-your-phd/scholarships/azrael-scholarship.html |title=Azrael Scholarship |website=Pardee Rand Graduate School |language=en |access-date=2018-07-22}}</ref>

Azrael died on March 19, 2009, at his home in [[Sherman Oaks]], [[California]], after suffering from [[lymphoma]].<ref name=pr>{{Cite press release |url=https://www.rand.org/news/press/2009/03/20.html |title=Obituary: Jeremy R. Azrael Was Convener of Leaders Who Shaped Post-Communist Russian Economy |date=2009-03-20 |website=RAND Corporation |language=en |access-date=2018-07-22}}</ref> He was survived by his wife Julia Holm, son David Azrael, and three daughters ([[Deborah Azrael]], Ruth Azrael, and Abigail Azrael) from a previous marriage.<ref name="pr" /> After his death, several of Azrael's colleagues established the Jeremy R. Azrael Scholarship, a full-tuition scholarship awarded annually by the Pardee Rand Graduate School to a first-year student from a former state of the Soviet Union.<ref name=prgs/>
