'''Edward Emory Leamer''' (born May 24, 1944) is a professor of economics and statistics at [[UCLA]]. He is Chauncey J. Medberry Professor of Management and director of the UCLA Anderson Forecast.<ref>{{Cite web|title=About Us {{!}} UCLA Anderson School of Management|url=https://www.anderson.ucla.edu/centers/ucla-anderson-forecast/about-us|access-date=2020-07-24|website=www.anderson.ucla.edu}}</ref>

He attended Princeton (B.A., mathematics, 1966) and the University of Michigan (M.A., mathematics, Ph.D., economics, 1970).<ref>[http://www.anderson.ucla.edu/faculty/edward.leamer/ Edward E. Leamer Home Page] and [http://www.anderson.ucla.edu/faculty/edward.leamer/documents/Leamer%20Vita.pdf CV] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150402131745/http://www.anderson.ucla.edu/faculty/edward.leamer/documents/Leamer%20Vita.pdf |date=2015-04-02 }}</ref>

Leamer is the author of 4 books and over 100 articles on a range of subjects especially including applied [[econometrics]] and quantitative [[international economics]].

Leamer was the [[Vice President of the United States|vice presidential]] nominee on [[Laurence Kotlikoff]]'s independent ticket in the [[2016 US presidential election]].

Leamer is known amongst economists for his paper "Let's Take the Con Out of Econometrics",<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Leamer|first=Edward E.|date=1983|title=Let's Take the Con Out of Econometrics|url=https://www.jstor.org/stable/1803924|journal=The American Economic Review|volume=73|issue=1|pages=31–43|jstor=1803924|issn=0002-8282}}</ref> widely referred to as Leamer's critique, which is said to have catalyzed the implementation of more rigorous research designs in the economic sciences.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Edward Leamer Deserves a Nobel Prize for Improving Argumentation That Uses Statistics · Econ Journal Watch : Sensitivity analysis, econometrics, specification search, data mining, natural experiments, replication, statistical inference|url=https://econjwatch.org/articles/edward-leamer-deserves-a-nobel-prize-for-improving-argumentation-that-uses-statistics#:~:text=The%20Leamer%20critique%20caused%20economists,than%20he%20already%20has%20been.|access-date=2021-02-04|website=econjwatch.org}}</ref>
