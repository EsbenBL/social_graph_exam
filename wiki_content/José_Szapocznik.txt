'''José Szapocznik, Ph.D.''', (born in [[Havana]], [[Cuba]]), is a clinical [[psychologist]] of [[Poles|Polish]]-[[Cubans|Cuban]] ancestry and one of the nation's leading family therapists specializing in [[Hispanic]] families.  He is the developer of One-Person [[Family therapy|Family Therapy]] and Brief Structural Therapy.  He is a leading scholar on Hispanic [[psychology]].<ref name=fj>Brian J. Distelberg, Family Journal, "History of Evidence-Based Practices: An Interview With José Szapocznik", [http://tfj.sagepub.com/cgi/content/abstract/1066480707313823v1]</ref>

He is director of the Center for Family Studies at the [[University of Miami]],<ref name=cffs>[http://www.cfs.med.miami.edu/Docs/WhatsNew.htm Center for Family Studies website] {{webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20100609101734/http://www.cfs.med.miami.edu/Docs/WhatsNew.htm |date=2010-06-09 }}</ref> where he has been inducted into its [[Iron Arrow Honor Society]].  He has authored numerous scientific publications and has received numerous awards for his work on behalf of Hispanic families.
