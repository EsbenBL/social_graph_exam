'''Dirk Helbing''' (born January 19, 1965) is Professor of [[Computational Social Science]] at the Department of Humanities, Social and Political Sciences and affiliate of the Computer Science Department at ETH Zurich.

== Biography ==

Dirk Helbing studied physics and mathematics at the [[University of Göttingen]]. He completed his doctoral thesis at [[Stuttgart University]], on modeling social processes by means of game-theoretical approaches, [[stochastic]] methods, and [[complex systems theory]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://podium.nzz.ch/person/prof-dr-dirk-helbing/|title=NZZ Podium: Dirk Helbing|work=Neue Zürcher Zeitung|access-date=2017-05-12|language=de-CH}}</ref> In 1996, he completed further studies on traffic dynamics and optimization.

In 2000, he became a full professor and Managing Director of the Institute for Transport and Economics at [[Dresden University of Technology]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://tu-dresden.de/bu/verkehr/ivw/osv/die-professur/beschaeftigte|title=Mitarbeiter der Professur Ökonometrie und Statistik, insb. im Verkehrswesen|website=TU Dresden|access-date=2017-05-12}}</ref> Helbing was elected as a member of the [[German Academy of Sciences Leopoldina]] in 2008<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.leopoldina.org/de/mitglieder/mitgliederverzeichnis/member/1327/|title=Mitgliederverzeichnis|website=www.leopoldina.org|language=de|access-date=2017-05-12}}</ref> and of the [[World Academy of Art and Science]] in 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.worldacademy.org/view/general-listing|title=World Academy of Art & Science: General Listing|website=www.worldacademy.org|access-date=2017-05-12}}</ref> In January 2014 Prof. Helbing received an honorary PhD from [[Delft University of Technology]] (TU Delft).<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://delftoutlook.tudelft.nl/article/honorary-doctorates/|title=Honorary Doctorates - Delft Outlook|work=Delft Outlook|access-date=2017-05-12}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.ethz.ch/de/die-eth-zuerich/portraet/auszeichnungen/ehrungen-und-preise-an-eth-angehoerige-2014.html|title=Ehrungen und Preise an ETH-Angehörige 2014|website=www.ethz.ch|language=de|access-date=2017-05-12|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20170926190903/https://www.ethz.ch/de/die-eth-zuerich/portraet/auszeichnungen/ehrungen-und-preise-an-eth-angehoerige-2014.html|archive-date=2017-09-26|url-status=dead}}</ref> Since June 2015 he is affiliate professor at the faculty of Technology, Policy and Management at TU Delft, where he leads the PhD school in "Engineering Social Technologies for a Responsible Digital Future".<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.tudelft.nl/en/tpm/research/projects/engineering-social-technologies-for-a-responsible-digital-future/|title=PhD Program in Engineering Social Technologies for a Responsible Digital Future|website=TU Delft|language=nl-NL|access-date=2017-05-12}}</ref>

===Living Earth Simulator===

Helbing was the Principal Investigator on a project named ''FuturICT Knowledge Accelerator and Crisis Relief System'', a computing system working on [[big data]]sets, conceived as sort of a ''[[crystal ball]]'' of the world.<ref name="D. Weinberger" /> The core of the system is the ''Living Earth Simulator'', a computing machine attempting "to model global-scale systems — economies, governments, cultural trends, epidemics, agriculture, technological developments, and more — using torrential data streams, sophisticated algorithms, and as much hardware as it takes".<ref name="D. Weinberger" /> However, the project lost in the final round of the application for funding from the [[European Commission]] of €1 billion.<ref name="D. Weinberger">[[David Weinberger]], ''The Machine That Would Predict the Future'',''[[Scientific American]]'', Dec 2011, Vol. 305, Issue no. 6</ref> Despite this, the ideas developed by the group have influenced international research programs[[DARPA|.]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.darpa.mil/attachments/BreakoutSessionFrontiersSocialScience.pdf|title=Breakout Session 5: Frontiers in Social Science|last=DARPA|date=23 June 2016|website=Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency|access-date=15 June 2017}}</ref> Since 2017, the FuturICT 2.0 project is being funded by the European Commission's FLAG-ERA program.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.flagera.eu/flag-era-jtc-2016-project-kick-off-seminar/|title=FLAG-ERA JTC 2016 Project Kick-off Seminar|date=28 April 2017|website=FLAG-ERA|access-date=15 June 2017}}</ref>
