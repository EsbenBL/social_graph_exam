'''Stephan Schulmeister''' (* 26 August 1947) is an [[Austria]]n jurist and [[economist]], son of journalist [[Otto Schulmeister]].

He worked at the [[Austrian Institute of Economic Research]] from 1972 to 2012, mainly in the fields of „midterm prognosis, long-term economic development, financial markets and international trade". His numerous publications, which tend to be critical of [[Neoliberalism|neoliberal]] policies,<ref>[[die tageszeitung]]: ''[http://www.taz.de/1/archiv/print-archiv/printressorts/digi-artikel/?ressort=me&dig=2009%2F05%2F05%2Fa0063&cHash=fe7b18ed35 Marktreligiöse Wissenschaft].'' 5 May 2009</ref> and his support for a pan-European New Deal<ref>die tageszeitung: ''[http://www.taz.de/1/archiv/digitaz/artikel/?ressort=pb&dig=2010%2F07%2F10%2Fa0046&cHash=f435564d38 Wohl dem, der jetzt zahlt].'' 10 July 2010</ref> made him one of the most well-known Austrian economists. His fields of study are [[Industrial Economics]], Innovation and international competition, foreign economics and international economic trade, financial markets and business strategies.

Schulmeister has been a visiting scholar at various organizations outside Austria, such as [[New York University]] and the [[University of New Hampshire]].
