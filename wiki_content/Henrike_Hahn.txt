'''Henrike Hahn''' (born 4 September 1970) is a German politician who has been a [[Member of the European Parliament]] since July 2019.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.europarl.europa.eu/meps/en/197457/HENRIKE_HAHN/home|title=Home {{!}} Henrike HAHN {{!}} MEPs {{!}} European Parliament|website=www.europarl.europa.eu|language=en|access-date=2019-07-30}}</ref> She is a member of Alliance 90/The Greens (German: ''[[Bündnis 90/Die Grünen]]'' or ''Grüne'') at national level, and sits with the [[European Green Party|Group of the Greens/European Free Alliance]] in the European Parliament.

== Academic career ==
Hahn is a political scientist, having studied at [[University of Sorbonne Nouvelle Paris 3|Sorbonne-Nouvelle]] in [[Paris]], [[University of Michigan]] in [[Ann Arbor, Michigan|Ann Arbor]], [[Wayne State University]] in [[Detroit]], and [[Ludwig Maximilian University of Munich]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://henrike-hahn.de/ueber-mich|title=Henrike Hahn {{!}} Europa-Spitzenkandidatin - Grüne Bayern|last=Hahn|first=Henrike|website=Henrike Hahn|language=de-DE|access-date=2019-07-30}}</ref>

== Early career ==
For many years, Hahn worked as a consultant for technology-oriented companies, with a focus on strategy consulting, market research, and competitive analysis in Paris and Munich. From 2010 to 2015, she worked for a member of the [[Landtag of Bavaria|Bavarian State Parliament]]. In 2017, she worked as research associate at the [[Bundestag|German Bundestag]]. She also worked as a researcher on Europe and Transatlantic relations at the Centre for Applied Policy Research (CAP) at [[Ludwig Maximilian University of Munich]] (LMU); as visiting researcher at the American Institute for Contemporary German Studies (AICGS); and in the research team on [[European integration]] at the [[German Institute for International and Security Affairs]] (SWP).

== Political career==
Hahn has been an active member the Bavarian Greens since 2012, having also served as the spokeswoman for the state working group for economy and finance. She has worked as an assessor for the Bavarian Greens, and has been a member of the national executive committee of Alliance 90/The Greens since October 2017. She previously ran for election to the European Parliament in 2014.

Since the [[2019 European Parliament election|2019 European elections]], Hahn has been a [[Member of the European Parliament]]. She has since been serving on the [[Committee on Industry, Research and Energy]]. In addition to her committee assignments, she is part of the Parliament’s delegations for relations with [[China]] and the [[United States]].<ref>[http://www.europarl.europa.eu/meps/en/197457/HENRIKE_HAHN/home Henrike Hahn] [[European Parliament]].</ref>

==Other activities==
Hahn is a member of [[Amnesty International]], the [[German Alpine Club]], the [[Bund für Umwelt und Naturschutz Deutschland|German Federation for the Environment and Nature Conservation]] ([[Friends of the Earth Germany]]), and [[Greenpeace]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://henrike-hahn.de/ueber-mich|title=Henrike Hahn {{!}} Europa-Spitzenkandidatin - Grüne Bayern|last=Hahn|first=Henrike|website=Henrike Hahn|language=de-DE|access-date=2019-07-27}}</ref>

== Personal life ==
Hahn was born in Munich, and grew up in [[Oberpframmern]]. She currently lives in [[Neuhausen-Nymphenburg|Munich-Neuhausen]] with her two daughters.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.abendzeitung-muenchen.de/inhalt.muenchner-gruenen-kandidatin-im-az-interview-was-die-europawahl-mit-der-neuhauser-kita-zu-tun-hat.04591aac-675e-483a-8a7f-617e31c2c965.html|title=Europawahl 2019: Interview mit der bayerischen Grünen-Kandidatin Henrike Hahn|last=Germany|first=Abendzeitung|website=www.abendzeitung-muenchen.de|language=de|access-date=2019-07-30}}</ref>
