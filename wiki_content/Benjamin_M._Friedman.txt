'''Benjamin Morton Friedman''' ({{IPAc-en|ˈ|f|r|iː|d|m|ə|n}}; born 1944) is an [[United States of America|American]] [[Political economy|political economist]], who is the William Joseph Maier Professor of Political Economy at [[Harvard University]]. He is a member of the [[Council on Foreign Relations]], the [[Brookings Institute]]'s Panel on Economic Activity, and the editorial board of the ''[[Encyclopædia Britannica]]''. He is a recipient of the John R. Commons Award, given by the economics honor society [[Omicron Delta Epsilon]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.omicrondeltaepsilon.org/awards.html|title = Omicron Delta Epsilon - the International Economics Honor Society}}</ref>

==Biography==
Friedman received his [[Bachelor of Arts|A.B.]], [[Master of Arts|A.M.]], and [[Doctor of Philosophy|Ph.D.]] degrees, all in economics, from Harvard University.  He also received an M.Sc. in economics and politics from [[King's College, Cambridge]] as a [[Marshall Scholarship|Marshall Scholar]].  He has been on the Harvard faculty since 1972.  Currently Friedman is a member of the [[Committee on Capital Markets Regulation]].
