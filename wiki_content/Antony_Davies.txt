'''Antony Davies''' (born 4 April 1965) is an [[United States|American]] economist, speaker, and author. He is the [[Milton Friedman]] Distinguished Fellow at the Foundation for Economic Education.

==Early life and education==
Davies was born in [[Savannah, Georgia]] to Alan and Margaret Davies.  He was raised in [[Montoursville, Pennsylvania]] and graduated from Bishop Neumann High School in [[Williamsport, Pennsylvania]] in 1983.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://sjnra.org/index.php/pages/alumni-emails.html|title=Alumni Emails -- St. John Neumann Regional Academy|work=sjnra.org}}</ref> Starting at an early age, he acted with the Community Theatre League.<ref>{{Cite web | url=http://www.ctlshows.com/about/history/history/page.aspx?id=1247 | title=History &#124; Community Theatre League - Williamsport PA Plays, Musicals, Concerts, Comedy, Family Entertainment, Student Productions, Children's Theatre, Broadway, Theater}}</ref> He graduated cum laude with a [[Bachelor of Science|B.S.]] degree in Economics with minors in Mathematics and Philosophy from [[Saint Vincent College]] in 1987.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.stvincent.edu/newsDetailWF.aspx?id=2147489220|title=Daniel Finn, Antony Davies Final Speakers in Student Affairs Series April 7|work=stvincent.edu}}</ref>{{deadlink|date=September 2021}}

In 1994, he received his [[Ph.D.]] in Economics from the [[University at Albany]] in [[Albany, New York]], where he studied under Kajal Lahiri.<ref name="albany.edu">{{cite web|url=http://www.albany.edu/~klahiri/student.htm|title=List of Graduated Students and Their Dissertations|work=albany.edu}}</ref> His [[Ph.D.]] thesis addressed analysis of multi-dimensional panel data in econometrics, and it received the ''Distinguished Dissertation Award''.<ref name="albany.edu"/><ref>{{cite web|url=https://scholar.google.com/citations?view_op=view_citation&hl=en&user=rDKXPgIAAAAJ&citation_for_view=rDKXPgIAAAAJ:mVmsd5A6BfQC|title=Google Scholar Citations|work=google.com}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.cambridge.org/US/academic/subjects/economics/econometrics-statistics-and-mathematical-economics/analysis-panels-and-limited-dependent-variable-models|title=Analysis of Panels and Limited Dependent Variable Models|date=11 February 2010|work=Cambridge University Press}}</ref>

==Professional history==
While a college student, he founded [[Paragon Software]] with [[Mark E. Seremet]]. Davies's first faculty appointment was at [[West Virginia Wesleyan College]], after which, he moved on to [[West Virginia University]] where he taught [[econometrics]]. During the [[dot-com bubble|dot-com era]], he left academia to serve as [[Chief Financial Officer]] and [[Chief Analytics Officer]] at Parabon Computation, where he was co-inventor on a patent for generating supercomputing power from idle Internet-connected computers.<ref>[http://patft.uspto.gov/netacgi/nph-Parser?Sect1=PTO1&Sect2=HITOFF&d=PALL&p=1&u=%2Fnetahtml%2FPTO%2Fsrchnum.htm&r=1&f=G&l=50&s1=6,463,457.PN.&OS=PN/6,463,457&RS=PN/6,463,457 U.S. Patent No. 6,463,457], System and Method for the Establishment and Utilization of Networked Idle Computational Power.</ref> In 2001, he joined the faculty at [[Duquesne University]] where he helped design the economics major, and he remains employed today.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.duq.edu/academics/schools/business/faculty-and-research/faculty-directory/davies-phd-antony |title=Antony Davies, Ph.D. - Palumbo Donahue School of Business - Duquesne University |work=duq.edu |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150711055413/http://www.duq.edu/academics/schools/business/faculty-and-research/faculty-directory/davies-phd-antony |archive-date=2015-07-11 }}</ref>

In 2006, he co-founded an Internet discovery firm, Repliqa, that was purchased by [[indiePub|indiePub Entertainment]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.prweb.com/releases/2006/09/prweb444296.htm|title=Repliqa.com Secures Financing - Presents at ETRE 2006|date=29 September 2006|work=PRWeb}}</ref> In 2007, he was named a [[Mercatus Center|Mercatus]] Affiliated Senior Scholar and, in 2015, a Strata Research Fellow.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.strata.org/event/speaker-series-antony-davies/ |title=Strata Upcoming Events - – Speaker Series: Antony Davies |work=strata.org |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150415161301/http://www.strata.org/event/speaker-series-antony-davies/ |archive-date=2015-04-15 }}</ref> He conducts research on the economic effects of government policy. As a Mercatus Scholar, Davies has given lectures on economic policy topics for staff at the U.S. House of Representatives.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://mercatus.org/video/tax-reform-simplification-good-policy|title=Tax Reform: Simplification is a Good Policy|work=mercatus.org|date=2013-10-07}}</ref>

Starting in 2012, he produced a series of videos on economics and statistics for the [[Institute for Humane Studies]], the [[Foundation for Economic Education]], and Certell.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.learnliberty.org/speakers/antony-davies/|title=Learn Liberty - Antony Davies|work=Learn Liberty}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WkNPjC0gVSo|title=Antony Davies - Certell}}</ref> In 2015, he worked as Associate Producer for the [[Moving Picture Institute]] on their video series, ''FI$H: How An Economy Grows.''<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.thempi.org/films/howaneconomygrows.html|title=Fi$h: How An Economy Grows|work=thempi.org|access-date=2015-06-22|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150711031518/http://www.thempi.org/films/howaneconomygrows.html|archive-date=2015-07-11|url-status=dead}}</ref> Davies is a long-time faculty member at the [[Institute for Humane Studies]] and the [[Foundation for Economic Education]].<ref>{{cite web|url=https://www.theihs.org/guest-lecturers/antony-davies |title=Antony Davies |work=theihs.org |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150710161903/https://www.theihs.org/guest-lecturers/antony-davies |archive-date=2015-07-10 }}</ref>

Davies has published [[op-ed]]s in newspapers and magazines throughout the country including the ''Wall Street Journal'', ''Los Angeles Times'', ''New York Daily News'', ''Forbes'', ''Investors Business Daily'', and ''CNN.com''.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://antolin-davies.com/press/|title=Antony Davies, Ph.D. - Press|work=antolin-davies.com}}</ref> In addition, Davies co-authors a regular monthly column in the ''Pittsburgh Tribune-Review'', and has co-authored a college-level economics text published by Cognella.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://triblive.com/search/?search=%22antony%20davies%22&btnG=Search|title=Search - Tribune-Review|work=triblive.com}}</ref> He is also a regular commentator on [[Blaze Media|The Blaze TV]].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://mercatus.org/video/antony-davies-discusses-bank-bailouts-blaze|title=Antony Davies Discusses Bank Bailouts on The Blaze|work=mercatus.org|date=2013-11-21}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://mercatus.org/video/antony-davies-discusses-big-business-working-big-government-blaze|title=Antony Davies Discusses Big Business Working with Big Government on The Blaze|work=mercatus.org|date=2013-01-29}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|url=http://mercatus.org/video/antony-davies-discusses-idea-1-trillion-coin-blaze|title=Antony Davies Discusses the Idea of the $1 Trillion Coin on The Blaze|work=mercatus.org|date=2013-01-14}}</ref>
