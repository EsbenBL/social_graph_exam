'''Luciano Gallino''' (15 May 1927 – 8 November 2015) was an Italian [[sociologist]].

Born in [[Turin]], between 1956 and 1971 he worked in the  Research Department of Social Relations of [[Olivetti]].<ref name=bio>{{cite news|title=Addio a Luciano Gallino, sociologo, editorialista, uno dei maggiori esperti del mercato del lavoro |url= http://torino.repubblica.it/cronaca/2015/11/08/news/addio_a_luciano_gallino_sociologo_editorialista_uno_dei_maggiori_esperti_del_mercato_del_lavoro-126902802/|accessdate=2 December 2015|work=[[La Repubblica]]|date=8 November 2015}}</ref><ref name="la sta">{{cite news|last1=Fabrizio Assandri|title=Martedì l’addio al sociologo Luciano Gallino|url=http://www.lastampa.it/2015/11/08/cronaca/addio-al-sociologo-luciano-gallino-m92neYcYGkQZ9A3GS3rmBO/pagina.html|accessdate=2 December 2015|work=[[La Stampa]]|date=8 November 2015}}</ref> He was instrumental in introducing Sociology into Italian universities, at a time when both spiritualist and Communist scholars regarded the discipline as largely irrelevant.<ref name="Catalogo">{{cite encyclopedia |last1=Dell’Arti|first1=Giorgio|last2=Parrini|first2=Massimo|title=Gallino, Luciano|encyclopedia=Catalogo dei viventi|year=2008|publisher=Marsilio|location=Venezia|pages=810–811|ISBN= 978-8831795999}}</ref>

From 1971 to 2002 he taught sociology at the [[University of Turin]].<ref name=bio />  He was also President of the Italian Council of Social Sciences from 1979 to 1988, and President of the Italian Association of Sociology from 1987 to 1992.<ref name=bio />  
 
Gallino was author of several books and a columnist for various publications, and he directed the magazine ''Quaderni di Sociologia''.<ref name=bio /><ref name="la sta"/>  His studies mainly focused on the sociology of economic processes in the labor market.<ref name=bio /> He cooperated with [[Romano Prodi]] when the latter served as Prime Minister of Italy.<ref name="Catalogo"/>
