'''Beth A. Simmons''' (born 1958) is an American academic and notable international relations scholar. She is the Andrea Mitchell University Professor in Law, Political Science and Business Ethics at the [[University of Pennsylvania Law School]].<ref>[https://www.law.upenn.edu/cf/faculty/simmons3/ Penn Law Faculty: Beth Simmons<!-- Bot generated title -->]</ref> She is a former Director of the [[Weatherhead Center for International Affairs]] at [[Harvard University]] and Clarence Dillon Professor of International Affairs at the Department of Government. Her research interests include international relations, political economy, international law, and international human rights law compliance.

== Early life ==
Simmons was born in 1958 in the [[San Francisco Bay Area]] in California and attended [[Monta Vista High School]] in [[Cupertino, California]] where she excelled in speech, debate, and music. She earned a BA in political science and philosophy ''[[summa cum laude]]'' from the [[University of Redlands]], an MA in international relations from the [[University of Chicago]], and an MA and PhD in government from Harvard, where she was a student of international relations theorist [[Robert Keohane]].

== Career ==
Simmons taught as an assistant professor at [[Duke University]] (1991–1996) and as an associate professor at the [[University of California, Berkeley]] (1996–2002) before joining the faculty of [[Harvard University]] in 2002, where she was Clarence Dillon Professor of International Affairs and Director of the [[Weatherhead Center for International Affairs]] at [[Harvard Law School]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Beth Simmons|url=http://hls.harvard.edu/faculty/directory/11015/Simmons|website=Harvard Law School|accessdate=14 July 2017}}</ref>

In 2016, she became Andrea Mitchell University Professor in Law and Political Science at the [[University of Pennsylvania Law School]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Beth Simmons|url=https://www.law.upenn.edu/cf/faculty/simmons3/|website=Penn Law|publisher=University of Pennsylvania|accessdate=14 July 2017}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Simmons elected to American Philosophical Society|url=https://www.law.upenn.edu/live/news/7041-simmons-elected-to-american-philosophical-society#.WWuqNtMrLOQ|website=University of Pennsylvania|accessdate=16 July 2017|date=11 May 2017}}</ref>

Simmons served as President of the International Studies Association from 2011-2012.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.isanet.org/ISA/Governance/President/PastPresidents.aspx|title=Presidents of ISA}}</ref>  She was succeeded as President by [[Etel Solingen]] of the University of California, Irvine.
