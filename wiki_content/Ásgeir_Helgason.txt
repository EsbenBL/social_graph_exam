'''Ásgeir R. Helgason''' (born 1957) is an [[Iceland]]ic scientist working at [[Karolinska Institutet]] in Sweden. Since 2002 he has been an associate professor in psychology at the Departments of Oncology-Pathology and Public Health at the [[Karolinska Institutet]] and [[Reykjavik University]], Iceland.

He is best known for his population based research on [[sexual function]]<ref>31st NACS conference, September 4–7, 2008 13:10-14.00 Ásgeir Helgason, Iceland/Sweden: Opening lecture. "Waning orgasm pleasure after radical treatment for localized prostate cancer and impacts on .</ref> and [[emotional isolation]]<ref>Emotional isolation in elderly men: [http://www.pauldickman.com/publications/emotional_isolation.pdf].</ref> in elderly men and [[prostate cancer]] patients, [[patient trade-off]]<ref>PhD thesis (1997): Prostate Cancer Treatment and Quality of Life – a Three Level Epidemiological Approach.</ref> and his work on [[smoking cessation]] and [[quitline]]s.<ref>Smoking cessation - quitlines:[https://archive.today/20130113030309/http://eurpub.oxfordjournals.org/cgi/content/abstract/14/3/306].</ref> Helgason was a prime mover in the establishment of the Swedish and Icelandic national quitlines for smoking cessation (1998) and responsible for their development. He was also engaged in the development of a similar telephone based [[proactive]] treatment for people who seek help for controlling their [[alcohol (drug)|alcohol]] consumption (alcohol [[quitline]]).<ref>{{cite journal|title=The national alcohol helpline in Sweden: an evaluation of its first year|first1=Kozma|last1=Ahacic|first2=Lena|last2=Nederfeldt|first3=Ásgeir R.|last3=Helgason|date=1 January 2014|journal=Substance Abuse Treatment, Prevention, and Policy|volume=9|pages=28|doi=10.1186/1747-597X-9-28|pmid=25015403|pmc=4100057}}</ref>

Other work includes research on [[motivational interviewing]] and [[palliative care]]<ref>{{cite journal|title=Death talk: gender differences in talking about one's own impending death|first1=Bragi|last1=Skúlason|first2=Arna|last2=Hauksdóttir|first3=Kozma|last3=Ahcic|first4=Ásgeir R.|last4=Helgason|date=11 March 2014|journal=BMC Palliative Care|volume=13|issue = 1|pages=8|doi=10.1186/1472-684X-13-8|pmid=24618410|pmc=3975272}}</ref>
Followed by an ethical analysis of facilitating death talk in end-of-life care<ref>{{cite journal|title=Using motivational interviewing to facilitate death talk in end-of-life care: an ethical analysis|first1=Isra|last1=Black|first2=Ásgeir R.|last2=Helgason|date=1 March 2018|journal=BMC Palliative Care|volume=21|issue = 1|pages=17(1):51|doi=10.1186/s12904-018-0305-5|pmid=29562885|pmc=5863449}}</ref>

Ásgeir has two sons, Hugi and Muni, after [[Odin]]'s ravens [[Hugin and Munin|Huginn and Muninn]]. His  brother-in-law is writer and humorist [[Tim Moore (writer)|Tim Moore]], his father is scientist [[Helgi Valdimarsson]] and he is a brother of scientist [[Agnar Helgason]].
