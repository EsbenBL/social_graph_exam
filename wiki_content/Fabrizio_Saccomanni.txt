'''Fabrizio Saccomanni''' (22 November 1942 – 8 August 2019) was an Italian economist, civil servant and the former Director General of the [[Bank of Italy]]. He served as [[Ministry of Economy and Finance (Italy)|minister of economy and finance]] between April 2013 and February 2014. From 13 April 2018 until his death, Saccomanni was the [[chairman]] of [[UniCredit]].

==Early life and education==
Saccomanni was born in [[Rome]] on 22 November 1942.<ref name=bofitaly>{{cite web |title=Fabrizio Saccomanni |url=http://www.bancaditalia.it/bancaditalia/direttorio/diregen;internal&action=_setlanguage.action?LANGUAGE=en |publisher=Bank of Italy|access-date=27 April 2013|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130106051347/http://www.bancaditalia.it/bancaditalia/direttorio/diregen%3Binternal%26action%3D_setlanguage.action?LANGUAGE=en|archive-date=6 January 2013}}</ref><ref name=cosmo30apr>{{cite journal|title=The new Italian government of Prime Minister Enrico Letta|journal=Cosmopolis|date=30 April 2013|url=http://www.cosmopolis.ch/english/politics/e0015300/italian_government_letta_e01530000.htm|archive-url=https://archive.today/20130620023000/http://www.cosmopolis.ch/english/politics/e0015300/italian_government_letta_e01530000.htm|url-status=dead|archive-date=20 June 2013|accessdate=6 June 2013}}</ref> He held a master's degree in economics and business, which he received from [[Bocconi University|the Bocconi University]] in 1966.<ref name=bocconialumni/> He also took postgraduate courses in monetary and international economics at [[Princeton University]].<ref name=bofitaly/>

==Career==
Saccomanni worked at [[Banca d'Italia|the Bank of Italy]] most of his career.<ref name=reufactbox>{{cite news|title=Factbox: Key ministers in Enrico Letta's new Italian government|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/us-italy-vote-ministers-idUSBRE93Q09D20130427|accessdate=27 April 2013|newspaper=Reuters|date=27 April 2013}}</ref> His tenure at the bank was only interrupted when he worked at [[International Monetary Fund|the International Monetary Fund]] (1970–1975) and at the [[London]]-based [[European Bank for Reconstruction and Development]] where he served as vice president from 2003 to 2006.<ref name=bio21sep>{{cite news|last=Biondi|first=Paolo|title=Saccomanni seen replacing Draghi as Bank of Italy chief|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/italy-cenbank-idUSL5E7KL62N20110921|accessdate=27 April 2013|agency=Reuters|date=21 September 2011}}</ref> He also served as the chairman of the foreign exchange policy committee of [[European Monetary Institute|the European Monetary Institute]] from 1991 to 1997 in addition to his post at the Bank of Italy.<ref name=peterson2008/>

He was the director general of the Bank of Italy where he started his career in June 1967.<ref name=bocconialumni/><ref name=gavin27apr>{{cite news|title=Italy's Letta names new government|url=https://www.reuters.com/article/italy-vote-idUSL6N0DE0A020130427|accessdate=27 April 2013|newspaper=Reuters|author=James Mackenzie|author2=Gavin Jones}}</ref> He was appointed director general on 2 October 2006 and reappointed in 2012.<ref name=bofitaly/> He was also a board member of [[Bank for International Settlements|the Bank for International Settlements]] and an alternate to the governor of the Bank of Italy in the Governing Council of [[European Central Bank|the European Central Bank]].<ref name=bocconialumni/>

Saccomanni was a council member of the [[European Council on Foreign Relations]], an international think-tank which conducts research on European foreign and security policies.<ref name="ecfr">{{cite web|title=ECFR Council Members |url=https://www.ecfr.eu/council/members|work=The European Council on Foreign Relations|accessdate=9 August 2019}}</ref>

===Minister of Economy and Finances===
On 27 April 2013, prime minister-designate [[Enrico Letta]] announced that Saccomanni would serve as minister of economy and finances in [[Letta Cabinet|his cabinet]].<ref name=bbc27apr>{{cite news|title=Italy PM-designate Enrico Letta agrees new government|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-europe-22323850|accessdate=27 April 2013|work=BBC|date=27 April 2013}}</ref> His term began on 28 April and he replaced [[Vittorio Grilli]] in the post.<ref name=dinmore28apr>{{cite news|last=Dinmore|first=Guy|title=Mayhem greets Italy’s grand coalition|url=http://www.ft.com/intl/cms/s/0/2005ef0e-b020-11e2-8d07-00144feabdc0.html#axzz2Rq3zbiMI|accessdate=29 April 2013|newspaper=Financial Times|date=28 April 2013|location=Rome}}</ref> Saccomanni was one of the technocrats in [[Letta Cabinet|the Letta cabinet]].<ref name=brenda9may>{{cite journal|last=Dionisi|first=Brenda|title=It's a governissimo!|journal=The Florentine|date=9 May was replaced by [[Pier Carlo Padoan]] as minister of economy and finances on 22 February 2014 when the [[Renzi Cabinet|government]] led by [[Matteo Renzi]] was formed.<ref>{{cite web|title=Minister|url=http://www.mef.gov.it/en/ministero/ministro.html |archive-url=https://archive.today/20140715124842/http://www.mef.gov.it/en/ministero/ministro.html|url-status=dead ==Work and later life==
Saccomanni published a book in 2008 about experiencing financial crisis, ''Managing international financial stability: National tamers versus global tigers''.<ref name=peterson2008>{{cite web|title=Bank of Italy's Saccomanni on Financial Stability|url=http://www.piie.com/events/print.cfm?EventID=95|publisher=Peterson Institute for International Economics|accessdate=27 April 2013|date=11 December 2008}}</ref>

In 2011, he was named by his alma mater, Bocconi University, as the alumnus of the year for his "professionalism, entrepreneurial spirit, integrity, responsibility and open-mindedness."<ref name=bocconialumni>{{cite web|title=Alumnus of the Year: Fabrizio Saccomanni|url=http://www.alumnibocconi.it/alumnus-of-the-year-fabrizio-saccomanni|work=Bocconi Alumni Association|accessdate=27 April 2013}}</ref>

Saccomanni died in [[San Teodoro, Sardinia]], on 8 August 2019 at age 76.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.repubblica.it/economia/2019/08/08/news/fabrizio_saccomanni_economista_ed_ex_ministro_delle_finanze_muore_a_76_anni-233174679/|title=Fabrizio Saccomanni, economista ed ex ministro delle Finanze, muore a 76 anni|work=La Repubblica|date=8 August 2019|access-date=8 August 2019}}</ref>

Upon his death, UniCredit Chief Executive Jean Pierre Mustier stated, "I have lost a friend of great intelligence and humanity, highly competent with a fine sense of culture and wit".<ref name=uk.reuters.com>{{Cite web|url=https://uk.reuters.com/article/uk-unicredit-chairman-idUKKCN1UY1W8|title=Chairman of Italy's UniCredit Fabrizio Saccomanni dies suddenly at 76|date=8 August 2019|work=Reuters}}</ref> As a result of his death, deputy chairman Cesare Bisoni assumed  Saccomanni’s role at UniCredit.<ref name="uk.reuters.com"/>
