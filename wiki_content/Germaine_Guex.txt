{{short description|Swiss psychologist and psychoanalyst (1904-1984)}}

{{Expand French|Germaine Guex|date=January 2012}}
'''Germaine Guex''' (April 17, 1904 in [[Arcachon]], [[France]]–November 20, 1984 in [[Lausanne]], [[Switzerland]])<ref>{{Cite book |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=LYy8CQAAQBAJ |title=The Abandonment Neurosis |last=Guex |first=Germaine |date=2015-06-12 |publisher=Karnac Books |isbn=9781781814581 |pages=ix |language=en}}</ref> was a [[Switzerland|Swiss]] psychologist.<ref>Jean-Daniel Zbinden, « L'organisateur André Repond » in Christian Müller (dir.) Portraits de psychiatres (suisses) romands, Payot-Lausanne, 1995, ({{ISBN|2-601-03139-5}}).</ref><ref>Catherine Fussinger, « Du rôle des femmes et des hommes dans le développement de la pédopsychiatrie en Suisse romande (1930 - 1950) », in Jacqueline Carroy (dir.) Les femmes dans les sciences de l'homme (XIXe-XXe siècles) : inspiratrices, collaboratrices ou créatrices ?, Éditions Seli Arslan, 2005 ({{ISBN|2842761081}}).</ref><ref>Corinne Dallera, Nadia Lamamra, Du salon à l'usine : vingt portraits de femmes. Un autre regard sur l'histoire du canton de Vaud, 2003, p. 213-228, coédition : CLAFV & ADF & Ouverture (2003) ({{ISBN|2884130950}})</ref> She was particularly known for her work on [[Abandonment (emotional)|abandonment syndrome]] in [[psychoanalysis]].

Born in [[Arcachon]], [[France]], Guex achieved the French ''[[baccalauréat]]'' and studied at the [[Rousseau Institute]] in [[Geneva]]. In the 1930s, Guex established a pioneering medical-pedagogical service in [[Monthey]], dedicated to the psychoanalytical treatment of children. Her monograph ''La névrose d'abandon'' (1950; English: ''Abandonment neurosis''), later re-published in 1973 under the title ''Le Syndrome d'abandon'', made her reputation international.<ref>{{HDS|48477}}</ref>  In it, she describes "abandonment neurosis" as a type of [[neurosis]] distinct from other previously defined categories.<ref>{{Cite book |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=yFf4Zlf0nFoC |title=Frantz Fanon's 'Black Skin, White Masks' |last=Silverman |first=Max |date=2005 |publisher=Manchester University Press |isbn=9780719064487 |pages=24 |language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite book |url=https://books.google.com/books?id=PsvZpv0ZRw0C |title=The Language of Psychoanalysis |last=Laplanche |first=Jean |last2=Pontalis |first2=Jean-Bertrand |date=1988-12-31 |publisher=Karnac Books |isbn=9781781810262 |pages=270 |language=en}}</ref>
