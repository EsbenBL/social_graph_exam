'''David A. Bositis''' is an American expert on voting rights and redistricting.<ref name="bio">{{Cite web|url=http://www.jointcenter.org/index.php/news_room/joint_center_scholars/david_a_bositis|title=David A. Bositis|access-date=November 7, 2008|publisher=Joint Center for Political and Economic Studies|year=2008|author1=Joint Center for Political|author2=Economic Studies|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20081106101229/http://www.jointcenter.org/index.php/news_room/joint_center_scholars/david_a_bositis|archive-date=November 6, 2008|url-status=dead}}</ref><ref name="usatoday">{{cite news|url=https://www.usatoday.com/news/politics/election2008/2008-11-04-voterturnout_N.htm|title=Passionate race drives a massive turnout|access-date=November 7, 2008|publisher=[[USA Today]]|author=Jill Lawrence | date=November 5, 2008}}</ref> He is a former a senior research associate at the [[Joint Center for Political and Economic Studies]] in [[Washington, D.C.]]<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://jointcenter.org/blog/voter-suppression-wrong|title=Voter Suppression is Wrong {{!}} Joint Center for Political and Economic Studies|website=jointcenter.org|access-date=2017-07-26|quote="David Bositis, who is not a Joint Center staffer, and has not worked at the Joint Center for over a year."}}</ref>

Bositis is frequently consulted by major newspapers, including ''[[The New York Times]]'' and ''[[The Washington Post]]'' for his input on issues of race in politics.<ref name="nyt">{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2008/11/02/us/politics/02first.html?em|title=Obama-Inspired Black Voters Warm to Politics |access-date=November 7, 2008|work=[[The New York Times]]|author=Susan Saulny | date=November 2, 2008}}</ref><ref name="washpost">{{cite news|url=https://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2008/10/20/AR2008102002601.html|title=Black Turnout Could Decide House Races|access-date=November 7, 2008|work=[[The Washington Post]]|author=Lyndsey Layton | date=October 21, 2008}}</ref><ref name="usatoday2">{{cite news|url=https://www.usatoday.com/news/politics/election2008/2008-03-09-obamademographics_N.htm?loc=interstitialskip|title=Obama sees benefits, limits to black support|access-date=November 7, 2008|publisher=USA Today|author=Charles Babington for the [[Associated Press]] | date=March 9, 2008}}</ref>

Bositis received his [[bachelor's degree]] from [[Northwestern University]], and a [[master's degree]] and [[doctorate]] from [[Southern Illinois University]].<ref name="bio"/> Bositis joined the Joint Center in 1990, and designs and manages national surveys, mainly geared towards minorities.<ref name="bio"/>

Bositis is the author of at least six books and numerous articles which focus on minority politics, especially black representation and voting patterns and the effects of redistricting on minority representation.<ref name="usatoday"/>
