'''Gérard Roland''' (born 1954) is a [[Belgian]] [[economist]], and a professor of [[economics]] and [[political science]] at the [[University of California, Berkeley]]. He graduated from the [[Université Libre de Bruxelles|Free University of Brussels]] in 1988. His early work was on the [[political economy]] of [[communism]]. After 1990, he became one of the world's most renowned and influential scholars in [[transition economics]]. He is a member of [http://www.cerge-ei.cz/people/executive-and-supervisory-committee the Executive and Supervisory Committee (ESC)] of [[CERGE-EI]].

==Selected works==
* "The Design of Reform Packages under Uncertainty", (with [[Mathias Dewatripont|M. Dewatripont]]), ''[[American Economic Review]]'', 1995, vol 83, pp.&nbsp;107–1223
* ''Transition and Economics: Politics, Markets and Firms''. MIT Press 2000
