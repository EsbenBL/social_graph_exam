'''Miriam M. Johnson''' (January 12, 1928 – November 21, 2007) was an American [[Sociology|sociologist]] and professor emerita of the [[University of Oregon]]'s Sociology Department.

==Life==
Miriam Johnson was born Miriam Massey in Atlanta, Georgia, on January 12, 1928, to Leola and Herbert Massey. While attending the [[University of North Carolina, Chapel Hill]], she met [[Benton Johnson]]. They were married in 1951. In 1955 they had a son (Shannon), followed by a daughter (Rebekah) in 1957. Johnson died on November 21, 2007, in [[Eugene, Oregon]], at the age of 79 of [[lung cancer]] after a short illness.<ref name=":0">{{cite journal|title=Miriam Johnson 1928–2007|journal=American Sociological Association|date=2007-02-01|first=Stockard|last=Jean|author2=Benton Johnson|volume=36|issue=2|url=http://www.asanet.org/footnotes/feb08_R/obit.html|access-date=2010-02-22 }}</ref>

==Career==
Johnson was educated at [[Georgia College & State University|Georgia State College for Women at Milledgeville]], and the [[University of North Carolina, Chapel Hill]], where she studied sociology and met her husband [[Benton Johnson]]. In 1948 she pursued graduate studies at [[Radcliffe College]] with advisor [[Talcott Parsons]] in [[Harvard University]]'s Department of Social Relations, earning a PhD. in 1955.<ref name=":0" /><ref>{{Cite thesis|title=Instrumental and expressive components in the personalities of women.|url=https://www.worldcat.org/title/instrumental-and-expressive-components-in-the-personalities-of-women/oclc/76989288&referer=brief_results|date=1955|language=English|first=Miriam Massey|last=Johnson|degree=Ph.D.|publisher=Radcliffe College|doi=|oclc = 76989288}}</ref>

She taught at the Women's College of the University of North Carolina (now the [[University of North Carolina at Greensboro]]) until 1953. Johnson and her husband moved to Oregon in 1957, when he joined the [[University of Oregon]] faculty. She returned to sociological work in 1972, with a particular emphasis on gender and family roles. In 1973, she helped organize the University of Oregon's [[Center for the Study of Women in Society]], and later served as its director.<ref>{{Cite web|date=Winter 2008|title=In memoriam—Miriam M. Johnson|url=http://csws-archive.uoregon.edu/docs/publications/NewsletterArchive/2008_winter.pdf|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=January 31, 2021|website=Center for the Study of Women in Society|publisher=University of Oregon|page=2}}</ref> Her last book, ''Strong Mothers, Weak Wives'', was published in 1988.<ref>Johnson, Miriam M. 1988. ''Strong Mothers, Weak Wives: The Search for Gender Equality''. Berkeley, California: University of California Press.[http://ark.cdlib.org/ark:/13030/ft0k40038c/]</ref>
