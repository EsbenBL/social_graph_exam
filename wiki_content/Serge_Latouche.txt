'''Serge Latouche''' ({{IPAc-en|l|ə|ˈ|t|uː|ʃ}}; {{IPA-fr|latuʃ|lang}}; born January 12, 1940) is a [[French people|French]] [[emeritus]] [[professor]] of [[economics]] at the [[University of Paris-Sud]]. He holds a degree in [[political sciences]], in [[philosophy]] and in economy.

== Work ==
Latouche is a specialist in North-South economic and cultural relations, and in the epistemology of the social sciences. He has developed a critical theory towards economic orthodoxy. He denounces [[economism]], [[utilitarianism]] in social sciences, [[Consumerism|consumer society]] and the notion of [[sustainable development]]. He particularly criticizes the notions of [[economic efficiency]] and [[economic rationalism]]. He is one of the thinkers and most renowned partisans of the [[Degrowth|degrowth theory]].<ref>[http://www.solutionslocales-lefilm.com/en/characters/serge-latouche] {{webarchive |url=https://web.archive.org/web/20120426085712/http://www.solutionslocales-lefilm.com/en/characters/serge-latouche |date=April 26, 2012 }}</ref> Latouche has also published in the ''[[Mouvement Anti-Utilitariste dans les Sciences Sociales|Revue du Mauss]]'', a French anti-utilitarian journal.
