'''Nina Therese Kasniunas''' (born February 19, 1972) is an American political scientist and writer. She is the Arsht Professor in Ethics and Leadership in the Center for People, Politics, & Markets at [[Goucher College]].

== Early life and education ==
Kasniunas was born in 1972.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.newspapers.com/clip/19958547/the_baltimore_sun/|title=Town hall draws overflow crowd|last=West|first=Paul|date=2009-08-13|work=The Baltimore Sun|access-date=2018-05-10|language=en|via=Newspapers.com}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=http://id.loc.gov/authorities/names/n2009065676.html|title=LC Linked Data Service: Authorities and Vocabularies (Library of Congress)|website=id.loc.gov|others=The Library of Congress|access-date=2018-05-10}}</ref> She earned a bachelor's degree from [[Indiana University Bloomington]] in 1995. She completed a master's degree in 2002 at [[Loyola University Chicago]] where she earned a doctorate in political science in 2009.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|url=http://www.goucher.edu/learn/academic-centers/people-politics-and-markets/faculty/nina-kasniunas|title=Nina Kasniunas|website=Goucher College|language=en|access-date=2018-03-02}}</ref> She completed her dissertation titled ''Impact of Interest Group Testimony on Lawmaking in Congress'' under doctoral advisor [[Raymond Tatlovich]].<ref>{{Cite thesis|last=Therese|first=Kasniunas, Nina|title=Impact of Interest Group Testimony on Lawmaking in Congress|date=2009|publisher=Loyola University Chicago|url=https://ecommons.luc.edu/luc_diss/220/|language=en}}</ref>

== Career ==
In 2011, Kasniunas began teaching as an assistant professor at [[Goucher College]] in the department of political science and international relations.<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|url=http://wingatewire.com/2017/05/02/profile-of-nina-kasniunas-goucher-college/|title=Profile of Nina Kasniunas: Goucher College|last=Harvey|first=Christina|date=2017-05-02|website=Wingate Wire|access-date=2018-03-02}}</ref> Although not originally from Maryland, she is known for connecting students with the [[Baltimore|Baltimore City]] community through fieldwork and research.<ref name=":0" /> Kasniunas is the Arsht Professor in Ethics and Leadership in the Center For People, Politics, & Markets at Goucher College.<ref name=":1" />

She has served on the admissions committee and as an adviser in the development in the Goucher College Video App, a new digital format for students to apply for acceptance at the institution.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bustle.com/articles/38526-video-applications-for-college-are-now-a-thing-at-goucher-college-so-elle-woods-had-the|title=Your Selfie Video Might Get You Into College|last=Nuseibeh|first=Nuzha|work=Bustle|access-date=2018-03-02|language=en}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.cbsnews.com/news/video-replaces-rigorous-college-admissions-process/|title=Colleges get creative in bid to ease stressful application process|date=2016-04-09|work=CBS News|access-date=2018-03-02|language=en}}</ref>

== Awards ==
In 2017, Kasniunas received the All IN Campus Democracy Challenge Champion Award for her efforts to increase student voting rates.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://www.goucher.edu/news/all-in-challenge|title=Nina Kasniunas Honored|date=2017-10-19|website=Goucher College|language=en|access-date=2018-03-02}}</ref>
