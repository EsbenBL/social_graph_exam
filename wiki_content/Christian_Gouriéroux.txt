'''Christian Gouriéroux''' (born 1949) is an [[econometrics|econometrician]] who holds a [[Doctor of Philosophy]] in mathematics from the [[University of Rouen]]. He has the Professor exceptional level title from France.  Gouriéroux spends six months from every year teaching at the [[University of Toronto]], and the other half of his year teaching at the [[Center for Research in Economics and Statistics]] (CREST) in [[France]], at the [[University of Paris]] and the "Paris Graduate School of Economics, Statistics and Finance" ([[École Nationale de la Statistique et de l'Administration Économique|ENSAE]] Paris Tech).

Gouriéroux has published in journals worldwide, and was a recipient of the [[Koopmans Prize]] (with two fellow partners) for their project, "General Approach to Serial Correlation" in 1985–1987.  He was also awarded the Silver Medal of the [[Conseil National de Recherche Scientifique]] by the French Ministry of Research. He is a fellow of the Econometric Society.

==Biography==
Gouriéroux completed his undergraduate studies in economics and statistics at ENSAE.<ref name="et_interview_cambridge_u_press">{{Cite journal|url=https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/econometric-theory/article/et-interview-christian-gourieroux-and-alain-monfort/6F1D0F28C2B3D0710E9F0678EC66CCC7|title=The ET interview: Christian Gouriéroux and Alain Monfort|year=2012|doi=10.1017/S0266466611000818|last1=Ghysels|first1=Eric|last2=Renault|first2=Eric|journal=Econometric Theory|volume=28|issue=4|pages=889–914|s2cid=120107194}}</ref> He has received Doctorat honoris causa from Université de Mons-Hainaut, Université de Neuchâtel, as well as HEC Montréal.
