'''Jules Sedney''' (28 September 1922{{spnd}}18 June 2020) was a [[Suriname]]se politician, and [[Prime Minister of Suriname]] from 20 November 1969 to 24 December 1973. In 1980, he became governor of the [[Central Bank of Suriname]],<ref name="bank"/> but had to flee the country in 1983<ref name="exile"/> after a dispute with [[Dési Bouterse]].<ref name="dwt"/> Sedney returned to Suriname in 1989.<ref name="star"/>

==Early years and education==
Sedney was born in [[Paramaribo]]. In 1939, Sedney was certified as a classroom assistant, and began working.<ref name="aangeboden">{{cite news|url=https://www.gfcnieuws.com/aangeboden-doctor-jules-sedney-jarig-95-jaar-oud/|title=Aangeboden Doctor Jules Sedney jarig: 95 Jaar oud|website=GFC Nieuws|access-date=19 June 2020|language=nl}}</ref> He passed the customs officer certification exam in 1942 and became a customs officer in Paramaribo.<ref name="aangeboden"/>  In 1948, Sedney left for Amsterdam to study economics at the [[University of Amsterdam]]. During his studies, he joined the organisation ''Wie Eegie Sanie'' (Our Own Things), founded by [[Eddy Bruma]] to promote [[Sranan Tongo]] and Surinamese culture.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://dbnl.nl/tekst/_tsj002195201_01/_tsj002195201_01_0097.php|title=It stribjen fan 'Wie Eegie Sanie'.|website=De Tsjerne via Digital Library for Dutch Literature|author=Jules Sedney|language=fy|date=1952|access-date=19 June 2020}}</ref>  He obtained his doctorate in economic sciences in 1955 at the [[University of Amsterdam]].<ref name="star">{{cite news|url=https://www.starnieuws.com/index.php/welcome/index/nieuwsitem/59214|title=Oud-premier Jules Sedney ingeslapen |website=Star Nieuws|access-date=19 June 2020|language=nl}}</ref>

==Political career==
In 1958, Sedney became Minister of Finance for the [[National Party of Suriname|NPS]].<ref name="dwt"/> He later joined the breakaway [[Progressive National Party (Suriname)|Progressive National Party]] (PNP) founded by [[:nl:Just Rens|Just Rens]].<ref name="dwt"/> In the [[1969 Surinamese general election]], Sedney's party bloc took third place.  They then formed a government with the first-place [[Progressive Reform Party (Suriname)|VHP]] bloc led by [[Jagernath Lachmon]].<ref>{{cite book|author=Gonesh, Evert G. |title=Jagernath Lachmon: Verbroederingspolitiek in Suriname |year= 2015|publisher=LM Publishers|location=Utrecht|language=nl|isbn=978-94-6022-411-9}}</ref> In 1961, Sedney argued for the creation of a scientific institute in Suriname, which later developed into the University of Suriname (currently known as the [[Anton de Kom University]]).<ref name="vhp">{{cite news|url=https://www.starnieuws.com/index.php/welcome/index/nieuwsitem/59220|title=In Memoriam Dr. Jules Sedney|website=Star Nieuws|access-date=20 June 2020|author=Chan Santokhi|author-link=Chan Santokhi|language=nl}}</ref>

From 1969 to 1973, Sedney served as Prime Minister of [[Suriname (Kingdom of the Netherlands)|Suriname]]. He led a multi-ethnic government that he believed to have the checks and balances necessary to grow the Surinamese economy.<ref name="state">[https://www.governmentattic.org/3docs/Suriname%20SSG.pdf Suriname], US Department of State Self Study Guide, accessed 6 October 2010</ref> In 1969, Sedney embarked on the first steps towards the [[independence of Suriname]]: he proposed a Koninkrijkscommissie (Kingdom Commission) which would set the path towards independence. The commission came into effect on 5 January 1972.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.srherald.com/redactioneel/2014/11/25/de-held-van-de-onafhankelijkheid/|title=De held van de onafhankelijkheid|website=Suriname Herald|access-date=19 June 2020|language=nl}}</ref> However, Sedney later became a vocal critic of the same commission, arguing that the [[Netherlands|Dutch]] [[Labour Party (Netherlands)|PvdA]] was acting in undue haste.  From Sedney's perspective, independence should have been planned more carefully over a longer time period.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.nickerie.net/News2005/2005-11-24%20-%20tgf%20-%20Suriname%20was%20niet%20voorbereid%20op%20onafhankelijkheid.htm|title=Suriname was niet voorbereid op onafhankelijkheid|website=de Telegraaf via Nickerie.net|access-date=19 June 2020|language=nl}}</ref> He also raised concerns about ethnic tensions.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.nytimes.com/1972/11/03/archives/surinam-delaying-her-independence-government-fears-racial-strife.html|title=SURINAM DELAYING HER INDEPENDENCE|website=The New York Times|date=3 November 1972}}</ref>

In 1980, Sedney became the governor of the [[Central Bank of Suriname]].<ref name="bank">{{Cite web|url=https://www.cbvs.sr/nl/over-cbvs/directory|title=Directory – Centrale Bank van Suriname|date=27 March 2015|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150327213841/https://www.cbvs.sr/nl/over-cbvs/directory|archive-date=27 March 2015}}</ref> In 1983, he was fired after a dispute with [[Desi Bouterse]]<ref name="dwt"/> about a suspicious loan.<ref name="exile">{{cite news|url=https://www.digibron.nl/viewer/collectie/Digibron/offset/1/zoekwoord/Jules+Sedney/id/e6cd9435675d1a83a9d2abd68aa67497|title=Z2i2k Herrenberg in Colombia stinkt|website=Reformatorisch Dagblad via Digibron|date=13 July 1983|access-date=19 June 2020|language=nl}}</ref> In January 1983, he went into exile in the Netherlands.<ref name="exile"/> Sedney's [[citizenship]] was revoked on 21 July 1983<!-- dutch news reports tend to be 1 day late --> after he testified in a Dutch court against [[:nl:Henk Herrenberg|Henk Herrenberg]], the Surinamese ambassador to the Netherlands.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.digibron.nl/viewer/collectie/Digibron/offset/3/zoekwoord/Jules+Sedney/id/1d732fb65c0a146b23a3d749478617ce|title=Surinamers nationaliteit ontnomen|website=Reformatorisch Dagblad via Digibron|date=22 July 1983|access-date=19 June 2020|language=nl}}</ref> He was allowed to return to Suriname in 1989.<ref name="star"/>

==Later life==
Sedney was the oldest [[Contract bridge|bridge player]] of Suriname.<ref name="dwt"/> In 1997, he published ''De toekomst van ons verleden'' (The Future of Our Past), a political history of Suriname from 1948 onward.<ref name="star"/> This book included evaluations of Suriname's parliamentary democracy prior to the [[1980 Surinamese coup d'état|coup d'état]] and provided recommendations for strengthening democracy.<ref>{{cite web|url=https://dbnl.nl/tekst/_oso001199801_01/_oso001199801_01_0025.php|title=OSO. Tijdschrift voor Surinaamse taalkunde, letterkunde en geschiedenis. Jaargang 17|date=1998|access-date=19 June 2020|language=nl|website=Digital Library for Dutch Literature}}</ref> Successive editions were published in 2010 and 2017.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.srherald.com/suriname/2020/06/18/oud-premier-jules-sedney-heengegaan/|title=Oud-premier Jules Sedney heengegaan|website=Suriname Herald|access-date=19 June 2020|language=nl}}</ref> In 2016, he was awarded honorary membership by the [[Progressive Reform Party (Suriname)|VHP]].<ref name="vhp"/> In 2016, the New Harbour in Paramaribo was renamed ''[[Jules Sedney Harbour|Dr Jules Sedney – Haven van Paramaribo]]'' to recognize his involvement in its founding.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://unitednews.sr/nieuwe-haven-heet-voortaan-dr-jules-sedney-haven-van-paramaribo/|title=NIEUWE HAVEN HEET VOORTAAN DR.JULES SEDNEY-HAVEN VAN PARAMARIBO|website=United News|access-date=19 June 2020|language=nl}}</ref>

Sedney died on 18 June 2020.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Suriname|date=19 June 2020|title=Voormalig premier van Suriname Jules Sedney overleden|url=https://www.waterkant.net/suriname/2020/06/19/voormalig-premier-van-suriname-jules-sedney-overleden/|access-date=19 June 2020|website=Waterkant|language=nl}}</ref> He had been hospitalized just prior to his death.<ref name="dwt">{{cite news|date=18 June 2020|title=Oud-premier Jules Sedney overleden|language=nl|website=De Ware Tijd|url=http://dwtonline.com/laatste-nieuws/2020/06/18/oud-premier-jules-sedney-overleden/|access-date=19 June 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210302231251/http://dwtonline.com/laatste-nieuws/2020/06/18/oud-premier-jules-sedney-overleden/|archive-date=2021-03-02}}</ref>
