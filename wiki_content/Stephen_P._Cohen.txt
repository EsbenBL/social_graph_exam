'''Stephen Philip Cohen''' (1936 – October 27, 2019) was an American [[Political science|political scientist]] and professor of security studies.<ref>{{cite web|title=Stephen Philip Cohen|url=http://www.spp.nus.edu.sg/stag/Faculty_Stephen_Philip_Cohen.aspx|archive-url=https://archive.is/20121224193428/http://www.spp.nus.edu.sg/stag/Faculty_Stephen_Philip_Cohen.aspx|url-status=dead|archive-date=December 24, 2012|publisher=Lee Kuan Yew School of Public Policy:National University of Singapore|access-date=November 19, 2012}}</ref>  He was a prominent expert on [[India]], Pakistan and [[South Asia|South Asian]] security,<ref name="Newswire">{{cite news|title=Media Advisory - Rising India's "Pakistan Problem"|url=http://www.newswire.ca/en/story/530205/media-advisory-rising-india-s-pakistan-problem|access-date=November 19, 2012|newspaper=Canada Newswire|date=April 6, 2009}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.dnaindia.com/india/report_kashmir-conflict-will-last-for-100-years-author-stephen-p-cohen_1549551|title=Kashmir conflict will last for 100 years: Author Stephen P Cohen|date=May 31, 2011|work=[[Daily News and Analysis]]|access-date=August 15, 2011}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|last=Siraj Akbar|first=Malik|title=The Future of Pakistan|url=http://tribune.com.pk/story/319797/the-future-of-pakistan/|access-date=November 19, 2012|newspaper=The Express Tribune|date=January 15, 2012|quote=and was a Senior Fellow at the Washington DC-based think tank the Brookings Institution and considered as the "dean of the Pakistan experts." He was known as one of the world's most trusted civilian authorities on the Pakistani military and its relationship with the civilian governments.}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Stephen P. Cohen, Leading Expert on India, Joins Brookings as Senior Fellow|url=http://www.brookings.edu/about/media-resources/news-releases/1998/19981009scohen|publisher=Brookings|access-date=November 17, 2012|date=October 9, 1998|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160304042303/http://www.brookings.edu/about/media-resources/news-releases/1998/19981009scohen|archive-date=March 4, 2016}}</ref> He was a senior fellow in [[foreign policy]] studies at the [[Brookings Institution]] and an emeritus professor at the [[University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign]].<ref name="Texas">{{cite web|title=Lecture|url=http://www.utexas.edu/cola/insts/southasia/events/6029|publisher=The University of Texas at Austin:South Asia Institute}}</ref><ref>{{cite web|title=Stephen P. Cohen|url=http://acdis.illinois.edu/aboutacdis/profile.html&pid=53|publisher=University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign|access-date=November 17, 2012|url-status=dead|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20130624184354/http://acdis.illinois.edu/aboutacdis/profile.html%26pid%3D53|archive-date=June 24, 2013}}</ref> He authored, co-authored or edited at least 12 books,<ref name="Newswire"/> and named as one of America's 500 most influential people in foreign affairs, and was a fixture on radio and television talk shows.<ref name="Texas"/>
