'''Sushil Baldev Wadhwani''' {{post-nominals|country=GBR|CBE}} (born 7 December 1959<ref>{{cite web|url=http://www.debretts.com/people/biographies/browse/w/20418/Sushil+WADHWANI.aspx|title=Dr Sushil Wadhwani, CBE |publisher=[[Debrett's]]|accessdate=22 April 2011}}</ref>) was a member of the [[Bank of England]]'s [[Monetary Policy Committee (United Kingdom)|Monetary Policy Committee]] from June 1999 to May 2002.<ref>{{cite web|url=http://business.timesonline.co.uk/tol/business/economics/article551252.ece|title=Sushil Wadhwani, CBE|work=The Times|accessdate=22 April 2011}}</ref> He did his schooling from [[Mayo College]], [[Ajmer]], and his further education at [[Stanmore College|Stanmore Sixth Form College]].<ref>{{cite web |url = https://www.theguardian.com/education/2001/mar/06/furthereducation.uk1| title = College Days | date = 6 March 2001 | publisher = The Guardian}}</ref> He is also the founder and [[chief executive officer]] of [http://www.waniasset.com Wadhwani Asset Management].<ref>{{cite web|url=http://bai-conference.com/index.php?id=166&L=1|title=Dr. Sushil Wadhwani|publisher=Alternative Investor Confidence|accessdate=22 April 2011}}</ref>
