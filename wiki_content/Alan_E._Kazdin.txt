'''Alan Edward Kazdin''' (born January 24, 1945<ref>"Alan Edward Kazdin". ''Contemporary Authors Online''. November 11, 2007. Retrieved on December 18, 2010.</ref>) is Sterling Professor of [[Psychology]] and [[Child Psychiatry]] at [[Yale University]]. He is currently emeritus and was the director of the [[Yale Parenting Center and Child Conduct Clinic]].<ref name="YPC">"Alan Kazdin, Ph.D ABPP, Center Director." The Yale Parenting Center, http://childconductclinic.yale.edu/kazdin.</ref> Kazdin's research has focused primarily on the treatment of aggressive and antisocial behavior in children.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Alan Kazdin Ph.D. {{!}} Yale Parenting Center|url=https://yaleparentingcenter.yale.edu/people/alan-kazdin-phd|access-date=August 23, 2020|website=yaleparentingcenter.yale.edu|language=en}}</ref>

In 2008, he served as the President of the American Psychological Association.<ref>"Alan E. Kazdin, Ph.D." http://alankazdin.com/dr-alan-kazdin/ {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140116082926/http://alankazdin.com/dr-alan-kazdin/ |date=January 16, 2014 }} Accessed January 14, 2014.</ref><ref>{{Cite web|last=Nauert|first=Rick|date=September 14, 2011|title=Mental Health Care Reform Urged by Top Scientists|url=https://psychcentral.com/news/2011/09/14/mental-health-care-reform-urged-by-top-scientists/29412.html|access-date=August 23, 2020|website=psychcentral.com|language=en-US}}</ref>

== Education ==
Kazdin earned his B.A. in Psychology from [[San Jose State University]] and Ph.D. in Clinical Psychology from [[Northwestern University]].

== Achievements ==
His awards include MERIT Award from [[National Institute of Mental Health]], Lifetime Contribution Award to Psychology from the [[American Psychological Association]] and Distinguished Service Award to the Profession of Psychology from the [[American Board of Professional Psychology]].

In addition to his own published work, Kazdin has been editor of six journals: ''Behavior Therapy'' (1979–83), ''Journal of Consulting and Clinical Psychology'' (1985–90), ''Psychological Assessment'' (1989–91), ''Clinical Psychology: Science and Practice'' (1994–98), ''Current Directions in Psychological Science'' (1999–2004), and ''Clinical Psychological Science'' (since 2012).  He was editor-in-chief of the eight-volume ''Encyclopedia of Psychology'' (2000, APA/Oxford University). Also, he has edited two book series: ''Developmental Clinical Psychology and Psychiatry'' (Sage Publications, 1983–99) and ''Current Perspectives in Psychology'' (Yale University Press, 2000–08), and co-edited a book series ''Advances in Clinical Child Psychology'' (with Benjamin Lahey, 1977–92).<ref name="Vita" />
