'''Truman Fassett Bewley''' (born July 19, 1941) is an American economist. He is the Alfred Cowles Professor of Economics at [[Yale University]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://cowles.econ.yale.edu/faculty/bewley.htm|title = Welcome &#124; Cowles Foundation for Research in Economics}}</ref> Originally specializing in [[mathematical economics]] and [[general equilibrium theory]], since the late 1990s Bewley has gained renown for his work on [[sticky wages]].<ref>"Why Wages Do Not Fall in Recessions," ''The Economist'', February 26, 2000: http://cowles.econ.yale.edu/news/bewley/tfb_00-02_wages.htm</ref> In Bewley's 1999 book ''Why Wages Don't Fall During a Recession'',<ref>{{cite book |last=Bewley |first=Truman F. |year=1999 |title=Why Wages Don't Fall During a Recession |url=https://archive.org/details/whywagesdontfall00bewl |url-access=registration |publisher=Harvard University Press |isbn=9780674952416 }}</ref> hundreds of interviews with executives, labor leaders, and other professionals establish morale as an important factor in why businesses are reluctant to decrease employee compensation at times of low demand.

In [[general equilibrium theory]], Bewley (1972) established key existence results for models with infinitely many goods.<ref>{{cite journal |last=Bewley |first=T. |year=1972 |title=Existence of Equilibria in Economies with Infinitely many Commodities |journal=[[Journal of Economic Theory]] |volume=4 |issue= 3|pages=514–40 |doi= 10.1016/0022-0531(72)90136-6}}</ref>

Due to Bewley (1977),<ref>{{cite journal |last=Bewley |first=Truman F. |year=1977 |title=The Permanent Income Hypothesis: A Theoretical Formulation |journal=J. Econ. Theory |volume=16 |issue= 2|pages=252–92 |doi= 10.1016/0022-0531(77)90009-6|url=http://www.dtic.mil/get-tr-doc/pdf?AD=ADA031359 }}</ref> Bewley is the namesake of Bewley models, the class of [[incomplete markets]] general equilibrium models in which agents face idiosyncratic income shocks and achieve ''partial'' insurance via, for example, a [[risk-free bond]] or capital.<ref>{{cite journal |last=Bewley |first=Truman F. |year=1986 |title=Stationary Monetary Equilibrium with a Continuum of Independently Fluctuating Consumers |url=http://dido.wss.yale.edu/P/cp/p06b/p0671.pdf }}</ref><ref>{{cite book |last1=Ljungqvist |first1=L. |author-link=Lars Ljungqvist |last2=Sargent |first2=T. J. |author2-link=Thomas Sargent |year=2004 |title=Recursive Macroeconomic Theory |url=https://archive.org/details/recursivemacroec02edljun |url-access=registration |publisher=The MIT Press }}</ref> Aiyagari (1994),<ref>{{cite journal |last=Aiyagari |first=S. Rao |year=1994 |title=Uninsured Idiosyncratic Risk and Aggregate Saving |journal=[[Quarterly Journal of Economics]] |volume=109 |issue= 3|pages=659–84 |doi= 10.2307/2118417|jstor=2118417 |url=http://www.minneapolisfed.org/research/WP/WP502.pdf }}</ref> Huggett (1993),<ref>{{cite journal |last=Huggett |first=Mark |year=1993 |title=The Risk-Free Rate in Heterogeneous-Agent Incomplete-Insurance Economies |journal=J. Econ. Dyn. Control |volume=17 |issue= 5–6|pages=953–69 |doi= 10.1016/0165-1889(93)90024-m|hdl=2142/30021 |hdl-access=free }}</ref> and [[Per Krusell|Krusell]] and Smith (1998)<ref>{{cite journal |last1=Krusell |first1=P. |first2=A. A., Jr. |last2=Smith |year=1998 |title=Income and Wealth Heterogeneity in the Macroeconomy |journal=[[Journal of Political Economy]] |volume=106 |issue= 5|pages=867–96 |doi= 10.1086/250034|s2cid=17606592 }}</ref> are examples of Bewley models, each with many hundreds of citations according to [[Google Scholar]].

Bewley was elected a fellow of the [[American Academy of Arts and Sciences]] in 2005.<ref name=AAAS>{{cite web|title=Book of Members, 1780–2010: Chapter B|url=http://www.amacad.org/publications/BookofMembers/ChapterB.pdf|publisher=American Academy of Arts and Sciences|access-date=June 25, 2011}}</ref>  In 2012 he was elected a distinguished fellow of the [[American Economic Association]].
