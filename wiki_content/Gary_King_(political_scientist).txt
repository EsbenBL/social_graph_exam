'''Gary King''' (born December 8, 1958) is an American [[political science|political scientist]] and [[quantitative research|quantitative]] [[methodology|methodologist]]. He is the Albert J. Weatherhead III [[Harvard University Professor|University Professor]] and Director for the [[Institute for Quantitative Social Science]] at [[Harvard University]]. King and his research group develop and apply empirical methods in many areas of social science research, focusing on innovations that span the range from statistical theory to practical application.<ref>{{cite web|title=Biography|url=http://gking.harvard.edu/|publisher=Gking.harvard.edu|accessdate=26 January 2014}}</ref>

==Biography==
In 1980, King graduated ''[[summa cum laude]]'' with a [[Bachelor of Arts]] degree in political science from the [[State University of New York at New Paltz]].<ref name="Big Data">{{cite magazine|url=https://issuu.com/sunynewpaltz/docs/spring.2017.final.issu|title=Big Data: what we know vs. all the rest|magazine=SUNY New Paltz Alumni Newsletter|pages=16–17|date=Spring 2017}}</ref> In 1981 he earned a [[Master of Arts]] degree and in 1984 a [[Doctor of Philosophy]] degree in political science at the [[University of Wisconsin–Madison]] in [[Madison, Wisconsin|Madison]].

King's career in [[academia]] began in 1984, when he became an [[assistant professor]] in the Department of Politics at [[New York University]]. He joined the faculty of Harvard's Department of Government in 1987 and has taught there since. He has also been a visiting fellow at [[Oxford University]]. To date, he has authored or coauthored eight books (six published and one forthcoming) and more than 175 journal articles and book chapters, and has won more than 55 prizes and awards for his work.

King is one of 25 professors with "Harvard's most distinguished faculty title".<ref name="Big Data" />

He is the step-brother of the [[sociologist]] [[Mitchell Duneier]].{{cn|date=July 2019}}

==Business==
King co-founded the [[data analytics]] companies [[Crimson Hexagon]] and Learning Catalytics{{cn|date=September 2021}} and the educational technology companies [https://perusall.com/ Perusall] and [http://theopenscholar.com/ OpenScholar]. Crimson Hexagon and its nearest competitor merged in 2018; the new company is called [https://www.brandwatch.com/ Brandwatch].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Brandwatch|title=Brandwatch & Crimson Hexagon Merge|url=https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/brandwatch--crimson-hexagon-merge-300724674.html|access-date=2020-08-22|website=www.prnewswire.com|language=en}}</ref>   Learning Catalytics was acquired by Pearson in April 2013.<ref>{{cite web|author= |url=http://www.pearsoned.com/pearson-acquires-ed-tech-startup-learning-catalytics/#.U9mKPpRgZoA |title=Pearson Acquires Ed Tech Startup, Learning Catalytics™ &#124; Pearson News |publisher=Pearsoned.com |date=2013-04-22 |accessdate=2015-10-18}}</ref>
