'''Evelyn Blackwood''' is an American anthropologist whose research focuses on gender, sexuality, identity, and kinship. She was  awarded the [[Ruth Benedict Prize]] in 1999, 2007 and 2011.  Blackwood is an [[Emeritus|emerita]] professor of [[anthropology]]  at [[Purdue University]].

== Biography ==
Blackwood graduated from [[The King's College (New York City)|The King's College, New York]] with a BA in [[psychology]]. She earned an MA in anthropology at San Francisco State University, and a PhD from Stanford University in 1993.<ref name="EB">{{cite web |title=Evelyn Blackwood |url=https://www.evelynblackwood.com/publications?_ga=2.69607955.2066196574.1567347666-1043264257.1567347666 |website=Evelyn Blackwood |accessdate=2 September 2019}}</ref> Blackwood was an assistant professor at Purdue University from 1994 to 2000, associate professor from 2000 to 2010 and professor from 2010 to 2017. She is currently an emerita professor at the university.<ref name="Purdue" /> Blackwood, a lesbian,<ref>{{cite book |last=Blackwood |first=Evelyn |chapter=Falling in Love with an-Other Lesbian: Reflections on Identity in Fieldwork |title=Taboo: Sex, Identity and Erotic Subjectivity in Anthropological Fieldwork |editor-first=Don |editor-last=Kulick |editor2-first=Margaret |editor2-last=Willson |publisher=Routledge|year=2003}}</ref> works on gender, sexuality, identity and kinship as it relates to different cultural societies in West Sumatra, Indonesia and the United States.<ref name="Purdue">{{cite web |title=Evelyn Blackwood |url=https://www.cla.purdue.edu/directory/profiles/evelyn-blackwood.html |website=Purdue University |accessdate=2 September 2019}}</ref>

Blackwood was the recipient of a [[Fulbright]] Senior Scholarship in 2001<ref name="Fulbright Indonesia">{{cite web |last1=Pepinsky |first1=Thomas |title=US Fulbright Scholar Granatees |url=https://www.aminef.or.id/c/uploads/2017/12/FA_Aminef_Value_Content.pdf|website=Fulbright in Indonesia |accessdate=2 September 2019}}</ref> and a 2007 [[Martin Duberman]] Fellowship (Center for Lesbian and Gay Studies, [[City University of New York]]) for her research on sexuality and identity in Indonesia and Southeast Asia.<ref name="CLAG">{{cite web |last1=Sinanovic |first1=Jasmina |title=Fellowships and Awards (CLAGS Reports) |url=https://clags.org/articles/fellowships-and-awards-clags-reports/ |website=Center for LGBT Studies |accessdate=2 September 2019}}</ref> The research resulted in several publications, including the monograph, ''Falling into the Lesbi World: Desire and Difference in Indonesia (2010)'' which won the 2011 Ruth Benedict Prize."<ref name="Purdue" />

Her current research combines anthropology and history to "explore the construction and negotiation of identity, selfhood, and sexuality among baby boomers in the U.S., focusing on women in the first generation of 'out' lesbians in the San Francisco Bay Area in the 1970s."<ref name="Purdue" />
