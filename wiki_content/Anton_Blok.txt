'''Anton Blok''' (born 1935 in [[Amsterdam]]) is a Dutch [[anthropology|anthropologist]], famous for studying [[Sicilian Mafia|the Mafia]] in [[Sicily]] in 1960s.  Anton Blok was a visiting professor at the [[University of Michigan]] (1972–73) and the [[University of California, Berkeley]] in 1988. From 1973 until 1986, he served as full professor of [[cultural anthropology]] at [[Radboud University]], [[Nijmegen]]. He then accepted a chair at the [[University of Amsterdam]], where he remained until his retirement. For that occasion, thirty of his international colleagues and former students contributed essays in his honor, an edited volume titled as ''Miniature Etnografiche'' (SUN, 2000). Now a professor ''emeritus'' professor at U Amsterdam, Dr. Blok also spent one semester at [[Yale]] as a [[fellow]].
