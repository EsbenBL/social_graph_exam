'''Helen Zerlina Margetts'''<ref name=Doctorate>{{cite thesis | degree = Ph.D. | last = Margetts | first = Helen Zerlina | date = 1996 | title = Computerisation in American and British central government 1975-95: policy-making, internal regulation and contracting in information technology |publisher= [[University of London]] | oclc = 556741174 }}</ref> {{post-nominals|country=GBR|size=100%||OBE|FBA}} (born 15 September 1961),<ref name=Congress>{{cite web |title= Margetts, Helen |url= http://id.loc.gov/authorities/names/no94002890.html |publisher= Library of Congress |access-date= 31 August 2016 |quote= data sheet (b. 9-15-1961) }}</ref><ref>{{Cite book|title = Who's Who|date = November 2015|isbn = 9780199540884|chapter = MARGETTS, Helen Zerlina}}</ref> is Professor of Internet and Society at the [[Oxford Internet Institute]] (OII), [[University of Oxford]] and from 2011 to 2018 was Director of the OII. She is currently Director of the Public Policy Programme at The [[Alan Turing Institute]]. She is a [[political science|political scientist]] specialising in [[Information Age|digital era]] governance and [[politics]], and has published over a hundred books, journal articles and research reports in this field.

== Career ==
Margetts obtained her first degree, a [[Bachelor of Science|BSc]] in mathematics, from the [[University of Bristol]].<ref name="oii"/> In her early career she was a computer programmer and [[systems analyst]] with [[Rank Xerox]],<ref name="guardian">{{Cite news|title = IT news|url = https://www.theguardian.com/technology/2004/apr/29/onlinesupplement.insideit1|newspaper = The Guardian|date = 28 April 2004|access-date = 20 January 2016 |issn = 0261-3077|language = en-GB|first1 = Jack|last1 = Schofield|first2 = Eric|last2 = Doyle|first3 = S. A.|last3 = Mathieson}}</ref><ref name="oii"/> after which she took up postgraduate study at the [[London School of Economics]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|title = Helen Margetts {{!}} Associate Members {{!}} Academic {{!}} Profiles|url = http://www.politics.ox.ac.uk/associates/helen-margetts.html|website = www.politics.ox.ac.uk | publisher = [[University of Oxford]]|access-date = 20 January 2016}}</ref> There she earned a [[Master of Science|MSc]] in Politics and Public Policy (awarded in 1990) and a [[Doctor of Philosophy|PhD]] in Government (in 1996).<ref name=":0" /> From 1994 to 1999 she lectured at [[Birkbeck College, London]].<ref name=":0" />

Margetts is Professor of Internet and Society at the [[University of Oxford]],<ref name="oii">{{cite web | title = Professor Helen Margetts | url = http://www.oii.ox.ac.uk/people/?id=2 | website = oii.ox.ac.uk | publisher = [[Oxford Internet Institute]] |access-date= 20 January 2016 }}</ref> a fellow of [[Mansfield College, Oxford|Mansfield College]]<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.mansfield.ox.ac.uk/index.php/professor-helen-margetts |title=Professor Helen Margetts  |author=<!--Not stated--> |website=Mansfield College, Oxford |access-date=1 July 2019 }}</ref> and from 2011 to 2018 was Director of the [[Oxford Internet Institute]] (OII). Prior to joining the OII in October 2004, she was a Professor in Political Science and Director of the Public Policy Programme at [[University College London]].<ref name="guardian" /><ref>{{Cite web|title =Digital Advisory Board profile - Professor Helen Margetts |publisher=Government Digital Service|url = https://gds.blog.gov.uk/profileprofessorhelenmargetts/|website = gds.blog.gov.uk|access-date = 20 January 2016 }}</ref>

Amongst her research projects at the OII, she has used a variety of methods to investigate how the Internet can affect the relationship between citizens and government, and how informational cues can affect the success of online petitions and charity fundraising.<ref>{{Cite book|title = Encyclopedia of Social Media and Politics|chapter-url = https://books.google.com/books?id=qS91AwAAQBAJ|publisher = SAGE Publications|date = 20 December 2013|isbn = 9781452290263|language = en|first = Amanda|last = Clarke|pages = 938|chapter = Oxford Internet Institute|editor-first = Kerric|editor-last = Harvey}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title = First day 'is crucial for success of e-petitions'|url = https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-politics-23441223|website = BBC News|access-date = 20 January 2016|first = Ed|last = Lowther|date = 4 September 2013}}</ref> In March 2011 she was an expert witness for the [[Parliament of the United Kingdom|UK Parliament]]'s [[Public Administration Select Committee]]'s investigation into the cost of publicly funded information technology projects.<ref>{{Cite book|title = Government and IT - a Recipe for Rip-offs: Time for a New Approach, Twelfth Report of Session 2010-11, Vol. 2: Oral and Written Evidence|url = https://books.google.com/books?id=WUgVhui46FcC|publisher = The Stationery Office|date = 28 July 2011|isbn = 9780215561077|language = en|first = Great Britain: Parliament: House of Commons: Public Administration Select|last = Committee}}</ref>

Margetts is a Fellow of The [[Alan Turing Institute]] and is currently Director of the Public Policy Programme at the institute.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.turing.ac.uk/people/programme-directors/helen-margetts |title=Professor Helen Margetts OBE |author=<!--Not stated--> |website=Alan Turing Institute |access-date=1 July 2019 }}</ref>

Margetts holds many advisory positions, including sitting on the UK Government's Digital Economy Council, the Home Office Scientific Advisory Council, the board of the [[Ada Lovelace Institute]], and (from 2011-2015) the Government Digital Advisory Board.<ref>{{Cite web|title = Introducing the Digital Advisory Board {{!}} Government Digital Service|url = https://gds.blog.gov.uk/2012/04/25/introducing-the-digital-advisory-board/|access-date = 20 January 2016 |date = 25 April 2012|website = GOV.UK|last = Lane Fox|first = Martha|author-link = Martha Lane Fox, Baroness Lane-Fox of Soho}}</ref><ref>{{Cite magazine|title = Digital Advisory Board to support Government Digital Service (Wired UK)|url = https://www.wired.co.uk/news/archive/2012-04/24/digital-advisory-board-gds|magazine = Wired UK|access-date = 20 January 2016|last = Solon|first = Olivia|date = 25 April 2012|archive-url = https://web.archive.org/web/20160310063506/http://www.wired.co.uk/news/archive/2012-04/24/digital-advisory-board-gds|archive-date = 10 March 2016|url-status = dead}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|title = Government launches Digital Advisory Board|url = http://www.computerweekly.com/news/2240149027/Government-launches-Digital-Advisory-Board|website = ComputerWeekly|access-date = 20 January 2016 |language = en-GB|date = 25 April 2012|last = Hall|first = Kathleen}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|url=https://committees.parliament.uk/writtenevidence/430/html/|access-date=2021-08-12|website=committees.parliament.uk}}</ref>

She was appointed an [[Order of the British Empire]] (OBE) in the [[2019 New Year Honours]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thegazette.co.uk/London/issue/62507/supplement/N1|title=2019 New Year Honours List|date=29 Dec 2018|website=The London Gazette|access-date=28 Dec 2018}}</ref> In July 2019 she was elected as a Fellow of the [[British Academy]].<ref name="FBA">{{cite web |url=https://www.thebritishacademy.ac.uk/sites/default/files/New%20Fellows%202019_0.pdf |title=New Fellows 2019 |publisher=[[British Academy]] |access-date=2019-07-21 }}</ref>

== Awards ==
In July 2019 Helen was elected a Fellow of the [[British Academy|British Academy (FBA)]]. In March-April she held the John F Kluge Senior Chair in Technology and Society at the [[Library of Congress]], Washington DC.  She was awarded an [[Order of the British Empire|OBE]] for services to social and political science in the 2019 New Year’s Honours List. 

In 2018 she was awarded the [https://www.gov.tum.de/en/awards/friedrich-schiedel-prize-for-politics-and-technology/ Friedrich Schiedel Prize] by the [[Technical University of Munich]], for research and research leadership in politics and technology.  

Her co-authored book ''Political Turbulence'' won the W.J.Mckenzie Prize of the [https://www.psa.ac.uk UK Political Studies Association] for best politics book in 2017. 

She was elected a Fellow of the [[Academy of Social Sciences]] in 2011. 

In 2003 Margetts and [[Patrick Dunleavy]] were presented with the 'Political Scientists Making a Difference' award by the UK Policy Studies Association, in recognition for their work on a series of policy reports assessing the state of Government on the Internet for the UK [[National Audit Office (United Kingdom)|National Audit Office]].<ref name="oii" />
