'''Henry Carter Adams''' (December 31, 1851 – August 11, 1921) was a U.S. economist and Professor of Political Economy and finance at the [[University of Michigan]].<ref>[http://www.nndb.com/people/489/000082243/ Data and picture]</ref><ref>[https://www.jstor.org/pss/1822690 First page of extensive biography]</ref>

== Early years ==
Adams was born in [[Davenport, Iowa]] on December 31, 1851, son of Ephraim Adams and Elizabeth S.A. Douglass, and grandson of Ephraim Adams, of [[New Ipswich, New Hampshire]].<ref name="BDA1906">{{harvnb|Johnson|1906|p=40}}</ref> His father was a missionary of the "[[Iowa Band]]" from [[New England]]. He graduated from [[Iowa College]] in 1874,<ref name="BDA1906" /> now called [[Grinnell College]], which was co-founded by his father.<ref>{{cite book|url=https://books.google.com/books?id=iyxQNEx9H40C&pg=PA117 |title=The Social and Political Thought of American Progressivism |access-date=2008-06-07 | last=Eisenach |first=Eldon J.|isbn=9781603843546 }}</ref>

He was superintendent of schools at Nassau, Iowa, from 1874 to 1875, and became fellow of political economy at [[Johns Hopkins University]], from 1876 to 1889.<ref name="BDA1906" /> He went to [[Andover Newton Theological School|Andover Theological School]] in 1878, then studied at [[Heidelberg University|Heidelberg]], [[Berlin University|Berlin]], and at the [[Ecole Libre des Sciences Politiques]] in [[Paris]], from 1878 to 1879.<ref name="BDA1906" /> He received the degree [[Ph.D.]], from Johns Hopkins in 1878, and the honorary degree [[LL.D.]] from [[Iowa College of Law|Iowa College]] in 1898.<ref name="BDA1906" /><ref>Johns Hopkins University archives, [https://jscholarship.library.jhu.edu/bitstream/handle/1774.2/36866/commencement1878-re-creation.pdf?sequence=1&isAllowed=y June 13, 1878: Doctors of Philosophy].</ref> Adams' degree was one of the first four PhDs to be awarded by Hopkins, which opened in 1876.<ref>{{cite book |last1=French |first1=John C. |title=A History of the University Founded by Johns Hopkins |date=1946 |publisher=JHU Press |location=Baltimore |pages=340}}</ref>

== Career ==
Adams became a lecturer at [[Cornell University|Cornell]], from 1880 to 1883, and associate professor of political science there, from 1883 to 1887, also lecturer on political science at the [[University of Michigan]], from 1880 to 1887, and professor of political economy and finance there from 1887 until his death.<ref name="BDA1906" /> He also became a lecturer at the Johns Hopkins University from 1880 to 1882. While at Cornell, he delivered an address on "The Labor Problem," which resulted in his dismissal from the Cornell faculty when a critic accused him of "sapping the foundations of our society."<ref>University of Michigan: [http://faculty-history.dc.umich.edu/faculty/henry-carter-adams/memorial-0 The Michigan Alumnus 520-524]</ref> In his first year at Michigan he becoming head of the newly created Department of Economics. "For him economics was more than a study of data and statistics; he saw it as the very bone and sinews of our national life...."<ref name="umich.edu">University of Michigan: [http://faculty-history.dc.umich.edu/faculty/henry-carter-adams/memorial The Michigan Alumnus 12]</ref> At Michigan, he also worked with [[John Dewey]].

He was appointed [[statistician]] of the [[Interstate Commerce Commission]] in 1887 and was in charge of the transportation department in the eleventh [[U.S. Census, 1890]].<ref name="BDA1906" /> He was elected a member of the [[International Statistical Institute]], was president of the [[American Economic Association]], from 1895 to 1897, vice-president of the [[American Statistical Association]], secretary of the Michigan Political Science Association, and served as associate editor of the [[International Journal of Ethics]].<ref name="BDA1906" />

== Personal life ==
In 1890, he married Bertha Wright of [[Port Huron, Michigan|Port Huron]] and they had three sons, Henry Carter Adams Jr., later with the [[International Mercantile Marine]] at [[New York City]], Dr. Theodore W. Adams, later on the staff of Doctor Reuben Peterson, and Thomas H. Adams, a senior in the University of Michigan.<ref name="umich.edu" />

Adams died on August 11, 1921, in [[Ann Arbor, Michigan]].<ref name="umich.edu" />

== Works ==
He published:<ref name="BDA1906" /><ref name="umich.edu" />
* ''Lectures on Political Economy'' (1881)
* ''History of Taxation in the United States, 1789 to 1816'' (1884)
* ''Public Debts'' (1887)
* ''The State in Relation to Industrial Action'' (1887)
* ''Relation of American Municipalities to Quasi-Public Works'' (1888)
* ''The Science of Finance'' (1888), which was later translated into the [[Japanese language]].
* ''Statistics of Railways in the United States'' (6 vols., 1888–1898)
* ''Economics and Jurisprudence'' (1897)
* ''[https://openlibrary.org/books/OL20434811M/Description_of_Industry_An_Introduction_to_Economics Description of Industry: An Introduction to Economics]'' (1918)
