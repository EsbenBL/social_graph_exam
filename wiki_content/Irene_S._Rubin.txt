'''Irene Sharp Rubin''' (born May 3, 1945) is an [[America]]n [[political scientist]], currently a Professor Emerita of [[Public Administration]] at [[Northern Illinois University]]. She researches the politics of [[public budgeting]] at various levels of [[American government]], and she has written methodological texts on how to conduct and analyze [[Interview (research)|research interviews]].

==Education and early career==
Rubin received a BA in East Asia studies in 1967 at [[Barnard College]], followed by an MA in East Asia studies in 1969 from [[Harvard University]].<ref name=cv/> She then pursued a PhD in Sociology at the [[University of Chicago]],<ref name=kim19>{{cite journal |title=The Political Science 400: With Citation Counts by Cohort, Gender, and Subfield |doi=10.1017/S1049096518001786 |date=April 2019 |last1=Kim |first1=Hannah June |last2=Grofman |first2=Bernard |journal=PS: Political Science & Politics |volume=52 |issue=2 |pages=296–311 |url=https://www.cambridge.org/core/services/aop-cambridge-core/content/view/C1EDBF7220760F01A5C4A685DB3B3F44/S1049096518001786a.pdf/political_science_400_with_citation_counts_by_cohort_gender_and_subfield.pdf |access-date=19 January 2020|doi-access=free }}</ref> which she completed in 1977.<ref name=cv/>

Rubin began her career as a [[sociologist]], ultimately transitioning into [[urban studies]] and then political science and public affairs. From 1976 until 1979, Rubin was a professor of sociology at [[Lewis University]] in [[Romeoville, Illinois]], where she was also Director of  Urban Career Studies.<ref name=cv/> In 1979 she became a professor at the [[University of Maryland]] Institute for Urban Studies, before moving to Northern Illinois University in 1981 where she remained as a professor of political science and public administration for the rest of her career.<ref name=cv/>

==Career==
Rubin spent the bulk of her career, from 1981 until her retirement in 2004, at Northern Illinois University. During this time, she published articles across prominent urban affairs<ref>{{cite journal |first1=Irene S. |last1=Rubin |first2=Herbert J. |last2=Rubin |title=Economic Development Incentives: The Poor (Cities) Pay more |journal=Urban Affairs Review |volume=23 |issue=1 |date=1 September 1987 |doi=10.1177/004208168702300104|s2cid=153468055 }}</ref> and public administration<ref>{{cite journal |first=Irene S. |last=Rubin |title=Municipal enterprises: Exploring budgetary and political implications |journal=Public Administration Review |volume=48 |issue=1 |date=January 1988 |pages=542–550 |doi=10.2307/975517|jstor=975517 }}</ref> journals, as well as being an author or editor of 8 books.<ref name=cv/> These include four single-authored books published between 1982 and 2002.

Rubin's most-referenced work, written with Herbert Rubin, is an introductory textbook on interview research called ''Qualitative interviewing: The art of hearing data'', which was first published in 1995. The book introduces students to the concept of obtaining and analysing data from qualitative interviews, and describes methodological approaches for obtaining high-quality data from interviews.<ref>{{cite journal |first=Cameron M. |last=Ford |title=Review of Qualitative interviewing: The art of hearing data |journal=Journal of Product Innovation Management |volume=13 |issue=5 |date=September 1996 |pages=469–470 |doi=10.1016/S0737-6782(96)90188-5}}</ref> One of her single-authored books, the ''Politics of Public Budgeting'', was originally published in 1990 and had been released in 9 editions by 2019.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.sagepub.com/hi/nam/the-politics-of-public-budgeting/book259267 |title=The Politics of Public Budgeting |publisher=SAGE Publishing |date=February 2019 |access-date=19 January 2020}}</ref> John Pitney Jr. wrote that the book is a classroom text or reference volume that introduces "how money is raised and spent at all levels of government".<ref>{{cite journal |first=John J. |last=Pitney Jr. |title=Reviewed Works: Budget Reform Politics: The Design of the Appropriations Process in the House of Representatives 1865-1921 by Charles Stewart; The Politics of Public Budgeting: Getting and Spending, Borrowing and Balancing by Irene S. Rubin |journal=American Political Science Review |volume=84 |issue=4 |date=December 1990 |pages=1409–1411 |doi=10.2307/1963323|jstor=1963323 }}</ref> Rubin was also the editor of a 2008 book in the American Society for Public Administration Classics Series, called ''Public Budgeting: Policy, Process and Politics''.<ref name=cv/> She was the editor-in-chief of ''[[Public Budgeting & Finance]]'' from 1995–1996,<ref>{{cite book |title=Women in Public Administration: Theory and Practice |first1=Maria J. |last1=D'Agostino |first2=Helisse |last2=Levine |year=2011 |publisher=Jones & Bartlett Learning |page=153}}</ref> and the editor-in-chief of the ''[[Public Administration Review]]'' from 1997–1999.

Rubin has won several awards from major professional organisations. In 1994, she was awarded the Charles H. Levine Memorial Award for Excellence in Public Administration from the [[American Political Science Association]], given for "a public administration faculty member who has demonstrated excellence in teaching, research and service to the wider community".<ref name=levine>{{cite web |url=https://www.aspanet.org/ASPA/Make-Connections/Awards/Levine-Memorial-Award.aspx |title=Charles H. Levine Memorial Award for Excellence in Public Administration |publisher=American Political Science Association |year=2015 |access-date=19 January 2020}}</ref> She also received the James L. Blum Award for distinguished service in budgeting from the American Association for Budget and Program Analysis,<ref name=Blum>{{cite web |url=https://aabpa.memberclicks.net/blum-award |title=James L. Blum Award Recipients |publisher=American Association for Budget and Program Analysis |access-date=19 January 2020 |year=2019}}</ref> and several best paper awards from flagship journals.<ref name=cv/>

Rubin has had one of the largest impacts of any active political scientist, as measured by citations. In 2019, Rubin was listed as the 13th most cited active emeritus political scientist at an American university in a citation analysis by the political scientists Hannah June Kim and [[Bernard Grofman]].<ref name=kim19/> Rubin's work has also been referenced in media reports on public budgeting.<ref>{{cite news |url=https://www.nytimes.com/2007/06/15/business/15subsidy.html |work=The New York Times |last=Johnston |first=David Cay |date=15 June 2007 |access-date=19 January 2020 |title=Assisting the Good Life}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://beritagar.id/artikel/telatah/bencana-alam-dan-ketidakberpihakan-politik-anggaran |work=Beritagar Merawat Indonesia |last=Hidayah |first=Siti Nurul |date=31 December 2018 |access-date=19 January 2020 |title=Bencana alam dan ketidakberpihakan politik anggaran |language=Indonesian}}</ref><ref>{{cite news |url=https://fajar.co.id/2019/06/25/birokrasi-standar-iso-dan-zi/ |work=Fagar Borneo |first=Saor |last=Silitonga |date=25 June 2019 |access-date=19 January 2020 |title=Birokrasi Standar ISO dan ZI |language=Malay}}</ref>
