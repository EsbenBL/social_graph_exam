'''Michelle K. Ryan''' is a Professor of Social and Organisational Psychology at the [[University of Exeter]] and (part-time) Professor of Diversity at the [[University of Groningen|University of Groningen, The Netherlands]].

==Education==
She completed her undergraduate and a Ph.D. study in psychology from [[Australian National University]] in 2004 for her thesis ‘A gendered self or a gendered context? A self-categorization appraisal of gender differences’.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://openresearch-repository.anu.edu.au/bitstream/1885/43232/3/thesis_ryan.pdf|title=A gendered self or a gendered context? A social identity approach to gender differences|last=Ryan|first=Michelle|date=August 2003|website=ANU Open Research Repository|access-date=3 February 2020}}</ref>

==Research==
Her research focuses on gender and gender differences, particularly the impact of gender in the workplace.<ref name=":02">{{Cite web|url=http://psychology.exeter.ac.uk/staff/profile/index.php?web_id=michelle_ryan|title=Prof Michelle Ryan Staff Profile|website=University of Exeter, Psychology|access-date=3 February 2020}}</ref>

Together with [[Alex Haslam]], she coined the term "[[glass cliff]]" to describe how the experiences of women who have broken through the [[glass ceiling]] differ from those of their male counterparts.<ref>{{Cite journal|last1=Ryan|first1=Michelle|last2=Haslam|first2=Alexander|date=2005|title="The Glass Cliff: Evidence that Women are Over-Represented in Precarious Leadership Positions"|journal=British Journal of Management|volume=16|issue=2|pages=81–90|doi=10.1111/j.1467-8551.2005.00433.x|via=doi:10.1111/j.1467-8551.2005.00433.x}}</ref> Their idea was named by the ''[[The New York Times|New York Times]]'' as one of the ideas that shaped 2008.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.nytimes.com/2008/12/14/magazine/14Ideas-section4B-t-006.html|title=New York Times: Annual Year in Ideas|last=Thompson|first=Clive|date=12 December 2008|website=New York Times Magazine|access-date=3 February 2020}}</ref> The term was shortlisted as Word of the Year by the [[Oxford English Dictionary]] in 2016.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://languages.oup.com/word-of-the-year/2016-shortlist/|title=Word of the Year 2016 – Shortlist|website=Oxford Languages|access-date=3 February 2020}}</ref>

==Fellowships and other activity==
In 2004 she was awarded a [[Research Councils UK]] academic fellowship. She was Associate Editor for the [[British Journal of Social Psychology]] between 2010 and 2012.<ref name=":02" /> In 2014 she undertook a [[British Academy]] Mid-Career Fellowship to examine the role of identity in explaining perceptions of work-life balance.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.thebritishacademy.ac.uk/publications/british-academy-review/35/time-out|title=Time out!|last1=Üskül|first1=Ayşe|last2=Ryan|first2=Michelle|date=Spring 2019|website=The British Academy Review, No. 35|publisher=The British Academy|access-date=3 February 2020}}</ref> At the University of Exeter she has held the roles of Faculty Associate Dean (Research), Dean of Post-graduate Research and inaugural Director of the Exeter Doctoral College.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://issuu.com/universityofexeter/docs/doctoral_brochure_aw/3|title=University of Exeter Doctoral College brochure|website=University of Exeter|access-date=3 February 2020}}</ref>

She holds a [[European Research Council]] Consolidator Grant examining the way in which context and identity shape and constrain women’s careers.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://erc.europa.eu/projects-figures/erc-funded-projects/results?items_per_page=10&f%5B0%5D=funding_scheme%3AConsolidator%20Grant%20%28CoG%29&page=24|title=ERC Funded Projects (CIC)|website=European Research Council|access-date=3 February 2020}}</ref>

Ryan has been appointed director of the Global Institute for Women's Leadership at the Australian National University, with effect from 1 July 2021.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=2021-02-04|title=Leading light on women's leadership joins ANU|url=https://www.anu.edu.au/news/all-news/leading-light-on-women%E2%80%99s-leadership-joins-anu|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=2021-02-04|website=Australian National University|language=en}}</ref>
