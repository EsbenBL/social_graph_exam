'''Barbara Geddes''' (born October 15, 1944) is an American [[political science|political scientist]]. One of the main important theorists of [[authoritarianism]] and empirical catalogers of authoritarian regimes,<ref name=":0">{{Cite journal|last=Pepinsky|first=Thomas|date=2014|title=The Institutional Turn in Comparative Authoritarianism|url=https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/british-journal-of-political-science/article/abs/institutional-turn-in-comparative-authoritarianism/DF3E2FF1E79A8D41E3E60F1D45A1B5C5|journal=British Journal of Political Science|language=en|volume=44|issue=3|pages=631–653|doi=10.1017/S0007123413000021|issn=0007-1234}}</ref> she is currently a full professor at the Department of Political Science at the [[University of California, Los Angeles]].<ref>{{cite web |title=Barbara Geddes |url=https://polisci.ucla.edu/people/barbara-geddes |publisher=University of California at Los Angeles}}</ref> Her 2003 book ''Paradigms and Sand Castles'' is an influential research design book in [[comparative politics]]. 

==Research==
Geddes studies various authoritarian regimes and classifies them in five typologies: Military Dictatorships, Single-party Dictatorships, Personalist Dictatorships, Monarchies, and Hybrid Dictatorships.<ref>{{Cite book|title=Dictators and dictatorships : understanding authoritarian regimes and their leaders|last=Ezrow|first=Natasha|publisher=Continuum|others=Frantz, Erica|year=2011|isbn=978-1-4411-1602-4|location=New York|oclc=705538250}}</ref> "Geddes' (1999) categorization of personalist, party, and military regimes and her use of this classification to examine theories regarding the survival of dictatorships  and the likelihood of democratic transitions have been path breaking."<ref>{{cite book |last1=Gandhi |first1=Jennifer |title=Political Institutions Under Dictatorship |date=2008 |publisher=Cambridge University Press |location=New York, NY |isbn=978-0-521-89795-2 |page=xxi}}</ref> In a 2014 article, the classification had seven typologies: dominant party regimes, military regime, personalist regimes, monarchies, oligarchic regimes, indirect military regimes, or hybrids of the first three.<ref>{{Cite journal|last=Geddes|first=Barbara|last2=Wright|first2=Joseph|last3=Frantz|first3=Erica|date=2014|title=Autocratic Breakdown and Regime Transitions: A New Data Set|url=https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/perspectives-on-politics/article/abs/autocratic-breakdown-and-regime-transitions-a-new-data-set/EBDB9E5E64CF899AD50B9ACC630B593F|journal=Perspectives on Politics|language=en|volume=12|issue=2|pages=313–331|doi=10.1017/S1537592714000851|issn=1537-5927}}</ref> Thomas Pepinsky has described Geddes's work on authoritarianism as "path-breaking."<ref name=":0" />

She is also interested in collapse of and transition between regimes. Her earlier work, "investigated bureaucratic reform and corruption in Brazil, the politics of economic policy making in Latin America, and political bargaining over institutional choice."<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://polisci.ucla.edu/people/barbara-geddes|title=UCLA Department of Political Science|website=polisci.ucla.edu|access-date=2019-04-07}}</ref> Geddes has a regional focus on Latin America.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://polisci.ucla.edu/people/barbara-geddes|title=UCLA Department of Political Science|website=polisci.ucla.edu|access-date=2019-04-07}}</ref>

==Awards==

In 2014, the Comparative Politics Section of the American Political Science Association awarded Geddes the Bingham Powell Graduate Mentoring Award.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.apsanet.org/section-20-powell-graduate-mentoring-award|title=American Political Science Association > MEMBERSHIP > Organized Sections > Organized Section 20: Powell Graduate Mentoring Award|website=www.apsanet.org|access-date=2019-04-07}}</ref> "The Autocratic Regimes Data Set" that Geddes created with Joseph G. Wright and Erica Frantz, political scientists at Penn State University and Michigan State University, respectively, garnered the Lijphart/ Przeworski/ Verba Data Set Award in 2015, also awarded by the Comparative Politics Section of the American Political Science Association.<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.apsanet.org/section-20-lijphart-przeworski-verba-data-set-award|title=American Political Science Association > MEMBERSHIP > Organized Sections > Organized Section 20: Lijphart/ Przeworski/ Verba Data Set Award|website=www.apsanet.org|language=en-US|access-date=2019-04-07}}</ref>
