'''David Michael Garrood Newbery''', [[Order of the British Empire|CBE]], [[British Academy|FBA]] (born 1 June 1943),<ref name=Congress>{{cite web | title= Newbery, David M. G. | url= http://id.loc.gov/authorities/names/n81144267.html | publisher= Library of Congress | access-date= 26 October 2014 |quote= data sheet (b. 6-1-1943) }}</ref> is a Professor of Applied Economics at the [[University of Cambridge]]. He got this position in 1988.<ref name="Cambridge">[http://www.econ.cam.ac.uk/faculty/newbery/ Faculty page at University of Cambridge]</ref> He specializes in the field of [[energy economics]], and he writes on the regulation of electricity markets. His interests also include [[climate change mitigation]] and environmental policy, [[Privatization|privatisation]], and risk.<ref>{{cite book | page = [https://archive.org/details/whoswhoeconomics00blau/page/n625 601] | title = Who's Who in Economics | url = https://archive.org/details/whoswhoeconomics00blau | url-access = limited | first1=Mark | last1=Blaug |first2= Howard R. |last2=Vane | publisher = Edward Elgar Publishing | year = 2003 | isbn = 978-1840649925}}</ref>

==Early life==
Newbery was born on 1 June 1943 at Fulmer Chase, [[Fulmer]], [[Buckinghamshire]], [[England]]. He studied at [[Portsmouth Grammar School]] from 1954 till 1961, where he won Best Science Candidate in Cambridge GCE A&S Level. He then proceeded to study Mathematics and Economics at [[Trinity College, Cambridge]], obtaining a B.A. in 1964, [[Master of Arts (Oxbridge and Dublin)|M.A. (Cantab.)]] in 1968 and a Ph.D. degree in 1976.<ref name="David Michael Garrood Newbery">[http://www.econ.cam.ac.uk/people-files/emeritus/dmgn/CV-Newbery-full.pdf |title= David Michael Garrood Newbery]{{Dead link|date=July 2019 |bot=InternetArchiveBot |fix-attempted=yes }}</ref>

== Career ==

In 1965 he was pre-elected to Churchill Teaching Fellowship. In 1966 he got a position of University Assistant Lecturer at Faculty of Economics. Later he was a Director of Department of Applied Economics from 1988 till 2003. He also served as a Professor II at Tromso University, Norway from 2011 till 2013.

In 1981 Newbery co-authored a book and several articles with Nobel-laureate [[Joseph Stiglitz]]. The name of the book was ''"The Theory of Commodity Price Stabilization: A Study in the Economics of Risk"''

Besides his role as Professor of Applied Economics at Cambridge, he serves as Vice-Chairman of Cambridge Economic Policy Associates,<ref>[http://www.cepa.co.uk CEPA]</ref> Director of the Cambridge Electricity Policy Research Group<ref>{{Cite web |url=http://www.electricitypolicy.org.uk/people/newbery/index.html |title=Electricity Policy Research Group profile |access-date=3 April 2009 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20090107054506/http://www.electricitypolicy.org.uk/people/newbery/index.html |archive-date=7 January 2009 |url-status=dead }}</ref> and an occasional consultant to the [[World Bank]].<ref>[http://www.eeinstitute.org/participating-professors/david-newbery Profile at EE Institute]</ref> In 1988–2003 he was Director of the Department of [[Applied economics]]. He was a member of the [[Competition Commission]] in 1996–2002, and chairman of the Dutch electricity market surveillance committee. He was elected a Fellow of the [[British Academy]]<ref name="Cambridge" /> in 1991. He is a member of the Academic Panel of [[Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs|DEFRA]].<ref name="Cambridge" /> Since 1966 he has been a fellow of [[Churchill College, Cambridge|Churchill College]], and was President of its [[Common Room (university)|Senior Combination Room]] (SCR) in from 2010 to 2019.<ref name="SCR">[http://scr.chu.cam.ac.uk/ Churchill College SCR]</ref>

== Honours ==
*Newbery was appointed [[Commander of the Order of the British Empire]] (CBE) in the 2012 Birthday Honours for services to economics.<ref>{{London Gazette|issue=60173|supp=y|page=8|date=16 June 2012}}</ref>
* He was elected Fellow of Econometric Society in 1989.
* He was awarded Frisch Medal of the Econometric Society in 1990.
* He was awarded Harry Johnson Prize by Canadian Economics Association in 1993.
* He received the IAEE 2002 Outstanding Contributions to the Profession of Energy Economics Award.
* He got an Honorary Degree at the University of Antwerp in 2004.<ref name="David Michael Garrood Newbery"/>
* He was president of International Association of Energy Economics in 2013.
