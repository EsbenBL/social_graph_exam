'''Nicole Y. Weekes''' is an American psychologist and neuroscientist whose work focuses on the [[Fight-or-flight response|psychological and biological response]] to [[Stress (biology)|stress]].<ref name="Official" /> She is the Harry S. and L. Madge Rice Thatcher Professor of Psychological Science and Professor of Neuroscience at [[Pomona College]] in [[Claremont, California]].<ref name="Official">{{cite web |title=Nicole Weekes |url=https://www.pomona.edu/directory/people/nicole-weekes |publisher=Pomona College |access-date=26 August 2021 |language=en}}</ref>

== Early life ==
Weekes earned her bachelor's degree from [[Boston University]] and her doctorate from the [[University of California, Los Angeles]].<ref name="Official" />

== Career ==
Weekes began teaching at [[Pomona College]] in 1998.<ref name="Official" /> In 2016, she was appointed Associate Dean of Faculty to help lead efforts to further diversify its ranks.<ref name="Promoted TSL">{{cite news |last1=Rod |first1=Marc |title=Nicole Weekes Promoted to Pomona Diversity Leadership Position |url=https://tsl.news/news6029/ |access-date=28 August 2021 |work=[[The Student Life]] |date=30 September 2016}}</ref> She has won the Wig Distinguished Professor Award, the college's highest faculty honor, in recognition of her teaching on three occasions, in 2001, 2006, and 2011.<ref name="Official" /><ref>{{Cite web|title=Wig Awards|url=https://www.pomona.edu/faculty/wig-awards|url-status=live|access-date=2021-08-27|publisher=Pomona College|language=en|archive-date=April 20, 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210420161329/https://www.pomona.edu/faculty/wig-awards}}</ref>
