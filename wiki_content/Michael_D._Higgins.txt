'''Michael Daniel Higgins''' ({{lang-ga|Mícheál Dónal Ó hUigínn}}; born 18 April 1941) is an Irish politician, poet, [[Sociology|sociologist]], and broadcaster,<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-15500225|title=Who is Michael D Higgins?|last=O'Leary|first=Jennifer|work=BBC News|date=28 October 2011|access-date=28 October 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111028173011/http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-northern-ireland-15500225|archive-date=28 October 2011|url-status=live}}</ref> who has served as the ninth [[president of Ireland]] since November 2011. He served as a [[Teachta Dála]] (TD) for the [[Galway West (Dáil constituency)|Galway West]] constituency from 1981 to 1982 and 1987 to 2011. He served as [[Minister for Children, Equality, Disability, Integration and Youth|Minister for Arts, Culture and the Gaeltacht]] from 1993 to 1997 and [[mayor of Galway]] from 1981 to 1982 and 1990 to 1991. He was a [[Seanad Éireann|senator]] from 1973 to 1977, after being [[Nominated members of Seanad Éireann|nominated by the taoiseach]] and from 1983 to 1987 for the [[National University of Ireland (constituency)|National University of Ireland]].<ref name=oireachtas_db>{{cite web|url=https://www.oireachtas.ie/en/members/member/Michael-D-Higgins.S.1973-06-01/|title=Michael D. Higgins|work=Oireachtas Members Database|access-date=21 September 2009|archive-date=26 July 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180726234159/https://www.oireachtas.ie/en/members/member/Michael-D-Higgins.S.1973-06-01/|url-status=live}}</ref> He was the President of the [[Labour Party (Ireland)|Labour Party]] from 2003 to 2011, until he resigned following his [[2011 Irish presidential election|election]] as president of Ireland.<ref name="ExP">{{cite news|title=Higgins declared president elect with one million votes|newspaper=[[The Irish Times]]|date=29 October 2011|url=http://www.irishtimes.com/newspaper/breaking/2011/1029/breaking2.html|access-date=29 October 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111030124716/http://www.irishtimes.com/newspaper/breaking/2011/1029/breaking2.html|archive-date=30 October 2011|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/worldnews/europe/ireland/8856598/Michael-D.-Higgins-wins-Irish-presidential-election.html|title=Michael D. Higgins wins Irish presidential election|work=The Daily Telegraph|date=31 May 2011|access-date=30 October 2011|location=London|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111029145812/http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/worldnews/europe/ireland/8856598/Michael-D.-Higgins-wins-Irish-presidential-election.html|archive-date=29 October 2011|url-status=live}}</ref>

He has used his time in office to address issues concerning [[justice]], [[social equality]], [[social inclusion]], anti-[[sectarianism]], [[anti-racism]], and reconciliation. He made the [[State visit of the President of Ireland to the United Kingdom|first state visit]] by an Irish president to the [[United Kingdom]] in April 2014.
 
Higgins ran for a second term as president of Ireland in [[2018 Irish presidential election|2018]] and was re-elected in a [[landslide victory]]. Higgins attained the largest personal mandate in the [[history of the Republic of Ireland]], with 822,566 [[first preference votes]]. Higgins' second [[Irish presidential inauguration|presidential inauguration]] took place on 11 November 2018.

==Early life==
Higgins was born on 18 April 1941 in [[Limerick]].<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.imdb.com/name/nm1014980/bio|title=Michael D. Higgins|website=IMDb|access-date=6 November 2017|archive-date=24 August 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200824131756/https://www.imdb.com/name/nm1014980/bio/|url-status=live}}</ref> His father, John Higgins, was from Ballycar, [[County Clare]], and was a lieutenant with the Charleville Company, 3rd Battalion, 2nd Cork Brigade of the [[Irish Republican Army]]. John, along with his two brothers Peter and Michael, had been active participants in the [[Irish War of Independence]].<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://uk.news.yahoo.com/michael-d-higgins-father-fought-irish-independence-061031682.html|title=How Michael D Higgins's father fought for Irish independence|access-date=6 November 2017|language=en-GB|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171107013540/https://uk.news.yahoo.com/michael-d-higgins-father-fought-irish-independence-061031682.html|archive-date=7 November 2017|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=http://irishpost.co.uk/military-files-reveal-michael-d-higgins-fathers-role-in-irelands-battle-for-independence/|title=Files reveal President Higgins' father's role in independence|date=12 December 2015|work=Irish Post|access-date=6 November 2017|language=en-GB|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160427081437/http://irishpost.co.uk/military-files-reveal-michael-d-higgins-fathers-role-in-irelands-battle-for-independence/|archive-date=27 April 2016|url-status=live}}</ref>

When John's father's health grew poor, with alcohol abuse as a contributing factor, John sent Michael, aged five, and his four-year-old brother to live on his unmarried uncle and aunt's farm near [[Newmarket-on-Fergus]], [[County Clare]]. His elder twin sisters remained in Limerick.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.irishtimes.com/newspaper/weekend/2011/0625/1224299577059.html|title=Michael D's ode to the presidency|work=[[The Irish Times]]|date=25 June 2011|access-date=30 October 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121024052718/http://www.irishtimes.com/newspaper/weekend/2011/0625/1224299577059.html|archive-date=24 October 2012|url-status=live}}</ref> He was educated at Ballycar National School, County Clare and [[St. Flannan's College]], [[Ennis]].<ref>{{Cite web|date=15 June 2012|title=President returns to Clare to his childhood school|url=https://www.irishtimes.com/news/president-returns-to-clare-to-his-childhood-school-1.1066311|access-date=27 October 2020|website=The Irish Times|language=en|archive-date=12 September 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210912122241/https://www.irishtimes.com/news/president-returns-to-clare-to-his-childhood-school-1.1066311|url-status=live}}</ref>

As an undergraduate at [[NUI Galway|University College Galway]] (UCG), he served as Vice-Auditor of the college's [[Literary and Debating Society (NUI Galway)|Literary and Debating Society]] in 1963–64, and rose to the position of Auditor in the 1964–65 academic year. He also served as President of [[NUI Galway Students' Union|UCG Students' Union]] in 1964–65. In 1967, Higgins graduated from the American [[Indiana University Bloomington]] with a Master of Arts degree in Sociology.<ref name=INDIANAPRESS>{{cite web|last=Press Release 31 October 2011|title=IU extends congratulations to alumnus who will become Ireland's ninth president|url=http://newsinfo.iu.edu/news/page/normal/20147.html?emailID=20147|publisher=The Trustees of Indiana University|access-date=20 December 2012|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121225234158/http://newsinfo.iu.edu/news/page/normal/20147.html?emailID=20147|archive-date=25 December 2012|url-status=live}}</ref> He also briefly attended the [[Victoria University of Manchester|University of Manchester]].<ref>{{cite web|title=Irish President meets University President|url=http://www.manchester.ac.uk/discover/news/article/?id=9116|publisher=University of Manchester|access-date=9 February 2015|date=23 November 2012|quote=President Higgins first arrived in Manchester in 1968 and spent much of his time attending lectures and seminars in the Dover Street Building.|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20150210102523/http://www.manchester.ac.uk/discover/news/article/?id=9116|archive-date=10 February 2015|url-status=live}}</ref>

In his academic career, he was a Statutory Lecturer in the Department of Political Science and Sociology at UCG<ref name=":0" /> and was a visiting professor at [[Southern Illinois University Carbondale|Southern Illinois University]].<ref>{{Cite web|last=Pallasch|first=Abdon|date=14 May 2014|title=President Michael Higgins Visits the Midwest|url=https://irishamerica.com/2014/05/president-michael-higgins-visits-the-midwest/|access-date=27 October 2020|website=Irish America|language=en-US|archive-date=1 November 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201101154116/https://irishamerica.com/2014/05/president-michael-higgins-visits-the-midwest/|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite web|date=26 January 2011|title=Michael D Higgins, Ireland's 'political intellectual'|url=https://villagemagazine.ie/michael-d-higgins-ireland%e2%80%99s-%e2%80%98political-intellectual%e2%80%99/|access-date=27 October 2020|website=Village Magazine|language=en-US|archive-date=28 October 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20201028134416/https://villagemagazine.ie/michael-d-higgins-ireland%E2%80%99s-%E2%80%98political-intellectual%E2%80%99/|url-status=live}}</ref> He resigned his academic posts to concentrate fully on his political career.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.britannica.com/biography/Michael-D-Higgins|title=Michael D. Higgins {{!}} president of Ireland|work=Encyclopedia Britannica|access-date=6 November 2017|language=en|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171107033042/https://www.britannica.com/biography/Michael-D-Higgins|archive-date=7 November 2017|url-status=live}}</ref>

He is a fluent [[Irish language]] speaker<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.independent.ie/opinion/analysis/lise-hand-seans-fava-beans-and-chianti-on-hold-as-hopefuls-start-softly-2891347.html|title=Sean's fava beans and Chianti on hold as hopefuls start softly|last=Hand|first=Lise|work=Irish Independent|date=29 September 2011|access-date=29 September 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20110930235036/http://www.independent.ie/opinion/analysis/lise-hand-seans-fava-beans-and-chianti-on-hold-as-hopefuls-start-softly-2891347.html|archive-date=30 September 2011|url-status=live}}</ref> and also speaks Spanish.<ref>[http://www.uimp.es/blogs/prensa/2012/08/25/el-presidente-irlandes-dedica-tres-semanas-del-verano-a-aprender-espanol-en-la-uimp/ El presidente irlandés dedica tres semanas del verano a aprender español en la UIMP] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20140508222953/http://www.uimp.es/blogs/prensa/2012/08/25/el-presidente-irlandes-dedica-tres-semanas-del-verano-a-aprender-espanol-en-la-uimp/ |date=8 May 2014 }} Universidad Internacional Menéndez Pelayo, 25 August 2012. {{in lang|es}}</ref>

==Political career (1973–2011)==
===Seanad and Dáil Éireann===
Higgins originally joined [[Fianna Fáil]] in UCG while a [[Adult learner|mature student]] and was elected its branch chairman in 1966; he switched to the Labour Party shortly thereafter.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.thephoenix.ie/phoenix/subscriber/library/volume-28/issue-25/contents.pdf|title= Pillars of Society – Michael D. Higgins pg40|work=[[The Phoenix (magazine)|The Phoenix]]|date = 16 November 2010|access-date=16 November 2010}}</ref> He was a Labour candidate in the [[1969 Irish general election|1969]] and [[1973 Irish general election|1973]] general elections but was unsuccessful on both occasions.<ref name=elecs_irl/> One of the people who canvassed for him was future leader of the [[Labour Party (Ireland)|Labour Party]] and [[Tánaiste]], [[Eamon Gilmore]], who was then a UCG student. Higgins was appointed in 1973 to the [[Members of the 13th Seanad|13th Seanad Éireann]] by [[Taoiseach]] [[Liam Cosgrave]]. He was first elected to [[Dáil Éireann]] at the [[1981 Irish general election|1981 general election]] as a Labour Party [[Teachta Dála|TD]].<ref name=oireachtas_db/> He was re-elected at the [[February 1982 Irish general election|February 1982 election]]; he lost his seat at the [[November 1982 Irish general election|November 1982 election]] (blaming his loss in part on his opposition to the [[Eighth Amendment of the Constitution of Ireland|Eighth Amendment]]),<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.irishtimes.com/newspaper/archive/1982/1126/Pg008.html#Ar00801|title=Defeated TDs claim pro-abortion smear|newspaper=The Irish Times|date=26 November 1982|access-date=1 August 2016|quote=Mr Higgins declared that the notion had been propounded throughout the campaign that he had become too radical and was in favour of abortion}}</ref> but returned to the Seanad when he was elected by the [[National University of Ireland (constituency)|National University of Ireland]] panel. He served as [[Mayor of Galway]] on two occasions, 1982–1983 and 1991–1992. Within the Labour Party during the 1980s he was one of the main figures, along with [[Emmet Stagg]], who opposed going into coalition.{{citation needed|date=October 2020}}

Higgins returned to the Dáil at the [[1987 Irish general election|1987 general election]] and held his seat until the [[2011 Irish general election|2011 general election]].<ref name=elecs_irl>{{cite web|url=http://www.electionsireland.org/candidate.cfm?ID=3234|title=Michael D. Higgins|work=ElectionsIreland.org|access-date=19 September 2009|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101202032940/http://electionsireland.org/candidate.cfm?ID=3234|archive-date=2 December 2010|url-status=live}}</ref> In 1993, he joined the Cabinet as [[Minister for Children, Equality, Disability, Integration and Youth|Minister for Arts, Culture and the Gaeltacht]]. During his period as minister he scrapped the controversial [[Censorship in the Republic of Ireland#The Troubles: RTÉ and Section 31 of the Broadcasting Authority Act|Section 31]] of the Broadcasting Act, re-established the [[Irish Film Board]] and set up the Irish language television station, Teilifís na Gaeilge (later renamed [[TG4]]).<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-northern-ireland-15500225|title=Who is Michael D Higgins?|last=O'Leary|first=Jennifer|date=28 October 2011|work=BBC News|access-date=6 November 2017|language=en-GB|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180629050225/https://www.bbc.com/news/uk-northern-ireland-15500225|archive-date=29 June 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> He was appointed to the Labour Party front bench in 2000. In 2003, Higgins succeeded [[Proinsias De Rossa]] in the symbolic position of the President of the Labour Party, while continuing as the party's spokesman on foreign affairs.{{citation needed|date=November 2012}}

Higgins indicated his interest in contesting the [[2004 Irish presidential election|2004 presidential election]] for the Labour Party. The party decided on 16 September 2004 against running a candidate in the election, seeing [[Mary McAleese]] as unbeatable.<ref name=":0">{{Cite news|url=https://www.independent.ie/irish-news/michaels-rise-from-humble-beginnings-to-first-citizen-26787032.html|title=Michael's rise from humble beginnings to first citizen – Independent.ie|work=Independent.ie|access-date=6 November 2017|language=en|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171107014209/https://www.independent.ie/irish-news/michaels-rise-from-humble-beginnings-to-first-citizen-26787032.html|archive-date=7 November 2017|url-status=live}}</ref>

In October 2010, he announced he would not be standing at the [[2011 Irish general election|2011 general election]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.irishtimes.com/newspaper/breaking/2010/1126/breaking40.html|title=FG's Burke to retire at next election|last=Burke-Kennedy|first=Eoin|author2=Minihan, Mary|work=The Irish Times|date=26 November 2010|access-date=26 November 2010|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20101201122128/http://www.irishtimes.com/newspaper/breaking/2010/1126/breaking40.html|archive-date=1 December 2010|url-status=live}}</ref> He had until this point been living in a modest two-bed apartment at Grattan Hall on Mount Street, Dublin. He also has a family home in [[Galway]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.herald.ie/news/from-twobed-dublin-flat-to-the-delights-of-the-aras-for-michael-d-2927598.html|title=From two-bed Dublin flat to the delights of the Aras for Michael D|last=O'Connor|first=Niall|author2=Doyle, Kevin|work=Evening Herald|date=7 November 2011|access-date=7 November 2011|archive-date=12 September 2021|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210912122244/https://www.independent.ie/regionals/herald/news/from-two-bed-dublin-flat-to-the-delights-of-the-aras-for-michael-d-27993285.html|url-status=live}}</ref>

===Second term (2018–present)===
On 10 July 2018, Higgins announced that he would stand for a second term as president in the [[2018 Irish presidential election]].<ref name=RTE_20180710>{{cite news|url=https://www.rte.ie/news/ireland/2018/0710/977616-presidential-election/|title=Michael D Higgins to seek second term as president|work=[[RTÉ News]]|date=10 July 2018|access-date=10 July 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180710164652/https://www.rte.ie/news/ireland/2018/0710/977616-presidential-election/|archive-date=10 July 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> Higgins won the [[2018 Irish presidential election|2018 presidential election]] with 56% of the vote on the first count (822,566).<ref>{{Cite web|url=https://www.irishtimes.com/news/politics/presidential-election/results|title=RESULTS: Presidential Election|website=The Irish Times|language=en|access-date=10 March 2020|archive-date=9 March 2020|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20200309164532/https://www.irishtimes.com/news/politics/presidential-election/results|url-status=live}}</ref> His nearest rival, [[Peter Casey]], finished on 23% (342,727).<ref>{{cite news | last1=Leahy | first1=Pat | last2=McGee | first2=Harry | date=29 October 2018 | title=Higgins begins preparations for second term after landslide victory | url=https://www.irishtimes.com/news/politics/higgins-begins-preparations-for-second-term-after-landslide-victory-1.3678961 | work=The Irish Times | access-date=30 October 2018 | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181029042151/https://www.irishtimes.com/news/politics/higgins-begins-preparations-for-second-term-after-landslide-victory-1.3678961 | archive-date=29 October 2018 | url-status=live }}</ref>
On 11 November 2018, Higgins was inaugurated as president of Ireland in St. Patrick's Hall, [[Dublin Castle]], in a ceremony attended by [[Taoiseach]] [[Leo Varadkar]], and former presidents [[Mary Robinson]]  and [[Mary McAleese]], as well as representatives of all political parties. The ceremony was held in the evening, so that Higgins could attend [[Armistice Day]] commemorations in the morning.<ref name=RTE_20180711>{{cite news|url=https://www.rte.ie/news/ireland/2018/1111/1010112-inauguration-of-michael-d-higgins-to-take-place/|title=President stresses need for diversity and inclusion in speech|work=[[RTÉ News]]|date=11 November 2018|access-date=11 November 2018|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181112021631/https://www.rte.ie/news/ireland/2018/1111/1010112-inauguration-of-michael-d-higgins-to-take-place/|archive-date=12 November 2018|url-status=live}}</ref>

On 3 July 2019, Higgins began a three-day state visit to [[Germany]]. While visiting Germany, Higgins met with [[German Chancellor]] [[Angela Merkel]] and [[President of Germany]] [[Frank-Walter Steinmeier]].<ref>{{Cite news |first=Tony |last=Connelly |url=https://www.rte.ie/news/ireland/2019/0703/1059836-president-higgins-germany/ |title=President Higgins on three-day State visit to Germany |work=News Ireland |publisher=[[RTÉ]] |date=3 July 2019 |access-date=21 May 2021 |archive-date=21 May 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210521220638/https://www.rte.ie/news/ireland/2019/0703/1059836-president-higgins-germany/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

In July 2021, Higgins sent a letter to the [[Oireachtas]] expressing concern that there had been a tendency in recent years for him to be sent a large volume of complex legislation to be signed in a short period.<ref>{{cite news|title=President Higgins expresses concern over volume of legislation |url=https://www.rte.ie/news/2021/0729/1238070-higgins-letter-oireachtas/ |publisher=[[RTÉ News and Current Affairs]] |access-date=29 July 2021 |archive-date=29 July 2021 |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20210729170337/http://www.rte.ie/news/2021/0729/1238070-higgins-letter-oireachtas/ |url-status=live }}</ref>

On 15 September 2021, Higgins declined an invitation to attend a church service with Britain's [[Elizabeth II|Queen Elizabeth]] in October to mark Northern Ireland's centenary.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.rte.ie/news/ireland/2021/0915/1246949-centenary-partition-service/|title=President will not attend NI centenary service with Queen Elizabeth|publisher=[[RTÉ News and Current Affairs]]|first=Samantha|last=Libreri|date=15 September 2021|accessdate=17 September 2021}}</ref> Two days later on 17 September, the President defended his decision not to attend the service and decided it would be "inappropriate to attend".<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.rte.ie/news/ireland/2021/0917/1247297-michael-d-higgins/|title=President: Title of NI event is not 'neutral statement'|publisher=[[RTÉ News and Current Affairs]]|date=17 September 2021|accessdate=17 September 2021}}</ref> [[Pope Francis]] described Higgins as a "wise man of today" during an audience in the Vatican.<ref>{{cite news|url=https://www.breakingnews.ie/ireland/pope-francis-says-president-michael-d-higgins-is-a-wise-man-of-today-1185764.html|title=Pope Francis says President Michael D Higgins is a ‘wise man of today’|publisher=BreakingNews.ie|date=17 September 2021|accessdate=17 September 2021}}</ref>

==Writing==
[[File:MD Higgins poem.jpg|thumb|A piece of Higgins' poetry on a plaque in [[Cahir]].]]
As well as having a successful political career Higgins has had a career as a poet and broadcaster and has produced works of non-fiction.<ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.theguardian.com/world/2014/apr/08/michael-d-higgins-irelands-poet-president|title=Michael D Higgins: Ireland's poet president|last1=McDonald|first1=Henry|date=8 April 2014|work=The Guardian|access-date=6 November 2017|last2=correspondent|first2=Ireland|language=en-GB|issn=0261-3077|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20171107112456/https://www.theguardian.com/world/2014/apr/08/michael-d-higgins-irelands-poet-president|archive-date=7 November 2017|url-status=live}}</ref><ref>{{Cite news|url=https://www.irishcentral.com/news/micheal-daniel-higgins-the-man-who-will-be-irelands-new-president-132773323-237419881|title=Michael Daniel Higgins, the man who will be Ireland's new president|date=28 October 2011|work=IrishCentral.com|access-date=6 November 2017|language=en|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20181116080853/https://www.irishcentral.com/news/micheal-daniel-higgins-the-man-who-will-be-irelands-new-president-132773323-237419881|archive-date=16 November 2018|url-status=live}}</ref> He has contributed widely to political and philosophical journals on numerous subjects, among them ideology, the sociology of literature, clientelism in politics,<ref>{{cite book |last1=Higgins |first1=Michael D |title=The Limit of Clientelism. C. Clapman, Private Patronage and Public Power |date=1982 |publisher=Frances Pinter. |location=London|isbn=0312647174|oclc=874469771}}</ref> regionalism and the politics of the media. He wrote and presented a television film on Montserrat, entitled ''The Other Emerald Isle'' for [[Channel 4]] and his documentary on the life of [[Noel Browne]], for [[Raidió Teilifís Éireann|RTÉ]], has also been screened.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.libertiespress.com/New_and_Selected_Poems_SIGNED_BY_THE_AUTHOR/73/ |title=New And Selected Poems |work=Liberties Press |access-date=19 November 2011 |url-status=dead |archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111024174641/http://www.libertiespress.com/New_and_Selected_Poems_Signed_by_the_Author/73/ |archive-date=24 October 2011 }}</ref>

Higgins has had poems published in a number of periodicals, as well as publishing four collections of his poetry, including ''The Betrayal'' (1990), his second book of poems ''The Season of Fire'' (1993) and his latest book ''An Arid Season'' (2004).<ref>{{cite news|url=http://news.xinhuanet.com/english2010/world/2011-10/29/c_131219364.htm|title=Profile: Michael D Higgins: politics and poetry|date=29 October 2011|last=Kennedy|first=Yvonne|agency=Xinhua|access-date=29 October 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111102004904/http://news.xinhuanet.com/english2010/world/2011-10/29/c_131219364.htm|archive-date=2 November 2011|url-status=live}}</ref> His personal notes and work books reside at the [[National Library of Ireland]].<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.independent.ie/entertainment/arts/seamus-heaney-declutters-home-and-donates-personal-notes-to-national-library-2970392.html|title=Seamus Heaney declutters home and donates personal notes to National Library|date=21 December 2011|access-date=21 December 2011|first=Lyndsey|last=Telford|work=[[Irish Independent]]|archive-url=https://archive.today/20120802045337/http://www.independent.ie/entertainment/arts/seamus-heaney-declutters-home-and-donates-personal-notes-to-national-library-2970392.html|archive-date=2 August 2012|url-status=live}}</ref>

news|url=http://www.belfasttelegraph.co.uk/news/local-national/republic-of-ireland/clean-fight-helped-higgins-win-day-16070271.html |archive-url=https://archive.today/20120730113128/http://www.belfasttelegraph.co.uk/news/local-national/republic-of-ireland/clean-fight-helped-higgins-win-day-16070271.html |url-status=dead |archive-date=30 July 2012 |title=Clean fight helped Higgins win day |date=28 October 2011 |work=The Belfast Telegraph |access-date=28 October 2011 }}</ref> He was a noted critic of U.S. foreign policy under the Ronald Reagan administration. In 2005, in response to a column for the ''[[Irish Independent]]'' by [[Kevin Myers]] about the riots then erupting across immigrant areas in France and Britain, he said "the contents of his column today go beyond his usually crafted cowardice, staying one step on the safe side of prosecution for incitement to hatred or racism."<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.independent.ie/opinion/columnists/kevin-myers/kevin-myers-feral-rioters-all-have-one-thing-in-common-a-lack-of-father-figures-2844058.html|title=Feral rioters all have one thing in common – a lack of father figures|last=Myers|first=Kevin|work=[[Irish Independent]]|date=10 August 2011|access-date=28 September 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111210125916/http://www.independent.ie/opinion/columnists/kevin-myers/kevin-myers-feral-rioters-all-have-one-thing-in-common-a-lack-of-father-figures-2844058.html|archive-date=10 December 2011|url-status=live}}</ref>

Higgins has voiced his support for the [[Campaign for the Establishment of a United Nations Parliamentary Assembly]], an organisation which campaigns for democratic reformation of the [[United Nations]], and the creation of a more accountable international political system.<ref>{{Cite news|url=http://en.unpacampaign.org/supporters/overview/?mapcountry=allhea&mapgroup=hea|title=Supporters|last=Assembly|first=Campaign for a UN Parliamentary Assembly|work=Campaign for a UN Parliamentary Assembly|access-date=21 September 2017|language=en-US|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180124055113/http://en.unpacampaign.org/supporters/overview/?mapcountry=allhea&mapgroup=hea|archive-date=24 January 2018|url-status=live}}</ref>

Higgins's eclectic mix of interests also extends to sport; he is a regular at the [[Galway Races]] each summer. He has also previously served as President of [[Galway United F.C.]], is a well known football supporter and regularly attends [[League of Ireland]] games.<ref name="irishexaminer">{{cite web|url=http://www.irishexaminer.com/sport/soccer/michael-d-higgins-interview-statesman-of-the-game-384378.html|title=Michael D Higgins Interview: Statesman of the game|publisher=www.irishexaminer.com|access-date=10 October 2016|date=27 February 2016|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161012074629/http://www.irishexaminer.com/sport/soccer/michael-d-higgins-interview-statesman-of-the-game-384378.html|archive-date=12 October 2016|url-status=live}}</ref> In [[2014 President of Ireland's Cup|2014]] the [[Football Association of Ireland]] introduced a new [[association football in the Republic of Ireland|association football]] [[super cup]], the [[President of Ireland's Cup|President's Cup]], in his honour. On 25 February 2014, the cup itself was officially unveiled with a ceremony at [[Áras an Uachtaráin]].<ref name="rte1">{{cite web|url=http://www.rte.ie/sport/soccer/irish/2014/0225/506528-president-unveils-cup-for-decider/|title=President Michael D Higgins unveils Cup for new competition between SSE Airtricity League and FAI Cup winners|publisher=www.rte.ie|access-date=5 October 2016|date=25 February 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161011230120/http://www.rte.ie/sport/soccer/irish/2014/0225/506528-president-unveils-cup-for-decider/|archive-date=11 October 2016|url-status=live}}</ref><ref name="lfaprescup">{{cite web|url=https://leagueofirelandhistory.wordpress.com/tag/lfa-presidents-cup/|title=LFA President's Cup|publisher=leagueofirelandhistory.wordpress.com|access-date=5 October 2016|date=2 March 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20161014102227/https://leagueofirelandhistory.wordpress.com/tag/lfa-presidents-cup/|archive-date=14 October 2016|url-status=live}}</ref> Higgins subsequently attended the inaugural final at [[Richmond Park (football ground)|Richmond Park]] on 2 March 2014.<ref name="president">{{cite web|url=http://www.president.ie/en/diary/details/president-attends-the-inaugural-presidents-cup-fixture|title=President attends the inaugural President's Cup fixture|publisher=www.president.ie|access-date=10 October 2016|date=2 March 2014|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20160427085128/http://www.president.ie/en/diary/details/president-attends-the-inaugural-presidents-cup-fixture|archive-date=27 April 2016|url-status=live}}</ref>

He is the subject of the song "Michael D. Rocking in the Dáil" by popular [[Tuam]] band [[The Saw Doctors]]. The song first appeared as a B-side on the 1994 single "Small Bit of Love" and is also on the 2002 compilation ''Play It Again, Sham!''.<ref>{{cite news|url=http://www.independent.ie/national-news/its-all-thanks-to-the-saw-doctors-that-michael-ds-rockin-in-the-aras-2920589.html|title=It's all thanks to the Saw Doctors that Michael D's Rockin in the Aras|work=[[Irish Independent]]|date=29 October 2011|access-date=29 October 2011|archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20111101102236/http://www.independent.ie/national-news/its-all-thanks-to-the-saw-doctors-that-michael-ds-rockin-in-the-aras-2920589.html|archive-date=1 November 2011|url-status=live}}</ref>
