'''Colin Hay''' (born 9 November 1968)<ref name=Congress>{{cite web |title= Hay, Colin, 1968- |url= http://id.loc.gov/authorities/names/n95075078.html |publisher= Library of Congress |access-date= 12 August 2014 |quote= data sheet (Hay, Colin S.; b. 11-09-1968) }}</ref> is Professor of Political Sciences at [[Sciences Po]], Paris and Affiliate Professor of [[Political Science|Political Analysis]] at the [[University of Sheffield]], joint editor-in-chief of the journal ''[[Comparative European Politics]]''.<ref name=About>{{cite web |title=Comparative European Politics: about this journal |url= http://www.palgrave-journals.com/cep/about.html |publisher= [[Palgrave Macmillan]] |access-date=24 February 2016 }}</ref> and Managing Editor of the journal ''[[New Political Economy (journal)|New Political Economy]]''.<ref>{{cite web |url=https://www.tandfonline.com/action/journalInformation?show=editorialBoard&journalCode=cnpe20 |title=New Political Economy Editorial board |author=<!--Not stated--> |date=31 October 2020 |website=tandfonline.com |publisher=New Political Economy |access-date=31 October 2020 |quote=}}</ref>

== Education ==
Hay studied Social and Political Science at [[Clare College, Cambridge]], and moved to the Department of Sociology at [[Lancaster University]] to research his [[PhD]] under the supervision of [[Bob Jessop]].<ref name="profile">{{cite web|url=http://www.cee.sciences-po.fr/en/le-centre/research-team/856-colin-hay.html|title=Colin Hay|publisher=Sciences Po|access-date=2014-11-02}}</ref>

== Career ==
After completing his PhD, Hay worked at the [[University of Birmingham]], where he was head of the Department of Political Science and International Studies between 2002 and 2005. He moved to [[Paris]] in 2013.<ref name="profile"/>
