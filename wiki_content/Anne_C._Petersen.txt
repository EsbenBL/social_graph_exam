'''Anne Cheryl Petersen''' (born September 11, 1944) is an American psychologist. She is one of the founders of the [[Association for Psychological Science]].<ref name=":0">{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=Anne C Petersen|url=https://www.nsf.gov/od/oise/OISE-AC/Bios/Petersen%20-%20Bio.pdf|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=}}</ref> In 2000, she was listed as one of the most influential psychologists by the ''Encyclopedia of Psychology''.<ref name=":1">{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=Women Leaders: Anne C Petersen, PhD|url=https://www.apa.org/monitor/julaug07/petersen#|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=American Psychological Association}}</ref> Her work focuses primarily on adolescent development and gender issues.<ref>{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=Anne C Petersen|url=http://worldcat.org/identities/lccn-n79014013/|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=World Cat Identities}}</ref>

== Early life and education ==
Petersen was born in [[Little Falls, Minnesota]] on September 11, 1944, to Rhoda Pauline Studley and Franklin Hanks. Her mother, Studley, was a secretary and her father, Hanks, was a coach and a teacher.<ref>{{Cite web|last=Publishers|first=E. C. M.|title=Rhonda Pauline Studley|url=https://www.hometownsource.com/morrison_county_record/announcements/rhonda-pauline-studley/article_51d96cb7-e749-5b24-873c-5231af0e4b70.html|access-date=2020-11-28|website=hometownsource.com|language=en}}</ref>

Petersen studied as an undergraduate at the [[University of Chicago]], receiving a Bachelor of Arts in Mathematics in 1966.<ref name=":0" /> She continued her education at the University of Chicago, receiving a Master of Science in Statistics in 1972, and a Doctor of Philosophy in Measurement, Evaluation, and Statistical Analysis in 1973.<ref name=":0" />

== Career and research ==
Petersen has published over 300 research articles concerning areas of psychology, especially adolescent health and development, gender issues, science policy, and global issues.<ref name=":0" /> Petersen worked as a professor at [[Pennsylvania State University]] from August 1982 to March 1992. On July 1, 1987, she became the first dean of the College of Health and Human Development and Family Studies at Pennsylvania State University.<ref>{{Cite web|title=History of the College of Health and Human Development {{!}} Penn State College of Health and Human Development|url=https://hhd.psu.edu/about/history-college-health-and-human-development|access-date=2020-11-28|website=hhd.psu.edu}}</ref> In 1992, Petersen accepted a position as a professor at the Institute of Child Development at the [[University of Minnesota, Twin Cities]]. From May 1992 to March 1995, Petersen worked at the University of Minnesota, Twin Cities.<ref name=":3">{{Cite web|last=|first=|date=|title=Anne C. Petersen|url=https://www.researchgate.net/profile/Anne_Petersen4|url-status=live|archive-url=|archive-date=|access-date=|website=Research Gate}}</ref>

Petersen continued her professional career at [[Stanford University]]. From August 2006 to June 2009, Petersen was a deputy director and professor at the [[Center for Advanced Study in the Behavioral Sciences]] at Stanford .<ref name=":3" /> In 2010, she accepted a position as a research professor at the [[University of Michigan]]. From January 2010 to the present, Petersen has been at the Center for Human Growth and Development at the University of Michigan.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Anne C. Petersen {{!}} U-M LSA International Institute|url=https://ii.umich.edu/ii/people/all/a/annepete.html|access-date=2020-11-28|website=ii.umich.edu|language=en}}</ref>

== Philanthropy ==
Petersen was the Senior Vice President Programs and Corporate Officer of the [[W. K. Kellogg Foundation]] for 10 years.<ref name=":4">{{Cite web|date=2014-06-24|title=Anne C. Petersen|url=https://www.youthactionnet.org/people/anne-c-petersen|access-date=2020-11-28|website=YouthActionNet|language=en}}</ref> The Kellogg Foundation funds programs that benefit children from disadvantaged communities in primarily low income areas.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Who We Are|url=https://www.wkkf.org:443/who-we-are/overview|access-date=2020-11-28|website=W.K. Kellogg Foundation|language=en-US}}</ref> As Senior Vice President, Petersen was responsible for all United States, Latin American, and southern African programs in the organization.<ref>{{Cite web|title=Board and Staff – GPA|url=https://globalphilanthropyalliance.org/board-and-staff/|access-date=2020-11-28|language=en-US}}</ref>

Following her work with the Kellogg Foundation, Petersen founded the non-profit, public foundation Global Philanthropy Alliance (GPA),<ref name=":5">{{Cite book|last=Petersen|first=Anne C.|url=https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK195986/|title=Biosketches of Committee Members|last2=Joseph|first2=Joshua|last3=Feit|first3=Monica|last4=Committee on Child Maltreatment Research|first4=Policy|last5=Board on Children|first5=Youth|last6=Justice|first6=Committee on Law and|last7=Medicine|first7=Institute of|last8=Council|first8=National Research|date=2014-03-25|publisher=National Academies Press}}</ref> which funds "early stage youth-led entrepreneur organizations in Africa."<ref name=":4" /> The Alliance makes small grants to organizations in African countries.<ref>{{Cite web|title=What We Do – GPA|url=https://globalphilanthropyalliance.org/what-we-do/|access-date=2020-11-28}}</ref> An African board and country committees help to fund the GPA. In addition to being one of the founders of the Global Philanthropy Alliance, Petersen is also the president.<ref name=":5" />
