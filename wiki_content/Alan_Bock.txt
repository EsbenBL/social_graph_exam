'''Alan W. Bock''' (December 3, 1943 â€“ May 18, 2011)<ref>Justin Raimondo, [http://www.antiwar.com/blog/2011/05/18/alan-bock-rip/ RIP, Alan Bock], [[Antiwar.com]], May 18, 2011.</ref> was an [[United States of America|American]] [[libertarianism|libertarian]] author. He was a senior editorial writer and former editorial page editor for the ''[[Orange County Register]]'' for over 25 years.<ref name=about>{{cite web|title=Alan Bock|url=http://www.alanbock.com|publisher=Alan Bock|accessdate=12 May 2013}}</ref>  He wrote regular columns for [[WorldNetDaily]], [[LewRockwell.com]], and [[Antiwar.com]] and was a contributing editor at ''[[Liberty (1987)|Liberty]]'' magazine.<ref name=about /> He had also been published in ''[[The American Conservative]]''.<ref>Bock, Alan (2004-11-08) [http://www.amconmag.com/article/2004/nov/08/00013/ Libertarian Resistance], ''[[The American Conservative]]''</ref>

==Career==
Bock was also an active public speaker having experience in radio and television. He spoke at such forums as the [[Cato Institute]], [[Reason Foundation]], the [[Drug Policy Foundation]], [[American Enterprise Institute]], Freedom Summit, the Liberty Editors' Conference and the Festival of Freedom. In the 1970s, Bock spent eight years in Washington, D.C., where he worked for two different congressmen. He then formed a libertarian lobbying organization called Libertarian Advocate. He also spent some time working as a radio talk-show host where he appeared on [[CNN]], [[Fox News]], [[MSNBC]], [[PBS]], and countless others. He contributed to ''[[Reason (magazine)|Reason]]'', ''[[The Freeman|Freeman]]'', ''[[National Review]]'', and ''[[Harvard Business Review]]''.<ref name=about />

==Education==
Bock attended the [[University of California, Los Angeles]] on a National Merit scholarship.<ref name=about />

==Views and Positions==
Bock had been critical of both parties for their support of an aggressive foreign policy.<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://original.antiwar.com/bock/2004/07/30/out-toughing-the-republicans/|title = Out-Toughing the Republicans|date = 30 July 2004}}</ref> He had also been critical of [[imperialism]] and nation-building, and believed that "the U.S. empire is in the process of winding down."<ref>{{Cite web|url=http://original.antiwar.com/bock/2009/07/05/independence-empire-dont-mesh/|title = Independence, Empire Don't Mesh|date = 6 July 2009}}</ref>

==Death==
Bock died on May 18, 2011 after entering hospice due to cancer.<ref>{{cite news|title=Liberty loses a friend: In memory of Alan Bock|url=http://www.ocregister.com/opinion/bock-301133-alan-liberty.html|publisher=The Orange County Register|accessdate=18 May 2011|first=Cathy|last=Taylor}}</ref><ref>[http://www.ocregister.com/articles/-301196--.html Register editorial writer Alan Bock, 67, dies], Orange County Register, May 19, 2011.</ref>  He was 67 and was living in [[Lake Elsinore, California]].<ref name=about />
