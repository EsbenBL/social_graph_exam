'''Jeffrey Marc Wooldridge''' (born 1960) is an [[United States|American]] [[econometrics|econometrician]] at [[Michigan State University]]. He is known for his theoretical contributions to analysis of [[Cross-sectional data|cross-sectional]] and [[panel data]].

After graduating in [[computer science]] and [[economics]] from the [[University of California, Berkeley]] in 1982, Wooldridge earned a Ph.D. in economics from the [[University of California, San Diego]] in 1986. He spent five years as an assistant professor of economics at the [[Massachusetts Institute of Technology]], before joining faculty at [[Michigan State University]], where he became a professor in 1993. He was designated University Distinguished Professor in 2001.<ref>http://econ.msu.edu/faculty/wooldridge/jmwvita.pdf</ref>

Wooldridge is a Fellow of the [[Econometric Society]] and of the [[Journal of Econometrics]]. He is also known as the author of the popular econometrics textbooks ''Introductory Econometrics: A Modern Approach'' (6th edition, 2016) and ''Econometric Analysis of Cross Section and Panel Data'' (2nd edition, 2010).
